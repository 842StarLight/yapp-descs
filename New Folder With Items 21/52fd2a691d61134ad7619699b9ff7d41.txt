<h2>Radar Applications Engineer</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p><b>About the Company: </b> RADA Technologies LLC is a defense radar company specializing in the design, development, production and sales of All-Threat, Air Surveillance radars. RADA offers advanced pulse-Doppler, multi-mission, AESA radars for tactical applications. These software-defined radars introduce hemispheric spatial coverage with excellent performance-to price ratio.</p>
<p>Our location is in Germantown, MD with a planned move to a larger manufacturing facility upon completion. Our small size is ideal for innovation, collaboration and professional growth, and our combined staff expertise is both comprehensive and diverse. We are always interested in adding new members to our team who thrive in this environment.</p>
<p><b>Job Description: </b>The Applications Engineer will be responsible for the technical interactions with our customers to best demonstrate the capabilities of our systems, understand the customers end use application, and develop specific solutions to address their needs. The Application Engineer will be responsible for field testing, providing support and training to our customers, and for the technical transition from the design department into production. They will be an integral part of the business capture process for the company.</p>
<p><b>Experience: </b> 3-5 years of experience in RF and Radar systems, or military experience as a Radar repairer or operator. A degree in Electrical Engineering, Physics, Computer Science or Mathematics is preferred.</p>
<p>The successful candidate will:</p>
<ul>
<li>Be a US citizen capable of attaining a US Security Clearance. Candidates already in possession of a clearance are preferred.</li>
<li>Have an understanding of Radar types, concepts and theory of operation</li>
<li>Be physically capable of lifting up to 50lbs alone, or 100lbs in a 2-person lift</li>
<li>Be willing to travel up to 50% of the time</li>
<li>Be able to plan and execute radar test plans</li>
<li>Be able to work well both individually and as part of a team.</li>
</ul>
