<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Position Summary
</b></h3>
<div>The Senior Compensation Analyst is responsible for supporting the ENGIE Insight Leadership (current business) and executive leadership teams of ENGIE Impact by leading (as the point of contact for NorAm) the strategy, design, implementation and administration of ENGIE Impacts Compensation (Base and Variable) plans.<br>
<br>
</div>
<div></div>
<br>
<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Role Description
</b></h3>
<div>Compensation Analysis:
</div>
<ul>
<li>Partners with ENGIE Impact Leadership and HR Business Partners to ensure complete and accurate market analysis, job evaluation, and internal leveling of new and updated positions in an effort to ensure both external competitiveness and internal equity.
</li>
<li>In partnership with ENGIE Impact Leadership, responsible for the design, administration, and refinement of new pay practices focused on the evolving Sustainability Solutions consulting business.
</li>
<li>Creates complex workforce forecasts including cost structure impacts driven by headcount and pay practices.
</li>
<li>Project manages enterprise wide compensation projects. Actively Partners with ENGIE North America Business Unit (ENGIE Noram BU) Compensation.
</li>
<li>Reviews current salary practices while working collaboratively (including with ENGIE NorAm BU) to design compensation frameworks, creating or modifying job families, that support and align with strategic initiatives in the business, aligning current plans.
</li>
<li>Designs appropriate metrics and monitors effectiveness of compensation policies, guidelines and procedures.
</li>
<li>Models compensation cost implications, identifies trends, changes in legal requirements, benchmarks (wages and total rewards) and makes recommendations for senior management.
</li>
<li>Organize remuneration committees and salary reviews
</li>
<li>Works closely with HRBPs on matters related to offers, retention and departures, advising on all Comp&amp;Ben issues.
</li>
</ul>
<div>Variable Pay program(s) design and administration:
</div>
<ul>
<li>Responsible for the design of the annual Short Term Incentive program (STI) used in the U.S.
</li>
<li>Partners with Leadership to design, model, and administer performance based bonus programs for individuals and groups.
</li>
</ul>
<div>Sales Incentive Program Design and Administration:
</div>
<ul>
<li>Contributes to sales compensation philosophy and provides decision-making with regard to the compensation alternatives and/or recommendations.
</li>
<li>Project manages monthly, quarterly and annual Incentive Payout, including exceptions, year end “true-up”, new hire quota setting tools and approaches, manager or employee training, through to lessons learned.
</li>
<li>Partner with the Finance organization to model commission rates and cost analyses of the sales compensation plans.
</li>
<li>Stays abreast of market trends and serve as “go to” resource for industry awareness and knowledge on compensation.
</li>
</ul>
</div>
