<p><i>
Purpose:</i></p>
<div><p>
The position leads a team responsible for managing credit risk for the Bank’s ~$10 billion U.S. energy portfolio which covers the Oil &amp; Gas, Pipelines, Refining, Oilfield Services and Chemicals sectors. The candidate will have coverage responsibility for all products that result in direct credit risk to bank clients across these sectors, and will work closely with the various product teams responsible for marketing these products. The candidate will report to the Head of U.S. Credit Risk and will be located in Houston.</p></div>
<br>
<p><i>
Key Accountability:</i></p>
<div><div>
<ul><li>
Adjudicate credit requests with delegated credit authority with broad product coverage including loans, loan products, derivatives, commodities, banking products, etc.</li></ul>
<ul><li>
Ensure that the management of portfolio credit risk is performed in a manner that is in compliance with governing regulations, consistent with all internal policies and procedures and operates within the established Enterprise and U.S. risk appetites.</li></ul>
<ul><li>
Participate in the U.S. Credit Committee as a voting member to contribute to oversight, governance, management and establishment of risk appetite and policies for U.S. Credit Risk Management; as well as provide representation for the energy portfolio.</li></ul>
<ul><li>
Assist in the training and development for junior members of team.</li></ul>
<ul><li>
Be familiar with and maintain best practices in line with industry standards and regulatory expectations.</li></ul>
<ul><li>
Assist Head of U.S. Credit Risk to communicate with senior management, head office and the relevant risk committees to ensure proper risk management and sound corporate governance across the U.S. operations.</li></ul>
<ul><li>
Represent the U.S. Credit Risk Management along with the Head of U.S. Credit Risk at the Enterprise Senior Credit Committee including presentation of credits/transactions for the U.S. energy portfolio.</li></ul>
<ul><li>
Participate in review and drafting of local credit risk policies and procedures.</li></ul>
<ul><li>
Participate in, and assist Head of U.S. Credit Risk with, internal audit and regulatory reviews.</li></ul>
<ul><li>
Understand how the Bank’s risk appetite and risk culture should be considered in day-to-day activities and decisions.</li></ul>
<ul><li>
Create an environment in which the team pursues effective and efficient operations, while ensuring the adequacy, adherence to and effectiveness of day-to-day business controls to meet obligations with respect to credit risk.</li></ul>
<ul><li>
Build a high performance environment and implement a people strategy that attracts, retains, develops and motivates the team by fostering an inclusive work environment; communicating vision/values/business strategy and managing succession and development planning for the team.
</li></ul>
</div></div>
<br>
<p><i>Education &amp; Experience:</i></p>
<ul>
<li>
Minimum Bachelor’s degree. Master's degree, recognized professional designation in a specialized area of finance or business, or equivalent, is an asset</li>
<li>
At least 5-7 years of credit risk experience with progressively higher levels of responsibility</li>
<li>
Specific experience and knowledge of the relevant energy sectors</li>
<li>
Extensive knowledge of corporate lending products, counterparty derivative transactions and structures, and documentation</li>
<li>
Expert knowledge and understanding of US regulatory considerations and issues</li>
<li>
Leadership skills as demonstrated by the management of a team of risk management professionals
</li>
<li>Bring an in-depth and highly contemporary knowledge of the evolving risk management environment</li>
<li>
An expert ability to balance competing or conflicting goals of various departments/countries/stakeholders, requiring a mature, diplomatic approach and highly developed negotiation and influencing skills</li>
<li>
Expert communication/facilitation and presentation skills for developing communication strategies for executive approval</li>
<li>
Provide training, mentoring and coaching internally</li>
<li>
Effectively liaise with industry participants, regulatory bodies and various other external and internal parties</li>
<li>
Solid working knowledge of credit risk management frameworks and processes</li>
<li>
Strong relationship building skills to build an effective working relationships across the local credit risk organization, as well as US and Toronto based senior management</li>
<li>
Highly organized with an ability to work in a fast-paced, multi-stakeholder environment and manage multiple priorities</li>
<li>
Creative problem solving and the ability to motivate others and implement new solutions</li>
</ul>
