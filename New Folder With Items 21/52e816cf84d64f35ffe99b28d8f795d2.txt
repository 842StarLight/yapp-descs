<h2>Power Systems Offering Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<b>Introduction</b><br>
Ready to re-imagine the product management role? At IBM, as an Offering Manager, you are responsible for the full lifecycle of an offering: from validating need to determining the business model and defining the solution requirements. Going beyond the traditional focus of timetables and budgets, you use your keen insights into what customers want in their own business plan - and work across disciplines to build the best solutions to a problem. If being the CEO of your offering excites you, consider an offering management career at IBM.<br>
<br>
<b>Your Role and Responsibilities</b><br>
<div></div>
<div>Job High level Overview:</div>
<div>
Globally responsible for the IBM Power Systems Enterprise and Cloud Offerings. This technically includes hardware and software used by most of the worlds top industries to drive their intensive workloads. The day to day activities include managing existing products in the field with related IBM revenue, bringing new compelling products and capabilities to market to drive and maintain IBM revenue and profit growth.<br>
</div>
<div>
<br>
Job Details:</div>
<div>
Drive and manage both the revenue and cost elements of Power Systems Enterprise offerings: profit &amp; loss (P&amp;L).</div>
<div>
Oversee and interlock with marketing, sales, and development organizations related to respective offerings</div>
<div>
<br>
Responsibilities include:</div>
i.Management of memory development engineers, hardware procedure engineers (software), characterization engineers, modeling, and memory subsystem project managers<br>
ii.Global execution responsibilities for development and delivery of the Memory Subsystem supporting IBM Systems Group roadmap.
<br>
iii.Maintain Technology Development hardware roadmap and goals.
<br>
iv.Support schedule, project checkpoints, resource sizing and staffing to ensure project deliverables in a highly matrixed global environment.
<br>
v.Project manage with high level of collaboration across hardware, firmware, system, subsystem, and software stack.<br>
vi.Drive execution interlock, foster optimization, and establish process improvements.<br>
<br>
