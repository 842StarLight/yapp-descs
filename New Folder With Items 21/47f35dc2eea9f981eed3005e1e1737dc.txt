<h2>Sourcing Manager – Technology Sourcing – Datacenter & Co-Location Services Sourcing</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div>Job Description<br>
<br>
<p><b>Sourcing Manager – Technology Sourcing – Datacenter &amp; Co-Location Services Sourcing</b></p>
<p><b>
Austin, TX, Foster City, CA, Ashburn, VA or Denver, CO</b></p>
<p>
Visa Technology Procurement is seeking a Sourcing Manager to support the Datacenter and Co-location services category. The two primary functions of this role are: (1) identify and negotiate cost savings and (2) negotiate contractual language. This role supports the VP of IaaS Engineering and will manage a large portfolio of Visa Datacenter and Co-location services suppliers and deals.</p>
<p><b>
Category Expertise</b></p>
<ul>
<li><p>
Work with the Category Executives and Visa Technology to create the Supplier / Product Strategy for the Datacenter and Co-location services sub-category.</p></li>
<li><p>
Understand the Visa product and service requirements, the suppliers providing the data center and co-location services, their sales and service models, and effectively communicate these to Sourcing executives and Technology stakeholders.</p></li>
<li><p>
Perform market analysis to support the Visa requirements and drive recommendations to the Technology teams. The Sourcing Manager will be expected to be an expert in the Data Center and Co-location space.</p></li>
<li><p><b><i>
This role supports Visa’s Operations and Infrastructure organization and maps to the Global Sourcing Director who manages Compute, Storage and Data Center/Co-location procurement for Visa, globally</i></b></p></li>
</ul>
<p><b>
 Sourcing Capabilities - Commercial &amp; Contract Negotiations</b></p>
<ul>
<li><p>
Negotiate and drive best-in-class deals for Visa. This is a strategic role and will include numerous enterprise-level engagements.</p></li>
<li><p>
Drive multi-vendor strategies to optimize Visa’s leverage and reduce operational risk.</p></li>
<li><p>
Effectively use RFX events to support Visa’s Technology organization.</p></li>
<li><p>
Perform savings tracking and report realized savings on a continuous basis.</p></li>
<li><p>
Negotiate contract terms and conditions as a first line of review, in partnership with Visa Legal.</p></li>
<li><p>
Define and communicate the Sourcing Strategy to business partners, Sourcing leadership and other stakeholders.</p></li>
</ul>
<p><b>
 Relationship Building</b></p>
<ul>
<li><p>
Build strong relationships with Technology business partners to drive Sourcing engagement. This role requires constant engagement with business partners and deep involvement in their projects and planning activities.</p></li>
<li><p>
Provide continuous written and verbal updates to the business partners, suppliers and other stakeholders on the status of deal.</p></li>
<li><p>
Regularly update executives at all levels of the company on deal status, category reporting, etc. This role will have direct visibility to senior-level executives across Visa.</p></li>
</ul>
</div>
<br>
<div>Qualifications<br>
<br>
<p><b>Basic Qualifications:</b></p>
<ul><li><p>
4 years of work experience with a Bachelor’s Degree or at least 2 years of work experience with an Advanced degree (e.g. Masters, MBA, JD, MD) or 0 years of work experience with a PhD degree</p></li></ul>
<p><b>
Preferred Qualifications:</b></p>
<ul>
<li><p>
5+ years of experience as a sourcing and/or procurement professional, preferably with a focus on Data Center and Co-location sourcing and/or Technology spend/vendor relationships.</p></li>
<li><p>
3+ years of experience assisting in, or leading, the development and management of category plans or sourcing strategies.</p></li>
<li><p>
Demonstrated understanding of the Data Center and Co-location, categories, including major suppliers in the category, key contract terms and emerging market trends.</p></li>
<li><p>
Excellent written, oral and presentation skills and an ability to synthesize complex and technical information into clear recommendations on course of action.</p></li>
<li><p>
Demonstrated technology agreement negotiation experience.</p></li>
<li><p>
Experience managing technology business users, including excellent relationship management skills.</p></li>
<li><p>
Ability to influence at an executive level both internally and externally.</p></li>
<li><p>
High level of self-motivation and initiative, and ability to operate as a team player.</p></li>
<li><p>
Willingness to offer thought leadership, to think creatively, and to offer new ways to approach old problems</p></li>
<li><p>
Undergraduate degree required, preferable in business, economics, computer science or engineering related field; graduate degree in business, economics or engineering a plus</p></li>
</ul>

Additional Information<br>
<br>
<p><b>Additional Information about Interviewing with Visa in Technology Sourcing:</b></p>
<p><b>
In Visa Technology Sourcing, we get technical fast. Our business partners demand it and we like to support them the right way! This is a highly technical and highly engaged role. If you apply and are granted an interview, you can expect the following:</b></p>
<ul>
<li><p>
A detailed, onsite skills test, in which you’ll be given a technical problem and will be asked to model it in a spreadsheet and present an executive-ready communication about it.</p></li>
<li>
<p>
You can expect to be asked detailed questions about your background:</p>
<ul>
<li><p>
If you are a career Sourcing person, expect to be asked about deals you managed, including the spend, success factors and your sourcing strategy.</p></li>
<li><p>
Expect to be asked fundamentals about technology.</p></li>
<li><p>
You’ll meet with technical business partners as well as Visa Sourcing execs to assess your sourcing and business acumen.</p></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p>
Don’t let any of the above worry you! We demand a lot of ourselves and we like to be up front about what we look for on this team. If you’ve got the chops, we’ve got a role for you!</p>
