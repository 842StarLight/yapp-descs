<div><b>
The Opportunity</b></div>
<p></p>
<div>
Goodman is seeking a professional, skilled individual for our Controls Design Engineer 3 position.</div>
<div>
The Controls Engineer is responsible for the design, configuration, integration and implementation of manufacturing process control systems. This includes supporting process changes and projects to accommodate new models or products and to improve the operational efficiency of manufacturing equipment. Skilled, hands-on problem solving is mixed with software programing of various types of controls components. The Controls Engineer also works directly with the production engineering and maintenance teams to provide enhanced troubleshooting and controls software support for manufacturing equipment downtime issues. If you meet the qualifications listed below, then we invite you to apply for our open position by visiting our website http://www.careersatgoodman.com and submitting your resume.</div>

<div><b>
About Goodman</b></div>
<p></p>
<div>
Houston-based Goodman is a leading supplier of heating, ventilation and air conditioning (HVAC) products for residential and commercial use and is a member of Daikin Industries, Ltd. . Goodman's products are predominantly marketed under the Goodman® and Amana® brand names, and are sold through company-operated and independent distribution networks with more than 950 total distribution points throughout North America. Goodman is ISO 14001:2004 accredited, an international certification that recognizes manufacturing processes and policies that are more environmentally sustainable. Daikin Industries is a Fortune 1000 company with more than 44,000 employees worldwide and is recognized as the number one HVAC manufacturer in the world. The company has manufacturing operations in 18 countries and a sales presence in more than 90 countries and is headquartered in Osaka, Japan. For more information, visit www.goodmanmfg.com</div>

<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Responsibilities
</b></h2>
<div><div><div>
<ul>
<li>Support manufacturing process control systems and automation.</li>
<li>
Understand and troubleshoot various PLC, HMI, VFD and servo programs and modify as required.</li>
<li>
Diagnose issues with plant Ethernet, Ethernet/IP, EtherCAT and DeviceNet communication networks.</li>
<li>
Provide effective controls support to resolve manufacturing equipment failures, faults and runtime issues in a timely manner to minimize downtime and lost production.</li>
<li>
Identify root causes of equipment issues and develop solutions to eliminate reoccurrence.</li>
<li>
Install and support the interface of manufacturing equipment to the plant network for recipe control and data collection.</li>
<li>
Interpret and prepare electrical drawings and schematics.</li>
<li>
Support various external devices connected to systems such as label printers and scanners.</li>
<li>
Identify and implement process improvements to increase the operational efficiency of manufacturing equipment.</li>
<li>
Document program changes and improvements for lessons learned and group communication.</li>
<li>
Interface well with other teams to solve production problems.</li>
<li>
Assist production engineering and maintenance teams in troubleshooting and with repairs.</li>
<li>
Prepare technical reports and presentations.</li>
<li>
Analyze problems in a high pressure production atmosphere.</li>
<li>
Adapt well to changing responsibilities and work environment.</li>
</ul>
