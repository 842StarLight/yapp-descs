<h2>Staff Accountant</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>At Leanpath, we’re on a mission to make food waste prevention everyday practice in the world’s kitchens. Our customers include leading foodservice and hospitality organizations—including Google, IKEA, Aramark, and Sodexo. We provide our customers with cutting-edge technology and services to help them cut their food waste in half and change the kitchen culture to operate more sustainable operations.</p>
<p>
Since our founding in 2004, we have catalyzed a global food waste movement, creating a new technology category for food waste prevention, engaging thousands of front-line foodservice workers, and growing our offices to include locations in Portland (Beaverton), Oregon, London, Singapore, Barcelona and Melbourne.</p>
<p>
Are you interested in working in a purpose-driven organization and tackling one of the largest global issues of our time? If so, we encourage you to read on.</p>
<p><b>
The Role</b></p>
<p>
We are looking for a Staff Accountant, who will be based in our headquarters office in Beaverton, OR. This new role will work hand-in-hand with our Senior Accountant as we grow our finance team. This person will not only assist in the accounting for the operation of the business but will also be someone that is excited for the opportunity to influence a wide variety of accounting, financial, and business activities with a focus on improving operational efficiency. This is a tremendous growth opportunity for the right candidate who is looking to be an impact player in a fast-growing business.</p>
<p><b>
Essential Duties and Responsibilities</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Assist in the monthly financial close, including preparation of journal entries, standard accruals and correcting entries</li>
<li>Reconcile balance sheet accounts to ensure financial results are accurately represented and in conformance with US GAAP</li>
<li>Create detailed documentation of processes and procedures</li>
<li>Verify and code credit card statements</li>
<li>Reconcile and verify sales &amp; use tax payments</li>
<li>Accounts payable, including internal expense report reimbursement</li>
<li>Accounts receivable, collections, and invoicing</li>
<li>Drive and participate in continuous process improvement efforts</li>
<li>Assist with special projects and other duties as assigned</li>
<li>Assist in implementation of new ERP and billings software</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Basic Requirements</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor's Degree in Accounting</li>
<li>2+ years accounting experience</li>
<li>CPA/CMA designation preferred, not required</li>
<li>Public accounting experience preferred, not required</li>
<li>Solid understanding of internal control objectives and applications</li>
<li>Strong problem solving and analytical skills</li>
<li>Self-motivated, independent and proactive worker with a high level of organization, professionalism and attention to detail</li>
<li>Familiarity and willingness to work in a deadline-oriented, fast-paced team environment</li>
<li>Professional level verbal and written communication skills</li>
<li>Ability to meet deadlines and organize time, projects, and detailed information</li>
<li>Ability to prioritize and adjust to the changing demands of the role</li>
<li>Experience with QuickBooks, NetSuite, Advance Excel</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Compensation</b></p>
<p>
Salary is dependent on experience. We pay 100% of the employee’s premium for health, dental and vision coverage (employee pays for spouse or family). We currently offer 20 days of PTO to start, and offer a flexible work environment with a group of smart, committed, friendly and supportive colleagues.</p>
