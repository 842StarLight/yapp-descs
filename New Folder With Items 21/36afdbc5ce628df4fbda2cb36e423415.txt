<h2>Engagement Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>Company Description</p>
<p>
Govini is a data and analytics firm dedicated to transforming the business of national security through data science and machine learning. Through its analytic platform, Govini delivers objective, decision-grade information at scale to the U.S. national security enterprise, so that leaders can better direct investments to innovation and modernization. Govini has offices in Arlington, Virginia, San Francisco, California, and Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania.</p>
<p>
Job Description</p>
<p>
We are seeking driven, outcome-oriented Engagement Managers to join our Advisory Services team. Engagement Managers are responsible for delivering solutions to the data challenges presented by the most senior leaders of the U.S. national security enterprise. They are creative, entrepreneurial, and can see the big picture. Responsible for leading teams, they also roll up their sleeves and will wear many hats to lead the team to success. Engagement Managers are responsible for planning and managing projects and putting Govini data and software in the hands of government users. They are are passionate about our users’ success and ensure excellence in delivery, ranging from answering tough analytic challenges to collaborating with Govini’s Product team to send market demand and user needs into Govini’s offerings.</p>

<p>Qualifications</p>
<p>
To succeed in this job, you must understand people, care about technology, and have business acumen. You like people - a lot - and are eager to find ways to help them succeed. You are comfortable in board room meetings presenting ideas, making compelling arguments, and maintaining flexibility in thought to recognize someone else may be right. You bring the conversation back to what matters, and prioritize resources to ensure goals are met. You are conscientious and detail-oriented. You’re excited by connecting people and sharing ideas. You like to play devil’s advocate and ask the “What if?” and “How do we fail?” questions. You regularly assess individual and team performance to reduce cognitive biases and refine products and internal processes.
</p>
