<h2>Director of Purchasing</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div></div>
<div><div>
<div>
<div>KushCo Holdings is a publicly traded company that has successfully positioned themselves as a “one stop shop” for the cannabis market through a combination of organic growth and strategic acquisitions.</div>
<div>
</div>
<div>
Our brands include Kush Supply Co., a dynamic sales and distribution platform that has become the nation’s largest and most respected distributor of vaporizer products, packaging, supplies, and accessories. Kush Energy, a provider of ultra-pure hydrocarbon gases and solvents to the cannabis sector. The Hybrid Creative, a premier creative design agency for cannabis and non-cannabis brands and Koleto Innovations, our research and development arm driving intellectual property development and acquisitions.</div>
<div>
<br>

<div><b>
About the Role</b></div>
<div>
This individual will be responsible for developing and manage strategy to optimize performance across the purchasing department. This role will lead the standardization and transformation of the purchasing department on a global scale by establishing a clear vendor path for growth, cost savings, spend analysis, contract reviews, pricing, supplier management and purchasing.</div>
</div>
<div><div>
<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>
What you will do (Essential Functions)</b></h3>
<div><ul>
<li>
Project and time management: Ability to manage large, complex projects and break them down and execute into manageable pieces, develop functional processes, and deliver them in a successful and timely manners</li>
<li>
Improve the suppliers’ on-time delivery performance to enable inventory reductions throughout the supply chain and X-Factory dates</li>
<li>
Develop negotiation strategies and participate in negotiations with vendors on price, delivery, terms, etc.</li>
<li>
Ensure compliance with policies and programs in alignment with sourcing strategy
</li>
<li>Develop and execute a global market leading purchasing strategy including purchasing, production lead-times, OTIF for Pos status and supplier payment terms</li>
<li>
Coordinate with cross-functional teams to develop process/project plans (typical functions include Planning, Sourcing, Logistics, Regional DC Teams and Sales in order to improve process flows and product availability at the warehouse)</li>
<li>
Strategically develop and implement commodity strategies covering all commodities - direct and indirect, and supplier processes throughout a global enterprise to deliver maximum value, leverage, and standardization</li>
<li>
Establish and maintain measurable performance metrics for all levels of purchasing activities to include, but not limited to, supplier performance, internal order placement performance, buyer activity levels, financial (AP) performance, and material shortage elimination</li>
<li>
Analyze and negotiate annual multi-million-dollar contracts</li>
<li>
Manage and mentor the purchasing team of 6-10 employees and monitor daily department workload of incoming requisitions, special quote requests, and to ensure the purchasing staff is meeting the company’s needs in a timely manner</li>
<li>
Develop the purchasing expertise of the team</li>
<li>
Problem solve and answer frequent questions on price, price disputes, returns, delivery, quality, quantity, freight, packaging, definitions, and sourcing, accounting, sales, and upper management. This may involve research, data extraction, and frequent interaction with vendors and internal personnel</li>
<li>
Review commodity pricing and news on a daily basis, especially scrap steel, foundry additives, stainless steel surcharges, and rubber</li>
<li>
Meet all company work schedule and attendance expectations and perform within the physical and environmental demands of the job</li>
<li>
In conjunction with the CFO and other senior managers, determine the degree to which we use cash to enhance our profitability when making large purchases</li>
<li>
Review department staffing requirements and determine work assignments and functions within the department</li>
<li>
Maintain the capital general ledger list for the company</li>
</ul></div>
</div></div>
