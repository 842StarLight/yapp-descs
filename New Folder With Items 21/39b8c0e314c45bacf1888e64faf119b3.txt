We are excited to share the opportunity for an experienced <b>BIM Technician/VDC Specialist</b> to join our team and look forward to reviewing your resume.</p>
<p>
As a <b>BIM Technician/VDC Specialist</b> with Wilson Electric, you are the go-to person on the project for modeling, documentation, and verification of design intent. You will contribute to the team by using your knowledge and skillset to manage the process of virtually constructing a building/project and documenting the design contract documents accurately (i.e. constructability drawings for field personnel).
</p>
<p><b>Your experience in the following areas will drive your success in this role.
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Your experience with VDC modeling for specific design discipline as well as maintaining models for project-specific requirements</li>
<li>Your ability to work as part of the stakeholder team, including pre-construction, operations, client representatives and VDC lead</li>
<li>Your team approach to participating in collaborative model coordination meetings</li>
<li>Your ability to conduct field inspections to ensure installation is accurate to VDC models and determine areas needing adjustment</li>
<li>Your willingness to assist with business development and participate in proposals and interviews, when necessary</li>
</ul>
<p><b>We have outlined the skill-set necessary for a candidate's success in this role below.
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Must have experience in 3D AutoCAD and Navis Works.</li>
<li>Fluency of most current modeling application software for VDC process, including but not limited to AutoCAD, Navisworks, and Revit</li>
<li>Understanding of construction design and document coordination, including construction nomenclature and techniques (means and methods)</li>
<li>Understanding of 3D coordination with other trades (i.e., Mechanical and Plumbing)</li>
<li>Ability to propose for 3D model incorporation, alternate methods of installation (collision avoidance)</li>
<li>Working knowledge of basic Electrical codes and constructability of a project</li>
</ul>
