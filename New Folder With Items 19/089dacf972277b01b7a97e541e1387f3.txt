About the Planning and Public Works Department: </b>Planning and Public Works is a collaborative, innovative, and mission-driven department providing vital infrastructure services to the residents of Pierce County. We are a department that develops, trains, and promotes our employees. Join our team and help us plan, build, and maintain a more livable Pierce County.<br>

<br>
Learn more about our dynamic department at www.piercecountywa.org/ppw.<br>
<br>
<b>Why It's a Great Opportunity:</b><br>
<br>
This is an opportunity to help build and become and integral part of the new Lands Asset Management team that performs duties related to managing lands in the custody of Planning and Public Works. This position will help develop the Lands Asset Management program and step into a role that is essential to carrying out the sections functions and tasks. The tasks will vary in nature and will be unique with each project or assignment. The position will include a variety of office and field work including negotiations for real property and property rights, lease development and negotiation, coordination with other divisions, field inspections, and various property management tasks. It is a senior role that will have an amount of autonomy and will rely on solid judgment along with leadership and problem solving skills.<br>

<div><div><b>POSITION SUMMARY:
</b></div></div>
<br>
<div><div>
<b>Our next successful team member can look forward to...</b><ul>
<li>Negotiate for purchase or lease of land parcels.
</li>
<li>Effect claim settlements with property owners for the acquisition of land and improvements; inform owners of their rights, applicable laws, and regulations.
</li>
<li>Review right of way strip maps, commissioners' records, plat books, engineers' reports and other records or materials. Make, or acquire, title searches. Inspect property, contact property owners and obtain permission for a variety of County activities, such as right of way staking or temporary construction permits.
</li>
<li>Understand and write legal property descriptions.
</li>
<li>Prepare and review exhibits and documents for presentation to elected officials and the general public; represent department's position at council and public meetings.
</li>
<li>Arrange for the maintenance, protection, sale and removal of buildings.
</li>
<li>Conduct extensive research efforts, using all government and private resources available.
</li>
<li>Prepare cost estimates.
</li>
<li>Participate in the selection, coordination, and monitoring of consultants providing title review, real property appraisal, and other related services.
</li>
<li>Prepare resolutions and ordinances related to the sale of real property.
</li>
<li>Conduct activities associated with managing real property.</li>
</ul>
<div>
<br>
<b>
Our next successful team member should possess the following...</b><br>
<br>
<b>Knowledge:</b><ul>
<li>Real estate title and appraisal work and practices used in real property transactions.
</li>
<li>Laws and procedures involved in the acquisition and leasing of land.
</li>
<li>The methods and techniques used in negotiating and appraising, acquiring and managing property and property damage claims.
</li>
<li>Legal instruments affecting the transfer, sale and lease of real property.
</li>
<li>Federal and state laws and regulations pertaining to real estate acquisition and appraisal.</li>
</ul>
<br>
<b>Skills In:</b><ul>
<li>Effective communication both verbally and in writing
</li>
<li>Using modern computer software and programs
</li>
<li>Negotiations
</li>
<li>Research
</li>
<li>Critical Thinking
</li>
<li>Problem Solving</li>
</ul>
<br>
<b>Ability to:</b><ul>
<li>Work independently and exercise sound judgment.
</li>
<li>Efficiently negotiate with property owners and to overcome resistance from others in accomplishing county goals.
</li>
<li>Read and interpret routine maps and property descriptions.
</li>
<li>Establish and maintain effective relationships with co-workers, property owners and the public.
</li>
<li>Keep records and prepare reports on activities as required.
</li>
<li>Read and understand construction plans as they relate to acquisition of property and property damage.
</li>
<li>Understand and follow written and verbal instructions.
