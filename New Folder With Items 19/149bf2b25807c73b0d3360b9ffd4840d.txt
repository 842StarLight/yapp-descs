<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>What You'll Do Here:
</b></h2>
<div><div><div>
<div>We have an immediate need for a Technical Writer at our Boston (Seaport) location! We would also consider candidates in Raleigh, NC.</div>
<div></div>
<br>
<div>
You will work with partners, development teams, and SMEs across the organization to ensure the success of our instructors, students, internal partners, and others by developing outstanding help, support, and training content that is current, accurate, clear, usable, useful, and easy to find.</div>

<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>
Responsibilities</b></h2>
<ul>
<li>
Research, write, and maintain user-focused topic-based help, release notes, and other training and support content for multiple online educational platforms and products for higher education, K-12, and technical schools. Audiences include instructors, students, and technical implementers.</li>
<li>
Ask questions, engage with partners and SMEs across the organization, and use the tools and platforms you are detailing to understand users' real-world needs and problems. Apply that knowledge to develop content that ensures success.</li>
<li>
Demonstrate user focus, attention to detail, and adherence to DITA and style standard methodologies.</li>
<li>
Apply "docs as code" practices to support quality and maintainability.</li>
<li>
Implement content reuse effectively.</li>
<li>
Contribute to information architecture development.</li>
<li>
With UX and development teams, provide UI microcopy for new and changing application features.</li>
<li>
Following Agile principles, deliver frequent iterative improvements to the documentation. Build agile stories, tasks, bugs, and epics with emphasis on user stories and clear acceptance criteria.</li>
<li>
Take ownership of and complete tasks and projects with minimal guidance.</li>
<li>
Work effectively as part of an agile technical communications team.</li>
<li>
Peer review other writers' work.</li>
<li>
Follow and contribute to style guidelines.</li>
<li>
Identify and help implement process improvements for the team.</li>
<li>
Infrequent (less than 10%) travel.</li>
</ul>
</div></div></div>
