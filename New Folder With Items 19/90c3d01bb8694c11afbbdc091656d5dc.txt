<h2>Account Executive III, Sales</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>It's fun to work in a company where people truly believe in what they are doing!
</p>
<p>The Sales Account Executive generates advertising revenue by calling on established agencies, cold-calling new prospects and convincing potential clients of the merits of television advertising.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>Essential Duties and Responsibilities:
</p>
<ul>
<li>Implements strategies to consistently grow revenue and exceed revenue goals.
</li>
<li>Establishes credible relationships with local business community.
</li>
<li>Makes sales calls on existing and prospective clients.
</li>
<li>Maintains assigned accounts and develops new accounts.
</li>
<li>Prepares and delivers sales presentations to clients.
</li>
<li>Explains to customers how specific types of advertising will help promote their products or services in the most effective way possible.
</li>
<li>Provides clients with information regarding rates for advertising placement in all media.
</li>
<li>Develops advertising schedules with clients and station personnel responsible for placing advertising into station media.
</li>
<li>Works with clients and station personnel to develop advertisements.
</li>
<li>Performs other duties as assigned.
</li>
</ul>
<p>Requirements &amp; Skills :
</p>
<p></p>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor’s degree in Marketing, Advertising or Mass Communications, or a related field, or an equivalent combination of education and work-related experience.
</li>
<li>Minimum one year’s experience in sales, preferably in the media field.
</li>
<li>Valid driver’s license with an acceptable driving record.
</li>
<li>Experience achieving long-range objectives and implementing the strategies and actions to achieve them.
</li>
<li>Proficiency with computers, telephones, copiers, scanners, fax machines and other office equipment.
</li>
</ul>
<p>If you like wild growth and working with happy, enthusiastic over-achievers, you'll enjoy your career with us!</p>
</div></div>
