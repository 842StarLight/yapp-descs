<h2>Behavioral Specialist</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<p></p>
<div><div>
<div><div>Under general supervision, is responsible for working with the program's Social and Emotional Learning (SEL) team to implement a program wide model for social and emotional learning to effect positive behavioral change in students; providing supportive interventions to families, staff and/or students; and complying with Federal, State, county and District policies, regulations and/or procedures. Exercises no supervision.<br>
<br>
<b>Additional information about this position:</b><ul>
<li>City positions are paid on a bi-weekly basis equating to 26 pay periods a year. The annual salary for this position will be paid in equal portions over the 26 pay periods.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>This position is anticipated to work 220 days a year, subject to change based on Pre-K 4 SA operational needs.</li>
</ul>
This position is funded by sales tax that has been approved by the citizens of San Antonio for a limited time period. This position, therefore, is subject to at-will employment status, and is unclassified and has no disciplinary appeal rights to the Municipal Civil Service Commission.
</div></div>
<br>
<br>
<div></div>
<div><div>
<p><b>ESSENTIAL JOB FUNCTIONS:</b></p>
</div></div>
<br>
<div><div>
<ul><li>Work collaboratively with SEL (Social and Emotional Learning) team members, teachers, and educational center Directors to engage in program-wide implementation of high-quality SEL instructional model.</li></ul>
<ul>
<li>Provides daily direct, in-classroom support to children with social and behavioral needs.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Provides ongoing professional development that supports the school plan for high quality SEL instructional practices and continuous improvement.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Acts as liaison with the public and community for the purpose of supporting individual student development goals and connecting families to resources within the community.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Administers programs and services within the SEL school model for the purpose of improved functioning and ensuring program eligibility and compliance with established guidelines.
</li>
<li>Develops interventions and target goals as part of Response to Intervention (RTI) and for the purpose of providing a safe and effective educational environment for students with challenging behaviors.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Assists in developing procedures and training materials for teaching staff for the purpose of enhancing the SEL program model for students and ensuring that program operations are in compliance with established guidelines.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Conducts workshops, training, in-service presentations, etc. in classroom management techniques and other topics for the purpose of developing skills and establishing effective and nurturing relationships with students and families.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Coordinates handling of child abuse and neglect cases through contact with school staff and outside agency personnel for the purpose of complying with mandated requirements.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Assist with the planning and delivery of parent workshops and informational sessions.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Conducts or participates in workshops, committees, and conferences designed to promote the intellectual, social, and physical welfare of students.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Participates in parent conferences, when appropriate, and provides instructional suggestions to parents, as needed.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Participates in a variety of professional development trainings to further develop professional skills and to stay current with research and practice.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Maintains accurate records and documentation.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Assist teachers in completing paperwork for students receiving special education services and attends Admissions, Review and Dismissal meeting.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Perform related duties and fulfills responsibilities as required.</li>
</ul>
</div></div>
<br>
<br>
<div></div>
<div><div><p><b>JOB REQUIREMENTS:</b>
</p></div></div>
<br>
<div><div>
<ul><li>Bachelor's Degree in Early Childhood Education, Special Education, and/or related field from an accredited college or university.</li></ul>
<ul>
<li>Three (3) years teaching experience in a special education and/or early childhood classroom in an accredited program.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>This position requires a valid Class "C" Texas Driver's License OR valid driver's license from another state with the ability to obtain a valid Class "C" Texas Driver's License within 30 days of becoming a Texas resident. Use of your own personal vehicle may at time be required and proof of Liability Insurance will be required.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Valid teaching certificate with required endorsements to teach early childhood education from any State's Educator Certification Department and the ability to obtain certification in Texas.</li>
</ul>
<b>
PREFERRED QUALIFICATIONS:</b><ul>
<li>Master's Degree, concentration in Special Education and/or Early Childhood Education from an accredited college or university.</li>
</ul>
<b>
APPLICANT INFORMATION:</b><ul>
<li>If selected for this position, official transcripts, diplomas, certifications and licenses must be submitted at the time of processing. Unofficial transcripts and copies of other relevant documents may be attached to the application for consideration in advance.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Please be advised that if selected for this position, information regarding employment history as it relates to the qualifications of the position will be needed for employment verification. Applicants claiming military service to meet the experience requirement for this position may attach a DD214 to the application.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Unless otherwise stated, applicants are permitted to substitute two years of related full-time experience for one year of higher education or one year of related higher education for two years of experience in order to meet the minimum requirements of the job. One year of full-time experience is defined as 30 or more hours worked per week for 12 months. One year of higher education is defined as 30 credit hours completed at an accredited college or university.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Applicants selected for employment with the City of San Antonio in this position must receive satisfactory results from pre-employment drug testing and background checks. If required for the position, additional background checks may be conducted.</li>
</ul>
<b>
PHYSICAL REQUIREMENTS:</b><ul>
<li>Physical requirements include occasional lifting/carrying of 35 pounds, visual acuity, speech and hearing; hand and eye coordination and manual dexterity necessary to operate computer keyboard and basic office equipment. Subject to sitting, standing, bending, reaching and stooping to perform the essential functions. Working conditions are in a classroom environment. Special education teachers may be required to lift and position students with physical disabilities; control behavior through physical restraint; and assist non-ambulatory students.</li>
</ul>
