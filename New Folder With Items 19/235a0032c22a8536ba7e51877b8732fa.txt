<h2>Project Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">Through the responsible development of innovative and sustainable building products, CertainTeed, headquartered in Malvern, Pennsylvania, has helped shape the building products industry for more than 110 years. Founded in 1904 as General Roofing Manufacturing Company, the firm's slogan "Quality Made Certain, Satisfaction Guaranteed," inspired the name CertainTeed. Today, CertainTeed is a leading North American brand of exterior and interior building products, including roofing, siding, fence, decking, railing, trim, insulation, drywall and ceilings.
<br><br>
A subsidiary of Saint-Gobain, one of the world’s largest and oldest building products companies, CertainTeed has more than 6,300 employees and more than 60 manufacturing facilities throughout the United States and Canada. www.certainteed.com
<br><br>
The Project Manager position is responsible for planning, budgeting, overseeing and document all aspects of projects assigned. The Project Manager will work closely with upper management to make sure that the scope and direction of each project is on schedule, as well as other departments for support.
<br><br>
<b>ESSENTIAL DUTIES:</b><br>
Effectively manage project scope from sales to closeout
<br><br>
Communicate and follow up with clients about specific projects and their goals
<br>Maintain customer expectations; Nurture client relationships
<br>Ensure all project documents are submitted and approved
<br>Ensure that the purchase order received is compatible with the terms of the project proposals
<br>Develop accurate and detailed project plans for a variety of projects
<br>Ensure all aspects of projects are properly and thoroughly documented
<br>Create and manage project schedules and budget; track and report project milestones
<br>Communicate project requirements and status to cross functional teams
<br>Diagnose and resolve project issues
<br>Provide project status reviews on a regular basis to Sr. Management
<br>Relate suggestions for continuous improvement
<br>Conduct project closeout meetings designed to capture the lessons learned of the project
<br><br>

75% of the time

<br>10% of the time
<br><br>
Review and understand competition
<br>5% of the time
<br><br>
Payment of commissions to sales
<br>10% of the time
<br><br>
Travel
