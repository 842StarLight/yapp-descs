<h2>Project Manager, NPI</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div><div>
<div><b>:</b></div>
<div></div>
<div>
<br>
The New Product Introduction PROJECT MANAGER is responsible for managing component level changes into the production lines and supporting the production launch of new products for specific engineering systems, i.e: Upper Body, Chassis, Charger, Battery, Interior, etc.</div>
<div></div>
<div>
<br>
This requires the project manager to lead a cross-functional team to ensure engineering, product, and manufacturing validation targets have been met for each change. As well, the project manager is responsible for the tactical implementation for the change and ensuring factory readiness for change execution; they are seen as the subject matter expert for their part subsystem within the production organization.</div>
<div><b>
Responsibilities:</b></div>
<ul><li>
Drive all product change activities for new or revised products into the factory/factories. Clear target is to meet or exceed project/program milestones…..”non-event launch” mentality with each</li></ul>
<ul>
<li>
Participate in, and lead “cross functional” change control forums. Provide clear updates on status and recovery plans as necessary to bring projects back to target</li>
<li>
Work with design engineering to create, validate, and maintain the MBOM/product deviations for responsible group of parts</li>
<li>
Ensure all factory systems (MRP, ERP, MES, etc) are validated and prepared for each transition</li>
<li>
Oversee the complete manufacturing validation for responsible group of parts….drive supply chain readiness and handover from launch planning to production planning; oversee factory transition; and follow-up on excess/obsolescence and post change activities.</li>
<li>
Steer overall validation plan creation (Success criteria/KPIs) for the product change or coordinated group of products to change</li>
<li>
Environment, health, and safety taken into account in all activities and manufacturing validations</li>
<li>
Ensure that NPI processes are followed in all doings (PCA, MRT, etc)</li>
<li>
Organization and follow-up of open points/actions that are required to secure a non-event introduction of the change; ensure conveyed and well communicated as actionable items.</li>
<li>
Interact, integrate, and drive results across cross-functional teams in engineering, quality, production, production control, sales, service, etc.</li>
<li>
Author timely project status, summary, and issue resolution tracking reports.</li>
<li>
Support the Program Manager in driving Production Launch activities for major vehicle updates as it relates to responsible group of parts.</li>
<li>
Various other activities as assigned to support NPI and/or other production departments</li>
</ul>
<div><b>
Requirements:</b></div>
<ul>
<li>
Production Operations Knowledge - Has a firm understanding of how complex production lines operate including key items such as industrial workings between facilities/departments, line balance, OEE, error proofing, etc.</li>
<li>
Background in manufacturing/industrial engineering and supply chain highly desired</li>
<li>
Understanding of how the manufacturing validation process runs in relation to new products (methodology, validation plans, associated KPIs)</li>
<li>
Good understanding of design for manufacture, process design, process validation, and assembly methods.</li>
<li>
Interpersonal skills dealing with all levels of an organization.</li>
<li>
Strong written and verbal communication skills. Well versed with common workplace software (word processor, spreadsheet, database, etc). Excellent organizational skills</li>
<li>
Able to be a creative thinker and devise creative solutions to complicated problems</li>
<li>
Excellent problem solving skills; adverse in various problem solving methodologies</li>
<li>
Team player; ability to work in a fast-paced, multi-cultural environment with cross-functional teams.</li>
<li>
Previous experience with Manufacturing Execution Systems (MES) desired</li>
<li>
Previous experience with ERP systems (SAP, Oracle, etc) is beneficial</li>
<li>
Ability to read and interpret basic mechanical drawings or CAD models</li>
<li>
Has a good understanding of production line layout, flow and modeling. Should understand the following:</li>
<ul>
<li>
job shops, batch and flow production</li>
<li>
takt, touch and cycle time</li>
<li>
bottle neck identification and methods for resolving</li>
<li>
work station organization and setup / lean manufacturing principles</li>
<li>
statistical process control</li>
<li>
designing single flow lines to build multiple products</li>
</ul>
<li>
Minimum of two (2) years’ experience (PM) / five (5) years’ experience (SR.) / ten (10) years’ experience (Staff) in automotive, high tech, or other high volume/high complexity manufacturing in a similar capacity coupled with demonstration of exceptionable ability.</li>
<li>
Senior and Staff positions are expected to be able to provide direction and mentor others with in the team; must exhibit this ability</li>
<li>
Bachelor’s Degree in engineering or related technical field required (Master’s Degree in supporting field <b>highly</b>preferred)</li>
<li>
Strong motivation to support the company mission is necessary to succeed</li>
</ul>
