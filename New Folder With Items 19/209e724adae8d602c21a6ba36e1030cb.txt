<h2>Membership & Annual Giving Associate</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div><div>
<div><div><div>
<div>
<p>The Membership &amp; Annual Giving Associate develops, manages, and organizes strategies and tasks relating to the museum’s renewable funding programs. With independent judgement and a strong sense of initiative, the Membership &amp; Annual Giving Associate systematizes and innovates ways to meet unrestricted, operating revenue development goals. The position is responsible for planning and executing appeals, visitor engagement opportunities, and member events in an effort to reach new audiences and engage existing annual supporters throughout the year. Focuses on turning both individual and organizational prospects and visitors into members, and members into donors through customer relationship management and outreach strategies.</p>
<p><b>
Responsibilities:</b></p>
<p>
Strategize and manage membership renewals, upgrade, and acquisition campaigns</p>
<p>
Determine objectives and strategies, then execute, track, and analyze plans to drive membership revenue. Develop mailings, events, and promotions to gain new, renewed, and upgraded individual (General and Director’s Circle) and corporate memberships.</p>
<ul>
<li>
<i>Solicitation and renewal mailings:</i> Systematize and streamline timely electronic and direct-mail individual and corporate membership solicitations.</li>
<li>
<i>Membership processing:</i> Serve as a personal point of contact for member relations. Process memberships and acknowledge accordingly. Oversee and execute individual general and corporate member stewardship mailings, benefits distribution, etc.</li>
<li>
<i>Lead sales team: </i>Provide museum colleagues at the Admission Desk, the Store, and the Creative Workshop with the materials and talking points they need to successfully sell memberships, and help them maintain energy and enthusiasm.</li>
<li>
<i>Generate leads: </i>Collaborate with museum colleagues to leverage audience engagement to build the membership pipeline.</li>
<li>
<i>Marketing materials &amp; collateral:</i> Work with Marketing &amp; Engagement, Advancement Communications, and special vendors, to request and coordinate collateral needs, including stationary, membership brochures, etc.</li>
</ul>
<p>
Budgeting</p>
<p>
Project and track membership revenue and expense budgets, including costs for membership events.</p>
<p>
Reporting, data analysis, and data and list management</p>
<p>
Process and manage memberships in Altru, with a keen attention to detail.</p>
<ul>
<li>Develop reports and tools to track and evaluate progress toward individual and corporate membership count and revenue goals.</li>
<li>Analyze trends and compare to internal, regional, and national benchmarks in order to inform membership appeal and upgrade strategies.</li>
</ul>
<p>
Member events
</p>
<p>In close collaboration with the Manager of Advancement Events, plan lively and engaging member activities (such as previews or behind-the-scenes access) designed to encourage growth in membership. These events include (but are not limited to):</p>
<ul>
<li>Preview Parties for 3-4 Docent Gallery exhibitions each year</li>
<li>New member events
<ul>
<li>Work with MAG Marketing &amp; Engagement (or outside vendors and community partners, when appropriate) to design invitations, emails tailored to members, as well as general membership promotion around MAG, in the community, and on social media</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Annual Fund</p>
<p>
Serve as MAG’s primary liaison to the department of Annual Giving (AG) Programs within UR Central Advancement in order to coordinate and execute [minimally] annual electronic and direct mail appeals to a broad member and donor base.</p>
<ul>
<li>Work with UR’s Central Advancement AG contact to identify target audiences, conceptualize appeal strategies, then develop an annual calendar of mailing waves. Coordinate internal MAG approval of annual appeal calendar, and of the content of each appeal.</li>
<li>Compile content (prompts or full composition, as well as images) for/from museum Director, the Director of Advancement, and/or Marketing &amp; Engagement.</li>
</ul>
<p>
Coordinate annual renewable (non-grant) corporate support</p>
<p>
Identify, cultivate, and solicit annual gifts from businesses and corporations for unrestricted operating support through corporate memberships (“pARTnerships”) and sponsorships of key museum initiatives that require renewable funding, such as annual events/programming, galas, exhibitions, and other special projects.</p>
<ul>
<li>Working closely with the Assistant Director of Leadership Annual Giving, review current corporate members to determine likely candidates for increased gifts. Identify current corporate members and donors for more personal cultivation and solicitation.</li>
<li>Work with UR central advancement Prospect Development office, as well as external resources, to source corporate prospect leads. Develop strategies and appeals to engage new business targets.</li>
</ul>
<p>
Other duties as assigned</p>
<p><b>
Qualifications:</b></p>
<p>
Bachelor's degree with 2-3 years experience including some writing experience; or an equivalent combination of education and experience.</p>
<p>
Preferred Qualifications</p>
<p>
A minimum of two years’ experience in Advancement/Fundraising preferred, or a combination of relevant experience and education. Experience in project management a plus. Must have a proven track-record in developing and executing [marketing] strategies to reach and engage target audiences. Must be able to work successfully with diverse groups, including museum staff, outside agencies, corporate sponsors, and tourism/hospitality representatives. Necessary skills sets include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Effective written, verbal, and interpersonal communication skills</li>
<li>A strong sense of organization, attention to detail, planning, and time management.</li>
<li>High proficiency in Microsoft Office suite of software (especially Excel) and/or database/reporting software(s), as well as use of various social media outlets.</li>
<li>Initiative to tactically plan then implement execution of Director’s strategic vision and programmatic oversight.</li>
<li>Budgeting skills.</li>
<li>Must be able to work evenings and weekends as needed.</li>
<li>Self-starter and multi-tasker.</li>
<li>Demonstrated success in working both independently and as a team member to create and implement fundraising strategies.</li>
</ul>
