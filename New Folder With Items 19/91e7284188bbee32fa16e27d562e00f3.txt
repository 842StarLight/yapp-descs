<h2>Technology Underwriting Trainee</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">Our Technology department is seeking a Technology Underwriting Trainee to join our growing team in our Salem, VA or Worcester, MA office.
<br><br>
<b>Position summary:</b><br>
Trainee level and entry position for the Technology Underwriting career path. Through a structured onboarding and training program, develop a basic understanding of business and underwriting foundations and core Hanover guidelines and practices. Operates with a high degree of coaching and oversight while actively engaging in both structured and on-the-job learning experiences designed to accelerate the advancement of core learning objectives. Through action based learning and in-flight experiences will demonstrate achievement of learning objectives and advancement in both understanding and applying UW fundamentals which should result in obtaining Underwriting Level of Authority.
<br><br>
<b>Anticipated start date:</b> June 1st, 2020. This is a Full-time, Exempt role.
<br><br>
<b>Responsibilities/Essential Functions:</b><br>
<ul>
<li>Successful achievement of learning and development milestones</li>
<li>Build understanding of the Hanover structure, and enterprise and Technology strategies</li>
<li>Develops understanding of how own role fits into the overall business operations and how it interacts with other areas of the business</li>
<li>Develop understanding of important internal relationships needed to successfully perform the UW role</li>
<li>Begins to build knowledge of the Technology Renewal and New Business (NB) workflow including systems, processes, tools and guidelines</li>
<li>Develop basic understanding and the purpose of, coverages provided by Hanover and features of Property, General Liability, WC, Auto and Umbrella insurance and can interpret policy provisions</li>
<li>Gain exposure to and basic utilization of underwriting systems</li>
<li>Understands and begins to apply the Hanover approach to underwriting assessment</li>
<li>Independently handles less complex new and renewal accounts within defined authority levels</li>
<li>Makes recommendations on risk selection, coverage, pricing and managing of sales proposals with oversight</li>
<li>Demonstrates an understanding of the significance of the claims loss ratio for the pricing of a risk</li>
<li>Starts to develop an understanding of why different rates are applied to different types of risks and the factors that can affect the premium rate for an individual risk</li>
<li>Analyzes financial information to determine performance, identify business trends and recommend process improvements</li>
<li>Demonstrates an awareness of the data that indicates how an underwriting portfolio is performing</li>
<li>Understands the partnership strategy and sales process</li>
<li>Understands pipelining concepts and Hanover practices and guidelines</li>
<li>Understands the Hanover Value Proposition and is able to articulate it to others (internally or externally)</li>
<li>Knows the importance of positive customer experiences in developing good relationships.</li>
</ul>
<b>Position Requirements:</b><br>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor’s Degree with a GPA of 3.0 or higher preferred</li>
<li>Robust internship and leadership experience</li>
<li>Legal right to work in the United States without requiring sponsorship</li>
<li>Ability to relocate at the conclusion of the training period within the region</li>
<li>Generally less than 1 year underwriting experience</li>
<li>Developing understanding of UW fundamentals and concepts</li>
<li>Marketing and sales orientation</li>
<li>Entrepreneurial minded and innovative</li>
<li>Communication (written and verbal) - strong communication skills written and verbal. Effectively communicates ideas, questions, or concerns in one-on-one situations. Understands and articulately answers questions when asked. Practices active listening</li>
<li>Judgment and Decision Making – beginning to apply technical knowledge to inform decision making. Understand decision making processes and procedures and the role they play within them. Applies critical thinking to decision making. Demonstrates ability to apply judgment within their daily responsibilities (e.g. evaluating risks, need for added input or support, general decision making)</li>
<li>Agile and Adaptive: Ability to adapt to new situations quickly and prioritize tasks and responsibilities</li>
<li>Team Work and Collaboration: Demonstrated experience working within and across teams. Understands and appreciates the power of working together to achieve targeted outcomes</li>
<li>Prioritization and Desk Management: Understands work flows and processes and applies to work prioritization. Clarity around deadlines and work priorities and seeks support if they are at risk of being compromised</li>
<li>Ability to use a personal computer and other standard office equipment</li>
<li>Ability to work in a fast paced or stressful environment</li>
<li>Ability to sit and/or stand for extended periods</li>
<li>Ability to travel (by car or plane) est. 10-20%</li>
<li>Ability to operate a motor vehicle.</li>
</ul>
