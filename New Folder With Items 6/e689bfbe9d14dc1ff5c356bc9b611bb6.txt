<p>We are seeking a <b>Veterinary Pathologist II </b>for our Safety Assessment site located in Reno, NV.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>The following are responsibilities related to the <b>Veterinary Pathologist II</b>:</p>
<ul>
<li>Responsible for independently performing gross and microscopic evaluation of tissues, recording results, preparing reports delineating pathological findings, and assisting with necropsy supervision.</li>
<li>With minimal supervision, work with customers as needed to market company services and to assure customer satisfaction.</li>
<li>Write comprehensive report narratives detailing all test article effects. As needed, work with study directors to ensure data is accurately integrated into study reports.</li>
<li>May serve as a study director or principal investigator.</li>
<li>May function as a peer review pathologist or may participate in a pathology working group (PWG).</li>
<li>Provide training, technical guidance and leadership to laboratory staff and may provide training, guidance, and leadership to less experienced pathologists.</li>
<li>May serve as a consultant in pathology-related issues.</li>
<li>May provide scientific expertise for experimental design and other aspects of research studies as needed.</li>
<li>Perform all other related duties as assigned.</li>
<br>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p>The following are minimum qualifications related to the <b>Veterinary Pathologist II</b> position:</p>
<ul>
<li>Education: Doctoral degree (D.V.M./V.M.D.) from an accredited veterinary school, or acceptable international equivalent in veterinary medicine.</li>
<li>Experience: 3 or more years of pathology experience post-D.V.M.; working knowledge of clinical pathology; specialized training and/or experience in toxicologic pathology preferred.</li>
<li>An equivalent combination of education and experience may be accepted as a satisfactory substitute for the specific education and experience listed above.</li>
<li>Certification/Licensure: American College of Veterinary Pathologists (A.C.V.P.) board-certification required to enter the grade at the minimum experience level. Additional years of directly related experience may substitute for the board-certification.</li>
<li>Other: Demonstrated success in technical proficiency, scientific creativity, collaboration with others and independent thought. Works on problems of diverse scope in which analysis of data requires evaluation of identifiable factors. Exercises judgment within generally defined practices and policies in selecting methods and techniques for obtaining solutions. Computer literacy in word processing, spreadsheet and database software.<br>
</li>
</ul>
