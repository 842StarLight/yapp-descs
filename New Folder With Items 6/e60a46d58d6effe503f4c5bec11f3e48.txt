<h2>Medical Education Analyst</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<h1 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Job Description
</b></h1>
<p></p>
<p><b><i>Job Summary </i></b>The Medical Education Analyst (MEA) is primarily responsible for event analytics reporting to help ensure accurate information is communicated to internal partners, provide structure and consistency in information shared and develop and advance meaningful measures of success. In addition, this position will assist with support for Medical Education programs, projects and process improvements.
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b><i>Principle Responsibilities: (essential job duties and responsibilities)
</i></b></p>
<ul>
<li>Ownership of event analytics and reporting for the team on a monthly, quarterly, annual and ad hoc basis
</li>
<li>Develop and advance meaningful measures of success
</li>
<li>Ensure compliance with all company policies and procedures
</li>
<li>Communications lead for the medical education team to include weekly updates, RVP weekly reports, social media strategy and any additional communication necessary
</li>
<li>Ownership of medical education website including content updates and liaising with IT department to provide content and manage internet/e-mail queries.
</li>
<li>Ownership of Smartsheets- to include training, quality control and sheet or form creation
</li>
<li>Maintenance and updates of National Bioskills lab database
</li>
<li>Other projects and duties as deemed necessary for team support
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b><i>Experiences:
</i></b></p>
<p>Excellent communication and interpersonal skills. Experience working in a matrixed organization a plus. Ability to build long-term relationships with faculty that can be leveraged to achieve desired outcomes. Exceptional project management skills, with the ability to manage multiple projects at one time, effectively prioritize and remain detail oriented. Must work well in a fast-paced environment, be able to multi-task, and remain extremely detail-oriented. Intermediate level computer skills. Ability to maintain complete confidentiality and discretion in business relationships and exercise sound business judgment.
</p>
