<h2>Buyer - Energy Acquisition</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Job Description
</b></h2>
<p>Position Summary
<br>
Purchases and sells natural gas to meet customer needs. Optimizes the use of portfolio assets.
</p>
<div>***WGL is also open to hiring at the Sr. Buyer Level depending on experience
<br>
<br>
Tasks and Responsibilities
<br>
<ul>
<li>Acts upon opportunities to enhance portfolio value through short-term trading
</li>
<li>Participates in implementation of portfolio optimization strategies
</li>
<li>Analyzes wellhead, city-gate, storage supplies, and identifies potential problems
</li>
<li>Assists in the administration of contracts for gas supply services; develops and interprets contract language; evaluates supplier performance and works with supplier to ensure contract compliance; Negotiates in both buying and selling supply services
</li>
<li>Communicates with suppliers and pipeline representatives on commodity purchasing volumes and deliveries
</li>
<li>Builds and maintains relationships with potential gas supply and gas transportation counterparties
</li>
<li>Purchases commodity via the Intercontinental Exchange Platform or Instant Messenger
</li>
<li>Serves as liaison for supply problem resolution between Washington Gas and suppliers on any of the 4 interstate pipelines that serves WGL’s territory
</li>
<li>Analyzes delivery reports to ensure appropriate supply to city-gate
</li>
<li>Monitors storage service activities by activities reviewing the pipeline’s reports to match the reports Energy Acquisition maintains
</li>
<li>Nominates and schedules commodity purchases via electronic bulletin boards and Gas Pro system
</li>
<li>Coordinates with other Specialists in Capacity Administration and/or Back Office Administration to ensure that service requirements and pricing structures are confirmed and validated
</li>
<li>Identifies and reports on opportunities to enhance the portfolio value through short-term trading
</li>
<li>Creates standardized and custom reports in support of the business or support unit
</li>
<li>Works collaboratively with Risk and Credit staff to review the NAESB contracts for approval by Washington Gas to conduct business with potential suppliers for commodity transactions
</li>
<li>Serves as secondary operational contact with the company’s gas dispatch function and external optimization partners
</li>
<li>Prepares reports, participates in meetings and attends proceedings in support of the business unit’s planning and regulatory issues
</li>
</ul>
<br>
Qualifications, Skills and Abilities
<br>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor's Degree preferably in Business, Economics, Finance, Accounting, or Technology required
</li>
<li>3+ Years of experience in the natural gas industry or equivalent relevant experience, preferably in gas trading or scheduling required
</li>
</ul>
<br>
<ul>
<li>Demonstrated working knowledge of gas trading process and/or interstate gas pipeline systems preferred
</li>
<li>Proven ability to use computer software and applications, including Microsoft Office, for data management, analysis and reporting purposes
</li>
<li>Demonstrated ability to successfully influence/persuade to achieve desired business outcomes
</li>
<li>Demonstrated ability to write and present diverse ideas logically and concisely
</li>
<li>Demonstrated ability to prioritize workloads and to work independently to achieve business goals
</li>
<li>Proven ability to work at a fast pace to perform a wide variety of functions
</li>
</ul>
