<h2>Sales Operations Specialist</h2>

<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Job Overview:
</b></h2>
<div><div><div>
<div>Do you have a knack for facilitating processes? Would you relish a job that requires you to shepherd sales from start to finish? If so, then you may be the person who we need to fill our opening for a Sales Operations Specialist, a newly created position that will guide our sales department through customer and prospect sales. You will work with our legal and sales teams to provide quotes and contracts, and reduce the amount of time that deals spend in contractual stages by quickly securing approvals. If you’re extremely detail-oriented with experience building relationships with various stakeholders and using Salesforce, then apply now so that we can talk!</div>

<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Responsibilities:
</b></h2>
<div><div><div>
<ul>
<li>Day-to-Day work with the Sales teams to build, refine, and deliver sales quotes.</li>
<li>
Partner with Legal to work through quotes as they go through the Negotiating Contract phase</li>
<li>
Partner with the Cloud Services organization and Data Stewards to identify the key bottlenecks in the Quoting, Business Term Review, and quote approval processes</li>
<li>
Be a “Consulted” party in the projects to streamline and make more efficient the Quoting, Business Term Review, and quote approval processes</li>
<li>
Collaborate with the Legal team on the redlining process, to reduce the overall timeline of getting deals from Negotiating Contract to Close</li>
<li>
Simplify complex issues and effectively communicate in depth analysis to our customers.</li>
<li>
Gather and report insights through data and business analysis.</li>
<li>
Design best practices and build recommendations.</li>
<li>
Drive knowledge and expertise in our other quantitative tools, including Tableau and SFDC</li>
<li>
KPIs would include Conversion rate from Competing to Close, Negotiating Contract to Closed Won, and time from Negotiating Contract to Closed Won</li>
<li>
Manage ad-hoc requests concurrently.</li>
</ul>
</div></div></div>
