<b>Project Analyst</b><br>
<br>
The Project Analyst is responsible for assisting in the preparation and maintenance of financial analyses to support the development, asset management, and property management teams. This includes project due diligence, market research, budgeting, forecasting, financial modeling, and reporting. This position requires a keen awareness of the real estate market and thorough understanding of real estate fundamentals.<br>
<br>
Wangard Partners is an equal opportunity employer. All qualified applicants will receive consideration for employment without regard to race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, disability or veteran status.<br>
<br>
<b>Reports to: </b>Associate Development Manager<br>
<br>
<b>Essential Duties – Development</b>
<ul>
<li>Contribute to and maintain financial models / pro formas to support development project activities.</li>
<li>Coordinate with internal team members to compile development, construction, operating, and financing assumptions for the pro forma models.</li>
<li>Research and forecast project income and expenses, including municipal-specific utility rates, real estate taxes, and development fees.</li>
<li>Maintain accurate job cost reports for development projects by ensuring correct coding is applied to all invoices and check requests.</li>
<li>Conduct market research and track local trends for commercial and multifamily properties.</li>
<li>Run scenario analyses to aid in determination of optimum product, pricing, and financing for development sites.</li>
<li>Perform ad hoc research and analysis as requested.</li>
</ul>
<b>Essential Duties – Asset Management &amp; Acquisitions</b>
<ul>
<li>Assist in developing and maintaining multi-tenant Argus models for commercial properties within the portfolio &amp; to evaluate potential acquisitions.</li>
<li>Assist with performing periodic valuations of Wangard’s commercial properties to assist with sell/hold and refinancing decisions.</li>
<li>Analyze commercial lease proposals to ensure acceptable returns are achieved.</li>
<li>Assist with financial due diligence for acquisitions, to include review of leases, tenant sales reports, tenant ledgers, CAM reconciliations, property financial statements, capital expenditures, reciprocal easement agreements, service contracts, and utility accounts.</li>
</ul>
<br>
<b>Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities</b><br>
To perform this job successfully, an individual must be able to perform each essential duty well. The requirements listed below are representative of the knowledge, skills, and abilities required. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions of the job.
<ul>
<li>Knowledge of real estate fundamentals and financial terms, principles, modeling, and returns is preferred.</li>
<li>Working knowledge of Microsoft Office Suite programs, with advanced knowledge in Excel, is preferred. Experience with ARGUS Enterprise and CoStar preferred.</li>
<li>Ability to perform advanced math functions and financial analysis.</li>
<li>Excellent organizational practices and attention to detail.</li>
<li>Ability to take initiative and work well independently and with a team.</li>
<li>Strong time management skills. Ability to execute on assigned tasks in a timely manner.</li>
<li>Ability to maintain a professional demeanor and adapt to a changing, project-based workload.</li>
<li>Excellent written and verbal communication skills. Ability to communicate effectively in English.</li>
</ul>
<b>Professional Experience</b><br>
Two (2) years of relevant experience in commercial real estate development, investment analysis/underwriting, asset management, or valuation preferred.<br>
<b>Education</b><br>
Completion of a bachelor’s degree, with a major or emphasis in real estate, finance, or related field is preferred. Equivalent work experience will be considered.<br>
<b>Physical Demands</b><br>
Physical demands include the ability to lift up to 30 pounds. The employee will be regularly required to sit; stand; walk; reach using feet, legs, back, arms and hands; talk, and hear. The employee may occasionally be required to climb. The employee will be required to work at a personal computer and talk on the phone for extended periods of time. Must possess the ability to detect auditory and/or visual alarms and be available to work extended/flexible hours and weekends, as needed. Reasonable accommodations will be made to a successful candidate that cannot meet the physical demands.<br>
<b>Attendance/Travel</b><br>
This position is a full-time hourly position. This position requires the availability to work some nights, weekends, and emergency situations. This position also requires regular attendance and active participation/planning at all company functions and events.<br>
<b>Learning and Development</b><br>
Maintain a commitment to ongoing professional development and career growth.<br>
