<h2>Corporate Training & Organizational Development Manager</h2>

<b>COMPANY BACKGROUND:</b><br>
Morgan Corporation is the largest manufacturer of medium duty freight and refrigerated van and truck bodies in North America. Founded in 1952, as a small welding shop in Morgantown, Pennsylvania, on the site where Conestoga wagons were manufactured through the late 1800s, we have experienced tremendous growth and continue to look for additional expansion opportunities around the world. With a total of eleven manufacturing and five service facilities strategically located across the U.S. and Canada, Morgan is able to provide the quality, service, and prompt delivery our customers expect.
<br><br>
A subsidiary of J.B. Poindexter, a privately held diversified manufacturing company, Morgan Corporation offers a vast variety of career opportunities, internally as well as within the J.B. Poindexter family of companies. We offer competitive salaries and progressive benefits including health, dental, vision, life insurance, disability plans, 401K, paid time off, tuition reimbursement and more. We pride ourselves in team member development, performance, recognition, and a culture of diversity and respect. Whether you are taking the next step of your career or just beginning, joining Morgan’s winning team means being part of a great home away from home. You will be challenged. You will be proud.
<br><br>
<b>POSITION SUMMARY:</b><br>
The Corporate Training &amp; Organizational Development Manager will assume the lead role for the design and development of corporate training programs and work closely in collaboration with other managers at all levels of the organization, including senior leaders, and be the main point of contact for all training and development matters.
<br><br>
Success in the role requires the ability to assess talent and skill gaps within the organization, develop solutions to address those gaps through internal and external training, deliver effective coaching and training directly, act as a master facilitator for workshops and training sessions, leverage technology to support training and organizational development efforts, and manage vendors effectively to achieve the company’s training and development strategy.
<br><br>
<b>KEY DUTIES &amp; RESPONSIBILITIES:</b><br>
1. Training and Development Strategy
<br><ul>
<li>In conjunction with business leaders, develop and execute the company’s training and development strategy that achieves:</li>
<li>The rapid integration of unskilled, entry level labor into the company</li>
<li>The mitigation of key skill gaps (e.g. welding, painting, sheet metal fabrication, sales)</li>
<li>The development of leaders and leadership ability across all functions and all levels</li>
<li>The reduction of safety incidents and consistent safety performance</li>
<li>The reduction of quality issues</li>
<li>The decrease in production cycle time</li>
<li>The reduction of waste (e.g. scrap, inefficient processes, etc.)</li>
<li>A 90%+ promotion from within metric for Supervisors and above by 2019</li>
<li>At least one “ready now” successor for all mission critical positions by 2018</li>
</ul>
2. Training Management and Delivery
<br><ul>
<li>Establish and ensure execution of training programs that address key business needs</li>
<li>Welding</li>
<li>Painting</li>
<li>Safety</li>
<li>Sales</li>
<li>Production</li>
<li>Materials</li>
<li>Procurement</li>
<li>Quality</li>
<li>Finance</li>
<li>Implement, manage, and maximize adoption of Learning Management System (LMS)</li>
<li>Report on all training activities, cost of training, and return on investment</li>
<li>Deliver training directly as needed</li>
<li>Coordinate with trainers and departments to deliver on the corporate training and development strategy</li>
<li>Ensure execution of annual compliance training and certifications for all employees</li>
<li>Ensure delivery of soft skills training inclusive of communication, organization, planning, time management, and critical conversations</li>
</ul>
3. Organizational Development
<br><ul>
<li>Lead the company’s succession planning process</li>
<li>Identify the talent and skill gaps in the organization</li>
<li>Develop solutions and plans to address gaps in conjunction with leaders at all levels</li>
<li>Establish job hierarchies for all functions and communicate these in conjunction with department heads</li>
<li>Work with compensation function to develop relevant base salary ranges for each job level</li>
<li>Work with recruiting function to address talent gaps</li>
<li>In conjunction with business leaders, understand the future direction of the organization and plan to ensure an adequate supply of the required competencies and skills for success</li>
</ul>
4. Knowledge Management
<br><ul>
<li>Build corporate knowledge base as part of the LMS to preserve knowledge and business continuity</li>
<li>Minimize the company’s risk stemming from retirements and other exits</li>
<li>Facilitate the rapid integration of leaders into new roles as part of a job rotation development program</li>
</ul>
5. Leadership Development
<br><ul>
<li>Define the Morgan leadership inventory</li>
<li>Ensure Morgan leadership traits are integrated into the recruiting and performance management processes</li>
<li>Build, support, deliver training and coaching to support leadership transitions at all levels</li>
<li>Establish Leadership Talent Pool to develop potential successors across all functions</li>
</ul>
6. Other duties as assigned.
