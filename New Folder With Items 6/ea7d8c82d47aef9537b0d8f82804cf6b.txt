<h2>Process Engineer - Welding</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>Develop and support welding and assembly processes that meet customer specifications and timing for new and existing products as well as meet customer cost targets.</li>
<li>
Work with DURA plant personnel and cross functional teams, to ensure flawless launch of such processes for new contracts awarded</li>
<li>
Manage suppliers to ensure on-time delivery of welding and assembly equipment and tooling. Ensure all aspects of the assembly process, including the design, construction, trials, qualification and maintenance plans, meet specifications. As well as provide updates and status reports on the equipment to program leaders and management teams.</li>
<li>
Participates in DFM activities and discussion to improve product designs and manufacturing methods that support more robust processes and improved quality.</li>
<li>
Review product GD&amp;T and process simulations to determine manufacturing feasibility.</li>
<li>
Develop Welding Procedure Specifications and Procedure Qualification Records to document and support critical welding processes.</li>
<li>
Identify any destructive or mechanical testing that will need to be done prior to production launch.</li>
<li>
Leads the resolution of manufacturing process problems and customer concerns to promote continuous improvement of production efficiency and customer satisfaction.</li>
<li>
Lead continuous improvement activities including parameter improvement exercises, efficient design of joints, training activities, etc. Participate in various other lean events.</li>
<li>
Assist in the development of equipment and tooling standards.</li>
<li>
Assist in the identification and development of new equipment and tooling sources.</li>
<li>
Other duties as assigned by the Process Engineering Supervisor.</li>
<li>
Travel should be expected, primarily in North America with some travel to Europe as needed.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>
STANDARD QUALIFICATIONS:
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Degree in Welding or Manufacturing or other related engineering field is preferred. Extensive experience can be substituted for education.</li>
<li>
5-7 years of related experience</li>
<li>
Candidates should have a proven track record planning, managing, and buying off equipment and tooling.</li>
<li>
Be knowledgeable in lean manufacturing and principles, waste reduction, and six sigma</li>
<li>
Experience with GMAW, Laser Welding, RSW, and Projection Welding processes in both manual and robotic applications is preferred</li>
<li>
Must be proficient with basic GD&amp;T as well as destructive and non-destructive testing of welds per industry and automotive standards.</li>
<li>
Be proficient in AutoCAD and/or Solid Works</li>
<li>
Proficient with Microsoft Office tools (Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook)</li>
<li>
Strong organizational and time management skills is preferred</li>
</ul>

ABOUT DURA AUTOMOTIVE SYSTEMS</b></p>
<p>
DURA Automotive Systems, a Lynn Tilton company, is a global automotive supplier specializing in the design, engineering and manufacturing of innovative solutions that propel the vehicle development initiatives of automakers.</p>
<p>
DURA is focused on advancing global sustainability technology in three core areas:</p>
<ul>
<li>
Driving fuel economy improvement through vehicle lightweighting and intelligent mechatronics</li>
<li>
Advancing safety through innovative structural vehicle architectures and advanced driver control solutions</li>
<li>
Preserving our environment through sustainable manufacturing practices</li>
</ul>
<p>
The company’s products include mechatronic control systems, including shift-by-wire systems; electronic systems, including actuators and Advanced Driver Assist Systems (ADAS); lightweight structural body systems, including aluminum closures; and exterior systems, including window systems and exterior trim.</p>
<p>
DURA is headquartered in Auburn Hills, Michigan, USA; with global sales of $1.7 billion. The company has 12,000 employees worldwide and operates 40 facilities in 16 countries. Strong investments in new innovation programs and access to the extensive network of Lynn Tilton technology companies that span virtual gaming, electronics, fashion and aerospace industries have catapulted DURA’s future product offering to anticipate and satisfy the next generation of customer needs. DURA is nationally certified in the United States by the Women's Business Enterprise Council.</p>
