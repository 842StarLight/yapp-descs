Jumpstart Your Career with Nielsen’s
<br>Early Career Program in Operations!
<br>Founded in 1923, Nielsen is the global authority on the consumer. We help over 20,000 clients, from Coca-Cola to Walmart to Apple to Disney, in 105 countries understand consumers and grow their businesses, every day. The company is organized into two areas: What Consumers Watch (now Nielsen Global Media), serving media, telecom and tech industries; and What Consumers Buy (now Nielsen Global Connect), serving consumer goods, retail, financial services and automotive industries.
<br>At Nielsen, we invest in you. Nielsen’s Operations Early Career Program offers a powerful and unique experience for top undergraduates interested in a leadership career with the world’s leading provider of global marketing information, consumer insights, and business media. The Early Career Program (ECP) is a challenging 24-month rotational program designed to develop leaders who will position Nielsen for growth while at the same time investing heavily in your growth holistically to set you up for success.

Develop Your Future with Nielsen
<br>The Operations track develops future Nielsen leaders who can provide clients with the innovative data management and technology solutions they need to make critical decisions. Associates take on roles of increasing responsibility as they participate in exciting development experiences. Our next program begins July 2020 .
<br><br>
Rotational Assignments
<br>The program includes four six-month rotations over two years. These assignments provide challenging on-the-job learning and development opportunities. Associates work on high priority assignments in various business functions.
<br><br>
<b>Examples in Operations include:</b><br>
Understand Metrics That Drive Business Value – Establish key metrics to be used in determining acceptable performance levels of a business process.
<br>Deepen Analytical Skills – Analyze performance and delivery data for global department to identify root causes and develop improvement focused action plans.
<br>Challenging the Status Quo – Identification of process issues impacting data quality and Nielsen employee efficiency and implementation of solutions to correct these issues.
<br>Program Management - Rolling out new products or features as part of large scale changes to a line of business with cross functional exposure or leading a team.
<br><br>
<b>A day in the life of an Associate may include:</b> conducting project status updates with team members and key stakeholders; attending Lunch &amp; Learn training programs; working on project deliverables; and meeting with project stakeholders.
