<h2>Venue and Events Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><b>SUMMARY:</b></p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Serve as the Boston Symphony liaison to those seeking to rent Symphony Hall space. Represent venue as it relates to code compliance, city ordinance and etc.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p><b>RESPONSIBILITIES:</b></p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p><b>CONCERT SALES &amp; MANAGEMENT</b></p>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li> Sell Symphony Hall as a performance venue through local, regional and national media outlets.</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li> Sell the Hall and other function/performance spaces for rental for the purposes of rehearsals, lectures, auditions, recordings, functions, etc.</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li> Manage the production process for all incoming performances including, but not limited to: scheduling rehearsals, load-in/out, and staff times, managing City permit requirements when applicable, oversee all outside production vendors and staff.</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li> Manage the coding and prompt payment of concert promoters and concert service vendors who work do business with the BSO, Inc. in connection to a venue rental.</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li> Create sales packages for concert promoters including recommended price points, custom House scaling to meet promoter budget projections, coordinate promotional material for website and oversee all communication between the promoter and the BSO Box Office.</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li> Create and manage individual contracts and budgets for each venue rental including all contract revisions, outside labor &amp; equipment agreements and audio/video recording/broadcast rights agreements.</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li> Manage the accounts receivables related to all venue rental business including the collections and processing of promoter payments for outstanding expenses recouped through ticket sales and, when necessary, client invoices.</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<p><b>FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT</b></p>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li> Draft and manage the 7500 program.</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li> Make recommendations for Capital Equipment purchases and draft all requests for equipment (coordinate vendor quotes, proposed installation dates, total costs, product life expectancy, capital expenditures by fiscal year, etc).</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li> Reconcile cash receipt and journal entries for all event payments, deposits, and IDV’s related to Outside Concerts.</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li> Reconcile and invoice all internal and external based revenue (cash, credit, IDV), through EBMS, for Outside Concerts.</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li> Report on 7500 EBMS-based Accounts Receivable information to the Business Office and Department supervisor to track outstanding accounts, total sales and revenue figures and reconciling these with the Business Office monthly reports.</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li> Produce the City of Boston PILOT Program Analysis in conjunction with the Controller. This program tracks the sources of local NPO revenue (other NPO’s vs. commercial clients). This is an EBMS based report who’s final contents are edited and approved in conjunction with the Controller.</li></ul>
<p><b>DEPARTMENT/PERSONNEL MANAGEMENT (&amp; MISC)</b></p>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li> Manager on Duty for all outside rentals of Symphony Hall. Oversee a staff of approximately 50 people from four different departments (Facilities, Security, Operations and Marketing/Events). Plan and execute all events in keeping with BSO policy and regulations set forth by the City of Boston, Commonwealth of Massachusetts and the US Government.</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li> Manage the BSO House Doctor Program – a volunteer program that allows local area medical staff to sit for BSO performances while providing medical coverage for the House.</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li> Manage the Boston Police detail requests for all public performances at Symphony Hall.</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li> Book Orchestra personnel private rehearsals and lesson at Symphony Hall when needed.</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li> Oversee Receptions and Food &amp; Beverage activities at Symphony Hall and Tanglewood as needed.</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li> Personally assist in the physical set up and break down of events and/or standard operating areas on an as needed basis,</li></ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>QUALIFICATIONS:</b></p>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li> Sales experience related to high volume and large capacity venues.</li></ul>
<ul><li> Customer service experience related to venue sales.</li></ul>
<ul><li> Basic knowledge of facility maintenance and local fire/inspectional code.</li></ul>
<ul><li> Ability to handle multiple projects simultaneously and to deliver these on deadline.</li></ul>
<ul><li> Ability to coordinate with other staffers at multiple levels to deliver projects on deadline.</li></ul>
<ul><li> Ability to work with a variety of personnel in a multi-faceted performing arts NPO.</li></ul>
<ul><li> Ability to work in a fast paced and occasionally unpredictable environment.</li></ul>
<ul><li> Prior experience in a NPO/performing arts environment.</li></ul>
<ul><li> Prior experience in dealing with the City of Boston and other government bodies a plus.</li></ul>
<ul><li> Excellent computer skills including familiarity with:</li></ul>
<p>o Microsoft Office Suite (esp. Excel)</p>
<p>o Relational database software (Microsoft Access, EBMS preferred)</p>
<ul><li> Knowledge of general accounting and budget practices in both corporate and non-corporate environments.</li></ul>
