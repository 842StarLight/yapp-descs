<h2>Senior Associate, Strategic Initiatives</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><b>Company Profile</b></p>
<p>
Consortium Health Plans (www.consortiumhealthplans.com) is an independent company that focuses exclusively on its member / owner Blue Cross Blue Shield Plans’ national account growth efforts. Today, BCBS Plans serve more than 27 million total national account members, which represents over a quarter of the 103 million total Blue Cross Blue Shield members (one in three Americans).</p>
<p>
Consortium Health Plans strives to position Blue Cross Blue Shield Plans as the preferred carrier of national accounts. Of the 27 million national account members covered by BCBS Plans, nearly 25 million (92%) hail from Consortium’s 21 member Plans.</p>
<p>
Consortium Health Plans is proud of its team of experts in healthcare, technology, software development, data and analytics, actuarial science, underwriting, sales and marketing, and communications. Consortium’s executive leadership team encompasses a broad range of expertise, from strategic planning, benefits consulting, clinical delivery and data analytics, to sales and account management, marketing, and staff development.</p>
<p><b>
The Role:
</b></p>
<p>The Senior Associate, Strategic Initiatives will own multiple projects that directly support the strategic objectives of the company. The Senior Associate, Strategic Initiatives will also serve as a direct partner to Consortium’s Deputy Chief of Staff.</p>
<p><b>
The Candidate:</b></p>
<p>
Blue Cross Blue Shield Plans are seeking new innovative approaches to grow their National Account client base. Consortium Health Plans is seeking a versatile high-performer with a passion for health care who can work collaboratively with Consortium staff and member Plans to execute dynamic, transformational strategies.</p>
<p>
This individual would work closely with the Consortium’s Deputy Chief of Staff and Chief of Staff to support strategy development and ensure successful implementation of those strategies. Preferably, this person would draw upon experience from roles in a management consulting firm, a health care consulting firm, or equivalent – with a proven track record in data analysis, project management, and strategy execution. Specific health care experience is not a prerequisite.</p>
<p>
The Senior Associate, Strategic Initiatives must have strong interpersonal and communication skills (both verbal and written) and a clear understanding of how to navigate a project’s critical path, identify and resolve risks/issues, and coordinate across different functions and stakeholders to achieve a given objective. This individual is actively seeking to expand their business acumen, hone their technical skillset, and build their emotional intelligence to become a more well-rounded professional.</p>
<p>
 <b>Essential Responsibilities:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Manage Consortium Insights, a platform that Consortium Health Plans uses to host its various tools and solutions, provide marketing and sales resources, and disseminate information to its member plans</li>
<li>Conduct analyses and develop associated reports that support the Blue National Account strategy or Consortium’s strategic objectives</li>
<li>Support the development of operational dashboards that will be used to inform strategic and operational decisions</li>
<li>Provide direct support to the Deputy Chief of Staff for all Chief of Staff and CEO-led initiatives, priorities and responsibilities. Deputy Chief of Staff and Chief of Staff responsibilities include thought partnership, corporate strategy creation / iteration, KPI tracking, written material development, key meeting facilitation, interface with other Blue System entities, stakeholder management, etc.</li>
<li>Manage or support key strategic initiatives and work streams in support of the Blue National Account strategy</li>
<li>Comply with all information privacy and security policies as specified in the “Consortium Health Plans Information Privacy &amp; Security Policy Manual”</li>
</ul>
