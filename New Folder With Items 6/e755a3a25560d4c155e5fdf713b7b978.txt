<h2>Director, Affiliate Accounts and Business Development</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div><div>
<p>POSITION OBJECTIVE</p>
<p>
The Director, Affiliate Programs and Business Development, contributes to the strategic growth and success of Executive Education at the Weatherhead School of Management. This position will lead the marketing and sales of Affiliate partnerships to accelerate the growth of Executive Education. The director works with the Director of Custom Corporate Programs and Business Development to meet annual revenue and contribution margin goals. The position will take the lead on prospecting, securing and maintaining Affiliate Partnerships. This position is also responsible for representing the entire portfolio of executive education offerings including custom programs to targeted corporate accounts. The director will identify and link the needs of corporations with the resources available at the school by developing effective relationships with executive leadership teams at these client companies.</p>
<p>
Internally, the position will serve as a key link to the external business community and be responsible for contributing important market and client information to ensure effective internal decision-making. The reputation of the Weatherhead School of Management will be enhanced as a result of marketing, developing and delivering distinctive education experiences regionally, nationally and internationally.</p>
<p></p>
<br>
<p>
ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS</p>
<ul>
<li>Responsible for generating interest, securing commitments (signed contract where appropriate) and increasing participation in Executive Education programs – focusing on Affiliate Partnerships and Custom – by establishing a relationship and maintaining frequent communication with key leaders and decision makers within targeted organizations. With the Director of Business Development, identify and select key/strategic accounts to target. Represent portfolio of Weatherhead School of Management Executive Education offerings, degree programs, events and other opportunities (i.e., Fowler Center, corporate recruiting, action learning, development activities, etc.) to the corporate community. Personally develop new local and regional business relationships. Handle incoming requests for information about Affiliate Partnerships. (40%)</li>
<li>Primary contact for Affiliate and Custom client organizations (after commitment is secured) through all phases of program development, preparation and delivery experience. Participate in design and development meetings as necessary. Identify faculty director and work with the faculty director to secure faculty to successfully deliver the program on budget and on time. Establish budget for the program and communicates to the program delivery team. Responsible for working closely with the project delivery team to deliver a “WOW” experience for the client. Communicate with internal and external stakeholders to apprise them of the project’s status, changes, issues and/or concerns, and any pertinent information regarding the client and/or their experience. Negotiate components of Affiliate Program packages (size, timeframe, cost, flexibility within, etc.) balancing the needs of the client organization with the school’s business needs. Ensure positive financial standing of affiliate accounts; manage billing, monitor revenue, and coordinate collection, consolidation, and evaluation of financial data. Prepare special reports as requested. Create a customized Competency Grid for each affiliate organization by matching their competencies with each Open Enrollment program. Monitor Affiliate activity to ensure that the program budget is being fully utilized and that the participants’ and sponsors’ expectations are being exceeded. (30%).</li>
<li>For own Key Accounts, must be able to generate interest, conduct client needs analysis, prepare proposals (working with Director of Business Development, Executive Director, and Program Delivery Team, where necessary), and secure commitments (signed contract where appropriate). Must be able to establish effective relationships and maintain frequent communication with key leaders and decision makers at target corporations (CEO, CIO, COO, CHRO, etc.). Through communications/discussions, gain an understanding of the company’s training and development philosophy, relationships with other universities (regionally and nationally), and current and past connections with the school and university (at the corporate and individual level). (25%)</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<br>
<p>
NONESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS</p>
<ul>
<li>Participate in meetings and discussions on projects and decisions that impact the entire Executive Education team. Includes contributing ideas for program development of new programs based upon understanding and knowledge of market needs. (3%)</li>
<li>Propose improvements to existing database to improve financial record-keeping. Manage affiliate mailbox. Enter and/or oversee relevant current and potential affiliate inquiry and registration data entry. May train temporary and student workers on database and mailbox management to support registration and payment processes as assigned. (1%)</li>
<li>Perform other duties as assigned. (1%)</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<br>
<p>
CONTACTS</p>
<p>
Department: Daily contact with Executive Education staff.</p>
<p>
University: Daily contact with university departments as needed, marketing, alumni and corporate relations, information technology group, legal, finance, university central offices including general accounting.</p>
<p>
External: Daily contact with prospective and current client organizations, including many Fortune 500 companies, mid-sized corporations, select national corporations, professional associations and not-for-profit organizations. Direct contacts include professionals and directors of HR/OD function, Senior Leaders and Chief Operators. Contact with program participants.</p>
<p>
Students: Contact with current and prospective Executive Education clientele.</p>
<p></p>
<br>
<p>
SUPERVISORY RESPONSIBILITY</p>
<p>
This position has no direct reports at this time.</p>
<p></p>
<br>
<p>
QUALIFICATIONS</p>
<p>
Experience: 5 or more years of experience working in a corporate, consulting or academic setting with a history of increasing responsibilities in selling and marketing high end services or solutions. Minimum of 3 years successful experience selling and marketing services to high-level executives. Experience identifying market segments and customers, and track record of growing the breadth and depth of client relationships over time is preferred.</p>
<p>
Education/Licensing: Bachelor’s degree required. Master’s degree in business, education, organization development or related field required.</p>
<p></p>
<br>
<p>
REQUIRED SKILLS</p>
<ul>
<li>Ability to think creatively and strategically and act proactively with a high sense of commitment</li>
<li>Proven successful experience of managing programs and budgets</li>
<li>Excellent communication, interpersonal and networking skills</li>
<li>Experience and skills of operating effectively in a large complex organization and broad sector/commercial awareness</li>
<li>Ability to develop strong working relationships with faculty</li>
<li>Demonstrated ability to manage multiple projects with tight deadlines and consistently meet deadlines</li>
<li>Team-oriented, flexible and customer-service oriented manner</li>
<li>Proven ability to successfully interact with a diverse group of people</li>
<li>Ability to work effectively in a dynamic environment with multiple and sometimes conflicting priorities</li>
<li>Proficiency in Microsoft Office (Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Access, Outlook).</li>
<li>Ability to meet consistent attendance.</li>
<li>Ability to interact with colleagues, supervisors, and customers face to face.</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<br>
<p>
WORKING CONDITIONS</p>
<p>
Fast paced constantly changing working environment requiring adaptability and flexibility. Job may require long hours, including evenings and weekends. Job requires travel, including driving of personal vehicle to and from client appointments.</p>
