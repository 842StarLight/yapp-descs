<div><div><div>

<p><b>Job Summary</b><br>
The Annual Giving Officer will work with partners in Advancement and Athletics, with a goal to secure annual fund leadership gifts to support Susquehanna University and its athletics program. The Annual Giving Officer is responsible for maximizing annual gifts to the Orange and Maroon Club, including all varsity and club sports, though the cultivation, solicitation and stewardship of assigned prospects, as well as solicitation of all athletic alumni, parents and friends of the university. A primary focus will be to increasing alumni participation through athletic giving by direct mail, special campaigns and email campaigns.</p>
<ul>
<b>
Specific Responsibilities</b><br>
<li>Manage a portfolio of approximately 100 leadership annual giving prospects. Develop, document and implement engagement and solicitation strategy for each assigned prospect. A minimum of 70 personal visits are required per fiscal year with additional expectations in outreach and contact via personal and digital communications.</li>
<li>Communicates with alumni, parents and friends based on athletic department priorities through direct mail, email, social media, special campaigns, phone calls and face to face meetings. Regular attendance at University athletic events and related alumni athletic events.</li>
<li>Focused effort on increasing alumni participation through athletics giving campaigns, including annual “30 for 30” campaign, as well as leveraging annual fund solicitations for athletics donors.</li>
<li>Revise and grow the Business and Industry program to educate and connect with local business and community partners on sponsorships and gifts.</li>
<li>Work in partnership with Director of SUF, Director of Athletics and coaches to develop a deep understanding of athletic programs and communications to alumni, including athletics sponsorship outreach.</li>
<li>Work with all members of the division as appropriate to advance the goals of the university. This will include work and attendance as assigned for events such as Homecoming, Family Weekend, the Milestones donor relations event, the scholarship dinner and other similar events and activities that promote the mission and vision of the university.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div><b>Required Qualifications</b></div>
<div>
<p><b>Knowledge, Skills and Abilities</b><br>
Excellent organizational and project management skills; proficiently skilled with database software, e-mail, calendar and Microsoft Office; knowledge of or ability to learn Advancement software (currently Ellucian Colleague Advancement); ability to manage multiple tasks; ability to effectively interact with alumni, parents, students, faculty, and administration; skilled in written and oral communication, as well as, an attention to accuracy; ability to work independently and take initiative; ability and willingness to travel (frequent local travel, occasional regional travel). Ability and willingness to work evenings and weekends around university events and activities as needed.</p>
<p><b>
Education</b><br>
Bachelor’s degree required.</p>
<p><b>
Experience</b><br>
Two years of fundraising, sales, or marking experience or equivalent is required. Valid U.S. driver’s license</p>
<p><b>
Physical Demand</b><br>
Normal office environment. Occasional evening and weekend work required.</p>
