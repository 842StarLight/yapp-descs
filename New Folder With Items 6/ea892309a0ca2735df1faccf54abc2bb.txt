<h2>Director of Admissions</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>Reporting to the Chief Enrollment Management Officer, the Director of Admissions oversees a team of professionals who are responsible for engaging and assisting undergraduate and graduate students and their families through each of the stages of the student admissions cycle. The Director will build on the successes of the university, engaging potential students, aligning communications with the university’s brand identity, ensuring effective and efficient customer service, and enhancing students and families ability to complete the admissions process. The Director is responsible for participating in the development, implementation, and assessment of a strategic enrollment plan and for meeting recruitment goals for team and self.</p>
<div><div>
<div>Duties and Responsibilities
</div>
<p></p>
<div>
<div>
<div><b>Duty/Responsibility</b></div>
<div>
<p>Use interpersonal and management skills to lead, support, motivate, assess, and develop a team of professional admissions counselors.</p>
<p>
Articulate to prospective students and parents the admission process and policy, financial aid opportunities, academic programs, and campus activities.</p>
<p>
Maintain our robust CRM (Slate).</p>
<p>
As a working director, responsible for an area of recruitment based on experience and background – international, adult, graduate etc.</p>
<p>
Work closely with off campus marketing, consultant, and search firms.</p>
<p>
Continually evaluate and recommend or initiate positive change in the admission and recruitment process.</p>
<p>
Utilize data to identify and market to target populations of high quality and diverse students.</p>
<p>
Implement a comprehensive enrollment management action plan to ensure achievement of annual recruitment goals.</p>
<p>
Work closely with administration and faculty in joint recruitment efforts, utilizing both to optimize enrollment.</p>
<p>
Maintain up-to-date knowledge of campus activities, academic programs, and participate in campus events.</p>
