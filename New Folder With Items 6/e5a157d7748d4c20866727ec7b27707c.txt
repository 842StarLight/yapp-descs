<h2>Manager of Community Support</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<p>The <b>Manager of Community Support</b> contributes to the success of One Hope United by reporting to and working with, the Chief Development &amp; Marketing Officer to significantly increase the contribution of auxiliary and ambassador boards to fund the programs and services of OHU. The Manager will be responsible for overseeing for coordinating all aspects of the auxiliary and ambassador boards’ associated events and activities.</p>
<p><b>
Position Responsibilities</b></p>
<ul><li>
Serves as the primary liaison and support for Lake County Ambassador Board, Chicago Ambassador Board, and Chicago Auxiliary Board - leading volunteer recruitment efforts, managing events and coordinating meetings for the groups in accordance with their charters.</li></ul>
<ul>
<li>
Trains volunteer on best practices related to events and revenue growth strategies by utilizing a strategy screen to illustrate the return on investment for the organization and determine the continuation, elimination and/or creation of events</li>
<li>
In coordination with Chief Development and Marketing Officer, develops and manages financial goals for affiliate groups’ events and activities to support OHU annual budget.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
Responsible for the organization and support of additional affiliate groups resulting from service growth and expansion.</li>
<li>
Secures individual financial contributions from members of affiliate boards and facilitates their solicitation of others.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
Manages the coordination and implementation of all aspects of Ambassador and Auxiliary Board event planning, working with event consultants, OHU staff, and volunteers. Develops and produces all event materials, communicates with vendors, manages contracts, and oversees event production including Golf Tournament, Duck Derby, Hope After Dark, Rockin’ for Hope and other events developed by affiliate groups in support of contributed revenue goals.</li>
<li>
Maintains accurate financial records for events and works with internal and external vendors responsible for event logistics. Cultivates a strong working relationship with the Chair and members of the Boards and coordinates communication with the OHU Executive Staff and Board of Directors’ Fund Development Committee.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
Drives volunteer solicitation of event sponsorships, contributions for auctions and ticket sales/support for related events.</li>
<li>
Works with OHU Marketing &amp; Communications team as the resource for the development of collateral materials, advertising and promotion of events.</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<br>
<p><b>
Position Requirements and Specifications</b></p>
<p>
This position includes the following requirements:</p>
<ul>
<li>
Bachelor’s degree in Liberal Arts, Communications, Business, Hospitality, Event Management or other relevant field or equivalent combination of training and experience.</li>
<li>
Relevant experience of 3 to 5 years working and/or volunteering in development, non-profit, hospitality, or customer service positions.</li>
<li>
Creative individual with the ability to think strategically and deal with changing priorities, emergent issues, multiple projects, staff and volunteers simultaneously.</li>
<li>
Self-motivated, results-oriented, confident, and customer-oriented, with strong people skills. Must be highly flexible and able to adapt and respond to change.</li>
<li>
Ability to establish and maintain strong rapport and positive relationships.</li>
<li>
Articulate with strong interpersonal and communication skills, including verbal, listening, and presentation skills required.</li>
<li>
Ability to work with minimal supervision.</li>
<li>
Computer proficiency with Microsoft Outlook, Word, PowerPoint and Excel required. Experience with CRM tools database packages preferred.</li>
<li>
Ability to maintain the confidentiality of privileged information.</li>
<li>
Ability to work under pressure and meet critical deadlines.</li>
<li>
Cultural competency; experience and/or ability to work and interact effectively with a diverse, multicultural audience.</li>
<li>
Flexibility to work evenings, nights and weekends as needed to support affiliate board activities and OHU Fund Development programs or events</li>
<li>
Ability to travel to OHU sites across multi-state service area approximately 10-15% of the time</li>
<li>
Valid driver’s license and reliable transportation required.</li>
</ul>
<p>
Additional Information: This position is highly visible, and incumbent interacts with all departments, as well as a range of external constituencies. Must have professional appearance and demeanor. Incumbent is required to gain an understanding of the organization, its mission, vision, values, and operating philosophies, and how developmental activities impact the operation and success of the organization. Individual is expected to be present at development, marketing, and other events sponsored by OHU for its employees, volunteers, and other professional participants and audiences. Position requires early morning, evenings, and weekends.</p>
