<p>
New York Life’s primary distribution channel is the career Agency sales force of more than 12,000 agents, operating in virtually every major city in the United States. Every agent is affiliated with one of our 117 General Offices. These offices are operated by Agency management teams responsible for agent and management recruiting, development, and sales. Approximately half our agents operate their businesses out of our General Offices, while the other half maintains independent office locations.</p>
<p>
Our General Offices are divided up among four Zones. In addition to our Zone offices, an Agency Home Office team responsible for strategy, finance, administration, standards, training, marketing and communications supports our agents and field managers. The Agency operation also comprises teams charged with growing our business in key market segments, including the middle-income market, the advanced (high-net-worth) market, select cultural markets, and the women’s markets. Agents sell individual life insurance, individual annuities, and long-term care insurance products, as well as mutual funds.</p>

Role Overview:</p>
<p>
The Field Development, Prospecting &amp; Retention (FDPR) - Prospecting Initiatives team is accountable for the design, codification, implementation and operational oversight of national prospecting sourcing strategies aimed at empowering agents to secure new clients via self-identified “warm nest” networks, in concert with corporate prospecting (local events, educational seminars, etc.) and corporate marketing lead generation (digital advertising, owned/earned marketing channels &amp; external affiliate partnerships) programs.</p>
<p>
The <i>Senior Associate - Agent Prospecting Strategist </i>role will be a critical support player in the end-to-end design of agent warm prospect sourcing strategies and tactics: Strategy, initiative design, launch planning, operational oversight and continuous improvement. These programs will teach agents and Field Management “how to fish” via targeted sourcing activities for new cliental aligned to their aspirational target market. This role is expected to evolve over time as the Prospecting Initiative team matures, new efforts are undertaken, and historical programs are rationalized. The ideal candidate will be able to shape the role based on their qualifications and performance in collaboration with the broader FDPR team.</p>
