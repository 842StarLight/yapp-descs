<p></p>
<br>
<p>
The <b>Operations Technician</b> is responsible for the effective operation, repair and changeovers of production equipment. This position will work with others to achieve factory business objectives of safety, productivity, customer service, quality, cost and environmental protection. The primary accountabilities of the role include safety compliance, daily production, quality and sanitization procedures, equipment maintenance procedures, data collection, housekeeping, problem solving and continuous improvement as part of a work team. The Operations Technician works within a structure that requires considerable interaction with others in the same production unit as well as other production units and support functions.</p>
<p></p>
<br>
<p><b>
Responsibilities for this position include:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Operates, monitors, and performs changeovers and maintenance of automated and manual equipment throughout the production facility through computer terminals, control panels and/or by manual means.</li>
<li>Develops and maintains production schedules to ensure production systems are managed in an effective, reliable and efficient manner.</li>
<li>Unloads materials and supplies from delivery vehicles and delivers those materials and supplies to storage and production areas as needed.</li>
<li>Performs quality sampling and statistical process control activities both manually and through computer keyboard throughout the manufacturing process.</li>
<li>Troubleshoots and performs repairs and other maintenance on production equipment, supported by maintenance personnel as needed.</li>
<li>Prepares and loads finished product for shipping using SAP software system and working with local warehouse and distribution network employees.</li>
<li>Operates forklift, robotic palletizer, stretch wrapper &amp; other warehousing equipment.</li>
<li>Performs Housekeeping activities as per established protocol and conducts/participates in area housekeeping assessments per requirement.</li>
<li>Understands and is accountable for full compliance with safety and other EOHS requirements.</li>
<li>Understands and is accountable for following all Quality, GMP, AIB and Micro [C&amp;S Guidelines] standards and guidelines.</li>
<li>Works with other employees and management on continuous improvement projects and process design.</li>
<li>Meets business needs by flexing scheduled work hours or contributing additional work hours when needed.</li>
<li>Understands and complies with Hill’s policies, safety standards, Good Manufacturing Practices (GMPs) and Food Safety.</li>
<li>Identifies and reports Food Safety issues to management.</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<br>
<p><b>
Basic Qualifications:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>High school diploma or equivalent.</li>
<li>Willing and able to work any shift schedule, including nights and weekends.</li>

</ul>
