<h2>Business Development Manager - Transportation</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<p>Reporting to the Global Director Business Development the Business Development Manager (BDM) role is to develop and execute Strategic Sales Campaigns in support of the regional/theater lead and in coordination with corporate sales objectives laid out on annual basis.
</p>
<ul>
<li>The BDM will support detailed market research, analysis, segmentation, and campaign targeting. Develop and proactively maintain sufficient knowledge of the target industry by building relationships with industry experts to learn growth trends and issues, and with senior level executives to discover potential opportunities to effectively align and apply OSIsoft’s technologies to meet customer's business goals
</li>
<li>Within 90 days of start, main deliverable is an agreement with the Director Global Business Development and aligned with RSM/TVP priorities on where to focus business/market development strategic sales efforts (the “Targeted Segment”). The BDM will be supported by Global New Logos team to quantify and present the market opportunities, ease of entry, and achievability in the respective region/theater. (Collectively the “Sales Strategy”).
</li>
<li>Upon agreement of the Sales Strategy, the BDM will develop and execute demand generation, prospect, drive account penetration and/or partner development strategies to develop qualified pipeline in Targeted Segment(s). The BDM will coordinate with Marketing to ensure adequate marketing collateral and sales tools are available to pursue the campaign. Marketing will be responsible for creation, maintenance, and delivery of targeted regional marketing campaigns/content in alignment with the BDM.
</li>
<li>A successful Business Development Campaign will:
<ul>
<li>Create and deliver effective messaging and materials in coordination with Campaign Managers – Lead creation;
</li>
<li>Drive engagements and meetings (prospecting and discovery) - lead written and verbal sales engagements addressing the business needs of the customer to propose technology-based solutions;
</li>
<li>Act as subject matter expert on the customer development, prospecting and conducting discovery, qualifying Opportunities and bringing them to 30% to close;
</li>
<li>Ensure smooth transition to appropriate Account Manager(s) to drive Opportunity close in agreement with RSM/TVP.
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>The BDM shall leverage OSIsoft global and local partner relationships that can positively influence the purchase of OSIsoft products and services. In select cases, the BDM will qualify and nurture regional partners that are focused in Target Segments in the region.
</li>
<li>The BDM shall maintain and deliver accurate sales forecasts within the Targeted Segments in the Region and report on Campaign effectiveness. The BDM will conduct periodic “Review Calls” with RSM/TVP and Corporate Leadership to review OSIsoft’s current position in the Segment, campaign effectiveness, strategic opportunities or threats, and key future activities.
</li>
<li>Qualify and nurture existing target accounts and prospects within North American Railways and metro commuter operations, while building new prospects in “targeted segments”.
</li>
<li>Maintain personal knowledge and skill levels with OSIsoft products and sales support applications according to guidelines established by OSIsoft through recurrent annual training within the Company or designated external resources.
</li>
</ul>
<p>Qualifications:
</p>
<ul>
<li>Undergraduate or advanced degree in Engineering preferred
</li>
<li>5-10 years in direct enterprise-level software sales required with a track record of sustained success
</li>
<li>Minimum of five years’ experience working in or selling rail or intermodal transportation software, transportation data acquisition, controls and equipment, or other areas of supply chain management systems. Additional experience in Data Centers/Facilities, Manufacturing and/or Telecommunications are a plus.
</li>
<li>Experience in an entrepreneurial sales environment, selling recently developed products, or selling into new markets is a plus
</li>
<li>Ability to articulate the business values of the PI infrastructure (i.e. not sell on feature/function but explain clearly how capabilities related to business challenges).
</li>
<li>Ability to know how to get the right information from the right people (both internal and external)
</li>
<li>Comfort and experience speaking and presenting to all levels of an organization
</li>
</ul>
