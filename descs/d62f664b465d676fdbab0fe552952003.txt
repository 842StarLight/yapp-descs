<p><b>General Description (major areas of responsibility of this position):</b></p>
<p>A person in this position is responsible to safely drive a class B vehicle, requiring a CDL (requiring a valid DOT CDL with medical card, and hazmat endorsement), to regularly perform deliveries to support customer purchases and improved relationship (which may include managing and replenishing consignments), recovering used batteries, performing customer installations, and delivery loading/unloading, assists in training others, and records the successful processes and procedures required to carry out all functions. May perform deliveries requiring DOT HOS records and medical clearance. Experience with safe driving practices and material handling equipment and processes is significant. Excellence is demonstrated consistently and process improvements are contributed.</p>

<p><b>Physical Requirements / Working Conditions:</b></p>
<p>The physical demands described herein are representative of those that must be met by an employee to perform the essential functions of this job. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions.</p>
<p>Physical strength is imperative within this job. This is primarily a non-stationary position working in a non-temperature-controlled environment with extended periods of time spent sitting to operate a motor vehicle, standing or walking to collect, store, retrieve, pack for shipping, and count parts requiring the ability to, lift, carry, climb, reach, balance, stoop, kneel, crouch, high finger dexterity, and continuous mental and visual attention with eyesight correctable to 20/20 and specific vision requirements of close vision and the ability to adjust focus. Peripheral vision and depth perception are also required for driving and warehouse safety. The employee occasionally operates a keyboard/mouse, answers telephone calls and occasionally bends, reaches, and pushes and pulls file drawers to file documents. The employee moves within the building and customer facilities to collect, move, and deliver large batteries, which requires the use of stairs and ladders. Employees in this job must carry up to 75 lbs, or push carts with product weighing up to 3,000 lbs. Participation in group lifts for heavier weight is required. Use of hand tools is required. Personal protective equipment consists of safety glasses and safety shoes, and may include gloves.</p>
