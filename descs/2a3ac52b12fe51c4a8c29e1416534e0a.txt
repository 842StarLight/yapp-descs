<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Locations:
</b></h2>US-Miami, FL
<p></p>
<div><p>Final Eye Specialists assist with ongoing jobs, assessing project specs and performing a thorough, non-language final-eye review of documents before the conclusion of the project. This is a unique opportunity in a financially stable and rapidly growing international communications services company. TransPerfect’s services include: translation, interpreting, voice-overs, subtitling, and cultural marketing campaigns. A Final Eye Specialist’s principal duty is to review a project in its final stage and approve it for release to the client.
</p></div>
<p></p>
<div><h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>DESCRIPTION
</b></h2></div>
<ul>
<li>Understanding TransPerfect’s quality standards and making sure they are upheld
</li>
<li>Understanding the client’s expectations and making sure they are upheld
</li>
<li>Consulting with the Project Managers and sales team to discuss the client’s expectations or specifications
</li>
<li>Pre-flighting source files and discussing any findings with the project manager
</li>
<li>Properly utilize all appropriate supplemental materials (refer to client PAQ, reference material, glossaries, etc.)
</li>
<li>Proofread all documents with a strong focus on names, numbers, missing text, punctuation/spelling, grammar, adherence to instructions, proper use of glossaries/memory/reference materials or other significant issues
</li>
<li>Providing feedback to project managers or others who are involved in the project
</li>
<li>A Final Eye Specialist must be familiar with the life-cycle of a normal job. He/She should have a keen eye for detail, manage his/her time effectively, communicate information or feedback clearly to Production, and be able to work comfortably in MS Office and PDF formats
</li>
</ul>
<div><h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>REQUIRED SKILLS
</b></h2></div>

</li>
<li>Attention to detail
</li>
<li>Ability to learn new skills quickly
</li>
<li>Exceptional problem solving/critical thinking skills
</li>
<li>Ability to maintain professionalism, focus and result-orientation under pressure and tight deadlines
</li>
<li>80% on either Phase 1 or 1.2 Final-Eye tests, the second score must be a minimum of 70%
