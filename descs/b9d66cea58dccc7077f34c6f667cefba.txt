<p><b>
Position Summary</b></p>
<p></p>
<br>
<p>
The SAM Analyst III is consultative in nature, heavy in analysis, interpretation, and advisory responsibilities. Use of data gathered via IT Asset Management (ITAM) related toolsets, in addition to Software Asset Management (SAM) knowledge of specific license rules, CDK agreements, processes, current and future environment, and other relevant inputs in order execute and mature the SAM Program to reduce financial and security risks.</p>
<p></p>
<br>
<p><b>
Position Responsibilities &amp; Essential functions</b></p>
<p></p>
<br>
<ul>
<li>
Act as lead for Software Asset Management function, educate on specific and tangible benefits to the audience, articulating needs and cooperation cross departmentally</li>
<li>
Heavy use of Software Asset Management tool and other internal resources, Responsible for data quality, completeness, creating cross department linkages, and conducting quality checks</li>
<li>
Conduct formal vendor audits or internal audits acting in a lead role in data analysis, articulate actions for risk mitigation, and audit results</li>
<li>
Conduct detailed review of software contracts for product use rights, software agreements, master contracts, and advising Legal, drafting Terms and Conditions (T&amp;Cs) to reduce risk to the company and deciphering and meticulously documenting an audit trail</li>
<li>
Identify processes improvements, data improvements, gather evidence for use in business cases while performing detailed work with all required SME areas to develop process/data, communications, and education/training</li>
<li>
Provide optimization recommendations to the business inclusive of infrastructure changes, contractual or licensing options, and tracking measurement of progress</li>
<li>
Analyze and advise on risk and opportunity within software agreement structure during negotiations and audits from a software asset view while considering use, deployment, and infrastructure of the environment</li>
<li>
Develop trusted relationships with relevant businesses, including legal, procurement, finance, tax, infrastructure, engineers, architecture, project management, development, data or process owners, IT operations, business users, vendors/resellers, etc.</li>
<li>
Act as team Subject Matter Expert (SME) educating and guiding as appropriate</li>
</ul>
