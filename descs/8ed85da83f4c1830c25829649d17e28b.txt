<h2>Senior Financial Analyst</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<div>
<div>
<b>Akima, LLC</b> is an Alaska Native Corporation (ANC) with 30 years of federal contracting experience and over 7,500 employees supporting more than 14,300 Iñupiat shareholders in the NANA region of Alaska. Akima provides the backbone infrastructure to support more than 40 socio-economically disadvantaged small businesses (SDBs) with mature business processes and shared back-office support services normally only found in a Fortune 500 defense contractor. Our employees form one of the broadest skill sets in all of government and industry delivering services and solutions in fields as diverse as aviation, construction, facilities and logistics, information technology, protective services and detention management, and systems engineering. We continually look for the very best and brightest to support our commitment to superior outcomes across today’s most complex and critical missions worldwide. To learn more about Akima, LLC and opportunities across our family of companies, visit www.akima.com</div>
<div></div>
<div>
<br>
<div>
Akima, LLC is looking to hire a Senior Financial Analyst to join our growing and dynamic organization. In this role, you will be responsible for financial planning, reporting, and analysis supporting one or more subsidiaries within the Technology Solutions and Products Group, Akima, LLC. The ideal candidate will be an exceptional communicator and have a collaborative, strategic work style. This is the perfect position for a strategic thinker who is a highly analytical problem solver, and enjoys being challenged, working in a hands on capacity as part of a fast-paced team. We are looking for someone who is able to see the big picture, but is diligent managing details.</div>
</div>
<br>
<div></div>
<div></div>
<div><b>Responsibilities:</b></div>
<ul>
<li>Lead the creation of monthly and quarterly financial business review to provide insight into business.
</li>
<li>Prepare monthly forecast, risk assessment and variance analysis to senior management</li>
<li>
Prepare financials for monthly close and forecasting process,
</li>
<li>Analyze both the monthly forecast and current quarter and future projections.
</li>
<li>Act as focal point for communication of financials
</li>
<li>Develop long-range financial models in collaboration with the management team
</li>
<li>Provide financial leadership to the business, helping drive profitable growth for the Akima subsidiary.
</li>
<li>Be a trusted business partner for executive management and business units.
</li>
<li>Collaborate across organizations/functions including sales, operations, line of business and project management
</li>
<li>Ensure subsidiary readiness for year-end audit
</li>
<li>Manage revenue and cost adjustment submission, ensuring accuracy and review of proper back up information.</li>
<li>
Ensure revenues are properly recognized, and participate in pre-close job cost/revenue reviews with corporate accounting staff.
</li>
<li>Monitor and assess financial impact changes of Indirect rates, rate pools, and allocations</li>
<li>
Support the Corporate Accounting staff on audit inquiries.</li>
<li>
Manage DSO and Cash Flow</li>
</ul>
</div>
<p></p>
<div>
<b>
Qualifications</b><div>
</div>
<div><b>
Minimum Requirements:</b></div>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<div>Undergraduate degree in Finance or Accounting</div>
</li>
<li>Ten or more years of professional government contracting experience in financial forecasting and annual budget preparation across multiple financial disciplines</li>
<li>Advanced level skills in Excel, Powerpoint, Costpoint and Cognos required</li>
<li>Experience with Organizational level Finance support of various programs and/or projects in excess of $70 million in annual revenue.</li>
<li>Experience with Deltek Costpoint (project/account/org structures, cost and burden process, revenue recognition formulas, inquiries and reports).</li>
<li>Experience preparing EACs.</li>
<li>Solid understanding of revenue recognition</li>
<li>Experience with Time &amp; Materials, Firm Fixed Price, and Cost Plus type Government contracts</li>
<li>Working knowledge of Indirect rates, rate pools, and allocations</li>
<li>Solid Understanding of GAAP, Revenue Recognition and Deferred Revenue</li>
<li>Strong business acumen coupled with financial prudence</li>
<li>Ability to work under pressure in fast paced challenging environment</li>
<br>
</ul>
</div>
