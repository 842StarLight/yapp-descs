<h2>Technical Service Representative</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<p><b>ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
<b>
Technical Field Support: </b>Visit body shops and jobber stores; Provide consultation, trouble shooting, employ root-cause analysis; Resolve shop/product and technical problems; Provide on-site, in person, technical service &amp; council on TAT products; Consult on selection &amp; proper use of Transtar product. Record daily activities, including problems, opportunities and open issues.</li>
<li>
<b>
Technical Phone Support:</b> Manage tech support phone line; Respond to customer inquiries on the telephone or e-mail; Resolve product and customer issues over the phone, by email or text. Log all calls in CRM, record opportunities for improvement, open issues, problems and follow up in a timely manner. Use SOP’s when possible</li>
<li>
<b>
</b><b>Shop Complaints / Claims:</b> Assist with quickly, correctly resolving user issues; Close open product claims; Assist with product investigation where necessary; Complete, maintain &amp; process pertinent paperwork &amp; records involving product complaints &amp; other advisory functions; Administer customer complaints and warranty issues. Follow up with customers regularly to move customer satisfaction.</li>
<li>
<b>
No Mix Basecoat Tech Support &amp; Installations:</b> Support NM paint line sales growth through color matching, color documentation (formulations); Schedule &amp; complete system installations &amp; training at shops / distributors; Conduct shop audit &amp; submit analysis; Provide software support for No Mix PLUS; Support car cutting program. Follow up with every No Mix customer to determine customer satisfaction after installation at 2, 6 and 12 weeks to increase sales per No Mix installs.</li>
<li>
<b>
Product Testing &amp; Evaluation:</b> Conduct field testing on specified products; Document &amp; report findings on testing &amp; field trials; Communicate with R&amp;D / Marketing; Assist with follow up research testing.</li>
<li>
<b>
Sales Support:</b> Support RM and Territory Managers in the field; Conduct field presentations, demos; Conduct field training with TAT customers (shops, jobbers) &amp; sales personnel (STARS, sales meetings); Work collaboratively with TAT management to develop training curriculums as requested.</li>
<li>
<b>
Special Projects:</b> Assume responsibility for special projects as assigned.</li>
<li>
<b>
Learning &amp; Development:</b> Expand technical knowledge and trouble shooting ability. Attend regular training in Brighton; Actively participate in &amp; contribute to personal &amp; team learning &amp; development. Field Training &amp; Discovery: Develop a "personal plan" for informal learning in the field; Use, spray &amp; test TAT &amp; competitive products; Actively participate in &amp; contribute to personal learning &amp; development.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>
EDUCATION:</b> Minimum 2-year technical degree and at least 5 years of experience in a professional body shop and three years of experience in a body shop as a painter.</p>
<p><b>
CERTIFICATES, LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Current driver license</li>
<li>
I-CAR certification - preferred, not required)</li>
<li>
Certification by a major color coat manufacturer in application of refinish coatings - preferred, not required</li>
<li>
Valid passport</li>
</ul>

SUPERVISORY RESPONSIBILITIES AND REPORTING</b>: This position has no direct supervisory responsibilities.</p>

<p>
In the absence of a department supervisor, this position reports to the Director of Training and Technical Services.</p>
<p><b>
LANGUAGE SKILLS:</b> Ability to read, analyze, and interpret common technical and scientific journals. Ability to respond to common inquiries or complaints from customers or regulatory agencies. Ability to effectively present information to technical and non-technical personnel including - shop owners, painters, body men, jobber personnel, Transtar sales personnel, management and other staff.</p>
<p>
Ability to speak, read and write in English and Spanish (U.S position) or French (Canadian position) is required in some geographic areas.</p>
<p><b>
MATHEMATICAL SKILLS:</b> Ability to work with mathematical concepts such as apply concepts such as fractions, percentages, ratios, and proportions to practical situations.</p>
<p><b>
REASONING ABILITY:</b> Ability to apply principles of logical or scientific thinking to a wide range of intellectual and practical problems. Ability to define problems, collect data, establish facts, and draw valid conclusions. Ability to interpret an extensive variety of technical instructions in mathematical or diagram form and deal with several abstract and concrete variables.</p>
