<h2>Specialist, Regulatory Affairs (Human Factors)</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<p>West Pharmaceutical Services, Inc. is a leading manufacturer of packaging components and delivery systems for injectable drugs and healthcare products. Working by the side of its customers from concept to patient, West creates products that promote the efficiency, reliability and safety of the world's pharmaceutical drug supply. West is headquartered in Exton, Pennsylvania, and supports its customers from locations in North and South America, Europe, Asia and Australia. West's 2017 sales of $1.6 billion reflect the daily use of approximately 112 million of its components and devices, which are designed to improve the delivery of healthcare to patients around the world.<br>
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>Job Summary:
</b></p>
<p>In this role, you will be responsible for applying human factors and usability engineering methods throughout the design and development processes for West’s Products, coordinating regulatory submissions for West’s products and processes support West customers regulatory activities and maintain knowledge of current US and international regulations/guidelines/policies applicable to West’s products and services.<br>
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>Essential Duties and Responsibilities:
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Develop and complete Human Factors and Usability Engineering Process activities with guidance for West products, including HF studies (formative, summative), and related documentation according to FDA guidelines and applicable standards.
</li>
<li>Be the users and patient champion and voice in guiding the product requirements and user interface development. Understand and develop user’s needs, provide human factors technical expertise and guidance for concepts and design.
</li>
<li>Complete regulatory assignments with guidance in support of R&amp;D and commercial programs with a focus on authoring, maintenance and tracking of CMC dossiers for submission to Health Authorities [DMFs, MAFs, 510(k)’s], customer requests, etc. in compliance with regulations, guidelines and procedures.
</li>
<li>Support project development teams and communicate regulatory progress, decisions, strategies, etc.
</li>
<li>Support DVT strategy and related documents review.
</li>
<li>Support pre-clinical studies and international clinical studies.
</li>
<li>Review assigned technical project documentation, certifications, correspondences, and external communications, as needed.
</li>
<li>Provide assistance with customer regulatory requests, including regulatory letters, compliance questionnaires and LOAs.
</li>
<li>Develop specific objectives and manage work assignments, with minimal guidance, in an effort to achieve personal objectives in conformance with overall business goals.
</li>
<li>Maintain effectiveness when experiencing major changes in work responsibilities or environment; adjusting effectively to work within new work structures, processes, requirements, or cultures.
</li>
<li>Challenge processes remain open to ideas and changes to continuously improve and seek better alternatives and drive change.
</li>
<li>Adhere to all applicable government and West regulations, practices and procedures to maintain compliance.
</li>
<li>Clearly and effectively communicate team/project progress/status, decisions, timelines, etc.
</li>
<li>Maintain working knowledge of computer software packages including MS Word, MS Excel, MS Project, MS PowerPoint, Adobe, SharePoint and LOA request system, eCTD and others as required.
</li>
<li>Other duties as assigned
</li>
</ul>
<div><b>Basic Qualifications:
</b></div>
<ul>
<li>Education: Minimum of BS or BSE in Human Factors, Ergonomics, Industrial Engineering or extensive formal education in human factors related areas with bachelor’s degree in Science or related disciplines
</li>
<li>Experience: 3-5 years working in a human factors capacity within the medical device / pharmaceutical / healthcare sector specific related with medical device constituent of combination product, design control activities and human factors related activities
</li>
<li>Knowledge and experience with human factors related activities (such as HF studies protocols and reports (formative, summative)), related documentation according to FDA guidelines and applicable standards, such and task analysis, uFMEA, heuristic analysis is a plus<br>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>Preferred Knowledge, Skills and Abilities:
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Excellent interpersonal, communication and listening skills
</li>
<li>Be familiar with industry standards regarding human factors and usability (FDA, IEC 62366-1, HE75) in the medical device development process
</li>
<li>Able to understand and focus on empathy and users/patients experience
</li>
<li>Familiarity with FDA guidance documents and standards related to human factors, combination products, delivery system and design controls is a plus.
</li>
<li>Knowledge of medical device regulatory affairs and design control process, direct interactions with regulatory agencies and management of regulatory support systems is a plus.
</li>
<li>Demonstrated ability to manage multiple tasks/projects/priorities simultaneously is required.
</li>
<li>Able to balance theoretical tools and practical experimentation to develop concepts and ideas
</li>
<li>Able to be aware of all relevant SOPs as per Company policy as they are related to the position covered by this Job Description
</li>
<li>Able to comply with the company’s safety policy at all times
</li>
<li>Able to comply with the company’s quality policy at all times.
</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Travel Requirements:
</b></p>
<ul><li>Must be able to travel up to 10% of the time, with higher levels possible due to customer critical or business needs
</li></ul>
<p><b>Physical &amp; Mental Requirements:
</b></p>
<p>Sitting and/or standing for extended periods may occur as well as getting to and from offices and building sites. If specifically necessitated by customer requirements, must be able to see with or without corrective lenses and distinguish color differences for product and safety issues. Hearing ability should be sufficient to hear and react to audible alarms from distances up to 150 feet.<br>
