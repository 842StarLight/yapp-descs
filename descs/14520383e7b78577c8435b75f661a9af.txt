<h2>Onsite Pharmacy Auditor</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<p><b>This position requires heavy travel
</b></p>
<p></p>
<p>The Onsite Pharmacy Auditor monitors the contractual and regulatory obligations of Prime’s network pharmacies by traveling to pharmacy locations and performing standard audits. This position is responsible for educating Prime’s network pharmacies on contract and regulatory compliance procedures, assessing pharmacy compliance, identifying claim billing errors and remediating audit issues. The Onsite Pharmacy Auditor assists in the development and enhancement of audit tools and processes to effectively identify erroneous claims, non-compliant pharmacy practices or potential pharmacy reimbursements.
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>Responsibilities:
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Travel to participating Network Pharmacies to observe pharmacy practices and review documentation in accordance with department audit procedures
</li>
<li>Evaluate potential pharmacies to include in onsite trips considering such information as Profiler risk score, prior audit history, and/or referrals, and submit recommended audit plan to manager for approval; finalize onsite audit plans and schedule trips in accordance to Prime’s travel and expense policy
</li>
<li>Analyze individual pharmacy claims and conduct claims reviews while onsite at the pharmacy to ensure compliance with contractual and regulatory obligations; Identify and escalate potential non-compliant, suspicious, or fraudulent pharmacy activity management and/or Prime’s SIU for further investigation and review
</li>
<li>Educate external pharmacy staff on results of observations of pharmacy practices, proper submission of on-line transactions and the use of Prime Therapeutics’ Pharmacy Provider Manual at all stages of the audit process
</li>
<li>Represent Prime in a positive and professional manner during all pharmacy interactions and effectively de-escalate potentially confrontational situations
</li>
<li>Identify opportunities to improve onsite audit processes and drive efficiency and innovation; collaborate with department staff, the training and QA team, and department leadership to develop and implement new or enhanced practices
</li>
<li>Adhere to audit completion turnaround times, quality metrics, and other performance indicators established within Pharmacy Audit; Ensure travel arrangements and expenses are appropriately tracked and submitted in accordance with company policy
</li>
<li>Track audit activities to support data analysis and reporting efforts within the department
</li>
<li>Prepare initial audit reports to be sent to the network pharmacies
</li>
<li>Other duties as assigned
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>Required Minimum Qualifications:
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>High school diploma from an accredited school or equivalent GED
</li>
<li>Current Registration with State Board of Pharmacy in the state where position is located
</li>
<li>National Pharmacy Technician Certification through PTCB or ExCPT (CPhT)
</li>
<li>2 years experience in retail, long term care, specialty, home infusion, or mail order pharmacy or combined pharmacy and PBM experience
</li>
<li>1 year of previous experience in audit or quality assurance in a highly regulated environment
</li>
<li>Must be eligible to work in the United States without need for work visa or residency sponsorship
</li>
</ul>
