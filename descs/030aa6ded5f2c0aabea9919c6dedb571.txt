<h2>Admissions Coordinator Holy Cross</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>Under the direction of the Director of Marketing, Sales, and Communication, in cooperation and collaboration with the Marketing and Sales Team, the Move-In Coordinator/Marketing Assistant will provide.</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>
Disclaimer: The following statements are intended to describe the general nature and level of work assigned to this position. They are not intended to be construed as an exhaustive list of all responsibilities, duties and skills required of the individual in this position. Other duties may be assigned as deemed necessary or appropriate by management</b>.</p>
<p></p>
<p><b><i>
ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS</i></b></p>
<p>
Know, understand, incorporate, and demonstrate the Mission, Vision, Values and Guiding Behaviors of Trinity Health and Trinity Senior Living Communities in behaviors, practices, and decisions. Consistently demonstrate (leads by example) Sanctuary Values to all internal and external customers (residents, visitors, volunteers, and associates.) Create and maintain an atmosphere of warmth, propagating a calm environment throughout the community. Actively participates / intervenes with direct care as needed and as a role model in ongoing Sanctuary Training of personnel. Execute assignments in a culture that is shared and collaborative across all divisions within TSLC. Manages lists for residents and future residents including management of all related files. Be the primary resource for implementation of the REPS system for code status change, deposit date, etc. Constantly communicates with the depositors to lower their stress and anxiety about moving. Assists new depositors to ensure the timely completion of all move-in paperwork and payment of all fees, including applications, medical and financial statements, reservations agreements, and medical assessment. Identifies partners to work with depositors as they go through the process of getting ready to move: real estate agents, movers, estate sale companies, planners and packers. Manages Move-in Program. Meets with the depositors to assist them with the furniture placement/space planning of their new residence. Walks through residence and develops a punch list of any items to be repaired/replaced/cleaned prior to resident move-in. Coordinates with Housekeeping and Maintenance to ensure completions of punch list items prior to resident move in. Guides new residents through all additional move-in procedures. Schedules the actual move-ins into the new residences. Maintain inventory list and attends meetings to insure preparation is in place for move in. Communicates to staff the arrival of new residents through established channels. Orientation of new residents, including independent and assisted living. Arranges, maintains and delivers welcome packets/program. Manages storage bin and garage assignments along with programing new fabs/ pendants/ keys and ordering dinner name plates. Signs leases with new residents. Assists residents wishing to transfer within the community including apartment selection, lease signing, required paperwork, and welcoming packets/program. Manage reservations for Sales Events. Assist the Marketing Director with depositor retention activities. Help plan, coordinate logistics and set-up for all Sales Events and Marketing Meetings. Report statistical information regarding the marketing activities for preparation of weekly and monthly reports by the Sales team. Thoroughly familiar with and assists in implementation of all elements of the Marketing Plan. Assists with direct mail, e-blasts and e-mail campaigns. Assists with the management of the marketing calendar and appointment schedule for the Sales team. Assist with making decorating choices in model apartment. General receptionist responsibilities such as answering phone, greeting of guests, and offering refreshments or accommodations of guests. General administrative responsibilities in the management of office activities, including operating all business systems, phones, hospitality items, and ordering of needed supplies. Practices good stewardship with time and materials and other resources available. Maintain a working knowledge of applicable Federal, State, and local laws and regulations, TSLC Corporate Integrity Program, Code of Ethics, as well as other policies and procedures in order to ensure adherence in a manner that reflects honest, ethical and professional behavior. Maintain Do Not Contact/ Bad Address list from returned mailings. Assist with advertising of events by creating/ distributing flyers to in-house residents and creating mailing labels for calendars that are mailed.</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>
 </b><b><i>MINIMUM QUALIFICATIONS</i></b></p>
<p>
High School diploma or equivalent. 3-5 years of work experience in similar settings, office management and support. Must be a team player who is self-directed in a fast paced environment and to seek out and accomplish tasks. Able to handle multiple projects simultaneously. Experience working with REPS database. Team player with leadership skills. Able to build relationships using personal consultative skills. Takes responsibility of own knowledge level and training to accomplish the job. Previous experience working in long-term care or with the geriatric population preferred. Must lead by example and possess superior customer service skills and professionalism Flexibility to adapt to ongoing change and work in a fast-paced, customer driven environment. Interpersonal skills to drive collaboration, commitment and productivity when working with cross-functional teams, customers and end users. Self-starter, highly motivated with high energy level. Demonstrates superior written and verbal communication and presentation skills. Intermediate proficiency with Microsoft product suite (MS Word, Excel, Power Point, etc.); basic knowledge and experience with electronic mail and calendaring system in PC LAN environment. Ability to type with speed and accuracy. Possesses a high degree of personal accountability, responsibility and independent decision making abilities with the skills to plan, organize, develop, implement and interpret programs, goals, objectives, policies and procedures of the organization. Position requires minimal travel (less than 10%) within the Community’s geographic region and to home office in Livonia, MI.</p>
