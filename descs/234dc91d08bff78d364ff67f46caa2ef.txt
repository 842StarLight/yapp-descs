<p><b>Position Description: Program Director PD</b></p>
<p>
Arch Systems Inc. Arch, is a CMS award winning contractor, and seeks a Program Director PD for the Research, Measurement, Assessment, Design and Analysis RMADA 2 indefinite delivery/indefinite quantity IDIQ contract, for the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, Centers for Medicare &amp; Medicaid Services. Candidate should have a minimum of 10 years of OCIO experience and working knowledge of the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Innovation CMMI Center models. Previous experience with RMADA IDIQ task orders is highly desired. PMP certification is required.
</p>
<p>S/he shall be capable of effectively communicating with individuals representing various professional disciplines, stakeholders including but not limited to healthcare consumers and service providers and providing technical assistance, as required, at every stage of project and model development. S/he will be responsible for overseeing the contract, it's management and task order delivery.</p>
<p><b>
Responsibilities:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Understands and represents Arch's mission, vision, and values to all internal and external customers</li>
<li>
Engage clients in appropriate communication that manages client expectations and builds a collaborative relationship with the client</li>
<li>
Interact with government and private sector clients, partners, and Arch staff in a professional and accountable manner, and as a representative of Arch management</li>
<li>
Contribute to business development efforts for new sales and marketing to promote the business, including responses to requests for quotes, sources sought, proposals, and/or presentations as request</li>
<li>
Partner with IT and Business to ensure operations and services are implemented and performed in accordance with contractual Statement of Work SOW, budget, and technical proposed solutions, and approved by executive management</li>
<li>
Direct project team members in order to successfully fulfill contract obligations with the highest quality and in a timely manner</li>
<li>
Develop and maintain project plans to include Level of Effort LOE</li>
<li>
Report project progress on a routine basis to Arch management - provide budget analysis and advise management of areas of concern, give recommendations for improvement of such areas, and implement such changes as necessary to ensure delivery of services on-time and within budget
</li>
<li>Ensure IDIQ and task order requirements are met including quality of product and services, cost control, timeliness and business/stakeholder relations</li>
<li>
Ensure an adequate quality assurance program and process are in place and strictly adhered to for all products and services</li>
<li>
Submit timely and accurately monthly cost reports, including a plan to correct any cost variances or projected rate adjustments</li>
<li>
Develop and prepare agendas, presentations and facilitate meetings for project-based work and / or Arch corporate work, as required by contracts and Arch executive management</li>
<li>
Review and approve invoices, timesheets, accounting and billing to client agencies in accordance with Arch policies and at the request of the Arch CFO</li>
<li>
Interview and recommend hiring of staff members for project and task order assignments and contribute to performance evaluations and assessments as required</li>
<li>
Perform other duties as requested</li>
</ul>

<li>
Experience in CMMI models, CMS policy and regulations, healthcare systems, technical assistance, analysis of large health care data sets, and managing effective business/stakeholder communications is required</li>
<li>
Consulting work history as a successful Project Director in healthcare quality related field; PMP certification required.</li>
<li>
Knowledge of health care and health policy expertise</li>
<li>
Maintains professional certification with continuing education credits</li>

Must have successful experience managing government contract</li>

Must be a "doer" who thrives on getting things done</li>
