<h2>SBA Business Development Officer</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>JOB PURPOSE<br>
Generate new SBA loans through existing relationships with business professionals, outside marketing efforts and other business referral sources. Maintains a quality network of business relationships that serve as recurring sources of referrals for new SBA lending opportunities and growth for the bank that are primarily self-generated.<br>
<br>
ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES<br>
<ul>
<li>Perform marketing efforts to include outside direct calls, group presentations, direct mail campaigns as appropriate to achieve production goals.</li>
<li>Perform site visits on properties representing new / loan requests as well as follow up on existing borrower properties.</li>
<li>Responsible for accurate and complete presentation of financial and background data on loan requests.</li>
<li>Oversee real estate market trends, prices, and lease rates in order to competently present loan for approval and to keep Bank advised of economic impacts.</li>
<li>Partner in the servicing and collection of loans in markets of responsibility as required by the Bank and SBA.</li>
<li>Monitor progress of construction projects which were generated.</li>
<li>Maintain an expert working knowledge of all SBA policies and SOP guidelines regarding eligibility.</li>
<li>Demonstrates compliance with all bank regulations for assigned job function and applies to designated job responsibilities – knowledge may be gained through coursework and on-the-job training. Keeps up to date on regulation changes.
</li>
<li>Follows all Bank policies and procedures, compliance regulations, and completes all required annual or job-specific training.
</li>
<li>Maintain a working knowledge of Bank's written policies and procedures regarding</li>
</ul>
Bank Secrecy Act, Regulation CC, Regulation E, Bank Security and other regulations as applicable to this job description.
<br>
<ul>
<li>May be asked to coach, mentor, or train others and teach coursework as subject matter expert.
</li>
<li>Actively learns, demonstrates, and fosters the Umpqua corporate culture in all actions and words.
</li>
</ul>• Takes personal initiative and is a positive example for others to emulate. • Embraces our vision to become "The World's Greatest Bank." • May perform other duties as assigned.<br>
<br>
SUPERVISORY RESPONSIBILITIES None<br>
<br>
REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES<br>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor’s degree in the field of business, accounting, or finance or equivalent work experience.</li>
<li>8+ year’s prior work experience as a SBA Business Development Officer with a proven successful sales track record.
</li>
<li>Ability to calculate figures and amounts such as discounts, interest, commissions, proportions, percentages.</li>
<li>Ability to define problems, collect data, establish facts, and draw valid conclusions.</li>
<li>Ability to interpret an extensive variety of technical instructions in mathematical or diagram form and deal with several abstract and concrete variables.</li>
<li>Demonstrated ability to develop new and sustainable referral sources and generate leads.</li>
<li>Excellent written and verbal communication.
</li>
<li>Ability to organize and prioritize, manage and perform multiple tasks.
</li>
<li>Detail-oriented and able to meet strict deadlines.
</li>
<li>Ability to work effectively with individuals and groups across the company to manage internal and external customer relationships.
</li>
<li>Proficiency with personal computers and related software packages such as Word and Excel.
</li>
</ul>Individuals and entities suspended, debarred, revoked, or otherwise excluded under the<br>
SBA or Government-wide-debarment regulations are not permitted to conduct business with SBA, including participating in an SBA –guaranteed loan. Umpqua Bank is responsible for consulting the System for Awards Management’s (SAM) Excluded Parties List System (EPLS) or any successor system to determine if an employee or an Agent has been debarred, suspended or otherwise excluded by SBA or other federal agency.<br>
