It is defined by what we do, how we do it and how others see us. It is a culture of ethics and integrity.</p>
<p><b>
Job Summary:</b> As the Product Compliance Analyst, you serve as a primary contact for the development of new company policies and forms, compliance review and guidance, including guidance relating to products, advertising and marketing materials and initiatives, state filing of products and forms with state regulators through the System for Electronic Rates and Forms Filings (SERFF), and the processes for gaining expeditious regulatory approval and implementation of products and forms, as assigned.</p>
<p><b><i>
You will serve as a primary contact for the development of new company policies and forms, compliance review and guidance, state filing of products and forms with state regulators through the System for Electronic Rates and Forms Filings (SERFF), and the processes for gaining expeditious regulatory approval and implementation of products and forms. In this role, you will serve as a liaison for the organization and cultivate relationships with state regulators as well as key contacts at the state.</i></b></p>
<p>
Your Impact:</p>
<ul>
<li>
Independently files policies with state regulators and secures approval of all individual and group products and other regulatory forms such as applications, consumer guides, advertising and marketing materials.</li>
<li>
Serves in a lead role in filings by compiling all required information and disseminating necessary information to others; drafts policies and administrative forms under limited direction.</li>
<li>
Serves as a resource to operational areas such as marketing and information technology, as well and product development groups on compliance issues. Monitors in-force products for compliance and provide guidance related to these products.</li>
<li>
Supports state regulatory requirements such as company reporting and responses to surveys and other insurance department communications.</li>
<li>
Monitors and reviews state and federal legislative developments and makes necessary recommendations.</li>
<li>
Recommend standards, processes and procedures that enable enterprise to comply with regulatory requirements at the federal and state level.</li>
<li>
Develops and maintains relationships with key partners in the enterprise; including Legal, Marketing and Information Technology.</li>
<li>
Establish and nurture contacts with state regulators as well as key contacts at the state.</li>
<li>
Applies knowledge of area of business supported (i.e. marketing/sales, product drafting, etc.) to make recommendations on standards and group processes.</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<br>
<p><i>
May perform additional duties as assigned.</i></p>

<p>
Requirements:</p>
<ul>
<li>
Bachelor’s degree, in a related discipline, or equivalent</li>
<li>
Typically has a minimum of five years of experience in a state filing compliance function with the insurance industry.</li>
<li>
FLMI, AIRC designation and/or other compliance designations preferred.</li>
<li>
Solid knowledge of state filing compliance within the insurance industry.</li>
<li>
Knowledge of the regulatory and legal framework for insurance products.</li>
<li>
Strong decision-making, task management, and priority management skills.</li>
<li>
Knowledge of SERFF filing system.</li>
<li>
Good knowledge of Microsoft Office Suite and other related business software.</li>
</ul>
