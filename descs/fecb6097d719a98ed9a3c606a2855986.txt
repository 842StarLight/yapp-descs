<h2>Arcade & Cinema Technician</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div>
<p>The Arcade &amp; Cinema Technician is responsible for all the operations of the arcade. The job duties are to oversee, and maintaining the FatCat's standard of operation efficiently and safely and by helping manage operations, including part inventory, repairs, expenses, providing preventative maintenance, ordering redemption, training, and ensuring that the arcade is running smoothly. In addition, the Arcade &amp; Cinema Technician will also play an essential role as the cinema projectionist being responsible for ensuring that all video, film, and audio equipment functions efficiently and meets high quality standard in proper presentation of all screenings and special events. This position will also assist in a minor role with IT support in Windows environments, networking concepts, and working with software and hardware such as video and audio equipment. Minimal travel will be required with notice.</p>

<p><b>
Essential Functions:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Ensures that arcade games are being serviced and maintained.
</li>
<li>Responds to customer complaints of machine malfunction; analyzes the mechanism to find the root of the problem.
</li>
<li>Manages the installation, maintenance, and repairs, both mechanical and circuitry issues in arcade game machines.
</li>
<li>Keeps a clean and organized workshop.
</li>
<li>Ability to conduct basic electronic troubleshooting.
</li>
<li>Aid supporting the point of sale software, VOIP phones, and merchant processing hardware.</li>
<li>
Collaborate with IT Department in supporting network hardware and servers.</li>
<li>
Manages the transporting of machines and ensures machines are assembled with proper equipment, electrical parts, hand tools, and soldering irons.
</li>
<li>Minimal on call support responsibility.
</li>
<li>Must be able to take and follow direction from operations management.
</li>
<li>Ability to work in an environment where there is a population of children.
</li>
<li>Maintain all hardware elements of theater projectors, audio, and video systems of the center.</li>
<li>
Ingest and build film playlists including films, pre-show content, in-house advertising, trailers, movie licenses, and features prior to opening presentation.</li>
<li>
Provide expert knowledge in the set up and operation of Digital Cinema projections, HDTV and video projectors for studio movie previews, premieres and other screenings of movie content.</li>
<li>
Operation motion picture projection equipment to facilitate exhibition on 5-8 screens, 25 to 35 shows per day.
</li>
<li>Scheduling of automated playback and attention to quality control a must.
</li>
<li>Daily maintenance of projection equipment.</li>
<li>
Responsible for every aspect of theatre operation included but not limited to digital projection, maintenance, box office, etc.</li>
<li>
Ability and willingness to perform all floor staff and janitorial duties.</li>
<li>
Maintains facilities that are clean, safe and in good repair.</li>
<li>
Performs other maintenance and repair tasks.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>
Qualifications:
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Must be 18 or older.</li>
<li>
High School diploma and some technical training.</li>
<li>
Minimum one year of technical experience or equivalent training through classwork or previous employment.</li>
<li>
Travel is limited but must be willing and able to do so with notice.
</li>
<li>Good problem-solving and diagnosing skills.</li>
<li>
Basic understanding of configuring and troubleshooting TCP/IP Networking.</li>
<li>
Windows 10 experience with user accounts and permissions helpful.</li>
<li>
Screen sharing support experience.</li>
</ul>
