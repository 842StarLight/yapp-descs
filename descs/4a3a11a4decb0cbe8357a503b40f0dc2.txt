<div><b>
Description</b></div>
<p>The Engineering Manager leads a team of 5-10 engineers to plan and execute the development of new tool programs. This role is responsible for the ideation, design, and development of new products and components from concept to production. The role is also responsible for maintenance and improvement of existing products.</p>
<p></p>
<br>
<p><b>
Organizational Responsibilities</b></p>
<p>
This position reports to the Director of Engineering &amp; Innovation. The position works closely with engineers, drafters, and technicians as well as other stakeholders across the business including product management, sourcing, operations, marketing, and outside vendors.</p>
<p></p>
<br>
<div><b>Responsibilities</b></div>
<div><ul>
<li>
Lead the design and development of new product programs for cable termination and low-pressure hydraulics. Also responsible for sustaining support in these categories.</li>
<li>
Serve as the lead engineer and Greenlee subject matter expert for hydraulic systems</li>
<li>
Develop a deep understanding of customer needs and applications in the target categories. Lead the translation of customer needs into functional requirements and design concepts.</li>
<li>
Identify technology gaps and develop a roadmap that supports a 5-year vision for the target categories</li>
<li>
Organize engineering tasks to meet completion dates and technical specifications</li>
<li>
Work closely with all stakeholders to achieve program objectives on time and within budget</li>
<li>
Hire, train, and supervise group members; actively develop direct reports</li>
<li>
Develop and oversee project timelines and budgets</li>
<li>
Ensure proper staffing on new and existing programs and work to improve resource efficiency</li>
<li>
Oversee laboratory testing and verification in the target categories</li>
<li>
Evaluate progress on new product programs and react as required to ensure successful execution</li>
<li>
Proactively improve new product development processes</li>
<li>
Perform all duties within Emerson’s Code of Conduct</li>
</ul></div>
<br>
</div>
<div></div>
<br>
<div>
<div><b>Requirements</b></div>
<div><ul>
<li>
A Bachelor of Science degree in mechanical engineering, mechanical engineering technology, or related discipline is <b>required</b>. MS or MBA preferred.</li>
<li>
Experience in mechanical engineering or a related environment is <b>mandatory</b> for this position.</li>
<li>
This position requires the ability to guide the design and validation of multiple, concurrent new product programs. Products may involve complex assemblies and/or a wide variety of part types and manufacturing methods, including castings, machining, weldments, and injection molded plastics.</li>
<li>
3+ years of experience in the design and development of hydraulic systems is required</li>
<li>
Preference will be given to candidates with prior hands-on experience in machine design encompassing electromechanical systems</li>
<li>
Experience with SolidWorks or similar CAD software and associated analysis tools</li>
<li>
Solid interpersonal and communication skills are required. Must be able to clearly communicate with internal stakeholders, customers, and suppliers orally and in writing.</li>
<li>
Prior experience in technical project management against established deadlines</li>
<li>
Manufacturing or test lab experience is strongly preferred</li>
<li>
Prior supervisory experience is desirable</li>
<li>
5-10 years of experience in mechanical engineering</li>
<li>
The successful candidate will be able to demonstrate a sense of urgency and a passion for new product development. Some travel required, including internationally.</li>
</ul></div>
