<h2>Engineer-Controls</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>This position is located in Waukesha, WI.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Husco Automotive provides individuals the opportunity to pursue a career within a fast-paced, industry leading and entrepreneurial organization. The Controls Engineer is responsible for technical support for large scale projects – typically, new product lines, development and/or implementation of new manufacturing technology in support of new product lines or major improvements in existing product lines. The key to success in this position is problem solving and utilizing technical knowledge to get to the root cause of a problem rather than simply identifying symptoms. A successful Controls Engineer is self-motivated and a natural trouble-shooter who is able to react quickly under pressure, prioritize workload, and manage several tasks concurrently.<br>
<br>
<b>PERFORMANCE OBJECTIVES</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Design, develop, debug and deliver electrical controls hardware and software for the customers of the Automotive Division</li>
<li>Design, develop, debug, and deliver automation controls (PC, PLC, and Robot based)</li>
<li>Work as part of an execution team and assume complete responsibility of automation and/or system control function from start to finish of the project.</li>
<li>Function effectively in a team environment, hold process reviews, and training sessions as needed, and support sales quoting efforts as required.</li>
<li>Write specifications defining the functional scope of assembly and/or test machine requirements.</li>
<li>Specify electrical drawings to communicate desired process</li>
<li>Write code (PLC, VB, and LabVIEW) to execute process</li>
<li>Write equipment support manuals.</li>
<li>Support integration on manufacturing floor.</li>
<li>Support field installation, startup and debug of systems</li>
<li>Good written and verbal communication with internal and external customers.</li>
<li>Ability to work well with multiple tasks and under tight deadlines.</li>
<li>Able to work well with colleagues and produce results within a fast-paced and high pressure atmosphere. Combine strong attention to detail with an orientation towards results/execution.</li>
</ul>
<p><br>
<b>MINIMUM QUALIFICATIONS</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor’s degree in Electrical or Mechanical Engineering required.</li>
<li>Minimum of 4 years’ experience working in systems engineering, design, assembly, and/or material handling. Preferably related to robotic automation systems.</li>
<li>PLC programming experience is required. (Allen Bradley and/or Siemens preferred)</li>
<li>Robot integration code experience.</li>
<li>LabView experience preferred.</li>
<li>HMI programming experience, VB program knowledge needed</li>
<li>Ability to travel up to 35%</li>
<li>Understanding of OSHA safety standards required for automated assembly and test equipment.</li>
<li>Ability to read electrical, mechanical, and pneumatic prints.</li>
<li>Must have good electrical wiring and debugging experience.</li>
<li>Effective problem solving, judgment, and independent thinking skills for troubleshooting, repair, and recognition of acceptable or unacceptable product quality.</li>
<li>Able to handle frequent interruptions, meet tight deadlines, and handle conflict in an effective manner.</li>
<li>Experience with Microsoft Office products.</li>
<li>(Authorized to work in the United States.)</li>
</ul>
<p><br>
<b>PHYSICAL REQUIREMENTS</b></p>
<ul>
<li>In a standard workday, this person will be required to sit 50%, stand 20%, walk 20%, squat or kneel, twist, and drive/travel 10%.</li>
<li>Will lift from 1 to 35 pounds. Lifting may include miscellaneous materials if required.</li>
<li>Fine manipulation is needed for PC work and use of other office equipment.</li>
<li>Visual requirements include ability to read and follow directions.</li>
<li>This person may come in contact with office hazards. Temperature and humidity are normal for this position and there are no unusual atmospheric conditions. Will be required to wear personal protective equipment in posted areas of the plant, including safety glasses, ear plugs, and safety shoes.</li>
<li>Total awareness of the plant environment as it pertains to the safety of other employees, operation of equipment, handling of equipment/materials, and visitors required.</li>
</ul>
