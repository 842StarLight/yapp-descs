<h2>Global Financial Crimes Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><b>Job Description:</b></p>
<p></p>
<div>
The Global Financial Crimes (“GFC”) Manager executes substantive money laundering, economic sanctions and fraud compliance and operational risk practices relevant to the manager's specific area of responsibility. The Global Financial Crimes (“GFC”) and Manager is accountable for the requirements of the Global Compliance and Financial Crimes Enterprise Policies, Compliance Operational Risk Management Program (“CORM Program”),the Global Compliance Enterprise Policy (“GC Policy”), the Operational Risk Management – Enterprise Policy (“ORM Policy”) and the Enterprise Fraud Risk Management Policy and works directly or through compliance officers for the Front Line Units (“FLUs”) and Control Functions (“CFs”) to complete those requirements, engaging in the following activities:<br>
<br>
<ul>
<li>Create and maintain a regulatory inventory, communicate regulatory changes to and engage the FLU/CF through the compliance and operational risk officers in assessing impacts of regulatory changes for Financial Crimes.</li>
<li>Develop and maintain relevant compliance and operational risk policies to ensure they reflect regulatory requirements for Financial Crimes.</li>
<li>Conduct risk assessments, scenario analysis, and ops loss analysis related to Financial Crimes.</li>
<li>Apply judgment and experience to determine monitoring and testing coverage plans and related metrics for Financial Crimes.</li>
<li>Contribute to the identification, collection, validation and recording of internal loss events and “near misses”. Contribute to root cause analyses on internal loss events and develop remediation plans to strengthen controls.</li>
<li>Contribute to identification, review and documentation of relevant external loss events.</li>
<li>Monitor and business processes, controls, and metrics, open issues and control enhancements to drive remediation of control weaknesses by the FLU/CF</li>
<li>Test the effectiveness of the front line unit and control function compliance and operational risk controls for Financial Crimes.</li>
<li>Identify aggregate, report and escalate compliance and operational risks, issues and control enhancements for Financial Crimes and ensure the compliance and operational risk officers for the FLU/CF are aware of issues.</li>
<li>Executes governance and management routines.</li>
<li>Identify compliance and operational risk training needs, provide subject matter expertise to support development of training curriculum, and inspect FLU/CF and third party participation for Financial Crimes.</li>
<li>Advise compliance and operational risk peers and business leaders in preparations for and participate in regulatory exams and audits for Financial Crimes.</li>
<li>Inspect that gap closure plans and commitments made regarding actions in response to Matters Requiring Attention (“MRAs”) and other actions related to Financial Crimes are completed.</li>
<li>Escalate regulatory relations concerns to Regulatory Relations.</li>
<li>Ensure Compliance and Operational Risk “owned” issues (i.e., Internal Audit, Regulator and Self Identified issues) are addressed appropriately and timely.</li>
<li>Supports the GFCC executive in serving as the Global Compliance and Operational Risk point of contact for the Company with regulatory supervisors, law enforcement and other government agencies on matters relating to Financial Crimes.</li>
<li>Partners in the filing of all regulatory reports for the Company related to Financial Crimes, as requested by the GFCC executive.</li>
</ul>
<br>
This role is also responsible for ensuring consistent quality of activities, processes and outputs by establishing effective review practices across teams or processes. This role may contribute as a manager responsible for providing leadership direction to attract, assess, develop, motivate and retain a team or may act as an individual contributor. This role may have Money Laundering Reporting Officer responsibilities.</div>
<p></p>
<p>
Fixed Income Institutional Trading Financial Crimes Manager</p>
<ul>
<li>
The Global Financial Crimes (GFC) Manager will drive execution of compliance and operational risk processes used to identify and mitigate risks associated with anti-money laundering (AML), counter terrorist financing, economic sanctions, and fraud for the Institutional Fixed Income, Currencies and Commodities (FICC) \businesses</li>
<li>
Role will partner with business, operations and other financial crimes teams to analyze business processes, internal controls, client behavior and transaction activity to identify risks and trends, control deficiencies and potentially suspicious behavior.</li>
<li>
Candidate should be a FICC subject matter expert with a demonstrated understanding of money laundering and market abuse risks in an institutional trading business</li>
<li>
 Role will be responsible for driving and maturing execution of the Financial Crimes compliance and operational risk program in FICC, with reporting to the Financial Crimes Executive for Global Markets.</li>
<li>
This role includes, but is not limited to, monitoring and testing, issue management; and risk assessment activities used to identify deficiencies and oversee improvements in the control environment.</li>
<li>
Candidate must be comfortable facing off with business and technology leaders in the FICC business to drive education and foster adherence to internal requirements, as well as compliance with external laws, rules, and regulatory guidance for financial crimes.</li>
</ul>
