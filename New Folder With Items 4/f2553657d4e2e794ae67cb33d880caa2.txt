<div><b>Description of Job</b></div>
<div>
<p>This position works for the Right of Way Unit in Region 4 and serves as the designated expert in property appraisal, including performing appraisals, performing review appraisals, contracting for appraisal services, providing valuation support for appraisal waivers and litigation, and serving as a resource for guidance and oversight on appraisal matters.<br>
<br>
<b>It is expected that this position may exist through December 2024. The position will be abolished and the employee separated from CDOT when funding ceases. </b><b>The employee in this position may apply for other similar positions when they near the ending of this project in 2024.</b><br>
<br>
<b>Major duties and responsibilities</b> include, but are not limited to:<br>
<b>Appraisals</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Perform appraisals and prepare appraisal reports to be used for the acquisition of real estate interests This, under the exercise of the power of eminent domain, required for transportation related projects;
</li>
<li>Perform appraisals and prepare appraisal reports to be used for the sale, exchange or lease of CDOT owned properties;
</li>
<li>Initiate in-depth research studies by collecting, verifying, and reporting data deemed necessary for the valuation of subject properties;
</li>
<li>Perform highest and best use analyses for all valuation assignments which form the basis for selection of comparable properties in the cost, sales comparison and income approaches to value;
</li>
<li>Physically inspect and analyze all appraisal projects including rural, agricultural, mountain, suburban, industrial, publicly-owned, and commercial properties;</li>
</ul>
<b>
Appraisal review services</b><ul>
<li>Review contract fee appraisals originally negotiated and contracted under the Purchase Order contracting process;
</li>
<li>Review all submitted property owner appraisals for proposed project acquisitions and all Local Public Agency submitted appraisals, completing and signing fair market value and appraisal review reports;
</li>
<li>Monitor documents for compliance with CDOT, State of Colorado and Federal requirements, including physical inspection of projects, subjects and all comparables in all areas, rural and urban;
</li>
<li>Produce written reports describing the reviewer's conclusions regarding the appraisal report's level of adequacy of the appraisal process, the reasonableness of the conclusions reached, and the compliance levels required;</li>
</ul>
<b>
Appraisal Consultant Management</b><ul>
<li>Physically inspect and analyze all properties in each transportation projects; Employ methods, rules, regulations, and negotiation techniques to secure required Purchase Order agreements with independent fee appraisal contractors;
</li>
<li>Coordinate with CDOT purchasing unit to create and finalize all contracting documentation;
</li>
<li>Physically inspect and show properties/projects and explain potential valuation problems to contract fee appraisers;
</li>
<li>Negotiate final fees for contract appraisals on projects;
</li>
<li>Complete consultant evaluation forms for contract appraisal services;
</li>
<li>Appraisal advise and support for the Regional offices and internal and external customers;</li>
</ul>
<b>
Regional expert on appraisal and appraisal review functions, quality assurance, training, developing and coordinating policy and procedures with Region personnel</b><ul>
<li>Serve as consultant and adviser to the Region office and Attorney General's Office on property valuation issues;
</li>
<li>Litigation support where issue of just compensation is resolved in condemnation actions;
</li>
<li>Attend and provide expert witness testimony at depositions and valuation trials;
</li>
<li>Other job duties as assigned.</li>
</ul>
