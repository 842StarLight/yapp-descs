<p>
The District Director of Stores (DDOS) is responsible for creating partnerships to achieve business initiatives while acting as One Team; planning and making informed decisions; exhibiting effective leadership skills that encourage living our Purpose and Values; assessing store performance with the goal of increasing sales; and managing and controlling the district budget.</p>
</div>
<p><b>Requirements:</b></p>
<div>
<p><b><i>Business Partnering:</i></b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Create and maintain business partnerships with Store Management and Store Team, Regional Vice President of Stores, Buyers, Planners, Human Resources, and Asset Protection.
</li>
<li>Prioritize and schedule store visits to achieve business results.</li>
<li>
Listen to associate and customer issues and effectively resolve disputes with the help of business partners.</li>
<li>
Share all information that impacts the work with your business partners.</li>
<li>
Partner with Human Resources to strengthen hiring best practices, performance management, succession planning, and workplace culture.
</li>
<li>Partner with Buyers and Planners to address profit, assets, and merchandise opportunities.
</li>
<li>Partner with Asset Protection to address shrink.</li>
<li>
Compile monthly overview notes from stores and prepare a synopsis quarterly report for Regional Vice President of Store Operations.</li>
<li>
Prepare reports as required.</li>
</ul>
<p><b><i>
Leadership:</i></b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Develop Store Management by rewarding and recognizing behaviors that develop strong talent and drive results that contribute to the Company’s success.</li>
<li>
Coach store management to exemplify behaviors that inspire customers and associate loyalty.</li>
<li>
Establish and foster an optimistic, inclusive, and collaborative work environment that encourages creativity and innovation.</li>
<li>
Maintain and establish high performance standards for stores in the district.</li>
<li>
Provide information and reinforce the use of all Stein Mart Development Programs and job specific training programs. It is the responsibility of the District Director of Store to ensure these programs are properly utilized.</li>
<li>
Give performance feedback that is timely, actionable, and specific.</li>
<li>
Foster a culture of providing valuable feedback through the Quarterly Performance Check-In process for Store Managers</li>
<li>
Approve annual performance evaluations for non-management associates and salary increases for Assistant Managers.</li>
<li>
Give performance improvement plans when necessary.</li>
<li>
Create Store Management succession plans that you share with Regional Vice President.</li>
<li>
Develop and track progress of Store Managers, Assistant Store Managers, and Merchandise Managers; building the districts’ internal talent pool to create career opportunities for associates and build bench strength for store leadership.
</li>
<li>Develop a district A-Team that includes a full roster with back-ups.</li>
<li>
Effectively recruit and select Management Staff.</li>
<li>
Work with Store Managers and Assistant Managers to develop and evaluate recruiting plans that serve each store’s unique market.</li>
<li>
Actively and consistently recruit Store and Assistant Store Manager candidates to meet staffing needs.</li>
<li>
Evaluate turnover rates and work to reduce turnover.</li>
<li>
Use the Behavioral Interviewing process and resources to conduct legal and effective interviews.</li>
<li>
Manage time efficiently to ensure deadlines are met; such as: new store openings, preparing for holiday seasons, inventory, and recruiting efforts.</li>
</ul>
<p><b><i>
Operational:</i></b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Ensure sales, credit, OPINC, expense control, and shrink goals are achieved and that the team understands their respective roles in achieving these goals as One Team.</li>
<li>
Analyze information (i.e. Performance Sales Reports and business development reports) to understand sales opportunities.</li>
<li>
Drive financial results by creating business plans and holding GM accountable to implement those plans.</li>
<li>
Manage human resources documentation and information reporting.</li>
<li>
Submit payroll/status changes to Corporate Human Resources in a timely manner.</li>
<li>
Review associate files periodically during store visits.</li>
<li>
Answer questions about benefits, payroll, and other employment issues.</li>
<li>
Assess store cash management.</li>
<li>
Review audits.</li>
<li>
Partner with Store Management and Asset Protection to identify and resolve potential loss/audit issues.</li>
<li>
Observe cashiers’ interactions with customers, use of POS, and front of store operations and give feedback at each visit.</li>
<li>
Partner with Store Management to review receiving process and scheduling to ensure product is received and on the floor within 24 hours of delivery.</li>
<li>
Review and respond to audits.</li>
</ul>
<p><b><i>
Merchandising:</i></b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Understand Stein Mart’s advertising philosophy, promotional strategies, merchandising guidelines and each store’s unique style.</li>
<li>
Give constructive feedback to optimize selling space and maximize selling potential of each store.</li>
<li>
Confirm that store merchandise is creatively placed for maximum appeal and work is completed to a set standard.</li>
<li>
Give feedback on overall floor maintenance, recovery and front of store presentation.</li>
<li>
Ensure customer merchandise needs and wants are met in a timely manner.
</li>
<li>Partner with Store Managers to develop and assess proficiency of the Assistant Managers for merchandise management and presentation.</li>
<li>
Review the store assessment process periodically to check that work is being performed accurately, efficiently and timely.</li>
<li>
Track and evaluate sales against business plans.</li>
<li>
Assess business by regularly walking the floor with Store Management Teams on each store visit and using the Penetration Reports, Store Visit Report and Business Development reports to determine sales strengths and opportunities for sales growth.</li>
<li>
Review sales figures and partner with Buyers and Planners to ensure that each store in the district has the correct merchandise to maximize sales.</li>
<li>
Engage in strategic planning with business partners to increase sales.</li>
</ul>
<p><b><i>
Customer Service:</i></b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Exhibit a positive and helpful attitude when speaking with customers, business partners and associates.</li>
<li>
Answer customer inquiries about products, services, store policies and complaints.</li>
<li>
Research each store market to develop a thorough understanding of the retail competition, customer base, demographics and employment rate.</li>
<li>
Partner with Store Management Teams and Corporate staff to develop strategic plans for servicing customers, enhancing Stein Mart’s reputation, and growing store business.</li>
</ul>

<p><b>Qualifications:</b></p>
<div>
<p><b><i>Education &amp; Experience:</i></b></p>
<ul>
<li>
High school graduate or equivalent (GED) required.
</li>
<li>Associate’s or Bachelor’s Degree preferred.
</li>
<li>5 to 7 years applicable retail work experience, including a minimum of 5 years management experience.
</li>
<li>Demonstrated expertise in merchandising skills, numeric reasoning to include proficiency in: adding, subtracting, multiplying, dividing, and calculating percentages.
</li>
<li>Proficient in business-related computer software in a Windows environment (i.e. Microsoft Windows, Word, Excel, Outlook, etc.) and ability to navigate the internet.</li>
</ul>
<p><b><i>
Behavioral:</i></b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Should exhibit a strong interest in working with people.
</li>
<li>Comfortable with change, enjoys variety in work and thrives on creating effective solutions.
</li>
<li>Demonstrated ability to assume a leadership role with supervisory capacity.
</li>
<li>Demonstrated ability to effectively communicate both verbally and in writing.
</li>
<li>Results oriented with a willingness to follow through, make informed decisions, complete tasks and problem-solve.
</li>
<li>Exhibited attention to detail with high degree of accuracy.</li>
</ul>
<p><b><i>
Physical:</i></b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Moderate physical effort required.
</li>
<li>Standing and walking will constitute 90% of a work shift.
</li>
<li>Stooping and lifting various merchandise contents of up to 20 lbs. or more on an as needed basis.</li>
</ul>
<p><b><i>
Travel:</i></b></p>
<ul><li>
Weekly travel throughout the district is required.</li></ul>
