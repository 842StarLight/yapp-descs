<b>ABOUT THE DEPARTMENT:</b><br>
The City of Houston Legal Department strives to provide the highest quality municipal legal services to the City, its elected and appointed officials, and its employees in the most efficient manner feasible through adherence to the following guiding principles:<br>
<br>
<ul>
<li>Quality &amp; Service – Achieving high customer satisfaction as judged by our clients</li>
<li>Partnership &amp; Teamwork – Working cooperatively internally and with our clients to achieve the City's goals</li>
<li>Integrity &amp; Candor – Acting with a commitment to honesty and ethical behavior</li>
<li>Dignity &amp; Diversity – Demonstrating esteem for the worth of each individual</li>
<li>Innovation &amp; Imagination – Seeking novel and creative approaches to achieving the City's objectives</li>
</ul>
<br>
Composed of over approximately 100 of Houston's finest attorneys across ten sections, the Legal Department has the pivotal role of providing litigation support, negotiating and drafting contracts, handling taxation matters, advising on personnel actions, dealing with the acquisition and sale of City property, overseeing City elections, drafting ordinances, advising on ethics and conflicts of interest, and dealing with neighborhood protection and quality of life issues. Every employee is committed to the Department's motto – "Service Through Law."<br>
<br>
<b>ESSENTIAL JOB FUNCTIONS</b><br>
The City of Houston Legal Department seeks a, experienced, thorough, detail-oriented title examiner who is self-motivated and can work independently on various transactional real estate assignments.<br>
<br>
<b>Responsibilities include, but are not limited to:</b>
</div>
<ul>
<li>Performing title searches to determine the City's property interest in certain property including fee, easement, and reversion.
</li>
<li>Researching the Real Property Records and other instruments to identify clouds on title, property ownership, and conveyance history, including reviewing plats, deeds, and other recorded and unrecorded instruments.
</li>
<li>Preparing title memos as needed.
</li>
<li>Examining lot and block subdivisions and acreage tracts.
</li>
<li>Reviewing various databases to ensure the proper execution of documents.
</li>
<li>Regularly communicating with various client departments to provide status updates and request additional information.
</li>
<li>Opening and closing files.
</li>
<li>Working in Word, Outlook, Excel, geographic and grantor-grantee indices, and other software programs.
</li>
<li>Familiarity with probate, family, and civil court documents.</li>
</ul>
<br>
