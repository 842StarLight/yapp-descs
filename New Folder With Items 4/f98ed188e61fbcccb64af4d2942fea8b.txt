<h2>Division Supply Chain Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><b>Parker Hannifin</b>
</p>Founded in 1917, Parker Hannifin Corporation is a $14.3 billion, global company. With annual sales of $14.3 billion in fiscal year 2018, Parker Hannifin is the world's leading diversified manufacturer of motion and control technologies and systems, providing precision-engineered solutions for a wide variety of mobile, industrial and aerospace markets. The company has operations in 50 countries around the world. Parker has increased its annual dividends paid to shareholders for 62 consecutive fiscal years, among the top five longest-running dividend-increase records in the S&amp;P 500 index. Parker’s engineering expertise and broad range of core technologies uniquely positions the company to solve some of the world’s greatest engineering challenges. By partnering with customers, Parker improves their productivity and profitability and seeks new ways to solve humanity's biggest challenges.<p><b>
Major Duties</b>
</p>This position will be under the Gas Turbine Filtration division of Parker Hannifin. The Division Supply Chain Manager will develop and manage the Supply Chain Team comprising of Customer Services including AFM Order Management, Scheduling and Procurement personnel to deliver the GTF Supply Chain strategy. The Division Supply Chain Managers’ (DSCM) role also includes management of the S&amp;OP &amp; PFEP Processes to support the manufacturing / processing cycle, and ensure timely logistical processing of product. The DSCM is responsible for driving lean enterprise systems throughout the division supply chain. SUPERVISION/INTERACTION The DSCM will directly report to the General Manager with functional accountability to the Group Supply Chain Manager. The DSCM will manage the complete material control process including procurement, production/inventory control, and transportation logistics. Will interact with all levels of division management including Operations, Sales, Engineering, Quality Control, Marketing and Finance; and will coordinate best practices with other DSCMs and the Group Supply Chain Manager. Key supplier and key customer contact essential.<p><b>
Responsibilities</b>
</p>• Develop and implement the GTF Supply Chain strategy in accordance with Parker Policy, the WIN Strategy and the GTF Strategy. • Manage the materials function within the division considering manufacturing capacity, schedules, workloads; perform make vs buy analysis/recommendations pertaining to in house manufacturing vs. outsourcing, quality control and workforce balance. • Establish and maintain Premier Customer service; and liaise with AFM Premier Customers on a regular basis to understand their evolving requirements. • Lead all Supply Chain Management activities within the division. This will include establishing preferred supplier selection criteria based on supplier evaluations of price, quality, delivery, certifications, compliance with governmental procurement regulations and group programs. Will establish and implement standard/automated re-order procedures. Will develop crisis management and supplier backup contingency programs; negotiates long term contracts and framework agreements in coordination with group staff and policies. • Responsible for continuous improvements programs with suppliers including management of plant variances directly affected by the supply chain such as Inventory, Freight and GRNI. • Responsible for managing the division operational inventory to ensure optimised stocking levels for immediate production items and forecasting for future requirements; and establishing and implementing standard/automated delivery procedures for just in time supplier stocking items. • Responsible for facilitation of the S&amp;OP Process. • Responsible for managing the division's overall strategic production planning practices taking into consideration short term/long term production requirements and integrating materials availability; will establish and implement procedures for special order request, engineering changes and part substitutions, and sales market demand for products. • Manage the division’s transportation logistics to ensure distribution of products direct to customers, or through distribution centers / warehouses are completed with optimum service at the most efficient cost. • Represent the supply chain at the division level. • Provides leadership by promoting team concepts, coaching, counselling, retaining, mentoring, training, talent management, and individual development; and drives a culture of accountability and results through strategy deployment and effective performance management.<p><b>
Qualifications</b>
</p>• 10-15 year’s managerial experience in supply chain management. • Degree in business, engineering, or materials management with certification in supply chain management preferred. • Experience in leading the supply chain function. • Must be an innovator and embrace lean enterprise principles and practices with practical experience in implementing lean technologies and practices. • Experience in developing and delivering strategic objectives. KEY COMPETENCIES • Excellent problem solving skills. • Possesses analytical and technical skills required to understand business practices and recommend proper automation opportunities. • Must have advanced computer skills and be able to understand electronic data processing. Systems (IT) experience beneficial. • Excellent written, verbal, and interpersonal, communication skills for effective interface with all internal and external contacts.<p><b>
