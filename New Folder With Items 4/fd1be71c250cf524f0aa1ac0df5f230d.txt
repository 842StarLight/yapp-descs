<h2>SALES ENGINEER</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p><b>Position Summary: </b></p>
<p>The Technical Promotions Engineer acts as a technical resource who develops relationships with and educates potential specifiers (private, DOTs etc.) in the benefits of concrete pipe and box to assist in obtaining sales.</p>
<p><b>Essential Duties / Responsibilities: </b></p>
<ul>
<li>Call on Engineers / Specifiers.</li>
<li>Develop a prioritized call list of engineers and agencies within the territory.</li>
<li>Develop relationship with DOT leadership, materials, bridge, construction, and hydraulics engineers to keep them educated on AASHTO and local issues.</li>
<li>Identify and rewrite key specifications within the territory that need modifications to make Rinker Materials' pipe and box products more competitive. This includes municipalities, counties, DOTs, and private engineers.</li>
<li>Obtain specification changes that favor concrete pipe and box.</li>
<li>Solicit upcoming project information from engineers while making calls.</li>
<li>Track # of projects; $ value of projects: and $ contribution of any specification changes achieved.</li>
<li>Communicate major project information with sales staff.</li>
<li>Conduct college engineering class presentations.</li>
<li>Conduct lunch and learn presentations.</li>
<li>Establish active direct mail marketing campaign to appropriate specifiers/ engineers/ contractors to address the constant barrage of alternate products literature and advertising.</li>
<li>Maintain active participation in appropriate Professional Associations including, but not limited to, the local Concrete Pipe Association.</li>
<li>Attend appropriate local trade shows and handle Rinker trade show booth when necessary.</li>
<li>Position requires excellent communication and organizational skills and attention to detail.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Qualifications: </b></p>
<ul>
<li>Education / Experience: B.S. or M.S., Civil Engineering; with prior experience in precast or prestressed concrete industry, preferred.</li>
<li>Structural engineering skills and P.E. a plus.</li>
</ul>
