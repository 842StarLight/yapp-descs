<h2>Freight Logistics and Customs Coordinator</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>American Vulkan Corporation, www.vulkan.com, is a dynamic and diverse manufacturing company in Central Florida, exporting and importing precision manufactured products worldwide. With a military and non-military customer base, our inventory spans from HVAC tube connectors weighing less than one ounce to mega-ship couplings weighing 30,000 pounds. American Vulkan is a leader in its field with state-of-the-art manufacturing equipment in a climate-controlled shop and warehouse. In addition to a competitive salary, we offer an exceptional comprehensive benefits package. EEO.</p>
<p>
If you are the right person for this opportunity, you will possess the following knowledge, experience, and skills:</p>
<ul>
<li>Calculate weight, volume, shipping costs or cost of goods to be moved.</li>
<li>Inform clients of factors such as shipping options, timelines, transfers, or regulations affecting shipments.</li>
<li>Prepare and verify shipping documentation, such as including bills of lading, packing lists, commercial and pro-forma invoices and Shipper Letter of Instruction (SLI) for relevant shipments, dock receipts, or certificates of origin.</li>
<li>Arrange delivery (including freight forwarders) or storage of goods at destinations.</li>
<li>Verify adherence of documentation to customers, customs, insurance, or regulatory requirements.</li>
<li>Determine efficient and cost-effective methods of moving goods from one location to another. All freight quotes for domestic and international shipments must be received in writing.</li>
<li>Make arrangements with customs brokers to facilitate the passage of goods through customs.</li>
<li>Complete customs paperwork.</li>
<li>Recommend shipping solutions to minimize cost or environmental impacts and packing or shipping methods.</li>
<li>Explain regulations, policies, or procedures.</li>
<li>Coordinate and participate in activities of workers engaged in packing, shipping merchandise, ensuring completeness of load and even distribution of weight.</li>
<li>Analyze shipping information to make routing decisions.</li>
<li>Track goods or materials in the delivery process.</li>
<li>Examine documents to verify adherence to requirements.</li>
<li>Execute sales or other financial transactions, including negotiating financial arrangements.</li>
<li>Troubleshoot problems, contact vendors or claims adjustment departments to resolve shipment problems or contact service depots to arrange for repairs.</li>
<li>Work closely with Sales, Operations, and Finance to maintain and update shipment allocation report and communicate lead times.</li>
<li>Maintain accuracy of all shipment and inventory data on Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) system.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Microsoft Office 365 Software – Word, Excel, Outlook, PowerPoint</li>
<li>Hand-held scanner that reads bar codes on goods</li>
<li>Personal Protective Equipment (PPE): Employees must use safety shoes, safety glasses, and hearing protection while on American Vulkan Corporation’s (AVC) property where applicable. Traveling employees must also comply with customers’ safety policies.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Observe and comply with American Vulkan Corporation’s (AVC) rules and regulations.</li>
<li>Must agree to (and pass) a background check, physical (if applicable), and drug screening testing.</li>
<li>Experience in working with Customs Broker and tariff codes.</li>
<li>Proficient in applying the correct Incoterms.</li>
<li>Proficient in creating Certificate of Origin and North American Free Trade Agreement (NAFTA) certificates.</li>
<li>Bi-lingual (Spanish) a plus.</li>
<li>This position reports directly to the Supply Chain Manager.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>High school diploma or equivalent.</li>
<li>Minimum of 3 years of International Freight Logistics experience.</li>
</ul>
