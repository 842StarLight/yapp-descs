<h2>Business Development Advisor - Institutional Asset Management</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<b>Position Overview</b><br>
<br>
At PNC, our people are our greatest differentiator and competitive advantage in the markets we serve. We are all united in delivering the best experience for our customers. As a Business Development Advisor within PNC's Institutional Asset Management organization, you will be based in Minneapolis, MN.<br>
<br>
<b>Job Profile</b><br>
<br>
<ul>
<li>Identifies prospective IAM clients and/or referral sources through education/positioning of IAM capabilities to generate awareness, deliver solutions and drive new revenue. Develops the most complex and sophisticated new client opportunities. Provides portfolio and/or relationship management services to institutional clients with large and complex account types. As assigned, manages accounts in accordance with investment objectives, incorporating PNCs investment approach, philosophy and platform. Responsible for delivering the PNC investment experience to clients where assigned. Responsible for maintaining and growing assets under management.</li>
<li>
Calls on prospects, Centers of Influence and clients to sell and/or refer the full spectrum of PNC Institutional Asset Management products and services, generally at client location. Assesses new business opportunities on a consistent basis, including completing book of business reviews as assigned, responding to new business inquiries and requests for proposals, and maintaining territory/focus lists. Responsible for retaining and growing existing relationships.</li>
<li>
Engages with management and/or partners to establish and execute the appropriate activities, tactics and strategies to generate, identify and pursue new opportunities and successfully close the sale. Engages the appropriate internal partners either to refer to or assist in closing business for PNC Capital Advisors, PNC Investment Advisory Solutions and PNC Retirement Solutions, or any other applicable business segment, as they arise. Provides management with insight on industry trends, best practices and product needs. Meets with and educates partners to position the value of IAM capabilities. Drives team member accountability and participation. Works alongside product partners to maximize the overall relationship across the firm.</li>
<li>
Responsible for providing portfolio and/or relationship management services to sophisticated clients with large and complex account types. As assigned, manages accounts in accordance with investment objectives that have been developed with the client, incorporating PNCs investment approach, philosophy and platform. Responsible for delivering the PNC investment experience to clients where assigned, providing actionable advice, market insight and, as assigned, providing reporting on investment results.</li>
<li>
Responsible for maintaining and growing assets under management. Will lead the sales efforts for the assigned territory and/or prospect list through the introduction and expansion of financial services to the client. Executes risk management practices through the adherence to PNC's policies and procedures. As assigned, supports other in-market IAM sales teams by providing industry insights, making introductions/connections to COIs, clients and prospects, providing strategic guidance on deals within your segment, and, as necessary, serving as the sales leader for specific deals or territories.</li>
</ul>

Competencies</b><p>
Client Relationship Management – Knowledge and ability to determine and satisfy client needs and maintain a partnering relationship during engagements; includes influencing, communicating, presenting, facilitating, managing and developing others.</p>
<p>
Decision Making and Critical Thinking – Understanding of the issues related to the decision-making process; ability to analyze situations fully and accurately, and reach productive decisions.</p>
<p>
Effective Communications – Understanding of effective communication concepts, tools and techniques; ability to effectively transmit, receive, and accurately interpret ideas, information, and needs through the application of appropriate communication behaviors.</p>
<p>
Investment Consulting – Knowledge of and ability to use various techniques to design and implement investment strategies according to investment objectives as well as to provide effective portfolio management and to maximize investment returns.</p>
<p>
Portfolio Management – Knowledge of and ability to utilize the tools, techniques, and acceptable business practices associated with managing a client's securities investments.</p>
<p>
Products and Services – Knowledge of major products and services and product and service groups; ability to apply this knowledge appropriately to diverse situations.</p>

Selling. – Applies knowledge of banking products and services to meet customer needs and enable them to achieve their financial goals. Identifies prospecting approaches to identify potential customers and create opportunities to sell financial solutions and ideas to customers.<br>
