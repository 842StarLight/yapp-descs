<h2>IT Project Manager- Behavioral Efficiency</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><b>IT Project Manager- Behavioral Efficiency
</b></p>
<p>Location: San Francisco, CA; or flexible for the right candidate<br>
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>Position Description:
</b><br>
ICF is seeking an IT Project Manager with experience in the energy industry to join the Commercial Energy IT team. As a Technical Project Manager, you will be responsible for the overall project management of our behavioral efficiency and customer engagement programs and the face of ICF to our utility clients. You will be the go-to resource for customers and internal stakeholders, providing insight on how our behavioral efficiency program and software solution works and what it can do. You will learn the software inside and out and will work with customers and developers to help grow our software in an environment with rapid deployments and constant innovation. As a Project Manager, you will own specific client implementations and partner with people from various business areas to drive an on-time, on-budget implementation.
<br>
<br>
We're looking for a Project Manager who will focus on launching and managing ongoing operations for programs with our utility partners. This person will be both in charge of client communication and internal project management surrounding a launch. To be successful, you’ll need to collaborate with both internal and external resources, including utility program staff and ICF project implementation, product/engineering, marketing, finance teams. The right candidate needs to be adept and effective at cross functional communication and coordination.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>Our implementation team’s background includes technical project management and software implementation consulting. Preferably, you have experience with behavioral efficiency, customer engagement and/or IT project and software solution implementation within the energy space, including project oversight, client management, and team management. This position may require occasional travel to client sites.
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>What You Will Do:
</b></p>
<p></p>
<p><b>Project Management
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Coordinate project delivery on medium to large behavioral energy and IT projects for the energy efficiency sector, including partnering with ICF subject matter experts on project execution
</li>
<li>Develop project plans, track schedule and budget and oversee implementation
</li>
<li>Maintain client relationships with program sponsors as well as project leads from client organizations
</li>
<li>Coach and mentor junior and mid-level staff
</li>
<li>Actively communicate with other teams to promote client needs and ensure delivery of work as outlined in scopes of work. Effectively communicate program status and health of project to clients.
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>Launch Management
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Create and maintain standard operating procedures and documentation for launches.
</li>
<li>Act as a trusted advisor for our utility partners managing the execution of the program launches including planning, project management, and client engagement focusing on an on-time launch and a satisfied customer.
</li>
<li>Proactively communicate with customers to ensure effective and delightful user experiences.
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>Development and New Business Support
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Test new development and own user acceptance of new features.
</li>
<li>Monitor questions and develop upstream solutions to recurring problems
</li>
<li>Usher new features through the development cycle and drive their success.
</li>
<li>Support business development, technical sales, and proposal activities
</li>
<li>Work closely with ICF’s IT program area leads to pursue new project opportunities in the utility, energy, and/or energy efficiency sectors
<br>
</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Travel up to 20%
</b></p>
