<div><b>
Summary</b></div>
<div>
The Manager, Supply Chain, KGV<b> </b>has responsibility for executing the development and implementation of global &amp; regional supply chain plans, initiatives and activities that drive flawless programs and products launch for the Strategic Product Business Unit. This position also has responsibility for working across a highly-matrixed organization of product managers, engineers, category managers, managers SPE (supply performance engineering), and P&amp;IC managers (production and inventory controls) to meet the SPEED + QUALITY + DELIVERY + COST + CASH + CAPACITY (SQDCCC) targets for the plants and fulfillment centers.</div>
<br>
<div></div>
<div><b>
Primary Responsibilities:</b></div>
<div><b>
General</b></div>
<ul>
<li><div>Work collaboratively across various supply chain teams as well as with internal and external suppliers, departments and strategic product business unit to create end-to-end supply chain plan and network (re)design that reflect all stakeholders’ inputs and support products launch.</div></li>
<li><div>Develop and execute robust, accurate and on-time supply chain plans by implementing the supply chain processes across the Planning – Make or Buy – Source – Deliver methodology.</div></li>
<li><div>Create cohesive supply chain programs and plans with identifiable critical path, task dependencies, and major milestones, and manages the approved plans and schedules.</div></li>
<li><div>Track supply chain SQDCCC metrics, develop and execute continuous improvement initiatives.</div></li>
<li><div>Broaden and deepen the relationships with key suppliers into NPD initiatives, plan for growth and capacity requirements, design for supplier manufacturability, driving efficiencies and cost reductions.
</div></li>
</ul>
<div><b>Planning</b></div>
<ul>
<li><div>Design components/assemblies level plan based on short, mid, long-term forecast.</div></li>
<li><div>Understand the cost drivers for the categories of materials &amp; services.</div></li>
<li><div>Partner with P&amp;IC on the planning of inventory in all configuration centers and work on matching supply with demand, based on regional demands, including phase in / phase out planning.</div></li>
</ul>
<div><b>Make or Buy</b></div>
<ul>
<li><div>Partner with Operations department to perform make or buy analyses. Assess Capacity and if the new material, process or service fit within the current internal network, or should be outsourced, either in the short or longer term, as product volume ramps up.</div></li>
<li><div>Set up and qualify alternatives for dual or multi-source if new product demand exceeds supply.</div></li>
</ul>
<div><b>Source</b></div>
<ul>
<li><div>Partner with Category Managers to develop “partners of choice” relationships with the preferred strategic suppliers. Plan for growth and capacity requirements, leverage on supplier capabilities for on-time-to-market, cost and launch readiness.</div></li>
<li><div>Gain supplier support and resources for innovation before competitors do, enabling first-mover advantage for new product launches. Engage with Engineering community to share supplier Ideas and drive design for configuration, supplier manufacturability and BOM cost reduction (VA/VE).</div></li>
</ul>
<div><b>Deliver</b></div>
<ul>
<li><div>Collaborate with Logistics Department to communicate and set up capabilities with 3PL partners. Clarify any special replenishment and distribution requirements.</div></li>
<li><div>Partner with Manager SPE to set up and optimize the supply and distribution network toward short lead time and delivery frequency tailored to ABC classification of the components/assemblies.</div></li>
<li><div>Identify and implement supply chain continuous improvement and resolve performance issues by systematically applying the SPE framework and toolkits.</div></li>
</ul>
