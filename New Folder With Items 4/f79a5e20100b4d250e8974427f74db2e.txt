Job Overview<br>
<br>
Mechatronics combines mechanical and controls engineering, software design, electronics hardware, sensors, and electrical harnesses to ensure the safest most technically advanced trucks on the roads. Within Mechatronics, members of the architecture team are true commercial vehicle engineers, owners of mechatronics technical roadmap, liaisons between feature owners and the trucking industry to ensure seamless feature integration, homogenously across vehicle platforms</div>
<div>
<br>
Responsibilities</div>
<ul>
<li><div>
The creation and maintenance of the Vehicle Messaging Matrix (VMM) through the use and administration of X-tools.<br>
</div></li>
<li><div>Active member of the global VMM networking group and participation within SAE’s networking committee.<br>
</div></li>
<li><div>Owner of one of the following CAN (controller area network) Buses; J1939 500k, J1939 250k, Diagnostic, Cabin, HMI CAN, Exterior CAN, or Chassis CAN.<br>
</div></li>
<li><div>Ownership entails the development of specification for the CAN, creation and release of physical wiring, charts and bills for all product lines.<br>
</div></li>
<li><div>Hardware in the loop (HIL) and product validation engineering (PVE) interface for major projects including; Technical liaison between integration testing/vehicle build-up, component owner and feature owner.<br>
</div></li>
<li><div>Sales/Marketing/Engineering platform management interface for major projects to define scope and dictate architecture for a given project.<br>
</div></li>
<li><div>Major task include: Review Product Definition, develop feature impact list by PAR, software maturity plan, and feature partitioning.<br>
</div></li>
<li><div>Global member of the next generation electrical architecture team.<br>
</div></li>
<li><div>Team member will be responsible for introducing both Ethernet and CAN FD into our vehicle platforms, ECU placements within a Domain, and the creation of an ethernet test bench.</div></li>
<li><div>
Contributor with the introduction of DOORs NG and our feature specification process</div></li>
</ul>
