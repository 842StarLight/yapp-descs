<h2>Assistant Manager of Operations</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<b>Description:</b><br>
<b>Position Overview:</b><br>
The Assistant Manager of Operations oversees the museum’s daily operations and ensures that public spaces meet the evolving needs of visitors and promote the highest level of customer service and exceptional visitor experiences. Supports cross departmental communication, supports marketing/membership initiatives and serves as back up to program and admissions departments.
<br><br>
<b>Position Responsibilities:</b><br>
Leadership
<br><br>
<ul>
<li>Support workforce planning processes including: assisting with hiring, advancing/promoting, terminating and/or other staff changes of status.</li>
<li>Serves as a role model for exemplary customer service. Engages visitors and consistently acts to expand community relationships by promoting membership, special events, classes and programs.</li>
<li>Ensures that all issues involving visitor safety and satisfaction are consistently and effectively implemented including visitor traffic flow, crowd control, emergency preparedness, facility maintenance and cleaning, gallery inventory management and timely response to visitor needs.</li>
<li>Provides back up for Welcome Desk including supporting marketing and sales initiatives and remains knowledgeable on cash handling and admissions operations.</li>
<li>Consistently demonstrates creative problem solving abilities, strong interpersonal and collaboration skills, professional maturity and self-reliance.</li>
</ul>
Accountability and Ownership
<br><br>
<ul>
<li>Demonstrates the ability to predict, analyze and quickly respond to customer and personnel issues.</li>
<li>Supports the implementation of all programming that affects the daily operations of the museum, including the set-up and breakdown of special events, parties and programs. Develops and maintains appearance standards of all public spaces.</li>
<li>Manages one weekend day per week and works evening events when needed to support programming, facility rentals and museum promotional events.</li>
<li>Oversees monthly staffing schedules in accordance with Operations staffing budget.</li>
<li>Maintains a basic level of knowledge and proficiency in the museum’s admissions and reservations systems.</li>
</ul>
Teamwork and Collaboration
<br><br>
<ul>
<li>Works in a team capacity with key Teaching and Learning and Exhibit staff to ensure that educational framework and initiatives are supported and communicated in all public spaces in and outside of the Museum. .</li>
<li>Works in a team capacity with program team and Senior Manager of Operations to develop and implement training and evaluation programs for staff and volunteers.</li>
<li>Partners with Senior Manager of Operations, Teaching and Learning and Human Resources to conduct ongoing training, scheduling, supervision and evaluation of frontline staff, facility staff and contract labor.</li>
<li>Contributes insight to Operations annual business plan and budget process. Creates individual outcomes that support business plans and museum long-range goals.</li>
<li>Provides input to the Senior Manager of Operations for purposes of strategic planning to support museum goals.</li>
</ul>
Stewardship and Customer Focus
<br><br>
<ul>
<li>Acts as an Ambassador and actively shares information within area communities to raise awareness of the museum and to drive visitation.</li>
<li>Proactively identifies, recruits and leverages expertise and resources to create win-win community partnerships.</li>
</ul>
Staff Development and Management
<br><br>
<ul>
<li>Hires, trains, evaluates and manages direct reports to ensure efficient, productive and knowledgeable staff and teams.</li>
<li>Completes the Performance Management process for staff and interns within timeline and meets all deadlines. Creates individual professional development plans for all direct reports.</li>
<li>Regularly observes Operations staff members including Discovery Guides and Leads and follows up with timely coaching to ensure constant improvement.</li>
<li>Issues progressive verbal and written discipline if necessary in compliance with the Stepping Stones and Museum Operations guidelines.</li>
</ul>
<b>Requirements:</b><br>
<ul>
<li>Minimum two (2) years work experience in a customer service and/or educational environment</li>
<li>College degree and/or equivalent combination of education and experience in customer service or related field. Previous experience working in a youth oriented environment preferred.</li>
<li>Focus on consistently providing quality service to all visitors; strong interpersonal and collaboration skills with visitors, staff, volunteers and community partners.</li>
<li>Excellent verbal and written communication skills.</li>
<li>Ability to work cross-functionally with museum departments and learn relevant content quickly.</li>
<li>Creative problem-solving skills; proven ability to recommend and implement process improvements and new initiatives; strong organizational skills and attention to detail.</li>
<li>Sensitive to diversity including individuals with physical challenges or special needs.</li>
<li>Strong Computer skills including Microsoft Office, Word, Excel, Power Point; proven ability to learn new systems and software.</li>
<li>Ability to kneel, walk and/or stand for prolonged periods of time; ability to lift and maneuver up to 10 – 50 pounds; operate and load/unload museum resources.</li>
<li>CPR/AED First Aid certification (provided by museum).</li>
</ul>
</div>
