<b>STATEMENT OF DUTIES:</b></p>
<p>The Center for Workforce and Community Education provides high quality, market-responsive training to support the evolving workforce. We serve businesses of all sizes, nonprofit organizations, government agencies, and individuals trying to improve their skillset for meaningful employment.</p>
<p>Non-Credit Instructors are subject matter experts who educate Workforce/Corporate Services clients. They provide and tailor training and courses to our business partners and may also deliver open enrollment courses for the general public. The Workforce/Corporate Services portfolio includes, but is not limited to, training on the following topics:</p>
<p><b>Leadership &amp; Management:</b> Supervisory, executive coaching, mentoring, project management/agile/scrum, and human resources topics.<br>
<b>Process Improvement:</b> Lean/Six Sigma and International Organization for Standardization (ISO) practices.<br>
<b>Office Technologies:</b> Microsoft Office suite, including Microsoft Project and Google suite.<br>
<b>Foundational Skills:</b> Communication, problem solving, inter-cultural awareness, time management, and basic computer skills.<br>
<b>Sales &amp; Customer Service:</b> Customer experience, consultative sales, and customer centric approaches.<br>
<b>Technical/Industry-Specific Training:</b> Engineers with machining and advanced manufacturing experience, ServSafe/TIPS, OSHA, and CPR.</p>
<p>Non-Credit Instructors may be subject matter experts in more than one area. They are expected to exhibit strong organizational skills and handle clients' confidential information in a sensitive manner, while maintaining the highest level of integrity in all client interactions and respecting their organizational cultures.</p>
<p><b>ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS, DUTIES, AND RESPONSIBILITIES:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Serve as a training representative of Bristol's Workforce Center, promoting Center's training portfolio and services.</li>
<li>
Provide regular status updates and feedback on training project implementation; refer questions and issues promptly to Center staff.</li>
<li>
Consult with clients in collaboration with Center staff to conduct assessment to identify areas of training needs, design effective training programs, and prepare educational material to client specifications.</li>
<li>
Conduct seminars, workshops, and individual training sessions, as scheduled.</li>
<li>
Keep attendance and other course-related records; return all materials and records in a timely fashion to Center staff upon conclusion of assignment.</li>
<li>
Perform other duties as assigned.</li>
</ul>

<b>
Additional Information:</b><br>
<p><b>POSITION STATUS: </b>This is a part-time, grant-funded, non-unit, non-benefited position, some nights and weekends may be required.</p>
<p>Non-Credit Instructors are part time instructors hired<b> </b><b>on an as needed basis</b><b>. </b>Courses may be offered as face-to-face sections on Bristol's campuses in Attleboro, Fall River, New Bedford, and/or Taunton, as well as various corporate sites. Courses may also be offered as online sections. Most training is delivered on weekdays, during normal business hours, on-site at the client facility. However, courses may be held in the evening and on weekends depending on need.</p>
<p>Please note: Positions will be available as needed and are contract-based. This may allow for multiple, overlapping training projects as needed.</p>
<p><b>SALARY: </b>Salary commensurate with qualifications and experience.</p>
