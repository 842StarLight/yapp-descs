<h2>Administration - Controller</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">GENERAL SUMMARY
<br>As
<br>a key member of the Division Management team, the Controller will report to the
<br>Corporate Controller and assume a strategic role in the management and
<br>financial reporting for the operating division. The Controller will have
<br>primary day-to-day responsibility for planning, implementing, managing, and
<br>controlling all financial-related activities of the division. This will include
<br>direct responsibility for general accounting, finance, forecasting, strategic
<br>planning, costing, payables, order entry, billing, cost accounting, bill of
<br>materials, business analysis and financial reporting. The Controller will also liaison with shared
<br>service financial staff as required.
<br><br>
ESSENTIAL JOB FUNCTIONS
<br>A. Lead
<br>Strategy, Planning, and Management.
<br>1. Act
<br>as Finance Manager/Controller and strategic business partner to division leadership
<br>team. Serve as a key member of NASG
<br>Finance team.
<br>2. Assess and evaluate
<br>financial performance of division with regard to long term operational goals,
<br>budgets and forecasts.
<br>3. Provide insight and
<br>recommendations to both short term and long term growth plan of the division.
<br>4. Support ERP systems and
<br>software to provide critical financial and operational information. Evaluate departments and make suggestions for
<br>automating processes and increasing working efficiency.
<br>5. Communicate, engage,
<br>and interact with local management group and NASG senior leadership team.
<br>6. Create and establish
<br>yearly divisional financial objectives that align with the company's plan for
<br>growth and expansion.
<br>7. Oversee controls and
<br>payback calculations of capital expenditures.
<br>8. Support consultants and
<br>auditors as directed/engaged by the shared services group.
<br>9. Implement policies,
<br>procedures and processes as deemed appropriate by shared service leadership
<br>team.
<br><br>
A. Direct
<br>Financial Analysis, Budgeting, and Forecasting.
<br>1. Prepare and present
<br>division monthly financial budgeting reports, monthly profit and loss,
<br>forecast, and variance reports.
<br>2. Review and analyze
<br>monthly financial results and provide recommendations.
<br>3. Maintain monthly operating
<br>budget and annual division operating budget.
<br>4. Provide the financial
<br>planning and analysis function for the division. Participate in creation of
<br>reports, software implementation, and tools for budgeting and forecasting.
<br>5. Participate in periodic
<br>conference calls with operations management, vendors, and shared service leadership
<br>team.
<br>6. Prepare periodic sales
<br>variance reports and explain variations to plan and/or trends.
<br>7. Work with the sales
<br>team to validate/audit all customer pricing changes.
<br><br>
A. Oversee
<br>Accounting, General Ledger, Administration and Operations.
<br>1. Lead the accounting
<br>department to ensure the proper functioning of all systems, databases, and
<br>financial software. Ensure regular
<br>maintenance and backup of all accounting systems. Supervise division financial staff.
<br>2. Review and ensure
<br>application of appropriate internal controls, and financial procedures.
<br>3. Ensure timeliness and
<br>accuracy of financial and management reporting data for federal funders,
<br>investors, and company's board of directors.
<br>4. Monitor financial
<br>information and provide for the preparation and timely filing of all local,
<br>state, and federal tax returns.
<br>5. Work with Human
<br>Resources to ensure appropriate legal compliance.
<br>6. Manage/Participate in
<br>the month end close process, constantly reviewing procedures while eliminating
<br>inefficiencies.
<br>7. Review all month-end
<br>closing activities including general ledger accounts, balance sheet accounts,
<br>and overhead cost allocation.
<br>8. Recruit, interview,
<br>hire, develop, and manage finance, accounting, and payroll staff as required.
<br>9. Serve as a key point of
<br>contact for external auditors related to the division. Manage preparation and support of all
<br>external audits.
<br>10. Coordinate and
<br>strategize methods used to attain team goals with Finance staff of the division
<br>and shared services team.
<br><br>
A. Manage Cost and Inventory Accounting.
<br>1. Understand bills of
<br>materials to ensure product costs are accurate and accounted for properly.
<br>2. Ensure timely and
<br>accurate input of bills of materials and price code changes.
<br>3. Ensure bills of
<br>materials and costing accurately reflect production operations.
<br>4. Analyze potential
<br>excess and obsolete inventory items as necessary
<br>5. Attend daily production
<br>meetings to understand all operations decisions affecting the business.
<br>6. Ensure accuracy of the
<br>physical inventory and reported results.
<br>Investigate and explain book to physical adjustments.
<br>7. Review scrap sales
<br>reporting to ensure accuracy.
<br><br>
A. Direct
<br>Cash Management for the Division.
<br>1. Oversee weekly cash
<br>management.
<br>2. Oversee Accounts Payable
<br>Department, approve/review large payables, sign checks, and authorize large
<br>wires and ACHs.
<br>3. Supervise Accounts
<br>Receivable management and provide guidance relating to the collection process.
<br>4. Work with the Division
<br>operations team to reduce inventory levels and approve Net Working Capital
<br>position.
<br><br>
A. Serve
<br>as Finance Business Partner for the Division.
<br>1. Attend all AP/QP
<br>meetings.
<br>2. Provide support for
<br>Financial Review meetings and review/approve financial related KPI data.
<br>3. Perform periodic
<br>walkthroughs of the plant with the Operations Manager/General Manager to
<br>discuss production and costing issues.
<br>4. Review labor reporting
<br>and cost, material cost, manufacturing overhead, distribution cost, quality
<br>cost, and inventory levels.
<br>5. Conduct formal meetings
<br>with manufacturing, finance and management personnel to discuss all plant
<br>issues. Develop and publish a formal
<br>agenda and recap with actions to be taken.
