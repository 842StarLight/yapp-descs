<h2>Instructional Systems Designer (ISD)</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div>
<p><b>Position Description: Instructional Systems Designer (ISD)</b></p>
<p>Reporting to the Division Deputy Director and Division Director, the Instructional Systems Designer applies analysis techniques and instructional design principles to the design and development of blended learning materials related to healthcare. The Instructional Designer will support the department in the continuous improvement of instructor-led workshops, online learning and other instructional delivery systems.</p>
<p><b>Responsibilities:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Understand and represent PRI?s mission, vision, and values to all internal and external customers</li>
<li>Engage clients in appropriate communication and interpersonal skills that manages client expectations and builds a collaborative relationship with the client</li>
<li>Interact with government and private sector clients, partners, and PRI staff in a professional and accountable manner, and as a representative of PRI management</li>
<li>Use instructional design knowledge and experience to create clear and engaging instructional materials that effectively meet the development needs of healthcare audiences and the goals of the organization</li>
<li>Create storyboards, audio and simulation scripts, job aids, scenarios, learning activities, instructor-led presentations, learning assessments and user guides using sound instructional design theory</li>
<li>Understand health related issues and content to have meaningful discussion with healthcare subject matter experts and other stakeholders</li>
<li>Provide support in Web applications, editorial and graphic design services to the department</li>
<li>Assisting with the development of presentation materials/curriculum and supporting the 508 and JAWs testing of curriculum efforts on contracts</li>
<li>Contribute to business development efforts for new sales and marketing</li>
<li>Balancing and prioritizing multiple projects to meet goals, deliverables, and required deadlines established on both internal and external basis</li>
<li>Perform other duties as assigned</li>
</ul>
</div>
<p></p>
<br>
<div>
<p><b>Requirements:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor’s Degree required with a minimum of three years’ experience in instructional design and development of healthcare related materials.</li>
<li>Experience working in the healthcare industry</li>
<li>Excellent Web development, graphics and multimedia skills</li>
<li>Strong background in ADDIE model, performance objective design and storyboard development</li>
<li>Development of re-usable instructor-led courses, eLearning courses, job aids and simulation exercises in an LCMS/LMS framework</li>
<li>Project Management experience</li>
<li>Knowledge of adult learning principles and concepts</li>
<li>Experience utilizing Web applications, survey tools, and courseware authoring tools (preferred)</li>
<li>Knowledge of Photo Shop Elements, Captivate, Adobe In Design and course development software</li>
<li>Experience deploying learning through a Learning Management System</li>
<li>Ability to learn and understand health related content. While instructional design is content agnostic, it is imperative that the instructional designer understands enough health related content to have meaningful discussion with healthcare experts and other stakeholders</li>
<li>Possess strong interpersonal skills, particularly in client-representative interactions</li>
<li>Interviewing skills and note taking skills</li>
<li>Ability to assimilate and chunk information</li>
<li>Writing correctly, clearly and concisely</li>
<li>Ability to collaborate with specialists in different areas (graphics, media, software engineering)</li>
<li>Ability to write stories, dialogues, scenarios, narration scripts</li>
<li>Articulate proposed approach to stakeholders</li>
<li>Being creative, thinking out of the box</li>
<li>Must understand basic instructional design principles (this is the foundation), write correct objectives, structure the content, etc. Must be familiar with content measurement and evaluation techniques to include Course Evaluations, Learning Measurement and Analysis, polling, and needs assessment/gap data aggregation</li>
<li>Understand technology and tools used in creating a course. While there are specialists in various functions, it is imperative that the instructional designer also understands these tools, their basic features, limitations and what it takes to build the designs they are proposing</li>
<li>Experience with 508 Compliance to include ability to apply JAWs Reader</li>
<li>Proficient in MS Office</li>
<li>Experience using PowerPoint, Access, and Project software packages</li>
<li>Must have no conflict of interest (COI) as defined in 1154(b)(1) of the Social Security Act (SSA)</li>
<li>Ability to obtain and maintain a US Government Security Clearance</li>
</ul>
<p>
Preference will be given to individuals who reside in, or are willing to relocate to, a recognized HUBZone area. (Go to www.sba.gov/hubzone for more information)</p>

FLSA status: Exempt</b></p>
