<h2>Senior Director Payor Relations & Contract</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<b>What You Will Do:</b><br>
<br>
<p><b> General Summary</b></p>
<p>
Under general supervision, serves as the primary strategist and contact with payer organizations on behalf of UMMS and its employed physician enterprise. Also reporting to the Senior Vice President Business Development &amp; System Strategy for work pertaining to UMMC, Kernan and USH hospitals, and the University of Maryland Faculty Practice,Inc. (FPI) and clinical faculty regarding combined physician/hospital risk-contracts and payor credentialing of the FPI physicians. Utilizes revenue protection strategies to assess contracting opportunities, and oversee contract negotiations/renegotiations, and management of contract/payer relationships to improve actual/expected revenues and maximize volume. Provides leadership to staff regarding all payer contract implementation and compliance, as well as financial performance of risk-contracts.</p>
<p><b>
Principal Responsibilities and Tasks</b></p>
<p>
The following statements are intended to describe the general nature and level of work being performed by people assigned to this classification. They are not to be construed as an exhaustive list of all job duties performed by personnel so classified.</p>
<p>
1. Oversee staff that work in conjunction with System and affiliated hospital leadership, key physicians, clinical and administrative staff, and service line product management to negotiate favorable contracts to improve revenue and help develop, plan and implement comprehensive marketing programs, based upon third-party payer needs,. Under the Sr. Director’s leadership, staff will also provide market feedback and engage and secure buy-in of hospital and physician leadership for awareness and compliance with appropriate market quality and patient safety initiatives, and will utilize appropriate marketing, sales and outreach tools to raise awareness, emphasize the strengths and competencies, and encourage consumers, physicians and insurers to use UMMS services.<br>
<br>
2. Third-party payer strategy and contracting:<br>
<br>
A. Establish and maintain relationships with key third-party payer management, including local, regional and national payers, reinsurers, and national networks. Serve as main resource regarding Managed Care market trends.<br>
B. Market hospital and physician services on behalf of UMMC, FPI, Kernan, USH and other affiliated sites. Payers include Health Maintenance Organizations, Preferred Provider Organizations, TPA’s, and other insurers.<br>
C. Introduce key UMMC faculty/programs and physician/hospital leadership to payer Medical Directors and payer staff.<br>
D. Negotiate/renegotiate facility and employed UMMS physician practice contracts with third-party payers on behalf of UMMS, UMMC, Kernan and USH.<br>
1. Work with constituent leadership to negotiate favorable/acceptable terms.<br>
2. Direct Payer Relations &amp; Contracting Care staff in contract implementation and ongoing compliance to maximize revenue cycle opportunities. E. Oversee staff responsible for facility and employed physician practice contract implementation and ongoing operational performance assessment to insure revenue cycle maximization and contract compliance.<br>
4. Global-Risk contracting and administration at UMMC and FPI:<br>
A. Lead staff in negotiation and renegotiation of hospital/physician risk-contracts to achieve regulatory compliance and optimum financial performance and volume. Primary contracting focus for both risk and/or non-risk, non-traditional contracts for Cardiac, Solid Organ, Cancer Care, Neurocare, Orthopedics and other service lines that Payers require non-traditional contracting for inclusion in their programs.<br>
B. Oversee development and negotiation of scopes of service to limit financial risk and to facilitate contract operational performance.<br>
C. Oversee ongoing analysis of financial reimbursement/contract performance of risk-contracts<br>
D. Oversee negotiation process to optimize reimbursement for FPI Physician practices and UMMC<br>
E. Collaborate with Physician/Hospital leadership to finalize contract language and payment rates. In addition, collaborate with hospital finance to prepare alternative rate application for submission to regulatory authority for approval.<br>
F. Provide leadership for implementation and training for operational performance, which includes contract administration, claim processing, billing and collecting from third-party payers, and payment of hospital and physicians.<br>
G. Work with hospital finance staff and physician/department leadership to improve contract performance measurement and revenue enhancement initiatives<br>
H. Improve actual/expected revenue on risk-contracts<br>
I. Renegotiate contracts to limit scope of service and maximize reimbursement for physician practices and UMMC<br>
J. Provide ongoing updates to inform constituents of performance<br>
5. Specialty Services and UMMC Center of Excellence Contracting:<br>
A. Provide leadership to staff responsible for negotiating on-demand and self-pay contracts to achieve optimal reimbursement and minimize financial/clinical risk<br>
B. Insure ongoing maintenance of self-pay policy/procedure through a collaborative process with FPI and UMMC finance<br>
C. Create and implement volume and revenue enhancement strategies supporting individual case requests.<br>
D. Facilitate all Requests for Proposals (RFP) and/or Requests for Information (RFI) from third-party payers regarding Center of Excellence credentialing and/or contracting.<br>
E. Support Payer Relations &amp; Contracting staff in assessment of financial and clinical opportunity/risk of RFI/RFP award.<br>
F. Support Payer Relations &amp; Contracting staff in evaluating payer’s existing provider network and determine opportunity for revenue and volume.<br>
G. Support Payer Relations &amp; Contracting staff in collaboration with UMMC Planning Department to assess market share potential/impact.<br>
H. Support staff in collaboration with Department heads, physician leadership, clinical staff and other key personnel to facilitate data/information response.<br>
I. Support Payer Relations &amp; Contracting staff in informing/advising and obtaining buy-in of clinical leadership on market requirements for center of excellence status and approach or agreement on participation.<br>
6. Physician Credentialing of UM Faculty Practices, Inc:<br>
A. Oversees provision of credentialing services/support provided to FPI.<br>
B. Leads staff responsible for maintaining FPI physician database and communication with payers insuring up-to-date physician lists for claim payment.<br>
C. Negotiates delegated credentialing agreements with all third-party payers.</p>
<p>
6. Other responsibilities:<br>
<br>
A. Reviews the work and assignments of staff ensuring efficiency and effectiveness of services provided and work performed.</p>
<p>
B. Develops and designs jobs and standards of performance; evaluates performance and provides positive and constructive feedback; recommends personnel actions such as merit increases, transfers, promotions and disciplinary actions.</p>
<p>
C. Encourages and supports staff educational, training and professional development activities.</p>
<p>
D. Maintains professional affiliations as appropriate and participates in professional activities to keep abreast of developments in the Payer Relations &amp; Contracting field.</p>

What You Need to Be Successful:</b><br>
<br>
<div>
<p><b>Education &amp; Experience:</b></p>
<p>
1. Bachelor's degree in Business Administration, Health Care, Finance or a related area of study required. Master's Degree preferred.</p>
<p>
2. A minimum of two to three years marketing experience, 7-10 years management or supervisory experience, 15-20 years health care experience, (5 at a mid to senior level), 10-15 years contract management with provider services responsibilities.</p>
<p><b>
Knowledge, Skills and Abilities</b></p>
<p>
1. Demonstrated proficiency in Managed Care, insurance concepts and environment including medical, contracting, insurance, physician practices, and marketing.</p>
<p>
2. Demonstrated ability to oversee, monitor, evaluate and motivate the performance of professional staff is required. A collaborative and motivational management style is required. Ability to build professional integrity.</p>
<p>
3. Highly proficient communication and interpersonal skills are required to work successfully with various levels of administrative and executive staff personnel and physicians within UPI/UMMS/UniversityCare, as well as the senior management of external customers.</p>
<p>
4. In-depth knowledge required if medical delivery systems, provider and insurer interface with focus on operational issues and systems.</p>
<p>
5. Strong negotiation and analytical skills.</p>
<p>
6. Project management skills, verbal and written communications skills.<b><br>
