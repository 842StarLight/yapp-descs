<div>
As an Energy Engineer at SmartWatt you will play a key role in the auditing and development of energy efficiency and renewable energy projects that are customized to our customer’s needs. At SmartWatt we never view solutions as one size fits all. In addition, as an experienced Engineer you will be looked to as a role model and mentor for our less‐experienced Engineers providing them coaching, support and training when necessary.</div>
<div></div>
<br>
<div>
<i>
Our</i><i> </i><i>offerings</i><i> </i><i>include</i><i> </i><i>stand</i><i>‐</i><i>alone</i><i> </i><i>lighting</i><i> </i><i>and</i><i> </i><i>mechanical</i><i> </i><i>system</i><i> </i><i>upgrades,</i><i> </i><i>HVAC</i><i> </i><i>control</i><i> </i><i>system</i><i> </i><i>integration</i><i> </i><i>and/or</i><i> </i><i>replacements,</i><i> </i><i>energy</i><i> </i><i>auditing,</i><i> </i><i>retro</i><i>‐</i><i>commissioning</i><i> </i><i>and</i><i> </i><i>performance</i><i> </i><i>contracting.</i>
</div>
<div></div>
<br>
<div>
Our Energy Engineers contribute to the team by:</div>
<div></div>
<br>
<ul>
<li>
performing on‐site energy efficiency audits on mechanical systems, controls systems, and lighting systems, and other energy using equipment,</li>
<li>
listening and learning from our customers to gain an understanding of their needs and desires,</li>
<li>
completing Energy Conservation Measure (ECM) energy calculations and savings projections,</li>
<li>
compiling costing workbooks and building cash flow,</li>
<li>
presenting findings to customers with the sales and development team, and</li>
<li>
collaborating with the SmartWatt construction team throughout implementation to ensure successful project completion,</li>
<li>
 providing mentorship and training to other team members as a senior level engineer. The core components needed to be successful in this role are (i.e. must haves):</li>
<li>
A Bachelor’s degree in Mechanical, Electrical or Energy Engineering</li>
<li>
At least 8 years of experience in an Energy or Mechanical Engineering role</li>
<li>
Active Driver’s License</li>
</ul>

The work environment:</div>
<div></div>
<br>
<div>
As an Energy Engineer you will split your time between an office setting working at a desk utilizing a computer and going to customer sites which will require occasional driving, walking and standing. While on customer sites the environment can vary and you there is potential to be exposed to hazardous conditions, extreme temperatures and/or dusty or noisy conditions; although this is rare.</div>
<div></div>
<br>
<div>
The travel required for this position is approximately 25‐50% of the time and will be mostly regional.</div>
