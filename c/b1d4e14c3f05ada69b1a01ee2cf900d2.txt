<h2>ER Investigator Sr</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<b>Position Overview</b><br>
<br>
At PNC, our people are our greatest differentiator and competitive advantage in the markets we serve. We are all united in delivering the best experience for our customers. As an Employee Relations Investigator Sr. within PNC's Employee Relations organization, you will be based in the PNC Footprint.<br>
<br>
<b>Job Profile</b><br>
<br>
<ul>
<li>Administers an organization's employee relations investigation function, ensuring that it remains in line with goals and strategies.</li>
<li>
Designing and supporting human resources policies and procedures that pertain to employee relations.</li>
<li>
Maintaining open communication between management and employees and advising on resolutions for employee relations.</li>
<li>
Implements corrective action plans for resolution of problematic issues, and provides general guidance on how to avoid or deal with similar situations in the future.</li>
<li>
Conducts investigative procedures in response to violations of rules, regulations, policies, and procedures.</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p>
PNC Employees take pride in our reputation and to continue building upon that we expect our employees to be:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<b>
Customer Focused</b> - Knowledgeable of the values and practices that align customer needs and satisfaction as primary considerations in all business decisions and able to leverage that information in creating customized customer solutions.</li>
<li>
<b>
Managing Risk</b> - Assessing and effectively managing all of the risks associated with their business objectives and activities to ensure they adhere to and support PNC's Enterprise Risk Management Framework.</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<b>
Competencies</b><p>
Confidential Investigations – Knowledge of and ability to implement confidential investigation and resolution processes concerning harassment complaints.</p>
<p>
Employee Counseling – Knowledge of and ability to counsel employees on a variety of issues related to the working environment.</p>
<p>
Employee Development Strategies – Knowledge of and ability to design, implement and monitor organizational strategies related to employee development programs, budgets, and systems.</p>
<p>
Employment Law – Knowledge of domestic and international laws governing employment, and the ability to interpret and take action on aspects of employment laws that impact the business.</p>
<p>
Establishing and Maintaining Trust – Knowledge of methods and ability to develop trusting business relationships and provide solicited, respected and valued perspectives to clients/peers that guide thinking, facilitate issue resolution and enhance client/peer capabilities.</p>
<p>
HR: Policies, Standards and Procedures – Knowledge of and ability to uphold the organization's and industry's standards, procedures and policies regarding human resources management.</p>
<p>
Internal Sales and Marketing – Knowledge of internal marketing strategies and ability to look, listen for, and communicate opportunities for providing new or enhanced services to internal clients.</p>
<p>
Workforce Diversity Management – Ability to understand, appreciate and use the unique contributions of associates of varied cultures, nationalities, ethnic backgrounds, genders, ages, points of view, etc.<br>
