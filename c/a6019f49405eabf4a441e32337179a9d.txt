<div><b>
What We Want:</b></div>
<div>
The Network Administrator is responsible for the implementation and support of the company’s network infrastructure, which includes the configuration and management of Palo Alto firewalls, Cisco switches and other related equipment.</div>
<div>
<br>
<b>
Responsibilities:</b>
</div>
<ul>
<li>
Install and support network hardware, including but not limited to, cabling, patch panels, switches, routers and wireless access points.</li>
<li>
Meet end-user needs by ensuring the uptime, performance, and security of the networks.</li>
<li>
Determine and diagram physical layouts that illustrate the physical location of and the connections between devices on the network.</li>
<li>
Maintain a network and systems architecture that supports the company’s mission and business objectives.</li>
<li>
Assists with maintaining a backup infrastructure and tests regularly to verify validity.</li>
<li>
Develop and implement network recommendations regarding standard configurations, documentation, management of change, performance and security.</li>
<li>
Work with third-party IT vendors for network configuration and installation projects.</li>
<li>
Monitor and ensure compliance with company standards and policies as needed.</li>
<li>
Perform additional network administration tasks as defined and requested by IT management.</li>
</ul>
<div>
<br>
<b>
Qualifications:</b>
</div>
<ul>
<li>
Considerable knowledge of TCP/IP, ethernet, network cabling; layer 2 and layer 3 switching, the OSI Model, MPLS, VPN and SSL technology, network management, diagraming and troubleshooting.</li>
<li>
Proven experience working with Cisco/Meraki Switches, Routers, Firewalls, and Wireless Access Points.</li>
<li>
Proven experience working with Palo Alto management.</li>
<li>
Network equipment installation.</li>
<li>
Network/Cisco certification required.</li>
</ul>
