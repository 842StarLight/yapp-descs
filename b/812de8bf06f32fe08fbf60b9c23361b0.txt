<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Responsibilities
</b></h2>
<div><div><div>
<ul>
<li><div>Records cash receipts in the proper company or resident account.</div></li>
<li><div>
Maintains necessary billing and financial information on resident business files.</div></li>
<li><div>
Maintains resident trust accounts and balances monthly.</div></li>
<li><div>
Accumulates records from the department managers and accomplishes the month-end closing, reporting all necessary information for the Corporate Office.</div></li>
<li><div>
Performs and coordinates all collection efforts with the nursing home Administrator.</div></li>
<li><div>
Maintains accurate records of payor sources, addresses, telephone numbers, etc.; explains bills to residents as necessary and maintains appropriate back-up records.</div></li>
<li><div>
Maintains system for recording accounts receivables.</div></li>
<li><div>
Knowledge of reimbursement system, allowable services and documentation required so billings are accurate.</div></li>
<li><div>
Reconciles room and board to monthly census.</div></li>
<li><div>
Maintains confidentiality of all information.</div></li>
<li><div>
Maintains effective communication with residents, families, facility staff and business office staff members</div></li>
<li><div>
Must be able to meet strict deadlines for billing and reporting.</div></li>
</ul>
</div></div></div>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Qualifications
</b></h2>
<div><div><div>
<ul>
<li>High school graduation or G.E.D.; two years of college level accounting courses are required or equivalent experience.</li>
<li>
One to two years’ experience in accounts receivable or bookkeeping is preferred in the long term care or healthcare industry.</li>
<li>
Experience managing bookkeeping staff members</li>
<li>
Knowledge of adding machines and computer software required.</li>
<li>
Good interpersonal skills needed to effectively work with residents, families and outside agencies.</li>
</ul>
