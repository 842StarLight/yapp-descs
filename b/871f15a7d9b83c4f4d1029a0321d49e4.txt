<h2>Privacy Counsel</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">Because you belong at Twilio
<br><br>
The Who, What, Where and Why
<br><br>
Twilio is growing rapidly and we're looking for a Privacy Counsel to be a key component of our robust privacy legal and ops team. This role will provide expert legal analysis on global privacy regimes, research emerging areas of privacy law, evaluate and mitigate risks, identify gaps in new legal requirements, provide subject matter expertise in negotiating privacy agreements, and assist internal business partners in remedying those gaps. Reporting to the Senior Privacy Counsel in San Francisco, this position is a critical part of Twilio's growth.
<br><br>
Who?
<br><br>
We are looking for a motivated self-starter who believes in the importance of data privacy principles while appreciating the complexities of putting those principles into practice. In particular, we're looking for:
<br><br>
<ul>
<li>An attorney, licensed to practice law and in good standing with your local bar</li>
<li>3+ years of legal experience, at least two of which should be in privacy. This range is negotiable if you have extensive privacy experience. In-house expertise is a major plus.</li>
<li>Functional knowledge of common privacy regulatory frameworks (e.g., GDPR, CCPA, ePrivacy, HIPAA, GLBA, and FTC guidelines, etc.)</li>
<li>Excellent organizational skills, effective communication skills, quick learner, and a strong sense of initiative.</li>
<li>Experience negotiating contracts with customers and vendors, including data protection terms.</li>
<li>Experience responding to data breach situations or managing data breach notifications is a plus.</li>
<li>Experience with advising a business on navigating the rapidly-evolving global privacy law landscape is a plus.</li>
<li>A certification such as CIPP/US, CIPP/E, or CIPP/A is a plus.</li>
</ul>
What?
<br><br>
As Privacy Counsel, you'll work as part of a robust privacy legal and ops team. Generally speaking, this role is responsible for:
<br><br>
<ul>
<li>Working closely with internal business teams on long-term data protection projects to guide their implementation of privacy controls</li>
<li>Assessing the risk of complying with emerging changes in privacy law and balancing the goals of growth and scaling with the risk to our data protection requirements</li>
<li>Providing subject matter expertise to the commercial legal and sales teams during negotiation of privacy and data protection agreements</li>
<li>Advising business teams on day to day questions about data privacy obligations and commitments</li>
<li>Determining the risk to the business of changes in data processing and advising business teams on new or changed uses of data</li>
<li>Development of new policy documents in response to changes in legal requirements</li>
<li>Coordination with the Twilio Security Incident Response Team in the event of an incident that requires the involvement of the Privacy Team</li>
</ul>

<br><br>
Twilio's legal team prides itself on the trust it has built across the business through consistently providing practical and sound legal advice. Your unique skill set and experience are critical to help support our thriving and growing business and reinforce the trust and partnership we have cultivated with teams across the company.

Twilio is a company that is empowering the world's developers with modern communication in order to build better applications.
