Under the motto <i>Active Life, Simplified</i>. we offer products within <b>Sport&amp;Cargo Carriers</b> (roof racks, roof boxes and carriers for transporting cycling, water and winter equipment by car), <b>Packs, Bags &amp; Lu</b>ggage (e.g. computer and camera bags, luggage and hiking backpacks), <b>RV Products</b> (awnings, bike carriers and tents for motorhomes and caravans) and <b>Active with Kids</b> (bike trailers, strollers and child bike seats).</p>

<p><b>JOB SUMMARY:</b></p>
<p>
The Product Designer is a critical member of the creative product design team charged with developing innovative design concepts to enhance the Case Logic and Thule brands. This role includes researching product categories, setting design direction, working with the development team and category manager from concept to final sample, maintaining cost objectives, and contributing knowledge to support the overall goals of both brands.</p>
<p><b>
RESPONSIBILITIES:</b></p>
<p><b><i>
Design:</i></b></p>
<ul>
<li>
This position will work on both Case Logic and Thule brand soft-goods. Understanding the design DNA of each brand and working to ensure that each product line supports the brand promise and aesthetic differences between them will be of critical importance.</li>
<li>
Create compelling design solutions and product lines through a rigourous product design process including consumer research, competitive benchmarking, ideation and prototyping.</li>
<li>
Finalizing design solutions by creating highly detailed and accurate specification drawings with a demonstrated understanding of bag construction and construction details.
</li>
<li>Meet project goals set forth by product management, including target costs, feature set requirements, and sample deadlines.
</li>
<li>Own the product design process, and seek to add value at every opportunity. Have the ability to adjust design processes to meet required timelines.
</li>
</ul>
<p><b><i>Communications:</i></b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Sell ideas internally with well-communicated research, well-reasoned design decisions, and excellent visual aids.</li>
<li>
Partner with internal stakeholders across all Thule offices to create functional, good looking product that can be successful all over the world.
</li>
<li>Create illustrations, sketches, renderings, dimensioned drawings and prototypes sufficient to communicate product ideas and sample requirements to development and vendors.</li>
<li>
Champion your ideas! Create energy and excitement for what’s new.
</li>
</ul>
<p><b>REQUIREMENTS:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Bachelor's degree in Industrial Design (BSID, BFA)
</li>
<li>Minimum of 2 years experience as a softgoods designer with track record of on-time delivery of successful softgoods products.
</li>
<li>Demonstrable experience creating designs in line with multiple brand DNA’s, illustrating aesthetic flexibility.
</li>
<li>Strong sketching skills and ability to do lateral form development.
</li>
<li>Highly refined sensitivity to form and the ability to translate design goals into manufacturable products.
</li>
<li>Expert with Design Related Software, mainly Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator, and experience Microsoft Powerpoint. Solidworks, AutoCAD, Rhino skills a plus.
</li>
<li>Ability to craft a product specification drawings/tech packs that ensure a complete, detailed and accurate sample build.
</li>
<li>Proven strong skills in quickly communicating function and form through hand and digital sketches and drawings
</li>
<li>Knowledge of cut and sew materials and processes including laminations, welding, sewing, foams, fabrics, and trims.
</li>
<li>Working knowledge of plastics, metals, foams, and associated manufacturing processes. Experience working directly with overseas vendors to realize concepts that meet costing, preserve design intent, and are easy to manufacture a plus.</li>
<li>
Ability to manage multiple products and work independently.</li>
<li>
Must present an impressive portfolio of soft good products. Work well under pressure, and have a proven ability to meet deadlines.
</li>
<li>Willingness to travel internationally as needed (less than 10%) Experience working in overseas factories a plus, but not required.
</li>
</ul>
