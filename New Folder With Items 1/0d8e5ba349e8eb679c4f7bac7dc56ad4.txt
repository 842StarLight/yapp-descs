This is your opportunity to demonstrate yourself; build on your sound business experience; fulfill your desire to learn and your passion to see the business across the company and constantly experience new situations; develop leadership and influencing skills.</p>
<p></p>
<p>
This position provides the successful candidate with an outstanding opportunity to be exposed to many different operations, managers and leaders of the firm in an interesting and challenging management services role. This exposure will serve to provide the candidate with experience in identifying critical aspects of a business process in both high level and detail. The position provides an outstanding opportunity to improve problem solving skills, which are critical to success in any organization. Audit focuses on training to quickly understand business objectives and process, identify key risks and core problems, and assess and recommend efficient solutions. Knowledge of these key business risks and sound management controls link directly to predicting a business unit's success in building shareholder value. Audit also concentrates on the effective communication of these ideas, both written and verbal. The art of negotiation, facilitation and personal relationship building is an inherent aspect of successful audit management. Good risk management, sound business practices and techniques are important concepts.</p>
<p></p>
<p>
Overall, Audit Services assists Manulife/John Hancock in achieving its vision and objectives and assists our customers in discharging their responsibilities. This is achieved by providing independent, objective internal audits and risk management consulting services throughout all areas of the Company.</p>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>
Position Description</b></h2>
<p></p>
<p>
The primary focus of this position is to work on internal audits of the General Account Investment and Asset Management groups of Manulife/John Hancock, based in Boston. The General Account Investment group handles a C$367 billion investment portfolio related to the insurance business and the Asset Management group leads C$432 billion third party funds (i.e. mutual funds, institutional client accounts), as part of the Global Wealth and Asset Management business. Audits of these businesses cover a wide range of asset classes in both the public and private markets, including equity, fixed income, derivatives, commercial mortgages, timber and agriculture. The investment groups audited include portfolio management &amp; trading (front office), investment operations (back office), middle office functions, compliance and sales &amp; marketing.</p>
<p></p>
<p>
The Associate Manager/Senior Auditor is accountable for completing assigned audit work of all types and the delivery of high quality, professional, efficient, valuable and risk-based audit services. The Associate Manager/Senior Auditor works under the direction of the Senior Audit Manager and the Senior Audit Director. This person will be involved in and assigned responsibility to complete specific components of the audit project in the Planning, Fieldwork and Reporting phases using an Agile Audit methodology approach.</p>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>
Responsibilities</b></h2>
<ul>
<li>
Performs internal audits in accordance with department methodology and Institute of Internal Audit (IIA) standards</li>
<li>
Performs all types of internal audits and Sarbanes-Oxley financial control walk-throughs and testing. Approximately 80% of the time will spent on internal audits and 20% on SOX control testing.</li>
<li>
Handles assignments within time budgets and target dates, reporting any timing problems or budget over-runs to the Senior Audit Manager</li>
<li>
Communicates/conducts interviews with management regarding the risks being led by business unit and assists in developing control objectives, “what could go wrong” statements and audit programs to evaluate these risks</li>
<li>
Documents controls to mitigate risks, writes and executes audit procedures to test the design and operating effectiveness of these controls</li>
<li>
Assists in presenting audit findings and recommendations to business unit management; problem solve and reach agreement on appropriate solutions to mitigate the risk/impact.</li>
<li>
Assists in preparing audit reports, including background/scope and rating of adequacy of control environments, making practical and helpful recommendations to improve controls and the efficiency &amp; effectiveness of operations; audits reports are to be clear, concise and well-organized, and issued within departmental service standards</li>
<li>
Follows up on open audit issues until resolved, as assigned.</li>
<li>
Provides on the job training and feedback to Staff Auditors assisting on assignments and contributing to their development.</li>
<li>
Uses data analytics to improve the effectiveness and efficiency of the audit process, with Microsoft Power BI (Business Intelligence), Excel and a Spreadsheet Audit Analytics Tool.</li>
<li>
Assists business unit management by providing risk management consulting support, education and training.</li>
<li>
Travel requirements range from 0 to 5%. The majority of audit work is in the Boston area.</li>
</ul>
