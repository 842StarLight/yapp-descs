<h2>Sr. Planogram Specialist</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<b>Description</b><div><b>
Position Summary:</b></div>
<br>
<div></div>
<div>Design and develop merchandising presentations and create planograms for product categories that incorporate the most current merchandise presentation standards, company financial objectives, reflect the Product Manager’s input and strategy for the category, and support an easy shopping experience for the customer.</div>
<br>
<div></div>
<div><b>Key Job Responsibilities:</b></div>
<p><b>Impact on Business</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Work with cross-functional team through Product Transition process to introduce new, exciting products and categories into retail stores while cost-effectively exiting obsolete products and categories in a manner that coordinates activities to minimize mark-downs, labor, and implementation costs.</li>
<li>
Create planogram versions to accommodate varying store assortments and physical store size differences.</li>
<li>
Create merchandising layouts that reflect the current merchant strategy to drive shelf edge retail sales.</li>
<li>
Work with merchant, inventory, loss prevention and field teams to ensure creation of category presentations that improve sales, minimize inventory costs, prohibit shrink, and engage and inspire customer engagement.</li>
</ul>
<b>
Innovation and Change</b><div></div>
<br>
<ul>
<li>Develop new methodologies and display approaches for new stores and categories (urban flagship stores, omni verticals, BO$$ categories).</li>
<li>Create financial models of profit and turns for each set created or modified and provide financial performance reports. Utilize such reports for the purpose of developing condensed category assortments to fit into smaller sized stores.</li>
<li>Document planograms using PC based space management software for all products in permanent categories to maximize sales and minimize inventory investment. Utilize historical sales information and ship pack quantity to determining product facings and maximize the space and operating efficiency.
</li>
<li>Leverage vendor data, competitive reviews, and industry trends to drive innovation, category focus, and excitement in retail merchandise presentations.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Communication/Interactions: Internal &amp; External Customers / Suppliers as well as Third Parties</b></p>
<p></p>
<br>
<ul>
<li>Prepare weekly merchandise communication materials that are communicated via the web for field operations implementation. Respond and offer support to field operations inquiries regarding all aspects of planogram implementation.</li>
<li>Partner with Store Planning to ensure that each store is assigned to the correct planogram category assortments for replenishment to direct the merchandise orders and forecast for future purchases.
</li>
<li>Collaborate with Fixtures &amp; Equipment, Signing, and Loss Prevention on the development and design of fixtures, signing, security devices, and other in-store displays associated with assigned product categories. Create updated fixture lists indicating changes in which ensure accurate fixture orders for new stores.
</li>
</ul>
<p><b>People Leadership</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
 Works closely with merchandising and inventory to ensure presentation meets the sales and inventory forecasts as determined by those teams, while meeting Visual Merchandising standards.</li>
<li>
High level of influence with cross-functional peers in design and development of coordinating functions integral to successful presentation (Signing, F&amp;E, LP).</li>
</ul>
<p><b>
Business Strategy</b></p>
<p></p>
<br>
<ul>
<li>
Develop minimum presentation inventory levels for each category planogram version.</li>
<li>Maintain the integrity of the planogrammed merchandise categories assigned by making appropriate changes when necessary based on the additions and deletions of product. Ensure the Planogram database is current with the most updated planogram files.</li>
<li>Input planograms using PC based space management software for all products in permanent categories to maximize sales and minimize inventory investment. Utilize historical sales information and ship pack quantity to determining product facings and maximize the space and operating efficiency.
</li>
<li>Support the maintenance of the Prototype/Planogram Lab.</li>
<li>Support the process in imaging the merchandise that is used in planogram versions.</li>
</ul>
<div><b>KPI’s</b></div>
<ul><li>
Varies based on yearly department goals and merchandising initiatives.</li></ul>
<div></div>

Merchandise planning and quantitative analysis skills</li>

Strong communication skills</li>
</ul>

<br>
</ul>
