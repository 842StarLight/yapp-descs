The Amazon Web Services (AWS) Worldwide Financial Services team is looking for an experienced Business Development Manager to join our fast-paced team and help drive AWS adoption in the Financial Services industry worldwide. As a Financial Services Business Development Manager focused on service development, you will have the opportunity to be part of a team focused on increasing technology adoption of cloud computing by engaging with C-level executives and influencers at all levels. You will work closely with teams across AWS to build relationships with financial institutions, from FinTech startups to the largest broker-dealers and exchange groups in the world. You will own identifying potential new AWS services, and improvements to existing services, through analysis of market opportunities and develop plans on how to take select new services to market, through incubation and on to mainstream adoption.<br>
<br>
As the Business Development Manager – Service Development for the AWS Financial Services Business Development team, you will lead the way in how AWS takes innovative new services and products to market in the Financial Services industry. You will work closely with the global team of Business Development Managers, Service Leaders, Product and Partner Marketing, Partner Teams, Professional Services, Training, Sales Enablement, Sales Operations, and Sales teams. You must be comfortable with leading and influencing cross functional global teams in dynamic organizations as most of your responsibilities will have interdependencies with other teams within AWS.<br>
<br>
The right candidate will have successful experience in growth-oriented leadership roles in the Financial Services technology space, a keen sense of ownership, strong analytical background, strong synthesis, structuring, and problem solving skills to translate ambiguity and often incomplete information into insights. A bias for ‘invent and simplify’, demonstrated experience in cross-functional stakeholder management and alignment, solid program management skills, a customer-obsessed and collaborative approach, strong data and metrics bias, and a curiosity to deeply understand adoption of new technology. Perhaps most importantly, the successful candidate will be a ‘Strategic Doer’ who balances analytical and strategic insight, with a pragmatic sense of how to get things done by leading cross functional teams through influence.<br>
