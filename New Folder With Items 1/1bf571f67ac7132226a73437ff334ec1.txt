MECHANIC POSITION SUMMARY<br>
Perform service and installation activities associated with store products and services in an accurate and timely manner. Must have a proven track record as a successful mechanic.<br>
<br>
ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES<br>
<ul>
<li>Demonstrates good customer service skills.</li>
<li>Drive vehicles with automatic and manual transmissions.</li>
<li>Services customer's vehicles, in customer service, repairing functions such as checking and filling fluids, checking and replacing filters, lubricating vehicles, oil changes, inspecting/servicing vehicles brake systems, component parts, tires, tire services, battery replacement, lights, steering and suspension, etc.,</li>
<li>Tires - Definitions, sizing, sidewall markings, inspection, repair, balancing, inventory, tire ancillary services, TPMS.</li>
<li>VIP, vehicle inspections, Workflow process, parking lot, bay area navigation, work orders.</li>
<li>Check and replace various filters and fluids</li>
<li>Follow all shop safety policies and procedures.</li>
<li>Explain services and products to customers.</li>
<li>Look up information for various applications.</li>
<li>Operate point of sale hardware and software.</li>
<li>Prepare and inspect work orders, VIP Checklist and Vehicle Inspection Form (Health Check) for completeness.</li>
</ul>
<br>
QUALIFICATIONS<br>
The requirements listed below are representative of the knowledge, skill, and/or ability required.<br>
<br>
EDUCATION and/or EXPERIENCE<br>
High school diploma or equivalency certificate is preferred. Must have a proven track record as a successful mechanic.<br>
<br>
LANGUAGE SKILLS<br>
Ability to speak read and write English is preferred. Ability to read and interpret documents such as safety rules, operating and maintenance instructions, and procedure manuals. Ability to speak effectively with customers and employees.<br>
<br>
COMPUTER SKILLS<br>
Good computer skills. Ability to use the company hardware and software.<br>
<br>
PHYSICAL DEMANDS<br>
While performing the duties of this job the employee is regularly required to stand; walk; use hands and fingers, handle, or feel; reach with hands and arms; work in awkward positions; transfer oneself from one work level to another; and move materials from one height to another. The employee is occasionally required to sit; climb or balance and stoop, kneel, crouch, or crawl. While performing the duties of this job, the employee is regularly required to communicate.<br>
