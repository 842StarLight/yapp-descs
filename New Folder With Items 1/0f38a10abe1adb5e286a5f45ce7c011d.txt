Role Overview
<br>The Deputy Program Manager has direct accountability for the success of assigned product line program(s) as measured in all the terms and requirements of the contract, by profitability/cost expectations set by the Senior Manager, Program Management, and Associate Director, Program Management. The Deputy Program Manager leads and coordinates the activities of all assigned Program Management Team (PMT) members and customer service representatives (CSRs).
<br><br>
<b>Primary Responsibilities:</b><br>
Leads small to mid-sized programs to ensure adherence to program plans, schedules and budgets for programs.
<br>Responsibility through all phases of the program management process, specifically ensuring the team achieves all deliverable outputs of each phase
<br>Provides program communications to team members, to the Senior Manager, Program Management, and Operations Manager and other functional groups
<br>Responsible for the delivery of products to the EVMS plan commitment related to cost and schedule. Establishes and ensures adherence to customer delivery targets related to program hardware, software and data items and owns the demand plan
<br>Conducts periodic internal and customer reviews
<br>Works with Senior Manager, Program Management, and as necessary, AC/BD, to develop and present bids and new proposals. Pursues new business and executes sales strategies.
<br>On a proactive basis, engage all supporting disciplines, such as Manufacturing, Supply Chain, Engineering, Quality, and others to gather program or proposal parameters to ensure up-to-date basis of pricing.
<br><br>
<b>Follow Business Development Execution process, to include:</b><br>
Upon RFP receipt, accomplish RFP Price Proposal/bid planning and development.
<br>Support RFP price development activities for one or multiple COLLINS AEROSPACE sites; integrate multiple price inputs into single COLLINS AEROSPACE response when required.
<br>Assemble price volumes for all proposals and ensure compliance with RFPs.
<br>Support Bid advocacy through senior executives via the Preliminary Bid Review (PBR)/Final Bid Review (FBR) and Passport processes.
<br>After award, develop price portion of contract kick-off briefing.
<br>Support program/project post award execution by performing analysis, tracking costs, and preparing required reports IAW required by CDRLs or Terms and Conditions.
<br>Complete Contract close-out checklist for final invoice analysis and confirmation
<br>Work closely with the established quote and customer support process to satisfy demand for accurate price, to include supporting the Original Equipment, Engineering, and After Market program managers.
<br>Build and maintain good relations with government offices and civilian customers.
<br>Work with financial analysts in finance department to maintain and report budget status of individual contracts and programs as requested by corporate officials.
<br>Employ excellent written and communication skills for initiating or responding to government and customer business issues, such as contract content analyses, terms and conditions (T&amp;C) analyses, etc.
<br>Create and suggest price strategies tailored to specific customer requirements.
<br>Maintain proposal price submissions and supporting information in both hard and electronic copies.
<br>Ensure quality product utilizing COLLINS AEROSPACE quality procedures.
<br>Perform other related duties as requirements develop. Ensure compliance with Collins Aerospace EHS and Ethical Policies and with Federal, State and local laws
<br>Compile and seek approval for explanatory narrative in cost volumes of proposals; support proposal reviews when assigned.
<br>Analyze contracts to identify points of negotiation.
