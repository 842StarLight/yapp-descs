<h2>Provider Contracting Specialist</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div>JOB SUMMARY:<br>
<br>
This position is responsible for identifying and evaluating opportunities, negotiating optimal reimbursement rates, and drafting contracts for an assigned provider network to ensure patient access to the highest quality, cost-effective providers of healthcare services in accordance with applicable regulations and the Company's policies, practices and procedures. In addition, this position is responsible for working with management in the aggressive execution of regional strategies based on market trends and client expectations as well as continuing evaluation of the region's network in terms of penetration and discounts.<br>
<br>
JOB ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES:<br>
<br>
1. Assist management in the identification and development of contracting priorities for assigned territory to support Network Development contracting initiatives.<br>
<br>
2. Negotiate and execute provider contracts to support penetration and discount objectives relative to competitive market tends and client expectations through comprehensive, aggressive execution of region specific contracting goals.<br>
<br>
3. Foster and maintain superior provider relationships. Respond to provider questions, issues, and problems that have been appropriately escalated and produce a solution.<br>
<br>
4. May manage relationships with leased networks.<br>
<br>
5. Maintain, and preferably exceed, contracting production and quality standards consistent with established regional goals.<br>
<br>
6. Prepare analyses, reports and presentations by collecting, analyzing, and summarizing information and trends.<br>
<br>
7. Serve as mentor to less experienced Provider Contractors through on-going instruction, coaching, modeling, and counseling aimed at developing the type of skills and behaviors that will result in successful resolution of provider contracting. Share provider contracting insights and information with less experienced contractors.<br>
<br>
8. Work with Regional Sales and Account Management to respond to and anticipate access requirements and network related performance guarantees. Use information derived from Requests for Proposals (RFP) to assess network adequacy and competitiveness in region.<br>
<br>
9. Partner with HCE to conduct financial analyses and monitoring of marker trends to identify, develop, and implement competitive reimbursement.<br>
<br>
10. Provide support in facilitating education and training opportunities for contract team members. Comply with mandatory training programs requirements, including Human Resources, product orientation and contracting tools and resources.<br>
<br>
11. Ensure compliance with established policies and procedures and with regulatory requirements (such as but not limited to HIPAA, integrity, product orientation, and contracting tools/resources).<br>
<br>
12. Collaborate, coordinate, and communicate across disciplines and departments.<br>
<br>
13. Demonstrate Company's Core Competencies and values held within.<br>
<br>
14. The position responsibilities outlined above are in no way to be construed as all encompassing. Other duties, responsibilities, and qualifications may be required and/or assigned as necessary.<br>
<br>
JOB SCOPE:<br>
<br>
Incumbent is considered to be a senior staff professional who knows and applies a comprehensive knowledge of principles, practices, and procedures related to the completion of complex or difficult negotiation and maintenance of provider network contracts. An incumbent works independently and has substantial latitude for unreviewed action or discretion and may have authorization to act outside standardized procedures, and provides assistance to less experienced Provider Contractor jobs in resolution of complex issues. An incumbent may act in a liaison capacity with other departments and may be called upon to evaluate progress and results as well as to recommend major changes in procedures. This job does not directly manage others but may be called on to provide project or work direction to less experienced Provider Contractors. The job may require overnight travel to meet with providers in assigned geographical or areas of responsibility. The incumbent will possess the skills necessary to strategize construct and negotiate complex financial rate structures and contract language, and can demonstrate successful negotiations that require this skill set.</div>
