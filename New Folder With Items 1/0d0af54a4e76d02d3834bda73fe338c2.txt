<h2>Service & Contract Coordinator - Chesapeake, VA</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<p>Alfa Laval is a leading global supplier of products and solutions for heat transfer, separation and fluid handling through our key products – heat exchangers, separators, pumps and valves. We currently play a vital role in areas that are crucial for society, such as energy optimization, environmental protection and food production.</p>
<p>
Alfa Laval’s products are used in the manufacturing of food, chemicals, pharmaceuticals, starch, sugar and ethanol. We are also used in nuclear power, onboard vessels; and in the engineering sector, mining industry and refinery sector as well as treating wastewater and creating a comfortable indoor climate.</p>
<p>
The Service &amp; Contract Coordinator is responsible for overall support of repair and reconditioning work for both our external and internal customers. Key areas include: provide quotations to our sales force, prioritize repairs, overall management / administration of each repair order, continually seek ways to improve the repair process and techniques.</p>
<p>
The Service &amp; Contract Coordinator contacts customers to discuss repairs, quote repairs, follow up discussions after repairs, invoicing, and working to maintain acceptable margins.</p>
<p>
You will also possess knowledge of Defense Federal Regulations Supplement (DFARS)/Federal Acquisitions Regulation (Far) Clauses and Government contracting. Government contracting experience is a key component of this position.</p>
<p>
This position will require the following:</p>
<ul>
<li>Formalize and quote repairs to end customers and internal Sales teams.</li>
<li>Opens and maintain repair orders.</li>
<li>Perform tasks within proprietary applications in ERP and Lotus Notes systems.</li>
<li>Works with the local management team to prioritize incoming repair jobs for scheduling in repair shop.</li>
<li>Manage job costs. Assures selling price and costs are in line to maintain acceptable margins.</li>
<li>Reviews and approves jobs for invoicing.</li>
<li>Review of shop quotes before pricing and finalization.</li>
<li>Interact and communicates technical repair information to workshop and end customers.</li>
<li>Assist Service Center team to meet published baseline measurements and goals.</li>
<li>Invoices repairs to end customers after completion.</li>
<li>If applicable, maintains Government status, inventory reports, records, and proprietary software.</li>
<li>Provides necessary support to Shared Services regarding Account Receivable issues.</li>
<li>Other facility responsibilities as required.</li>
</ul>
<p>
Your education will include a High School or GED equivalent. It is desirable you have five (5) year’s experience/knowledge of machining in a manufacturing, job shop or customer service environment.</p>
<p>
In addition, you will also have:</p>
<ul>
<li>Ability to read and work from blueprints</li>
<li>Ability to prioritize work and meet deadlines</li>
<li>Strong communication and interpersonal skills both written and verbal</li>
<li><b>Knowledge of DFARS/FAR Clauses and Government contracting</b></li>
<li>Demonstrate ability to perform effectively within a team environment</li>
<li>Some understanding of machining and welding techniques</li>
<li>Ability to work within stressful situations</li>
<li>Fundamental knowledge of standard word processing software and spreadsheets</li>
<br>
</ul>
