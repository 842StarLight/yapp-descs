<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Summary</b></h3>
<div>
<p><b>Organizational Location:</b> This position is with the Department of Homeland Security, within U.S. Customs and Border Protection, U.S. Border Patrol, Houlton Sector, Houlton Sector Headquarters, Hodgdon, ME.</p>
<br>
</div>
<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Responsibilities</b></h3>
<div>
<p>The Mission Support Assistant provides clerical support and assistance in applying technical procedures, methods and techniques in support of the mission and function of Sector and Station with U.S. Border Patrol Mission Readiness Operations. The position uses various systems to support the office including such activities as: arranging meetings and conferences, managing the manager's calendar, collecting an analyzing data, leading meetings on administrative and clerical procedures, personnel/payroll processing, budget, travel, workers' compensation, logistics, answering phones and greeting visitors.<br>
<br>
This position starts at a salary of $37,843.00 (GS-06, Step 1) to $54,670.00 (GS-07, Step 10) with promotion potential to $54,670.00 (GS-7 Step 10).<br>
<br>
Major duties and responsibilities include:
</p>
<ul>
<li>Assisting in one or more administrative support areas which include budget, logistics and procurement, human resources, and records and files management</li>
<li>Using various software packages such as word processing, spreadsheets, and databases</li>
<li>Developing, maintaining, and preparing reports and office records</li>
<li>Interacting with employees at all levels, working with on-site and inter-office personnel employees on basic staffing</li>
</ul>
</div>

<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Qualifications</b></h3>
<div>
<b>Experience:</b><br>
You qualify for the <b>GS-06</b> grade level if you possess 1 year of specialized experience equivalent to at least the next lower grade level, performing duties such as:
<ul>
<li>Preparing a variety of administrative documents and reports;</li>
<li>Monitoring transactions and record keeping;</li>
<li>Processing requests for services;</li>
<li>Problem solving and providing routine program information to users.</li>
</ul>You qualify for the <b>GS-07 </b>grade level if you possess 1 year of specialized experience equivalent to at least the next lower grade level, performing duties such as:
<ul>
<li>Coordinating administrative requirements with offices served;</li>
<li>Maintaining contact with offices having primary responsibility for administrative programs;</li>
<li>Obtaining the must up-to-date information on procedural requirements;</li>
<li>Assisting with routine administrative matters;</li>
<li>Maintaining databases;</li>
<li>Identifying trends or patterns and compiling narrative/data reports</li>
</ul>Experience refers to paid and unpaid experience, including volunteer work done through National Service programs (e.g., Peace Corps, AmeriCorps) and other organizations (e.g., professional; philanthropic; religious; spiritual; community, student, social). Volunteer work helps build critical competencies, knowledge, and skills and can provide valuable training and experience that translates directly to paid employment. You will receive credit for all qualifying experience, including volunteer experience.<br>
<br>
You must meet all qualification requirements, including education if applicable to this position, subject to verification at any stage of the application process by 10/25/2019.<br>
<br>
The Office of Personnel Management (OPM) must authorize employment offers made to current or former political appointees. If you are currently, or have been within the last 5 years, a political Schedule A, Schedule C, Non-career SES or Presidential Appointee employee in the Executive Branch, you must disclose this information to the Human Resources Office.<br>
<br>
<b>Background Investigation:</b> U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) is a federal law enforcement agency that requires all applicants to undergo a thorough background investigation prior to employment in order to promote the agency's core values of vigilance, service to country, and integrity. During the screening and/or background investigation process, you will be asked questions regarding any felony criminal convictions or current felony charges, the use of illegal drugs (e.g., marijuana, cocaine, heroin, LSD, methamphetamines, ecstasy), and the use of non-prescribed controlled substances including any experimentation, possession, sale, receipt, manufacture, cultivation, production, transfer, shipping, trafficking, or distribution of controlled substances. For more information visit <b>this link</b>.<br>
<br>
<b>Residency:</b> There is a residency requirement for all applicants not currently employed by CBP. Individuals are required to have physically resided in the United States or its protectorates (as declared under international law) for at least three of the last five years. If you do not meet the residency requirement and you have been physically located in a foreign location for more than two of the last five years, you may request an exception to determine if you are eligible for a residency wavier by meeting one or more of the following conditions:
<ul>
<li>Working for the U.S. Government as a federal civilian or as a member of the military</li>
<li>A dependent who was authorized to accompany a federal civilian or member of the military who was working for the U.S. government</li>
<li>Participation in a study abroad program sponsored by a U.S. affiliated college or university</li>
<li>Working as a contractor, intern, consultant or volunteer supporting the U.S. government</li>
</ul>
<b>Probationary Period:</b> All employees new to the federal government must serve a one year probationary period during the first year of his/her initial permanent federal appointment to determine fitness for continued employment. Current and former federal employees may be required to serve or complete a probationary period.<br>
<br>
<b>Agency Career Transition Assistance Program (CTAP) or the Interagency Career Transition Assistance Program (ICTAP) Eligibles:</b> If you have never worked for the Federal Government, you are not CTAP/ICTAP eligible. Information about ICTAP or CTAP eligibility can be found <b>here</b>. You must submit the supporting documents listed in the "Required Documents" section of this announcement. In addition, to be considered well qualified under CTAP/ICTAP, you must be placed in the Well-Qualified category for this position, as described in the How You Will Be Evaluated Section.

<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>How You Will Be Evaluated</b></h3>
<div>
<p>You will be evaluated for this job based on how well you meet the qualifications above.</p>
<p>We will review your resume and supporting documentation to ensure you meet the basic qualification requirements. If you meet the minimum qualifications, we will use an online self-assessment questionnaire to place you in one of three categories based on your experience, education and training. Your entire application package will then be reviewed. The final determination will be based on all the information provided in your application package.<br>
<br>
If you meet the minimum qualifications, you will be placed in one of the following categories:
</p>
<ul>
<li>
<b>Best Qualified:</b> Applicants possessing knowledge, skills and abilities and/or competencies substantially exceeding the minimum requirements of the position.</li>
<li>
<b>Well-Qualified:</b> Applicants possessing knowledge, skills and abilities and/or competencies clearly exceeding the minimum requirements of the position.</li>
<li>
<b>Qualified:</b> Applicants possessing knowledge, skills and abilities and/or competencies meeting the minimum requirements of the position.</li>
</ul>
<b>Veterans:</b> Qualified veterans who have a compensable service-connected disability of at least 10% are listed in the best-qualified category, except when the position being filled is scientific or professional at the GS-09 grade level, or higher. This position is not considered scientific/professional. Other 10 point preference eligibles and veterans with 5 point preference who meet the eligibility and qualification requirements are placed above non-preference eligibles within the category in which they qualify. For more information about veterans' preference visit <b>this link</b>.<br>
<br>
If you are best qualified, you may be referred to the hiring manager for consideration and may be called for an interview. To preview the job questionnaire, see https://apply.usastaffing.gov/ViewQuestionnaire/10623610<br>
<br>
<b>Knowledge, Skills, Abilities and Other Characteristics (KSAOs): </b>Candidates who meet the minimum qualification requirements are required to possess certain Knowledge, Skills, Abilities, or Other Characteristics (KSAOs) to successfully perform the functions of this position. Candidates will be asked to demonstrate their attainment of these KSAOs in their resume. The KSAOs are:
<ul>
<li>Ability to interpret, understand, and apply established concepts, principles, regulations, policies, procedures, and operating guidelines governing administrative support functions</li>
<li>Knowledge of a variety of software packages such as word processors, databases, spreadsheets and graphics</li>
<li>Ability to organize and analyze information to determine accuracy and make recommendations to improve operations</li>
<li>Ability to communicate both orally in writing with all levels of management and employees</li>
<li>Providing a high level of professional customer service and liaison to supervisors, managers and within and outside the agency</li>
</ul>
