<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Responsibilities</b></h3>
<div><ul>
<li>Regularly purchases a variety of technical and more complex items that require detailed specifications and off-site contract administration.</li>
<li>Ensures the simplified acquisition program operates efficiently, effectively and in accordance with existing laws, regulations, and policies.</li>
<li>Prepares and issues Request for quotations of more complex purchases and evaluates quotations on factors in addition to price.</li>
<li>Awards actions at or below the simplified acquisition threshold and within the scope of the incumbent?s warrant.</li>
<li>Holds pre-award and pre-work meetings with contractors and potential contractors to explain contract requirements, conditions, plans and specifications and answer questions.</li>
<li>Initiates modifications of purchases when it is determined in the best interest of the Government.</li>
<li>Duties listed above are at the full performance level.</li>
</ul></div>

<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Qualifications</b></h3>
<div>In order to qualify, you must meet the eligibility and qualifications requirements as defined below by the closing date of the announcement. For more information on the qualifications for this position, visit the Office of Personnel Management's General Schedule Qualification Standards.<br>
<br>
Your application and resume must clearly show that you possess the experience requirements. Transcripts must be provided for qualifications based on education. Provide course descriptions as necessary.<br>
<br>
<b>Specialized Experience Requirement:</b><br>
<br>
<b>For the GS-07:</b> You must have one year specialized experience equivalent to the GS-06 in the Federal service. Specialized experience is defined as the following: Determining whether items are to be procured from mandatory sources or from commercial sources; Serving as a contact for vendors on matters relating to payments on purchase orders or regularly procuring supplies, services and equipment; Using small purchase methods and procedures and/or determining the best method of procurement, giving consideration to urgency of need.<br>
<br>
<b>OR</b> one full year (18 semester hours) of successfully completed graduate level education in Accounting, Business, Law, Administrative Management, Marketing, Purchasing, Finance and Contracts;<br>
<br>
<b>OR</b> combinations of graduate level education that is directly related to the position and specialized experience. The education must have been obtained in an accredited college or university.<br>
<br>
<b>For the GS-08:</b> You must have one year specialized experience equivalent to the GS-07 in the Federal service. Specialized experience is defined as the following: Determining whether items are to be procured from mandatory sources or from commercial sources; Serving as a contact for vendors on matters relating to payments on purchase orders or regularly procuring supplies, services and equipment; Initiate procurement actions to correct problems, settle claims, make final decisions in disputes.<br>
<br>
<b>OR</b> two full years (36 semester hours) of successfully completed graduate level education in Accounting, Business, Law, Administrative Management, Marketing, Purchasing, Finance and Contracts;<br>
<br>
<b>OR</b> combinations of graduate level education that is directly related to the position and specialized experience. The education must have been obtained in an accredited college or university.<br>
<br>
<b>For the GS-09:</b> You must have one year specialized experience equivalent to the GS-08 in the Federal service. Specialized experience is defined as the following: Regularly purchased a variety of technical, more difficult items; completed procurement transactions that involved frequent disputes or revisions due to changes in quantities or other material facts; assured compliance with socioeconomic statutes and regulations; and conducted discussions with vendors.<br>
<br>
<b>OR</b> two full years (36 semester hours) of successfully completed graduate level education or master's degree in Accounting, Business, Law, Administrative Management, Marketing, Purchasing, Finance and Contracts;<br>
<br>
<b>OR</b> combinations of graduate level education that is directly related to the position and specialized experience. The education must have been obtained in an accredited college or university.<br>
<br>
Experience refers to paid and unpaid experience, including volunteer work done through National Service programs (e.g., Peace Corps, AmeriCorps) and other organizations (e.g., professional; philanthropic; religious; spiritual; community, student, social). Volunteer work helps build critical competencies, knowledge, and skills and can provide valuable training and experience that translates directly to paid employment. You will receive credit for all qualifying experience, including volunteer experience.<br>
<br>
<b>TIME IN GRADE REQUIREMENT:</b> If you are a current federal employee in the General Schedule (GS) pay plan and applying for a promotion opportunity, you must meet time-in-grade (TIG) requirements of 52 weeks of service at the next lower grade level in the normal line of progression for the position being filled. This requirement must be met by the closing date of this announcement.
