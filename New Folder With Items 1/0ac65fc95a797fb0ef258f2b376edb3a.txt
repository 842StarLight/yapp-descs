<p></p>
<p><b>
SUMMARY</b>: This position is responsible for conducting research to cultivate and solicit grant applications and writing high quality grant proposal narratives according to funding source guidelines on behalf of the organization.</p>
<p>
ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES include the following. Other duties may be assigned.</p>
<p>
1. Gathers, interprets and organizes demographic, labor market and other socioeconomic data.</p>
<p>
2. Prepares grant proposals, grant modifications in conjunction with field and administration office staff.</p>
<p>
3. Prepares contracts, nonfinancial agreements, memorandums of understanding (MOU), assists with budgets, and other formal agreements.</p>
<p>
4. Researches available grant funding opportunities and advises the Planning Director regarding such opportunities.</p>
<p>
5. Attends bidder’s conferences and other meetings related to funding applications/contracting as required.</p>
<p>
6. Prepares Board Recommendations to obtain approval to submit proposals or other funding source documents.</p>
<p>
7. Completes research and other technical documents as directed.</p>
<p>
8. Maintains good working relationships with staff, service providers, employers and funding source representatives.</p>
<p>
9. Negotiates and processes agreements, contracts and other funding documents.</p>
<p>
10. Highly organized and is detailed driven under pressure of deadlines.</p>
<p>
11. Possesses excellent written and verbal communication and is skilled in Word, Excel, Power Point and Google Docs.</p>
<p><b>
QUALIFICATION REQUIREMENTS:</b> To perform this job successfully, an individual must be able to perform each essential duty satisfactorily. The requirements listed below are representative of the knowledge, skill, and/or ability required. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions.<br>
<br>
EDUCATION and/or EXPERIENCE: Bachelor’s degree in a related field; or six years’ directly related experience and/or training; or equivalent combination of education and experience.</p>
<p>
COMPUTER SKILLS: Moderate computer skills required for data entry, Word, Excel, and other software programs, as required for the position.</p>
<p>
LANGUAGE SKILLS: Ability to read, analyze, and interpret Requests for Proposals (RFPs), funding source directives, financial reports, and legal documents. Excellent written and verbal communication skills. Ability to write narratives that conform to the specific grant guidelines style and format by their deadlines. Ability to effectively present information to top management, public groups and boards of directors.</p>
<p>
MATHEMATICAL SKILLS: Ability to calculate figures and amounts such as discounts, interest, commission, proportions, percentages, area, circumference, and volume. Ability to apply concepts of basic algebra and geometry.</p>
<p>
REASONING ABILITY: Ability to solve practical problems and deal with a variety of concrete variables in situations where only limited standardization exists. Ability to interpret a variety of instructions furnished in written, oral, diagram, or schedule form.</p>
<p>
CERTIFICATES, LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS: Access to transportation, California driver’s license, and minimum auto insurance as required by law.</p>
