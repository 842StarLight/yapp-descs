<h2>Lab Point of Care Specialist (Outside Sales/B2B)</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>McKesson Corp is a Fortune 7 company and is one of the largest providers of medicines, pharmaceutical supplies and health information technology (IT) products and services in the United States with revenues of $208.4 billion in 2018. The company was founded in 1833 by John McKesson and Charles Olcott in New York with a focus on importing and wholesaling pharmaceutical products. United by our ICARE values, McKesson's 78,000 employees work together every day to make better care possible for patients around the globe.

<br>
<b>Current Need</b>
<br>
<br>
We are seeking a Lab Point of Care Specialist to join our team. The territory for this position is Montana, Idaho, Wyoming, Nevada, Utah, Colorado, Arizona and New Mexico. The candidate may reside anywhere within the territory.
<br>
<br>
<b>Position Summary</b>
<br>
<br>
The Laboratory Point of Care Specialist is expected to identify and develop new business opportunities, penetrate and retain existing business, to meet/exceed annual sales and gross profit $ plans. The LPOC will drive the implementation of secured business across the McKesson organization and will represent the McKesson Laboratory Solutions organization across the business promoting cross functional collaboration.
<br>
<br>
<b>Key responsibilities</b> will include but not be limited to:
<br>
<br>
<b>Accountable for Results</b>
<ul>
<li>Accountable for identifying long-term and short-term business needs and owning plans to address and win opportunities in the POL, Health System and Corporate Accounts segments to support the POL Strategy</li>
<li>
Owns agreed objectives and targets and accepts responsibility for performance.</li>
<li>
Meets sales, training and retention goals.</li>
</ul>
<b>Strategic Agility and Selling Skills</b>
<ul>
<li>Retains, extends and wins new business through effectively identifying opportunities and strategically positioning McKesson Laboratory Solutions.</li>
<li>
Ability to own and manage the overall POL account strategy for assigned Key Accounts and focus vendor products.</li>
<li>
Ability to articulate the clinical, operational and economic value proposition for the McKesson (or Laboratory) portfolio.</li>
<li>
Establishes productive professional relationships with key personnel within assigned accounts. This includes executive level as well as the service line and departments of: laboratory, clinical care coordination, and supply chain.</li>
<li>
Analyze customer information to assist customer and Account Manager in proper formulary of testing within the practice</li>
</ul>
<b>Influence and Collaboration</b>
<ul>
<li>Establishes and builds strong internal and external client relationships that allow for future growth, profitability and predictability.</li>
<li>
Coordinates the involvement of internal colleagues and management in order to meet the customer’s expectations and maximize performance.</li>
<li>Networks, interfaces and effectively communicates with internal colleagues and peers to share information and best practices while remaining aware of industry/customer organizations that impact business.</li>
<li>
Demonstrates strong leadership with colleagues, peers, and internal stakeholders, and can effectively manage “up and down” the organization with a positive influence.</li>
<li>
Train, educate and influence McKesson’s sales force. Work closely with Area Sales Managers and Regional Vice Presidents to prioritize Lab opportunities</li>
<li>Review and support proposal development, regulatory information, and guidance for laboratory setup.</li>
</ul>

Has a strong technical understanding of the offering and knows when to engage pre-sales/technical assistance. Acts as a source of expertise for others in sales area. May necessitate broad competitive knowledge and have demonstrated a proven track record of success.
