<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Duties</b></h2>
<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Summary</b></h3>
<p></p>
<div>
<p>Reclamation is a contemporary water management agency. Reclamation is best known for Hoover Dam on the Colorado River, Grand Coulee Dam on the Columbia River and Folsom Dam on the American River. Today we are the largest wholesaler of water in the country and the second largest producer of hydroelectric power in the western United States. View our video titled "Traditions, Talent, and Teamwork" here.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
</div>
<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Responsibilities</b></h3>
<p></p>
<div>
<div>Reclamation is seeking a career professional looking for an opportunity to capitalize on his/her expertise as a Program Manager. You can make a difference in the West by assisting in unique, complex, and critical issues and determine the direction for major activities within the Region.<br>
<br>
Would you enjoy ensuring the safe and efficient operation of Government facilities? Responsibilities include carrying out the development and execution of integrated programs within the Upper Colorado Region.<br>
<br>
<b>NOTE:</b> This position is designated as a NON-CRITICAL SENSITIVE (Moderate Risk with access to Secret - T3) position. It requires a fully completed and favorably adjudicated National Security Background Investigation. Failure to successfully meet these requirements will preclude the candidate's selection. <b>This is a condition of employment.</b><br>
<br>
Location: Bureau of Reclamation, Upper Colorado Region, Office of the Regional Director, Salt Lake City, Utah. For living conditions in Salt Lake City, Utah, please visit: SLC.gov or Visit SL.<br>
<br>
<b>MAJOR DUTIES</b><br>
<ul><li>Serves as a Deputy Regional Director and participates with the Regional Director in the management of the Upper Colorado Region with delegation of full authority for assigned offices and activities in the Region.</li></ul>
<br>
<ul><li>Participates in the administrative, technical, and professional management of assigned offices and activities, and in the development of programs, policies, and procedures which affect the economy, efficiency, and effectiveness of those assigned offices and activities such as budgeting, human resource management, EEO, acquisition management, information management, and property management; legislative review and compliance; legal management and coordination; resource management; environment management; operations and maintenance; conservation and rehabilitation of resources, structures, and other facilities; and special studies and projects.</li></ul>
<br>
<ul><li>Develops and manages programs to determine the economic, political, social, cultural, environmental, and engineering feasibility of proposed projects; directs the development, negotiation, and review of contracts and agreements necessary for a sound business approach in resource management, including repayment contracts; and assists agency personnel and project beneficiaries in the operation, maintenance, and management of completed projects, including the enforcement of statutory and contractual requirements.</li></ul>
<br>
<ul><li>Provides managerial/supervisory oversight to assure compliance with established information technology (IT) security requirements and procedures in accordance with Reclamation's IT security plan, and ensures adequate IT security funding is planned.</li></ul>

<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Qualifications</b></h3>
<div>
<p>SPECIALIZED EXPERIENCE: Specialized experience must demonstrate the applicant has been equipped with the particular knowledge, skills, and abilities to successfully perform the duties of the position, and that is directly related to the work to be performed.<br>
<br>
<b>GS-15:</b> Your resume must reflect at least one year of specialized experience equivalent in difficulty and complexity to the GS-14 grade level in the Federal service.<br>
<br>
<b><i>Specialized experience for the GS-15:</i></b><i> Managing or personally performing professional, scientific, technical or administrative work in support of the operation and maintenance of dams or hydropower facilities, water control systems or associated facilities; planning, design, construction, operation, or maintenance activities for civil works projects related to power or the storage and conveyance of water such as canals, dams, pipelines, or tunnels; land or water activities inherent in the overall program management of water resources projects. These examples are not all inclusive.</i><br>
<br>
<b>Applicants who do not meet the definition of specialized experience will be determined as not qualified and will be ineligible for consideration.</b><br>
<br>
Experience refers to paid and unpaid experience, including volunteer work done through National Service programs (e.g., Peace Corps, AmeriCorps) and other organizations (e.g. professional; philanthropic; religious; spiritual; community, student, social). Volunteer work helps build critical competencies, knowledge, and skills and can provide valuable training and experience that translates directly to paid employment. You will receive credit for all qualifying experience, including volunteer experience.</p>

<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>How You Will Be Evaluated</b></h3>
<div>
<p>You will be evaluated for this job based on how well you meet the qualifications above.</p>
<p></p>
<p>For evaluating the quality of candidates for referral to the selecting official, a three-phased process will be used. This phasing will be used in conjunction with HireMe for greater Subject Matter Expert (SME) input in evaluating the quality of candidates.<br>
<br>
<b>Phase 1:</b> Applicant resumes will be reviewed by Human Resources to ensure they meet the basic qualification requirements (see Definition of Specialized Experience). If applicants meet basic qualifications and score at least 90 on the initial phase, they will continue on to the second phase and will be given an interim score based on the applicant assessment questionnaire, which will take the applicant approximately 30 minutes to complete when initially applying.<br>
<br>
With information received from the applicant assessment questionnaire in the first phase, applicant's will be evaluated on the following "Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities (KSA's)":<br>
KSA 1: Ability to provide leadership to a large, diverse, complex organization.<br>
KSA 2: Ability to analyze significant problems/issues and make decisions.<br>
KSA 3: Establish and build relationships with individuals in diverse discipline and represent the organization with other agencies, state and local governments, and public groups.<br>
KSA 4: Ability to provide leadership and empower towards the mission goals.<br>
KSA 5: Ability to use innovative methods to accomplish strategic/workforce planning.<br>
<br>
Applicant's answers to questions will be verified against information provided in their online resume. Be sure that the resume clearly supports the responses to all the questions by addressing experience, education and/or training relevant to this position. Applicants must make a fair and accurate assessment of their qualifications. If a determination is made that you have rated yourself higher than is apparent in your description of experience, competencies, skills, and/or education/training OR that your resume/application is incomplete, you may be rated ineligible or your score may be lowered.<br>
<br>
<b>Phase 2:</b> All candidates who pass the first phase will be contacted via email and asked to submit narrative responses, within one week, which will address the competencies listed below. Subject Matter Expert(s) will review the score and narrative responses:<br>
1. Leading Change<br>
2. Leading People<br>
3. Results Driven<br>
4. Business Acumen<br>
5. Building Coalitions<br>
<br>
<b>Phase 3:</b> Candidates will be given a final score which combines the scores from the first and second phases. Those candidates who score as best qualified will be referred to the selecting official for consideration.</p>
