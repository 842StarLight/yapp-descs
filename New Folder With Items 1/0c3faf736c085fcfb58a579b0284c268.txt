<h2>Organizational Development Specialist- Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<div>Grant Thornton is seeking a Manager to join its Operations Transformation service line and take an active role in engagement execution, project management and key business development activities.<br>
<br>
Grant Thornton Public Sector helps executives and managers at all levels of government maximize their performance and efficiency in the face of ever tightening budgets and increased demand for services. We give clients creative, cost-effective solutions that enhance their acquisition, financial, human capital, information technology, data analytics, and performance management. For more information, visit grantthornton.com/publicsector.<br>
<br>
At Grant Thornton, our professional staff applies traditional, cutting-edge approaches and methods to a variety of analyses. As part of our team, the Manager will utilize various methodologies and models to execute client projects.<br>
<br>
<br>
ESSENTIAL DUTIES:<br>
<ul>
<li>Analyzes organizational structures, responsibilities, reporting relationships and work processes to determine company-wide programs and/or training initiatives that need to be changed or implemented in order to increase organizational effectiveness.
</li>
<li>Typical responsibilities may include process analysis and improvement initiatives (Change Management) as well as developing methods for measuring effectiveness of all programs that support organizational development.</li>
<li>
May partner with other HR professionals to develop, assess, facilitate and/or lead organizational development interventions, programs and processes to support business objectives and strategy implementation.</li>
<li>
Perform engagement management responsibilities, including performance reviews, task delegation, project scheduling, project financials, quality review and client management.</li>
<li>
Meet or exceed sales targets for new and follow-on work.</li>
<li>
Manage business development activities, such as proposals, capture, account teams, whitepapers, conferences, and/or other thought leadership materials.</li>
<li>
Meet or exceed targeted billing hours (utilization).</li>
<li>
Act as performance manager (coach) for up to four coachees outside of engagement responsibilities.</li>
<li>
Interview campus and/or experienced candidates.</li>
</ul>
