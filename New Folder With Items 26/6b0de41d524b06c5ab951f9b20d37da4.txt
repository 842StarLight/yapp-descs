<div>
As the Field Warehouse Coordinator, you perform a variety of inventory management, order picking, receiving, shipping and housekeeping tasks in accordance with defined job processes, with a focus on safety and quality in a 24-hour Logistics Center. Your position will primarily focus on Orthopedic loaner fulfillment operations. You will interact with a Warehouse Control System to perform assigned functions, as well as comprehend and demonstrate compliance with Standard Operating Procedures and work instructions. You will provide constructive input to enhance the work process/environment by making suggestions and implementing process changes in coordination with the Field Inventory Location team to ensure the achievement of Orthopedic Field Logistics objectives.</div>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<div>
Your Key Responsibilities:</div>
<ul>
<li>Manage implant inventory including replenishments, National loaners, returns, and expired goods</li>
<li>Perform check-in and put-away process for Orthopedic products including: scanning inventory, completing inventory transfers, ordering replacement parts, and sorting mixed sets</li>
<li>Check-in instrument sets to ensure all required instruments are present in set and order replacement parts when necessary</li>
<li>Provide general support to the operations including the assembly of new instrument sets</li>
<li>Receive in loose finished goods inventory and put to stock or match with appropriate sets</li>
<li>Complete shipping functions to include: forwarding and issuing sets to customer, communicating incomplete set information to customer service, assembling packing lists, and completing shipping manifest</li>
<li>Communicate with other company departments regarding product dispositions and potential problems</li>
<li>Perform housekeeping duties as directed to maintain a safe, clean working environment</li>
<li>Take "On Call" duties on a rotating basis, providing 24-hr support if necessary for a seven day period</li>
<li>Execute all safety policies, rules and regulations, reporting all incidents/accidents/injuries immediately upon discovery to Supervisor/Safety Coordinator</li>
<li>Deliver and pickup devices in hospital/surgical center designated staging areas as needed</li>
<li>Operate all equipment in a safe, efficient manner following prescribed work methods, including but not limited to Hand-trucks, RFID scanners, inventory management software, simple hand tools for assembling warehouse equipment, box cutters, and PPE</li>
<li>Support and give input towards special projects as required by the Supervisor or Distribution Manager</li>
</ul>

<b>
Qualifications</b><br>
<ul>
<li>Minimum High School Diploma or equivalent degree required; Associate's or Bachelor's degree preferred</li>
<li>Minimum two (2) years of relevant work experience required</li>
<li>Experience in a high-volume Logistics/Warehouse Operations environment preferred</li>
<li>Orthopedic loaner process/program or hospital sterile processing experience preferred</li>
<li>Proven ability to do simple math, count accurately, read and write legibly</li>
<li>Strong interpersonal, effective communication, and collaboration skills, with ability to contribute positively to the morale of the team</li>
<li>Demonstrated flexibility/sense of urgency, taking direction and accomplishing tasks quickly and efficiently without sacrificing quality</li>
<li>Demonstrated ability following policies, SOPs, regulations and legal requirements</li>
<li>Experience with AS400 or Optimize WCS (Warehouse Control System) preferred</li>
<li>Requires working knowledge of Microsoft Office (Word, Excel, Outlook)</li>
<li>5S or Lean experience, training, or certification is helpful</li>
<li>Requires ability to perform repetitive physical requirements including: lifting up to 50lbs, constant standing/walking on concrete floors 95% of the time, and bending/twisting/squatting</li>
<li>Requires up to 10% domestic travel</li>
</ul>
