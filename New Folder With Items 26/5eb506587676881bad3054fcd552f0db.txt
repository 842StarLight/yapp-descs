<h2>Systems Designer</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p>American Alarm &amp; Communications, Inc. (AACI), a leader in the security systems industry, is seeking a System Designer to join our team! Since 1971, we have been providing the best possible security technology and customer service to protect homes and businesses across New England. If you are ready to take your career to the next level, it’s time to join our team!</p>
<p><b><i>Summary of duties and responsibilities: </i></b></p>
<p>Responsible for working with Sales and Purchasing to assemble a bill of materials and labor estimate for each pending customer project. Accurately determine the necessary equipment quantities, pricing and labor needed to complete the job according to AACI’s standards and customer’s expectations. Create and maintain database to increase productivity and accuracy. Position requires the ability to be able to communicate effectively with management, office, technical staff and external customers.</p>
<ul>
<li>Provide sales with technical support on both a pre and post sales basis by assisting with system programming and design.</li>
<li>Coordinate planning, designing, Fire alarm system equipment submittals and drawings</li>
<li>Ensure that customer contract requirements are met.</li>
<li>Confers with engineers, managers, customers, and others to discuss projects, prepare documents</li>
<li>Creates AutoCAD drawings, general equipment diagrams, and battery calculations</li>
<li>Reviews and comments on customer’s drawings and calculations</li>
<li>Monitors the status of the project to ensure design progress and documentation delivery is submitted according to schedule</li>
<li>Reviews and verifies project documents for completeness, format, and compliance with contract requirements</li>
<li>Works to get answers through various channels and develop data necessary for various projects</li>
<li>Visit customer locations for site audits and commissioning on occasion</li>
<li>Work with the sales and technical team to interpret project specifications and drawings, determine system layout, and develop bill of materials to include appropriate amount of job-related labor to perform the project.</li>
<li>Develop and maintain knowledge of current and new technologies</li>
<li>Research and assist with computer applications for continuous process improvement as technology changes to increase productivity and accuracy of quotes</li>
<li>Develop and maintain a database of commonly installed equipment/systems used for labor estimating and evaluation of actual vs. estimated labor utilization results or reports</li>
<li>Promote a team environment</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Experience/Qualifications: </b></p>
<ul>
<li>Position requires experience in battery calculations, voltage drop, sequence of operations, and knowledge of NFPA standards is required.</li>
<li>Must have experience with reviewing bid documents for purposes of evaluating and preparing comprehensive bill of materials and scope of work descriptions.</li>
<li>Position requires the ability to demonstrate planning, organizing and implementing skills which allow the successful completion of a project by a specific due date and able to effectively handle stressful situations and able shift priorities as necessary with a strong sense of urgency.</li>
<li>Candidates must have proficiency in the use of computer applications including; Auto CAD LT or equivalent and various estimating software packages.</li>
<li>Position requires excellent written and verbal communications skills must be able to read and effectively interpret general business documentation.</li>
<li>Must be responsible, self-motivated, self-starter, personable and well-organized able to provide superior customer service skills to internal and external customers. Position requires ability to manage multiple tasks simultaneously.</li>
<li>A minimum of NICET Level II Certification in fire alarm and a minimum of two years experience with CAD or similar work. Systems Technician Low Voltage “D” or Journeyman Electrician “B” License a plus.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Education: </b></p>
<p>The Design Engineer Level I shall possess a two-year or 4 year electronics degree from an accredited college or university and a minimum of 2 years working experience in the electronics field with working knowledge of low voltage communications systems.</p>
<p><b>Travel: </b></p>
<p>Occasional travel to customer and vendor site is required.</p>
