<h2>Agency Account Director</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><b>Position Summary:</b></p>
<p>The Account Director is responsible for account management, marketing strategy, and campaign implementation, as it relates to their assigned National University System affiliate partner. They are responsible for developing marketing strategies and plans to meet the goals and objective of the system affiliates, in partnership with the Marketing Services team. The Account Director then independently manages execution of the plans, ensuring that campaigns launch on time and within budget and makes recommendations for adjustments as necessary. The Account Director is responsible for implementing effective data tracking and must use quantitative data to refine plans as necessary to achieve affiliate enrollment goals. This role independently handles additional projects as assigned and supervises an Account Manager (and additional staff as assigned) in their day-to-day tasks, managing their workload and work quality, assisting with prioritization and guiding their career development.</p>
<p><b>Essential Functions:</b></p>
<p><b>Marketing Strategy Development:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Collaborates in the development of marketing strategies for the National University System Affiliates in support of their goals such as increasing enrollments and institutional awareness in their educational and geographic sectors.</li>
<li>Utilize expert level of digital and traditional media strategies and best practices to implement effective and cost-efficient campaigns for assigned affiliates; analyze quantitative results to aid in refining strategies and campaigns for continuous improvement.</li>
<li>Marketing strategy and campaign execution that includes: maximization of leads/enrollments; elevation of corporate image and message; improvement of quality and processes; increased efficiencies; effective database mining, actionable quantitative analysis. Activities include, but are not limited to: strategic/executional recommendations; tactical execution/implementation; expectation-setting relative to planning; work scheduling; deliverables, results and budget management and reporting; project management; content writing/editing; list procurement; materials delivery; actionable quantitative analysis; vendor integration and management.</li>
<li>Develop specialized knowledge of products, markets, customers and competitors in support of sales and marketing management. Stay abreast of key developments in the relative markets served by the department and the activities of the competition and work with marketing management and team to create responsive strategies.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Managerial and Mentorship:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Manage and mentor assigned staff members in their day-to-day tasks, priorities, and work load. Work with the staff to create a solid foundation for the department, ensuring that project requests are tracked for timely delivery and client budgets are properly tracked, accurate reporting of leads, and assigned projects are completed within standard expectations of quality and within the given timeframe to ensure positive outcomes.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Project Management:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Manage the project details related to lead cultivation programs for system affiliates to result in increased lead conversion, brand awareness, word-of-mouth and referrals. This includes working with the Information Technology department to create necessary email protocols, landing pages, micro sites, edits to website content, etc. The lead cultivation and lead nurturing strategies require the Manager to work closely with interdepartmental colleagues to ensure high levels of quality within established timeframes.</li>
<li>Collect and analyze data from a variety of sources, both internally and externally to accurately assess market trends and identify marketing opportunities. This includes consumer research, industry news, competitive information, student/prospect data, etc.</li>
<li>Undertake special projects and other duties as requested. Work with colleagues to research and identify appropriate topics and target audience for system affiliate programming; conduct surveys and other assessments, as needed, to determine students' and organizations' priorities and needs.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Other:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Performs miscellaneous job-related duties as assigned.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Supervisory Responsibilities:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>This position is likely to manage one Project Manager, though such assignments are at the discretion of the Director of Account Services.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Requirements:</b></p>
<p><b>Education:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor's degree required, Master's degree desired.</li>
<li>Responsible, relevant work experience and training beyond the minimum (see below) may be substituted.</li>
<li>Course work or experience should include emphasis on marketing and/or business administration, and digital and traditional marketing strategy management,</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Experience</b>:</p>
<ul>
<li>Minimum of five (5) to seven (7) years of work experience that includes marketing â€“ ideally both digital and traditional, and sales, advertising, project management, business community partnerships, fundraising, and/or educational admissions programs.</li>
<li>Must have demonstrated ability to meet or exceed goals as well as establishing and maintaining business relationships.</li>
<li>Previously agency experience strongly preferred</li>
<li>Management experience, or relevant education or project/resource management is preferred.</li>
<li>Experience in utilizing data analytics to aid in strategy and campaign development</li>
<li>Experience with educational institutions is desirable.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Technical / Functional Skills</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Demonstrated ability to manage multiple projects simultaneously, set and reach goals, self-motivated; detail-oriented with the ability to look at the big picture, goal and service oriented; professional image and demeanor.</li>
<li>Must possess an understanding of marketing, advertising and digital marketing, as well as the ability to develop a thorough understanding of admissions procedures, any of the National University System Affiliate organizations and their programs and services, both degree and non-degree programs, and elements of educational programs including certifications, accreditation, program requirements and financial aid.</li>
<li>Highly capable of data analysis to measure impact of marketing strategies and to utilize data to develop recommendations for improvement.</li>
<li>Strong interpersonal, communication and collaboration skills are necessary, as is the ability to represent the department professionally and credibly within the University community and throughout the community-at-large.</li>
<li>Candidate must be able to communicate and work with individuals from diverse backgrounds and experiences</li>
</ul>
