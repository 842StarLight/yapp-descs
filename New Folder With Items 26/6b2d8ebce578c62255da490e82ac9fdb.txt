<h2>Business Operations Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div>Brookhaven National Laboratory is a multipurpose research institution funded primarily by the U.S. Department of Energy’s Office of Science. Located on the center of Long Island, New York, Brookhaven Lab brings world-class facilities and expertise to the most exciting and important questions in basic and applied science—from the birth of our universe to the sustainable energy technology of tomorrow. We operate cutting-edge large-scale facilities for studies in physics, chemistry, biology, medicine, applied science, and a wide range of advanced technologies. The Laboratory's almost 3,000 scientists, engineers, and support staff are joined each year by more than 4,000 visiting researchers from around the world. Our award-winning history, including seven Nobel Prizes, stretches back to 1947, and we continue to unravel mysteries from the nanoscale to the cosmic scale, and everything in between. Brookhaven is operated and managed by Brookhaven Science Associates, which was founded by the Research Foundation for the State University of New York on behalf of Stony Brook University, and Battelle, a nonprofit applied science and technology organization.<p></p>
<p>
Organizational Overview<br>
</p>
<p>
Business Services provides high-quality business services to support the Lab's research initiatives by participating in cross-functional teams with a focus on risk mitigation and cost management, which is a critical component to the Lab's success. Led by Chief Financial Officer George Clark, Brookhaven’s Business Services Directorate includes the Budget Office, Fiscal Services Division, Information Technology Division, and Procurement &amp; Property Management Division. The Directorate manages a broad range of financial activities, including the planning and implementation of the Lab’s fiscal matters and its approximately $600 million annual budget.</p>
<p></p>
<p>
Position Description<br>
</p>
<p>
The Business Operations Manager (BOM) reports to the CFO and is matrixed to the Instrumentation Division (IO). The BOM will work closely with the IO Director, Deputy Director and Dvision Chief Operating Officer to manage a diverse portfolio of over 50 projects estimated at ~$20M.</p>
<p><b>
Essential Duties and Responsibilities:</b></p>
<p>
This will include:</p>
<ul>
<li>
developing and monitoring the processes used in formulating a budget</li>
<li>
tracking overall performance using financial review and analysis</li>
<li>
performing and coordinating problem analysis</li>
<li>
ensuring validity of data</li>
<li>
Day-to-day responsibilities will include budget planning, labor forecasting, development of current year cost plans, analysis of current year cost, rate development and preparation of financial summaries for management, periodic program reviews and Laboratory planning exercises.</li>
<li>
Provide day-to-day management and supervisory oversight of deployed or division staff</li>
<li>
Exercise professional judgment and decision making in the execution of assignments.</li>
<li>
Maintain, develop and employ capabilities to execute organization’s plans</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p>
Position Requirements<br>
</p>
<p><b>
Required Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities:
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor’s degree in Finance, Accounting or related field and a minimum of 15 years full-time, contemporary relevant experience, to include at least 5 years supervisory experience</li>
<li>
Expertise in the development of budget and forecasting models</li>
<li>
Skilled financial analyst with demonstrated ability to summarize and present financial data to support management decision making processes</li>
<li>
Ability to develop and effectively monitor rates for internal recharge of labor and organizational burdens</li>
<li>
Previous experience supervising staff at varying levels and with diverse responsibilities along with demonstrated ability to effectively mentor junior staff</li>
<li>
Advanced utilization of integrated business software and application of data base management tools</li>
<li>
Organizational and analytical skills are essential as is the ability to utilize Microsoft Excel and Outlook effectively</li>
<li>
Communication skills, both verbal and written</li>
<li>
The ability to interact with diverse and international populations, as well as all levels of management within the Division, Laboratory and the DOE Program Offices</li>
</ul>

Preferred Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities:
</b></p>
