<h2>Fresh Meat Facility Lead</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>Responsible for managing fresh pre-packaged meat facility with annual operation costs of $75M. Incumbent has decision making authority for all aspects of plant operations, including product quality, store servicing, raw material procurement, receiving and inspection, and finished goods shipping and quality assurance.<br>
<br>
Principle Duties and Responsibilities:<br>
1. 3rd party Management: Manage 3rd Party processing partner, and all aspects of internal customer needs.<br>
<ul><li>Manage our third party processing partner with daily responsibility for plant operations, raw material management, production yields and quality, shrink, transportation, and all aspects of meeting the needs of the internal customers (merchandising and the stores) to provide fresh pre-packaged meat products (over 250 skus and 2 million pounds per week) with a service level of 97% or higher while optimizing plant operations and controlling all costs in the facility with operation costs of $75M per year.</li></ul>
2. Product Specification Maintenance: Develop and maintain meat item specifications and accurate yield and shrink expectations for business modeling.<br>
<ul>
<li>Develop and maintain specifications for all production meat items – tray size and color, trim specs, quality grade, attributes, packaging and labeling. Ensure consistency across enterprise in delivery of the quality and the brand. Monitor delivery and execution, and performance, of products at the store level and with consumer. Identify and implement corrective action where and when appropriate.</li>
<li>Develop and maintain accurate yield and shrink expectations to be used in financial modeling of ad and turn business. Work with production facility(s) to identify opportunities to further merchandise or enhance value to under-utilized production trim or bi-product. Consistory search for strategies to increase efficiency in production, product usage, product standardization, packaging, case pack, etc. that could eliminate cost or waste from the production sales model.</li>
</ul>
3. Procurement, Supply Chain Management &amp; QA: Work with Procurement team, Supply Chain, Quality control and corporate brands to ensure efficiency of order to deliver process.<br>
<ul>
<li>Work with Procurement team to identify the optimal raw material suppliers that can deliver the specified product at the lowest net landed cost. This is to include, but not be limited to: yields and shrink, delivery charges, backhaul allowances, volume efficiencies or discounts, product consistency, payment terms, etc.</li>
<li>Collaborate and foster a strong business support partnership with the Great Local Brands’ leadership</li>
<li>Support merchandising and promotional objectives of Brands to drive and optimize sales and profit</li>
<li>Work closely within Supply Chain to manage the efficiency of order to delivery windows, and how plant hours can best be scheduled to service consumer demand. Help to develop and improve a Supply Chain model that optimizes working capital, service level, quality/freshness, and shelf-life flexibility for the stores and consumers.</li>
<li>Meet established supply chain goals, such as service levels, merchandise force outs, buyer adjust accounts, buyer/DC loss, turns, and inventory dollars</li>
<li>Analyze and create recommendations regarding item trends, seasonal updates, and product supply issues</li>
<li>Work with Quality Control and AUSA Corporate Brands to ensure that all suppliers and supply chain pieces have met the GFSI or equivalent food safety standards for the delivery of raw materials, or finished product to our facilities.</li>
</ul>
4. Transportation:<br>
<ul>
<li>Responsible for campus Transportation planning and coordination for all inbound, outbound and continuous moves</li>
<li>Manages overall campus transportation spend</li>
<li>Communicates requirements for, and maintains FMF compliant tractors and trailers for all outbound shipments</li>
<li>
</ul>
5. Financial Management: Accountable for meeting established financials, all aspects of the P&amp;L at the plant and translating financial impact.<br>
<ul><li>Responsible to deliver the financials as established in the business case, which call for operating earnings improvement in the meat departments of over $140M over 10</li></ul>
Develop accurate projected costs and item movement to pricing to support margin projections<br>

<ul>
<li>Bachelor’s Degree in related field required</li>
<li>10+ years in procurement/merchandising and leadership roles</li>
</ul>

o Skilled at leading organizational change</div></div>
