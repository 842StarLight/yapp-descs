<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Scope of the Position
</b></h2>
<div><div><div>
<div>The Cost Estimating Engineer analyzes product specifications and then applies specialized knowledge of methodologies, techniques, principles and processes to determine labor, material and other direct and indirect costs involved in producing metal products.</div>
</div></div></div>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Responsibilities
</b></h2>
<div><div><div>
<ul>
<li>This individual will be responsible for quoting metals products throughout the North American operations and will be responsible for all aspects within the quoting management for all OEM’s and developing new business. Strategically reduces cost by implementing lean manufacturing techniques.</li>
<li>
Interact with manufacturing, sales, internal engineering, and tooling vendors to formulate estimates and resolve issues</li>
<li>
If not supplied by customer, determines weights and raw material specification</li>
<li>
Identifies equipment, labor , raw material purchased components, packaging and other requirements needed to produce the product
</li>
<li>Compete Tooling RFQs and send out to tooling vendors</li>
<li>
Determine manufacturing processes required to produce product and properly estimate order of operations. Complete Workbook for approval.</li>
<li>
Secure vendor pricing and apply standard cost factors to determine price of manufacture</li>
<li>
Provide recommendation of tooling and process for review with Estimating manager
</li>
<li>Complete Cost Estimating Sheet, applying profit mark up, to determine the recommended selling price for review with Management.</li>
<li>
Assist in completing customer quotation forms as necessary.</li>
<li>
Confer with engineers, design and manufacturing on changes and adjustments to cost estimates</li>
<li>
Prepare estimates used by management for planning, organizing and scheduling work</li>
<li>
Maintain data in information system or database</li>
</ul>
</div></div></div>
