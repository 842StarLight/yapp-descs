<h2>Psychological Operations Specialists</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p><b>Overview</b></p>
<p>Psychological Operations Specialists areinfluence experts, who assesses the information needs of a target populationand craft messaging to influence and engage target audiences.</p>
<p><b>Job Duties </b></p>
<p>· Researching andanalyzing methods of influencing foreign populations</p>
<p>· Developing a varietyof marketing and media products</p>
<p>· Operating andmaintaining PSYOP equipment, such as ground tactical vehicles and sheltersystems, loudspeaker systems, state of the art computers, analog and digitalrecording and playback devices and communication systems.</p>
<p>· Disseminating andbroadcasting important information via television, radio, Internet andnewspaper media.</p>
<p>· Traveling to overseaslocations in peace, crisis and conflict to assist U.S. and foreign governments,militaries and civilian populations.</p>
<p><b>Requirements </b></p>
<p>Those who want to serve must first take theArmed Services Vocational Aptitude Battery (ASVAB), a series of tests thathelps you better understand your strengths and identify which Army jobs arebest for you.</p>
<p><b>Training </b></p>
<p>Psychological operations specialists attendmilitary police One Station Unit Training (OSUT), which combines Army BasicCombat Training and Advanced Individual Training in a 20-week course.</p>
<p>Upon graduation, psychological operationsspecialists will attend Airborne training, followed by a 10-day PsychologicalOperations Specialist Assessment and Selection program. This program allows anopportunity to assess each Soldier’s capabilities by testing theirpsychological, intellectual, physical and problem-solving (dilemma-based) withteam event assessments.</p>
<p>If the recruit passes, they’ll move on to thePsychological Operations Specialist Qualification Course to develop thenecessary skills of a PSYOP Soldier. The course is currently divided into fivephases:</p>
<p>· In-Processing</p>
<p>· Language and Culture</p>
<p>· PSYOP Core</p>
<p>· PSYOP CulminationExercise</p>
<p>· Graduation</p>
<p><b>Helpful Skills </b></p>
<p>· Interest in foreigncountries, cultures and languages</p>
<p>· Ability to analyze andorganize information</p>
<p>· Skilled at buildingrapport in unfamiliar surroundings</p>
<p>· College-level aptitudein the social sciences</p>
<p><b>Required ASVAB Score(s)</b><br>General Technical (GT): 107</p>
<p><b>Compensation </b></p>
<p>Total compensation includes housing, medical,food, special pay, and vacation time.</p>
<p><b>Earn Cash For In Demand Jobs </b></p>
<p>You could earn up to $40,000 in cash bonusesjust for enlisting under certain Military Occupational Specialties.</p>
<p><b>Education Benefits </b></p>
<p>In the Army, qualified students can earnfull-tuition, merit-based scholarships, allowances for books and fees, plus anannual stipend for living expenses.</p>
<p><b>Future Civilian Careers </b></p>
<p>The skills you learn will help prepare you fora career with the Department of Defense, State, other federal governmentagencies or related fields in the commercial industry.</p>
<p>The training you receive may prepare you forother fields, such as public relations, sales, marketing and advertising.Because of the time you’ll spend studying foreign cultures and social systems,you can explore a future career in the diplomatic service or as ananthropologist, journalist, social scientist, statistician, market analyst,advertising consultant or sales manager.</p>
