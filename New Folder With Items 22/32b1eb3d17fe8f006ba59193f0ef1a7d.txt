<div>L3 Integrated Land Systems is a global leader in the development and production of advanced night vision and electro-optical technology and systems for the U.S. military, federal agencies, public safety and sporting communities, and international markets. As part of L3 Technologies, Inc., and its ISR Systems Business Segment, Integrated Land Systems consists of leading and complementary military and commercial divisions: Insight Technology, Electron Tube Operations (ETO) and EOTech.<br>
</div>
<div></div>
<div>L3 Insight Technology is located in Londonderry, N.H., 50 minutes from Boston, MA, 45 minutes from the Seacoast, and 45 minutes from the White Mountains. Londonderry is a close-knit, vibrant community that provides its residents, families and businesses with efficient services, inviting public spaces, a top-tier school system, easy-access airport and diverse options for housing, recreation and transportation. These qualities attract industry-leading, global companies that are drawn to Londonderry’s educated work force, access to commercial transport and superior quality of life. New Hampshire is a sales-tax-free and income-tax-free state.<br>
</div>
<div></div>
<div>This position is responsible for providing overall management for complex product development projects through the product concept, system requirements, engineering design, and design verification phases. Projects may be the development portion of externally funded programs or internal funded research and development programs. For all assigned projects, the Engineering Project Manager is responsible for the cost, schedule, risk management and technical performance of development projects. The Engineering Project Manager works closely with the Business Development, Engineering, and Program Management teams to develop and execute sound product development projects.<br>
<br>
Establishes program schedule to include the development of Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) and Integrated Master Schedule (IMS) and manages scope to ensure continued alignment with contract or internal requirements and budgets. Monitors and assesses program performance against plan (schedule and financial), identifies risk, develops and implements corrective action when necessary to initiate program changes.<br>
<br>
Working as a matrix manager, directs the project team to include resource allocation and management. Through expert communication and leadership skills, establishes a common understanding among team members on technical approach, scope, schedule, risk and financial objectives. For internally funded programs, works with Supply Chain team to execute to procurement plan.<br>
<br>
Controls expenditures within the limitations of the project budget and regularly reports on project budget to actual performance via the Estimate to Complete (ETC) process.<br>
<br>
Demonstrates ethical and professional behavior in accordance with company values. Performs other duties, as assigned.<br>
</div>
