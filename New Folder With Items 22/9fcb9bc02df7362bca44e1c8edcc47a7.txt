<h2>Manager of Public Programming and Special Projects</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<ul>
<p>Founded in 1961, Hudson Institute is a research-focused public policy think tank headquartered in Washington, D.C. The Institute’s mission is to promote American leadership and global engagement for a secure, free, and prosperous future. For more than half a century, Hudson Institute has been challenging conventional thinking by developing new ideas and practical solutions to shape critical foreign and domestic policy decisions.</p>
<p>The Manager of Public Programming and Special Projects, working in Hudson Institute’s Public Affairs office, organizes public events, conferences, panels, and meetings at Hudson Institute, including managing event promotion, logistics, and post-event announcements; creating and overseeing the editing process for public email communications; and managing the production of Hudson Institute’s <i>Annual Report</i>.</p>
<p>I. Events<br>
</p>
<p></p>
<li>Schedule and maintain a master event calendar with more than 70 events per year including panel discussions, conferences, private meetings, and research luncheons.</li>
<br>
<li>For each event, manage event logistics, coordinating additional spaces as necessary.</li>
<br>
<li>Work with Hudson fellows and staff to obtain information necessary for drafting and editing event invitations and a description to be posted on hudson.org.</li>
<br>
<li>Serve as POC for fellows, panelists, and vendors, making occasional travel, hotel, and restaurant reservations as needed.</li>
<br>
<li>Produce event materials including handouts and bios, and arrange for book sales and report distribution when appropriate.</li>
<br>
<li>Create and oversee web listings of events, posting all information prior to events and coordinating after events with the Technical Manager to ensure pages contain proper post-event information and media.</li>
<br>
<li>During events, take photos of speakers and coordinate assistance from staff and interns to support events.<br>
</li>
<li>Coordinate with Press Secretary and Technical Manager to promote events across Hudson’s social media platforms, and work with Press Secretary to coordinate media coverage.</li>
<p></p>
<p>II. Communications</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<br>
<li>Create public email invitations and newsletters (experience with Campaign Monitor or a similar software is a plus). Draft or compile emails and oversee the editing process.<br>
</li>
<li>Maintain a contact database, including numerous subject-specific email lists, and work with fellows to expand these lists as appropriate.<br>
</li>
<li>Maintain a balanced and optimized schedule for sending public emails—monitor open rates, click rates, subscription rates, and unsubscribe rates to maximize email open and engagement rates, improve content of future emails, and support contact retention.</li>
<p></p>
<p>III. Annual Report</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<br>
<li>Oversee the production of Hudson Institute’s Annual Report, including creating a production and draft schedule; work with Hudson’s Public Affairs Director to organize the contents and compose the text; coordinate with the graphic designer to oversee layout and art direction; and connect with staff and fellows as necessary to obtain information about their activities, including research, visuals, and publications to be included in the text.<br>
</li>
<li>During the drafting process, coordinate with Hudson’s Public Affairs Director, with input from other senior management, editorial decisions for the Annual Report.</li>
<p></p>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Qualifications</b></h2>
<p></p>
<p><b>Additional Performance Requirements</b></p>
<br>
<p></p>
<br>
<li>Commitment to Hudson Institute’s mission and supporting the work of the Institute’s scholars and staff.<br>
</li>
<li>Demonstrated interest in the work of the Institute and general knowledge of the policy-making environment.</li>
<li>Excellent verbal, writing, and interpersonal skills.<br>
</li>
<li>Well organized, self-directed, attentive to detail, and committed to the team.<br>
</li>
<li>Ability to multi-task, work under pressure, and meet deadlines.<br>
</li>
<li>Technology savvy essential, with experience in, or demonstrated capacity to learn, Campaign Monitor and Salesforce. Graphics skills a plus.<br>
</li>
<li>Strong character and personable manner.<br>
</li>
<li>Serve as a liaison between Development and Public Affairs, providing support for development events and development event promotion as necessary.<br>
</li>
<li>Various editing and writing tasks as needed.</li>
</ul>
