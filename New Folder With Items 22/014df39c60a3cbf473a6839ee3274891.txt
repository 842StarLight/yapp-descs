<h2>Assistant Production Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div>
<p>Job Summary:</p>
<p><b>
Unity in Diversity</b></p>
<p>
Our team members are as diverse as our music venues. With its owned/operated/managed venues, Live Nation Clubs and Theaters, a division of Live Nation Entertainment, is wide-ranging, including the legendary Fillmore venues and the intimate House of Blues clubs throughout the United States. Live Nation Clubs and Theaters is the country’s preeminent group of intimate music venues, featuring superior sound and lighting technology in one-of-a-kind custom-designed environments aimed to bring fans and artists together in unparalleled musical environments.</p>
<p></p>
<p>
Our mission is to create a profitable and principled global entertainment company. To celebrate the diversity and brotherhood of world culture. To promote racial and spiritual harmony through love, peace, truth, righteousness and non-violence. Our venues bring fans as close to artists as possible and our aim is to create teams that are just as close.</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>
The Role:</b></p>
<p>
The Assistant Production Manager is responsible for ensuring the success of show and event productions by managing the technical and personnel aspects of the Production department through detailed preparation. This position supports our Warner Theater.</p>
<p><br>
<b>
What You’ll Need:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Minimum 3 years of prior management experience in a live entertainment venue.</li>
<li>
3 years minimum of Tour and Stage Production experience.</li>
<li>
Experience in stage lighting, pro audio systems and basic video systems.</li>
<li>
Ability to handle multiple projects simultaneously.</li>
<li>
District of Columbia Responsible Alcohol Awareness Training Certification or Equivalent</li>
<li>
Ability to make sound decisions; sometimes with limited information.</li>
<li>
Computer literate in Windows applications.</li>
<li>
Must possess superior interpersonal communication and organizational skills.</li>
<li>
Flexible Schedule (days/nights, late hours, weekends, and holidays)</li>
<li>
Tolerant of all cultures, music and art forms</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><i>
Preferred:
</i></p>
<ul>
<li>Experience in a live music environment</li>
<li>
Some college or college degree in related field</li>
<li>
Point of sales system knowledge</li>
<li>
Experience interviewing and hiring staff; behavioral based interviewing skills a plus</li>
<li>
Interest in Night Life</li>
<li>
Cash handling experience</li>
<li>
Experience recognizing valid identification</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><i>
Physical Demands/Working Environment:</i></p>
<ul>
<li>
Working environment is fast-paced and often loud and stressful</li>
<li>
Position requires extended periods of prolonged standing, lifting, bending, reaching and working on your feet</li>
<li>
Must be able to lift or move up to 75 lbs. using proper lifting techniques</li>
<li>
Tolerance of loud noise level in working environment</li>
<li>
Able to wear a radio earpiece during the scheduled shift</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>
What You’ll Do:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Ensure positive and creative environment for management team</li>
<li>
Maintain regular communications with Venue and Corporate Management, and facilitate proper interdepartmental communication and organization</li>
<li>
Assist in conducting staff evaluations (training, 90 day and Annual). Set development and performance goals and monitor progress.</li>
<li>
Assist in recruitment and hiring of new managers and staff; ensure complete training is provided for all staff members</li>
<li>
Ensure hires/disciplinary/termination’s follow all Live Nation Clubs &amp; Theaters guidelines.</li>
<li>
Assist in maintaining department manuals and training materials for all production positions.</li>
<li>
Develop and maintain department manuals and training materials for all production positions.</li>
<li>
Assist and support production department personnel with job functions as needed.</li>
<li>
Assist in creating budgetary requirements and tracking the financial aspects of department.</li>
<li>
Create and maintain daily band cost spreadsheet.</li>
<li>
Create and maintain inventory and maintenance log.</li>
<li>
Ensure all procedures are cost effective.</li>
<li>
Creates daily and weekly show schedule.</li>
<li>
Assist in scheduling appropriate number of staff while maintaining labor costs within the assigned budgetary guidelines.</li>
<li>
Assist in managing stage, sound, and lighting crews.</li>
<li>
Coordinate operational feedback with Talent Buyer.</li>
<li>
Advance technical details for shows/events.</li>
<li>
Assist with the maintenance of audio, lighting, backline, video and CD-ROM systems advising on repairs when needed.</li>
<li>
Advance food and beverage/hospitality requirements.</li>
<li>
Ensure information is distributed to Operations Managers in a timely basis.</li>
<li>
Responsible for safe and consistent operation of all equipment.</li>
<li>
Advise on design and purchase of site lighting systems (patio, restaurant, etc.)</li>
<li>
Attends production and operations meeting as outlined by Talent Buyer.</li>
<li>
Maintain “past show” files.</li>
<li>
Maintain accurate vendor records, following Purchase Ordering Systems.</li>
<li>
Responsible for documenting and delivering disciplinary actions to production crew.</li>
</ul>
