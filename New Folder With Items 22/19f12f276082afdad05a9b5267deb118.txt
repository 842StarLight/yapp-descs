<div><b>Brief Summary of Work for this Position</b></div>
<div>
<p>The Employer Development Coordinator manages and maintains purposeful relationships with external and campus partners to increase the number of internship, full-time and networking opportunities available for undergraduate and graduate students at UNCW. This role will support programming, operations, events, and customer-service as related to the Employer Development and Career Center teams.</p>
<p>
Employer Development &amp; Retention Support:<br>
Coordinates, manages, and supports a wide range of programs and services to retain and enhance professional relationships with new and existing employer partners local, regional, national and international. Also includes Career Center responses to new or expanding employer relationships generated by the Associate Director for Employer Development. This includes supporting and promoting employer activities such as on-campus recruiting, career fairs, information sessions, classroom presentations, internship and job postings, etc. These efforts include collaborative planning with Associate Director for Employer Development and all members of the Career Center team. Works with Career Center staff and employer representatives to determine most productive, appropriate and efficient processes, procedures and solutions for employer development engagement. Provides input on departmental programs and services. Understands that the Career Center’s constituents include our students, alumni, employers, faculty, staff, parents and the general public. Impact of work reaches beyond the department to the campus and community at large; and as such, impeccable customer service is required. Monitors the calendar and travel schedule of the Associate Director for Employer Development to help ensure timely and appropriate responses to the needs of employer partners. Manages all travel and budget expenses for the Associate Director for Employer Development. Works collaboratively with Career Center administrative staff, especially regarding budget allocations, facility reservations, travel arrangements, career events and employer visitations on campus. May assign and review the work of Career Center interns, Student Workers, and/or Volunteers.</p>
<p>
Data Collection &amp; Analysis:<br>
Works closely with the Associate Director from Employer Development to identify needed data, collect or generate data, and develop analytical reports. Data sets include information regarding Fortune 500 organizations, growing North Carolina organizations, employers by career section and geographic region, etc. Data gathering also includes monitoring survey trends and economic projections that affect employment in the local, regional and national areas, as well as, industry research and trends that influence future campus programming and growth. Provides feedback and outcomes data include data from employers who recruit on campus and/or post internships or internal jobs. Documents programs, events, and services within the Employer Development area, including participation, satisfaction, and outcomes for employers as well as our students. Provides periodic reports of these activities as needed, including mid-year and annual reports. Work to ensure efforts are in-sync with the strategic plans of the Career Center department, Division of Student Affairs, and the UNCW campus.</p>
<p>
Outreach: Provides learning opportunities for Career Center staff and others to insure awareness of developments in organizational or legal issues. Engagement with local networking groups such as the Wilmington Chamber, industry and other professional organizations, as well as human resources, talent acquisition and<br>
recruiting groups.</p>
<p>
The Employer Development Coordinator position reports to the Associate Director for Employer Development.</p>

<div><b>Primary purpose of the division, department, and/or grant</b></div>
<p>The UNCW Career Center is a comprehensive career counseling and resource center dedicated to empowering students and alumni as active participants in their own career development. This includes coordination and management of a wide range of programs and services to retain and enhance professional relationships with existing employer partners- local, regional, national and international. Additionally, the Career Center responds to new or expanding employer relationships generated by the Associate Director for Employer Development and further provides career and employment assistance for currently enrolled students and alumni through individual appointments, workshops, career events, programs, services and resources. Constituents include students, alumni, employers, faculty &amp; staff, parents and the general public.</p>

<div><b>University Information</b></div>
<p>The University of North Carolina Wilmington, the state’s coastal university, is dedicated to learning through the integration of teaching and mentoring with research and service. A public institution with nearly 16,500 students, the university is known for its superb faculty and staff and powerful academic experience. With an array of high-quality programs at the baccalaureate and master’s levels, and doctoral programs in marine biology, educational leadership, psychology and nursing practice. UNCW is consistently recognized at a national level for academic excellence and affordability by publications like U.S. News &amp; World Report, Kiplinger’s Personal Finance and The Business Journals. UNCW also recently earned the 2015 Community Engagement Classification from the Carnegie Foundation for the Advancement of Teaching, bestowed to higher education institutions dedicated to community involvement.</p>
