<h2>Cemetery Superintendent</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div></div>
<div><div>
<b>Town of Vernon</b> <b>Department:</b><b> Cemetery</b><br>
<b>Job Title:</b><b> Cemetery Superintendent</b> <b>Bargaining</b> <b>Unit:</b><b> Non-Union</b><br>
<b>Hourly Rate:</b><b> $27.24 - $31.24</b><br>
<br>
<br>
<b>General Statement of Duties</b><b>:</b> Highly responsible position involving management of operations for the Town of Vernon Cemeteries in accordance with the Cemetery Commission and Administration. Organizes and supervises the construction, care and maintenance of Town cemeteries facilities and public grounds.<br>
<br>
<b>Supervision Received</b><b>:</b> Receives general direction from the Town Administrator. Works with considerable independence.<br>
<br>
<b>Supervision Exercised</b><b>:</b> Directly supervises all divisional staff including full-time, seasonal and part-time employees.<br>
<br>
<b>Essential Job Function</b><b>:</b> Prepares and implements a plan to systematically maintain cemeteries, buildings and equipment adhering to plans, policies and procedures established by the Cemetery Commission, government agencies or other third parties; ensures compliance with State and Federal regulations for health and safety or other reasons; plans and prioritizes work according to activity, seasons or emergencies; assigns staff to maintain and care for turf and shrubs, including periodic spraying of herbicides and pesticides. Oversees the maintenance of cemetery equipment and facilities; reads maps and lays out grave sites; performs or supervises the use of heavy equipment in the digging of graves, avoiding damage to turf, markers and headstones; periodically inspects Town equipment to ensure OSHA compliance; supervises and performs the proper lay-out and installation of foundations for markers and headstones; supervises and performs mowing operations. Recommends equipment purchases and specifications; initiates purchase of supplies and materials as needed; maintains departmental inventory of materials, supplies and equipment. Trains subordinates, including seasonal workers, in maintenance activities and standard safety procedures. Applies Cemetery Rules, Regulations and Policies to deal effectively with the public in cemetery matters. Oversees sales of cemetery lots and maintains records of same; receives and accounts for burial permits and cemetery charges. Prepares budget recommendations for the department and administers the approved annual budget. Performs other related duties as assigned. Regular and punctual attendance.<br>
<br>
<b>Physical Demands:</b> The physical demands described here are representative of those that must be met by an employee to successfully perform the essential functions of this job. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions of this job.<br>
<br>
While performing the duties of the job the employee is regularly required to talk and hear, reach with hands and arms, stand, walk, use hands to finger, grasp, handle, feel or operate objects, tools or controls. The employee occasionally is required to climb, kneel and crouch. Hand-eye coordination is required to operate computer and other various office equipment.<br>
<br>
The employee frequently must lift, push, pull and/or move up to 50 pounds. Specific vision abilities required by this job include close vision, distance vision, color vision, peripheral vision, depth perception and the ability to adjust focus.<br>
<br>
<b>Work Environment</b><b>: </b>The work characteristics described here are representative of those an employee encounters while performing the essential functions of this job. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individual with disabilities to perform the essential functions of the job.<br>
<br>
While performing the duties of this job the employee regularly works near moving mechanical parts and in outside weather conditions. The employee frequently is exposed to wet and/or humid conditions, fumes or airborne particles, toxic or caustic chemicals.<br>
<br>
Must be able to concentrate on fine details with some interruption. Needs to attend to task/function for more than 60 minutes at a time. Must be able to understand and relate to specific ideas, generally several at a time and to understand and relate to theories behind several related concepts. Must be able to remember task/assignment given to self and others over long periods of time.<br>
<br>
The noise level in the work environment is moderate to loud.<br>
<br>
<b>MINIMUM QUALIFICATIONS</b><br>
<br>
<b>Knowledge, Skills &amp; Abilities </b><b>:</b> Knowledge of the techniques and methods used in the care of turf and shrubs; familiarity with the operation of mechanical and heavy equipment; vocational training in soils and/or tree management work desirable; working knowledge in budget preparation, purchasing and contract administration; excellent supervisory skills; ability to develop a cemetery facilities maintenance program in meeting seasonal deadlines; ability to apply common sense understanding to carry out instructions furnished in written, oral or diagrammatic form; ability to work in varying weather conditions including heat, cold, rain and snow; ability to work independently; ability to recruit, train and supervise cemetery employees; ability to plan, supervise and implement a pesticide spraying program in accordance with State regulations; ability to foster relationships and deal effectively with officials, business owners, outside agencies, employees, media, and the general public; ability to assign and schedule the work of the division in order to meet objectives.<br>
<br>
<b>Education &amp; Experience</b><b>:</b> Associates degree in facilities management, horticulture or a related field, and at least 5 years employment in the maintenance of buildings, parks, recreation, construction and/or preventative maintenance with at least two (2) years in a supervisory capacity; or high school diploma or equivalent (GED) with 7 years employment specializing in landscape planning, horticulture or some closely related field, with at least two (2) years in a supervisory capacity. Any equivalent combination of education and experience. Must possess a valid driver's license. Must be computer proficient with the ability to learn software programs specific to the job. Spraying license as a Grounds Supervisor is desirable.<br>
<br>
