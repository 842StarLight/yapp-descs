<p><b>
SUMMARY</b></p>
<p>
The Marketing Program Management Coordinator is responsible for coordinating and implementing marketing communication projects under the direct supervision of the Sr. VP of Marketing/Communications. He/she organizes public image campaigns and special events and participates in creating brand awareness through advertising and social media.</p>
<p><b>
 PRIMARY RESPONSIBILITIES</b></p>
<p>
Arranges proposals and presentations using marketing resource materials such as brochures, data, slides, photographs, and reports. Prepares status reports on marketing efforts by communicating with appropriate teams throughout the company. May liaise with the media, advertising agencies and public relations firms. Develops sales presentations and provides reports based on information collected such as marketing trends, competition, new products, and pricing. Maintains promotional materials inventory. Plans and keeps track of meetings, client events and trade shows. Maintains marketing related databases and prepares relevant reports. Co-ordinates social media and web updates. Co-ordinates involvement in conferences, exhibitions and marketing seminars. Responsible for data extraction, list or lead generation, or evaluating effectiveness of marketing campaigns. Manages subordinate staff in the day-to-day performance of their job. All other duties as assigned.</p>
<p><b>
QUALIFICATIONS</b></p>
<p>
To perform this job successfully, an individual must be able to perform each essential duty satisfactorily. The requirements listed below are representative of the knowledge, skill, and/or ability required. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions. Bachelor's degree (B. A.) from four-year college or university; or four to six years related experience and/or training; or equivalent combination of education and experience.</p>
<p>
Benefits include competitive salary, bonus plan, medical, dental, vision, group life &amp; long term care insurance, 401(k), tuition reimbursement program, and health club allowance.</p>
