<b>
Description</b><div>
</div>
<div>
Dow has an opening for a Senior Tax Accountant located in Bay City, Michigan. The Senior Tax Accountant is responsible to ensure all tax returns, tax provisions, and tax audit activities are carried out flawlessly. In addition, this role is responsible for developing and implementing strategic insights and recommendations to maximize shareholder value , ensuring the integrity of global financial reporting.</div>
<br>
<div>
</div>
<div>The Senior Tax Accountant analyses financial information required to prepare and account for federal, state, and local income tax returns. The main goals for this position are:</div>
<ul>
<li>Accurately and efficiently analyze U.S. and foreign income tax effects of U.S. subsidiaries’ activity and ensure proper reporting on the U.S. federal and state income tax returns of The Dow Chemical Company.
</li>
<li>Assist and support accounting policies and procedures of the Dow Chemical Company in accordance with US GAAP requirements and guidance.
</li>
<li>Support tax planning projects by providing accurate and timely analysis of transactions
</li>
<li>Provide audit support for the successful defense of tax positions that the company has taken and transactions that the company has concluded.</li>
</ul>
<br>
<div></div>
<div><b>
Core Responsibilities:</b></div>
<div></div>
<br>
<div>
Working with a high performance cross-functional team environment, providing mentoring support and hands-on management of all local tax issues, appeals, compliance, and legislative tax policy while simultaneously serving as the primary communication link to both internal and external clients.</div>
<ul>
<li>Understanding and interpretation of Local Tax Legislation, Dow Accounting
</li>
<li>Policies and Procedures and GAAP on all business activities.
</li>
<li>Ensuring the integrity of global financial information.
</li>
<li>Review, reconcile and liaise with tax accounting for the completion of timing ledger reconciliations and the overall timing ledger rollforward for the U.S. subsidiaries included in the consolidated group.
</li>
<li>Prepare and/or review income tax returns for federal, state (including franchise tax returns) and local jurisdictions.
</li>
<li>Calculate and source the Earnings &amp; Profits for U.S. and foreign subsidiaries. In addition to tax concepts, this requires an understanding of US GAAP and the underlying business purpose of the companies.</li>
<li>
Proficiency of CorpTax, the U.S. tax compliance preparation software.
</li>
<li>Tax calculations and documentation for quarterly tax and accounting accruals.
</li>
<li>Respond to audit inquiries from the IRS, state tax authorities, internal auditing, and external auditors.
</li>
<li>Understand, interpret and apply federal, state and local income tax law to the operations of Dow Chemical Company and its subsidiaries.
</li>
<li>Support Tax Planning, M&amp;A and other projects by providing analysis of company activity within area of expertise.
</li>
<li>Developing leveraging on functional competencies.
</li>
<li>The scope of this role is for a large to moderately sized geography or business.
</li>
<li>Must demonstrate technical Tax Accounting competencies
</li>
<li>Must demonstrate initiative and a certain degree of leadership within role
</li>
<li>Able to identify root causes of issues and discover/implement practical solutions
</li>
<li>Open to and proposes new ideas to improve existing work processes
</li>
<li>Challenges status quo in processes
</li>
<li>Helps to drive change to reduce complexity
</li>
<li>Uses customer/client information to improve work processes. Recognizes cross-functional as well as cross-Finance impacts and takes action to address needs.
</li>
<li>Acts as a Tax employee mentor or coach.</li>
</ul>
