<div><b>
Description</b></div>
<div>
Under the direction of the Engineering Team Leader, the Project Engineer executes the engineering and design requirements to furnish the deliverables set forth in the sales contract. The Project Engineer defines the systems and the equipment described in the sales order; and ensures that Vilter compression systems comply with and function to customer specifications and applicable codes.<br>
<br>
Success in this position requires the candidate to work effectively in both a collaborative and independent environment, must be safety conscious, good communication skills is a must both internally and externally with customers, high attention to detail is critical and have a strong motivation for delivering project objectives</div>
<div></div>
<br>
<div><b>
Responsibilities</b></div>
<div>
The Project Engineer's responsibilities may include, but are not limited to:</div>
<ul>
<li>
Reviews compression system design for compliance with engineering principles, company standards, contract requirements, and related codes and specifications.</li>
<li>
Consults with sales to determine degree of compliance with customer specifications and design requirements.</li>
<li>
Reviews and interprets codes and standards (NFPA, AWS, IBC, etc. ) for system compliance.</li>
<li>
Manage project design engineering and engineering support for packaged system orders from inception to completion including field start-up support.</li>
<li>
Make system component selections while generating a project Piping and Instrumentation Diagram (P&amp;ID).</li>
<li>
Review and approve production drawings, adding recommendations to enhance design, reduce costs or increase production efficiency.</li>
<li>
Contribute as a technical resource to the shop, customers, sales, and other departments to address and resolve questions and problems.</li>
<li>
Confer with vendors to investigate sources of special equipment, material or devices. Obtain quotes and prepare requisitions for same.</li>
<li>
Participates on cross functional teams to address new design issues and resolve problems to ensure the continuous, on-going improvement of processes and methods.<br>
</li>
</ul>
