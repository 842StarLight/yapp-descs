<h2>Staff Program Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>Role Overview:
</p>
<p>The Program Manager manages the design and commercialization aspects of key new products with responsibility for driving collaboration of cross-functional product commercial teams. Acts as the internal liaison between engineering, marketing, manufacturing, and RA/QA for the purpose of scheduling projects, driving the timely launch of projects, budgeting resources, and reporting on the progress of several projects that are simultaneously being prepared for commercialization.
</p>
<p>Additionally, the Program Manager engages externally in conjunction with engineering, marketing, manufacturing, RA/QA and sales management, on an as needed basis, to support innovative commercialization efforts.
</p>
<p>The Program Manager has no direct reports but will, on a specific project basis, direct efforts of engineers, technicians, designers and outside consultants. The ability to organize, lead and motivate people toward the achievement of a common goal is a primary requirement for the position. Deals with all members of product development team internally and with the medical community, as well as vendors and consultants externally.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>Specifically, this role manages projects for robotics and surgical enabling technology programs, which includes management of all development work related to software, hardware, packaging and labelling development.
</p>
<p>The project manager provides project management perspective and considers impact of decisions on schedule and budget.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>Responsibilities:
</p>
<ul>
<li>Core team leader/project manager of significant and complex strategic projects. Direct responsibility for delivering key focus products on time, within budget and with minimal long term sustaining engineering required (30%)
</li>
<li>Interfaces with all levels of staff and management in the negotiation and exchange of information. Develops and cultivates cross-departmental relationships. Collaborates with key project sponsors in bringing together a full range of cross-functional expertise and competencies necessary for the successful commercialization of targeted projects. Develops and maintains detailed project schedules and budgets for assigned projects. Drives the tactical project execution. Routinely reports on the status of the project relative to the schedule and budgets. (25%)
</li>
<li>Contributes to the Design History File (DHF). Ensures a quality focus and customer needs are met. This includes participating in design control discussions including requirements development, risk management, product design reviews, engineering cadaver labs, software bug resolution meetings, verification &amp; validation planning, and design transfer planning. (20%)
</li>
<li>Participates in Engineering Design Reviews, Vendor Review Meetings and other cross-functional meetings, as needed. Documents the proceedings and follows up on any incomplete action items (10%)
</li>
<li>Assists in revising existing R&amp;D policies, procedures and processes. Participates in writing new procedures, where appropriate. (5%)
</li>
<li>Assists in the creation of accurate forecasts for products and works cross-functionally to ensure the delivery of those forecasts. (5%)
</li>
<li>May supervise tasks of members of project team where appropriate and when needed. (5%)
