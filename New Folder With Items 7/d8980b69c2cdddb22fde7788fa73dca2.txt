<h2>Inventory Coordinator</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><b>JOB SUMMARY</b></p>
<p>Hydra-Flex is looking for an inventory coordinator who is detail-oriented, exceptionally organized, able to manage complex systems, has excellent communication, math and analytical skills, and possesses relevant computer software know-how. Inventory coordinators can expect to work with associated documentation, complete tasks alone and in teams, manage multiple inventories, and report to and advise company management.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Successful inventory coordinators should have at least five years’ experience in inventory roles, excellent time-management skills and knowledge in organizational techniques. Ideal candidates will also be able to work extended hours if needed and have experience in building strong working relationships.</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>PRIMARY RESPONSIBILITIES</b></p>
<p>1. Own material flow both physically and electronically through the company product line build stations, from incoming parts through outgoing finished goods.</p>
<p>2. Work with team to ensure inventory accuracy through process development and training.</p>
<p>3. Ensure that inventory cycle counts are timely, accurate, thorough and properly managed.</p>
<p>4. Analyze inventory transactions to facilitate root cause investigation on inventory discrepancies.</p>
<p>5. Work with cross functional teams to perform root cause analysis and implement corrective action.</p>
<p>6. Create methods for effective and accurate put away of production inventory that may include incoming and outbound staging areas, use of priority lanes, Kanban systems.</p>
<p>7. Create and maintain inventory location setup in ERP and audit accuracy and thoroughness. May include Bin-Location audits, inventory identification audits, location identification audits, and corrective action for findings.</p>
<p>8. Develop and maintain effective storage systems for finished goods, continually responding to changes in finished goods targets based on sales volume and customer expectations.</p>
<p>9. Ensure that company safety and risk management procedures pertaining to inventory control are followed.</p>
<p>10. Participate in inventory control kaizen events to improve accuracy and reduce waste, including topics on: storage density, intuitive design and setup, ergonomics, loss and damage prevention.</p>
<p>11. Process any scrap or rework due to Engineering Change Notices.</p>
<p>12. Manage the parts delivery process where inventory parts are requested for non-production purposes such as: Marketing, Engineering, Sales, Quality.</p>
<p>13. Coordinate and transact the disposition of inventory during product phase out initiatives.</p>
<p>14. Assist in preparing reports and communicating results to management.</p>
<p>15. Participate and support Lean initiatives regarding inventory control.</p>
<p>16. Participate and support visual management and barcode initiatives.</p>
<p>17. Participate and support in physical inventory audits.</p>
<p>18. Other duties as assigned.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p><b>KNOWLEDGE AND SKILL REQUIREMENTS</b></p>
<p>1. High school diploma or GED</p>
<p>2. 5 years inventory experience</p>
<p>3. Forklift certification</p>
<p>4. Strong organizational, problem-solving and analytical skills</p>
<p>5. Acute attention to detail</p>
<p>6. Strong interpersonal skills and enjoys working within teams</p>
<p>7. Ability to manage changing priorities with grit and enthusiasm</p>
<p>8. Proven ability to handle multiple projects and meet deadlines</p>
<p>9. Excellent written and verbal communication skills</p>
<p>10. Experience with MS Office Suite and ERP software</p>
<p>11. Ability to understand and follow written and verbal instructions</p>
<p>12. Ability to lift 50 lbs</p>
<p>13. Ability to stand 80% of the time</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p><b>COMPETENCIES</b></p>
<p>1. <b>ORGANIZING, PLANNING &amp; DECISION MAKING</b>: Analyzes problems, identifies issues, establishes priorities and accomplishes outcomes in a timely manner; anticipates needs and develops alternative courses of action in recognition of changing conditions. Uses time efficiently; Develops realistic action plans.</p>
<p>2. <b>KNOWLEDGE AND SKILL</b>: Assesses own strengths and weaknesses; acquires training or skills to apply up-to-date knowledge to achieve results; demonstrates innovativeness in applying leading edge technology and/or new procedures and approaches.</p>
<p>3. <b>JOB COMMITMENT</b>: Willingly accepts responsibility and performs expected and unexpected tasks as needed. Demonstrates initiative and tenacity; Asks for help when needed; Takes independent actions and calculated risks; exerts extra effort when necessary to get the job done. Performs effectively under stressful conditions. Is consistently at work and on time; Ensures work responsibilities are covered when absent.</p>
<p>4. <b>RELATING TO OTHERS</b>: Expresses ideas and concerns clearly and persuasively in positive or negative situations, both orally and in writing. Focuses on solving conflict, not blaming; Listens to others without interrupting; Keeps emotions under control; Remains open to others’ ideas and tries new things. Accepts responsibility for own actions; Follows thru on commitments. Works and interacts with and is supportive of co-workers. Sacrifices self-interest when necessary to accomplish group goals. Keeps others properly informed and solicits opinions and feedback of other team members.</p>
<p>5. <b>EXTERNAL &amp; INTERNAL RELATIONS</b>: Provides guidance and willingly helps and/or acts as a resource in obtaining assistance. Manages difficult or emotional situations; Responds promptly to needs. Interacts appropriately and respectfully with vendors, customers, peers and subordinates. Promotes a positive company image.</p>
<p>6. <b>QUALITY</b>: Performs work consistent with expected standards of performance; exhibits accuracy, thoroughness and effectiveness, etc. in carrying out work duties. Looks for ways to improve and promote quality. Applies feedback to improve performance.</p>
<p>7.<b> LEADERSHIP: </b>Organizes a group of people to achieve a common goal. The ability to influence people and teams.</p>
