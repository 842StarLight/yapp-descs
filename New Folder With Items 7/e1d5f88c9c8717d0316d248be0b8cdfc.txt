<h2>Community Center Coordinator</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Overview
</b></h2>
<div><div><div>
<div>To further the mission of The Salvation Army, the Community Center Coordinator will be responsible for organizing and implementing Salvation Army mission-driven recreational programs and special events; scheduling and coordinating building usage; maintaining operations of building and equipment; facilitating relationships with rental groups and overseeing volunteer groups. This is a full-time regular position with an expectation of working (40) forty hours per week.</div>
</div></div></div>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Responsibilities
</b></h2>
<div><div><div>
<div>This job description is not all inclusive. It is intended to identify the essential duties of the position. Other duties, responsibilities and tasks may be assigned by corps officer/administrator.</div>
<ul>
<li>Maintain positive work atmosphere by behaving and communicating in a manner that fosters good relationships with co-workers and supervisors. This would include, but not be limited to such actions: resolution of conflicts in a professional manner; courteous treatment of staff, visitors and clients; respect of others’ property and person, professional and appropriate communication to and about co-workers, supervisors, and subordinates.</li>
<li>Support mission of The Salvation Army by treating every client and colleague with individual respect and dignity, and without discrimination of any kind.</li>
<li>Foster relationships with youth and families who participate in Community Center programs.</li>
<li>Be knowledgeable about all Salvation Army programs and events, promote them to youth and family participants.</li>
<li>Support the implementation of new initiatives such as: open gym, sports leagues, tournaments, The Salvation Army Outdoors programs, etc.</li>
<li>Develop, plan, implement programs and mediate materials such as calendars, schedules, flyers, and announcements to West 7th Area Community.</li>
<li>Create and maintain a room rental and room reservation database for the facility.</li>
<li>Oversee concession sales, inventory and purchasing.</li>
<li>Coordinate the community center programs and operations as they relate to building usage, equipment and enforcement of policy and procedure.</li>
<li>Maintain security of building, grounds and vehicles.</li>
<li>Drive The Salvation Army vehicles for senior groups, day camps, Northwoods summer camps, and other community center activities.</li>
<li>Facilitate guest relations for rental groups.</li>
<li>Provide direct supervision and scheduling for as-needed and on-site recreational program aides and volunteers as appropriate for open gym hours, rentals and other events.</li>
<li>Assist in recruiting, and training volunteer groups.</li>
<li>Assist with the collaboration with Twin Cities Corps in the development of special events.</li>
<li>Assist with the collaboration with outside organizations and agencies to develop a full-service recreational ministry to West 7th Area Community.</li>
<li>Keep work areas, storage areas and program spaces clean and orderly.</li>
<li>Pick-up food rescue items based on assigned schedule. . Support Kitchen Coordinator in processing these resources.</li>
<li>Monitor and log building usage for statistical purposes.</li>
<li>Report monthly statistics, food rescue logs and financial information as required.</li>
<li>Support annual Red Kettle Campaign by assisting Corps &amp; Community Center Director in fundraising efforts.</li>
<li>Assist with seasonal programs as needed.</li>
<li>All other duties as assigned.</li>
</ul>
</div></div></div>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Qualifications
</b></h2>
<div><div><div><ul>
<li>Associate’s degree in relevant field or two years of experience working in children and youth, adult and older adults recreational programs or ministries.</li>
<li>Ability to work well with families and youth from diverse cultural and socioeconomic groups.</li>
<li>Ability to accept supervision and work with a team of co-workers.</li>
<li>Ability to organize work, set priorities, meet deadlines, and complete tasks with a minimum of direction.</li>
<li>Must have excellent oral, written, and interpersonal communication skills.</li>
<li>Demonstrates mature judgment, motivation and initiative.</li>
<li>Ability to maintain confidentiality.</li>
<li>Demonstrates strong individual and group leadership abilities.</li>
<li>Must be willing to work weekdays, evenings and occasional Saturdays.</li>
<li>Ability to use Salvation Army approved word processing, spreadsheet, data base management, and graphics/desktop publishing software packages.</li>
<li>Valid Driver's License. (Must meet the guidelines provided by the State of residence and the automobile insurance provider as pertaining to corps’ passenger vans/buses.)</li>
<li>Must have a valid Commercial Driver’s License (CDL) with a class B Passenger Endorsement or be able to obtain within the first 90 days of employment.</li>
<li>Must pass The Salvation Army background checks and meet the MVR driver’s qualifications.</li>
<li>Must be willing to support the mission of The Salvation Army.</li>
<li>It must be understood by the incumbent that the provisions of Safe From Harm will apply upon commencement of employment and also understood that the required Safe From Harm training be satisfactorily completed within the first thirty (30) days of employment.</li>
<li>Must have current CPR and First Aid certification or be able to obtain within 90 days of employment.</li>
<li>Must be trained in The Salvation Army Outdoors program or able to obtain within 90 days of employment.</li>
</ul></div></div></div>
