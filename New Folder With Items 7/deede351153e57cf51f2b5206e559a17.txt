<h2>Principal Administrative Analyst</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<div>
<b>Responsibilities</b><p></p>
<p>
Center Administration</p>
<ul>
<li>Identify, establish, and implement internal operating systems and procedures to ensure smooth operation of the Center:</li>
<li>Organize and staff regular meetings of the IDDRC Leadership Team drawn from the directors of programs that utilize the Center. The Leadership Team is responsible for developing and implementing academic activities within the IDDRC, including reviewing research protocols, mentoring early-stage investigators, fostering training opportunities, assessing innovation, and assessing, assigning and monitoring the collaborative use of IDDRC space and other shared resources.</li>
<li>Knowledgeably carry out IDDRC Leadership Team’s recommendations.</li>
<li>Establish and implement systems to efficiently and equitably share 23 interview rooms, 5 medical exam rooms, 4 EEG labs, 3 large conference rooms, and 3 discussion rooms among 15-20 research groups conducting either neuropsychiatric human phenotyping studies or research centered on quantitative methodologies.</li>
<li>Develop and manage cost-sharing plans for shared Center resources (e.g., equipment, personnel) and databases to monitor usage of these resources.</li>
<li>In collaboration with Center research groups, manage operation and supply of mail/copier rooms, break rooms, and waiting rooms.</li>
<li>In collaboration with Center research groups, organize participation in existing seminar and lecture series.Coordinate Center affinity groups and seminar series.</li>
<li>In collaboration with Center research groups, assist with grant and contract submissions, and promote smooth integration of Center investigators with grants management and other administrative personnel.</li>
<li>In collaboration with Center research groups, assist with submission and tracking of IRB/ IACUC submissions.</li>
<li>Assist with other research center logistical support tasks as needed.</li>
<li>Serve as Center liaison with Semel Institute and UCLA administrative and service offices.</li>
</ul>
<div>
Center Finances<br>
<ul>
<li>Manage the application of the sales and service account for the Microscopy Core and Single Cell RNA Core for the IDDRC.</li>
<li>Responsible for approving new account requests from different investigators interested in using IDDRC services. This includes confirming requisite approvals for those projects/accounts and obtaining fund manager approval for the use of specified account numbers.</li>
<li>Responsible for the timely billing of all Cores, which includes proper record keeping of various accounts from different investigators and the ability to use database search fields for running monthly reports of scan totals.</li>
<li>Responsible for troubleshooting errors.</li>
<li>Responsible for proper record keeping and updating of encumbrances after each billing cycle, where charges are deducted and encumbrances fluctuate with each payment and each additional encumbrance request.</li>
<li>Responsible for any amendments to the Sales and Service applications, as well as annual renewals.</li>
<li>Track any major income and expense differences, as compared to the original budget, and notify the Center Director of these differences. Recommend action when necessary.</li>
<li>Responsible for the financial management of the Center Director’s annual budgets.</li>
<li>Prepare annual salary negotiations for all IDDRC investigators</li>
<li>Assist departmental staff in acquiring proper fund manager approvals.</li>
<li>Act as the liaison between the Center Director and other campus or external contacts in terms of the Director’s financial matters.</li>
<li>Review the Center Director’s financial accounts to ensure proper closing of funds and timely activation of new funds.</li>
<li>Resolve any financial errors on the Center Director’s accounts with the proper entities.</li>
<li>Assist the Lab Managers of the IDDRC with financial management system and resolve any questions about account errors and balances through research with the available campus financial systems and/or inquiries to departmental fund managers.</li>
<li>Under the direction of the Center Director, assist the Lab Managers in all instances of purchases, reimbursements, and all other financial matters, in terms of proper account use and distribution of expenses.</li>
<li>Act as the liason for salary negotiations and changes for Faculty members with Joint Appointments in other areas.</li>
<li>Coordinate with the other departments, including but not limited to IDP, Neurology, etc. in the transfer of appropriate funds</li>
</ul>
</div>
<ul>
<li>Manage budgets including Center administrative budget and shared financial resources.
</li>
<li>Generate, analyze, and interpret monthly Fund Balance Reports; project expenditures and resolve potential overdrafts or underexpenditures.</li>
<li>Process LVOs, Purchase Requisitions, Travel Express, PAC, and other campus purchasing mechanisms.</li>
<li>Research and identify problem invoices, and maintain open liens.</li>
<li>Review PAN, NPEAR reports, Queue, and H/I status invoices and take appropriate action in a timely manner.</li>
</ul>
<div>
Academic Personnel<br>
<ul>
<li>Act as the liason between Faculty and Semel Academic Personnel regarding issues with joint appointments and merit advancement</li>
<li>Assist in preparation of Joint Appointment paperwork for faculty</li>
<li>Assist in preparation of Merit Advancement paperwork for Junior Faculty</li>
</ul>
<br>
Other Organizational and Administrative Duties<br>
<ul>
<li>Manage offices and all space related to the IDDRC, which includes overseeing renovations and any necessary improvements.</li>
<li>Resolve any conflicts that come up with the online scheduling system and the online website/database for Microscopy Core Services.</li>
<li>Serve as a liaison between the Center Director and the website programmers and/or coordinators to resolve issues or make necessary improvements to the website and scheduling system.</li>
<li>Supervise the Lab Manager(s) of the IDDRC</li>
<li>Perform administrative duties, such as copying, mailing materials, answering calls, and filing.</li>
<li>Participate in coordination meetings with various contacts, including meetings with the Center Director, new IDDRC investigators, and UCLA departmental staff.</li>
<li>Develop advertising or informational material for Sales and Service accounts as needed.</li>
<li>Create draft website pages for the website programmer, as needed.</li>
<li>Maintain all various paperwork (i.e. purchasing, website-related items) in an efficient record-keeping system.</li>
<li>Respond to customers’ inquiries and concerns, which may include updating the information available on the website.</li>
<li>Assist the Lab Managers in IDDRC in space management and over-arching issues with the functionality of the Center.</li>
<li>Grant access to Microscopy suite and if necessary, coordinate with DGSOM Facilities to request after-hours/weekend access to Semel</li>
<li>Coordinate bi-annual All Users Town Hall Meeting</li>
<li>Meet weekly with Lab Manager(s) to discuss issues related to project accounts and any equipment and supply replacement/repairs needed.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div>
Center Grant Submission and Reporting<br>
<ul>
<li>In collaboration with Center research groups personnel, with particular attention to opportunities for collaborative submissions:</li>
<li>Assist in planning and preparation of research funding proposals and submissions.</li>
<li>Support preparation of non-scientific aspects of proposals and edit other aspects.</li>
<li>Ensure adherence to both UCLA policies and funding agency (e.g., NIH, NSF, DOE, State of California, private foundations) guidelines.</li>
<li>Assist with preparation and submission of progress reports for existing and continuing grants to funding agencies including independently preparing non-scientific aspects and editing drafts. This includes formulating budgets, budget justifications, all general paperwork pages, formatting pages and text limitations to adhere to agency policies, and any other needs that should arise.</li>
<li>Prepare paperwork for the Office of Contracts and Grants Administration, such as EPASS, 700 forms, and any other forms needed.</li>
<li>Attend all research and grant-related trainings or informational sessions provided by the campus and OCGA to update proposal preparers.</li>
<li>Maintain proper files of grant submissions and all correspondence.</li>
<li>Prepare for review any necessary correspondence for Principal Investigators to funding agencies and campus entities regarding grant submissions, continuations, progress reports, re-budgeting requests, and carryover requests.</li>
</ul>
<br>
Center Personnel<br>
<ul>
<li>With Center Director and other Center faculty, identify shared staffing needs.</li>
<li>Prepare job descriptions, submit requests for job postings and advertisements, coordinate candidates’ interviews, and complete hiring process for open positions.</li>
<li>In conjunction with Semel Human Resources submit personnel paperwork for hiring, resignation, layoff, change in status, change in funding, and change in effort.</li>
<li>Process visa applications through Semel Human Resources and/or Semel Office of Education department, when necessary.</li>
<li>Establish independent contracts where appropriate.</li>
<li>Monitor and approve timesheet submissions for shared staff.</li>
<li>Assist with any changes made to employment distributions and title changes</li>
</ul>

<b>
Qualifications</b><p></p>
<p>
Demonstrated ability and experience in financial management.<br>
Demonstrated ability to conduct organizational and statistical analyses and research.<br>
Knowledge of sponsored research policies, procedures and funding sources.<br>
Skill in analyzing information, problems, situations, practices or procedures to: define the problem or objective; identify relevant concerns or factors; identify patterns, tendencies and relationships; formulate logical and objective conclusions; and recognize alternatives and their implications.<br>
Skill in managing a complex workload and ability to plan and prioritize competing deadlines.<br>
Demonstrated ability to manage office services.<br>
Demonstrated ability to communicate effectively in writing and orally to a diverse constituency.<br>
Ability to design and implement business computing systems.Ability to communicate requirements accurately to programming staff.<br>
Understanding of research standards, models, and practices.<br>
Skill in working as a part of a team and collaborating with colleagues.<br>
Skill in writing job descriptions.<br>
Ability to accurately track financial information.<br>
Knowledge and skill in working with UCLA policies, particularly in UCLA Accounting / Extramural Fund Management as related to sales and service applications, interdepartmental recharges, and post-award spending.<br>
Knowledge of the full range of logistical services required to sustain academic and business functions.<br>
Demonstrated supervisory skills to perform hiring, training, and corrective functions.<br>
Demonstrated excellence in attention to detail, time management, and planning and executing center projects, events, and development.<br>
Demonstrated writing ability to generate clear, concise and logical memoranda, correspondence and other materials. Skill in using word processing software.<br>
Demonstrated ability to analyze administrative needs and to develop organizational strategies to address these needs.<br>
Demonstrated ability to independently organize and prioritize tasks in order to effectively manage a diverse and shifting workload.<br>
Demonstrated ability to perform detailed work with a high degree of accuracy.<br>
Demonstrated ability to track progress of projects to ensure timely completion.<br>
Strong communication and interpersonal skills to develop and maintain professional cooperative working relationships with Center staff and faculty colleagues and personnel in other campus departments, as well as with personnel in funding agencies and vendor entities.<br>
Ability to effectively delegate and supervise within a team-effort milieu.<br>
Working understanding of generally accepted accounting principals, policies and procedures.<br>
Demonstrated ability to interpret, analyze, and evaluate financial information, to monitor and project performance, to develop and manage complex budgets, and to prepare and explain financial reports.<br>
Working knowledge of UCLA Purchasing and Accounting Systems, Financial Systems, and Payroll/Personnel Systems.<br>
Skill in utilizing UCLA on-line financial reporting systems, BruinBuy, Travel Express, PAC (purchasing and accounts payable) and QDB (query data base) to perform financial analysis.<br>
Skill in preparing and processing UCLA forms including, but not limited to, purchase requisition, department deposit record, departmental repair authorization, travel expense voucher, check request, limited value order and interdepartmental recharge request.<br>
Working knowledge of federal and non-federal contract and grant policies and procedures.<br>
Demonstrated ability to interpret, explain, and apply funding agency and University policies and procedures regarding contracts and grants administration.<br>
Working knowledge of student fellowship policies and procedures.<br>
Working knowledge of visa policies and procedures.<br>
Demonstrated strong skills in using Microsoft Access, Word, and Excel to create financial worksheets, maintain database files, create correspondence, and prepare reports.<br>
Demonstrated excellent written and verbal communication skills.</p>
