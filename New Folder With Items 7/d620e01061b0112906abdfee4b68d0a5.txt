<h2>Procurement Specialist</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader">
<b>Overview / Responsibilities</b><b>
</b>
</h2>
<div><div><div>
<div>Wood is currently seeking a <b>Procurement Specialist</b> to support their operations in Tucker, GA. U.S. Only: Authorization to work permanently in the U.S. without sponsorship required. Heavy experience in power &amp; energy/renewables EPC/EPCM projects a must. May perform executive level job duties, such as establish and carry out departmental or organizational goals, policies, and procedures, direct and oversee an organization's financial and budgetary activities, consult with other executives, staff, and board members about general operations, analyze financial statements, sales reports, and other performance indicators, identify key strategic opportunities to improve organizational/cross-company performance, policies, and programs. This role will be based in Tucker, GA (Atlanta).<br>
</div>
<div></div>
<div><b>General Summary:</b></div>
<div></div>
<br>
<div>
This position is responsible for effectively and professionally conducting and coordinating the activities associated with purchasing materials and/or equipment. The incumbent has thorough knowledge and understanding of procurement concepts, principles and practices, and the techniques of ensuring task completion, project delivery processes, and supplier relationship expectations. The incumbent understands the role of procurement and of this position in the project delivery process, understands the integration of procurement with other project functions, accomplishes value-added expectations, and performs assignments in accordance with established policies and procedures. The incumbent has advanced knowledge of a subject area or commodity.<br>
</div>
</div></div></div>
<div></div>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader">
<b>Skills / Qualifications</b><b>
</b>
</h2>
<div><div><div>
<div>
<div><p><b>Essential Duties &amp; Responsibilities:</b></p></div>
<br>
<div><div>
<p>
Role:
</p>
<ul>
<li>Coordinate the procurement of goods and administer assigned purchase orders to accomplish project or group objectives and obligations.</li>
<li>
May serve as the lead buyer or purchasing coordinator on medium to large size projects or tasks and may act as a subject matter expert in regard to a commodity or specialized subject area.
</li>
<li>Purchasing assignments typically will consist of major purchases of complex, critical or high-risk materials and equipment, or independently handle all purchases on a small project.</li>
<li>
Prepare bidder lists, qualify bidders, prepare requests for quotation, and conduct bid evaluations.</li>
<li>
Recommend source selections, plan and lead purchase order negotiations, and prepare and issue purchase orders.</li>
<li>
Obtain necessary reviews and approvals of bidder exceptions, bid evaluations, recommendations, purchase orders, and change orders prior to execution.</li>
<li>
Utilize established policies, procedures, systems, and tools to assure maximum contribution.
</li>
<li>Evaluate facts surrounding purchase order-related problem areas, determine the most appropriate remedial actions, and implement such actions through available means.</li>
<li>
Administer assigned purchase orders, including change order processing, ensuring compliance with contractual terms and applicable regulations, invoice processing and verification, and performance evaluation; ensuring supplier compliance in a cost-effective, timely, and responsive manner.
</li>
<li>Identify and advise management of purchase order-related risks.</li>
<li>
Development of potential and qualified suppliers, including performance history.</li>
<li>
Maintain purchase order and purchasing process documentation.</li>
<li>
Preparation of proposal deliverables, such as pricing of materials, labor estimates, and narratives.</li>
<li>
Assist Supply Chain Management in providing other support services as requested.</li>
</ul>
</div></div>
<br>
<div><div>
<p>
Accountability:</p>
<ul>
<li>
Accountable to the assigned Procurement Manager.</li>
<li>
Timeliness, accuracy, and adequacy of the purchasing activities.</li>
<li>
Fulfillment of the purchase order requirements.</li>
<li>
Effective management of change activity to protect the company from claims.</li>
<li>
Timeliness, accuracy, and adequacy of purchase order-related problem identification, status reporting, and corrective action.</li>
<li>
Effectiveness of relationships with suppliers, management, clients, and support organizations.</li>
<li>
Maintain an understanding of business trends, industry issues and challenges, and supply market trends, and the implications on the area of assignment.</li>
<li>
Determine effective methods of delivering desired results.
</li>
<li>Assurance of the quality of tasks performed and outputs provided.</li>
<li>
Management of procurement-related risks for assigned areas of responsibility.</li>
<li>
Compliance with established policies and procedures.</li>
</ul>
<p>
Relationships:</p>
<ul>
<li>
Reports to the appropriate Project Supply Chain Manager or other assigned Supply Chain Manager for performance of work and achieving objectives.
</li>
<li>Works under minimal supervision. No direct reports. Supervises the work of less senior buyers and procurement support personnel who assist with the purchasing activities.</li>
<li>
Coordinates with project or departmental personnel in performance of the purchasing activities.</li>
</ul>
<p>
Authority:</p>
<ul>
<li>
Commits, spends and controls client and company dollars within limits established in company policies or procedures.
</li>
<li>Makes decisions within the scope of procedures and assures compliance with applicable supply chain policies and procedures.</li>
</ul>
<p>
Project Delivery Knowledge:</p>
<ul><li>
Understands the project delivery processes as used for various contracting scopes (i.e. design, consulting, design-build, program management, construction management), and their relationship to Enterprise Procurement procedures.</li></ul>
<p>
Resources/Capabilities</p>
<ul>
<li>
Understands major technical, organizational, managerial, and business issues related to operations management.</li>
<li>
Understands and experienced in commercial procurement, contract laws, practices and standards as they relate to Wood operations management and project delivery.</li>
</ul>
<p>
Communications:</p>
<ul>
<li>
Has good communications and writing skills.</li>
<li>
Must be able to communicate effectively with suppliers, project teams, clients, and others to organize and motivate achievement.</li>
</ul>
<p>
Systems:</p>
<ul><li>
Uses personal computer in support of electronic mail, and standard Microsoft Office software.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Familiar with the capabilities of a materials management system, and uses it effectively for performing and coordinating the purchasing activities.</li></ul>
</div></div>
</div>

<div>
<div><p><b>
Education, Experience &amp; Certifications:</b></p></div>
<br>
<div><div>
<p>
Education:</p>
<ul><li>
Degree in Business, Law, Engineering or other applicable field, with training in Purchasing preferred. Experience in lieu of a degree is acceptable.</li></ul>
<p>
Experience:</p>
<ul>
<li>
Minimum of 10 years of purchasing experience, including soliciting, negotiating, drafting, formulating, and administering purchase orders.</li>
<li>
Some experience in supervising procurement staff preferred.
</li>
</ul>
<p>Certifications/Registrations:</p>
<ul><li>
None required. A.P.P., C.P.M., CPSM or CSM Certification from the Institute for Supply Management (or other international equivalent) is desirable.</li></ul>
