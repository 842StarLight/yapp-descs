<h2>Reverse Logistics Processor</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<div>
<div>As a leading worldwide provider of navigation devices and wearable technology, our focus is on developing, designing and supporting superior products. Our advanced technology promotes performance, safety and ease of use in every market we serve. That makes us an engineering and technology company with a keen focus on recruiting talent in those fields. But the same vertical integration business model that keeps all design and manufacturing processes in-house also gives us the incentive to hire top performers from different backgrounds, including operations, finance and accounting, information technology, sales, marketing and communications.</div>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<div>
In operations and facilities, we literally do it all…in-house. We are looking for skilled, energetic individuals who want to join our quality, international shipping, manufacturing, office services, planning, purchasing, receiving, returns and warehouse teams. These associates do everything from delivering packages and purchasing supplies to assembling aviation panel mounts and ensuring our large shipments of goods are exported according to applicable international laws. Together, they proactively support associates and customers alike. If you’re ready to work – and have fun, too – come, help us put the pieces together!</div>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<div>
When Garmin products come back home, our reverse logistics team makes sure everything goes smoothly. It’s not just processing consumer and retail returns; there’s also reboxing new and newly overhauled units for return to stock, making sure everything matches the Bill of Materials and using engineering change orders as needed.</div>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<div>
We are looking for a full-time reverse logistics processor for our headquarters in Olathe, Kansas. In this role you will be responsible for processing credits toward customer accounts for returned items and receiving product into Oracle and MS Access. Other essential functions include:</div>
<div><ul>
<li><div>
Re-Route customer repair RMA’s, aviation units and other receipts to appropriate areas</div></li>
<li><div>
Perform data entry of inventory transactions accurately</div></li>
<li><div>
Rebox and weigh new and newly-overhauled units for return to stock</div></li>
<li><div>
Sort, box and weigh accessories for return to stock</div></li>
<li><div>
Prioritize workload using First In, First Out (FIFO)</div></li>
<li><div>
Perform Oracle transactions accurately to ensure proper inventory count</div></li>
<li><div>
Pick accessories as needed from stock for use in the rebox process</div></li>
<li><div>
Perform cycle counts of inventory as required</div></li>
<li><div>
Work closely with Engineers and Planners preparing shop orders and other special projects as assigned</div></li>
<li><div>
Operate RF sealer and standard test equipment</div></li>
</ul></div>
<div>
Due to the amount of resources and expense exhausted in training for this position, a minimum of one year from the start of this position will be required before an associate is eligible for a job transfer.</div>
</div>
<p></p>
<div>
<b>
Qualifications</b><div>
</div>
<div>
Qualified Candidates possess a High School Diploma or GED and the ability to lift up to 55 pounds to effectively perform the essential functions of this job description. Other requirements include:</div>
<div><ul>
<li>
Requires the ability to stand, sit, and/or twist for extended periods of time</li>
<li>
Requires the ability to visually distinguish minor cosmetic imperfections and defects on Garmin products</li>
<li>
Must be detail-oriented and have the ability to maintain accuracy in a fast paced environment</li>
<li>
Ability to prioritize, handle multiple tasks, and remain flexible to meet department demand</li>
</ul></div>
<div>
Previous experience using Oracle and prior reverse logistics work experience in a large warehouse environment are highly preferred!</div>
