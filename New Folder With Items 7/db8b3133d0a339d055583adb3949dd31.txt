<h2>Transmission System Planning Project Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div>
<p><b>Description</b></p>
<p>The Transmission System Planning Project Manager should have a deep and broad base of knowledge regarding electric transmission systems and transmission system planning processes. The candidate will perform transmission planning studies consisting of power flow, transient stability, transfer capability, and economic dispatch associated with congestion management scenarios. The candidate will be responsible for planning, scheduling, conducting, and coordinating work efforts with the project manager. Study efforts will require judgment in the independent evaluation, selection, adaptation and modification of standard engineering techniques for electric transmission system planning procedures and criteria. The candidate will perform work which involves conventional engineering practices, but may involve a variety of complex features in regards to electrical system power flows and stability. The candidate will be responsible for teaching and training less experienced engineers with regards to transmission planning studies and evaluations. Other duties as assigned.</p>
<p><b>Qualifications</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Minimum Education: Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering, advanced degree is a plus (MSEE, MBA, or PhD)</li>
<li>Minimum of 7 years of experience with a broad range of transmission system studies</li>
<li>Experience with multiple power system planning, studies, and operations such as transient stability, short circuit, and voltage analysis; NERC, FERC, and Regional requirements, policies and procedures; production costing; risk assessment techniques; TPL compliance; generator interconnection; and harmonics analysis.</li>
<li>Strong computer skills using industry software such as PSS/E, PSLF, EMTP/ATP, PSCAD, SKM, ETAP, and Promod</li>
<li>Ability to execute a transmission system planning study with support from other staff with little to no direction</li>
<li>Knowledge of numerous power regions of the country and independent system operators such as: PJM, MISO, NEISO, SPP, CAISO, WECC, SERC, ERCOT, NYISO</li>
<li>Knowledge of NERC and FERC standards</li>
<li>Proven ability to lead, educate, and train a team of engineers and analysts in regards to transmission system planning studies</li>
<li>Strong communication skills (both verbal and written) and presentation skills</li>
<li>General business and financial calculation knowledge</li>
<li>Effective Project Management skills</li>
<li>Strong analytical and critical thinking skills</li>
<li>Ability to work with existing and new clients to identify new projects, articulate to the clients our ability to perform those projects, and translate that into the sale of consulting services</li>
<li>Ability to communicate with various business contacts to understand their needs and translate them into a scope of work that meets their desired outcomes</li>
<li>Ability to perform under pressure and tight deadlines and to be effective in on-site work environments</li>
<li>Ability to manage multiple projects concurrently, including identification of critical path items to ensure deliverable deadlines are met</li>
<li>Ability to focus on the big picture to effectively and efficiently provide services that meet client needs, while also having the ability to identify and analyze details that are critical to project outcomes</li>
<li>Willingness to learn new service offerings as client needs and the market requires changes to the service offerings provided by our group</li>
<li>Willingness and ability to develop a broad set of service offering types to allow for flexibility to go where the work is</li>
<li>Willingness to cross sell other services offered by Burns &amp; McDonnell outside the project manager’s core set of services</li>
<li>Willingness to negotiate with clients for new service agreements</li>
<li>Professional registration preferred</li>
</ul>
