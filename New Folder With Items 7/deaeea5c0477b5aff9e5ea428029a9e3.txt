<h2>Business Development Representative</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div><div>
<div>Heap builds the product analytics tool to automatically capture customer interactions, make sense of them, and make them actionable for anyone. We enable customers like Twilio, Casper, WeWork to understand their millionth customer as well as they understood their first.</div>
<div>
</div>
<div>
We're looking for a Business Development Representative [outbound sales development] who is excited about consultative selling to Enterprise prospects. You will work closely with our marketing and sales teams to identify and engage prospective clients. You will learn about the analytics goals of prospective customers to find out if they are a good fit for Heap. You'll also be an early team member of Heap's London office!</div>
<div>
</div>
<div><b>
At Heap, this means you will:</b></div>
<ul>
<li>
Reach out to new prospects in our target market and get them excited about Heap</li>
<li>
Set meetings with qualified prospects for handoff to Account Executives</li>
<li>
Communicate with AEs and educate them on details of opportunities</li>
<li>
Consistently achieve qualified opportunity and closed business quotas</li>
<li>
Demonstrate the value of Heap through email and phone communication</li>
</ul>
<div><b>
What we're looking for:</b></div>
<ul>
<li>
Previous inbound or outbound sales development experience [1-2 years]</li>
<li>
A strong communicator - Analytics can get overwhelming. Can you empathize with our customers, zero in on their needs, and articulate how Heap satisfies those needs?</li>
<li>
Obsessed with prospect happiness - You will set the stage for an effective sales follow-up.</li>
<li>
A fast learner - You have an analytical mindset and are able to understand and explain a technical product within a matter of weeks.</li>
<li>
A master of email communication - You keep the inbox at zero.</li>
<li>
Comfortable talking to a variety of users - Heap plays a day-to-day role for a wide range of people: engineers, marketers, product managers, designers, data scientists, and CEOs. You’ll interface with some or all of these in an organization to close deals.</li>
</ul>
