<h2>Patient Account Representative I</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">Contributes to the Division of Clinical Operations' financial success by reviewing and processing claims during various stages of the revenue cycle in a timely and compliant manner to achieve the highest reimbursement. As well as confirming that, all operational service commitments are met for assigned responsibilities.
<br><br>
<b>Essential Job Functions:</b><br>
1. Responds to inquiries and resolves discrepancies involving moderately complex financial transactions in a timely manner, requiring a general understanding of work-area policies and procedures.
<br><br>
2. Reviews, follows up, and appeals for aged insurance balance accounts to ensure timely revenue collection through use of EDI clearinghouse, payer portals, worklog and reports for NextGen and/or Axium.
<br><br>
3. Reviews and processes delinquent unpaid accounts by patients accounts eligible for collections.
<br><br>
4. Processes referrals or authorizations as required by payers.
<br><br>
5. Processes claims for multiple payer types (Commercial, Managed Care, Blue Cross, Medicare, Medicaid, etc.).
<br><br>
6. Ensures assigned claims meet clearinghouse and/or payer processing criteria.
<br><br>
7. Ensures appropriate follow-up on assigned work lists.
<br><br>
8. Ensures work meets departmental productivity and quality review standards.
<br><br>
9. Communicates issues regarding claims follow-up process and potential resolutions.
<br><br>
10. Works with payer response reports and rejection reports to meet Departmental Productivity and Quality Review Standards.
<br><br>
11. Complies with federal, state, and company policies, procedures, and regulations.
<br><br>
12. Operates computers programmed with accounting software to record, store, and analyze information.
<br><br>
13. Operates 10-key calculators, typewriters, and copy machines to perform calculations and produce documents.
<br><br>
14. Debits, credits, and totals accounts on computer spreadsheets and databases, using specialized accounting software.
<br><br>
15. Checks figures, postings, and documents for correct entry, mathematical accuracy, and proper codes.
<br><br>
16. Reconciles or notes and reports discrepancies found in records.
<br><br>
17. Resolves discrepancies in accounting records.
<br><br>
18. Accesses computerized financial information to answer general questions as well as those related to specific accounts.
<br><br>
19. Generates reports to ensure timely information is available for management use.
<br><br>
20. Complies with federal, state, and company policies, procedures, and regulations.
<br><br>
21. Completes special projects as assigned.
<br><br>
22. Performs other duties as assigned or required.
<br><br>
<b>Marginal Job Functions:</b><br>
<b>Required Knowledge, Skills and Abilities:</b><br>
<b>Knowledge:</b><br>
1. General knowledge of MS Office Suite (Outlook, Word and Excel).
<br>2. Clerical - General knowledge of administrative and clerical procedures and systems such as word processing, managing files and records, designing forms, and other office procedures and terminology.
<br>3. English Language - General knowledge of the structure and content of the English language including the meaning and spelling of words, rules of composition, and grammar.
<br>4. Customer and Personal Service - General knowledge of principles and processes for providing customer and personal services. This includes needs assessment, meeting quality standards for services, and evaluation of user satisfaction.
<br><br>
<b>Skills:</b><br>
1. Active Listening - Proficient skills in giving full attention to what other people are saying, taking time to understand the points being made, asking questions as appropriate, and not interrupting at inappropriate times.
<br>2. Reading Comprehension - Proficient understanding of written sentences and paragraphs in work-related documents.
<br>3. Speaking - Proficient skills in talking to others to convey information effectively.
<br>4. Critical Thinking - Basic skills in using logic and reasoning to identify the strengths and weaknesses of alternative solutions, conclusions, or approaches to problems.
<br>5. Mathematics - Basic skills in using mathematics to solve problems.
<br><br>
<b>Abilities:</b><br>
1. Problem Sensitivity - The ability to tell when something is wrong or is likely to go wrong. It does not involve solving the problem, only recognizing there is a problem.
<br>2. Number Facility - The ability to add, subtract, multiply, or divide quickly and correctly.
<br>3. Mathematical Reasoning - The ability to choose the right mathematical methods or formulas to solve a problem.
<br>4. Deductive Reasoning - The ability to apply general rules to specific problems to produce answers that make sense.
<br>5. Information Ordering - The ability to arrange things or actions in a certain order or pattern according to a specific rule or set of rules (e.g., patterns of numbers, letters, words, pictures, mathematical operations).
<br><br>
<b>Physical Requirements:</b><br>
1. Speech Recognition - Must be able to identify and understand the speech of another person.
<br>2. Speech Clarity - Must be able to speak clearly so others can understand you.
<br>3. Near Vision - Must be able to see details at close range (within a few feet of the observer).
<br>4. May be exposed to short, intermittent, and/or prolonged periods of sitting and/or standing in performance of job duties.
<br>5. May be required to work nights or weekends.
