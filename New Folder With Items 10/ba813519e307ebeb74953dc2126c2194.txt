<h2>Standardization Instructor Pilot - CH-47F</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><b>GENERAL DESCRIPTION:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Tyonek is seeking applications/resumes for a Standardization Instructor Pilot in anticipation of contract award to perform the following day to day duties:</li>
<li>
Will be required to provide New Equipment Training NET support to the United States Army units and Foreign Military Sales FMS cases receiving, updating or modifying the CH-47F Cargo Helicopter.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>
DUTIES:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Train and evaluate CH-47F Pilots and Instructor Pilots.
</li>
<li>Prepare training for systems academics, flight instruction, simulator support, operation of installed mission equipment, aircraft and team tactical employment, and system sustainment under day, night, night systems, enclosed cockpit, simulated instrument, and simulated combat conditions.
</li>
<li>Manage the team's internal standardization and crewmember training sustainment program similar to Commander's Aircrew Training Program to ensure team members continue to meet aviation qualification, currency, and proficiency requirements.
</li>
<li>Responsible for maintenance and certification of air crewmember training folders; monitoring of currency, medical, and proficiency requirements; and implementation of the no- notice proficiency evaluation program.
</li>
<li>Perform rated crewmember duties, and assisting with training material development and refinement.</li>
<li>
Provide instruction and provide support training activities as part of a team required to travel to unit locations in both CONUS and OCONUS locations on newly applied CH-47F Modification Work Orders MWO and government flight missions.</li>
<li>
Provides flight instruction as an aircraft instructor pilot.
</li>
<li>Provides flight training on procedures, aircraft systems, operational procedures, emergency procedures, malfunction analysis, navigation, aerodynamics, radio operations.
</li>
<li>Conducts hands-on flight exams in aircraft as well as oral and written flight exams.</li>
<li>
Serves as an Instrument instructor examiner provides both inflight instruction and exams along with written and oral instructions and exams.</li>
<li>
Conducts analysis of the CAAS Software Training program and software for the CH-47F Project Office while serving as a member of the Integrated Product Team.
</li>
<li>Performs CAAS Software Training to fielded CH-47F aviation units and responsible for the updates and loading of CAAS Software.</li>
<li>
Performs duties as an academic instructor and instructor/operator on the CH-47F Transportable Flight Proficiency Simulator TFPS.</li>
<li>
Conducts the ferry of helicopters from assigned locations to the assigned destination.
</li>
<li>Evaluated annually to US Government ATM and AR 95-20 standards</li>
<li>
Other duties as assigned</li>
</ul>
<p><b>
EDUCATION/EXPERIENCE REQUIREMENTS:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
FAA Commercial Pilot or Airline Transport Pilot rating</li>
<li>
Rotorcraft helicopter CFI/CFII or DOD rotary wing instructor rating</li>
<li>
Must be able to maintain FAA Class II medical certificate
</li>
<li>Qualified as an Instrument Flight Examiner.</li>
<li>
Minimum 1500 Pilot in Command time. Instrument instructors, additionally, 150 hours minimum of hood, weather and simulator time combined.</li>
<li>
Must be a qualified CH-47F IP/ SP</li>
<li>
Must have or be able to obtain a valid US passport</li>
<li>
Must be able to travel outside the United States</li>
<li>
School trained air and ground Safety Course highly preferred</li>
<li>
Must have a Bachelor's Degree</li>
<li>
Must be a US Citizen</li>
<li>
Must be able to pass a background check, which could include a criminal history and credit check for access to Government facilities.</li>
<li>
Must have or be able to obtain and maintain a Secret Clearance.</li>
<li>
We maintain a drug-free workplace and perform post offer, pre-employment substance abuse testing.</li>
</ul>
