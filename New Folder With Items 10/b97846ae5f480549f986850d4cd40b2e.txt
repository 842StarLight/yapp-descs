<h2>Administrative Assistant II - Parks Department - Limited Term (Open)</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div></div>
<div><div>
<div>
<p>The County of San Mateo Parks Department is seeking an experienced individual for a <b>Limited-Term</b> <b>Administrative Assistant II</b> position. Under general supervision, this position will work within the Department's Administrative and Fiscal Services Unit and primarily perform payroll-personnel and marina related duties, including providing technical and specialized payroll and personnel-related support and managing the Coyote Point Marina system. The position will also assist in updating policies and procedures; internal budget documents; contract administration; and perform related work as required.<br>
<br>
Duties may include, but are not limited to, the following:</p>
<ul>
<li>Perform difficult, complex, and detailed office support work including technical and specialized payroll and personnel activities for the Parks Department;
</li>
<li>Coordinate human resources, payroll, and/or timekeeping processes; maintain personnel-related records; provide information to management and employees regarding payroll and personnel related matters;
</li>
<li>Assist with Coyote Point Marina accounting activities, including posting payments; filing fuel sales taxes with the State; processing deposits and accounts receivable; and completing month-end closing activities;
</li>
<li>Process invoices and track payments;
</li>
<li>Perform all personnel action processes including job changes, compensation changes, initiating hires, and processing retroactive pay;
</li>
<li>Research record keeping issues;
</li>
<li>Coordinate and execute employee onboarding in conjunction with supervisors and the Human Resources Department, process paperwork required for new employees, and provide employees guidance on how to navigate through available onboarding resources;
</li>
<li>Assist employees, supervisors, and managers with payroll, retirement, and benefit laws;
</li>
<li>Review and reconcile varied reports, journals, payroll, or related fiscal or statistical data;
</li>
<li>Audit and verify various information, including source data as well as manual and computer-produced reports;
</li>
<li>Collect information from a variety of sources and synthesize the data into a report format with recommendations;
</li>
<li>Provide information to County staff that requires the use of judgement and the interpretation of policies, rules, and/or procedures;
</li>
<li>Perform a variety of general office support work such as organizing and maintaining various files, typing correspondence, reports, forms, and specialized documents, and proofreading and checking materials for accuracy, completeness, and compliance with departmental policies and procedures;
</li>
<li>Provide back up to other positions when necessary;
</li>
<li>Assist management staff in analyzing budget needs and provide budget development support;
</li>
<li>Assist with the development, announcement, evaluation, processing, and monitoring of contracts, including negotiation with contractors and managing contract execution timeliness;
</li>
<li>Maintain auditing, accounting, and budget controls on a level not required by a professional accountant;
</li>
<li>Assist with the development of policies and procedures and streamline department and program processes;
</li>
<li>Respond to staff issues and work interdepartmentally to resolve them;
</li>
<li>Assist management staff in acquiring, maintaining, and reporting performance measure data and information;
</li>
<li>Assist the Reservations team with answering calls and assisting customers;
</li>
<li>Assist with special projects as assigned; and
</li>
<li>Perform related duties as assigned.</li>
</ul>
<br>
The <b>ideal candidate</b> will:<ul>
<li>Possess knowledge in budget monitoring and budget development support;
</li>
<li>Possess knowledge of the County's HRIS and timekeeping system;
</li>
<li>Possess knowledge of MOU and Civil Service provisions and human resource standards and practices;
</li>
<li>Possess knowledge in accounts payable and receivable;
</li>
<li>Possess knowledge of OFAS, the County's financial accounting system;
</li>
<li>Possess strong interpersonal skills, including conflict resolution skills, with the ability to work with employees across departments;
</li>
<li>Possess strong written and verbal communication skills and problem-solving skills;
</li>
<li>Possess skills in electronic content management and forms creation;
</li>
<li>Be experienced in information gathering, analysis and presentation;
</li>
<li>Be skilled in using office automation and communication technologies;
</li>
<li>Be able to manage a large and varied workload with changing and conflicting deadlines;
</li>
<li>Be able to work effective in a high-volume and fast-paced environment;
</li>
<li>Working knowledge of sub-professional accounting, bookkeeping and fiscal procedures; and
</li>
<li>Be able to work well both independently and in a team environment.</li>
</ul>
A competitive benefits package is associated with this position and varies depending on the classification of the position. For complete information regarding benefits please click here.<br>
<br>
<b>Note:</b> Limited term employees are assigned to assignments not to exceed three years. They receive medical and dental benefits, similar to regular employees, and a defined contribution retirement plan.</div>
<p></p>
<div><b>Qualifications</b></div>
<div><p><b><i>Education and Experience</i></b><b>: </b>Any combination of education and experience that would likely provide the required knowledge, skills and abilities is qualifying. A typical way to qualify is graduation from an accredited college or university with major work in Public or Business Administration or a closely related field and two years of experience in such fields as accounting, personnel management, office management or management analysis.<br>
<br>
<b><i>Knowledge of</i></b>: Principles of organization, administration and supervision; budgeting principles and practices; modern office methods and procedures associated with information technology; and principles and practices of sub professional accounting, bookkeeping and fiscal procedures.<br>
<br>
<b><i>Skill/Ability to</i></b><b>:</b> Analyze organizational structures and recommend procedural changes and prepare and present reports in narrative and graphic form; establish and maintain accounts and budgetary controls; learn quickly the laws and rules governing the operations of the department to which assigned; interpret and explain laws and rules to subordinates, other County departmental staff and to the general public; and plan, organize and supervise the work of clerical personnel effectively.</p></div>
<div><b>Application/Examination</b></div>
<div>
<p><b>This is a limited term recruitment. Anyone may apply.</b> A supplemental application form must be submitted in addition to our regular employment application form. The Parks Department will screen applications during the recruitment. The Parks Department will contact qualified candidates directly for a departmental interview.<br>
<br>
The examination will consist of an interview (weight: 100%). Depending on the number of applications a screening committee may select those applicants whose education and/or experience appear to best meet the needs of the position. <i>All applicants who meet the minimum qualifications are not guaranteed advancement through any subsequent phase of the examination.</i> All examinations will be given in San Mateo County, California, and applicants must participate at their own expense.<br>
<br>
<b>IMPORTANT: </b>Applications for this position will only be accepted online. If you are currently on the County's website, you may click on the <b>"Apply"</b> button. Online applications must be received by the Human Resources Department before midnight on the final filing date. <b>All examinations will be given in San Mateo County, California and applicants must participate at their own expense.</b><br>
