 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">

<p><b>Job Description:
</b></p>
<p>The District Business Manager is responsible for the supervision and leadership of healthcare representatives within the local geography, sales performance at a district level, product champion at a state level, and the business knowledge of the local landscape to assess key stakeholders plus future trends within the marketplace.
</p>
<p><b>Responsibilities:
</b></p>
<ul><li>Responsible for supervising operations of the District to include: hiring, coaching, representative development, performance management, and the assignment of key "priority" accounts within the medical community.
</li></ul>
<ul><li>Develops and implements strategic plans for the District
</li></ul>
<ul><li>Plans, organizes and monitors performance to achieve the business potential of the District, State, and the RBU.
</li></ul>
<ul><li>Works in the field with all members of District to coach and counsel on improvement of selling skills, product knowledge, and capabilities needed for successful representative development
</li></ul>
<ul><li>Establish and maintain an effective communications system among all members of the District, Region, and State Management Teams.
</li></ul>
<ul><li>Maintain required technical expertise in order to respond accurately to all questions regarding products, policies, and business-related issues from customers and representatives.
</li></ul>
<ul><li>Effectively plan and conduct plan of action and other meetings with members of the District and other State Management Teams.
</li></ul>
<ul><li>Develops and implements District business plan, manages District budget and overall responsibility for P&amp;L at District level in alignment with State/RBU expectations.
</li></ul>
<ul><li>Has complete understanding of all relevant compliance laws, policies and processes. Ensures actions of self and team are fully compliant.
</li></ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>Qualifications:
</b></p>
<ul><li>BA/BS Degree required. MBA or advanced degree in a related field preferred.
</li></ul>
<ul><li>Previous pharmaceutical, biotech, or medical marketing/sales experience preferred with at least three to five years spent in a position with demonstrated leadership across peer and customer groups.
</li></ul>
<ul><li>Experience working with key thought leaders or high influence customers in large group practices, hospitals, or managed care organizations preferred.
</li></ul>
<ul><li>Strong organizational and analytical skills are also required and ability to analyze and draw appropriate conclusions using sales data/call reporting software/applications and able to adapt to Pfizer's long-range technology model in bringing Pfizer information to market.
</li></ul>
<ul><li>Previous supervisory experience preferred and ability to develop and motivate others, lead trough change, and deliver on Pfizer business imperatives is necessary to be successful in this role.
</li></ul>
<ul><li>This position will require travel as needed to develop internal and external relationships.
</li></ul>
