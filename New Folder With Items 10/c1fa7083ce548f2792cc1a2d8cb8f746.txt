<p><b>Department Overview</b></p>
<p>
The Land Rights Support department is responsible for establishing policies and procedures to provide the highest level of support for the management and protection of PG&amp;E’s land and land rights in connection with PG&amp;E’s operations. We provide oversight, guidance, technical direction and training to Land Management staff and business partners. We support the construction activities of various capacity, reliability, safety improvement, and facility relocation projects. We communicate with private and public or governmental bodies and agencies to resolve land rights related matters requiring higher level of expertise.</p>
<p></p>
<br>
<p><b>
Position Summary</b></p>
<p>
This job is represented by Engineers and Scientists of California (ESC) and is<b> </b>subject to collective bargaining.</p>

<p></p>
<br>
<p><b>
Job Responsibilities</b></p>
<p>
The Senior Land Agent provides oversight, advice, guidance, technical direction and training to Land Management staff and advises PG&amp;E management and other business partners on all matters relating to land rights, title issues, rearrangement of PG&amp;E facilities, third party uses of PG&amp;E’s lands and easements. The Senior Land Agent reviews title of both fee land and land rights for PG&amp;E projects as well as for those special projects as may be required by PG&amp;E. Responsibilities also include the review of land rights documents to ensure they are legally sufficient and conform to PG&amp;E’s policy. The Senior Land Agent is responsible for preparation of contracts for review of title and oversees contract agents. The Senior Land Agent may discuss title and land rights matters requiring higher levels of experience and expertise with private, public, or governmental bodies and agencies.<br>
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>Essential Job Functions:</b></p>
<p>
1. Oversight, Guidance, Technical Direction and Training:
</p>
<ul>
<li>Provides oversight, guidance, technical direction and training to Land Management staff on complex land rights issues, uses of PG&amp;E’s lands and easements by others including adherence to CPUC Code Section 851, and New Customer Connection issues.</li>
<li>
Provides high-level technical and legal support to Land Management staff, and business partners.
</li>
<li>Ensures adequacy of land rights and conformance to CPUC tariffs regarding acquisition of land rights</li>
<li>
Assists in the determination of cost liability when PG&amp;E facilities are required or requested by others to relocate and ensures adherence to PG&amp;E’s policy regarding relocations. Collaborates in developing and implementing strategies for dealing with complex relocation projects.
</li>
<li>Oversees and ensures adherence to various master agreements with transportation agencies including the Caltrans Freeway Master Contract as well as the Streets and Highway Code, governing the rearrangement of PG&amp;E’s facilities for highway and freeway construction.
</li>
<li>Plans and oversees the work of contract agents related to title reviews, including training and guidance.<br>
<br>
2. Legal Research and Determination of Land Rights:
</li>
</ul>
<ul><li>Research and respond to title questions.
</li></ul>
<ul><li>Knowledgeable of the Subdivision Map Act, the Subdivided Lands Law, the Professional Land Surveyors Act, various California legal codes pertaining to real property, easement law, notary law, recording law, taxation, and those portions of other codes, CPUC rules and PG&amp;E policies which are most frequently applied to PG&amp;E land rights and title matters, and consults with Law Department when appropriate.<br>
<br>
3. Line, Title, Timber, Water and Mineral Rights Review:
</li></ul>
<ul>
<li>Completes the more difficult and complex reviews of land title.
</li>
<li>Determines the condition of title to PG&amp;E’s fee property and facilities. Advises Business Partners which rights must be protected or terminated in connection with a proposed project.
</li>
<li>Prepares line reviews for PG&amp;E’s electric and gas transmission facilities using the Land Rights Unit’s standard line review formats which include a summary of PG&amp;E’s land rights and identification of special conditions or restrictions and any gaps in the rights of way.<br>
<br>
4. Property Purchase and Sales Review:
</li>
</ul>
<ul><li>Property Purchase: Reviews preliminary title reports prepared by outside title companies for the parcel to be acquired. Determines if the title to be conveyed to PG&amp;E is acceptable and initiates appropriate action. Make recommendations of action to be taken, if any, to clear title to the property as a condition to closing the transaction.</li></ul>
<ul>
<li>
Property Sales: Prepares title reviews for sales transactions. Involves a review of the chain of title, identification of grants out made by PG&amp;E affecting the property and review of the buyer’s preliminary title report prepared by a title company. Provides direction to the document writer on how title is to be conveyed. Reviews the historical use of the property while owned by PG&amp;E, as well as information which must be disclosed to the prospective buyer including identification of special conditions which may affect the sale transaction.<br>
<br>
5. Document Review:
</li>
<li>Reviews changes to Law Department approved standard documents.
</li>
<li>Provides direction and guidance to Land Management staff, and assists to assure the integrity, adequacy and legal sufficiency of documents relating to land rights in conformance with PG&amp;E’s policy.<br>
<br>
6. Represent PG&amp;E:
</li>
<li>Discuss title and land matters requiring higher levels of experience and expertise with private, public, or governmental bodies and agencies beyond the scope or authority of lower classifications.</li>
</ul>
