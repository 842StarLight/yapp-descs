<br>
<b>Location:</b> Santa Rosa, CA<br>
<br>
<b>Schedule: </b>Exempt<br>

<b>Job Summary</b>:<div>
<br>
Oversees the assessment, planning, implementation and evaluation of the Infection Prevention (IP) Program for St. Joseph Health Sonoma County ministries.<div>
<br>
<b>
Essential Functions:
</b><br>
</div>
<ul>
<li>Annually evaluates the system-wide program for surveillance, prevention and control of infection. Develops and revises the Infection Prevention plan according to the evaluation process, organizational assessment and surveillance activities.</li>
<li>
Coordinates the overall IP program for all SJH Sonoma County ministries. Integrates department activities with the rest of the organziation's strategic goals.</li>
<li>
Oversees the review and revision of IP policies to assure compliance with regulating and accrediting bodies as well as to include relevant recommended evidence-based practices.</li>
<li>
Maintains current professional practice standards as set forth by the Association for Professionals in Infection Control and Epidemiology.</li>
<li>
Coaches and develops the skills and talents of the IP departmentsâ€™ employees. Supervises the hiring, training, and performance reviews of Infection Prevention staff.</li>
<li>
Performs and oversees the submission of facility data to regulatory bodies.</li>
<li>
Directs and performs the ongoing collection and analytical review of infection prevention data.</li>
<li>
Leads and participates in performance improvement teams.</li>
<li>
Plans/provides educational programs for all hospital personnel.</li>
<li>
Provides consultation to hospital personnel and to outside agencies that interface with the facility.</li>
</ul>
<b>
Knowledge / Skills / Abilities:
</b><br>
<div></div>
<ul>
<li>Working knowledge and ability to demonstrate comprehension of Infection Prevention principles and practices.</li>
<li>
Knowledge of pertinent regulatory, accrediting, and advisory body requirements including, but not limited to, state and federal regulations, Joint Commission on Accreditation of Healthcare Organizations, and recommendations of the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention.</li>
<li>
Must be able to assume a leadership role in cost-effective prevention of transmissible diseases and emergency management of outbreaks of novel pathogens.</li>
<li>
Effective written and verbal communication skills, including the ability to educate and respond to hospital and external agency personnel.</li>

Critical thinking and decision making skills</li>

Must demonstrate a basic understanding of quality assurance monitoring.</li>
<li>
Must provide evidence of continuing education within the last year with emphasis on aspects of infectious diseases, infection prevention, or public health.</li>
</ul>
