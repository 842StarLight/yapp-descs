<p><b>Job-Specific Responsibilities</b></p>
<div>
<p>The Manager, Special Programs will report to the Assistant Director for Program Development, with dotted line reporting to the Director of Professional Development Programs and the Assistant Director for Program Operations. This position will actively assist in strategic planning, development, and review of the department's program and instructors. This position will regularly work on projects designated by the Director, Assistant Director, and Director of Marketing regarding program development and strategy. In addition, this position will manage the portfolio and relationship of the collaboration between Harvard Extension School and Trilogy Education Services, any new programs involving third parties, or special programs which fall outside of the typical open enrollment programs. In this capacity, this position will meet and discuss new program opportunities and work through managing institutional expectations, reviewing new program contracts, meeting with leadership and department heads, as well as providing information and up-to-date news to DCE at large. This position also provides program management for the unit's multiple annual offerings of the 5-day hybrid Essential Management Skills for Emerging Leaders (EMSEL) program, as well as delivery aspects of custom programs.<br>
</p>
<p><b>
Primary responsibilities include:</b><br>
</p>
<p><b>
Program Management:</b></p>
<p><br>
Makes strategic program decisions that effect and impact the success of the course design and the day-to-day program, seeks and maintains knowledge of best practices and plans accordingly, facilitates a seamless high-quality participant experience and maintains the highest level of customer service among team members.</p>
<p>
Staff Management. Defines and communicates work standards and performance expectations for one program coordinator (2 in FY21) through delegation and scheduled check-in meetings, advises and facilitates professional development, provides ongoing feedback, trains new staff and provides ongoing coaching, communicates, motivates and guides staff around new processes and policies, serves as role model.</p>
<p>
Instructor Communication. Communicates with instructors and serves as the main point of contact throughout the program process. Sets expectations for instructors and manages their needs while maintaining a professional and collaborative relationship. Communicates with instructors to collect program materials and works with coordinator to standardize materials and order assessments, textbooks, and business cases. Manages and updates assignments and deadlines in project management software for benefit of program coordinators.</p>
<p>
Online Content. Works with instructors to deliver online portions of programs while ensuring participant¿s high expectations for a quality program experience are met. Creates Canvas Catalog program microsites, writing program announcements, posting program materials, and fielding participant questions. Hosts web conferences.</p>

<p>
The Division of Continuing Education (DCE) comprises several entities serving about 30,000 students annually. The Harvard Extension School, the Harvard Summer School, the Harvard Professional Development Programs and the Harvard Institute for Learning in Retirement allow Harvard to serve the learning needs of students across their life. For more than a century, Harvard Extension School has made Harvard faculty and teaching accessible to a diverse community of local, national, and international students. Harvard Extension School enrolls approximately 16,000 students per year in approximately 46 graduate and undergraduate liberal arts and professional fields. More than 1,500 open enrollment courses are offered year-round with over 600 available online are offered at affordable prices to our communities across the world. Harvard Summer School offers approximately 450 courses on the Harvard Campus to 10,000 secondary school, college and adult learners and more than 30 international study abroad programs for up to 600 students each summer. The Division also includes the Harvard Institute for Learning in Retirement with approximately 550 active members and Harvard Professional Development Programs offering 80 short noncredit courses to almost 4,000 learners annually.<br>
