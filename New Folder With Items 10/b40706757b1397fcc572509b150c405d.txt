<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Job Description:
</b></h2>
<p></p>
<h1 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Responsibilities include
</b></h1>
<p>Analyze, debug, &amp; root cause issues found within Broadcom’s PCIe switch products and work with appropriate development teams (firmware, software, IC hardware, or board hardware) to provide a solution.
</p>
<p>Provide guidance to OEM customers on properly integrating Broadcom’s PCIe switch products into GP-GPUs, AI/ML platforms, storage servers and custom designs. Guidance consists of architecture reviews, schematic reviews, design-in support and training customers on product features.
</p>
<p>Develop product documentation for use by customers implementing PCIe switch.
</p>
<h1 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Other duties include
</b></h1>
<p>Develop software tools and methods to improve debug efficiency and customer satisfaction.
</p>
<p>Train field sales and support staff in the features and uses of Broadcom PCIe switch products.
</p>
<p>Occasional travel, some of which may be international.
</p>
<h1 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Requirements/Qualifications
</b></h1>
<p>Experience with firmware, software, hardware and PCIe protocol level debug.
</p>
<p>Experience developing and debugging C code.
</p>
<p>Prior experience in embedded SW debug or development (firmware, drivers, BIOS ) using PCIe technology.
</p>
<p>Skilled in organizing information.
</p>
<p>Self-motivated and a strong self-driven learner who learns new technologies quickly
</p>
<p>Good written and verbal communications skills
</p>
<p>Strong interpersonal skills. Must be able to communicate complex technical solutions with the customer and internal teams
</p>
<p>Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering or related field plus 8 years of relevant experience (or Masters Degree in related field plus 6 years of relevant experience).
</p>
<h1 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Desirable, but not required skills
</b></h1>
<p>Experience with SAS/SATA protocols, RAID, ARM or PPC embedded processors, logic analyzers, and oscilloscopes
</p>
