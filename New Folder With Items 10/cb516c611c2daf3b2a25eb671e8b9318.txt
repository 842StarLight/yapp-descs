<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Duties</b></h2>
<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Summary</b></h3>
<p></p>
<div>
<p>This position is located in the Office of the Assistant Secretary for Administration and Management (OASAM), Region VI, Division of Finance and Administrative Services. The incumbent is responsible for the acquisition of open market purchases up to the Simplified Acquisition Threshold (SAT) and $500,000 on GSA Schedule for the Dallas/Denver region consisting of approximately 1,400 customers in 11 states and 159 locations.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
</div>
<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Responsibilities</b></h3>
<p></p>
<div>
<p>Responsible for the acquisition of open market purchases. Drafts procurement documents including procurement plans, solicitation documents, negotiation memoranda and award instruments, and all other contractual matters relating to development evaluation, execution, administration, and modification. Determines method of solicitation and type of contract and prepares and issues solicitation documents to prospective contractors. Evaluates bids/proposals/quotations utilizing cost analysis or price analysis. Procures supplies or services through coordinating, analyzing, and planning contracting processes ranging from initiation to recommendation of award for a variety of supplies, services, and construction. Performs detailed analysis of bids or proposals received. Drafts final contract including specifications, packing and shipping requirements, inspection instructions, and all other special and standard clauses. Performs market research, implements FedBizops, develops the solicitation, negotiates contracts, performs contract administration and serves as technical expert and advisor. Has experience in applying the FAR. Develops, compiles, and automates various reports including statistical tabulations. Uses various automation software and hardware to produce a variety of documents. This software can include data base or spreadsheet software to enter, revise, sort or calculate and retrieve data for standard reports. Software may also include graphic software to provide graphic symbols, charts, etc.</p>
</div>

<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Qualifications</b></h3>
<div>
<p>Basic Educational Requirements for all grade levels:</p>
<p>
A.) A 4-year course of study leading to a bachelor's degree with a major in any field; OR<br>
B.) At least 24 semester hours in any combination of the following fields: accounting, business, finance, law, contracts, purchasing, economics, industrial management, marketing, quantitative methods, or organization and management.</p>
<p>
In addition to the Basic Educational Requirements, applicants at the GS-11 level qualify with: 3 full academic years of progressively higher level graduate education or Ph.D. or equivalent doctoral degree; or One (1) year of specialized experience to at least the GS-09 grade level in Federal service. Equivalent combinations of education and experience are qualifying for all grade levels for which both education and experience are acceptable. Examples of specialized experience at the GS-11 level include: executing and administering service contracts and supply contracts; conducting market research and awarding contracts; using knowledge of Federal Acquisition Regulations and/or Department of Labor Acquisition Regulations to select appropriate methods of contracting; and applying commonly used contracting methods and contract types relating to the performance of pre-award, post-award, and/or contract price/cost analysis functions.</p>
<p>
Education cannot be substituted for Specialized Experience at the GS-12 level. Examples of Specialized Experience at the GS-12 level include: developing, preparing, and presenting terms and conditions in bids or proposals related to the award of contracts; negotiating and awarding contracts, contract modifications, and/or subcontracts; administering the terms and conditions of contracts, including such aspects as preparing contract modifications, evaluation of performance under the contract, and contract termination; formulating policies and procedures for the acquisition of goods or services, participating in procurement management reviews and contract clearance, or developing positions on claims and protests related to contracts.</p>
<p>
Time-in-Grade Requirement: Applicants must have served at least one (1) year at the next lower grade level in the Federal service.</p>
