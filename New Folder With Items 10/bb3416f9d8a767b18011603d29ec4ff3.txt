<b>About Us</b><b>: </b>We are the Communications and Public Engagement section and are primary point-of-contact for the public regarding inquiries, media, publicity, and general information for Parks and Recreation. Our team is responsible for providing public information about the various recreational programs, services, and events offered by the Department. We effectively reach millions of visitors of our 181 parks and over 200 miles of trails in the County system. Our team's marketing and social media efforts strengthen the Department's brand and promote its recreational offerings and special events.<p><b>
About You</b><b>: </b>Do you enjoy communicating clearly and timely about departmental events? Public Information Assistants bring excitement to our team! This role creates and releases information via radio; television; social media including Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, LinkedIn and Snapchat; newspapers; magazines; newsletters; and outdoor advertisements such as billboards, posters, and even buses. You will be comfortable with creating and participating in live events including interviewing attendees and being on camera yourself. You should have prior experience with releasing public information and have excellent time management and planning skills. If this matches your experience then we are looking for you!</p>
</div></div>
<br>
<div><div><b>ESSENTIAL JOB FUNCTIONS:
</b></div></div>
<br>
<div><div>Writing news and publicity releases and featuring articles on various phases of departmental operations;<br>
<br>
Develops and maintains digital media content (e.g., posts, graphics, videos, etc.) and social media campaigns across the department's various social media platforms.<br>
<br>
Serves as media coordinator with representatives of television stations, newspapers, and magazines in the development of special features and projects and in the dissemination of information regarding the department.<br>
<br>
Researches new and emerging media for possible use in outreach and public communication.<br>
<br>
Develops and maintains external communications through radio and television stations, internet sites, and various social media platforms for announcements of departmental special services or activities.<br>
<br>
Designs, writes, and produces public information materials such as display advertising, brochures, flyers, direct mail materials, web sites, and social media content using a variety of graphics, desktop and mobile device publishing software, programming languages, and social media tools to develop or modify materials.<br>
<br>
Assists departmental staff in developing concepts for planning and presenting publicity programs regarding departmental operations.<br>
<br>
Speaks before various organizations, clubs, and special interest groups on departmental services, functions, or special activities.<br>
<br>
Develops and maintains working relationships with representatives of other public and private agencies to disseminate information concerning departmental operations.<br>
<br>
Researches and makes recommendations regarding the implication and effectiveness of the department's information dissemination activities.<br>
<br>
Obtains news items through interviews with departmental staff or by visiting departmental facilities and conducts research for additional information as necessary.<br>
<br>
Attends meetings of the Board of Supervisors, County commissions and committees, and communications-related workgroups and conferences to obtain information of interest to the department.<br>
<br>
Participates in special events, captures information, and acts as departmental representative to the news media and various social media platforms.<br>
<br>
Selects and writes captions for photographs that accompany news releases.<br>
<br>
Writes the department's newsletter as needed.<br>
<br>
Creates and leads social media and marketing campaigns.<br>
<br>
Contributes to the annual marketing strategic plan.<br>
<br>
Creates strategic plans for special projects and outreach campaigns.<br>
<br>
Maintains relevant website pages for the Department through the use of WordPress.
</div></div>

<div><div><b>REQUIREMENTS:
</b></div></div>
<br>
<div><div>
<b>Selection Requirements:</b><br>
<br>
Graduation from an accredited college or university with a Bachelor's degree or higher in communication, marketing, public relations, journalism, emerging media, business &amp; organization communication, mass media communications, news broadcasting, or closely related field* <b>and</b> two (2) year's experience in the dissemination of information to the public including the writing and placing of news releases or the writing of news and feature articles** for the various communication media***.<br>
<br>
<b>License</b><b>: </b>A valid California Class C Driver License or the ability to utilize an alternative method of transportation when needed to carry out job-related essential functions.<br>
<br>
<b>Specialty Requirements Information:</b><br>
<br>
<li>In order to receive credit for your college degree, you must include a</li>
<b> legible copy of the official diploma, official transcripts, or official letter </b>from the accredited institution which shows the area of specialization with your application or e-mail it to jkennington@parks.lacounty.gov within 15 calendar days of filing. Official documentation must be written in <b>English</b>. Degrees obtained outside of the United States must be evaluated for U.S. equivalency.<br>
<br>
**The dissemination of information to the public including the writing and placing of news releases or the writing of news and feature articles is defined as marketing board, legislative, <b>or</b> corporate initiatives; <b>and</b> marketing community <b>or</b><b> </b>departmental projects; <b>and</b> marketing community <b>or</b> departmental events.<br>
<br>
*** Various communication media is defined as experience with disseminating information through social media (Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, LinkedIn, and Snapchat) <b>and</b><b> </b>one or more of the following: radio; newspapers; magazines; television; newsletters; <b>or</b> outdoor advertisements such as billboards, posters, and buses.<br>
<br>
<b>Withholds:</b> There will be <b>no withholds </b>for this examination.<br>
<br>
<b>Out-of-class, temporary responsibility, and verification of experience letters</b><b>: </b>Will not be accepted.<br>
<br>
<b>Physical class:</b><b> </b>2 - Light. Light physical effort which may include occasional light lifting up to a 10 pound limit, and some bending, stooping or squatting. Considerable walking may be involved.<br>
<br>
<b>Desirable Qualifications</b><b>:</b><ul>
<li>Experience creating and managing social media campaigns for a large organization on multiple platforms, including but not limited to Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, LinkedIn, and Snapchat.
</li>
<li>Experience creating graphic designs through programs such as Adobe Suite, Canva, or equivalent software.
</li>
<li>Experience managing website content using programs such as WordPress or equivalent software.
</li>
<li>Experience creating presentations using PowerPoint, Canva slides, Google slides, Prezi, or equivalent software.</li>
</ul>

<div><div>
<p><b>Examination content</b><b>:</b> This examination will consist of three (3) parts.</p>
<p><b>
Part I:</b> an evaluation of training and experience based on the application and responses to the supplemental questionnaire weighted <b>10%</b>.</p>
<p><b>
Part II</b>: a writing project that will measure creative thinking, originality, and written communication weighted <b>20%</b>.</p>
<p><b>
Part III</b>: an oral interview covering training and experience, clerical, creative thinking, critical thinking, interacting with computers, sales and marketing, oral communication, organization and planning, originality, relationship building, speech clarity, social media, technical skills, and time management weighted <b>70%</b>.</p>
<p><i>
A passing score of 70% or higher on each part of the examination is required to be placed on the eligible list. Candidates may be tested on more than one part at a time or moved to additional parts of the examination before the prior part has been scored.</i></p>
<b>
Eligibility Information</b><b>: </b>The names of candidates receiving a passing score in the examination will be placed on the eligible list for a period of twelve (12) months following the date of promulgation.<br>
