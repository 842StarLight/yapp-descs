<h2>Account Manager-OH, MI, KY</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>GEHA is a leader in health services for federal employees and related customers, serving our chosen markets with low-cost offerings and best-in-class customer care, sustained by a nimble and efficient organization.
</p>
<p></p>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>SKILLS
</b></h2>
<p></p>
<p>The Account Manager serves as a service and sales representative for GEHA, and is a primary contact for federal agency personnel, members and prospective members in an assigned territory. Works to secure new members and retains current membership. Responsible for creating and maintaining strong client relationships with agency contacts essential to organization’s success in membership growth and customer satisfaction. Possesses strong communications skills to engage customers, answer insurance questions and sell GEHA Plans. Identifies marketing opportunities: conducts presentations, coordinates shipments of supplies, and assists in the resolution of claims and service issues. Participates in development and implementation of sales and service strategies and supports the business sales process and any new initiatives.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>DUTIES:
</p>
<ul>
<li>Meets with federal personnel representatives and plan enrollees to monitor the Plan’s services. Builds sales by prospecting for new customers and generates future revenue by nurturing existing customer relationships. Provides account service by acting as liaison between the company and assigned group of federal agencies and members. Assists with customer questions, concerns or requests, relays needs to the appropriate individual or department within the company and follows up with the customer.
</li>
<li>Plans, develops and executes sales process and strategies, through a customer relationship management program (CRM) and communication process for visiting/contacting federal agencies in an assigned territory. Meets an established production quota. Identifies those agencies with specific personnel functions and maintains account/agency records including contact and visit history and utilized records for planning and forecasting. Updates database accurately and regularly.
</li>
<li>Develops a monthly travel schedule of planned agency visits and other activities to meet established production and quality standards.
</li>
<li>Organizes and coordinates attendance at Open Season enrollment events, Agency Benefit Officers (ABO) Seminars, conventions, National Active and Retired Federal Employees (NARFE) meetings, health and wellness fairs, retirement briefings, and other federal events. Reports on these activities.
</li>
<li>Conducts presentations to member groups, retirees, and other designated parties to promote Plan benefits and services. Reports on activities.
</li>
<li>Provides creative input and marketing insights. Reports on regional marketing efforts and advertising programs for GEHA. Conducts market intelligence on competitors’ activities and market trends.
</li>
<li>Prepares monthly project report of all activities, including field office administrative tasks.
</li>
<li>Monitors and stays abreast of new developments and technology in their industry. Regularly attends annual training sessions, conferences and trade shows.
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p>REQUIREMENTS: Education and Experience
</p>
<ul>
<li>Requires a Bachelor’s degree in Marketing, Communications, English, Business Administration, or Business Management
</li>
<li>Requires three years of work experience in health insurance (claims, communications, customer services, sales, public relations or marketing) and/or sales and marketing
</li>
<li>Additional years of qualifying work experience may be considered in lieu of formal education
</li>
<li>Must be reliable and adaptive to change and meet deadlines
</li>
<li>Requires strong attention to detail and effective time management skills
</li>
<li>Exercises sound decision-making abilities
</li>
<li>Identifies workplace problems and makes recommendations for solutions
</li>
<li>Must be able to work independently, as well as to collaborate with members of a team
</li>
<li>Requires knowledge of MS Word and MS Excel, and MS PowerPoint, and SharePoint
</li>
<li>Requires the ability to research and analyze plan documents
</li>
<li>Requires superior verbal and written communication skills, and demonstrated presentation skills
</li>
</ul>
