<h2 class="jobSectionHeader">
<b>Position Summary
</b><br>
</h2>The Internal Control Manager partners with Finance Leadership to provide oversight and accountability for MPHC financial risk. They are accountable for ensuring proper processes and controls are in place to provide appropriate stewardship of company assets, to ensure compliance with regulators, and to maintain a control environment that matches the cultural needs of Martin’s Point.
<br>
<br>
This position works closely and collaboratively with leadership throughout MPHC in examining, analyzing, and establishing internal controls and the risk environment for financial management. They are responsible for establishing and monitoring internal controls relating to finance processes to meet the standards of external auditors, regulators and internal customers. This role is responsible for the development and monitoring of internal controls to validate and verify the financial processes within other business functions and departments throughout Martin’s Point; as we implement internal controls related to Model Audit Rule (MAR) they will be required to work with and manage the relationship with external consulting auditors in developing and testing a reasonable system of internal controls necessary to meet the requirements of MAR. They are also responsible for assisting Finance Leadership in creating and maintaining policies and procedures that comply with GAAP, SSAP, NAIC, CMS and IRS regulations.
<br>
<br>
Responsibilities also include support of audits including financial audits, BOI, Sales Tax, and CMS audits.
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader">
<br>
<b>Job Description
</b>
</h2>
<p><b>Key Outcomes:
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Performs internal control related activities and functions in a manner that ensures the adherence to general accepted accounting principles as well as all standards and regulations effecting the work.
<ul>
<li>Bases of accounting include GAAP, SSAP, and IRS
</li>
<li>Control systems for insurance products include MAR and ORSA.
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Evaluates and determines the appropriate internal control design for both financial processes and inter-departmental processes
</li>
<li>Proposes recommendations to various levels of management with regards to appropriate internal control design
</li>
<li>Serve as the “go-to” technical resource for internal controls within the organization and effectively communicate with and educate business partners, including non-finance individuals, on the importance of controls and an effective control environment.
</li>
<li>Partner with finance and non-accounting process owners to develop narratives, flowcharts, risk and control matrices and related internal control documentation
</li>
<li>Conducts audits and testing of processes, departments, or functional areas and identifies reportable issues and dimension of risk
</li>
<li>Assist in evaluation and resolution of identified control deficiencies, including monitoring of remediation efforts
</li>
<li>Establishes guidelines and monitors compliance with policies and procedures
</li>
<li>Supports development and implementation of MPHC’s ERM (Enterprise Risk Management) program while working in collaboration with leadership in the Compliance Department and throughout MPHC.
</li>
<li>Assists with the Annual Audit and preparation of monthly Financial Statements
</li>
<li>Responsible for establishing, adhering to and maintaining accounting standards, guidelines and compliance relating to Medicare Advantage.
</li>
<li>Responsible for developing and maintaining accounting department policies and procedures
</li>
<li>Contributes to monthly processing and distribution of corporate financial statements and other financial reporting.
</li>
<li>Assists the Corporate Controller directly with programmatic accounting tasks.
</li>
<li>Performs special projects or other duties as needed.
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>Accounting Controls/Audit:
</b></p>
<p>Responsible for detailed financial operating structure to keep cash controls to a maximum. All Corporate financials must follow either GAAP, Statutory Reporting or Corporate Accounting policies as determined by the Controller or Management. Coordinates and maintains a good working relationship with our audit firm and keeps auditors informed of corporate changes, status of company, and concerns. The position must also work closely with the Maine Bureau of Insurance and other regulators to ensure successful completion of audits.
