<h2>Enterprise Architect - Demand and Supply Planning Architect</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div></div>
<div>
<div>
<div>
<div>Role Description:</div>
<div>
The Demand &amp; Supply Planning Architect / Project Manager leads supply chain transformation projects. The Architect / PM is responsible for both the project management (timeline, project plan management, staffing, budget, stakeholder management) and architecture (overall solution design including end-to-end design, integrations, and data considerations) for Demand &amp; Supply Planning initiatives. The Architect / PM manages all workstreams of the project including functional and technical tracks, architects the end state design, and is responsible for the delivery of the engagement.</div>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<div>
Qualifications</div>
<ul>
<li>At least 5 years’ experience with deep understanding of demand and supply planning capabilities including demand forecasting, supply planning, inventory optimization, inventory management, S&amp;OP. Production planning a plus.</li>
<li>At least 5 years’ experience in full lifecycle implementations with leading demand and supply planning applications (e.g., JDA, Kinaxis, Anaplan, Aera, SAP, Oracle, etc.)</li>
<li>At least 3 years’ experience in supply chain project management, ideally for demand and supply implementations</li>
<li>Demonstrated ability to architect an end-to-end demand and supply planning solution</li>
<li>Deep understanding of demand and supply planning tools both from a configuration and process standpoint</li>
<li>Experience with multiple demand and supply planning implementations required</li>
<li>Deep knowledge of systems integration points for demand and supply planning systems required</li>
<li>Experience with multiple demand planning, supply planning, and sales &amp; operations tools a plus</li>
<li>Business background in a demand planning or supply planning role a plus</li>
<li>Bachelor’s Degree, preferably in Supply Chain Management or related field</li>
</ul>

<b>
Qualifications</b><div></div>
<br>
<div>Enterprise Architects define and ensure a comprehensive and coherent view across Business, Information, Systems and Technology. They go beyond designing IT systems to deliver business change, which may also be supported and enabled by IT. Enterprise Architects define services from a business perspective, both with and without automation. They make an initial grouping of services in components, using principles based on objectives and constraints. They have deep business and industry expertise, are familiar with industry standards, and can work at the boardroom and senior management level.</div>
