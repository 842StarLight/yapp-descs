<h2>Creative Operations Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">Curaleaf, Inc. is the largest multi-state operator in the fast-growing U.S. cannabis industry. We are a leader in cultivation, processing, product development, and advocacy, and are committed to offering the highest levels of customer care in our dispensaries. Curaleaf operates the largest branded retail network in the U.S. and has developed three national cannabis brands. Curaleaf's premium cannabis products are known for quality, reliability and innovation, and are available in multiple product formats and dosages to meet a variety of consumer needs.
<br><br>
The Creative Operations Manager role is a wonderful opportunity for a dynamic-go-getter to join the largest and fastest growing cannabis company in the world. This person will be responsible for facilitating the development and review of all content for the website(s), email(s), social media, advertising, packaging and all printed assets ensuring the successful design and implementation for all creative needs.
<br><br>
Creative Operations manages the flow of information and assets to and from the creative team, and monitors adherence to brand standards, legal and state approvals, and creative deadlines. The Creative Operations Manager will ensure effective processes and excellent communication across the marketing and creative teams.
<br><br>
Responsibilities
<br><br>
<ul>
<li>First things first, attention to detail is a must!</li>
<li>Manage creative process and workflow from creative brief submission to release of digital and print assets</li>
<li>Facilitate collaboration between state and national marketing leaders, copywriters, web &amp; graphic designers, PR &amp; Bloggers, sales, and legal teams</li>
<li>Review digital &amp; print assets for accuracy prior to review</li>
<li>Initiate creative concept meetings after brief submission to drive collaboration between creative team members</li>
<li>Liaison with retail marketing and sales teams to foster creative asset sharing</li>
<li>Ensure campaigns are represented as a full omni-channel experience</li>
<li>Manage internal and external vendors</li>
<li>Create and manage timelines for video, photography and copy and design.</li>
<li>Develop and refine effective workflow processes, scheduling strategies, and communication models</li>
<li>Communicate obstacles and improvement opportunities</li>
<li>Ensure adherence to standard operating procedures and strict file management systems</li>
<li>Develop, maintain, communicate and train internal teams and external resources/teams on brand standards</li>
<li>Maintain an understanding of all aspects of brand standards, design and production process</li>
<li>Keen ability to prioritize deadlines to meet business needs in a very fast paced and quickly changing environment</li>
<li>File management skills that deserve gold medals, can we say "Asset Library"?</li>
</ul>
Requirements
<br><br>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor's degree from an accredited university in Marketing, Communications, Project Management or Business Administration</li>
<li>At least 5-7 years' experience at a Manager level or higher in consumer packaged goods (CPG), retail, pharmaceutical, food production, or other regulated industries; Prior experience in the cannabis industry is neither a plus nor a minus</li>
<li>Experience leading creative teams of 10 or more people where every individual on the team may not report directly to you and may be internal or external resources</li>
<li>Excellent verbal and written communications skills</li>
<li>Strong relationship development and management skills</li>
<li>Outstanding organizational and planning skills; Must be flexible, resilient and able to keep focus in a fast-paced, constantly changing environment</li>
<li>Excellent time management skills and ability to multi-task and prioritize work</li>
<li>Strong attention to detail and problem-solving skills</li>
<li>Experience with Adobe Creative Suite, eCommerce and digital marketing platforms (such as HubSpot and Magento) are strongly desired</li>
<li>Strong Microsoft Office skills (especially Teams, Excel, Word and PowerPoint) are required</li>
</ul>
Curaleaf is an equal opportunity employer</div>
