<h2>Project Budget Sr Specialist</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>Purpose of Role:<br>
The primary purpose of this role is to assist with the execution of the capital budgeting, forecasting, and purchasing process for the Supply Chain Network. This includes responsibility for executing the PAR and Purchase Order process through Spend Management or other means. The Project Budget Specialist works closely with Regional Maintenance Mangers, Director DCSS, M&amp;E, Corporate Accounting, and field based team members in order to ensure capital improvements and purchases are forecasted and executed timely and accurately.<br>
</p>
<ul>
<li>
Lead and facilitate the collection and analysis of Cap/Exp project data to support the building of plans and forecasts.
</li>
<li>Communicate plan specifics and progress to leaders up to VP level.
</li>
<li>Produce variance, trend, and comparison data pertaining to past, current, and future projects and forecasts</li>
<li>
Develop the many tools needed to carry out the Cap/Exp budgeting and forecasting and assist in the training support of others as they use these tools.
</li>
<li>Assists other team members across the Supply Chain that participate in the purchasing and project execution process</li>
<li>
Work effectively with VP’s, Directors, Managers, and Individual Contributors in Supply Chain, Finance, Accounting, and Engineering and Construction at both the SSC and field locations to enhance both plan execution and Enterprise level reporting.
</li>
</ul>
<br>
Required Education/Experience:<ul>
<li>
Bachelor’s degree in Accounting, Finance, or Supply Chain</li>
<li>
Experience with Microsoft Office Suite, TM1, and Spend Management OR</li>
<li>
High school diploma and 5 plus years of experience AND</li>
<li>
Proficiency in MS Excel, PowerPoint, Access, and Spend Management</li>
</ul>
