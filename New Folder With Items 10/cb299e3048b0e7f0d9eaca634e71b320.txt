<div>
<p><b>Manager, Field Application Scientist Team
</b></p>
<p></p>
<p><b>Location: Rockville, MD or REMOTE (US)
</b></p>
<p></p>
<p><b>Job Description Summary:
</b></p>
<p>As a member of Lonza Personalized Medicine Team, you will work in partnership with the Technical, and Business and Commercial Development teams to support the use of the Cocoon Cell Manufacturing Platform. Your role is to manage a team of application scientists globally. In this capacity, you will assist in developing the customer experience, build internal and external training programs, and represent the cocoon from a technical perspective at conferences and customer site visits. You and your team will be the primary face of the cocoon to our clients in a supporting role .
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>Job Description and Key Accountabilities:
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Build and lead a team of field application scientists responsible for developing and maintaining, with the assistance of the internal technical team, cocoon training programs for both internal stakeholders and external customer focused programs
</li>
<li>Provide customer facing demonstrations of Lonza cell and gene therapy manufacturing processes including CAR-T, NK and HSC processes within the cocoon
</li>
<li>Forward looking vision for documenting and developing custom applications to fit unique customer needs
</li>
<li>Perform Cocoon and Cocoon cassette instrument trainings at customer sites and assist in developing and refining customer training SOPs
</li>
<li>Diligent work effort for identifying and tracking issues that arise with customers, and ensuring they are resolved in a timely manner
</li>
<li>Maintain customer satisfaction by assisting with process development and troubleshooting assays
</li>
<li>Develop and maintain relationships with key customers and participate actively as a team player in key account plans
</li>
<li>Facilitate adoption of products in clinical applications by working within appropriate regulatory frameworks and with the support of regulatory affairs
</li>
<li>Ensure a solid understanding of working in regulated environments ( GxP )
</li>
<li>Perform demonstrations of Lonza instruments
</li>
<li>Help new team members develop key competencies and share best practices
</li>
<li>Work with sales and business development teams to support activities such as technical seminars and conferences
</li>
<li>Perform other duties as assigned.
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>Minimum Required Qualifications, Skills and Education:
</b></p>
<p><b>Education: </b>B.A. or B.S. degree in a life science discipline
</p>
<p><b>Work Experience: </b>5-10 years experience in clinical cell processing or application involving clinical devices; or equivalent combination of education and experience
</p>
<p><b>Required:
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>3-5 years: Experience in clinical cell processing or application involving clinical devices; or equivalent combination of education and experience.
</li>
<li>Familiarity with basic computer logic concepts and project management software
</li>
<li>Strong time management and organizational skills
</li>
<li>Strong written communication and oral presentation skills
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>Preferred:
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>5 years: Proven medical sales experience and/or customer training, preferably in the hematology/oncology or cell and gene therapy field; or equivalent combination of education and experiences.
</li>
<li>Advanced degree in a life sciences or medical technology discipline
</li>
<li>Experience in cell manufacturing
</li>
<li>Prior experience in support of a regulated medical device is desired, and your technical expertise includes experience in clinical immunology, cellular therapy, or blood banking/processing
</li>
<li>Experience in laboratory instrument programming, clinical cell processing or applications involving clinical devices, development and testing of applications
</li>
<li>Project management and documentation expertise<br>
</li>
</ul>
