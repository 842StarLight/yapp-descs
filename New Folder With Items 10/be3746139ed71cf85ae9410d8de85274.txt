<h2>Senior Director, New Product Development</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><b>Summary Description:
</b></p>
<p>Reporting to the Chief Product Officer and General Manager, Assessments, the Senior Director, New Product Development will be held accountable for managing the overall new product development function and leading the development and implementation of GMAC’s innovation pipeline for the assessments line of business. The senior director will be responsible for hands-on leadership of the product development team as it contributes to GMAC’s mission to provide the solutions necessary for schools and candidates to discover and evaluate each other as well as GMAC’s desire to pursue revenue diversification.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>This role works in partnership with senior executives to identify target development areas and with staff, clients, partners, and vendors to explore, design and develop solutions and support the early-stage delivery of new products. Central to this is the identification of needs/pain points and opportunities in the marketplace; evaluation of the competitive landscape and creation of clear and robust business plans. The ideal candidate will be highly entrepreneurial, curious, analytically rigorous, and comfortable with ambiguity. They will also be familiar with agile development methodologies and the use of technology as an enabler of product innovation.
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>Key Responsibilities </b>:
</p>
<p>Plans, directs, manages and oversees all long and short-term activities and operations of the product development team. Identify and explore new digital product or platform opportunities in partnership with internal and external stakeholders. To include:
</p>
<ul>
<li>Engage with the industry ecosystem to develop relationships and surface needs and opportunities
</li>
<li>Lead the team in early and mid‐stage market research relevant to new product ideas &amp; concepts
</li>
<li>Perform needs’ and competitive analysis and conduct pilot/concept or other market tests to inform design and prototype of solutions.
</li>
<li>Optimize and leverage a solid understanding of the software development process including the ability to sketch out concepts for evaluation, create and deploy low resolution prototypes for testing/feedback, create and document detailed requirements and user stories.
</li>
<li>Pitch ideas and concepts to potential and existing clients, senior managers, and GMAC leadership.
</li>
<li>Create operational and financial models – including sensitivity analysis for business case.
</li>
<li>Drive initial market development and incubation of new products for the first 12‐18 months, including road mapping potential paths to scale
</li>
<li>Transition product to existing market facing teams upon conclusion of successful pilot
</li>
<li>Other responsibilities and duties, as assigned.
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>Minimum Requirements:
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Seven (7) years progressive professional experience in product development, product management, consulting or related field, with five years of experience developing or managing a digital product.
</li>
<li>MBA or Graduate Degree in Business, Economics, Engineering or other relevant field or Undergraduate Degree in relevant field plus three (3) additional years of work‐related experience.
</li>
<li>Ability to inspire, motivate and successfully lead internal and external teams based on five (5) or more years of managerial experience, with responsibility for budget, people and process development;
</li>
<li>Ability to propose and implement strategy to influence and support the senior leadership team
</li>
<li>Ability to resolve complex issues of significance to the organization that have direct impact on the success of the function both long-term and short-term
</li>
<li>Experience using a variety of approaches to surface problems/insights (e.g., one‐on‐one interviewing, conducting focus groups, doing empathy research, voice of the customer analysis, customer experience mapping, quantitative analysis, and survey research).
</li>
<li>Superior analytical skills and ability to prioritize objectives
</li>
<li>Exceptional communication and collaborative skills
</li>
<li>Technical proficiency in Excel, PowerPoint, JIRA, Trello, Adobe CS and other relevant software applications.
</li>
<li>Ability to travel up to 30% worldwide
</li>
</ul>
<p>The physical demands and work environment characteristics described are representative of those that must be met by an employee to successfully perform the essential functions of this job . Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions.
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>Desired skills/experience:
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Ten (10) years of closely related experience and results.
</li>
<li>Ability to quickly develop a deep knowledge of a marketplace, customer needs, and relevant capabilities, both internal and external, that might be used to address the need—especially if no prior industry experience.
</li>
<li>Ability to structure, organize, analyze, and present/communicate insights and “stories” effectively. Demonstrably experience surfacing unique observations/conditions alongside their short and long‐term implications.
</li>
<li>Experience with business case analysis for partnerships or small acquisitions (i.e. valuation, due diligence) as well as negotiating and contracting with partners and vendors.
</li>
<li>Proven experience to personally generate early sales as well as training and motivating others.
</li>
<li>Proficiency in developing and adapting business models, as well as identifying limitations in proposed business models.
</li>
<li>Agility to self‐direct response to feedback and refine strategy and approach as needed
</li>
<li>Experience analyzing and visualizing data using Stata, SPSS, R GEPHI or Python
</li>
<li>Professional experience in higher education and/or prior experience with support, design, or delivery of educational, interpersonal, cognitive or other types of individual assessments.
</li>
<li>Entrepreneurial self‐starter comfortable with ambiguity of tasks and objectives in a dynamic environment. Able to identify, network and collaborate with organizations with complementary strengths and develop mutually beneficial relationships.
</li>
</ul>
