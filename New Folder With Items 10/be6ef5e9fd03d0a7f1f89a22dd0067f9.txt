<h2>Life Cycle Engineer (Instrument Systems)</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>Quidel Corporation is a leading manufacturer of diagnostic healthcare solutions serving to enhance the health and well being of people around the globe with well known and respected products that provide healthcare professionals with accurate and cost-effective diagnostic information at the point of care (POC). Our core competencies and capabilities focus on immunoassay and molecular testing in the areas of: infectious disease, cardiology, women’s health, and virology. Quidel's Intrument Systems team is searching for a Life Cycle Engineer to focus on system level support of on-market instrument products, including monitoring field performance to identify needs for change; soliciting input from stakeholders for product improvements; prioritizing change requests and scheduling improvements according to resource and budget availability; and leading cross-functional teams to develop, verify and validate, and implement changes. Instrument design changes may be to mechanical or electronics hardware, software, or assay methods. assay integration, technology transfer and validation activities. The incumbent may also provide technical support to manufacturing in addressing issues that may / can cause product supply interruptions or considering manufacturing process improvement. This role will require cross funcational work across the organization: with Sales, Marketing, Technical Support, and Quality Assurance to understand field performance of on-market instrument products and to prioritize needs for change; with R&amp;D to understand and prioritize instrument changes (including assay metho; ds) needed to develop and commercialize new menu; within Instrument Systems to identify the appropriate personnel to develop design changes. The Life Cyclel Engineer trains personnel (e.g., Technical Support, Field Application Specialists) as necessary prior to change implementation, maintains design change documentation, and provides guidance to developers as needed to prevent implementation of designs that do not meet manufacturing standards. ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS: -Actively participates on design teams, understands Instrument Systems goals and timelines, and provides leadership to ensure change success. -Actively communicates project status, challenges, concerns and resource constraints to area management. -Communicates requirements for design changes to developers and coordinates development, design verification, design validation, and implementation -Actively solicits input from key stakeholders -Writes design change documentation and executes/supervise associated activities. -Maintains detailed and organized notebook records. -Aids in improvement of manufacturing processes.
