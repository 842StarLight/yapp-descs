<h2>Product Owner</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>The mission of the Syndigo product owner is to understand the needs of our users and how those needs fit into the overall vision for each product or feature.</p>
<p>
The Product Owner will be responsible for the end-to-end process related to his or her features, including: developing functional and non-functional business requirements, collaborate with engineering to build features according to business needs, train internal and external stakeholders on new product functionality, and function as the product/technical lead during sales cycles. This role will require someone who understands technology and business, a high degree of organization and attention to detail, and the ability to professionally interact with a diverse blend of personalities to reach resolution and maintain strong relationships.</p>
<p>
The preference would be for this role to be based in Syndigo's Nashville office, but candidates in Cleveland, Chicago, or Waukesha will also be considered.</p>
<p>
RESPONSIBILITIES</p>
<ul>
<li>Provide consistency and clarity to software development projects by identifying business needs and constraints, defining scope, and ensuring efficient execution</li>
<li>Responsible for defining requirements and recommending solutions in the form of epics, user stories and acceptance criteria</li>
<li>Identify all functional and non-functional requirements</li>
<li>Work with engineering and business stakeholders to groom and prioritize the backlog of user stories through the release and sprint cycles</li>
<li>Act as a proxy for the business throughout development, testing, and release activities to ensure the business needs are being met</li>
<li>Develop business analysis artifacts, such as business rules or process flows to clarify requirements</li>
<li>Coordinate with other departments in advance of feature and product launches to develop messaging to evangelize new releases, features, or updates</li>
<li>Conduct product training, both internal and externally, including, but not limited to: training internal trainer(s), collaborating with technical writer to product new product/feature documentation, introduce new functionality to internal employees or key customers</li>
<li>Participate in sales cycles as the technical/product lead. Prepare and deliver technical presentations/demos explaining products or services to customers and prospective customers.</li>
<li>Collect and document competitive intelligence</li>
</ul>
