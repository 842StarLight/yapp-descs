<h2>Program Manager, Facilities: Gear Central and Core Fitness</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>Manage Gear Central and Core Fitness center for Campus Recreation Services. Gear Central is the Student Life Center’s home-base for equipment sales and rental, daily locker rentals, racquetball court reservations, and in-house cleaning service for uniforms and equipment. The Core Fitness area is a state of the art fitness center providing cardio and strength machines and functional fitness opportunities for the university community.</p>
Responsibilities
<p>1. Forecasts, develops and implements policies and procedures in the areas of customer service, human resources and budgetary goals and objectives that are consistent with University of Utah’s policies.<br>
2. Markets and promotes new and existing programs and services to internal and external customers.<br>
3. Participates in University-wide committees to establish customer service standards and administrative plans and policies.<br>
4. Facilitates cooperative and collaborative community and institutional relationships.<br>
5. Ensures customer satisfaction by analyzing complaints, concerns and suggestions and providing appropriate follow-through.<br>
6. Develops departmental strategies consistent with the assigned departments continuous quality improvement program.<br>
7. Ensures competency of staff through the development and/or presentation of educational programs and through on going staff performance evaluations.</p>

Minimum Qualifications
<p>Bachelor’s degree in a related area or equivalency (2 years related work experience may be substituted for 1 year of education).<br>
Four to six years of progressively more responsible management experience. Background knowledge of hiring department’s area of specialization in order to analyze, plan and draw conclusions for recommendations to superiors. Demonstrated leadership skills in planning and directing employees and processes in order to effectively monitor and develop subordinates and to ensure the smooth operation of department. Master’s degree in a related area may be preferred.</p>
<p><b>
Department Specific Qualifications</b></p>
<p>
Bachelor’s degree, Master’s preferred. Two years full-time experience in program management, demonstrated experience of equipment sales, inventory control and POS software systems. Supervisory experience managing diverse fitness area, including knowledge of cardio and strength equipment; experience with court scheduling; supervision of student staff; inventory control; knowledge of risk management; excellent guest service skills required.</p>
<p>
Certification in CPR required.</p>

Preferences
<p><b>ESSENTIAL</b><b> </b><b>FUNCTIONS</b><br>
The following information is intended to be representative of the work performed by incumbents in this position and is not all-inclusive. The omission of a specific duty of responsibility will not preclude it from the position of the work is similar, related, or a logical extension of positions responsibilities.</p>
<p>
Position has full responsibility to organize, manage, and supervise the daily operations of Gear Central and The Core Fitness areas in the Student Life Center. This includes the management of daily sales and rental operations, the management of The Core fitness center, product purchasing, hiring, training and supervision of student staff, collaboration with fellow colleagues, and adherence to risk management and safe operations procedures.</p>
<p>
*Student Staff Supervision *<br>
Hire, train, schedule, evaluate, and process payroll for approximately 35-40 part time staff; supervise and manage daily operations; coordinate student trainings and supervision with<br>
current Graduate Assistant; manage and train student supervisors; ensure all staff are current PCI and CPR certified; help and support student staff with staff integration, professional development, and CRS scholarships.</p>
<p><b>
Administrative</b><br>
In collaboration with the Assistant Director of Facilities, assist with guidelines and procedures for Gear Central and The Core. Ensure compliance with University policies, rental fees, administrative requirements, and rental rates; submit participation reports and annual reports; evaluate and coordinate updates of POS rental system with program developer; daily accounting, budgeting and record keeping for Gear Central; backup management duties with daily accounting and staffing for Membership Services; work schedule includes evening shifts several nights weekly to ensure protocols and safety.</p>
<p><b>
Gear Central</b><br>
Manage the daily rental inventory, end of night rental inventory, maintenance of inventory, and replacement of inventory; manage the POS rental system; procurement and management of merchandise sales; equipment management; management of student staff uniforms; scheduling and monitoring area cleaning; documentation management of Lost and Found; problem solving.</p>
<p><b>
Core Fitness</b><br>
Daily monitoring and management of student staff in The Core fitness center. Manage documentation for staff that supervises The Core. Develop machine cleaning schedules for staff; submit maintenance requests; maintain area records pertaining to opening/closing checklists, and cleaning records; staff monitoring of policy training and enforcement.</p>
