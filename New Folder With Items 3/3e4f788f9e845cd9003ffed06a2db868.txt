<h2>Administrative Program Manager 2</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div><div>
<div><b>Supplemental Information</b></div>
<div><p><b>Job Number: OPH/AS/0913</b><br>
<br>
<b>This position is located within the Louisiana Department of Health l Office of Public Health l Capitol Region 2 l EBR Parish l Baton Rouge, LA.</b><br>
<br>
<b>Cost Center: 0326-8020</b><br>
<b>Position Number: 50563058</b><br>
<br>
<b>This vacancy is being announced as a Classified position and may be filled as a Job Appointment, Probationary Appointment or Promotional Appointment.</b><br>
<br>
<b><i>This position provides administrative support for matters impacting the programs and operations of LDH/Office of Public Health in Region 2 and assists the Public Health Regional Medical Director/Administrator (unclassified Physician IV) and the Assistant Regional Administrator (Program Mgr. 1B-LDH) with fiscal operations, personnel management and physical plant maintenance of Regional Office, and Health Unit Offices in Region 2 (5 sites). This position will also provide direct supervision of Administrative Coordinator 4 positions in Region 2. This position is responsible for administrative functions including purchasing, telecommunications, inventory property control, contracts and grants, safety/risk management and audits.</i></b><br>
<br>
<b><i>Routinely assists in planning, development and implementation of regional policy and procedure for delivery of OPH services. Serves as liaison between Region 2 and program administrators in OPH Central Office to verify compliance with procedures, eliminate duplication and maximize efficiency.</i></b><br>
<br>
<b><i>Responds to inquiries from elected officials, city, parish, state and federal agencies, and the public concerning Agency and program functions. Represent the Regional Medical Director/Administrator and OPH at various meetings.</i></b><br>
<br>
<b><i>90% Review the budget requests and calculate costs of requests. Review requests for positions, equipment, supplies and other expenditures submitted by staff members. Obtain additional information from staff or other sources (descriptions, costs, justifications, etc.) in order to present recommendation to Public Health Regional Medical Director/Administrator.</i></b><br>
<br>
<b><i>Prepare all budget forms, complete with justifications, and submit annual budget request to OPH Budget Office, following approval by Public Health Regional Medical Director/Administrator.</i></b><br>
<br>
<b><i>Monitor budget status throughout the year, reviewing current and projected expenditures and information, advising Public Health Regional Medical Director/Administrator of problems areas. Review and approve/deny requisitions for supplies and equipment in Region 2 facilities after determining justification for expenditure and availability of funds. Approve Petty Cash expenditures of all Region 2 offices. Serves as back up approver for purchases and expenditures for both the Regional Office and the health units within the region. Serves liaison for the region with the State Purchasing Office/Director.</i></b><br>
<br>
<b><i>Negotiate contracts on behalf of Public Health Regional Medical Director/Administrator. Prepare documents (contract forms, amendments, budgets and reports) for all professional services contracts according to DOA, LDH, and OPH policy and procedure. Obtain required signatures. Approve payments made to contractors. Routinely monitor contracts to assure availability of funding and provision of services.</i></b><br>
<br>
<b><i>Direct supervision of (6) Administrative Coordinator 4's in the parish health units. Functional supervision of 16 clerical support staff region-wide. Determine staffing priorities and coordinate work assignments throughout the region to provide optimal service delivery for multiple programs. Evaluate job performance, recommend disciplinary action, prepare and monitor supervisory plans, approve and monitor leave.</i></b><br>
<br>
<b><i>Identify staff development needs, arrange appropriate training, Provide on-going training to administrative and support staff for new programs, revisions in procedures, operations, reporting requirements, etc. Serves as a resource to all administrative support staff and other staff when needed, relative to program guidelines, policies and procedures of LaGov purchasing, LEERS, SAP payroll, Operation and Support Property Management, WIC, Reproductive Health, Immunization, Electronic Health Records and Vital Records to ensure coordination of complex administrative duties and clerical support of service delivery in the region.</i></b><br>
<br>
<b><i>Interview applicants and make recommendations on hiring of clerical staff to Regional Medical Director/Administrator.</i></b><br>
<br>
<b><i>Assist Regional Medical Director/Administrator and Assistant Regional Administrator in management of assigned positions and determine appropriate method for filling vacancies.</i></b><br>
<br>
<b><i>Approve Personnel Action (HR-1) requests in the absence of the Public Health Regional Medical Director/Administrator or Regional Assistant Administrator.</i></b><br>
<br>
<b><i>Audits office's/units payroll records to ensure that reports completed and maintained accurately according to policy. Serves as payroll liaison with LDH Human Resource. Communicates changes, informs and assist Time Administrators with any concerns, problems or new procedures.</i></b><br>
<br>
<b><i>Serves as designated Vital Records Local Registrar for Region 2. Provides administrative and technical resource to Parish Deputy Registrars and Vital records clerks. Coordinates Vital Records services for the region and provides liaison to State Vital Record clerks. Disseminates State policy and procedure and assists Local Deputy Registrars with any new laws affecting the filing or completion of death certificates as well as all other vital records procedures. Assist Funeral Home Directors and Central Office Vital Records state office with any concerns, problems or new procedures to ensure the smooth operation of all vital records offices in the region.</i></b><br>
<br>

<div><b>Qualifications</b></div>
<p></p>
<div>
<div>MINIMUM QUALIFICATIONS:</div>
<div>
A baccalaureate degree plus two years of professional level experience in administrative services.</div>
<div>
</div>
<div>
SUBSTITUTIONS:</div>
<div>
Six years of full-time work experience in any field may be substituted for the required baccalaureate degree.</div>
<div>
</div>
<div>
Candidates without a baccalaureate degree may combine work experience and college credit to substitute for the baccalaureate degree as follows:</div>
<div>
</div>
<div>
A maximum of 120 semester hours may be combined with experience to substitute for the baccalaureate degree.</div>
<div>
</div>
<div>
30 to 59 semester hours credit will substitute for one year of experience towards the baccalaureate degree.</div>
<div>
60 to 89 semester hours credit will substitute for two years of experience towards the baccalaureate degree.</div>
<div>
90 to 119 semester hours credit will substitute for three years of experience towards the baccalaureate degree.</div>
<div>
120 or more semester hours credit will substitute for four years of experience towards the baccalaureate degree.</div>
<div>
</div>
<div>
College credit earned without obtaining a baccalaureate degree may be substituted for a maximum of four years full-time work experience towards the baccalaureate degree. Candidates with 120 or more semester hours of credit, but without a degree, must also have at least two years of full-time work experience to substitute for the baccalaureate degree.</div>
<div>
</div>
<div>
NOTE:</div>
<div>
Examples of professional administrative service fields include: Accounting, Financial Auditing, Budget work, Contract/Grants work, Human Resources/Personnel, Information technology, Insurance, Purchasing/Procurement, Quality assurance, Records management, Risk management, and Safety. In the area of wholesale/retail sales, we will also accept store managers, business managers, regional sales managers, or district sales managers as experience in administrative services.</div>
<div>
</div>
<div>
NOTE:</div>
<div>
Any college hours or degree must be from a school accredited by one of the following regional accrediting bodies: the Middle States Commission on Higher Education; the New England Commission of Higher Education; the Higher Learning Commission; the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities; the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools; and the Western Association of Schools and Colleges.</div>
</div>

<div><b>Job Concepts</b></div>
<p></p>
<div>
<div>FUNCTION OF WORK:</div>
<div>
To manage administrative program areas and support functions for an organizational institution, office, or department.</div>
<div>
</div>
<div>
LEVEL OF WORK:</div>
<div>
Manager.</div>
<div>
</div>
<div>
SUPERVISION RECEIVED:</div>
<div>
Broad from an agency administrator or manager of a higher-level.</div>
<div>
</div>
<div>
SUPERVISION EXERCISED:</div>
<div>
Direct over 2 or more lower-level positions.</div>
<div>
</div>
<div>
LOCATION OF WORK:</div>
<div>
May be used by all agencies.</div>
<div>
</div>
<div>
JOB DISTINCTIONS:</div>
<div>
Administrative Program Manager jobs within this job series differ by a combination of independence, organizational control, complexity, and scope.</div>
<div>
</div>
<div>
Administrative Program Manager 2 positions differ from those allocated to the job of Administrative Program Director 2 by the absence of directing administrative programs and support functions and by the supervision exercised.</div>
</div>

<div><b>Examples of Work</b></div>
<p></p>
<div><div>Any level of this series may involve two-to-twenty administrative functions, such as; human resources, purchasing, information technology, inventory, property control, accounting, safety, risk management, records management, security, inventory, mail operations, contracts/grants, insurance, and budget.</div></div>
