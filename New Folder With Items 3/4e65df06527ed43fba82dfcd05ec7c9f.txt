<h2>Fitter/Welder</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><b>Company: </b>Gunderson LLC</p>
<p>
Gunderson traces its roots to 1919 when Chet Gunderson opened Wire Wheel Sales and Service Company of Portland, Oregon. His brother, Al Gunderson joined in 1923 and shortly thereafter Gunderson Brothers was born. They became a leading steel fabricator and manufactured thousands of vital ship components during World War II. Today, we are the market leader in intermodal railcar production. Our team of industry experts take great pride in our work - our manufacturing capabilities leverage the latest engineering software, training programs and production techniques. The result: proven innovation to deliver the highest quality, innovative design and on-time delivery.</p>
<p><b>
GENERAL POSITION SUMMARY:</b></p>
<p>
Fit and weld fabricated steel components to assemble structural forms in the assembly and production of ocean-going barges, marine equipment and or railcars. Perform Fitter Welder work accordingly at the direction of Gunderson Rail and Marine Supervisory personnel.</p>
<p><b>
PRIMARY RESPONSIBILITIES AND TRAINING NEEDS:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Trained in accordance with job responsibilities and applicable specifications</li>
<li>Tack parts together in preparation for welding</li>
<li>Set up and operate welding equipment</li>
<li>Visually inspect completed welds to assure adherence to specifications</li>
<li>Perform precise fitting work on components</li>
<li>Ability to work any shift preferred</li>
</ul> <p><b>BENEFICIAL JOB SKILLS (will be required to progress to top end of wage scale):</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Must be able to burn efficiently and with good quality using a hand-held oxy-fuel cutting torch</li>
<li>Ability to operate Carbon Arc cutting/gouging equipment (scarf)</li>
<li>Blueprint reading and layout skills</li>
<li>Ability to troubleshoot minor welding equipment problems</li>
<li>Able to do basic math fractions and read a tape measure</li>
</ul> <p><b>MINIMUM REQUIREMENTS:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Must be at least 18 years of age</li>
<li>Must consent to a background, drug screen, and physical</li>
<li>Able to follow safety procedures</li>
<li>Ability to follow verbal/written instructions</li>
<li>Ideal candidate possesses the ability to work autonomously or as part of a team</li>
<li>Good communication and interpersonal skills</li>
<li>Ability to work collaboratively and maintain effective working relationships with co-workers</li>
<li>Motivated to continuously improve and learn new skills</li>
</ul> <p><b>PHYSICAL REQUIREMENTS:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Able to sit, stand, walk, bend, twist, kneel, walk, climb, and reach 1-8 hours per day (must be comfortable working in confined spaces)</li>
<li>Capable of frequently lifting up to 50 pounds for the duration of an 8-10 hour shift</li>
<li>Ability to work in all weather conditions including extreme heat and cold</li>
<li>Comfortable working in an environment that contains dust and loud noises</li>
<li>Able to climb ladders and work off scaffolding (must be comfortable with heights)</li>
<li>Must be clean shaven as you will be required to wear Personal Protective Equipment which includes respirator</li>
<li>Ability to work in a fast-paced heavy industrial production environment</li>
</ul>
<p><b>PREFERRED EDUCATION AND/OR EXPERIENCE:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Able to do math fractions, read a tape measure and pass the written weld test</li>
<li>Ability to: weld and become qualified in 1/16" flux core wire in all positions with unlimited thickness plate.</li>
</ul>
