<h2>Field Service Engineer</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<div><b>Field Service Engineer</b></div>
<div>
<br>
<b>Type of Business: </b>Sales and service of business machines and software solutions</div>
<ul>
<li>Job Title: Field Service Engineer</li>
<li>Main Goals and Responsibilities: Must be able to interact sociably and professionally with a diverse client base, troubleshoot and repair a wide array of business machines equally divided between mechanical and theoretical problems, possess a basic to moderate understanding of computer networks and be responsible for inventory and time management. Initial training will be quite extensive for the first 90 days. Ongoing training and learning is a large component of the position.</li>
<li>Products and Services: Copiers, faxes, scanners and networked printers. Manufacturers include Konica Minolta, Lexmark, Kyocera, Muratec, Toshiba and Panasonic</li>
<li>Essential Activities: Interact with clients and overcome objections; 50%, Troubleshoot and repair equipment, including network related issues; 45%, Manage inventory; 5%</li>
<li>People Managed: Self-managed, must be able to establish and maintain relationships with clients</li>
<li>Primary Contacts: (external): administrative assistants, teachers, principals, law enforcement, owners, executives and managers, laborers (internal): management, admin, sales, fellow technicians, customer service, parts controllers, technical trainers</li>
<li>Behavioral Traits, Attitudes and Skills: Must be someone who thrives in adversity and change, possesses strong social and communication abilities, is highly self-motivated, has a strong work ethic and has an average to above average degree of competitiveness and desire to win. The ideal candidate will have a strong attention to results and have no problems being held accountable. Must possess both strong mechanical and computer skills.</li>
<li>Prerequisites: HS diploma with training or experience in computers and/or electronics, military experience a plus.</li>
<li>Toughest Part of Job: (startup): learning basic machine functions and acclamation to culture and procedures (ongoing): continuous improvement, change and adversity in the industry and organization, working with clients, demand to perform and work on equipment they may not be formally trained on</li>
<li>Compensation: hourly wage of $12-$13 for those with no experience, minimal overtime, proficiency bonus paid out monthly based on performance, possible advancement opportunities</li>
</ul>
