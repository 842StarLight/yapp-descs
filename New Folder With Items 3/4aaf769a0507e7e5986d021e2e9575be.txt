<h2>Design Center Consultant</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><ul><li>
<p><b>Job Description</b></p>
<p>
Design consultants will work with new homebuyers in selecting their interior finishes for their new home. This is an exciting experience for our clients but can also be a little overwhelming. As such, the design consultant is responsible for creating a positive, welcoming and innovative experience for the homebuyers. The design consultant will work with the homebuyers in assisting them with the color selections and options for interior finishes of the home. Flooring, cabinetry, countertops, plumbing, lighting, appliances, etc.</p>
<p>
Must have excellent interpersonal/communication skills, detail-oriented, and proficiency in Microsoft Office applications (Excel, Word and Outlook). Interior Design degree a plus, but not required.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p><b>
Primary Tasks &amp; Responsibilities</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Assess homebuyer(s) needs, preferences, and expectations regarding their lifestyle and specific products for their new home</li>
<li>Advise homebuyer(s) on interior design factors, such as space planning, layout and color coordination</li>
<li>Ensure that homebuyer(s) is aware of all options available for product upgrades</li>
<li>Provide complete product information detailing performance, durability, and maintenance of homebuyer(s) selections</li>
<li>Estimate material requirements and costs</li>
<li>Accurately record product selections using established department procedures, systems, and practices</li>
<li>Assists in resolving homebuyer(s) problems and/or concerns</li>
<li>Supports all design center and homebuilder processes</li>
<li>Is knowledgeable and able to communicate the design center experience and the overall new home buying experience</li>
<li>Will be working closely with sales counselors at all communities…building relationships</li>
<li>Learn and is knowledgeable about all products in the showroom – attend all Product Knowledge (“PK') training</li>
<li>Learn and is knowledgeable about all communities</li>
<li>Adhere to and manage builder time frames for lot file submission</li>
<li>Conduct browse appointments when necessary</li>
<li>Required Sales metrics management and average sales numbers are met</li>
<li>Performs other duties as assigned.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>May provide general guidance/direction to or train junior level support personnel or professional personnel.</li>
<br>
</ul>
<p><b>
</b></p>
<p><b>Work Environment</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Most of the time is spent sitting or standing in the same location or there may be a need to stoop regularly or move/lift light material or equipment (typically less than 8 pounds)</li>
<br>
</ul>
<p><b>
</b></p>
<p><b>Education &amp; Experience</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Experience in construction; including flooring, cabinet, and countertops</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor’s degree or equivalent in Interior Design</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Ability to effectively present information and respond to questions from groups or manager, clients, customers and the general public</li>
<li>Interpersonal skills to develop and maintain professional working relationships with builders, vendors, subordinates and co-workers</li>
<li>Ability to proactively identify, analyze, address and resolve problem areas, conflicts and issues; strong decision-making skills</li>
<li>Organizational skills to insure adequate tracking systems are utilized to maintain and track all required information associated with the ordering and production process</li>
<li>Working knowledge of internet, inventory, project management, spreadsheet and word process software and order processing systems</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Must be proficient with Microsoft Office Products</li>
<br>
</ul>
