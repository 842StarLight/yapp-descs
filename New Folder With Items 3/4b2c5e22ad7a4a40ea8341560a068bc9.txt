<p><b>Job Description</b></p>
<p>The Regional Turnover Manager is responsible for oversight of turnover projects of residential rental homes and small multifamily units in their assigned market. This role is also very customer-centric, requiring proactive and empathetic communication with our property owners, and delivering excellent outcomes for them, defined as affordable, quick, high-quality turns. This is a managerial position to hire, train and oversee a team of field professionals as well as building and working with a local vendor base. The SFM leads a team in a production environment to accomplish company goals.</p>
<p><b>Essential Job Functions</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Directly manage 2-6 turnover evaluators, inspectors, and turnover technicians</li>
<li>Manage the turnover process from move-out to rent-ready for up to 100 individual projects per month. Minimize project duration to ensure minimal vacancy loss for owners.</li>
<li>Validate budgets to ensure the best possible value for owners</li>
<li>Communicate with and set expectations for general contractors. Validate contractor quality and speed. Schedule with contractors, providing advanced notice to prevent mobilization gaps and distributing assignments based on contractor capacity.</li>
<li>Source new contractors and renegotiate pricing with existing vendors to ensure the best possible value for owners.</li>
<li>Coordinate the execution of national account portions of turnover projects</li>
<li>Coordinate closely with move-out and leasing teams to ensure a seamless transition at the commencement and completion of each project</li>
<li>Conduct training, counseling, and professional development for direct reports</li>
<li>Working in close coordination with other teams, effectively and efficiently schedule direct reports</li>
<li>Resolve exceptions to the turnover process, including process delays</li>
<li>Recommend process improvements based on experience and observation</li>
<li>Proactively keep owners informed and handle owner escalations</li>
<li>Other duties as assigned by the Regional Manager</li>
