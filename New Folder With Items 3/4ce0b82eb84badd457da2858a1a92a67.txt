<h2>Network Operations Supervisor</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><b>About Frontier Communications</b></p>
<p>
Frontier® Communications provides communications services to urban, suburban, and rural communities in 29 states. Frontier offers a variety of services to residential customers over its FiOS® and Vantage™ fiber-optic and copper networks, including video, high-speed internet, advanced voice, and Frontier Secure® digital protection solutions. Frontier Business™ offers communications solutions to small, medium, and enterprise businesses.<br>
<br>
<b>The Network Operations Supervisor will act as am Advanced Technical Services (ATS) Supervisor</b><br>
<br>
<b>ATS Supervisor – Job Description and Requirements</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Work with the Triad team analyzing current challenges and resolving issues</li>
<li>
Work with DPI team resolving issues</li>
<li>
Work with IT/ OSS teams diagnosing and resolving issues</li>
<li>
Resolve and enhance field experience</li>
<li>
Drive the Service Incident Report (SIR) process for ATS team</li>
<li>
Values Frontier Communications mission and values and fully understands what providing the BEST ”Customer Experience” means</li>
<li>
Is highly customer focused and goes above and beyond to ensure a great customer experience.</li>
<li>
Actively listens on floor and intervenes if agent is not delivering an outstanding customer experience.</li>
<li>
Demonstrates a sense of urgency in resolving customer impacting issue, involving other organizations as needed in resolution or escalating as necessary</li>
<li>
Understands the impact of Broken Promises / missed commitments and actively works to eliminate them.</li>
<li>
Always includes discussion of the customer when providing feedback.</li>
<li>
Strong customer service skills and ability to inspire the same within their team – We cannot let our customers down.</li>
<li>
Understands how actions can affect the business. Has ability to create a customer centric and results oriented culture.</li>
</ul>
<p>
 <b>Performance Excellence/Management:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Understands and knows how to achieve a balanced scorecard (Field Tech calls, TSO BOLD chats, Offline Tickets, Outages / Common Cause functions, AHT, ASA, ACW, Attendance, Productivity, Adherence).</li>
<li>
Drives performance in team and holds self and others accountable.</li>
<li>
Delivers consistent results. Identifies and motivates bottom performers to achieve results. Does not tolerate poor or inconsistent performance.</li>
<li>
Keeps team informed of the team scorecard and how they are performing relative to other centers.</li>
<li>
Able to identify gaps in both technical and soft skills (empathy, patience, etc.) for self and others.</li>
<li>
Is open to feedback and takes action to remedy performance or behavioral shortcomings for self and team.</li>
<li>
Is always striving for better performance…is not satisfied with “good enough” or just meeting objectives</li>
<li>
Is always learning and willing to learn new things. Able to locate the answers in documents; be able to identify what is unknown, and then follow through to learn the answer</li>
<li>
Attends every training that is offered to their agents, as well as invests in themselves through attending Cornerstone courses/webcasts, articles, etc. Knows what they are coaching to.</li>
<li>
Can assess risks and make decisions.</li>
<li>
Ability to analyze and extricate data. Able to correlate KPI results to behaviors that will positively impact results.</li>
</ul>
<p>
 <b>Coaching Excellence:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Sets objectives and expectations early and revisits them often. Committed to dealing/addressing with team performance opportunities.</li>
<li>
Listens with empathy during coaching sessions. Should coach on how to move a number by changing behaviors</li>
<li>
Is present and visible on the floor. Utilizes tools/resources for live coaching on the floor.</li>
<li>
Formally (and publicly) recognizes at least 1 person on team each week. Uses recognition often to encourage good behavior.</li>
<li>
Embrace that the job of a coach is not a 40-hour workweek.</li>
<li>
Hosts regular huddles with team and is an effective communicator.</li>
<li>
Produces quality documentation that demonstrates his/her role in helping employees be successful (Coaching sessions, PIP, discipline, etc.)</li>
<li>
Can articulate the “why” when coaching in reference to customer experience and behaviors.</li>
<li>
Develops an excellent grasp of the CBA (collective bargain agreement) and can create a partnership with the union of understanding, support and success.</li>
<li>
Recognize talent and encourage continued personal and professional development.</li>
<li>
Delivers the right combination of positive feedback along with areas of opportunity.</li>
<li>
Understands that not all people learn the same way and finds alternate ways to teach.</li>
</ul>
<p>
 <b>Teamwork and Engagement (peers and associates):</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Is informed about culture club events and other promotions and actively encourages participation.</li>
<li>
Takes responsibility for performance of the center, not just his/her own team. Addresses bad behavior even when it is outside his/her own team.</li>
<li>
Knows team and takes a personal interest in each individual’s success.</li>
<li>
Fosters relationships within immediate center by teaming with all peers.</li>
<li>
Volunteers to help others, both peers and subordinates, without being told.</li>
<li>
Fosters relationships with other organizations (I&amp;M, DRC, Sales, Retention, Assignment, SPC, etc.). Ability to work cross-functionally to create/develop processes, procedures, or tools.</li>
<li>
Is Positive and Fun motivator, avoids any perception of favoritism, knows when and how to delegate.</li>
<li>
Acknowledge and praise desired behaviors every time they are observed even when the employee is not their direct report.</li>
</ul>
<p>
 <b>Leadership Qualities:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Takes responsibility for actions. Learns from mistakes.</li>
<li>
Is a role model for peers and agents. Is respected by associates and peers. Leads through actions.</li>
<li>
Always does the right thing, even when no one is watching.</li>
<li>
Develops own best practices and looks for best practices from others.</li>
<li>
Is self-motivated. Looks for opportunities to improve performance and culture without being asked.</li>
<li>
Continuously “Thinks out of the box” to come up with new ideas/solutions- open to new ideas</li>
<li>
Not afraid to hold those difficult conversations that can lead to corrective actions.</li>
<li>
Has ability to separate personal issues from professional expectations</li>
<li>
Is confident but humble.</li>
<li>
Is resilient and stays positive. Will not stop searching until the question is answered.</li>
<li>
Ability to adjust quickly and embrace change.</li>
<li>
Views coaching as a team building experience and an opportunity to share and learn from others</li>
<li>
Understands that meeting/ exceeding his objectives IS minimum expectation. A leader doesn’t stop there, leaders make the people around them better.</li>
</ul>
<p>
 <b>Conduct/Ethics:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Maintains confidentiality</li>
<li>
Is respectful to others and helps create a respectful workplace. Follows code of conduct without fail.</li>
<li>
Always represents the company in the most positive terms, does not engage in negative talk about Frontier Communications or Frontier Communications’ leadership or management team (at any level).</li>
<li>
Is honest with team and creates an environment that not being honest is unacceptable when working for them and Frontier Communications. Following the code of conduct is paramount.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>
 </b><b>Administration/Organization/Follow Through/Meeting Commitments:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Is timely in providing feedback. Doesn’t procrastinate or put off tasks.</li>
<li>
Is highly organized and well prepared. Follows through on commitments. Meets deadlines. Is dependable and can be counted on.</li>
<li>
Meets and/or exceeds objectives for day to day job (4x4 coaching, touchpoints, home visits, discipline, performance improvement plans, documentation, etc.)</li>
<li>
Ability to deal with multiple competing demands. Can manage time and set priorities to maintain performance goals.</li>
</ul>

 </b><b>Training/Education Required:</b></p>
<ul>

Bachelor’s Degree</li>
<li>
CCNA, PMP or CWNP a plus</li>
<li>
3 years Supervisory experience managing teams of at least 20 direct reports</li>
</ul>
