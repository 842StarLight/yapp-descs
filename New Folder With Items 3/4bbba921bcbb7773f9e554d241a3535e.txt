<p><b>ABOUT CLEAN OCEAN ACCESS</b></p>
<p>Clean Ocean Access (COA) was established in 2006 and is a growing citizen science environmental organization that became a non-profit organization in 2014. Located in Middletown, Rhode Island with a footprint primarily on Aquidneck Island, performing some off-island efforts to achieve our mission. Our mission is “action today so future generations can enjoy ocean activities” with a primary focus on eliminating marine debris, improving coastal water quality, and protecting and preserving shoreline access. COA is a casual and intense work environment where creativity, flexibility, and collaboration are essential and rely on planning, attention to detail, information technology, operational excellence, and continuous improvement.</p>

<p><b>JOB SUMMARY</b></p>
<p>COA is looking for a professional, relationship-building &amp; community-minded individual who cares deeply about the local environment and wants to be part of an action-packed organization. The Zero Waste Coordinator will expand the composting efforts of the Healthy Soils Healthy Seas Rhode Island Project with a focus on compost collections at restaurants and residents, zero waste at schools and events, and project communications. The individual must be very comfortable with public engagement and customer service, rooted in a solid understanding of the project goals and objectives related to composting, recycling, sustainability, diversion, waste management and ocean health. This position reports to the Executive Director and works directly with the programming and the communications team and may oversee interns and volunteers. The work effort involves the use of diverse technologies, systems, approaches, and closely working with the team using multimedia tools and science communication storytelling skills to advance the mission and impact of the organization.</p>
<p><b>RESPONSIBILITIES</b></p>
<p><b><i>Composting – </i></b>Responsible for identifying and cultivating commercial accounts (primarily restaurants) to implement food scrap diversion (composting), maximize recycling and minimize landfill waste, and working closely with compost collection operators for optimal account experience and outcomes. Perform waste audits where needed. Engage all accounts in visible marketing and outreach at their place of business. Advance food scrap diversion (composting) of residents to increase household participation in curbside pickup, market drop-off, or backyard composting. Identify and perform outreach events to engage neighborhood groups, community groups and other entities to advance composting efforts in Newport County. Coordinate commercial and residential efforts with collections operators and project partners and coordinate metrics for participatory action research. Take pride and ownership in data management and customer relationships.</p>
<p><b><i>Zero waste – </i></b>Responsible for activating zero waste sustainability efforts at schools and events in Newport County with a focus on waste reduction, diversion and management, enabled via metrics. These efforts should culminate into long-lasting systems (Green Teams) that engage individuals, businesses, decision makers and stakeholders to reduce waste and integrate reusable products in all operations, with a focus on eliminating single use material and maximizing food scrap diversion. Activities not limited to outreach, engagement, event strategies, planning, permitting, education, training, sustainable buying practices, workshops, trips to landfill and compost facilities, vendor compliance, steady state operations and reporting.</p>
<p><b><i>Communications – </i></b>Responsible for proactive efforts so that events and interactions lead to continuous storytelling via measurable results, interviews, expressions, and digital media (video and photography) to be used in all types of curated communications and publications to advance the Healthy Soils Healthy Seas Rhode Island Project. Assist the teams to advance the project goals with social media, print media, earned media, reporting, public speaking, grant writing, and data analytics.</p>
<p><b><i>Other –</i></b> Responsible for some office management activities, including management of interns and office volunteers. Assist with small to large scale fundraising events. Abide by existing and new Clean Ocean Access policies and procedures. Assist the Executive Director with other tasks and activities as required.</p>
