<h2>Contracts Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>II-VI Optical Systems' designs, manufactures, assembles and tests precision optical systems and components. Our products have been deployed on military platforms such as submarines, tanks, missiles, fighter jets and UAV's. II-VI Optical Systems has grown over the years from a provider of specialty optical materials to a full service provider of optical components and optical systems.</p>
<p>
Since its establishment in 1961, II-VI Optical Systems has maintained a commitment to customer satisfaction and continuous improvement. Our commitment to customer satisfaction is achieved by continuous training of our staff with focus on quality, on-time delivery, and cost reduction. From the initial customer inquiry to final delivery, meeting the customer's requirements is our primary objective.</p>
<p><b>
Basic Function:</b><br>
Directs and manages all customer non-disclosure agreements, contracts for all current programs and future programs. Provides expertise and guidance for contract administration and negotiation. Leads the Customer, DCAA, and DCMA auditing process. Works with Finance, estimation, and engineering to pull together and prepare the necessary supporting documentation. The Contracts Manager is responsible for the following groups and functions:</p>
<p>
Contracts: Responsible for coordinating review and signature of contractual obligations and regulations included in RFQ/RFP's and customer purchase orders, and advises business development personnel, senior management and the bid/proposal team of potential conflicts and liabilities. Is expected to be the internal expert on OS cost proposals and FAR/DFAR and will be called upon to assist or lead in customer negotiations. Responsible for management of the NDA/PIA process including review, signature coordination, database maintenance and record keeping. Working with legal counsel as appropriate, may draft contractual letters or agreements for customers and/or suppliers</p>
<p>
Responsible for analyzing contractual obligations and regulations, including customer terms and conditions, included in RFQ/RFP's and customer purchase orders. Advises business development personnel, senior management and the bid/proposal team of potential conflicts and liabilities and assists or leads customer negotiations accordingly. Maintains awareness of changes to FAR, DFAR, ITAR, EAR and other applicable government regulations associated with contractual requirements and export controls.</p>
<p>
Administration: Ensures sales orders, planning outlines, cost sheets and other documentation are prepared and released in a timely fashion in order to facilitate the order entry process for awarded contracts. [SL1] Also includes providing signed customer acknowledgments, finalizing changes with customers prior to order acceptance, requests for funding, etc. Will work with Purchasing to ensure appropriate flow down clauses are passed on to suppliers.<br>
</p>
