<h2>Assistant General Counsel</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<div>Are you creative and passionate about technology and ready to bring your experiences and accomplishments to work on cutting edge legal, business, and technology issues? Join the team that provides front-line legal support for Microsoft’s core productivity and collaboration products and services!</div>
<div></div>
<br>
<div>
Microsoft Corporation has an immediate opening for an Assistant General Counsel to join our team in Redmond, Washington supporting the engineering and compliance organizations responsible for Office 365, Outlook.com, OneDrive, and related offerings. This role will lead a team that provides front-line legal support on a broad range of policy, business, legal and strategic issues relating to these commercial and consumer productivity offerings across a variety of platforms and devices.</div>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>
Responsibilities</b></h2>
<div><b>
Primary responsibilities for this role include:</b></div>
<div></div>
<br>
<ul>
<li><div>
Counseling and advising business executives and others on a wide variety of complex and developing legal and business issues</div></li>
<li><div>
Manage a diverse team (both direct reports and “dotted-line” resources) dedicated to the achievement of current and future business objectives</div></li>
<li>
Provide coordinated advice to the engineering and business teams that design, develop, globalize, and deploy features to both services and software as the business delivers best-in-class user experiences, including privacy, security, intellectual property, and related legal, regulatory and policy issues</li>
<li>
Anticipate important legal and regulatory issues involving data use and privacy internationally that may impact worldwide go-to-market offerings, and devise creative approaches to mitigate legal and regulatory risks</li>
<li>
Collaborate with legal department colleagues and policy specialists on a variety of legal and regulatory issues, with an emphasis on privacy, security and regulatory compliance</li>
<li>
Support legal colleagues around the world who support our sales organization and seek input and coordination on novel or complex issues arising from customer and partner transactions; and</li>
<li>
Manage legal resources within budget and direct external counsel</li>
</ul>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>
Qualifications</b></h2>
<div><b>
Desired qualifications:</b></div>
<div></div>
<br>
<ul>
<li><div>
Excellent academic credentials, a J.D. or equivalent from an ABA accredited law school or equivalent non-U.S. law degree, and an active license to practice law in the US, and either licensed or willing and able to become licensed in the state of WA</div></li>
<li><div>
At least 10 years in-house experience with a public technology company and/or law firm experience working with technology companies</div></li>
<li><div>
Experience in legal counseling relating to data privacy, data security, online safety, and/or regulatory issues in the technology industry</div></li>
</ul>
<div></div>
<br>
<div><b>
Desired skills and attributes:</b></div>
<ul>
<li>
Management experience and the ability to motivate and lead others</li>
<li>
Commitment to diversity and respect for others</li>
<li>
Outstanding analytical, organizational, writing and oral communication skills, as well as strong record of effective cross-group collaboration in a diverse and matrixed environment</li>
<li>
Flexible and analytical thinker able to quickly and accurately identify legal issues in a complex environment and to develop solutions by exercising sound judgment that incorporates strong legal analysis, a pragmatic approach, and consideration of business goals, risk tolerance, and stakeholder priorities in ambiguous situations</li>
<li>
Keen business sense, ability to think strategically, results oriented, and desire to establish a trusted advisor relationship with business clients</li>
<li>
Demonstrated ability to work independently and efficiently, meet demanding deadlines, prioritize workflow and adjust to frequent workload fluctuations</li>
</ul>
