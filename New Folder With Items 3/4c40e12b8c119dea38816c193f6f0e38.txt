<h2>Clinical Applications Specialist</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>The ScottCare Corporation is looking for a Clinical Applications Specialist to add to our growing team! ScottCare is a leader in non-invasive cardiology diagnosis, rehabilitation and therapy. Since 1989, ScottCare has manufactured high quality telemetry and remote monitoring medical devices for hospitals and physician practices. ScottCare offers the most versatile and powerful systems on the market in addition to providing industry-recognized levels of service to support client's evolving needs. We are the market leader in cardiac rehabilitation telemetry and provide the industry's only single-platform solution for cardiovascular diagnostic monitoring and cardiac rhythm device management. Based in Cleveland, Ohio, ScottCare is a division of the Scott Fetzer Company (a wholly owned subsidiary of Berkshire Hathaway).</p>
<div>
<b>
BASIC PURPOSE/SCOPE</b><br>
As a Clinical Applications Specialist, you will be responsible for product installation and clinical application training for ScottCare's Rehab product line. When not in the field, you will assist our customers with phone and web based technical support and troubleshooting for Rehab hardware and software issues. You will be a subject matter expert concerning ScottCare's Diagnostics products and will be able to offer technical and operational guidance as required.<br>
<br>
<b>ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES</b><br>
<ul>
<li>Conduct product installation and training at hospitals and clinics throughout North America, adhering to established ScottCare installation and training processes and guidelines and established customer site policies.</li>
<li>Interact with ScottCare customers, which will include physicians, nurses, IT personnel, and any other end users of our products and services.</li>
<li>Contribute to the resolution of equipment and applications issues and immediately escalate any customer issues as required. Communicate status to customer. Effectively manage customer situations for best outcome.</li>
<li>Assist in continually improving the quality of our training programs.</li>
<li>Accurately complete and distribute all required paperwork and reports associated with installation and training.</li>
<li>Participate in pre-sales support, when necessary, by answering technical and clinical questions of the products and providing product demonstrations via remote sessions or on site visits.</li>
<li>When not traveling, provide phone or web based technical and applications support.</li>
<li>Identify, research, and resolve technical problems with ScottCare products in a timely manner.</li>
<li>Appropriately document and process complaints per the company's quality system procedures.</li>
<li>Perform other duties as assigned.</li>
</ul>
<br>
<b>DESIRED MINIMUM QUALIFICATIONS</b><br>
Education and Experience:<br>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor's degree required; Biomedical Engineering, Exercise Physiology, Nursing or other technical/clinical field</li>
<li>2+ years of Field Technical Support Experience</li>
<li>2+ years experience in ECG monitoring</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div>
Necessary Knowledge, Skills and Abilities:<br>
<ul>
<li>Certified Cardiographic Technician or Certified Rhythm Analysis Technician certification is a plus</li>
<li>Strong analytical, oral/written communication, and interpersonal skills.</li>
<li>Adaptive, innovative</li>
<li>Strong work ethic and customer focus.</li>
<li>Can work independently, as well as, in a team environment.</li>
<li>Solid attention to detail.</li>
<li>Ability to learn quickly and work well with others.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<p><b>
PHYSICAL DEMANDS</b><br>
The Clinical Applications Specialist will be sitting, walking, standing, twisting, bending, squatting, climbing, and kneeling intermittently daily. They will be required to reach or work below shoulder level. The Clinical Applications Specialist must be able to lift and move up to 50 lbs.</p>
<p><b>
WORK ENVIRONMENT</b><br>
The Clinical Applications Specialist works primarily indoors in an office environment where temperatures fluctuate, and the noise level is typically moderate.</p>
