<h2>Senior Advertising Policy Analyst</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">"The front page of the internet," Reddit brings over 330 million people together each month through their common interests, inviting them to share, vote, comment, and create across thousands of communities. Come for the cats, stay for the empathy.
<br><br>
As one of the fastest growing online advertising platforms, Reddit is seeking a thoughtful and deliberate advertising policy analyst to ensure that our advertising policy ( https://www.reddithelp.com/en/categories/advertising/policy-and-guidelines/reddit-advertising-policy ) is applied consistently and scales at pace with rapidly growing sales. This role is on the front lines of some of the most fascinating questions of online advertising policy development. This cross-functional position will be the main liaison from the policy team to the sales team, working to interpret and apply Reddit's existing advertising policy to our advertising platforms. The analyst will also work between the policy, sales, and legal teams to identify gaps in existing policies and propose and implement revisions and other updates.
<br><br>
<b>Responsibilities:</b><br>
<ul>
<li>Serve as focal point for all internal queries regarding the content and consistent application of Reddit's advertising policy ( https://www.reddithelp.com/en/categories/advertising/policy-and-guidelines/reddit-advertising-policy ).</li>
<li>Develop internal tools and guides to improve and standardize cross-functional understandings and applications of Reddit's advertising policy.</li>
<li>Identify gaps in Reddit's existing advertising policy and propose revisions in cooperation with the legal and sales teams.</li>
<li>Remain abreast of both industry and political trends regarding online advertising and assess their meaning and impact for Reddit's advertising operations.</li>
</ul>
<b>Qualifications:</b><br>
<ul>
<li>4+ years of relevant experience in advertising policy</li>
<li>A strong understanding of the online advertising industry and its associated policy issues</li>
<li>At least a bachelor's (or equivalent) degree.</li>
<li>Enthusiasm for tackling big-picture conceptual questions, with the ability to distill complex concepts into clear and concise language.</li>
<li>Impeccable organization skills and ability to manage multiple projects and tasks; project management experience a plus.</li>
<li>Ability to excel in a dynamic and fast-paced operational environment, take feedback, and collaborate with others.</li>
<li>A team-oriented disposition and a commitment to embody and uphold Reddit's Core Values ( https://www.redditinc.com/careers ).</li>
</ul>
</div>
