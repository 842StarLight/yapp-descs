<div></div>

<h4 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>
Our vision</b></h4>
<br>
<div>
</div>
<div>The Core Retail Services - Core Domain product team provides shared services products that enable an integrated and consistent foundation for all Cox Automotive solutions, including AutoTrader, Dealer.com, Dealertrack, HomeNet, Kelley Blue Book, vAuto, VinSolutions, and Xtime.<br>
</div>
<div></div>
<h4 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Our Initiatives/products that drive our vision are as follows:</b></h4>
<div></div>
<br>
<ul>
<li><div>
Common User: a central facility for creating and managing a user that will be shared across all Cox Auto applications</div></li>
<li><div>
Common Dealer: a central repository representing the organizational structure of our customers that will be shared across all Cox Auto applications</div></li>
<li><div>
Access Control: a central facility for managing what a user is allowed to do and where they are allowed to do it.</div></li>
</ul>

<h4 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>What is interesting about this role and the team?</b></h4>
<div></div>
<br>
<ul>
<li><div>
Common Retail Services is a team that serves as a framework for all of Cox’s Retail business units and brands, giving an enterprise-wide exposure to its team members.</div></li>
<li><div>
In this role you will have the unique opportunity to interface with leaders from various business units across Cox Automotive, as opposed to being siloed into one application or brand.</div></li>
<li><div>
The Core Domain team is the center building best-of-breed services that all Cox Auto products need. This lowers duplicative development and enables customers to use more Cox Auto products as a coherent suite, and as a product leader you will be directly instrumental in levering up our across-the-board footprint in the industry.</div></li>
</ul>

<h4 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>
As a Senior Product Manager, you will…</b></h4>
<div></div>
<br>
<ul>
<li><div>
Along with Director of Product Management, define product vision and objective measures of success and work closely with Architects and Software Engineering to oversee and approve product designs, enhancements, and quality.</div></li>
<li><div>
Track market trends and competitive offerings.</div></li>
<li><div>
Interact directly with customers and others to drive product direction and ensure capabilities meet market needs and customer goals.</div></li>
<li><div>
Collaborate with multiple Agile product development / software engineering teams responsible for developing and launching platform application and components.</div></li>
<li><div>
Lead the full product lifecycle from research and design through development and launch.</div></li>
<li><div>
Work closely with internal and external customers, stakeholders, and other Product Managers to build a collectively understood and prioritized product roadmap and backlog at the epic and feature level.</div></li>
<li><div>
Collaborate with other Product Managers to align backlogs and roadmaps across teams, departments, business units, and release trains.</div></li>
<li><div>
Triage and prioritize customer-reported bugs and customer-requested features and enhancements.</div></li>
<li><div>
Act as subject matter expert on assigned products, services, and capabilities, as well as in general areas of authentication security and permissions controls.</div></li>
<li><div>
Collaborate with Architects and Software Engineering to ensure products and services are secure, performant, and highly available.</div></li>
<li><div>
Collaborate with Operations to ensure appropriate monitoring, alerting, incident management, and problem resolution for products and services.</div></li>
<li><div>
Participate in product team iteration planning, release planning, and coordination of dependencies.</div></li>
<li><div>
Coordinate go-to-market activities with Sales, Marketing, Legal, Operations, Support, and other departments as needed.</div></li>
<li><div>
Lead and participate in various Agile ceremonies, including sprint reviews, stakeholder meetings, backlog grooming, and retrospectives.</div></li>
</ul>
