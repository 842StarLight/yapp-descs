<h2>Regional Collections Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<div><div>
<p>Under the direction of the Curatorial Field Manager, this position performs complex consultative<br>
and technical work for assigned museums/historic sites within the system of 31 State Historic Sites stewarded and<br>
operated by the Historic Sites Division (HSD) of the Texas Historical Commission (THC). This position offices at Star of<br>
the Republic Museum in Washington, Texas and travels to assigned historic sites within the system, as well as,<br>
regional repositories to work in partnership with sites staff and central staff to implement comprehensive plans for<br>
care and management of state-owned archeological, decorative and fine arts, architectural and archival collections.<br>
This position performs essential collections management duties including implementation of documentary control<br>
over all collections, identification of collections conservation priorities, exhibit development as they relate to<br>
collections, and registration of collections. Works under general supervision, with moderate latitude for the use of<br>
initiative and independent judgment.<br>
<br>
<b>ESSENTIAL DUTIES:</b><br>
<b>Collections Management
</b><br>
1. Ensure all assigned site collections are represented in Agency Collections Management Software Re: Discovery
</p>
<p>and confirm that these files are updated and complete.<br>
2. Identify and propose all acquisitions and deaccessions for decorative and fine arts, architectural and archival
</p>
<p>collections for assigned sites.<br>
3. Completes documentation that establishes ownership for Executive level and Commission approvals for all
</p>
<p>acquisitions and deaccessions.
<br>
4. Coordinate with Archeological Collections Manager in the management of all archeological collections for
</p>
<p>assigned sites.<br>
5. Monitor, maintain and care for artifacts on display and in storage in partnership with sites staff and repository
</p>
<p>curators.<br>
6. Collaborate with Site Managers, Curatorial Field Manager and Archeological Collections Manager to complete
</p>
<p>inter-site loans, conservation work, and implementation of division collections initiatives as they relate to<br>
assigned sites’ collections.
</p>
<p>7. Negotiate and coordinate exchanges or loans (and secure respective Executive level authority) for all assigned<br>
site collections.
</p>
<p>8. Participate in the planning of exhibits for assigned sites, including the selection and schedule of artifacts.<br>
9. Oversee assigned site collections while they are on exhibit and in storage (both in on-site and off-site
</p>
<p>repositories).<br>
10. Identify conservation priorities for assigned sites.
</p>
</div></div>
<p></p>
<div><div>
<p>11. Identify grant funding for assigned site collections and make budget recommendations, administer grants and<br>
oversee dedicated curatorial budgets.
</p>
<p>12. Collaborate with site and central HSD staff on exhibits and programming for assigned sites.<br>
13. Train sites staff in the appropriate cleaning and handling of museum artifacts on display, and in the identification
</p>
<p>of potential conservation issues.
<br>
14. Advise and train site staff in the maintenance and monitoring of assigned sites’ integrated pest management and
</p>
<p>environmental control programs.<br>
15. Oversee workflow management of volunteers/interns selected to aid in the completion of curatorial projects.
<br>
16. Prepare reports and correspondence.<br>
17. Provide curatorial consultant services to the public.<br>
<b>Interpretation
</b><br>
18. Assist in implementing the collections components of assigned sites’ master interpretive plans.<br>
19. Consult with sites’ staff in the development of interpretive and educational exhibition and programming.<br>
20. May assist sites’ staff in the presentation of educational and interpretive programming and tours.<br>
21. May assist sites’ staff in the development of content for social media content and outreach/offsite programming.<br>
<b>General</b><br>
22. Assist in budget planning for collections and exhibits.<br>
23. May write articles for local media and THC blog regarding collections.<br>
24. May train and assist in the supervision of collections interns and volunteers.<br>
25. Adhere to established work schedule with regular attendance.
<br>
26. Follow all THC safety guidelines/procedures and ethics requirements.<br>
</p>
<p><b>NON-ESSENTIAL DUTIES:
</b></p>
<p>27. Perform other duties as assigned.<br>
</p>
<p><br>
<b>QUALIFICATIONS/REQUIREMENTS:
 </b></p>
<p>Graduation from an accredited four-year college or university with a degree in public history with a concentration<br>
in material culture, museum studies, cultural anthropology with a concentration in material culture, interior<br>
design or closely related field;
 </p>
<p>Minimum four year’s work experience in decorative arts, fine arts, archives collections management;
 </p>
<p>Proficiency in collections management software Re:Discovery Proficio for museum and archives;
 </p>
<p>Valid driver’s license, acceptable driving record and ability to drive a state vehicle; and
 </p>
<p>Required to travel up to 35% of the work period.
<br>
</p>
<p><b>PREFER:
 </b></p>
<p>Master’s degree in museum studies, interior design, conservation, cultural resource management or closely<br>
related field;
 </p>
<p>Previous experience at the Star of the Republic Museum.<br>
<br>
<b>KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES:
 </b></p>
<p>Knowledge of professional museum and archival practices in preservation and management;
 </p>
<p>Knowledge of decorative arts history;
 </p>
<p>Knowledge of current museum interpretation methods and strategies;
 </p>
<p>Knowledge of exhibit development;
 </p>
<p>Knowledge of local and state laws related to collections and program areas;
 </p>
<p>Knowledge of program planning and implementation;
 </p>
<p>Knowledge of American and Texas history;
 </p>
<p>Knowledge of methods and techniques for historical research;
 </p>
<p>Effective verbal and written communication, human relations and organizational skills;
 </p>
<p>Skill in public speaking and museum interpretation;
 </p>
<p>Skill in operating a personal computer with word processing, database, spreadsheet and collections management<br>
software;
 </p>
</div></div>
<p></p>
<div><div>
<p>Ability to work in a setting requiring self-motivation/cooperative decision-making and to work effectively with<br>
diverse groups of people;
 </p>
<p>Ability to work effectively under pressure and meet strict deadlines while maintaining extreme attention to<br>
detail;
 </p>
<p>Ability to multi-task in a fast-paced environment;
 </p>
<p>Ability to adapt successfully and quickly to change and deliver quality results in a timely manner;
 </p>
<p>Ability to plan, organize and work independently, as well as within a team environment;
 </p>
<p>Ability to exercise sound judgment and discretion; and
 </p>
<p>Ability to maintain the highest level of confidentiality.<br>
<br>
<b>REGISTRATION, CERTIFICATION, OR LICENSURE:</b><br>
Must have or obtain a valid Driver’s License and Defensive Driving Course to be able to operate state vehicles.<br>
