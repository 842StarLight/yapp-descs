<h2>Staff Development Engineer</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div>
<p>Role Overview:
</p>
<p>The Staff Mechanical Engineer has responsibility for development of new products, following the SN New Product Development Process, including; 1) Observation of surgeries to gain knowledge of procedures and OR workflow, 2) Providing input to development of User Needs and generating objective Design Inputs, 3) Product design activities from concept development and prototyping to final documented design, 4) Verification and Validation activities including generation of test protocols, test activities, and report generation. In addition, provide engineering support to sales, marketing, and manufacturing efforts of the company as directed.
</p>
<p>Responsibilities:
</p>
<ul>
<li>(40%) Designs parts and assemblies for prototyping and development of mechanical systems. Performs and documents technical analyses of design features, conducts tolerance studies, and finalizes designs. Prepares complete design drawing packages and supporting technical documentation, applies technical rigor in problem solving and analysis. Prepares and leads design reviews and documents the outcomes, taking action items and maintaining records as required.
</li>
<li>(10%) Observes surgeries to obtain knowledge of procedure and workflow and in order to generate new product ideas to improve the process or the outcome. Contribute to risk management activities.
</li>
<li>(10%) Provides guidance and acts as a mentor to less experienced engineers in own group as well as acting as an adviser to those in other groups.
</li>
<li>(10%) Partners with Manufacturing Engineering to make sure manufacturing issues are resolved, appropriate tests are developed, and in the acquisition of new equipment for new product testing or manufacture, and assist in other documented requirements for quality and project objectives.
</li>
<li>(30%) Work with cross-functional teams to define system requirements (User Needs and Design Inputs) that are appropriate, realizable, and testable. Works with R&amp;D teams to ensure that verification and validation outputs meet design inputs and user needs.
</li>
</ul>
<p>Location: Mansfield, MA
</p>
<p>Requirements:
</p>
<p><i>Education:
</i></p>
<p>Minimum Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering (BSME)
</p>
<p>Masters in related engineering discipline preferred but not required.
</p>
<p><i>Licenses/ Certifications </i>: None required. PE a plus
</p>
<p><i>Experience:
</i></p>
<ul>
<li>6 years of hands-on design experience and product release in medical or similarly regulated field.
</li>
<li>CAD: Proficient in use of design software packages, Solidworks highly preferable
</li>
<li>Mechanical Drawing: Proficient in generating drawings and process documentation for use in fabrication and assembly.
</li>
<li>Manufacturing Methods: Proficient knowledge of various fabrication methods and assembly techniques.
</li>
<li>DFM: Proven understanding of design for manufacturing (DFM)
</li>
<li>Communications: Ability to provide information clearly and succinctly in both verbal and written communications.
</li>
<li>Competencies:
<ul>
<li>Has in-depth expertise in mechanical engineering and some knowledge of related disciplines.
</li>
<li>Has in-depth knowledge of best practices and how own area integrates with others; understands competition and can impact the factors that differentiate them in the market.
</li>
<li>Acts as a resource for the organization; capable of leading projects with substantial risks and resource requirements. May take on roles representing R&amp;D organization at company level.
</li>
<li>Solves complex problems in both design and operations; takes a new perspective on existing solutions; exercises judgment based on experience and analysis of multiple sources of information.
</li>
<li>Impacts a range of customer, operational, project or service activities for the entire R&amp;D organization; works within broad guidelines and policies.
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
