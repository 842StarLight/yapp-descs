<div><b>VP SALES, FINANCIAL SERVICES</b></div>
<div>
At SAI Global, the VP Sales, Financial Services will be responsible for developing a high performance sales culture and for building a team that can identify and develop target accounts in the Americas, especially the US, in order to optimize GRC revenue for SAI Global. This will include: Developing and executing the team sales plan; Leading major sales prospecting opportunities; Demonstrating how to close mid to large scale sales; Sales coaching for P2P solution sales; Team selection and professional development of all sales staff; Pipeline management, forecasting and reporting; Effective performance management; Negotiating and executing successful sales campaigns that result in closed business for the company.
</div>
<div></div>
<br>
<div>The ability to show BDMs how to present and demonstrate GRC risk management software offerings is critical to success. The VP Sales must be able to work in a fast paced environment and to manage simultaneous team sales campaigns as well as manage the transition of the closed deals to colleagues in professional services and support. Immediate documentation of deal-related activities is expected from team members and accurate reporting of deal status is a critical, measured, component of the job.</div>
</div>
<div><div>
<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Job Responsibilities:</b></h3>
<div><ul>
<li>
Lead, manage and coach the ‘Financial Services’ Sales team, to recruit, build and develop a high-performance solution sales team, including: manage identified sales channel, showing sales leaders how to leverage their teams to higher performance; introduce best practice sales methodology across the team; work closely with Vertical Sales Teams and Internal Teams to ensure strong, regular go-to-market programs to drive demand generation and to feed sales channels.</li>
<li>
Meet annual sales goals for our IRM solution and services, with a core focus on selling our Integrated Risk Management platform, SAI360, to Enterprise prospects.</li>
<li>
Qualify target accounts using “Solution Selling” methodologies and primarily hunt into prospected accounts to sign new logos.</li>
<li>
Execute a consultative sales process from opportunity identification, through negotiation of license agreement, in order to meet quarterly and annual sales goals.</li>
<li>
Manage all aspects of a research based, strategic sales process to identify new opportunities based upon understanding client pain points.</li>
<li>
Manage and coordinate the smooth transition from pre-sales to post-sales activities, efficiently moving target accounts from qualification through to evaluation, technical review, and proposal, following through with successful negotiation to deal closure.
</li>
<li>Deliver effective executive presentations to demonstrate compelling business use case and strategic partnership based upon SAI Global differentiation and unique value within the IRM and GRC space.</li>
<li>
Maintain accurate account and opportunity information in Salesforce.com, including timely updates to opportunity pipeline and monthly forecasts.</li>
<li>
Support delivery of timely response to requests for information (RFI) and proposal (RFP) for well-qualified opportunities.</li>
<li>
Work collaboratively with other internal departments, particularly product management, services, and customer support, to enable effective client implementations, in addition to ongoing support and satisfaction.</li>
<li>
Utilize Microsoft Office 365 tools to communicate and strategize with other SAI Global sales and service team members on cross selling opportunities within assigned territory.</li>
<li>
Represent SAI Global as a thought leader in the IRM and Ethics &amp; Compliance software / solutions markets, attending industry groups and conferences, while providing critical market perspective, trends insight, and feedback to SAI leadership.</li>
<li>
Actively engage in industry networking events to establish credibility and develop referrals for new opportunities.</li>
<li>
Work effectively in a home office environment, while being comfortable traveling (~40-50%) to meet onsite with clients, in addition to attending occasional, ad-hoc meetings onsite in our US-based offices (&lt; 5%).</li>
</ul></div>
</div></div>
