<h2>Technician II, Network Administrator</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<div>
<p><b>Technician II, Network Administrator</b></p>
 - (19000329)</div>
<div>
<div>
</div>
<div>
The<b> Network Administrator Technician </b>will provide expertise in local area network (LAN), wide area network (WAN), internet system, and storage area network (SAN) in support of complex client-server environments. Position will provide operational monitoring, support, analysis, and systems administration. Responsible for the configuration, troubleshooting, and performance tuning of data communications hardware and software<b>.<br>
</b>
</div>
<div></div>
<div>The <b>Remote Management Service (RMS)</b> role is to support the stable operation of GCI client IT environments both remotely and onsite. This includes installing, configuring, maintaining, and optimizing all network hardware, software, and communication paths while ensuring minimal network downtime. The person will also diagnose, resolve, and document hardware and software network problems in a timely and accurate fashion, and provide end user customer training and support where required. Install and maintain networks for all GCI RMS Clients.</div>
<div>
Specific responsibilities for the Remote Management Service role:</div>
<ul>
<li><div>
Collaborate with Industry Solutions Team and Sales to create optimal network performance for Clients.</div></li>
<li><div>
Work with end users and department heads to identify and document required network service levels.</div></li>
<li><div>
Work to meet service level agreements with end users.
</div></li>
<li><div>Install, configure, test, maintain, monitor, and troubleshoot end user workstation hardware, software, networked peripheral devices, cabling, and networking hardware and software products.</div></li>
<li><div>
Maintain servers, including e-mail, print, and backup servers and their associated operating systems and software.
</div></li>
<li><div>Maintain security solutions, including firewall, anti-virus, and intrusion detection systems.
</div></li>
<li><div>Maintain all network hardware and equipment, including routers, switches, hubs, and UPSs.
</div></li>
<li><div>Monitor and test network performance and provide network performance statistics and reports.</div></li>
<li><div>
Receive and respond to incoming calls, and/or emails regarding network connectivity problems.
</div></li>
<li><div>Perform on-site tasks such as: analyze, diagnosis, and resolution of complex network problems for a variety of end users, and recommend and implement corrective hardware and software solutions, including off-site repair.
</div></li>
<li><div>Recommend and perform network improvements, upgrades, and repairs.
</div></li>
<li><div>Support GCI Industry Solutions networking projects and new technology installations.
</div></li>
<li><div>Conduct research on network products, services, protocols, and standards for remediation.</div></li>
<li><div>
Occasionally evaluate and recommend for purchase networking hardware and software products.
</div></li>
<li><div>Occasionally write technical specifications for purchase of networking hardware and software products.
</div></li>
<li><div>Prepare and ensure accuracy of documentation.
</div></li>
<li><div>Occasionally respond to client emergency outages.</div></li>
<li><div>
Perform server maintenance.
</div></li>
<li><div>Perform and monitor system backups.</div></li>
</ul>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<div>
Essential Duties of the<b> Network Administrator Technician:</b>
</div>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<ul>
<li><div>Installs, configures, and supports the company’s local area network (LAN), wide area network (WAN), and internet systems, or a segment of a network system.
</div></li>
<li><div>Monitors network to ensure network availability to all system users and may perform necessary maintenance to support network availability.</div></li>
<li><div>
Responsible for direct customer communication and Tier I and Tier II escalation support.</div></li>
<li><div>
May monitor and test site performance to ensure sites operate correctly and without interruption.</div></li>
<li><div>
Supports in-network modeling, analysis, planning, and coordination between network and data communications hardware and software.
</div></li>
<li><div>Administer / perform network security measures.</div></li>
<li><div>
Provide event tracking, notification, simple remediation, and escalation support to a higher-level network administrator technician. Notifies technical support groups and assists with maintaining status of network systems.</div></li>
<li><div>
Provide daily summary report of events and system documentation updates. Audits and updates network documentation.<br>
</div></li>
</ul>
<div>
</div>
<div>This is a mid-level position for the network or server system administrator. Must have intermediate understanding of telecommunications technologies; be able to aid more senior level technicians. Perform routine assignments as directed under moderate supervision, with some latitude for independent judgment, receiving assistance in more complex assignments.</div>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<div>Knowledge, Skills and Abilities:
</div>
<ul>
<li><div>Knowledgeable in LAN/WAN technologies, internet, and server platforms.</div></li>
<li><div>
Overall understanding of the functions of internet such as DNS, DHCP, routing, servers, clients, browsers, and email.</div></li>
<li><div>
Proven knowledge of server software.</div></li>
<li><div>
Excellent verbal / written communications skills and the ability to explain and document technical activities.
</div></li>
<li><div>Ability to track and document multiple network events accurately.</div></li>
<li><div>
Ability to interact successfully with a wide variety of business, operating, and technical staff.<br>
</div></li>
</ul>
<div>
</div>
<div>Physical Requirements: Ability to perform sedentary work; physical effort may include stooping, kneeling, touching, feeling, reaching, standing, walking, pushing, pulling, lifting, fingering, grasping, talking, hearing, and repetitive motions; incidental lifting without assistance of up to 50 lbs (only 1 to 2 times per day); includes caring below shoulder level and walking with load using trained lifting techniques.</div>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<div></div>
<div>Working Conditions: Work is performed in an open-spaced office environment with minimal privacy. Work schedule is 40 hour workweek and position requires flexibility in hours, weekend and holiday work, after hour on-call duties and occasional travel to client site.</div>
