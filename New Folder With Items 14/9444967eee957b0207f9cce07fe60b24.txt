<h2>Field Service Engineer</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">The Applications Engineer is responsible for identifying and correcting automated product system issues at customer sites. This position supports the Raymond network automation team by assessing customer sites and assisting with system troubleshooting. It requires day-to-day interaction with internal/external service management, sales, engineering, product development, and customers via email and/or phone conversations. There will also be the need to communicate with suppliers and company partners. May have project management responsibility for small projects or subsets of large projects. Will require working with engineering teams to understand new products and to provide feedback of current products. Travel anticipated to be up to 25%, including internationally as needed.
<br><br>
<b>JOB RESPONSIBILITIES:</b><br>
<ul>
<li>Travels to customer sites as needed to study and gather information critical to the overall system.</li>
<li>Analyzes data to troubleshoot customer problems with AGV and additional Raymond material handling equipment.</li>
<li>Interprets AutoCAD layout drawings of AGV travel routes.</li>
<li>Supports external control requirements for proposed AGV systems (i.e. WMS, WCS, ERP, etc.).</li>
<li>Works with partners to develop complete solutions.</li>
<li>Assists sales team with pre-qualification and technical assessment. Defines and documents best practices for these activities, and works to continuously improve them.</li>
<li>Trains other employees, partners, and advocates for this process.</li>
</ul>
Also critical is the ability to multi-task and prioritize assignments, as well as the ability to plan, organize, and carry out technical activities. Good written and verbal communications skills, with the ability to present technical material to a technically diverse audience both orally and in writing, are also required.
<br><br>
Bachelor's Degree in an applicable engineering field, computer science, or a professional license, with a minimum of 3 years of engineering experience is required. Previous experience in the material handling systems industry is preferred, as is systems integration or networking exposure. The successful candidate will possess a high-level understanding of various software programming languages (C, C+, JAVA, SQL, MATLAB).
