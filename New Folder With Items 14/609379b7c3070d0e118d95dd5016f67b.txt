<h2>Director of Operations Finance</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><b>BACKGROUND</b></p>
<p>
Home Market Foods (HMF) is a dynamic enterprise that has become the market leader in a number of prepared meat-based meal and snack categories. The company has grown substantially across its markets through excellence in innovation, manufacturing, and customer service. This success has been nurtured by its ownership group and an expanding team of world-class executives. HMF is building on this track record through complementary acquisitions and a growing number of business development initiatives. HMF's business model is poised to continue to thrive and offer greater opportunities for its customers and team</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>
We are based in Norwood, Massachusetts, which is comfortably within the Boston metropolitan area. HMF's facilities include a state-of-the-art plant, and R&amp;D facility, as well as warehouse and Corporate functions. We are focused on continued growth and are executing against various initiatives that include additional product lines, new sales channels, greater penetration of our extensive customer relationships, as well as opportunistic acquisitions. Our success is directly dependent on attracting and retaining the best possible talent across our functions. To this end, we are posting for a strong Director of Operations Finance<br>
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>OVERVIEW OF ROLE</b></p>
<p>
The Director / Operations Controller has responsibility for all aspects of supply chain &amp; plant controllership functions, including operational metrics, cost accounting, logistics, maintenance, expense and inventory cost control. This leader will support senior management and operations with analysis and financial information and act upon analysis to maintain high quality standards and improve upon operational efficiencies, policies and procedures in place. The successful employee will provide leadership in the development of policies, systems, inventory management, internal controls and all manufacturing accounting and financial procedures. This leader will be an active member of the Operation Leadership Team and must bring a broad-based business acumen to their role.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p><b>
RESPONSIBILITIES
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Supervises and develops a team of three or more cost analysts and factory accountants</li>
<li>Prepares monthly operational metrics and presents findings and recommendations to senior management and operations. Drives best in class practices across manufacturing
</li>
<li>Establishes and maintains the organization's manufacturing accounting principles, practices, procedures, and initiatives</li>
<li>Analyzes manufacturing trends, costs and budgets</li>
<li>Drives continuous cost savings initiatives across the manufacturing organization</li>
<li>Works closely with the supply chain team to achieve business objectives through efficient cost control</li>
<li>Identifies, develops and implements progressive accounting methodology that drives prompt and robust decision-making</li>
<li>Prepares and analyzes cost reports, including direct labor, and variable labor and fixed overhead reports</li>
<li>Develops, maintains and performs effective audits and internal control procedures, including physical inventory at year end</li>
<li>Oversees a multi-million-dollar capital projects budget, including strategic planning and forecasts</li>
<li>Acts as a key financial resource for VP of Operations and his team, including integral involvement in operations strategy, capital project planning and budgeting</li>
<li>Actively seeks to identify and activate local, state and national incentives to support overall operational efficiency</li>
<li>Is a key leader in the Integrated Business Planning (IBP) Step III meeting, bringing key business performance insights to the operations &amp; supply chain teams</li>
<li>Act as a member of the Operations Leadership Team for the organization</li>
<li>Other duties as assigned</li>
<br>
</ul>
