<h2>Production Coordinator</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<div>
<div>
<b>Position Overview:</b><br>
Phillips-Medisize, a Molex Company, is seeking a Production Coordinator to maximize effectiveness of pre-production manufacturing by planning and communicating build activities to support manufacturing. Establish and maintain mutually rewarding relations with internal and external customers by ensuring customer expectations are being met.<br>
<br>
<b>Essential Functions:</b><br>
Customer Service<ul>
<li>
Communicate accurate and timely information regarding customer purchase and shipping requirements and concerns throughout the organization and provide feedback of capabilities to customers</li>
<li>
Negotiate on issues pertaining to quantity, pricing, and delivery</li>
<li>
Work collaboratively with all departments to provide the most efficient and effective service to our customers
</li>
<li>Research and follow-up on sales order changes, RFQs, cancellations, pricing issues, complaints, corrective actions, etc.</li>
<li>
Ensure appropriate recordkeeping and documentation associated with all customer service processes and procedures</li>
<li>
Represent the needs of customers regarding corrective and preventive action</li>
</ul>
Build Coordination<ul>
<li>
Manage build schedules on selected programs</li>
<li>
Serve as a liaison between Program Manager/Project Engineer and Operations</li>
<li>
Work with vendors and Operations to track the status of parts sent out for secondary operations</li>
<li>
Arrange for transportation of shipments when necessary and manage premium freight costs to minimize impact to the facility</li>
<li>
Inform Program Manager/Project Engineer of price changes, overtime premium and expedited changes</li>
</ul>
Scheduling<ul>
<li>
Forecast and monitor resources/machine requirements weekly to maximize efficiency and minimize overtime</li>
<li>
Monitor and coordinate production scheduling of products from beginning to end to ensure shipment per commitments</li>
<li>
Schedule all process areas efficiently for all programs by utilizing the manufacturing system to develop work schedules that facilitate the timely completion of job orders based on relative priority</li>
<li>
Ensure all required components, documentation, subassemblies, supplies and equipment are available to run jobs – this may include facilities other than GID.</li>
<li>
Work with vendors to track the status of parts sent out for secondary operations</li>
</ul>
Some programs may have vision requirements<br>
Other duties as assigned</div>
</div>
<div>
<p><b>Position Qualifications</b></p>
<div>
<b>Required Qualifications:</b><br>
<ul><li>Degree in production management or related field (concentration in scheduling, materials management or customer service)
</li></ul>OR<br>
<ul><li>Minimum two (2) years’ related experience in manufacturing facility with scheduling, inventory or materials management experience</li></ul>
<br>
<b>Skills and Abilities:</b><br>
<ul>
<li>Skills in problem solving techniques</li>
<li>
Proficiency in Microsoft Word and Microsoft Excel; or similar office related software</li>
<li>
Experience working with an ERP system</li>
<li>
Good interpersonal and communication skills (verbal and written)</li>
<li>
Good organizational skills and the ability to do multiple tasks at the same time</li>
<li>
Knowledge of basic inventory, production control techniques, JIT, work cells, self-managed teams</li>
<li>
Ability to work independently, as well as part of a team</li>
<li>
Ability to direct and motivate people</li>
</ul>
