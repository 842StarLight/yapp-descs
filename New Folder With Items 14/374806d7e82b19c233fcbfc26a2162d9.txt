<h2>Sales Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p><b>Sales Manager </b><br>Achieving growth and hitting <b>sales</b> targets by successfully managing the <b>sales</b> team. Designing and implementing a strategic <b>sales</b> plan that expands company's customer base and ensure it’s strong presence. Managing recruiting, objectives setting, coaching and performance monitoring of <b>sales </b><br>representatives.</p>
<p>Now that you’ve read the standard pitch for a sales manager come and join the growing team at SAS<br>Forks in Luxemburg WI!</p>
<p>The “right person” will be joining a bright, talented and passionate team of people with a reputation for excellence. Your role on this team will be to deliver your creative, communications and organization skills to develop existing and new SAS FORKS customers. You will also be instrumental in helping our organization develop the sales programs needed to achieve our long term strategic plan. We combine our serious work attitude with a fun work culture. The result: A place where you can achieve your personal goals in an atmosphere of professionalism and long term stability.</p>
<p>While you will be reporting to the President in the company, you will also have great latitude to develop your approach in taking SAS to a new level. We will also depend on your perspective in helping to develop new services and will look to you for input on our growth plans. It is our intent to find someone who will grow with us, it is not what you know that will go far with us but who you are. We have the standard job description stuff, compensation potential and fancy company literature to put in front of you at the right time, but in the meantime e-mail us to start the conversation….we’ll both know how to move forward from there if the conditions are right.</p>
<p>1. Become part of the team.<br>2. Learn current SAS Forks products and sales process.<br>3. Become SAS attachment expert.<br>4. Meet needs of customers through solution driven sales funnel.<br>5. Build motivated and self-driven team of outside sales team to drive sales revenue and fill production needs.<br>6. Manage sales staff through communication and monthly meetings.<br>7. Develop a yearly budget for revenue and manage monthly outcomes.<br>8. Monitor monthly travel and sales expense.<br>9. Train sales staff through bi-monthly meeting and ride along in the field.<br>10. You will oversee the entire parts and sales team.<br>11. Keep brochures, website and advertising up to date.<br>12. Set trade show schedule and delegate attendees.<br>13. Be director of sales staff, study travel plans and reports from field to find best route for sales staff visits.</p>
