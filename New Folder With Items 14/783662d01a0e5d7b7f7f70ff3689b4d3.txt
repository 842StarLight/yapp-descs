Job Description
</div>
<div>
<p>The PLC (Program Logic Controller) Technician, you will be responsible for developing, modifying, and programming a wide variety of PLCs and panel views as well as providing technical support to production personnel. You will also perform repairs on support equipment, buildings, fixtures and systems.</p>
<ul>
<li>Editing PLC program and servo, robotic or motor control parameters.</li>
<li>Perform advance troubleshooting, repair, and preventive maintenance activities.</li>
<li>Install, troubleshoot, and repair electronic motor drive systems.</li>
<li>Support new projects for new lines, upgrades, or improvements.</li>
<li>Interface with vendors on install or upgrade of existing equipment.</li>
<li>Install and troubleshoot variable frequency drives.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<div>Qualifications
</div>
<div>
<p><b>REQUIRED SKILLS</b></p>
<ul>
<li>PLC technician educational courses and programs integrate PLC programming with mechanics, electronics and process controls. Coursework in hydraulics, pneumatics, robotics, DCS, SCADA, electrical circuits, electrical machinery and human-machine interfaces, electrical motor controls, programmable logic controllers, and computer-aided design.</li>
<li>2-year Technical Degree or equivalent technical experience.</li>
<li>3-year experience in a related field.</li>
<li>Knowledge of 480v Electrical Power Distribution Systems and extrusion and conversion processes.</li>
</ul> <p><b>PREFERRED SKILLS</b></p>
<ul>
<li>General knowledge and troubleshooting of Allen Bradley Control Logix PLC with in depth knowledge of Siemens PLC’s HMI’s CPU’s.</li>
<li>Site support and software commissioning of Allen Bradley PLCs.</li>
<li>Possess good communications skills both verbal and written.</li>
<li>Capable of reading and interpreting technical manuals and electrical schematics</li>
<li>Possess the ability to follow, observe and enforce all safety rules and regulations.</li>
<li>Must be a self- starter, capable of working with limited supervision.</li>
</ul>
