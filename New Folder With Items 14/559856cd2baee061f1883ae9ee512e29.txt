<h2>Parts Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>Pacific Power Group is looking for a qualified and motivated Parts Manager for our Ridgefield, Washington branch. This is an exciting opportunity to work with a talented and dynamic team.</p>
<p>
Pacific Power Group, a privately held organization headquartered in Vancouver, Washington is a leading sales and service provider of new and remanufactured engines and commercial automatic transmissions, marine power, power generation products and commercial trucks. Pacific Power Group has facilities in Washington, Oregon, Alaska, Hawaii, California, Arizona, Texas and Oklahoma.</p>
<p>
Pacific Power Group offers a competitive total compensation plan which includes a comprehensive benefit package consisting of medical, dental, and vision insurance, flexible spending account participation, Paid Time Off (“PTO”), as well as company-paid holidays and a 401k program.</p>
<p>
The primary role of the parts manager is to provide leadership, direction, and guidance to the parts and warehouse team while growing parts sales, managing inventory levels and ensuring departmental profits. A key aspect of the position is to use leadership skills to continuously improve and to motivate workers toward shared team objectives. This leader will hold and participate in meetings to develop and implement processes and set goals that their team will use to reach overall operational goals. A successful candidate will work collaboratively with other departments and stakeholders to ensure the overall success of all branch initiatives.</p>
<p><b>
What you will do:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Work closely with Branch Manager to achieve branch objectives and initiatives.</li>
<li>Actively monitor staff while providing leadership, motivation and coaching on parts sales, managing of proper inventory levels and ensuring company/departmental procedures are followed.</li>
<li>Use key data to monitor, measure and influence over the counter and service department parts activity.</li>
<li>Pull, review and monitor daily parts financial and parts inventory reports to ensure key metrics are met.</li>
<li>Provide data and conduct frequent parts staff meetings.</li>
<li>When needed, process all other aspects of parts department functions including reaching solutions, providing estimates, parts procurement and pull/fill parts orders.</li>
<li>Manage all warehouse activity ensuring a safe, efficient and well-organized warehouse.</li>
<li>Oversee warehouse-related tasks including, shipping and receiving, parts pickup and delivery, organization and cleanliness.</li>
<li>Manage and coordinate inventory reduction efforts.</li>
<li>Responsibility and oversight for all other remote warehouses and inventory of technician vehicles.</li>
<li>Take part in performance reviews by providing feedback on direct reporting staff members.</li>
<li>Take part in staffing decisions and interviews.</li>
<li>Other task as assigned by management.</li>
<br>
</ul>
<p>
</p>
<p><b>What we need from you:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Exceptional leadership and business management skills.</li>
<li>Prioritization skills and maintains focus with interruptions, ability to guide work priorities for others.</li>
<li>Ability to work under pressure and maintain emotional control under stress.</li>
<li>Ability to motivate and inspire others.</li>
<li>Excellent written and verbal communication skills; ability to write clearly and concisely.</li>
<li>Great Interpersonal skills.</li>
<li>Superior customer service skills.</li>
<li>Ability to work cross-functionally with different branches, departments and leaders.</li>
<li>Critical thinking and problem-solving skills.</li>
<li>Basic computer skills including software, ERP’s, and various freight applications.</li>
<li>Good knowledge in Demand Planning process.</li>
<li>Knowledge of core process and factory guidelines.</li>
<li>Ability to interview, hire and train employees.</li>
<li>Valid Driver’s License</li>
<li>Clean Driving Record</li>
<br>
</ul>
<p>
</p>
<p><b>Experience/Education Required:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>4+ years of previous parts team management and leadership of others.</li>
<li>4+ years’ experience in warehouse and/or inventory management processes.</li>
<li>2+ years’ experience in generator/diesel engine field or equivalent industry (Preferred).</li>
<li>University, Community College or Trade School Degree preferred but waived with equivalent experience and demonstrated pattern leadership success and business acumen.</li>
<br>
</ul>
