<h2>Outreach Coordinator</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p><b>Outreach Coordinator </b></p>
<p>Are you a compassionate and outgoing person who loves working with seniors? Chelsea Senior Living, a local and reputable senior living organization, seeks a Community Outreach Coordinator for our Shrewsbury, NJ assisted living and memory care community. Candidate must be well-spoken and well-written with an upbeat, outgoing personality. This diverse position includes working closely with the Director of Community Relations in maintaining full accountability for all occupancy goals through a combination of outreach, networking events and the cultivation of relationships with prospects, families and professionals. Inquiries, tours, event planning and coordinating move-ins. Experience in sales or in the senior industry is a plus. Basic computer knowledge a Must. Work schedule is Sunday-Thursday.</p>
<p><b>SUMMARY</b></p>
<p>The Outreach Coordinator works with the Community Relations Director, for the lease-up of The Chelsea. Other responsibilities include cultivation of referral sources, community outreach and education on assisted living concepts in general and specifically of The Chelsea. Works to maintain a positive image of the residence.</p>
<p><b>ESSENTIAL DUTIES AD RESPONSIBILITIES</b></p>
<p>Include the following, other duties may be assigned:</p>
<ul>
<li>Understands, embraces and works toward the successful achievements of the mission of The Chelsea.</li>
<li>Respects residents’ confidentiality at all times.</li>
<li>Develops and nurtures a base of referral sources, making regular visits and phone contacts.</li>
<li>Participates in networking, special events, group presentations and referral development programs.</li>
<li>Effectively and efficiently updates and maintains lead management software.</li>
<li>Visits current residents in hospitals, sub-acute rehabilitation and also develops relationships with referral sources.</li>
<li>Work with the Community Relations Director in developing monthly/ quarterly marketing plans using the master marketing plan as a guide. Strives to meet predetermined monthly goals.</li>
<li>Works with the Community Relations Director handling inquiries regarding potential residency at The Chelsea. Deals with potential residents, residents’ families and responsible parties in a sensitive, courteous and competent manner.</li>
<li>Works with the Community Relations Director with extensive telemarketing follow- up to prospects. Referral sources, guiding them through The Chelsea sales “pipeline” including, but not limited to, appointment setting and overcoming prospect objections.</li>
<li>Assists the Community Relations Director with tours. In a sensitive and courteous manner, assists families in understanding The Chelsea assisting living concept and the required finances for residency.</li>
<li>Completes all required daily and weekly reports in a timely manner, presents a report of activities at all marketing meetings.</li>
<li>Maintains an up to date awareness of all competitors’ marking efforts and census.</li>
<li>Maintains an awareness of media coverage and brings pertinent press articles or broadcast to the attention of management. Identifies opportunities for positive media exposure.</li>
<li>Participates in the planning and coordination of special events, community workshops, and promotions, as needed.</li>
<li>Assists in the coordination of all marketing mailings.</li>
<li>Works with the Community Relations Director as a liaison and information resource to the community.</li>
<li>Attends local business association meetings and becomes an active participant in those groups.</li>
<li>Attends in-service educational sessions and other educational knowledge, skill and competency development opportunities as required.</li>
<li>Participates in the ongoing process of Quality Improvement to evaluate and improve upon the service and standards of The Chelsea.</li>
<li>Other duties, as assigned.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>QUALIFICATIONS: </b></p>
<p>To perform this job successfully, an individual must be able to perform each essential duty satisfactorily. The qualifications listed below are representative of the knowledge, skills and/ or abilities required to perform the job successfully. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions.</p>
<ul>
<li>Education/ Experience: Two to four years related experience or training, experience in sales to senior market. Computer literacy: proficiency in word processing and database programs. Understands marketing theory. Strong oral and written communication skills. Completion of annual in-service requirements.</li>
<li>Language Skills: Ability to read, write, understand and communicate effectively in English. Ability to interpret common scientific and technical journals, financial reports, and legal documents. Ability to respond to inquiries or complaints from residents, residents’ families, regulatory agencies or members of the community. Ability to effectively present information to upper level management, public groups and/ or boards of directors/agencies.</li>
<li>Reasoning Ability: Ability to define problems, collects data, establish facts and draw valid conclusions. Ability to interpret a variety of technical instructions and deal with several abstract and concrete variables.</li>
<li>Other Skills and Abilities: Must interact with ill and disabled residents and with those affected by Alzheimer’s disease or related memory impairments, residents’ families, other employees, regulatory agencies and the public. Must be able to cope with the mental and emotional stress of the position.</li>
<li>Certificates/Licenses/Registrations: Current NJ Driver’s license.</li>
<li>Physical Demands: Must be in good physical condition and able to move freely throughout the residence. While performing the duties of this position, the employee is regularly required to effectively communicate with the residents, residents’ families, other employees, regulatory agencies and the public. The employee must occasionally be able to push, left or move up to 50 pounds. Good observation skills are necessary for this position.</li>
</ul>
