<h2>Administrative Associate</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<p><b>Are you ready to join an organization where you can be a</b> <b>relentless force</b> <b>for a world of longer, healthier lives?</b></p>
<p>Envision all Americans enjoying ideal cardiovascular health free of heart disease and stroke. At the American Heart Association and American Stroke Association, we get to work toward that goal every single day. Is it easy? No. Is it worthwhile? you bet!</p>
<p>This is satisfying work that makes a real difference in people's lives. You can achieve professional growth with personal fulfillment. You will connect with people and make a lifesaving impact. You will partner with individuals, schools, lawmakers, healthcare providers and others to ensure everyone has access to healthier lifestyle choices and proper healthcare.</p>
<p><b>Responsibilities</b></p>
<p>The American Heart Association (AHA) has an excellent opportunity for an Administrative Assistant – Health Strategies in our SouthWest Affiliate office in Denver, CO. Performs complex administrative support and technical program assistance work. Work involves disseminating information, maintaining filing systems, and performing internal administrative support work. Responsibilities may include training others. Works under general supervision with moderate latitude for the use of initiative and independent judgment.</p>
<p><b>Essential Job Duties:</b></p>
<ul>
<li><p>Prepares, interprets, and disseminates information concerning interpersonal programs and procedures.</p></li>
<li><p>
Prepares, edits, and distributes correspondence, reports, studies, forms, and documents.</p></li>
<li><p>
Participates in the planning and execution of programs.</p></li>
<li><p>
Develops, coordinates, and maintains record keeping and filing systems for their area of responsibility.</p></li>
<li><p>
Responds to inquiries regarding rules, regulations, policies, and procedures.</p></li>
<li><p>
Coordinates meetings, fundraising events, and stewardship events.</p></li>
<li><p>
Handle work between units of the organization.</p></li>
<li><p>
Assist in compiling and analyzing data, making calculations, and preparing reports.</p></li>
<li><p>
May research, compose, design, or edit publications such as brochures, forms, and manuals as requested.</p></li>
<li><p>
May train others.</p></li>
<li><p>
May oversee the work of others.</p></li>
<li><p>
Performs related work as assigned.</p></li>
<li><p>
Knowledge of office management principles and practices and administrative procedures, and promotion of programs.</p></li>
<li><p>
Skill in using standard computer software programs, including Word, PowerPoint, and Excel, as evidenced by formal course work, training, or previous work experience <b>(Will be tested).</b></p></li>
<li><p>
Skill in composing logical, clear, and concise correspondence using appropriate grammar, spelling, and punctuation.</p></li>
<li><p>
Thorough knowledge of business letter writing format, style, and protocol.</p></li>
<li><p>
Skill in maintaining and organizing large amounts of data with precise attention to detail.</p></li>
<li><p>
Skill in applying quantitative/numeric/math skills to perform basic calculations such as averages, percentages and ratios.</p></li>
<li><p>
Skill in conducting basic research including bringing together and assimilating information pertinent to assignments using the Internet and other resources.</p></li>
<li><p>
Skill in communicating with others to effectively carry out essential job functions.</p></li>
<li><p>
Skill in establishing and maintaining effective working relationships with all levels in the organization and outside contact with healthcare professionals, volunteers and the public.</p></li>
<li><p>
Skill in properly handling confidential and sensitive information with tact and discretion.</p></li>
<li><p>
Proven ability in effectively managing multiple priorities involving multiple customers.</p></li>
<li><p>
You are able to make decisions quickly and independently with little to no direct supervision.</p></li>
<li><p>
Skill in effectively interpreting programs and plans.</p></li>
</ul>
<p><b>Qualifications</b></p>
<ul>
<li><p>High school diploma or GED equivalent required.</p></li>
<li><p>
College Degree preferred.</p></li>
<li><p>
You will a minimum of 1 year related experience.</p></li>
<li><p>
Ability to lift 20lbs from ground to waist level with or without reasonable accommodation.</p></li>
<li><p>
You should have at least intermediate knowledge with Microsoft Office 2010 used for word processing, email, presentations and spreadsheets. Advanced knowledge and skill with these programs is helpful. These skills are subject to testing.</p></li>
<li><p>
Must be at least 18 years old.</p></li>
</ul>
