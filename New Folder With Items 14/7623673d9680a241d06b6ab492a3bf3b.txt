<p><b>JOB TITLE </b>Director of Strategic Partnerships</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>
ABOUT THE COMPANY</b></p>
<p>
Atlas Lift Tech, Inc. is a US-based, privately held company that has been providing services and changing the culture in healthcare facilities. We provide proven and effective practices that reduce provider injury, improve patient safety, and produce a thriving and sustainable culture of safety. As the pioneers of the Safe Patient Handling and Mobility (SPHM) industry, we have been protecting care providers from serious injuries across the United States since 2009.</p>
<p>
Atlas has entered into a formal partnership with Arjo to enhance Safe Patient Handling and Mobility services and equipment options to existing, as well as new, clients. Arjo is a Swedish public, company that develops and sells medical equipment and solutions to Acute care hospitals and long-term care (LTC) facilities. The majority of their hardware focuses on devices associated with safe patient handling and mobility. The company manufactures a variety of mechanical lifts that help caregivers move and mobilize patients. They also make products that address Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT), bathing and cleaning needs. The Diligent business unit within Arjo consults with hospitals in designing and training professionals in safe patient care.
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>ABOUT THE JOB</b></p>
<p>
The Director of Strategic Partnerships reports to the Chief Operating Officer (COO) and may be based in our Headquarters in San Ramon, CA or Chicago, IL. The exempt position acts as the principal contact for coordination of activities between Atlas Lift Tech, Inc. and other strategic partners such as Arjo.
</p>
<p>This position is provided with a market-competitive salary, bonus opportunity, stock options and a robust benefits package.</p>
<p><b>
Requirements</b></p>
<p><b>
KEY RESPONSIBILITIES</b></p>
<p>
Atlas and Arjo will collaborate on offering hospitals and Long-Term Care (LTC) facilities end-to-end Safe Patient Handling and Mobility (SPHM) solutions, primarily in the United States. In this role, the Director will need to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Coordinate Arjo and Diligent selling activities with Atlas functions including sales, operations, HR, finance and the executive leadership in efficient ways to coordinate the activities of the two companies</li>
<li>Work as an advocate for client and Arjo while balancing that with the needs and overall best interests of Atlas</li>
<li>Act as the first point of contact for any questions, issues or concerns from Arjo relative to the fundamental workings of the relationship</li>
<li>Leverage a combination of skills to develop and oversee all critical projects that support Atlas’ short term and long-term vision</li>
<li>Collaborate with cross-functional teams to further our corporate goals</li>
<li>Develop and grow the company’s partnership function; identify, acquire, and nurture partnerships to help drive company growth goals, operating the function according to budget</li>
<li>Represent Atlas to our partners, representing the needs of our partners internally to the Atlas teams, and reconciling both to successful outcomes
</li>
<li>Travel to Arjo facility locations (e.g. Chicago) and conduct field visits in support of proposing new business</li>
<li>Complete other projects and tasks as required</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Be a subject matter expert on our partners core business, products, business model, growth plans and key motivations</li>
<li>Foster relationships with senior executives and key functional leads inside our strategic partners</li>
</ul>
