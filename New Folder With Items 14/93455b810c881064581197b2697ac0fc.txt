<h2>FMS Financial Analyst</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div>
<p>Key Role:</p>
<p>
Support Foreign Military Sales (FMS) case and administrative financial execution, including all financial aspects related to case and administrative execution, such as monitoring contract execution, preparing contract modification and purchase requisitions in Navy Enterprise Resource Planning (N–ERP). Post obligations and deliverables in the Management Information System, International Logistics (MISIL), prepare contract financial reports, process standard funding documents, and track charges and payments, financial reconciliation, and accounting and budgetary support. Provide support to process current year and prior year obligations and expenditures, develop FMS administrative spend plans and financial reports, and conduct and perform financial reviews. Identify and correct financial discrepancies, make recommendations, and prepare processes and tools to prevent and correct financial and accounting errors. Develop, submit, and answer data calls in support of the FMS administrative budget exhibits.</p>
<p></p>
<br>
<p>
Basic Qualifications:</p>
<ul>
<li>3 years of experience with C4I or FMS financial management tasks, including funding establishment, allocation and transfer in N-ERP, and administrative funding budget exhibit development, submission and execution
</li>
<li>Experience supporting Department of the Navy clients in a government contract environment
</li>
<li>Experience with MS Excel, including pivot tables, macros, and formulas</li>
<li>Knowledge of N-ERP</li>
<li>Active Secret clearance</li>
<li>BA or BS degree required</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<br>
<p>
Additional Qualifications:</p>
<ul><li>Knowledge of Program Budget Information System Database (PBIS), BeSMART, including Planning, Programming, Budget, and Execution, Case Execution Performance Tool (CEPT), FMS Admin Case Tracking System (FACTS), Defense Case Accountability System (DCAS), Computer Optimized Batch Reconciliation Application (COBRA), Electronic Document Access (EDA), Tri–Annual Review Tool (TART), Security Cooperation Enterprise System (SCES), Data Warehouse Business Intelligence System (DWBIS), and Budget Object Classification System (BOCS)</li></ul>
<p></p>
<p>
Clearance:</p>
<p>
Applicants selected will be subject to a security investigation and may need to meet eligibility requirements for access to classified information; Secret clearance is required.</p>
