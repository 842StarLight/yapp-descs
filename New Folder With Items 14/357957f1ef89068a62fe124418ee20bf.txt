<h2>Director of Workforce Solutions</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><b>POSITION SUMMARY</b></p>
<p>
Provide leadership to effectively supervise, evaluate, and continuously improve Workforce Solutions (WFS) programs, enrollment, and staff. Demonstrate effective organization, administration, and continuous improvement of the department and its staff. Interact with faculty, staff, students, and visitors. Collaborate with Career Services to strengthen employer relations. Provide statistical information for planning purposes, excellent customer relations, and administrative leadership that enable the department to achieve its goals and advance the mission of Marion Technical College (the “College”).</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>ESSENTIAL JOB DUTIES, FUNCTIONS AND RESPONSIBILITIES</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Provide leadership and customer service for WFS functional responsibilities, including, but not limited to: operational planning; enrollment management; testing, limited researching and developing of new products and services; selling products and services; learner assessment; program evaluation and improvement; and instructional and customer support services.</li>
<li>Develop effective partnerships with employers in all sectors and collaborate with College departments to meet workforce training and assessment needs; assess student learning to validate that training objectives are being met.</li>
<li>Market programs and services, and recruit, admit, advise, register, and manage the enrollment of non-credit students. Advise and assist students to begin and complete training, and plan their ongoing professional development.</li>
<li>Advance the College Strategic Plan by advising the Vice President concerning its’ integration into WFS strategies, goals, objectives, and an evaluation of outcomes.</li>
<li>Develop, implement, supervise, and maintain schedules for WFS operations and services, including, but not limited to, training, consulting, testing, MTCWorks, and customer communications and billing.</li>
<li>Advise and consult with academic departments relative to employer training needs, including credit and non-credit courses, apprenticeships, cooperative education, and other opportunities that advance the College’s capacity to meet workforce training needs and the regional economy.</li>
<li>Use data and other findings to continuously improve, including current research in the areas of employee training, organizational development, emerging labor markets, available tests and education in general. Initiate and complete WFS and interdepartmental projects; including seeking funding opportunities such as applying for grants and seeking contracts.</li>
<li>Participate in developing departmental goals, objectives, and assessments. Assist in evaluation of reports, decisions and results of department in relation to established goals. Recommend new approaches, policies, and procedures to effect continual improvements in efficiency of the department and the College.</li>
<li>Develop and maintain a team-based, customer service culture within the department. Maintain appropriate and accurate files on all department activities. Identify areas for improvement and implement a constructive plan to meet customer service standards. Schedule department meetings as required.</li>
<li>Consult with companies and academic departments to assist in industry or program specific certification testing. Manage the non-academic testing center. Work with Career Services to maintain continuous coverage for both areas, including scheduling and oversight of the Mobile Lab.</li>
<li>Maintain appropriate and accurate files on department activities, syllabi, and personnel; maintain MOUs, contracts, invoices, and other documentation for partnerships and sales. Travel to and act as liaison with employers, organizations, colleges, and agencies to develop and implement plans, conduct sales, consult, and collaborate on regional or state initiatives.</li>
<li>Develop a plan to integrate the College Strategic Plan into the department utilizing various strategies, goals, and objectives; evaluate the outcomes. Provide oversight and direction for the team, developing and implementing internal and external communications; ensure team-building, system thinking and collaboration where appropriate. Participate in College-wide Assessment, Strategic, and Department Plans.</li>
<li>Serve as the Records Retention Official for the WFS department. Create and maintain an electronic archiving system for records that are to be retained, including developing a scheduling system to properly destroy records, according to the approved College Records Retention schedule.</li>
<li>Plan, prepare, and effectively administer the departmental annual budget and operate within budgetary allocation. Administer the formal and informal requests for proposals through vendor solicitation quotes. Ensure vendors compliance for contracts and projects; monitor vendor’s performance in accordance with policy. Coordinate efforts with the Business Office and Facilities to plan, acquire, maintain, and secure department facilities and equipment. Submit budget and purchase orders, as needed.</li>
<li>Maintain user proficiency for software programs, systems, and databases used by the College.</li>
<li>Maintain and supervise employee levels within WFS. Coordinate efforts with the Office of Human Resources to recruit, supervise and evaluate employees within the department to: develop requirements and position descriptions; plan for succession; interview applicants for positions; recommend for hiring, promotion, or dismissal; and conduct and evaluate written performance appraisals completed on a timely, annual basis.</li>
<li>Assist department employees to develop a professional development and educational plan to fulfill College and professional goals and/or meet regulations. Participate in developing and implementing in-service programs, department meetings, and/or trainings.</li>
<li>Develop professional opportunities within the scope of work responsibilities, department, and College objectives. Demonstrate self-motivation and willing to research and continually update knowledge and skills, as well as provide input and accept additional and changing work responsibilities.</li>
<li>Maintain liaison with colleagues in other institutions and actively participate in professional associations. Attend community functions that may require creating and presenting information on the College programs and recruitment. Attend on- or off-campus College meetings and serve on departmental and College committees, as assigned.</li>
<li>Respond to inquiries from students, College employees and customers while maintaining standards of confidentiality and privacy, in a timely manner. Maintain confidentiality in accordance with the College, FERPA, student records, and any other protected documentation.</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>QUALIFICATIONS</b></p>
<p>
Bachelor's degree from a regionally accredited college or university in Business, Marketing, or related field required. Experience in consultative sales, grant writing, and designing, providing or managing training programs strongly preferred. Proficiency in Microsoft® Office programs (Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Outlook) with excellent computer and related technology skills required. Exhibit a strong background and knowledge of various social media and web technologies. Demonstrate the ability to work independently and complete projects with minimal direction and oversight. Previous experience in higher education highly desired. Willingness to work an irregular schedule as assigned. Ability to successfully pass an FBI/BCI and other background checks, prior to and after employment. Valid driver’s license or dependable transportation required.</p>
