<h2>Director of Global Supply Chain</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><b>About Natus:</b><br>
Founded in 1989, Natus Medical Incorporated is a leading manufacturer of medical devices and software and a service provider for the Newborn Care, Neurology, Sleep, Hearing and Balance markets. Natus products are used in hospitals, clinics and laboratories worldwide. Our mission is to improve outcomes and patient care in target markets through innovative screening, diagnostic and treatment solutions.</p>
<p><b>Job Summary:</b><br>
The Director of Supply Chain will have global responsibility for all global procurement and supply chain processes including, purchasing, demand management and coordination of global master production schedule processes and site planning. This leadership position plays a key role in implementing and driving a new sales and operations process approach that enables Natus to meet or exceed customer demands with speed and efficiency.</p>
<p><b>In this position you will:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Develop and implement a global sales and operations process that is concentrated on the end result of achieving customer-focused service excellence competencies.</li>
<li>Identify and drive process improvement initiatives as well as develop and foster a continuous improvement culture that motivates projects and delivers results.</li>
<li>Lead all global procurement processes.</li>
<li>Maximize our ability to efficiently satisfy customer delivery requirements through strategic short and long-term demand management and capacity utilization across all Operations and contract manufacturing locations.</li>
<li>Communicate with all Natus manufacturing locations and leadership to develop the overall inventory execution strategy.</li>
<li>With delivery performance as a priority, analyze inventories to determine how to increase inventory turns, significantly reduce on hand inventory levels/value quarterly, reduce waste/scrap.</li>
<li>Develop long-term global raw material supply strategy and manage relationships with key suppliers.</li>
<li>Design and implement supply chain demand processes that support business manufacturing and distribution strategies adapted to changing market conditions, new business opportunities, or cost reduction initiatives.</li>
<li>Develop procedures for coordination of supply chain management with other functional areas such as sales, marketing, finance production and quality</li>
<li>Reduce premium buys and manage all supplier waivers to reduce overall business risk through careful management of changing global electronic market.</li>
<li>Identify, generate and publish key metrics regarding product availability, shortages, purchase price variance, production capability and actual delivery.</li>
<li>Responsible for identifying potential issues in the supply and communicating to management and for working with factories, distribution centers, and suppliers to find and implement solutions.</li>
<li>Lead contingency planning efforts to assure appropriately positioned against product availability risk.</li>
<li>Monitors key sales and operations planning performance indicators, and coordinates cross-functional work terms to increase effectiveness of supply chain initiatives.</li>
<li>Manage activities related to strategic or tactical purchasing, material requirements planning, inventory control, warehousing or receiving. Monitor forecasts to identify changes or to determine their effect on supply chain activities.</li>
<li>Oversee make/buy analysis and work closely with the director of strategy and integration on all manufacturing and distribution strategies.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Travel Requirements: </b>You will be required to travel regularly with this role, both locally and internationally.</p>
<p><b>Qualifications:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor’s Degree or equivalent experience in a relevant field such as Operations Management, procurement, Planning, Supply Chain Management required, Master’s degree preferred.</li>
<li>Previous director level role in global procurement with 5+ years’ experience. At least 5 years’ leadership experience with direct management responsibilities for direct reports required.</li>
<li>Must have 5+ years proven Oracle experience.</li>
<li>CPIM, CSCP or similar certification preferred</li>
<li>At least 10 years’ experience in manufacturing, operations and/or global procurement in medical device or electronics industry required.</li>
<li>Good working knowledge of regulatory expectations and industry practices including industry standards ISO 13485.</li>
<li>Effective in a global organization, results driven, adept at critical thinking with multi-variables and problem identification.</li>
<li>Experience in process redesign, planning, change management and organization skills</li>
<li>Strong technical industry and functional experience in areas such as lean manufacturing, theory of constraints, value stream mapping activity based accounting</li>
<li>Proven track record of developing, implementing and successfully executing continuous improvement methodologies (scrum, lean, Six Sigma)</li>
<li>Cross functional understanding of work requirements to complete required strategic business objectives</li>
<li>Effective negotiating skills</li>
<li>Ability to work effectively across multiple cultures and drive change from a functional leadership role, able to build strong business relationships across all locations.</li>
<br>
</ul>
