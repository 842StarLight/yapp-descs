<h2>Accounts Payable</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p>The role of the Accounts Payable involves providing financial, administrative and clerical support to the organization. Their role is to complete payments and control expenses by receiving payments, plus processing, verifying and reconciling invoices. A typical Accounts Payable job description also highlights the day-to-day management of all payment cycle activities in a timely and efficient manner. <b>Paul Tarpaulins &amp; Merchandise, Inc. reserves the right to add or change duties at any time.</b></p>
<p><b>Job Responsibilities: </b></p>
<p>· Verify and approve all vouchers for payment.</p>
<p>· Prepares invoice deduction notices as necessary.</p>
<p>· Ability to classify charges, prepare statements and reports, and balance accounts.</p>
<p>· Maintain all accounts payable files.</p>
<p>· Prepare analysis of accounts, as required.</p>
<p>· Assist with accounts receivable and special projects, as necessary.</p>
<p>· Assist in monthly, quarterly, and year-end closings.</p>
<p>· Performs filing and copying.</p>
<p>· Set up vendors, maintain accounts, respond to inquiries, review statements and resolve invoice discrepancies.</p>
<p>· Monitor accounts to ensure payments are up to date.</p>
<p>· Process credit memos/request refunds when applicable.</p>
<p>· Monitor manual check logs to ensure processing for month end close.</p>
<p>· Prepare credit applications.</p>
<p>· Process recurring payments.</p>
<p>· Comply with relevant internal controls, policies and procedures.</p>
<p>· Monitor and analyze specific AP operations, such as invoice research and follow-up, problem documentation, and standardization of input.</p>
<p>· Check figures, postings, and documents for correct entry, mathematical accuracy, and proper codes.</p>
<p>· Debit, credit, and total accounts on computer spreadsheets and databases, using specialized accounting software.</p>
<p>· Classify, record, and summarize numerical and financial data to compile and keep financial records, using journals and ledgers or computers.</p>
<p>· Access computerized financial information to answer general questions as well as those related to specific accounts.</p>
<p>· Reconcile or note and report discrepancies found in records.</p>
<p>· Receive, record, and bank cash, checks, and vouchers.</p>
<p>· Match order forms with invoices and record the necessary information.</p>
<p>· Prepare, examine, or analyze accounting records, financial statements, or other financial reports to assess accuracy, completeness, and conformance to reporting and procedural standards.</p>
<p>· Report to management regarding the finances of establishment.</p>
<p>· Establish tables of accounts and assign entries to proper accounts.</p>
<p>· Perform bookkeeping and compilation services.</p>
<p>· Prepare sales tax return for various entities.</p>
<p>· Assist with annual audits, federal and state tax returns, workers compensation and general liability insurance.</p>
<p>· Assist in the preparation of financial statements including supporting scheduled as assigned.</p>
<p>· Prepare and process accounts payable checks.</p>
<p>· Perform other related duties as assigned or requested.</p>
