<p>Job Introduction</p>
<p>The Controls Engineer will provide support, leadership and direction to the Maintenance Technicians and perform controls function for the Press Maintenance Department. The Controls Engineer will support and carry out the assignments and responsibilities given them by the area Manager.</p>
<p>Major Responsibilities</p>
<p>Responsible for developing and training all maintenance employees, leading improvements in all press areas, and assisting and supporting the maintenance team in troubleshooting and area improvements across the Press Shop. Coordinate and track project activities and ensure all parts and tools are available for the projects.
</p>
<p>Knowledge and Education</p>
<p>1) Follow and enforce all plant safety rules and policies. 2) Understand and promote Magna’s philosophies (Magna Operational Principles, Corporate Constitution and Employee’s Charter). 3) Work with minimal supervision. 4) Supports Manufacturing Engineering in the development of standards and specifications, including a complete purchase parts list related to controls systems. 5) Plans and implements improvements within the Press Shop related to the specific controls systems. 6) Plan and Lead cross-functional project teams supporting activities related to controls issues. 7) Provides technical support for controls systems during the introduction of new equipment. Supervises the assembly and installation as well as assemble and installs new equipment and is able to make modifications to existing equipment as requested. 8) Ensures proper controls specific documentation delivery form the Original Equipment Manufacturer. 9) Provides technical support to Press Shop for resolving controls related issues. 10) Responsible in the Press Shop Department for Technical Training of all Maintenance Employees and the development of programs by conducting training classes, providing technical documentation, etc. (Specific to but not exclusive to Beckhoff, Siemens &amp; PILZ controls systems and safety systems) 11) Ensure properly documented equipment breakdown/activity reports. 12) Work with Press Shop maintenance Supervisor to prioritize task to be done during non-production hours. 13) Ensure scheduled maintenance task are completed and on time. 14) Identify training needs for Maintenance Technicians. Provide requirements to the Press Shop Manager and training department. 15) Provide feedback to maintenance supervisor for performance appraisal documentation. 16) Provide leadership and direction during equipment downtime situations. Make good judgment decisions on technical matters and interface effectively with production supervision. 17) Ensures Root Cause of downtime is properly investigated and addressed. 18) Being competent with computer office related software. (Outlook, Excel, Word, Power Point, etc) 19) Robotics troubleshooting on robots. 20) Electrical circuit troubleshooting. 21) Pneumatic troubleshooting. 22) Ensure new parts get ordered or returned unused part back to the correct stock location. 23) Attend meetings on the shop floor as needed. 24) Practices good housekeeping on the shop floor. 25) Deals with vendors both parts suppliers and equipment suppliers. 26) Sets up preventative maintenance procedures on electrical equipment for the Press department as established by the manufacturer’s specifications. 27) Maintain maintenance Quality standards and preventative/ predictive maintenance program for the Press Shop department to insure compliance with TS16949 Requirements. 28) Ensure all PLCs and robotic programs are backed-up on a weekly basis. 29) SPM Improvements by optimizing Robots and PLCs 30) Keep all prints up-to-date 31) Maintain safety standards of equipment in work area. 32) Promotes good housekeeping (5S) 33) Perform other duties as required.
</p>
<p>Work Experience</p>
<p>Minimum 5 years of automated manufacturing technical experience required - Strong technical background; previous experience with field-driven computer systems and inventory control</p>
<p>Skills and Competencies</p>
<p>- Must have a working knowledge of Fanuc &amp; Motoman Robots - Must have a working knowledge of Beckhoff, Siemens, Allen Bradley, Omron &amp; PILZ PLCs - Ability to work in a team environment - Must have a working knowledge of basic drafting (blueprint reading, dimension drawings, etc.) - Must have an understanding of basic engineering practices and functions - Ability to perform basic engineering calculations - Must be willing to work extended, weekends, or flexible hours as required to meet production needs - Must have excellent organizational and communication skills - Must have basic computer knowledge - Must have good analytical skills - Ability to oversee projects from start to finish
</p>
<p>Work Environment</p>
<p>Some of the physical demands for this position include the ability to lift 40 pounds, standing and walking for extended periods of time, and occasional bending and stooping.</p>
<p>Additional Information</p>
<p>-Good interpersonal, communication, and organizational skills -Tabware experience -Robotic programming -Background in implementing and maintaining systems to support maintenance and production</p>
