<div><b>Description</b></div>
<p></p>
<div>
<div>
<b>SPECIAL NOTIFICATION:</b> This position is exempt from the classified state service and the rules of the Division of Human Resources and the Idaho Personnel Commission.</div>
<div>
<br>
<b><i>
The Department of Administration is dedicated to providing responsive, cost effective and timely support services to Idaho's policy makers, public agencies and state employees as they service Idaho's citizens.</i></b><br>
</div>
<div>
Are you looking for an opportunity to serve the great state of Idaho? Do you crave a job where you can make a difference and serve the public? The Division of Purchasing is the central purchasing authority for state agencies, developing solicitations, and awarding and administering contracts for vital goods and services. Located in beautiful downtown Boise, the Division of Purchasing is seeking a qualified candidate to join a team of passionate and skilled employees in a <b>Limited Service</b> capacity as a <b>Buyer</b>. <i>It is expected that the duration of this Limited Service position will be through October 2021.</i><br>
<br>
<b>This Limited Service position also includes a comprehensive benefit package including: PERSI Retirement Benefits, Medical/Dental Benefits and Life Insurance for both the employee and eligible dependents. Paid time off includes vacation and sick leave that begin accruing immediately upon hire and Holiday pay.</b><br>
<br>
Please note: The successful candidate will be required to pass a fingerprint-based background check.</div>
</div>
<p></p>
<div><b>Example of Duties</b></div>
<p></p>
<div><ul>
<li>Consults with internal and external staff to assist with preparation of specifications and determine solicitation requirements;
</li>
<li>Performs market research on products, equipment, and services to ensure practicality, cost-effectiveness, and supply availability;
</li>
<li>Consults with staff, department purchasing personnel and legal counsel to assist with development of contract terms and conditions;
</li>
<li>Applies value analysis and life-cycle-costing formulas to purchases;
</li>
<li>Reviews department bid analysis;
</li>
<li>Reviews adequacy of bid rejection rationale in consultation with staff;
</li>
<li>Confirms selection of the appropriate supplier, in compliance with the State Procurement Act;
</li>
<li>Assists with contract renewals and amendments;
</li>
<li>Promotes cooperative relationships between vendors, departments, and Division of Purchasing;
</li>
<li>Recommends procedural changes to manager;
</li>
<li>Participates in efforts to standardize specifications.</li>
</ul></div>
<p></p>
<div><b>Minimum Qualifications</b></div>
<p></p>
<div><div>Good knowledge of:<ul>
<li>Purchasing methods and procedures;
</li>
<li>Principles of contract development, negotiation, and administration; and
</li>
<li>Principles of life-cycle costing, value analysis, and weighted-value methodology as used in evaluating bids for a wide range of goods and services.</li>
</ul>
<br>
Experience:<ul>
<li>Utilizing Microsoft Word Excel or similar software with at least intermediate proficiency.</li>
</ul>
</div></div>
<p></p>
