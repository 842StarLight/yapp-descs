<p></p>Primary Purpose:
<br>
Provide leadership as well as comprehensive and ongoing training and education to program analysts in order to develop their fullest potential and be prepared to have a significant positive impact throughout the corporation. Focus on the implementation and delivery of early career programs.
<p></p>
<p><b>Essential Duties and Responsibilities:
</b></p>
<p>Following is a summary of the essential functions for this job. Other duties may be performed, both major and minor, which are not mentioned below. Specific activities may change from time to time.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>1. Provide leadership and direction to program analysts by monitoring performance and providing constructive feedback and coaching with specific emphasis on professional development. Complete and deliver performance and salary reviews for the Leadership Development Program analysts if assigned.
</p>
<p>2. Design, implement, and manage a comprehensive educational curriculum for program analysts that include the following as needed: general banking knowledge, fundamental and intermediate financial services, leadership training, self-awareness, and sales concepts. Serve as a primary liaison to outside speakers and facilitators. Collaborate with line of business managers as needed.
</p>
<p>3. Prepare and deliver select training courses, seminars, and the BankSimulation experience as needed. Actively manage analysts’ rotational experiences. Ensure that analysts receive an engaging, challenging, and holistic experience.
</p>
<p>4. Assist with the LDP placement process to reach an optimal outcome between Executive Management, the analysts, and the needs of the organization.
</p>
<p>5. Design, implement and manage a structured off-boarding process post-graduation to assist analysts and managers in the assimilation to the new team and role.
</p>
<p>6. Conduct regular evaluations of all assigned programs’ curriculum and modify content as appropriate based on industry, enterprise processes, and organization strategy changes.
</p>
<p>7. Provide leadership, coaching, guidance and career counseling to program analysts, alumni, managers, and mentors on program objectives and post-program career development. Identify performance gaps and training opportunities.
</p>
<p>8. Design, facilitate, and manage continuing education activities geared to the development needs of the early career program alumni utilizing both traditional classroom and virtual technologies.
</p>
<p>9. Identify assigned programs’ retention/turnover trends by compiling and analyzing current and historical data. Utilize finding to drive future initiatives.
</p>
<p>10. Assist the Program Manager and Rotational Programs Practice Leader with recruitment and selection for all programs as needed.
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>Required Skills and Competencies:
</b></p>
<p>1. Bachelor’s degree in business related field or equivalent education and related training.
</p>
<p>2. Two-Five years of leadership and professional development training experience or financial services related work.
</p>
<p>3. Excellent interpersonal, verbal, and written communication skills.
</p>
<p>4. Strong problem solving, analytical, decision making, and administrative skills.
</p>
<p>5. Demonstrated proficiency in basic computer applications such as MS Office software products.
</p>
<p>6. Ability to endure light physical labor (e.g. moving chairs, tables, boxes, etc.).
</p>
<p>7. Ability to travel as needed, occasionally overnight.
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>Desired Skills:
</b></p>
<p>1. Graduate of the organization’s Leadership Development Program, Corporate Functions Development Program or Banking School
</p>
