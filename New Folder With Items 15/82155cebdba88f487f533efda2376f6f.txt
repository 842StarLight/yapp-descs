<h2>Senior Systems Engineer (PMA-260)</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><b>Title:
</b></p>Senior Systems Engineer (PMA-260)
<p>Key opportunity to provide key engineering support to the development and sustainment of capabilities for Aircraft Common Support Equipment (CSE) for our Naval Aviation Enterprise.
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>Role and Responsibilities
</b></p>
<p>Seeking a Senior System Engineer to fulfill this key Chief Engineer level positon for the Common Aviation Support Equipment Program Office (PMA-260) to support managing the procurement, development and fielding of Common Ground Support Equipment and Automatic Test Equipment that supports every Type/Model/Series aircraft within the Naval Aviation Enterprise. Successful candidate will apply engineering principles to lead the development and maintenance of the Naval Aviation Support Equipment (SE) Program investment strategy/process oversight to insure optimization of support, development of Common Support Equipment (CSE) requirements, and SE standardization throughout naval aviation. With demonstrated engineering expertise, the applicant will assist the Program Office to manage acquisition and life cycle support of fully developed, reliable, and supportable aviation CSE required for the operation and maintenance of weapons, weapon systems, subsystems, and other support equipment. This includes responsibility for CSE requirements planning, research, development, acquisition, funding, support, foreign military sales, and test. Serves as Director of the Automatic Test Systems (ATS) Executive Directorate and leads the Joint Services and U. S. Special Operations Command (USSOCOM) in the development of DoD ATS strategies based on an open systems approach and integrated diagnostics. The candidate will support PMA-260’s commitment to reduce life cycle costs, right-size the aviation support equipment inventory footprint, and modernize Automatic Test Systems and Ground Support Equipment.
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>Requirements
</b></p>
<p></p>
<p>Education/Experience: Position requires a MS or MA degree from an accredited college or university in Engineering, Mathematics, Physics or a related discipline. ALLOWABLE SUBSTITUION: A BS or BA degree from an accredited college or university in Engineering, Mathematics, Physics or a related scientific discipline and an additional four (4) years of recent and relevant experience may be substituted for a MS or MA degree. These four (4) years of experience are in addition to the required minimum of ten (ten) years to meet the “Experience” requirements below, therefore, requiring a total of fourteen (14) years of experience.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE
</p>
<p></p>
<p>At least ten (10) years of recent and relevant experience in an engineering position, three (3) of which must be directly related to naval systems. Demonstrate knowledge in the area of engineering expertise is required. Demonstrated experience with the System Engineering Technical Review (SETR) process is required.
</p>
<p></p>
<ul>
<li>Applicant must already have at least a SECRET security clearance.
</li>
<li>Experience with Microsoft Office productivity suite specifically Excel, PowerPoint, Project, and Word is required.
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p>Applicant selected will be subject to a government security investigation and must meet eligibility requirements for access to classified information.
