<h2>Loan Officer II - Corporate HQ</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<div>
<p>Develops and maintains banking relationships and makes a variety of loans based on defined lending authority by performing the following essential duties and responsibilities:</p>
<p><b>ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES</b><br>
Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions.</p>
<ul>
<li>Analyzes existing and potential customers' banking needs, earnings and financial conditions to determine which bank products are most appropriate, and to assess acceptable risk; approves/denies or recommends approval/denial within lending authority.</li>
<li>Provides advice and service to existing customers, and generates new business by calling on prospective customers regarding the establishment of banking relationships.</li>
<li>Manages existing loan portfolio, including the monitoring and collection of loan payments; modifies or extends loan terms or structure to protect bank's interest when required. Minimizes bank losses and exposure to losses.</li>
<li>Follows existing lending procedures and ensures lending compliance of loan portfolio with bank and regulatory procedures, policies, and requirements, and takes corrective action when non-compliance is identified.</li>
<li>Represents the bank in the community in order to identify, develop and promote additional business.</li>
<li>Keeps informed about the banking industry and all bank products. Maintains awareness of competitors' products and the bank's advantage.</li>
<li>Assists in training less experienced loan officers on lending procedures, policies, and regulations, and recommends improvements to procedures and provides functional guidance as needed.</li>
<li>May supervise employees at local or out-of-town locations as business needs dictate.</li>
<li>Performs other work-related duties as assigned by supervisor.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>SUPERVISORY RESPONSIBILITIES</b>:<br>

Responsibilities include interviewing and training employees; planning, assigning, and directing work; appraising performance; rewarding and disciplining employees; addressing complaints and resolving problems; and making recommendations regarding the hiring and terminating of employees.</p>
