<h2>Reserve Police Officer Level I</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div><div>
<div><b>Description</b></div>
<br>
<p></p>
<div>
<br>
The City of Anaheim is seeking Reserve Officers to perform general police and crime prevention duties to ensure the strict enforcement of State and local laws relating to public safety and welfare. Reserve Officers perform a full range of duties with only occasional instruction or assistance. Generally, Reserve Officers will serve in a supplemental role to Patrol Operations and during special assignments (i.e. City Council meetings). Reserve Officers have the authority to serve in the capacity of a Police Officer. Reserve Officers are required to work a minimum of 16 hours per month, attend regular meetings, and maintain current P.O.S.T. training standards.<div>
<br>
<b>
For more information about the hiring process, visit </b><b>http://anaheim.net/Recruiting</b><b>.</b>
</div>
</div>
<p></p>
<div><b>Essential Functions</b></div>
<p></p>
<div><ul>
<li>Receive calls through dispatch and respond to requests for police services; respond to medical aid requests and administer first aid as needed.
</li>
<li>Arrest suspects involved in suspected criminal behavior; pursue or search for suspects; detain suspicious persons.
</li>
<li>Investigate traffic accidents, direct traffic, remove hazards and report malfunctioning traffic equipment.
</li>
<li>Make car stops for traffic violations or suspicious activity; question reporting parties, suspects and witnesses and prepare departmental reports; secure crime scenes and collect evidence; testify and present evidence in court proceedings.
</li>
<li>Perform problem-oriented policing functions and duties.
</li>
<li>Based on assignment may be required to investigate crimes, perform administrative duties, conduct crime prevention or conduct training for other personnel.
</li>
<li>Perform related duties and responsibilities as required.</li>
</ul></div>
<p></p>
<div><b>Qualifications</b></div>
<p></p>
<div><ul>
<li>Knowledge of city, state and federal laws, statutes, ordinances and court decisions; departmental policies; criminal investigations principles and practices; law enforcement crime prevention, and problem-oriented policing principles; interviewing and interrogation techniques; collection of evidence; technology including various computer software and hardware.
</li>
<li>The ability to communicate clearly and accurately, both orally and in writing; exercise tact, self-restraint and good judgment; safely and appropriately use firearms and other weapons. Establish and maintain effective relationships with those contacted in the course of employment.
</li>
<li>Must be a High School Graduate or possess a G.E.D Certificate from an accredited school.
</li>
<li>Must be a minimum of 21 years of age upon completion of a CA Commission on Peace Officer Standards and Training (POST) approved Police Academy.
</li>
<li>Must be a United States Citizen or in the process of obtaining citizenship.
</li>
<li>Completion of a CA Commission on Peace Officer Standards and Training (POST) approved Basic Law Enforcement Academy (<b>a copy of your certificate must be attached to your application at the time of submission</b>).
</li>
<li>Possession of a valid California driver's license with a good driving record.</li>
</ul></div>
