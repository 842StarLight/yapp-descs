<h2>PRODUCTION PLANNER</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">Founded in 1925, MacLean-Fogg is a worldwide enterprise currently operating 40 global manufacturing facilities across 5 continents with annual sales in excess of one billion (USD) and a workforce of over 3,200 people. MacLean-Fogg is a privately held enterprise headquartered in Mundelein, IL USA and is comprised of two primary businesses, MacLean-Fogg Component Solutions and MacLean Power Systems. MacLean-Fogg Component Solutions (MFCS) is a leading manufacturer of fastener components, engineered components and engineered plastics for automotive, heavy truck, and other diverse industries. MacLean Power Systems (MPS) is a leading manufacturer of products for electric utility, telecommunications and civil markets.
<br><br>
ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES
<br><br>
Perform activities to plan, schedule and coordinate production of all assemblies and finished goods using Master Production Scheduling techniques based on review and analysis of inventory positions, production schedules, sales-analysis forecasts, and safety stocks
<br><br>
Coordinate and expedite production with department managers; monitor production against schedules
<br><br>
Attend daily production meetings and communicate changes in schedules to all affected manufacturing staff including purchasing and tooling where appropriate
<br><br>
Coordinate with Customer Service regarding delivery promises, new customer orders, past due orders and customer inquiries
<br><br>
Revise/write shop orders as required to implement Engineering Change Notices (ECNs); communicate task completion to engineering
<br><br>
Adjust/write shop orders for production rejections in accordance with NMR disposition
<br><br>
Analyze machine capacity; standard run size, safety stock requirements and manufacturing/shipping part processing each month
<br><br>
Coordinate with Purchasing Agent to ensure on-time delivery of bulk parts to meet customer need
<br><br>
Schedule vendor processing, inspection, and packaging.
<br><br>
Manage MPS module of EMS including adding new parts and maintaining forecasts
<br><br>
Coordinate change-over dates on part transitions with shipping department
<br><br>
Schedule sample jobs (E-jobs) and monitor progress as appropriate
<br><br>
Schedule production of prototype parts for engineering and R&amp;D work and monitor progress as appropriate
<br><br>
<b>QUALIFICATIONS :</b><br>
4 year degree in relevant field. 3-5 years previous manufacturing experience in a Production Control environment.
<br><br>
<b>TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS:</b><br>
Able to analyze total marketplace and source components Possess analytical ability Computer literate with strong spreadsheet analysis skills.
