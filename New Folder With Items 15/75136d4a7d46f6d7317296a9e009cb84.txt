<h2>Pharmaceutical Logistics Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><b>Summary/Objective </b>Under minimal supervision, the Logistics Manager is responsible for the integration and execution of logistics with business systems and processes, including but not limited to the development of policies, processes, procedures, systems and metrics to achieve business goals. The incumbent is also responsible for personnel management of assigned logistics staff.</p>
<p><b>
ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES </b>include the following. Other duties may be assigned.</p>
<ul>
<li>Enforce all policies and procedures at Experic including Regulatory, Safety, SOPs, Work Instructions, etc.
</li>
<li>Collaborate with other departments to integrate logistics with business systems or processes, such as customer sales, order management, accounting, or shipping.
</li>
<li>Create policies or procedures for logistics activities.
</li>
<li>Manage distribution center operation to ensure achievement of cost, productivity, accuracy, or timeliness objectives.
</li>
<li>Resolve problems concerning transportation, logistics systems, imports or exports, or customer issues.
</li>
<li>Analyze all aspects of corporate logistics to determine the most cost-effective or efficient means of transporting products or supplies.
</li>
<li>Negotiate transportation rates or services.
</li>
<li>Manager or coordinate comprehensive logistical or reverse logistical functions for product life cycles, including acquisition, distribution, internal allocation, delivery, recycling, reuse, or final disposal of resources.
</li>
<li>Perform personnel functions such as selection, training, evaluation, goal setting, performance management, corrective action, coaching and mentoring.</li>
<li>Analyze the financial impact of proposed logistics changes, such as routing, shipping modes, product volumes or mixes, or carriers.
</li>
<li>Direct inbound or outbound logistics operations, such as transportation or warehouse activities, safety performance, or logistics quality management.
</li>
<li>Monitor specific supply chain-based performance measurement systems.
</li>
<li>Monitor product import or export processes to ensure compliance with regulatory or legal requirements.
</li>
<li>Maintain metrics, reports, process documentation, customer service logs, training and safety records.
</li>
<li>Implement specific customer requirements, such as internal reporting or customized transportation metrics.
</li>
<li>Negotiate with suppliers or customers to improve supply chain efficiency or sustainability.</li>
<li>Plan or implement material flow management systems to meet production requirements.
</li>
<li>Develop risk management programs to ensure continuity of supply in emergency scenarios.
</li>
</ul>

</li>
<li>Confer with management or subordinates to resolve worker problems, complaints, or concerns.
</li>
<li>Participate in carrier management processes, such as selection, qualification, or performance evaluation.
</li>
<li>Plan or implement improvements to internal or external logistics systems or processes.
</li>
<li>Recommend optimal transportation modes, routing, equipment, or frequency.
</li>
<li>Train shipping department personnel in roles or responsibilities regarding logistics strategies.
</li>

<li>Complies with all company and site policies and procedures including but not limited to safety, standard operating procedures, and work instructions.</li>
<li>Remains current in profession and industry trends.</li>
<li>Makes a positive contribution as demonstrated by:
<ul>
<li>making suggestions for improvement</li>
<li>learning new skills, procedures and processes</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Is available for other duties as required.</li>
<li>Is available for overtime as needed.</li>
</ul>

<p>
Supervise the work of logistics specialists, planners, or schedulers.
</p>
<p><b>QUALIFICATIONS: EDUCATION and/or EXPERIENCE</b></p>
<p>
Bachelor's Degree required plus six (6) years of experience in cGMP Pharmaceutical Manufacturing or Packaging setting as a logistics manager or team leader; or a High School Diploma or GED and ten (10) years of experience in cGMP Pharmaceutical Manufacturing or Packaging setting as a logistics manager or team leader. Must have three (3) years of experience managing pharmaceutical logistics staff.</p>
