<p><b>Position Description:</b></p>
<p>The Mechanical Design Engineer will be responsible for engineered valve production orders, supporting development initiatives, and aiding in the production process through reject ticket disposition, diagnostic testing, and troubleshooting for both nuclear and non-nuclear customers. The individual must possess a strong technical background/education to adequately address the complexity of valve orders, design changes, and production issues.</p>
<p><b>Essential Responsibilities:</b></p>
<ul>
<li><p>Provide detailed input for drawings, quality assurance plans, and bills of material to ensure all are released accurately and on time for all orders assigned.</p></li>
<li><p>
Take ownership of all orders assigned from valve selection/quotation/design through shipment and customer support. This includes specification review, creation of design/seismic and other engineering reports, providing critical input for bills of material, QAPs, and drawings, creating testing and other miscellaneous procedures, dealing with production challenges, and interfacing with customers as needed.</p></li>
<li><p>
Provide quotation input (valve/part selection, design input, engineering time estimates) to inside sales on or before required due dates to ensure prompt customer service.</p></li>
<li><p>
Fully support plant wide continuous improvement, lean manufacturing, and cost reduction initiatives by contributing to an active CIP/lean program within the engineering department.</p></li>
</ul>
<p><b>Position Requirements:</b></p>
<p><b>Qualifications/Requirements:</b></p>
<ul>
<li><p>Bachelor’s Degree in Mechanical Engineering, Nuclear Engineering or equivalent engineering discipline from an accredited university required.</p></li>
<li><p>
1-3 years of experience in engineered manufacturing environment a plus</p></li>
<li><p>
Must have a high sense of urgency to plan and execute order requirements to meet shipment schedule as well as deal with ongoing shop floor issues, customer issues, and documentation creation.</p></li>
<li><p>
Interpersonal skills are critical for optimum communication with cross-functional departments as well as internal and external customers.</p></li>
<li><p>
Aptitude for mechanical and analytical work, with the ability to analyze situations and make sound recommendations.</p></li>
<li><p>
Ability to produce professional engineering reports and presentations in keeping with company policies &amp; objectives.</p></li>
</ul>
<p><b>Preferences:</b></p>
<ul>
<li><p>FE certification strongly desired; must be on a path to take the FE as sitting for the PE exam is a requirement of the position</p></li>
<li><p>
Familiarity with 2D and 3D CAD systems; Solidworks and ANSYS experience is preferred</p></li>
<li><p>
Working knowledge of nuclear industry requirements including ASME Sections II and III, 10 CFR 50 Appendix B, and NQA-1 is a plus</p></li>
<li><p>
Working knowledge of RCC-M code is a plus</p></li>
<li><p>
Electrical, pneumatic, and hydraulic systems knowledge is a plus</p></li>
<li><p>
Experience working in an industrial/manufacturing environment preferred</p></li>
</ul>
