<h2>Sr. Analyst, Enterprise Risk & Governance</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<div>Sr. Analyst, Enterprise Risk &amp; Governance - (190000BK)</div>
<div>
Description<div>
</div>
<div><b>
General Summary:</b></div>
<div>
The Sr. Analyst, Enterprise and Operational Risk Management supports HCA’s risk assessments, risk governance, operational risk management and enterprise risk management framework implementation, and supports the implementation of risk-based strategies in support of HCA’s Risk Management practice.<br>
</div>
<div></div>
<div><b>Duties and Responsibilities:</b></div>
<div></div>
<br>
<div><b>
Agent Based Risk Management&amp; Dealer Partnership Committee Governance and Administration</b></div>
<div><div>
<br>
</div></div>
<div></div>
<ul>
<li><p>Oversee the production and dissemination of monthly dealer performance reporting; perform data validation and exception analysis</p></li>
<li><p>
Support the DPC review and assessment process, including collaborating with Sales &amp; Marketing
</p></li>
<li><p>Maintain HCA’s DPC charter and related policies and procedures</p></li>
<div></div>
<li><p>
Perform QA/QC on all materials produced for DPC meetings, including checking for content accuracy and adherence to Risk Department presentation standards</p></li>
<div></div>
<li><p>
Manage the production, documentation, and archiving of all DPC materials</p></li>
</ul>
<div></div>
<div><b><br>
Operational Risk Management and Enterprise Risk Management
</b></div>
<ul>
<li>Assist in the implementation of the Operational Risk Management and Enterprise Risk Management frameworks
</li>
<li>Coordinate the quarterly Top Risk Inventory monitoring process; collect department-specific key indicators to identify, evaluate, control, and monitor the most significant and emerging risks.</li>
<li>
Support the execution of risk and control self-assessments (RCSAs) to identify, evaluate, control, and monitor undue concentrations of risk and potential control failures
</li>
<li>Support the Fraud Prevention team by analyzing and interpreting internal and industry data to identify potential fraud, and developing key metrics and reporting templates</li>
<li>
Oversee the development and/or renewal of policies, procedures, and flow charts</li>
</ul>
<div><b>
Risk Control Committee (RCC) Governance and Administration
</b></div>
<ul>
<li>Support the Risk Department’s RCC review and assessment process, including receiving special topic requests, recommending inclusion/exclusion in RCC meetings and communicating to stakeholders on the status of requests</li>
<li>
Maintain HCA’s RCC charter and related policies and procedures</li>
<li>
Perform QA/QC on all materials produced for RCC meetings, including checking for content accuracy and adherence to Risk Department presentation standards</li>
<li>
Manage the production and archiving of all RCC materials</li>
</ul>
<div><b>
Ad hoc/Special Requests</b></div>
<ul>
<li>Coordinate and aggregate content from Risk teams in response to ad hoc requests, including data/analysis requests and onsite visits from the HQ Global Risk Division</li>
<li>
Oversee the production of materials for department and other team meetings</li>
</ul>
<div><b>
Knowledge and Skills:</b></div>
<ul>
<li>Understanding of banks or financial services firm products, operations, and credit processes</li>
<li>
General knowledge operational risk concepts</li>
<li>
Detail-oriented and strong organizational skills</li>
<li>
Excellent written and verbal communication skills</li>
<li>
Ability to work in a dynamic business environment and manage multiple tasks</li>
<li>
Basic data querying skills (ex. SQL or SAS) preferred</li>
</ul>
<div></div>
<br>

</div>
<div>Bachelor’s Degree preferred. Four (4) or more years of relevant experience in Risk Management within the Financial Services Industry; Experience in Automotive Industry Finance, preferred.</div>
