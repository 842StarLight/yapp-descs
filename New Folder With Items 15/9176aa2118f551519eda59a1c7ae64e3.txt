<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Please review the following job description:
</b></h3>
<p></p>Perform complex operating functions in a high volume environment within Trust Operations in accordance with prescribed policies and procedures, including securities operations, mutual fund processing, systems management, trust accounting and fee processing. Provide quality service to the internal clients of Trust Operations: Wealth Division, Retirement &amp; Institutional Services and Sterling Capital Management. Provide guidance to other staff members in the more complex areas of Trust Operations functions and problem identification and resolution.
<p></p>
<p><b>Essential Duties and Responsibilities:
</b><br>
Following is a summary of the essential functions for this job. Other duties may be performed, both major and minor, which are not mentioned below. Specific activities may change from time to time.
<br>
1. Responsible for assigned Trust Operations functions to include: income and principal collection, income and principal distribution, account reconciliation, fee computation and collection, account opening and closing, daily balancing, report and statement rendering, subsystem maintenance, securities set-up and write-off, securities re-registration, securities purchases and sales, corporate actions processing, vault custody and controls, interface with brokers and correspondents. Fee calculation and reporting, operation of trust computer systems.
<br>
2. Investigate and resolve complex securities and/or account problems and effectively communicate resolution to trust officers and other Bank personnel internally and externally.
<br>
3. Assist manager with the scheduling of workloads to assure the timely and accurate completion of duties.
<br>
4. Train new employees in job duties.
<br>
5. Work on special projects; identify and recommend quality improvements; participate in departmental planning meetings.
<br>
6. Perform system testing for releases and system enhancements. Participate in the development and implementation of new system enhancements.
</p>
