<h2>2019-2020 Campus Graduate - Information Research</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">Description
<br><br>
Dow has an opening in Midland, MI or Lake Jackson, TX for an IT Specialist in a collegial, inclusive, and creative environment where you will have flexibility in specialization. This position is part of the Information Research group in Core R&amp;D. Information Research accelerates discovery in R&amp;D by building and leveraging new tools and applications for researchers and decision makers.
<br><br>
We seek highly motivated graduates who will learn all facets of IT including software development and analysis and collaborate with software architects and material science researchers to develop and install/configure software and database systems. We need someone who can learn quickly, work well both in teams and independently and is passionate about exploring new IT technologies.
<br><br>
<b>Duties and responsibilities include:</b><br>
Write robust, maintainable code to develop user-friendly front-end and/or back-end applications.
<br>Solve complex problems by analyzing &amp; designing new applications or adopting/integrating existing systems based on requirements gathered from scientists and researchers.
<br>Create architectural designs for systems to meet evolving needs.
<br>Explore new patterns in IT development and lead efforts to adopt them.
<br>Develop Proof of Concept projects to test application of new technologies.
<br>Uplift existing core applications to modern platforms.
<br>Work with a variety of people, both in person and virtually.
<br><br>
<b>Seeking candidates with the following:</b><br>
Excellent programming skills. Experience with object-oriented programming languages, preferably C#, and JavaScript, ideally Aurelia framework.
<br>Familiarity with data structures and algorithms as well as feature definition, design and feasibility.
<br>Solid understanding of software design patterns and principles.
<br>Experience with source control and continuous integration/continuous deployment discipline.
<br>Experience with SQL, NoSQL or Graph databases.
<br>Excellent communication skills.
<br>Familiarity with the research science lab environment and workflows.
<br>Must be a team player - will work across geographic boundaries with project leadership, other Subject Matter Experts, Support Network, and client base.
<br><br>
<b>Required Qualifications:</b><br>
A minimum of a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Computer Engineering, Chemical Engineering, Chemistry or a related discipline is required.
<br>A minimum of a 2.700 cumulative GPA is required.
<br>A minimum requirement for this U.S. based position is the ability to work legally in the United States. No visa sponsorship/support is available for this position, including for any type of U.S. permanent residency (green card) process.
<br><br>

<b>A robust total rewards program, including:</b> competitive base pay, variable pay that rewards individual, team, and Company performance, and comprehensive benefits
<br>On-going learning opportunities within a diverse, inclusive and rewarding work environment
<br>Career experiences that can span different Dow businesses and functions with opportunities for personal and professional growth
<br>The chance to work within a global company and interact with colleagues from around the world
<br>Opportunities that spark your imagination and ignite your passion to help others
