<div>
<p><b>Why work for the Federal Bureau of Prisons?</b><br>
<br>
You can have a meaningful career with an agency that truly values a diverse workforce. You will find a diverse workforce employed from entry level jobs to senior management positions. We protect public safety by ensuring federal offenders serve their sentences of imprisonment in facilities that are safe, humane, and provide reentry programming. Employees at correctional facilities perform correctional work regardless of their specific occupation.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
</div>
<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Responsibilities</b></h3>
<p></p>
<div>
<p>The Assistant Trust Fund Supervisor provides technical support and guidance to subordinate staff members and is designated the responsibility for the operation of various service functions such as the Warehouse, Laundry/Clothing Issue, Trust Fund (commissary) management, sales (Trust Fund Accounting and Commissary Systems) and the inmate telephone systems. The Assistant Trust Fund Supervisor serves under the supervision of the Trust Fund Supervisor, and is charged with assisting in the managerial responsibility of the inmate services throughout the institution/complex.<br>
<br>
Along with all other correctional institution employees, the Assistant Trust Fund Supervisor is charged with responsibility for maintaining security of the institution. The staff correctional responsibilities precede all others required by this position and are performed on regular and recurring basis.</p>

<b>Special Conditions of Employment Section:</b><br>
<br>
Initial appointment to a supervisory/managerial position requires a one-year probationary period.<br>
<br>
The incumbent is subject to geographic relocation to meet the needs of the agency.<br>
<br>
Appointment is subject to satisfactory completion of a urinalysis, physical, and background investigation. All applicants are subject to National Crime Information Center (NCIC) and credit checks.<br>
<br>
All applicants not currently working in an institution will be required to complete a qualification inquiry regarding convictions of misdemeanor crimes of domestic violence in order to be authorized to carry a firearm.<br>
<br>
The Core Value Assessment (CVA) is an in-person assessment that must be facilitated at a Bureau of Prisons Human Resource Servicing office. On the day of the scheduled interview, a CVA will be administered. The applicant assessment must be completed within a 70 minute time period and a passing score must be obtained. Further employment consideration will not be extended if the applicant fails to complete the examination or fails to achieve a passing score. Note: The Core Value Assessment will not be administered to current BOP employees.<br>
<br>
Successful completion of the "Introduction to Correctional Techniques," three-week training course at Glynco, Georgia is required.<br>
<br>
The addresses listed on the USAJOBS account/resume must be the primary residence at the time of application. You may be required to provide proof of residence.<br>
<br>
Additional selections may be made if vacancies occur within the life of the certificate.</p>
