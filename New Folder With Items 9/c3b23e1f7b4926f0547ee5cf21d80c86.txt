<h2>Commodity Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">Commodity Manager – Supply Chain
<br><br>
Overview
<br>We are looking for a highly skilled and motivated commodity manager responsible for developing and executing tactical and strategic commodity plans for our Accessories category at the Fluke North American operations. This individual will manage key supplier relationships, negotiate critical supply contracts, drive year over year operating margin expansion, working capital benefits, quality &amp; delivery improvements with suppliers. They will champion continuous development of suppliers to meet preferred status, participate in design reviews with R&amp;D and suppliers on manufacturability and material selection to achieve most optimal component cost.
<br><br>
<b>Responsibilities:</b><br>
Being largely self-directed with minimal assistance and direction from supervisor, perform the following functions:
<br><br>
Deliver annual improvements in material cost savings, supplier quality &amp; delivery and working capital efficiency.
<br>Evaluate existing vendor base, to establish current state. Once established, define future state for a best-in-class supply chain.
<br>Analyze received quotations, select or recommend new suppliers.
<br>Create and execute transition plans to drive supplier reduction and spend consolidation.
<br>Design and lead contract negotiations.
<br>Detailed understanding of supply &amp; demand factors relevant to commodity category and suppliers.
<br>Consistent utilization of total-landed-cost analysis to ensure optimal sourcing decisions.
<br>Develop supplier &amp; part / material preferences for use in new product development (NPD) projects and migration of legacy products and materials.
<br>Ensure strong, value-adding relationships with suppliers, internal customers, and cross-functional departments.
<br>Lead regular business reviews with suppliers.
<br>Manage supply base integration for new company acquisitions.
<br>Lead new and on-going supplier audit activities to ensure the supply base has robust process controls and capabilities to meet existing and future Fluke business requirements. Conduct supply chain risk reviews.
<br>Business travel requirements up to 30%.
<br><br>
<b>Qualifications:</b><br>
Bachelor’s degree in Supply Chain or Engineering, or relevant experience in supply chain management.
<br>5 years of purchasing or supply chain experience in a manufacturing environment.
<br>Experience using lean methodology processes in a manufacturing environment is a plus.
<br>Experience in a low volume and high mix demand for products environment.
<br>Negotiation, analytical and problem solving skills.
<br>Good organization, project management and follow-through skills
<br>Ability to prioritize, multitask and act in a proactive manner.
<br>Frequent travel to vendor locations is required
<br><br>
<b>Location:</b> Everett, WA - Fluke Park
