<h2>Special Events Coordinator</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p><b>POSITION SUMMARY</b><br>The Feeding South Florida (FSF) Special Events Coordinator (SEC) is responsible for meeting FSF’s community engagement and fundraising goals as they relate to FSF special events and third party events. The SEC is a visible position in the community that works closely with a variety of stakeholders to execute successful events that generate revenue, are well attended, within budget and serve as a gateway for deeper individual engagement with the organization. This position is a member of the fundraising team with clear monetary goals. The ideal candidate has large-scale nonprofit event logistics and fundraising experience. This position is very different from an event/party planner.</p>
<p><b>POSITION RESPONSIBILITIES</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Create, manage, and execute FSF events that are within budget, well attended and meet revenue goals.</li>
<li>Implement turn-key, third party events that are in line with FSF’s fundraising strategic plan.</li>
<li>Assist with the development of an annual fundraising event, streamline the current event strategy and improve current areas of opportunity.</li>
<li>Assist with the creation of a strategic annual sponsorship plan that provides both an ROI for the sponsor and supports FSF’s organization goals and objectives.</li>
<li>Expand and increase online event fundraising, building out a successful peer-to-peer program.</li>
<li>Recruit, manage and utilize committees and members for all FSF special events.</li>
<li>Ensure each event has a clear ticket sales and communication strategy.</li>
<li>Develop vendor relationships that will assist with the execution of the event.</li>
<li>Stay current with knowledge of other South Florida community and fundraising events, identifying scheduling conflicts and/or opportunities for partnership.</li>
<li>Represent FSF at public events and speaking engagements.</li>
<li>Ensure timely and accurate reporting including dashboards, weekly reports, paperwork, contract management/fulfillment, compliance with grant guidelines, spreadsheets and narratives, as needed.</li>
<li>Ensure all phone calls, e-mails, letters, etc. are returned as expeditiously as possible, preferably in the same day.</li>
<li>Maintain detailed records of all event activities and ensure all contact information is up to date in FSF’s CRM system, following established protocols.</li>
<li>Assist DoD and Executive team as necessary.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>QUALIFICATIONS</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor's Degree</li>
<li>A minimum of three years demonstrated event planning, management, and execution experience.</li>
<li>A minimum of three years nonprofit event fundraising experience.</li>
<li>Demonstrated knowledge of sponsorship and ticket sales strategies.</li>
<li>Successful recruitment and management of volunteer committees.</li>
<li>Strong peer-to-peer fundraising experience, utilizing software and online tools.</li>
<li>Self-motivated, innovative, and can work with considerable independence within the context of an overall strategy, team environment, and a variety of stakeholders.</li>
<li>Demonstrated ability to take primary responsibility for diverse number of projects and to complete them accurately and timely.</li>
<li>Desire and willingness to attend industry events and develop vendor relationships.</li>
<li>Passion for community engagement; a “people” person.</li>
<li>Highly literate in Microsoft Office Products (PowerPoint, Excel, Word, etc.) and CRM software (Raiser’s Edge NXT preferred).</li>
<li>Ability to travel throughout FSF’s service area.</li>
<li>Commitment and adherence to organization mission and values.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li>Based on OSHA Standards Lifting requirements are 50 pounds. When lifting loads heavier than 50 pounds, use two or more people to lift the load.</li>
<li>This position works primarily in an office and warehouse setting. There may be extended periods of driving, sitting, standing and/or bending, listening, talking and/or visual concentration, writing and/or computer use.</li>
<li>After business hours work is required occasionally throughout the year, including weekends and holidays. Frequent travel between quad-county areas is required. Occasional same day, out of town travel for meeting, conferences and special events using personal vehicle and/or public transportation is required.</li>
<li>Involves activities in both office and warehouse environments with exposure to the climate and South Florida weather.</li>
<li>The noise level in the work environment is usually moderate to loud.</li>
</ul>
