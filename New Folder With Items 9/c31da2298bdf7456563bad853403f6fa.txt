<h1 class="jobSectionHeader"><b><i>Position Summary:</i></b></h1>
<p>
The Business Analyst (BA) is responsible for analyzing the business needs of the product line to support product improvements and innovations, including software development efforts. The BA will work with business departments, stakeholders and product owner to learn, analyze and document current business processes and application functionality as well as elicit and document new requirements. The BA is responsible for writing business requirements and translating them into appropriate technical specifications to be used by the technology teams in new product development and testing, in accordance with Salesforce best practices. A proficient BA will learn and understand the business and advise the business on how to best leverage the capabilities inherent in Salesforce.</p>
<p>
The successful BA will be responsible for these activities related to Truckstop's Salesforce Sales and Service Cloud Solution. Experience with Zuora's CPQ product, or other CSQ experience is highly desirable but not required.</p>
<h1 class="jobSectionHeader"><b><i><br>

<h1 class="jobSectionHeader"><b><i>
Essential Job Functions:</i></b></h1>
<p>
The BA acts as a liaison between business departments, technology teams, and quality assurance team to ensure requirements are met and incorporated into system design, deployment and testing. Project assignments often require preliminary discussions with the business to bring clarity to the scope and objectives. The BA coordinates and fosters consensus among multi-component teams in the development of technology projects. The BA articulates the technology available in the Salesforce, and to advise on inherent best practice to leverage that technology.</p>
<p>
Follows standard requirements definition methodologies including developing Use Cases, High Level Designs, Wire Frames, Process Flows, User Stories, and other required documents. The BA could be involved in all phases from need identification to implementation. The business could engage the BA once the solution (business requirements and design) are defined and take them to enough detail that the requirements are testable and verifiable.</p>
