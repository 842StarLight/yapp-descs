<h2>Program Manager, Corporate Partnerships</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<div><div><div>
<div><b>What You Will Do:</b></div>
<ul>
<li>
Work with the Director, Corporate Partnerships on all aspects of the partnership sales process including but not limited to prospect research, prospect outreach, proposal development, sales collateral materials and presentations, prospect communication and other projects as assigned.</li>
<li>
Identify new partnership opportunities by identifying sellable programs and assets within the PVA organization, which may be uncovered by working collaboratively across the organization and with respective program leaders.</li>
<li>
Stay informed and up-to-date of industry trends as well as successes/wins achieved by others in the nonprofit sector as a benchmark, and use this to identify ways or assets that could be of value to prospective partners.</li>
<li>
Keep precise records, using the Eleo or other department approved online software and/or tools, of correspondence, conversations and communications with all prospects and existing partners for reporting and tracking purposes, and for information sharing with department and executive leadership.</li>
</ul>
<div><div><div>
<p><b>
Other Things You May Do:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Lead internal and external sales meetings and conversations.</li>
<li>
Develop strategies and strategic plans for prospective partners, to be reviewed and approved by the Director of Corporate Partnerships.</li>
<li>
Assist in sponsorship/partnership fulfillment and administrative functions, and take lead where necessary or as assigned by the Director of Corporate Partnerships.</li>
<li>
Travel will be required.</li>
</ul>
<div><div><div>
<p><b>
About You:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
You have knowledge of current corporate social responsibility and fundraising trends.</li>
<li>
You enjoy selling and the sales process.</li>
<li>
You establish relationships easily and can build personal connections with all levels of an organization.</li>
<li>
You are confident and demonstrate self-confidence without coming across as arrogant. You are self-assured about your abilities and opinions, yet convey an appropriate level of humility and respect for the abilities, opinions and ideas of others.</li>
<li>
You have a very strong attention to detail and are highly organized.</li>
<li>
You are entrepreneurial by nature and can identify new fund-raising opportunities in real time.</li>
<li>
You are persuasive and convincing. You can inspire and motivate people to behave in a desired manner.</li>
<li>
You are self-driven and can easily brainstorm and develop ideas in real-time.</li>
<li>
You are collaborative and able to work with colleagues and peers across organizations, internal and external.</li>
<li>
You are open-minded, willing to consider new ideas and adaptable to change and new situations.</li>
</ul>
<p>
PVA is an Equal Opportunity Employer</p>
