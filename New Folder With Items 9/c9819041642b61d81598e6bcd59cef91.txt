<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Duties</b></h2>
<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Summary</b></h3>
<div>
<p>As an Equipment Specialist you will serve as the Calibration Manager, you will oversee coordination of laboratory equipment installation, maintenance, repair, validation and calibration in conjunction with the scientists and technicians scheduling and facility needs at the Foreign Animal Disease Diagnostic Laboratory of the National Bio and Agro-Defense Facility.</p>
<br>
</div>
<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Responsibilities</b></h3>
<div><ul>
<li>The duties described are for the full-performance level. At developmental grade levels, assignments will be of more limited scope, performed with less independence and limited complexity.</li>
<li>The duties may include, but are not limited to:</li>
<li>Maintains the Calibration Laboratory Autoclave automated validation instruments, equipment, and software.</li>
<li>Provides training and mentorship to the calibration technician.</li>
<li>Monitors scientific developments in literature, within national and international laboratories, centers of excellence, and other entities to identify and prepare for use of new technologies.</li>
<li>Provides oversight for the routine and regular scheduling and maintenance of equipment.</li>
<li>Oversees routine inspections and maintenance of support fixtures, adapters, and seals to verify suitability and safety.</li>
<li>Compiles information and provides documentation to the customers to meet regulatory requirements as applicable.</li>
<li>Performs complex calculations and documents data results in a manner which ensures accuracy, legibility, traceability, security, and retrievability.</li>
</ul></div>

<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Requirements</b></h2>
<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Conditions of Employment</b></h3>
<ul>
<li>You must be a US Citizen or US National.</li>
<li>Males born after 12/31/1959 must be Selective Service registered or exempt.</li>
<li>Subject to satisfactory adjudication of background investigation and/or fingerprint check.</li>
<li>Successful completion of one-year probationary period, unless previously served.</li>
<li>Prior to an official offer, selectee will be required to sign a memorandum of understanding (MOU) acknowledging/accepting relocation from Orient Point, NY to Manhattan, KS. Failure to sign the MOU will result in withdrawal of the job offer.</li>
<li>For information regarding the National Bio and Agro-Defense Facility (NBAF) in Manhattan, KS visit the website: https://www.usda.gov/nbaf (must copy and paste to browser)</li>
<li>Subject to CJIS background investigation.</li>
<li>Testing Designated Position: Selectee must submit to a urinalysis to screen for illegal drug use prior to appointment and random testing thereafter. Final appointment and continued employment is conditional on negative results for illegal drug use.</li>
<li>Direct Deposit: Per Public Law 104-134 all Federal employees are required to have federal payments made by direct deposit to their financial institution.</li>
<li>Successfully pass the E-Verify employment verification check. To learn more about E-Verify, including your rights and responsibilities, visit E-Verify at https://www.e-verify.gov/</li>
<li>Vaccinations may be required.</li>
<li>Your position will require daily travel to and from either Orient Point, New York and Plum Island, or Old Saybrook, Connecticut and Plum Island aboard Government-provided marine vessels.</li>
<li>You may be exposed to risks and hazards of harmful biological, chemical and physical agents while working/reaching into incubators, hoods, centrifuges and other automated equipment.</li>
<li>You may have exposure to disease conditions and agents that would require personal protective equipment/clothing, safeguards and enrollment in a health monitoring program.</li>
<li>Your duties will include changing of clothes before entering the BSL3 space, shower before leaving from containment, standing or sitting for extended periods of time, high noise levels, working with and restraining animals.</li>
<li>You may have restrictions on contact with birds, sheep, goats, cattle, and swine outside of the laboratory due to potential contamination issues.</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Qualifications</b></h3>
<div>Applicants must meet all qualifications and eligibility requirements by the closing date of the announcement, including specialized experience and/or education, as defined below.<br>
<br>
<b>FOR THE GS-09 LEVEL: </b>Applicants must have one year of specialized experience (equivalent to the GS-07 level) that may have been obtained in the private or public (local, county, state, Federal) sectors which demonstrates:
<ul>
<li>Reviewing and applying technical specifications and requirements.</li>
<li>Analyzing equipment and forecasting spare parts requirements.</li>
<li>Reading and interpreting engineering change orders, equipment requisitions, shop orders, and work orders.</li>
</ul>
<b>OR</b> Master's or equivalent graduate degree in Engineering or Biological Sciences OR 2 years of progressively higher level graduate education leading to such a degree.<br>
<br>
Equivalent combinations of education and experience are qualifying for this grade level.<br>
<br>
<b>FOR THE GS-11 LEVEL: </b>Applicants must have one year of specialized experience (equivalent to the GS-09 level) that may have been obtained in the private or public (local, county, state, Federal) sectors which demonstrates:
<ul>
<li>Working as a skilled mechanic or maintenance worker, sales or service engineer, or planner or estimator in a technical group dealing with manufacture, utilization, repair, or operation of equipment.</li>
<li>Analyzing or supervising maintenance, repair, or production operations to determine methods of improvement.</li>
</ul>
<b>OR </b>Ph.D. or equivalent doctoral degree in Engineering or Biological Sciences OR 3 full years of progressively higher level graduate education leading to such a degree.<br>
<br>
Equivalent combinations of education and experience are qualifying for this grade level.<br>
<br>
<b>TRANSCRIPTS</b> are required if:
<ul>
<li>You are qualifying for the position based on education.</li>
<li>You are qualifying for this position based on a combination of experience and education.</li>
<li>This education must have been successfully completed and obtained from an accredited school, college, or university</li>
</ul>
<div>
<b>COMBINATION OF EDUCATION &amp; EXPERIENCE AT THE GS-09/11 GRADE LEVELS:</b> Applicants may have combinations of successfully completed education and specialized experience to meet total qualification requirements. The total percentages must equal at least 100 percent to qualify for that grade level.<br>
<br>
Experience refers to paid and unpaid experience, including volunteer work done through National Service programs (e.g., Peace Corps, AmeriCorps) and other organizations (e.g., professional, philanthropic, religious, spiritual, community, student, social). Volunteer work helps build critical competencies and can provide valuable training and experience that translates directly to paid employment. You will receive credit for all qualifying experience, including volunteer experience.</div>
