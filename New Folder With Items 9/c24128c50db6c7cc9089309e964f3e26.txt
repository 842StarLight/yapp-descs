<h2>Chemical Process Engineer</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>CannaCraft is one of the largest Cannabis companies in California, representing AbsoluteExtracts, Care By Design, Loud + Clear, HiFi Hops, and Satori Chocolates. We are looking for a dynamic Market Director to build our presence in the Colorado market! The Market Director is responsible for the development and growth of the CannaCraft family of brands with accountability for sales, account penetration, and customer acquisition. This position requires a high level of clear, effective communication and will work closely with the VP of Science.</p>
<p><b>
Essential Functions:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Knowledge and experience in technologies associated with continuous flow chemistry are preferred.</li>
<li>
Knowledge of and experience with in-situ reaction monitoring technology is preferred.</li>
<li>
Knowledge or expertise in principles of process control, including platform programming, automation development, and integration is preferred.</li>
<li>
Knowledge or expertise in crystallization process design, including final form screening/polymorph screening/salt screening, is preferred.</li>
<li>
Knowledge or expertise in systematic scaling-up or scaling-down processes is preferred.</li>
<li>
Experience in planning and executing the experimental design in a laboratory setting as well as operating in a pilot-plant or commercial environment.</li>
<li>
Application of key principles of chemical engineering to work within a matrix-based chemical development team to construct systems and design chemical processes that will efficiently support on-scale safe production of promising pharmaceutical candidates.</li>
<li>
Collaboration with process development chemists and analytical chemists to develop safer and more robust processes for laboratory and plant production.</li>
<li>
Expected to work closely with partner teams, including chemistry, analytical, and manufacturing operations to coordinate project related equipment and activities.</li>
<li>
Development of key process improvements and strategies leading to improved yield, throughput, purity, increased efficiency, capacity enhancement, and reduced cost.</li>
<li>
Maintain current knowledge of state-of-the-art techniques for improving engineering principles related to organic chemistry, analytical chemistry, process development techniques and cGMP regulations. Knowledge of root cause/batch failure analysis.</li>
<li>
Actively promote the results of ongoing projects within the group and to external clients.</li>
<li>
Help support and improve distillation of like molecules.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>
Other Experience/Skills:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Experience operating in a GMP environment is a plus.</li>
<li>
Strong technical writing skills are required for developing customer reports, presentations and internal documents.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>
Education/Knowledge Requirements:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Ph.D. degree in chemical engineering required, at a minimum.</li>
<li>
A minimum of 5 years' experience in the pharmaceutical or comparable chemical industry is required.</li>
<li>
Strong skills and knowledge in organic chemistry, especially as applicable to process chemistry. Excellent verbal and written communication/interpersonal skills, problem-solving skills, organizational skills, and the ability to work in a diverse team environment are essential. Proficiency with Microsoft software (Word / EXCEL / PowerPoint) is expected.</li>
<li>
Experience with engineering software and process modeling is preferred.</li>
</ul>
