<h2>Warehouse Person (Minneapolis)</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<div><div><div>
<p>The Warehouse Person is responsible for receiving and storing parts and supplies and supporting the parts area by maintaining the parts inventory. It's an critical position in a fast paced environment. You will have a feeling of accomplishment at the end of the day. Boyer - Come for the job. Stay for a career.</p>
<ul>
<li>All phases of the inventory process and flow, including inventory cycle counting</li>
<li>Shipping, receiving, picking and stocking of parts and materials</li>
<li>Receive and verify order quantities on shipments</li>
<li>Prepare order shipments, deliveries and customer will-call using computer software systems</li>
<li>Maintain shipping &amp; receiving records</li>
<li>Pick parts orders for the front counter sales</li>
<li>Stock parts shelves and assist customers as needed</li>
<li>Maintain professional image and positive attitude</li>
<li>Perform other duties as assigned</li>
<li>Follow all clean up and housekeeping procedures</li>
<li>Perform all duties and activities in a safe manner and report any safety issues immediately to supervisor</li>
<li>Ability to work in fast paced environment with capability to multi-task</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Physical Demands</b><br>
The physical demands described here are representative of those that must be met by an employee to successfully perform the essential functions of this job. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions. This position requires the individual to stand, sit, bend. Will need to push/pull up to 100 lbs. Occasionally lift to 100 lbs.</p>
</div></div></div>

Qualifications</div>

<p>The minimum education &amp; experience requirements to perform the essential duties and responsibilities of this position are: at least one-year experience in a warehouse environment.</p>
