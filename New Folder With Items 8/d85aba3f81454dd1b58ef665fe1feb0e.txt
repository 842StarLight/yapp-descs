<p><b>
Position Summary &amp; Responsibilities:</b></p>
<p>
Directs the Federal Surplus Property, State Surplus Property, FPV and LESO/1033 programs. Demonstrates leadership with subordinates. Supervises a staff of administrative, clerical and warehousing personnel. Develops, implements and enforces rules and regulations reading the four programs. Evaluates the effectiveness of staff and designs initiatives to assist staff in developing and improving job skills and performance. Provides support and recommendations for improvement of the three programs. Encourages innovation, creativity, cross discipline teamwork, employee involvement, intra-agency communication, and change management. Implements accountability, initiative, and responsibility with all subordinates.
</p>
<p>Directs the sales, management and marketing of the Federal Surplus, State Surplus, FPV and LESO/1033 programs. Serve as key liaison on all policy and financial matters pertaining to the programs. Develop, implement and direct contracts in accordance with state regulations as per: Federal Plan of Operations, and LESO/1033 Plan of Operations. Interpret, comply, and monitor all operations are in compliance with all applicable state and federal laws, mandates, and statutes.
</p>
<p>Develop policies and guidelines to ensure compliance. Enforces deliberate, intensive screening, marketing and advertising of property to ensure proper acquisition/sale/disposition of property. Develop inventory pricing guidelines within state and federal parameters. Maintains inventory accounting controls pursuant to state and federal rules and regulations. Serve as the governor appointed State of Kansas Coordinator for the LESO/1033 program. Includes the brokerage of federal surplus to law enforcement and compliance audits as mandated by federal regulations upon donees.
</p>
<p>Develop and implement the budget by developing strategic plans for allocating the agencies fee funded budgets to sustain agency operations while meeting organizational goals and objectives for all the programs. Accountable for implementing and controlling agency budgets by following accounting principles. Prepares weekly, monthly, and/or quarterly reports for GSA and management.
</p>

<p><b>Qualifications:</b></p>
<p><b>
Minimum Qualifications</b></p>
<p>
One year of experience in providing direction necessary to implement the objectives of an agency, program or organizational unit. Education may be substituted for experience as determined relevant by the agency. Must have a valid driver’s license.
</p>
<p><b>Preferred Qualifications</b></p>
<p>
Working knowledge of supply management principles, office administration, current market values of equipment, state property resolutions, and sales/marketing management. Eligibility and compliance training for participants of the Federal Surplus Property and LESO/1033 programs. Civil Rights training for Title 6 and Title 0 to ensure compliance under federal laws pertaining to GSA Civil Rights requirements. Minimum of three years leadership experience.
</p>
