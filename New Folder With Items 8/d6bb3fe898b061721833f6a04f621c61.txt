<h2>Senior Auditor Enterprise Risk Mgmt Prog</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div><div><div><div>
<div>
<div><p>The Senior Auditor Enterprise Risk Management facilitates JetBlue’s Enterprise Risk Management (ERM) program and is responsible for partnering with leadership to identify, evaluate, mitigate, and monitor enterprise risks. Furthermore, the Senior Auditor is a liaison for reporting findings and status to Corporate Audit Leadership, key stakeholders, JetBlue Senior Leadership, and/or the Audit Committee.</p></div>
<div><p>
The Senior Auditoralso assists with the annual audit plan by leading and performing internal audits and conducting continuous auditing and monitoring reviews, including participation in and/or monitoring project implementations. The Senior Auditor provides leadership with accurate, independent, and timely recommendations regarding control weaknesses and opportunities for improvement at all airline and support center locations, including but not limited to airport operations, purchasing and receiving, inventory, accounts payable, payroll, leases, sales and revenue recognition, cash management, employee benefits, and flight rewards.</p></div>
<div><p>
Essential Responsibilities</p></div>
<div><ul><li><p>
Facilitates the implementation of JetBlue’s ERM program and proactively provides key input to the enhancement and execution of the program, ensuring alignment with organizational/strategic objectives.</p></li></ul></div>
</div>
<div><div><ul>
<li><p>
Facilitates the identification, assessment, and prioritization of risks, remaining cognizant of changes in the risk environment and maintaining the company’s risk profile.</p></li>
<li><p>
Facilitates the development, implementation, and monitoringof risk response/mitigation plans, including risk drivers, mitigating controls, and key risk indicators (KRIs) that outline Leadership’s risk tolerance levels and appetite.</p></li>
<li><p>
Maintains the ERM Risk Dashboard and reports all relevant ERM matters (including status, findings, and risk positions) to Corporate Audit Leadership, key stakeholders, JetBlue Senior Leadership, and/or the Audit Committee to support efficient Leadership decision making.</p></li>
<li><p>
Develops and maintains strong working relationships with all levels of the company (including Senior Leadership) in order to partner with them and assist them in achieving their goals.</p></li>
<li><p>
Leads and performs internal audits (financial, operational, and/or Sarbanes-Oxley Act – Section 404) in accordance with the annual audit plan and department manual/guidelines.</p></li>
</ul></div></div>
<div><div><ul>
<li><p>
Prepares and finalizes planning and announcement memos, assists in determining audit scope, and prepares and executes audit programs.</p></li>
<li><p>
Works with process owners to obtain a full understanding of the control environment and prepares narratives, flowcharts, and risk assessment matrices to document risks and their respective mitigating controls.</p></li>
<li><p>
Analyzes audit evidence and documents work performed and conclusions reached.</p></li>
<li><p>
Monitors team status and keeps the Corporate Audit Managerinformed of the status of allaudits in progress and any issues noted.</p></li>
<li><p>
Supervises test work, performs detailed review of staff work papers (and peer reviews if necessary) to ensure compliance with department and IIA standards, prepares coaching notes, and ensures that coaching notes are satisfactorily closed.</p></li>
</ul></div></div>
<div>
<div><ul>
<li><p>
Provides consolidated written documentation (draft audit report) of all audit issues and agreed-upon corrective actions for closing meetings. Responsible for conducting closing meetings.</p></li>
<li><p>
Tracks outstanding audit issues and ensures that they are remediated timely.</p></li>
<li><p>
Other duties as assigned.</p></li>
</ul></div>
<div><p>
