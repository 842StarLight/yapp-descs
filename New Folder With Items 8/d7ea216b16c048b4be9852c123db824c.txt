<h2>Electrical Engineer</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<p>ARC is a company of innovators and problem solvers that help our Fortune 500 clients solve their most difficult manufacturing challenges through our technology solutions. We work hand in hand with top aerospace, defense, transportation, industrial and electronics companies to bring new productivity solutions to the forefront. Join us and you will be working on different challenges every day.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>This is an electrical engineering position for someone with analog and digital design experience specifically related to test fixturing, PCB schematic creation, and electrical cabinet assemblies. Additional knowledge with FPGA’s, radar and other military systems would be an added benefit. The candidate will work closely with other engineers to design, build, document, and support electro-mechanical assemblies used in industrial automated test and measurement equipment.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p><b>Responsibilities</b><b> </b><b>may include:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Design, document, prototype, and oversee the production of circuit card assemblies (CCA’s) for digital and analog test applications.</li>
<li>Design test solutions using automated test equipment (ATE), rack mounted test fixtures or adapters, and CCA’s.</li>
<li>Review customer’s statements of work (SOW) and design solutions that meet customer requirements.</li>
<li>Work with other engineering disciplines and sales to create project proposals for customers.</li>
<li>Work with other engineering disciplines to integrate test systems and customer solutions.</li>
<li>Document and develop prototype systems.</li>
<li>Participate in technical interchange meetings with customers and vendors.</li>
<li>Specify electrical components and sub assemblies for designs and work with project managers to coordinate manufacturing documents.</li>
<li>Some local and national travel will be required, but is typically less than 15%</li>
<br>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>Skills</b> <b>Requested:</b>
</p>
<ul>
<li>An understanding of electronics principles, including component theory, analog and digital design practices, and a well-developed ability to read and interpret schematics and other technical drawings.</li>
<li>Experience with the use of contemporary schematic capture software such as Altium or Mentor.</li>
<li>Experience with CAD software such as AutoCAD or Draftsight.</li>
<li>Experience with the use of common test equipment (DMMs, O’scopes, DAQs, Spectrum Analyzers etc.).</li>
<li>Self-motivated, proactive with a positive attitude and the ability to be hands-on with engineering projects.</li>
<li>Must work well within an engineering team as well as independently and without constant supervision.</li>
<li>Ability to think and act quickly in a fast-paced environment.</li>
<li>Ability to organize and prioritize multiple tasks to ensure deadlines are met.</li>
<li>Excellent verbal and written communication skills, with an emphasis on technical writing.</li>
<br>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>Education and Experience:</b></p>
<p>One of the following is required:</p>
<ul>
<li>A Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering, Computer Engineering, or related field of study, and two years of applied engineering experience relevant to ARC’s core competencies of automated test and measurement.</li>
</ul>
<p>- or -</p>
<ul><li>An Associate’s degree in related field plus four years of prior experience in a field relevant to ARC’s core competencies.</li></ul>
