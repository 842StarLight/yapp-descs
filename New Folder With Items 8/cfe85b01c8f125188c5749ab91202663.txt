<div>Business Manager Â¿ Diversity, Inclusion &amp; Outreach<br>
</div>
<div></div>
<div>Performs responsible administrative duties in the business operation of the Office of Diversity, Inclusion &amp; Outreach. Responsibilities include business management for departmental budgets, grant compliance, financial and statistical reporting, completion of personnel transactions, office purchasing, requisitions, travel, reimbursements, completion of surveys, maintenance of capital inventory and operational program expenses. Performs technical clerical work involving the coordination of day-to-day operations, including exercise of independent judgment. Work is performed under general supervision and performance evaluation is based upon completion of assignments and results obtained. The performance evaluation is conducted through the performance evaluation system and in accordance with the University Policies &amp; Procedures.<br>
</div>
<div></div>
<div><b><i>ORGANIZATIONAL RELATIONSHIPS<br>
</i></b></div>
<div></div>
<div>
<i>Reports to: </i>Executive Director of Diversity, Inclusion &amp; Outreach<br>
</div>
<div></div>
<div>
<i>Supervises: </i>Student Assistants<br>
</div>
<div></div>
<div>
<b><i>ESSENTIAL DUTIES - </i></b><i>May include, but not limited to the following:<br>
</i>
</div>
<div></div>
<ul>
<li>Manages accounting and personnel functions within the Office of Diversity, Inclusion &amp; Outreach (DIO).
</li>
<li>Assists individual staff with the management of grant budgets.
</li>
<li>Manages all accounts under the management of the Executive Director.
</li>
<li>Assists with the management and compliance of accounts.
</li>
<li>Assists with the preparation of budgets for grant requests.
</li>
<li>Manages all office purchasing, travel, and operational expenses for staff.
</li>
<li>Assists with purchasing for retention programs, mentor programs, GO Programs, grants, multicultural programming and DIO.
</li>
<li>Makes travel arrangements and processes requisitions and reimbursement requests for Executive Director and professional staff.
</li>
<li>Establishes and maintains a database for DIO and GO programs to track student retention.
</li>
<li>Manages compliance and timelines for grant reports for staff.
</li>
<li>Maintains capital equipment inventory.
</li>
<li>Reports needs for building maintenance.
</li>
<li>Develops and interprets policy and procedure for business related functions.
</li>
<li>Develops and maintains communication with various departments within the TWU Community and other various DIO program partnerships.
</li>
<li>Completes surveys and reports as required.
</li>
<li>Oversees, the security of records, data, equipment, materials, and facilities.
</li>
<li>Performs technical clerical work involving the exercise of independent judgment.
</li>
<li>Exercises independent judgment in selecting and transcribing data from documents that have not been pre-examined or coded.
</li>
<li>Makes appointments for supervisor; receives and screens visitors.
</li>
<li>Maintains a variety of complex and/or confidential records and files.
</li>
<li>Prepares annual appointment and budget forms, periodic reports, class and/or office schedules.<br>
</li>
</ul>

<b><i>KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS, AND ABILITIES - </i></b><i>The following are essential:<br>
</i>
</div>
<div></div>
<ul>
<li>Knowledge of modern business practices.
</li>
<li>Working knowledge of modern accounting principles and procedures.
</li>
<li>Ability to organize work effectively, conceptualize and prioritize objectives and exercise independent judgment based on an understanding of organizational policies and activities.
</li>
<li>Ability to integrate resources, policies, and information for the determination of procedures, solutions, and other outcomes.
</li>
<li>Knowledge of management methods and ability to provide administrative guidance and provide direct training and supervision as needed.
</li>
<li>Ability to apply budgeting and fiscal planning techniques within financial constraints.
</li>
<li>Ability to establish and maintain effective work relationships with students, faculty, staff, and the public.
</li>
<li>Ability to communicate effectively - orally, by phone, in person, and in writing.
</li>
<li>Ability to work indoors.
</li>
<li>Ability to distinguish the nature of objects by using the eye.
</li>
<li>Ability to use a personal computer and other office equipment, including university software and email.<br>
</li>
