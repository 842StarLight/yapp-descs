Hillrom is a $2.8B leading worldwide manufacturer and provider of medical technologies and related services for the health care industry, including patient support systems, safe mobility and handling solutions, non-invasive therapeutic products for a variety of acute and chronic medical conditions, medical equipment rentals, surgical products and information technology solutions. <div><b>
JOB SUMMARY:</b></div>
<div>
Responsible for successful implementation of process improvement through program and process assessment and development (clinical adverse event prevention); best-practice standards implementation; and resource/educational support.</div>
<div><b>
</b></div>
<div>
<b>
ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES: </b><b><i>â€“ Other duties may be assigned:</i></b>
</div>
<ul>
<li>
Implement post sales education regarding program analysis; development; implementation; and support related to the prevention of patient falls and increasing HCAHPS scores.</li>
<li>
Clinically assess health care organizations related to the effectiveness of current processes and protocols regarding the prevention of patient falls and addressing patient dissatisfaction.</li>
<li>
Collaboratively work with individual health care organizations to ensure successful implementation of program and process implementation resulting in customer satisfaction and increased patient satisfaction.</li>
<li>
Lead clinical configuration workshops to guide clinicians in their choice of clinical configurations of the system for enterprise and unit based settings.</li>
<li>
Develop and present individual health care organization assessment findings including gap analysis reports.</li>
<li>
Develop and implement account specific clinical programs and protocols to enhance patient outcomes and decrease adverse events.</li>
<li>
Provide caregiver education on specific facility or unit configurations and employ super user/train-the-trainer methodology.</li>
<li>
Deliver caregiver education on how to interpret system reports that will lead to positive change and improved patient outcomes.</li>
<li>
Responsibility for managing the backlog of clinical consulting days for assigned customers and working to convert backlog to delivered days.</li>
<li>
Present, develop, and manage clinical change management programs leading to successful technology adoption.</li>
<li>
Collect data, analyze, and present to customers to demonstrate program and consulting success. Prioritization of projects based on customer needs and the needs of the business.</li>
</ul>

QUALIFICATIONS:</b></div>
<div><ul>
<li>
RN, BSN, or other healthcare degree required with 3+ years spent in the hospital clinical environment working with all levels of hospital staff and leadership.</li>
<li>
Willingness to travel to customer locations 80%.</li>
<li>
Ability to design and successfully implement clinical change management and technology adoption programs.</li>
<li>
Ability to develop program education to implement best-practice and evidenced-based standards for high-level quality patient outcomes.</li>
<li>
Excellent presentation and report development skills</li>
<li>
Experience with Microsoft Office</li>
<li>
Must be able to work in team environment or independently making clinical consulting decisions without guidance.</li>
<li>
Strong conflict resolution skills, including the abilities to negotiate and influence others</li>
<li>
Ability to collaborate with Project Managers, Implementation Engineers and other internal stakeholders to ensure a quality customer experience</li>
<li>
Exceptional communication skills to successfully overcome resistance to change and drive clinical change management programs resulting in successful adoption of technology.</li>

Good problem solving and analytical skills to understand key customer clinical issues and define how IT solutions will solve challenges facing our customers today</li>

Experience with standards development based on healthcare industry trends.</li>
