<h2>Business and Finance Budget Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>If Temporary or Visiting, Estimated End Date: Position Summary and Duties
<p>The Business and Finance Budget Manager reports to the Assistant Vice President for Budget and Business Intelligence with responsibilities for supporting the Associate Vice President for Facilities and Campus Services, and working with other directors in Facilities and Campus Services, the Business and Finance Budget Manager provides policy guidance, strategic financial planning, budget management and directs the preparation and administration of annual and capital budgets. The Business and Finance Budget Manager will also be involved in broader University budget analyses.<br>
Responsibilities include, but are not limited to:<br>
1) Conduct statistical and financial analyses of Facilities, Campus Services, and other Business &amp; Finance strategies, policy initiatives and issues. Analyze and forecast major economic and financial trends. Direct the preparation and administration of annual operational and capital budgets for both short- and long-range fiscal planning. Analyze and track costs for all Facilities, Campus Services and other Business &amp; Finance budgets, including the collection, analysis and forecasting of financial information throughout the year. Monitor actual performance versus budget, researching and investigating any variances to expected results. Responsible for analytical support of Facilities’ budgetary issues such as deferred maintenance, cost summaries for construction projects, Facilities’ services, warehouse and other department functions.<br>
2) Serve as project lead for high-level assignments, performing complex ad hoc reporting and analysis for senior management. Responsible for providing data and working with the Controller’s Office on fixed assets, annual audit issues, construction-in-progress reports and utility accruals. Develop accounting policy guidance for transactions and activities of the department. Analyze and evaluate current business practices and recommend actions and best practices. Responsible for successful and timely completion of fiscal year-end closing. Provide and supervise internal control functions in the department, assuring control of financial resources, records and accountability are being met.<br>
3) Responsible for developing and maintaining process to ensure the management of contractual arrangements for construction projects, as well as the bookstore and Parkhurst food services. Responsible for providing audit information on auxiliary services as requested each year for the University’s annual internal and external audits. Responsible for the administration of risk management insurance premiums such as professional liability, workers’ compensation, vehicle, property and the Middle Atlantic Catholic Risk Management Consortium Group insurance. Responsible for processing of payments to the State of Maryland and/or Baltimore City regarding real property registrations and retail licensing taxes.<br>
4) Work with various campus departments in developing and implementing processes for interdepartmental budget needs. Responsible for developing, documenting, communicating and implementing internal personnel procedures and policies. Demonstrate continuous effort to improve budget operations and streamline work processes.</p>
Required Qualifications
<p>Bachelor’s degree and 5 to 8 years of experience in accounting, procurement, and business operations required.<br>
Possess strong human relations/customer service skills and demonstrate the ability to establish and maintain effective working relationships with diverse constituencies.<br>
Be skilled in the use of budget software and database systems.<br>
Strong ability to communicate effectively, both orally and in writing.<br>
Demonstrate success in working with diverse constituencies.<br>
Proficiency in Microsoft Office including Word, Excel, Outlook and PowerPoint.<br>
Ability to effectively interpret University and departmental personnel and accounting policies, procedures and practices.<br>
Ability to support the mission and values of a Catholic Jesuit education, as well as University goals for institutional diversity.</p>
Preferred Qualifications
<p>Master’s degree in accounting or related business field.<br>
Knowledge of and experience in higher education preferred but not required.</p>

<p>Loyola University Maryland is a Jesuit, Catholic university committed to the educational and spiritual traditions of the Society of Jesus and to the ideals of liberal education and the development of the whole person. Accordingly, Loyola inspires students to learn, lead, and serve in a diverse and changing world. Loyola’s beautiful, historic Evergreen campus is located in Baltimore, and its graduate centers are in Timonium and Columbia. Loyola enrolls 4,000 undergraduate and 2,000 graduate students across the Sellinger School of Business and Management, the School of Education, and Loyola College of Arts and Sciences. Founded in 1852, Loyola is one of 27 Jesuit colleges and universities in the United States and the first to bear the name of Saint Ignatius Loyola, founder of the Society of Jesus.</p>
