<h2>Strategic Planning & Operations Sr. Manager, Thoracic Early Asset</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>We have world-class cross-functional staff across the globe including sales and marketing, regional medical liaisons, oncology nurse specialists and customer account teams to ensure our discoveries go from bench to bedside.</p>
<p></p>
<p>
The Strategic Planning &amp; Operations Sr. Manager for Thoracic Early Asset is a critical role for Amgen’s US Oncology Business Unit. The Sr. Manager will report to the Executive Director/Brand Lead, with responsibility spanning AMG510 Marketing. Responsibilities will include leading the cross functional process for development of the US Local Product Strategy (LPS) for the brand. The LPS will serve as the foundational compass from which the Strategic Planning &amp; Operations Sr. Manager will ensure seamless execution of strategy across disciplines and development/tracking of key performance indicators (important metrics) to measure brand success at the strategic and tactical level. The Sr. Manager is the primary point in partnering with Business Analysis &amp; Information (BAI) and Finance to help develop the annual Long Range Plan (LRP), Latest Estimate (LE) forecasts, Brand Plan, and Quarterly Business Reviews (QBR). They will also analyze customer brand/competitive product utilization, contract performance, and market trends to identify further opportunities for growth, as well as areas for course correction where necessary. The Sr. Manager will also serve as the lead for operational management of the budget spend for the brand.</p>
<p>
Additionally, the Sr. Manager will partner with the Brand Lead in assessing the market potential for pipeline solid tumor assets. This will include detailing incidence/prevalence, input on the target product profile, assessment of unmet need, market sizing, disease state, patient journey, current and future competitive landscape as well as evaluation of potential companion diagnostics. This will require close partnership with the Global Product General Manager, the Product Team and additional cross-functional team members to ensure optimal preparation for future potential solid tumor product launches.</p>
