<h2>Clerk III</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div><div>
<div><div>Are you ready to be professionally challenged? Are you looking to be part of a dynamic team of engaged and committed professionals? Then the Health and Specialty Care System (HSCS) may be just the place for you! The HSCS is comprised of nine psychiatric hospitals located throughout the state, and one youth residential psychiatric center in Waco. The HSCS is a hub of excellence for forensic mental health and complex psychiatric care, with all facilities accredited by The Joint Commission. The hospitals provide state-of-the-art treatment that is recovery-oriented and science-based. Whether in a direct-care or support role, your contribution will make a difference in the life of a Texan.
<br>
<br>
The Clerk III works a Monday to Friday, 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM schedule in the Maintenance Department.
<br>
<br>
The Clerk III Property Clerk develops, coordinates, and maintains record keeping and filing systems for all Rusk State Hospital controlled capital assets to ensure accountability and control of State Property. The Property Clerk works under the general supervision of the Program Supervisor V and works closely with the Maintenance Department to coordinate storage and movement of assets. Completion of work requires use of established methods and/or techniques and decisions are varied but based on well-defined guidelines. The Property Clerk is accountable for assigned property. This position also creates and maintains Excel spreadsheets to track data. Must be able to work effectively in a fast-paced environment, adapt to changes, have good interpersonal skills and be able to perform property duties with a minimum of supervision. Must be able to perform normal lifting activities associated with the job and may be required to travel on occasion. Must be able to communicate effectively verbally and in writing. Must be able to understand and follow oral and written instructions. Must be able to work with computers and be familiar with office-related computer software. Must understand that the duties, location of work and shift pattern are subject to change as determined by administration in order to meet department and hospital needs.
<br>
<br>
Must understand that the duties may include exposure to blood or other potential infectious materials.

<b>Essential Job Functions:</b><br>
Attends work on a regular basis and may be required to work a specific shift schedule or, at times, even a rotating schedule, extended shift and/or overtime in accordance with agency leave policy and performs other duties as assigned.
<br>
<br>
1. Attends work on a regular and predictable schedule in accordance with agency leave policy and performs other duties as assigned.
<br>
2. Assigns and affixes property numbers to all property purchased and designated by policy as a capital/fixed asset for the purpose of control and accountability of these assets.
<br>
3. Inputs all pertinent information into the Asset Management section of the HHSAS Financial System in order to maintain current and dependable financial records of all Rusk State Hospital’s capital/fixed assets.
<br>
4. Conducts spot checks of location accounts as outlined in the Property Policies and Procedures Manual to ensure accurate reporting and adequate control of RSH's capital/fixed assets.
<br>
5. Stores in a secure location, organizes, and maintains appropriate documentation on surplus/obsolete capital/fixed assets for ease of inventory control and for preparation of area for surplus sale.
<br>
6. Prepares worksheets for and coordinates with other departments the annual inventory of capital/fixed assets to comply with HHSC policy for the control and accountability of assets.
<br>
7. Creates and maintains assigned spreadsheets and performs assigned reconciliations in order to check the accuracy and reliability of financial data, promote operational efficiency and encourage adherence to policy and procedures.
<br>
8. Attends and participates in all mandatory training to meet HHSC/RSH requirements, maintain job competence, and develop professionally.
<br>
9. Moving of state property, heavy lifting and transporting of state inventory.
<br>
<br>
Other duties as assigned include but are not limited to actively participating and/or serving in a supporting role to meet the agency’s obligations for disaster response and/or recovery or Continuity of Operations (COOP) activation. Such participation may require an alternate shift pattern assignment and/or location.<br>
