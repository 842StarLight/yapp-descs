<h2>1st Shift Quality Supervisor</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p><b>Summary/Objective: </b>The Quality Supervisor is primarily responsible for supervising and coordinating activities of personnel engaged in performing physical tests required for quality control of processes and products by performing the following duties:</p>
<p><b>Essential Functions: </b>The list of duties and responsibilities are not all inclusive and may be expanded to include other duties and responsibilities as management may deem necessary from time to time.</p>
<p>· Direct supervision and training of the Quality Technicians (3).</p>
<p>· Responsibility for all PPAP paperwork (including Capability Summary Sheet).</p>
<p>· Participates in the Contract Review Committee and the Material Review Board when the Quality Manager cannot attend.</p>
<p>· Responsible for the purchase, repair and calibration of all gauging within the facility.</p>
<p>· Filing and record keeping for the QA laboratory.</p>
<p>· Establishes or adjusts work procedures to meet testing schedules.</p>
<p>· The ultimate goal of this position is to provide and direct the support to manufacturing, sales, purchasing and scheduling through communication of quality standards and problem solving efforts.</p>
<p><b>Requirements: </b></p>
<p>· Experience with office computer software required, including demonstrated experience with Microsoft Office Suite software packages such as: Outlook, Word, and Excel</p>
<p>· Effective analytical skills</p>
<p>· Effective verbal and written communication</p>
<p>· Effective interpersonal and customer service skills</p>
<p>· Effective organizational and prioritization skills</p>
<p>· Ability to be detail-oriented</p>
<p>· Ability to operate a variety of standard business machines such as: computer, keyboard, calculator, fax, and photocopier</p>
<p>· Ability to apply basic mathematical concepts such as fractions, ratios, percentages, and proportions to practical situations</p>
<p>· Understand statistical concepts such as means, ranges, X-bar and R charts, upper and lower specs limits, upper and lower control limits, Cp Cpk Pp Ppk, normal and non-normal distributions, etc</p>
<p>· Ability to use quality measurement tools, including micrometers, Vernier measurement scales, depth gauges, point micrometers, calipers, pi tapes, snap gauges, feeler gauges, dial indicators, T-gauges, bore gauges, and protractors.</p>
<p>· Ability to read, analyze, and interpret general business periodicals, professional journals, technical procedures, or governmental regulations. Ability to write reports, business correspondence, and procedure manuals. Ability to effectively present information and respond to questions from groups of managers, clients, customers, and general public. Ability to train and evaluate employees.</p>
<p>· Ability to define problems, collect data, establish facts, and draw valid conclusions. Ability to interpret an extensive variety of technical instructions in mathematical or diagram form and deal with several abstract and concrete variables.</p>
<p><b>Work Environment: </b></p>
<p>· While performing the duties of this job, the employee is frequently exposed to moving mechanical parts. The employee is occasionally exposed to wet and/or humid conditions, fumes or airborne particles, outside weather conditions and vibration. The noise level in the work environment is usually moderate</p>
<p><b>Physical Demands: </b></p>
<p>· While performing the duties of this job, the employee is regularly required to stand, walk, handle steel samples and reach with hands and arms. The employee frequently is required to talk or hear. The employee is occasionally required to sit. The employee must occasionally lift and/or move up to 10 pounds. Specific vision abilities required by this job include close vision, distance vision, color vision, peripheral vision, depth perception and ability to adjust focus. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions of the job.</p>
<p><b>Required Education/Experience: </b></p>
<p>· Six months to a year related experience and/or training; or equivalent combination of education and experience</p>
