<h2>Business Development Representative</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p><b>Job Description</b><br>Southland Organics is looking for a motivated and talented Business Development Representative to join our growing team. The Business Development Representative is tasked with identifying and generating sales opportunities through both inbound and outbound calling activities. This position requires heavy outbound phone calling, qualification, consultative selling skills, and tight collaboration with the Account Executives to generate new opportunities. Core responsibilities also include prospecting, relationship development, and message delivery to set meeting appointments. The Business Development Representative will be expected to report on qualification and call activity and stay thoroughly informed on all products and the industry in general. The right candidate will be one who works well under pressure, thinks outside the box, easily initiates relaxed but informative two-way phone conversations with prospects, and is highly self-motivated. They will also understand how to assess a company’s needs and cater the outreach to each prospect specifically. The ideal candidate has a history of bringing in new prospects, hitting quotas, and loves the challenges that come with outbound prospecting in a competitive market.<br><b>Duties Include: </b></p>

At Southland Organics we help farmers and landscape professionals find natural solutions to natural problems. Each and every product is designed for the betterment of the environment, our lives and the lives of future generations. Southland Organics manufactures organic products in the following sectors: Animal Health, Plants and Crops, Lawn and Turf, and Bioremediation. We use microbiology to fight the invisible elements of bad microbes and dead soil. We use science to validate and improve the effectiveness of our products and lead by our core tenants; protect the environment, protecting future generations, creating healthy soil, flavor, nature over science, and putting the “culture” in agriculture.</p>
