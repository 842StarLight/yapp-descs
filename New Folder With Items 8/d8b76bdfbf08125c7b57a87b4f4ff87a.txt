<h2>Senior Escrow Officer</h2>

<div><b>About the Organization</b></div>
<div></div>
<div>Northwest Title has been serving the real estate title industry for more than 50 years! It has grown from a single office in Columbus in 1967 to 12 staffed locations today throughout Ohio and Northern Kentucky. Northwest Title’s professionalism and commitment to constant improvement have made it one of the most well-established regional title agencies. In addition, its owners also practice law, and through the Holfinger Stevenson Law Firm they provide legal services to help customers solve their toughest real estate related problems.</div>
<div></div>
<div><b>Description</b></div>
<div></div>
<div>The Senior Escrow Officer is responsible for the Escrow team that processes and handles of all files from the beginning to the closing. This position understands all parts of the file and is the first line of communication with our members when orders are received. The Senior Escrow Officer keeps in communication with the client during the closing process while the Escrow Team processes the file. This person is responsible for a file closed and disbursed under a deadline accurately, all the while providing excellent, professional service to our members.</div>
<div></div>
<div><b>Responsibilities</b></div>
<ul>
<li>First and primary point of contact with clients. Responsible for successful resolution of all files, ensuring an excellent client experience regardless of the outcome.</li>
<li>Responsible for understanding all parts of the escrow process to ensure that orders are opened with the end result in mind.</li>
<li>Coordinate communication with the realtors, lender, attorneys, buyers, sellers, members, etc., to prepare for settlement.</li>
<li>Ensure that all Schedule B-1 items on a title commitment are resolved before closing. Clearing title defects (obtaining documentary proof to resolve a defect, or obtain new signatures on documents needed to obtain clear and marketable title), and ordering mortgage/lien payoffs.</li>
<li>Prepare a Closing Disclosure (CD) and/or Settlement Statement that is compliant with a purchase contract, a lender, and federal/state requirements.</li>
<li>Responsible for the performance of a team, including financial performance, efficiency, and a smooth process for each transaction.</li>
<li>Meet with the Branch Manager regularly to discuss the performance of the Escrow Team.</li>
<li>Perform closing functions in accordance with company standards.</li>
<li>Create business relationships that maintain and increase the profitability of the office. Work with the Branch Manager and sales staff to identify potential clients.</li>
</ul>
<div><b>Position Requirements:</b></div>
<ul>
<li>At least 5 years’ experience as an Escrow Officer/Processor handling the responsibilities of this type of role. Preferred skills include the ability to conduct a closing and process short sale transactions.</li>
<li>Excellent organizational skills</li>
<li>Experience leading a team</li>
<li>Proactive Problem-solver</li>
<li>Ability to multi-task and work quickly but accurately</li>
<li>Strong, positive, and quality-focused customer service skills</li>
<li>Strong communication skills via email, phone, and in-person, including proper grammar and spelling</li>
<li>Knowledge of Microsoft Office Applications (Word, Excel, Outlook)</li>
<li>Experience with Ramquest title production software is a plus</li>
</ul>
