<h2>Program Director</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p><b>Program Director</b></p>
<p><b>Pittsburgh Glass Center</b><br>Full time year-round exempt status</p>
<p><b>Deadline to Apply: </b>Thursday, October 31st 2019</p>
<p><b>About Pittsburgh Glass Center</b><br>Pittsburgh Glass Center is a nonprofit, public access school, gallery and state-of-the-art glass studio dedicated to teaching, creating and promoting glass art. World-renowned artists come here to make studio glass art. People interested in learning more about glass come here to take a class, explore the contemporary gallery and watch live hot glass demonstrations.</p>
<p>As one of the top glass art centers in the world, we pride ourselves on providing exceptional resources and instruction to expand the skills and knowledge of our students and artists. We strive to foster a new generation of glass artists and enthusiasts here in the Pittsburgh region.</p>
<p>Pittsburgh Glass Center is an important arts organization in Pittsburgh that is helping the city connect its history as a major producer in glass to its creative future through the innovative use of glass as art.</p>
<p><b>Mission </b></p>
<p>Glass Art: We teach it. We create it. We promote it. We support those who make it.</p>
<p><b>Vision</b><br>PGC fuels a thriving glass art ecosystem of people, facilities and innovative programming that together shape our region’s creative economy. From the casually curious to the master artist, PGC welcomes and nurtures everyone so they are <b>amazed</b>, <b>inspired</b> and <b>transformed</b>­.</p>
<p><b>Guiding Principles</b></p>
<p>Our guiding principles of creativity, collaboration, sustainability, diversity, and excellence, enable us to extend our region's proud glass legacy far into the future.</p>
<p><b>About the position</b><br>The newly created position of Program Director (PD) will be responsible for overseeing all areas of artistic programing at PGC. Programing at PGC can be broken into three major categories - education, exhibitions, and artist residencies. PD will participate in strategic planning for PGC and oversee implementation of programing related goals.</p>
<p>PD will be responsible for maintaining yearly calendars for all programming activities and will oversee all aspects of implementation. PD will be responsible for overseeing the coordination of all artist residencies and exhibitions including artist selection, contracts, scheduling, installation logistics, and communicating artist and exhibition needs with other departments at PGC. PD will also be in charge of organizing PGC’s annual artist holiday sale.</p>
<p>PD will serve as staff liaison to the programming committee of the board of directors setting the agenda for quarterly meetings. The committee is asked to provide advice and suggestions in accordance with the strategic plan. PD will work with the programming committee, Executive Director, and other staff to plan programs 3-5 years in advance.</p>
<p>PD will work to develop relationships with teachers and local schools. In addition, the PD works with community and art related organizations to develop beneficial partnerships for PGC. The PD executes program plans for classes &amp; in-school residencies including: contacting and scheduling artists, teachers and school programs; processing contracts and paperwork including check requests; preparing printed program materials including all course and exhibition catalogs for distribution; maintaining ongoing communication with teachers, visiting artists and teaching assistants. The program director reports to the executive director.</p>
<p><b>Essential Duties and Responsibilities</b></p>
<p>· Conceptualizes creative and innovative artistic programming and oversees this programing including classes, residencies &amp; exhibitions.</p>
<p>· Works with instructors to develop content, course descriptions, and schedule for winter/spring and fall courses. Collates all information for PGC course catalogs and enter classes online.</p>
<p>· Manages and oversees all artist residencies.</p>
<p>· Programs and oversees all logistics for PGC exhibitions (3-4 per year).</p>
<p>· Creates curriculum for all in-school residencies and works with community and art related organizations to develop partnerships.</p>
<p>· Manages and coordinates the activities of the other programming staff including 3 direct reports, 2 indirect reports, and independent contractors.</p>
<p>· Serves as staff liaison to the board programming committee.</p>
<p><b>Knowledge, Skills and Abilities</b></p>
<p>· Excellent verbal and written communication skills with a flexible and collaborative work style.</p>
<p>· Ability to manage many diverse yet concurrent projects and responsibilities.</p>
<p>· Ability to think strategically.</p>
<p>· Proactive, self-directed and reliable.</p>
<p>· Detail-oriented and an exceptional organizer.</p>
<p>· Adaptable, able to think on their feet, and an active problem-solver.</p>
<p>· Self-aware, with good listening skills and the ability to accept feedback.</p>
<p>· Equally skilled at building coalitions and consensus and taking decisive action when appropriate.</p>
<p>· An appreciation for glass art, fun, creative with sense of humor.</p>
<p><b>Education/Experience Requirements</b><br>Bachelor’s Degree in Arts Administration, Art History/Studio Art, Education or related field required with a preference for Masters in related field. Minimum 5-8 years of experience in education, programming, or exhibitions with a preference for arts related experience. Understanding of and experience with childhood developmental levels and learning theory, especially as related to informal education. Demonstrated knowledge of leading practices in object-based learning.</p>
<p>Track record of successfully managing a team of people in a supervisory capacity. Successful project management experience and proficiency with current software and computer systems in an office environment. Experienced in working with artists within an organizational context, and in recognizing the potential of creative output within an environment that supports inclusive dialogue, original thinking and risk-taking.</p>
