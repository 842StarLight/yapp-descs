<h2>Account Executive, Department of Athletics</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<p><b>Job Description Summary
</b></p>Summary: The Account Executive is responsible for revenue generation through Football/Men’s Basketball tickets sales and portfolio of annual fund prospects. Football and Men’s Basketball ticket sales is inclusive of season tickets, packages, group tickets, and hospitality/suite options. Development responsibilities include acquisition, renewal upgrades, and major gift qualification. Performs prospecting and consultative selling via outbound phone calls from office and on/off site meetings. This position is also responsible for meeting and exceeding individual revenue targets as determined by the Director.
<p></p>
<p><b>Job Description
</b></p>
<p><b>Essential Functions:
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Meets or exceeds sales goals by initiating outbound phone contact with prospective ticket buyers and selling ticketing solutions.
</li>
<li>Maintains a high level of qualified sales activity.
</li>
<li>Attracts new business through referrals and other prospecting activities.
</li>
<li>Qualifies, cultivates, solicits, and stewards assigned donor prospects.
</li>
<li>Maintains up-to-date digital records of leads, prospects, sales activities, and customer account information using Wake Forest Ticket Sales Team computer tools and databases.
</li>
<li>Provides professional customer service and post-sales follow-up.
</li>
<li>Assists with other University projects and events as needed.
</li>
<li>Responsible for working events and home games such as the Winston Salem Open, Wake Forest Men’s Basketball, and Football home games.
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>Required Education, Knowledge, Skills, Abilities:
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>BA/BS with working knowledge of sales and customer service. An equivalent combination of education and experience may be accepted.
</li>
<li>Knowledge and ability to execute a consultative sales process from lead generation to implementation and customer support.
</li>
<li>Unquestionable integrity and a strong sense of professional ethics.
</li>
<li>Mature, professional, and respectful demeanor.
</li>
<li>Evidence of being self-starting, self-motivating, organized, entrepreneurial and creative in formulating and presenting solutions to customers over the phone.
</li>
<li>Proficiency in Microsoft Word, Outlook and Excel and familiarity with CRM software solutions.
</li>
<li>Strong interpersonal, communication, presentation, and listening skills.
</li>
<li>Ability to research and organize information and data accurately and with an attention to detail.
</li>
<li>Ability to prioritize and to execute multiple tasks in a time-sensitive, pressurized environment.
</li>
<li>Ability to work independently and as part of a team.
</li>
<li>Receptive to coaching, following instructions and executing directives.
</li>
<li>Ability to support a rotating work schedule as needed.
</li>
<li>Ability to meet requirements of the University's automobile insurance.
</li>
<li>Light work; exerting up to 20 pounds of force occasionally and/or a negligible amount of force constantly to move objects. Close visual acuity. Subject to a full range of physical activities, noise, and inside/outside environmental conditions. Not substantially exposed to adverse environmental conditions.
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>Preferred Education, Knowledge, Skills, Abilities:
</b></p>
<ul><li>Previous collegiate or professional ticket sales experience.
</li></ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>Accountabilities:
</b></p>
<ul><li>Responsible for own work.
</li></ul>
