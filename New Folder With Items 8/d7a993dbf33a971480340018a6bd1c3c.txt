<p>
Be a Champion for Your Community! Our people make a difference and leave the world a cleaner and more beautiful place. We thrive on developing our employees to take their careers to new heights and explore opportunities perhaps never imagined. We are proud to provide cost-effective and environmentally sound solutions for waste/recycling collection, transportation, processing and disposal. We truly are an environmental services company, and we are looking for our next environmental hero who has the entrepreneurial spirit and dedication to support our company mission: <i>Every day, Driven to Deliver Service First, Safety Always</i></p>
<p><b>
POSITION SUMMARY</b><b>:</b></p>
<p>
Direct the activities of drivers and helpers in order to provide safe, effective and courteous waste collection service to customers. The Route Supervisor is the first line supervisor responsible for the productivity of the collection crews</p>
<p><b>
PRIMARY DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES:</b></p>
<p>
 This list of duties and responsibilities is not all inclusive and may be expanded to include other duties and responsibilities as management may deem necessary from time to time.</p>
<ul>
<li>Hire and supervise drivers and helpers through scheduling their routes, implementing corrective action and managing other related issues</li>
<li>Plan routes and schedule drivers and helpers to provide the most efficient service to customers. Resolve missed pick-ups and/or schedule additional pick-ups</li>
<li>As the field representative, respond effectively and courteously to customer inquiries, special requests and/or complaints. May also interact with city, state or municipal government employees</li>
<li>Perform route audits to ensure safe and efficient procedures are followed and to identify possible training needs. Complete employee I-Cares and discuss results with drivers and helpers</li>
<li>Develop work schedules to match staffing levels, make work assignments and monitor progress to improve work process efficiency. May also plan routes to provide the most efficient service to customers while managing labor hours and disposal tons</li>
<li>Respond to driver reports of problem pick-ups such as unsafe conditions or potential hazardous material</li>
<li>Coordinate with Accounts Receivable, Customer Service and Sales departments to address concerns and/or changes in customer service. Coordinate with the Maintenance department to address concerns and/or changes to vehicle repairs</li>
<li>Responsible for the timely communication, correction and follow up of customer service related issues</li>
<li>Approve time sheets and completed route sheets for pay calculations. Maintain and review logs for DOT hours worked and other required records</li>
<li>Maintain adherence to Company policy in regards to operations and safety issues</li>
<li>May assist in investigation of accidents, injuries or property damage claims</li>
<li>May be required to drive route vehicles in order to cover shortages in staff or missed pick-ups</li>
</ul>
<p><b>KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Ability to communicate effectively with all levels of personnel, customers and vendors</li>
<li>Good written and verbal communication skills</li>
<li>Knowledge of DOT, OSHA and other regulations related to commercial driving and waste collection</li>
</ul>
<p><b>MINIMUM REQUIREMENTS:</b></p>
<p><b>
Education: </b>High School Diploma, GED and/or equivalent work experience</p>
<p><b>
Years of Experience:</b> Minimum two years of related Waste, distribution or transportation industry experience desired but not essential. Experience as a commercial driver, customer service representative or dispatch clerk is preferred. An equivalent combination of education, experience and certification may be considered.Minimum two years distribution or transportation experience</p>
<p><b>
Certifications: </b>Valid Class A or B CDL with air brakes endorsement. OSHA training certificate is a plus. Must meet all regulatory requirements to qualify to operate equipment supervised</p>
<p><b>
MACHINES, TOOLS, EQUIPMENT AND SOFTWARE USED:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Usual office equipment including calculator, copier, telephone, fax machine and computer</li>
<li>Microsoft Office applications such as Word, Excel and Outlook</li>
<li>Company systems applications including Trux</li>
<li>All types of waste collection trucks</li>
</ul>
<p><b>WORK ENVIRONMENT:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Occasional exposure to extreme weather conditions</li>
<li>May be exposed to chemicals, solvents and other refuse in and around trucks</li>
<li>Other risks associated with working around machines, tools, and trucks</li>
</ul>
<p><b>TYPICAL PHYSICAL DEMANDS:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Ability to safely operate all types of trucks</li>
<li>Ability to organize various office systems through adjusting, connecting, lifting, pulling, pushing, bending or folding for the purpose of proper records development and/or management</li>
<li>Ability to use a computer and related devices, such as keyboard or mouse or other related equipment, throughout the workday while seated at a desk or workstation</li>
<br>
</ul>
