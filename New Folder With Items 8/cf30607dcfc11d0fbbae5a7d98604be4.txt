<h2>Senior Contracts Administrator</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<div>Sr Contracts Admin</div>
<div></div>
<div><i>Sparton is a leading contract design and manufacturing company with a specialization in electronic and electro-mechanical assemblies. Our customers include, Military, Medical, Biotechnology and Aerospace companies that are highly regulated with specialized needs.</i></div>
<div></div>
<div>
<b>Basic Purpose of Position</b>: The Contracts Administrator will work in support of the Contracts Manager to administer all contracts for the business unit.</div>
<div></div>
<div><b>Primary Or Major Responsibilities:</b></div>
<ul>
<li>Responsible for managing primarily DoD contracts (e.g. FFP, CPFF/IF), negotiating contract terms and conditions, and collaboration with internal departments.</li>
<li>Assigned contracts may include the administration of large, long-term commercial and/or Engineering Service in support customer requirements contracts including but not limited to cell phone, pager, copier, communication and technology contracts.</li>
<li>Managing the contract database and all associated files as well as contract compliance to maximize efficiency, cost savings and reduce liability exposure.</li>
<li>Provide documentation and interpretation of contractual rights and obligations internally and externally, ensure that due diligence is practiced in complying with stated terms and conditions.</li>
<li>Review modifications, extensions and contract closeout documents.</li>
<li>Monitor performance and make recommendations for improvement when appropriate.</li>
<li>Prepare and distribute reports and statistical data to facilitate planning and maintenance of accurate contract records and files.</li>
<li>Coordinate with all levels of management to gather, analyze, summarize, and prepare recommendations regarding strategies, practices and procedures.</li>
<li>Participate in and provide support for assigned Corporate initiatives.</li>
<li>All other duties as assigned.</li>
</ul>
<div></div>
<div>
<b>Safety</b>:Ensure that all assignments are performed in compliance with all applicable Federal, State, Provincial and Company safety and health regulations, and where applicable, proper personal protective equipment is utilized.</div>
<div></div>
<div>
<b>Minimum Requirements</b>:</div>
<ul>
<li>Eight to ten (8 to 10) years of progressive preferred experience in government contracts, sales support and/or contracts administration or equivalent combination of education and experience may be substituted.</li>
<li>Preferred education level is a Bachelors Degree in contract administration, public administration, business management or a closely related field.</li>
<li>Proficient in contract development, contract standards and language.</li>
<li>Certified Federal Contracts Management (CFCM), Certified Professional Contracts Manager (CPCM) designations preferred, but not required.</li>
<li>Effective communication skills both written and oral with both internal and external contacts.</li>
<li>Computer literate in Microsoft Office Suite of programs such as Word, Excel, Access and Power Point, HTE, Adobe Acrobat Professional, procurement card software, and other electronic document management systems.</li>
<li>Must possess strong leadership and interpersonal skills, be very details orientated, and have strong organizational and time management skills.</li>
<li>For the Government Business Unit, must be a U.S. Citizen and qualify for a secret clearance.</li>
</ul>
</div></div>
