<p><b>TITLE:</b> AUDIOLOGIST</p>
<p><b>

<p><b>
RESPONSIBLE TO: </b>PRACTICE OWNER/PRACTICE MANAGER</p>
<p><b>
POSITION:</b></p>
<p>
The Audiologist is responsible for administering diagnostic audiological tests that allow the differentiation between types of hearing disorders. The Audiologist will provide hearing diagnostic evaluations, selection and fitting of hearing aids to all populations. Also, provides comprehensive diagnostic evaluations including ABR, VNG, ECOG, diagnostic hearing evaluations, tympanometry and acoustic reflex threshold (ART) testing, and tinnitus evaluations . The Audiologist counsels patients regarding test results and reviews options which best meet their hearing care needs. Performs hearing aid testing, selection, and fitting to all populations. Reviews pricing and sale of hearing aids as a part of the patient education process.</p>
<p><b>
JOB FUNCTIONS:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Administers Diagnostic Tests
</li>
<li>Performs Auditory Brainstem Response (ABR), Videonystagmography (VNG), Electrocochleography (ECoG), Tympanometry, Audio logical Evaluations, ETD testing and acoustic reflex threshold and tone decay testing.</li>
<li>Provides amplification services including, hearing aid assessment testing, real ear measurements and live speech mapping.
</li>
<li>Coordinates and interprets test results.</li>
<li>Consults with and makes recommendation on hearing disorders to physicians.</li>
<li>Based on test results, diagnosis audio vestibular disorders.</li>
<li>Coordinates care with physicians and other providers.</li>
<li>Completes chart notes for each patient seen and sends written report to referring physician and primary care physician</li>
</ul>

Manages Hearing Rehabilitation</b></p>
