<h2>Fleet Services Manager (AM)</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<p></p>
<div><div><div><div><div><div>
<div><div>
<b>The City of Santa Ana is looking for individuals who are results-oriented, possess great attitudes, demonstrate creativity and innovation, work efficiently, show a record of success and have a PASSION for public service. Having the best employees provides the best service to the community.</b><br>
<br>
Under general direction, plans, manages, organizes and directs the Fleet Maintenance Section.
</div></div>
<br>
<br>
<div></div>
<div><div>
<p><b>ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS INCLUDE BUT ARE NOT LIMITED TO:</b></p>
</div></div>
<br>
<div><div>
<div>Administers and directs the Fleet Services Division in the maintenance and purchase of City vehicles. Develops vehicle replacement cycles and calculates associated costs. Develops and recommends service fees, rental rates and overhead charges to recover program costs, yet maintain competitive with private industry. Manages Fleet staff to maintain shop effectiveness, meet customer requirements and quality and safety standards, as well as reduce repeat repair work orders. Utilizes fleet management software to measure workload efficiency and training needs, vehicle maintenance and warranty, inventory control and carrying costs, and driver operational negligence. Manages the various fuel dispensing systems; including purchasing, sales, billing, maintenance, and environmental compliance. Develops, administers and monitors Fleet Services section budget. Manages purchase and maintenance agreements. Researches, identifies and pursues federal, state and private grants and tax incentives pertaining to purchases and programs. Establishes goals and objectives, incorporating Customer Service-oriented principles into divisional procedures and practices. Prepares Council agenda items, reports and memoranda. Assists unit supervisors with problems the supervisors cannot resolve. Insures that all paperwork is completed accurately on new and replacement equipment and registration of new vehicles. Performs other related functions as assigned.<br>
</div>
</div></div>
<br>
<br>
<div></div>
<div><div><p><b>MINIMUM QUALIFICATIONS:</b>
</p></div></div>
<br>
<div><div>
<div>Seven years of full-time paid journey level experience in the repair and maintenance of light and heavy automotive equipment, two of which must have been in a supervisory capacity. Graduation from an accredited college, university, or an industrial education program with specialization in automotive technology or related field may be used in combination with experience and training which provides the following knowledge, skills, and abilities:</div>
<br>
Desirable Knowledge, Skills and Abilities:<br>
<div>
Knowledge of: the principles and practices of supervision and administration as applied to the operation of a fleet-management program, including management of automotive repair activities, administration of fleet acquisition/replacement program, and preparation of specifications for the purchase of equipment; budget preparation and control; occupational safety hazards and precautions applicable to equipment operations, maintenance and repair.</div>
<div>
Ability to: Analyze and recommend budgetary needs; evaluate vehicle operations; evaluate fuel needs: develop standards, policies and procedures; plan and supervise the work of auto-equipment services supervisors; prepare and present reports.</div>
<br>
<b>SPECIAL REQUIREMENT</b><br>
Must possess and keep current a valid California Class C Driver's License, as a condition of employment.<br>
<br>
Possession of a valid California Class A Driver's License is highly desirable.
</div></div>
