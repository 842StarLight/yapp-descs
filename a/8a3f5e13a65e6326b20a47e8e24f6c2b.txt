<h2>Analog and Digital Electronics Specialist</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">In the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE) serves as the Analog and Digital Electronics Specialist. Installs and services electronic equipment in the department's teaching and research laboratories and the Electronics Shop. Supervises the laboratory assistant in the front desk operations of the ECE Shop. Responsible for the development and maintenance of approximately nineteen Shop databases. System administrator for the ECE Department's and College of Engineering Dean's Office brass and electronic card keys. Responsible for the Shop's sales, BARC billing, and reconciliation. Responsible for equipment inventory control for the department. Uses electronic test equipment, hand and power tools, and soldering tools.
<br><br>
Minimum Requirements
<br><br>
Experience with electronics and computer systems with an aptitude to learn more advanced circuit design and implementation for the teaching labs, including the use of electronic test equipment. Proficient with relational databases and drawing programs, such as FileMaker and CAD. Must have excellent communication skills both verbal and written. The ability to work well with others in a very diverse workplace, with a range of students, faculty and staff. Must be able to problem solve and find solutions for all Electronic Shop operations in need of updating or change. Must be able to work independently and apply critical thinking in the decision making process.
<br><br>
Desirable Requirements
<br><br>
Bachelor of Science or equivalen combination of education and work experience preferred.

Electronics Shop Administration
<br><br>
Supervises the laboratory assistant in front desk operations of the ECE Shop. This involves overseeing the activities associated with scheduling, shipping and receiving, key distribution, and dispensing of electronic components to students.
<br><br>
Independently responsible for the development and maintenance of approximately nineteen ECE shop databases for lockers, sales, component stock, grant recharging, card reader systems, keys, purchases, personnel (faculty, staff, students, and researchers), loans, packages, inventory, homework boxes, and others.
<br><br>
Responsible for door signage, the design and development of floorplans and layouts for ECE offices and labs as it relates to moves and modifications.
<br><br>
Responsible for submitting monthly BARC transactions for student purchases, grant recharges, and other financial charges to the Budget Office.
<br><br>
Assists in seismic securing of furniture and moving and modifying furniture as needed.
<br><br>
Serves as back up to the Building Manager and the Department Safety Representative. Assigned special projects as needed.

Laboratory Maintenance
<br><br>
Oversees the ECE Department's analog and digital CE and EE labs. Serves as the electronics specialist, and in coordination with Information Technology staff, maintains Shop and instructional equipment and computers.
<br><br>
Responsible for working with instructors in preparation for analog and digital laboratory classes. This involves constructing projects, and sourcing electronic parts as needed for instruction. Installs and services electronic equipment in the department's laboratories and Electronics Shop.
<br><br>
Works with graduate students and researchers with projects dealing with digital and computer circuits. Advises on technical problems to help staff and students find solutions.
<br><br>
Maintains an inventory of supplies and performs general maintenance in teaching laboratories to keep an orderly, safe and secure environment.

Safety
<br><br>
Adheres to current safety regulations and precautions as prescribed by the Department of Labor, Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA), Uniform Building Code and EPA, including storage and disposal procedures, common shop safety practices, guidelines or policies per other applicable Federal, State, and local agencies.
<br><br>
Understands and adheres to the safety practices on the use of all hand and power tools associated with this position and electrical and other building and maintenance trades.
<br><br>
Must be able to instruct students and researchers in the proper usage of such hand tools: Hammers, saws, drills, soldering irons, wrenches, voltage testers, analyzers, and discrete component testers.
<br><br>
Responsible for the safe and secure operation of the Shop and teaching labs when dealing with the distribution of brass and card key access. This includes personnel and training verification before access to labs is granted.
<br><br>
Operates lifting devices such as a forklift, pallet jacks, and desk movers.</div>
