<p></p>
<p><b>Process Engineering Intern/Co-Op</b></p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>This posting is for multiple positions within The Hershey Company’s various engineering groups located in Hershey, PA. The goal of these engineering internship/co-op positions are to provide opportunities for students to gain real world engineering experience in the Consumer Packaged Goods and Food processing industry and to help students develop professionally in a progressive manner with an increasing amount of responsibility. The differences between these groups are listed below in this posting. All positions will give you an excellent opportunity to gain real world experience while working under the supervision of experienced Process Engineers to meet the Company goals pertaining to process design, product commercialization, or continuous improvement. These positions are ideally suited for students who are interested in and capable of working multiple rotations starting in August for the Fall 2020 semester.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p><b>Responsibilities:</b></p>
<p>Typical Student assignments &amp; responsibilities may include:</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li>Designing and conducting pilot and production scale processes and tests, documenting and evaluating data, and writing technical reports</li></ul>
<ul><li>Identifying process equipment through testing, establishing equipment operating parameters, and providing process flows diagrams</li></ul>
<ul><li>Specifying, purchasing, installing, and commissioning new process equipment.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Preparing proper documentation of project work, including PFD's, P&amp;ID's, engineering calculations, process design documentation, and process test reports</li></ul>
<ul><li>Performing material balance, fluid flow and heat transfer calculations</li></ul>
<ul><li>Working with multidisciplinary teams including personnel from manufacturing, product development, analytical research, quality assurance, industrial engineering, electrical engineering, and packaging engineering, equipment manufacturers, and installation contractors</li></ul>
<ul><li>Designing, troubleshooting and optimizing manufacturing processes and products</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Product Commercialization Engineering position:</p>
<p>The Product Commercialization engineering group is responsible for providing engineering support for the development of new confectionery and snack products and processes to deliver successful manufacturing solutions for the growth and innovation efforts at The Hershey Company.</p>
<p>This includes:</p>
<ul>
<li>Determining manufacturing feasibility by testing and evaluating products and processes in the Hershey pilot plant or at vendor facilities.</li>
<li>Developing process flow diagrams and identifying and documenting key processing parameters for each unit operation.</li>
<li>Supporting product sample production for consumer testing and sales samples.</li>
<li>Providing production startup support and training.</li>
<br>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>Education</b></p>
<p>Must be an undergraduate student majoring in Chemical, Mechanical, Agricultural, Biological, Food, Electrical or Automation Engineering, or similar program from an ABET accredited engineering program at the sophomore level or higher.</p>
