<h2>Director, Product Management</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>Comcast Spotlight, the advertising sales division of Comcast Cable, helps put the power of cable to use for local, regional and national advertisers. It is focused on providing multi-platform marketing solutions to reach audiences most effectively and efficiently. Headquartered in New York with offices throughout the country, Comcast Spotlight has a presence in nearly 80 markets with approximately 35 million owned and represented subscribers.<br>
</p>
<p></p>
<p>Comcast Spotlight offers clients easy-to-buy, easy-to-execute options customized around their business goals. We work with clients to provide them with customized, multi-screen media marketing solutions that utilize our suite of products in the way that's best suited to meet their objectives. Our media solutions provide advertisers with the ability to reach, engage and connect with their customers viewing content on any device or screen.<br>
</p>
<br>
<br>
<p></p>
<div>
<b>Job Summary:</b><br>
This position is the Demand Architect, who designs the technology stack vision to support the advertising sales strategy. This role creates the vision, technical epic creation and ensures executive alignment, for the Unified Planning and Self Service applications. This leader provides directives to product managers and engineering to ensure scope is delivered. This leader evaluates external vendor options and manages the feature scope definitions for vendor contracts.<br>
<br>
<b>Core Responsibilities:</b><br>
<ul>
<li>Partner with teams throughout the business unit (Internal/external Engineering, PMO, Training, Sales, Product Strategy, Product Marketing and Operations) to manage product feature design plus support go to market process as the architecture lead.</li>
<li>Responsible for designing, documenting and tracking the self service product roadmap milestones within the product management system. Create and maintain diagrams tracking integrations across the infrastructure.</li>
<li>Architecture lead in SAFe (Scaled Agile Framework) product planning sessions with Business analysts and dev teams</li>
<li>Report regular updates to teams and executives.</li>
<li>Provide guidance to training teams on processes within the tech platform</li>
</ul>
</div>
<p>
 Execution: Roll up your sleeves and work with the troops and get things done</p>
<p>
 Teamwork: Align internally and externally. Be influential.</p>
<p>
 Communication: Synthesize complex topics into concise, actionable points</p>
<p>
 Perseverance: Anything is possible when you’re committed. Hold it all together.</p>
<p>
 Solve problems: Listen, empathize and be resourceful about overcoming objections</p>
<ul>
<li>Directs, establishes, maintains, and plans the overall policies and goals for a product management department.</li>
<li>Sets new product strategy for program or product group.</li>
<li>Applies expertise in state-of-the-art product technologies and is relied on by top management to serve as a proxy for the market.</li>
<li>Defines, develops, and implements standard operational frameworks for products.</li>
<li>Coordinates with sales and marketing to assess client and industry needs and identifies possible solutions to meet those needs.</li>
<li>Accountable for profit and loss or budgetary management of assigned products.</li>
<li>Develops business cases and supporting documentation.</li>
<li>Oversees development of assigned new products and services and ensures customer’s needs are met. Provides product support and documentation and develops of product materials.</li>
<li>Manages lifecycle of new products and services.</li>
<li>Demonstrates excellent project management skills, with a proven track record of successful delivery of projects on time, in line with budget expectations and to strategy.</li>
<li>Consistent exercise of independent judgment and discretion in matters of significance.</li>
<li>Regular, consistent and punctual attendance. Must be able to work nights and weekends, variable schedule(s) as necessary.</li>
