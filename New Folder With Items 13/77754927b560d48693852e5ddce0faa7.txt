<h2>Brand Manager - Innovation</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p><b>About Role</b></p>
<p>The Brand Manager of Innovation will be responsible for collaborating with our executive management team to manage and evolve current brand’s packaging as well as take new products and packaging to the finish line. This role will be in close communication with both the internal teams as well as the client through the project, keeping everyone updated on all milestones throughout. This role requires strong organizational, communication, and creative skills with a keen attention to detail and excellent time management. In this role, you will need to be a self-starter who excels in a fast pace, dynamic, and at-times ambiguous start-up atmosphere, managing complex projects, and fostering collaboration with and amongst internal teammates, and generally being a team player. This is a full-time, salaried position reporting to the Director of Marketing.</p>
<p><b>Responsibilities</b></p>
<p>· Scope and plan product innovation projects: Set goals and objectives, create project plan including timeline, determine resources and budgets needed, identify risks and bottlenecks, develop contingency plans</p>
<p>· Work closely with operations team to ensure their ordering, productions, logistics and fulfillment strategies and timelines are aligned with product development timelines and strategies</p>
<p>· Develop a deep understanding of the Copperstate Farms brand as well as current and future brands to developed under its umbrella</p>
<p>· Work closely with brand and marketing teams to ensure their vision and strategy is being brought to life in product</p>
<p>· Perform continuous trend and market research analysis in packaging and cannabis product categories to apply to future brand development</p>
<p>· Work closely with customer service team to implement customer feedback directly into product improvement and new product development</p>
<p>· New vendor identification and vetting as it relates to product development</p>
<p>· Develop and execute gift/holiday/seasonal kit product and packaging</p>
<p>· Maintain brand standards, guidelines, and tool kits</p>
<p><b>Qualifications</b></p>
<p>· 5-7 years of experience as project manager with at least 5 years in a consumer-packaged goods industry or experience in an integrated agency context</p>
<p>· Project management experience leading projects in innovation and product optimization initiatives in CPG</p>
<p>· Strong communication skills, including but not limited to exceptional writing and excellent grammar</p>
<p>· Very detail oriented to ensure the projects stay on task and schedule</p>
<p>· Prior design agency or studio experience</p>
<p>· Solid understanding of Adobe Creative Suite as well as file specifications; resolution, color space and other technical requirements of assets needed for print packaging and non-packaging</p>
<p>· Familiarity with standard software project management methodologies, workflow and tools</p>
<p>· Self-driven and able to problem solve in complex situations</p>
<p>· Willingness to work, learn, and appreciate the complexities of the cannabis industry</p>
<p>· Flexibility to be available during non-traditional hours and travel to the greenhouse in Snowflake on a minimum monthly basis.</p>
<p>· Must be a minimum of 21 years of age and able to obtain an Arizona Dispensary Agent</p>
<p>· Bachelor’s degree</p>
<p><b>Desirable </b><b>Skills </b></p>
<p>· A solid understanding of the Medical marijuana laws and industry.</p>
<p>· Prior experience in a CPG project management role for the cannabis industry.</p>
<p>· EPA regulations knowledge</p>
<p>· PMP certification</p>
<p><b>About Copperstate Farms</b></p>
<p>Copperstate Farms, LLC was founded in 2016 and is a licensed producer and distributor of medical cannabis in the United States. The company operates a 1.7-million-square-foot facility and 40-acre greenhouse grow in Snowflake, Arizona. Copperstate Farms is dedicated to bringing growth to the local and state economy through the hiring of local laborers, material suppliers, and contractors. For more information, visit CopperstateFarms.com.</p>
