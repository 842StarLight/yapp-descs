<h2>Event Operations Supervisor</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<p>General Summary of Position: Reporting to the Director of Event Operations, the Event Operations Supervisor will assist with supervision and coordination of events held at Aquarium, Entergy® Giant Screen Theater, and Woldenberg</p>
<p>Riverfront Park properties. This position acts as a liaison between event client, vendors, and internal Audubon Nature Institute departments.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>To deliver on the Audubon mission, the Event Operations Supervisor will:</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Provide an internal guest experience of outstanding quality.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li>Responsible for generating new festivals, inclusion events, community relations events and theater events and building clientele year over year</li></ul>
<ul><li>Schedule, plan, execute and follow through on external meetings, on and off-site, including taking, transcribing and distributing minutes for events</li></ul>
<ul><li>Manage and respond to incoming inquiries</li></ul>
<ul><li>Establish and maintain a client database</li></ul>
<ul><li>Intake and process Pavilion inquiries</li></ul>
<ul><li>Plan, coordinate and execute group and wedding Pavilion events</li></ul>
<ul><li>Update departmental event calendar and send updates internally as events are booked</li></ul>
<ul><li>Collect and verify event insurance, permits and documents as they are due to Audubon</li></ul>
<ul><li>Plan and conduct Site visits with potential clients.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Assist in the development as needed for the departmental annual budgets.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Collect and compile competitive data regarding services and rentals.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Act as a liaison between event client, vendors, and internal Audubon Nature Institute departments; ensure customer satisfaction through attention to detail, excellent service skills and the ability to lead and take initiative.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Attend all In-House meetings regarding Event Operations and Production</li></ul>
<ul><li>Coordinate with festival security teams to ensure safety procedures are being followed and communicate Audubon security plans with clients</li></ul>
<ul><li>Continuously troubleshoot and address issues during events.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Ensure that all Event documents, data and lists are accurate in Delphi and Basecamp</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Operate a financially self-sufficient collection of museums and parks.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li>Continuously cultivate revenue opportunities with rental services, equipment services and cost savings initiatives</li></ul>
<ul><li>Streamline and asses rental fee structures for all components to include, but not limited to rental fees, electrical fees and labor fees</li></ul>
<ul><li>Schedule and follow up on client leads</li></ul>
<ul><li>Prepare client estimates and send to the Director of Event Operations for review. Once approved, send to client</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li>Assist in the review and follow up of the department monthly budget reports</li></ul>
<ul><li>Track and code departmental expenditures through the year</li></ul>
<ul><li>Event invoicing, collection of deposits/payments and recording deposits/payments</li></ul>
<ul><li>Logging, follow up and reporting of past due payments to the Director of Event Operations</li></ul>
<ul><li>Monthly updates of departmental gross and net revenue</li></ul>
<ul><li>Assist Director of Event Operations in budget review processes</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Weave quality entertainment through the guest experience.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li>Manage logistics for load-in and load-out of festivals and events.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Establish event concepts, plan and produce internal events focusing on Audubon’s mission.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Coordinate and relay operational needs</li></ul>
<ul><li>Oversee Event Operations web updates</li></ul>
<ul><li>Planning and disseminating of complete and accurate event details to internal departments through event orders</li></ul>
<ul><li>Represents the interest of the Director of Event Operations in their absence</li></ul>
<ul><li>Ensure that staffing requests and parking requests are submitted in a timely manner for all festivals and events</li></ul>
<ul><li>Assist with safety records and natural disaster planning</li></ul>
<ul><li>Input Confirmations and Lost event data in Delphi, updating per the event</li></ul>
<ul><li>Ensure that all rooms are booked within Delphi for each event</li></ul>
<ul><li>Update Event Operations calendar, Special Event calendar and Basecamp as bookings are confirmed</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Educate our diverse audience about the natural world.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li>Establish post inquiry client contact for event production once bookings have been processed.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Maintain awareness of current trends in festival productions and management.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Conduct site visits, discuss mapping options with clients and generate layouts.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Review and follow-up sales leads.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Receive incoming calls, correspondence and inquiries and respond directly or route to the appropriate parties</li></ul>
<ul><li>Oversee event logistics, execution, and conclusion as assigned</li></ul>
<ul><li>Assist with coordinating and relaying needs between festival, vendors and ANI departments</li></ul>
<ul><li>Prepare documents and maintain filing system for department</li></ul>
<ul><li>Manage Basecamp database and ensure that backups take place monthly</li></ul>
<ul><li>Manage departmental tasks through Basecamp</li></ul>
<ul><li>Coordinate small scale vendor equipment rentals</li></ul>
<ul><li>Assist with safety records and natural disaster planning</li></ul>
<ul><li>Complete and send out recaps report after each event</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>The above listed duties are general statements of required major duties and responsibilities performed on a regular and continuous basis. They do not exclude other duties as assigned.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>To deliver on the Audubon service promise, the Event Operations Assistant will:</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Welcome</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li>Make eye contact with every customer encounter, smile at every person, offer friendly phrases and greetings.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Wear identification badge.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Walk individual to destination instead of pointing; offer assistance to people who look confused.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Assume ownership of potential safety hazards (wipe-up spill, pick up trash). Always have the camera-ready look.</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Share</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li>Maintain positive attitude, pleasant tone of voice (remember it is not what you say, but how you say it).</li></ul>
<ul><li>Coach coworkers on service expectations.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Utilize the power of teamwork to give excellent service.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Know when to involve management.</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Inspire</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<ul><li>Know the answers to questions or seek out the answers.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Utilize proper phone etiquette (answer phone within three rings, smile before answering call, ask for permission and wait for response before placing caller on hold).</li></ul>
<ul><li>Ensure work area is free of food, drink, socialization, and personal business.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Follow Dress Code Policy on clothing and grooming.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Keep unpleasant concerns behind closed doors (vent backstage).</li></ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Supervision: Not applicable</p>
<p>Experience: General knowledge of standard office forms, formats, procedures and equipment. Festival or event experience preferred.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Education:</p>
<p>1. High school diploma or equivalent preferred.</p>
<p>Licensure/Certification: Valid Driver’s License Required.</p>
<p>Software Requirements: Strong computer skills and broad software knowledge including proficiency in Microsoft XP, Outlook e-mail and calendar, Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Publisher.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Machinery/Tools/Equipment Requirements: must have the ability to use mobile devices, computer, fax, scanner, phone and copy machine. General knowledge of standard office forms, formats and procedures.</p>
<p>Physical Requirements:</p>
<p>1. This is a physically demanding job which can require: exerting up to 75 pounds of force occasionally, or a negligible amount of force frequently to lift, carry, push, pull, or otherwise move objects, including the human body. May involve walking and standing most of the time, but may involve sitting for brief periods of time.</p>
<p>2. Sitting or standing, bending, stooping, and the ability to carry items weighing up to 45 pounds.</p>
<p>3. Constant adequate range of motion and mobility required.</p>
<p>4. Ability to type 55 WPM.</p>
<p>5. Regular near acuity with clarity of vision at 20 inches or less required.</p>
<p>6. Typical ability to collect and analyze numerical and written data and verbal information to reach logical conclusions and ability to determine the time, place and sequence of operations or actions required.</p>
<p>7. Regular talking and the ability to express and exchange ideas by means of language expected.</p>
<p>8. Regular hearing and the ability to perceive the nature of sounds.</p>
<p>9. Constant ability to read, record, or type data quickly and accurately required.</p>
<p>Environmental/Working Conditions:</p>
<p>1. Working long hours between breaks or meals required.</p>
<p>2. Ability to prioritize, organize and synchronize multiple tasks and work under and handle high stress in an appropriate manner required.</p>
<p>3. Ability to perform duties effectively with minimal to no supervision.</p>
<p>4. Must be able to deal with people and situations in a tactful and discrete manner.</p>
<p>5. May be exposed to high, medium, or low noise intensity.</p>
<p>6. Constant contact with co-workers required.</p>
<p>7. Affinity for animals preferred.</p>
<p>8. Exposure to fluctuations in temperatures may be required.</p>
<p>9. Exposure to outdoor conditions may be required.</p>
<p>10. Willingness and availability to work flexible hours, overtime, weekends and holidays on occasion.</p>
