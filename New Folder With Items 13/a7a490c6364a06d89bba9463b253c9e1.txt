<h2>Sales Enablement Program Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">Over the next 10 years, biotech will fundamentally rewrite the way we live. Gene editing and cell therapy will dramatically change how we treat cancer and other major illnesses. Biofuels and biomaterials will transform the cars we drive, the clothes we wear, and the makeup of everyday objects. Crop science and synthetic biology will produce sustainable and ethical food. Benchling's mission is to accelerate the research that propels us towards this future, and magnify its impact, through modern software.
<br><br>
Every day, scientists around the world use Benchling in their efforts to solve humanity's most pressing problems. For these scientists, Benchling is the central technology they use to conduct their research.
<br><br>
Benchling was founded by a team of MIT graduates and has raised funding from Benchmark, Andreessen Horowitz, Thrive Capital, and Y Combinator. Our customers include pharmaceutical giants, leading biotechs, and the world's most renowned research institutes.
<br><br>
We are looking for a Sales Enablement Program Manager who is looking to make a large impact in a high-growth sales environment.
<br><br>
RESPONSIBILITIES
<br><br>
<ul>
<li>Report directly to the Head of Sales Operations to build Benchling's first Sales Enablement function.</li>
<li>Develop and own a sales onboarding program that scales for SMB and Enterprise Account Executives.</li>
<li>Own the metrics and measurement of Sales performance and ramp time.</li>
<li>Partner with the Marketing team on sales collateral development, product training and positioning.</li>
<li>Work with the leadership team to identify training needs and implement learning programs that address them.</li>
<li>Partner with Sales leaders on hiring, onboarding and ongoing coaching of the Sales team.</li>
<li>Implement tools and processes that scale knowledge with consistent messaging across the sales team</li>
</ul>

<li>Are able to partner with several cross-functional teams.</li>
