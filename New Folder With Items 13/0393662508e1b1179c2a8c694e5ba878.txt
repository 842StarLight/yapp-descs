<p><b>COMPANY OVERVIEW</b></p>
<p>Based in Wheeling, WV, the Ziegenfelder Company is a private, family owned frozen novelty manufacturer that focuses solely on producing the highest quality frozen ice novelties, commonly referred to as twin pops. We are the largest manufacturer of twin pops in the country with production facilities in Wheeling, WV; Chino, CA; and Denver, CO.</p>
<p>The Ziegenfelder Company has averaged solid double-digit growth for the past 15 years while growing our tribe from 60 employees in one facility with three production lines to over 350 employees across three facilities and 10 production lines. Core to our success has always been making the highest quality, best tasting products in the market and selling them to valued customers at the best possible price. The Ziegenfelder Company owns and produces the brand Budget Saver, as well as, licensed and private labeled products for premiere retailers, such as Albertsons, Dollar General, Kroger, and Walmart. 24/7 operations in our three facilities, generates over 2.2 million pops per day.</p>
<p>Ziegenfelder’s growth and success is clearly due to our dedicated tribe. We value integrity, creativity and innovation, and are committed to positively impacting our workforce and the communities in which we live and work.</p>

<p><b>POSITION</b></p>
<p>As the Director of FP&amp;A you will be a key financial partner to Ziegenfelder’s CFO. This highly visible role<br>requires you to be a strategic and innovative thinker who is able to develop and implement processes and drive financial analysis while being a well-respected and valued partner across the organization. You will be responsible for leading the company-wide financial planning &amp; analysis processes, conducting analytics on total company financials, implementing new processes for a growing organization, managing &amp; implementing FP&amp;A system tools, and providing broad decision support analytics to assist business leaders.</p>
<p>In this capacity you lead our forecast and planning efforts across the company as well as all analytics and reporting. You think critically about the company’s short- and long-term strategic and financial priorities and actively seek out opportunities to drive and influence top-line value and operating leverage.</p>
<p>This role, supported by a strong Finance &amp; Accounting Tribe, will be responsible for developing and driving a proactive, collaborative partnership among the leadership team, driving constructive and engaging conversations, monitoring stakeholder feedback, and identifying trends and opportunities to advance Ziegenfelder’s overall business &amp; mission.</p>
<p><b>ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS</b><br><b>The Ziegenfelder Director, Financial Planning &amp; Analysis is responsible for: </b></p>
<ul><li>Ensure an efficient, effective and comprehensive process for developing financial plans and analysis</li></ul>
<p>that links to strategic objectives.</p>
<ul><li>Spearhead the implementation and optimization of the corporate forecasting tool and integration with</li></ul>
<p>an interactive dashboard platform such as Power BI.</p>
<ul>
<li>Perform in depth financial analysis, build strategic models, and develop fact based recommendations to help the leadership team make informed business decisions.</li>
<li>Drive alignment and consistency within the business areas including, Sales, Operations, Engineering,</li>
</ul>
<p>Marketing and P&amp;C. Partner with understand needs and provide tools and processes they can leverage.</p>
<ul><li>Gauge the company’s overall financial health, primarily by using key financial ratios such as the debt to</li></ul>
<p>equity ratio, current ratio, and interest coverage ratio.</p>
<ul><li>Examine and evaluate the cost-efficiency of each department, in light of what percentage of the</li></ul>
<p>company’s financial resources each department consumes.  * Prepare internal reports for executive leadership and supporting their decision making.</p>
<ul><li>Creating, updating, and maintaining financial models and detailed forecasts of the company’s future</li></ul>
<p>operations.</p>
<ul><li>Compare historical results against budgets and forecasts, and perform variance analysis to explain</li></ul>
<p>differences in performance and make improvements going forward.</p>
<ul>
<li>Lead and support any ad hoc financial analysis, modeling, and analytical support.</li>
<li>Forecasting analysis for customer modeling and business development to support company growth.</li>
</ul>
