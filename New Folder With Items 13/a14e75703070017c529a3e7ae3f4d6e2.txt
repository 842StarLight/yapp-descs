<h2>Marketing Manager - West Coast Region</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><i>We are passionate about bringing cool, authentic restaurants t</i><i>o airports that reflect a taste of place.</i></p>
<p>
The Marketing Manager is an integral part of the Marketing and Operations team and will be responsible for the development, management, and helping in the implementation of the Company’s commercial initiatives, in order to meet the goals of the organization. Responsibilities will include, but are not limited to, providing marketing related support during new openings, ongoing marketing support to existing company locations, the development and implementation of marketing related action plans, as well as providing analysis into existing marketing programs as to drive return for the organizations. This position will report directly to the Senior Marketing Manager.</p>
<p><b>
Essential Functions</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Act as a core member of the pricing process and a critical contributor to the implementation of new menu roll-outs, to include meeting target deadlines and managing the pricing process in conjunction with other department leads</li>
<li>Follow established processes for opening support to ensure all customer facing programs are executed correctly at the time of opening.This will require close collaboration with multiple departments, partners, agencies, and suppliers at various touchpoints throughout the restaurant development process (RDP); also to include adhering to post-opening procedures in order to actively meet the marketing demands of a new restaurant</li>
<li>Lead the development of action plans rooted in commercial competencies and that have an operational focus.Bring in cross-functional departments to vet and help support the implementation of these programs, in order to deliver on the organization’s focus on AIT, increased average check, and customer service</li>
<li>Take ownership of menu mix evaluation, implementation of visual merchandising best practices, and project management, in order to position the marketing team and drive successful program development and implementation; act as the key player in the execution and follow-up of marketing and commercial plans across accounts</li>
<li>Closely collaborate, partner, and develop strong relationships with each locations General Manager/Director of Operations and each Region’s Regional Vice President/Regional Director of Operations; drive the culture of an operationally centered organization and deliver on the needs of each airport</li>
<li>Represent the marketing team in a professional manner at all times with the goal of growing influence and trust in the organization</li>
<li>Ability to travel, up to 50% of the time, within North America (US and Canada)
</li>
<li>Regular attendance as required by SSP America attendance policy</li>
<li>Performs any other duties as assigned by manager</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor’s Degree required; marketing or related field desired<br>
</li>
<li>Minimum five (5) years’ experience in Sales &amp; Marketing, preferably in the hospitality or food service industry</li>
<li>Documented experience in multi-unit restaurant and or foodservice operations and knowledge of principles and methods for showing, promoting, and selling food and/or beverage products or services. This would include marketing strategy and tactics, product demonstration, sales techniques, and sales control systems</li>
<li>Knowledge of business and management principles involved in strategic planning, resource allocation,leadership technique, travel schedules, and coordination of people and resources</li>
<li>Knowledge of budget and analysis of measurements and metrics to review effectiveness and marketing initiatives</li>
<li>Technical competence and experience with Microsoft Office Suites (i.e. Word, Excel, and PowerPoint), social media sites, Point of Sale (POS) and inventory systems (i.e. MyMicros and MyInventory), and related marketing software that includes graphics</li>
<li>Ability to travel minimum up to 50%; including Canada</li>
</ul>
