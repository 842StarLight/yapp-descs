<div><b>What You’ll Do</b></div>
<div>As Compensation Manager you will be a proven leader in analyzing, designing and administering compensation structures including surveys, analytics, job families, across communities. You will operate as an astute thought partner who assesses the competitive marketplace in making recommendations on compensation programs. You will serve as in-house educator to all employees about compensation policies and practices which aid in the attraction and retention of talent. You also will thoroughly understand the broader landscape of leading-edge total rewards programs (compensation, recognition, etc.) ensuring that all programs remain competitive and align to Ontario’s values, goals, and strategic objectives.</div>
<br>
<div></div>
<div>Job Responsibilities:</div>
<ul>
<li>In collaboration with the VPHR and executive leadership provides the strategic coordination, guidance and regulatory requirements of Ontario Systems compensation philosophy and programs designed to meet Ontario Systems talent and financial strategies</li>
<li>Survey participation, conducts internal and external market pricing and exemption status of Ontario Systems jobs</li>
<li>Conducts job evaluation for market pricing, exemption status and internal salary structure placement and compensation programs and makes recommendations for salary grades and pay ranges for all jobs
</li>
<li>Maintains internal salary structure, incentive plan design, equity programs, drafts and manages plan documents</li>
<li>Leads compensation M&amp;A projects and integration efforts
</li>
<li>Manages the administration of direct compensation (executive, exempt, and nonexempt cash compensation programs) for staff, including the processing, recording, and reporting of compensation-related actions taken on salaried employees</li>
<li>Manages and facilitates the annual compensation merit, market, promotional and other incentive-based reward programs and budget</li>
<li>Works with VP, General Managers and Finance on the design, development and administration of sales compensation programs</li>
<li>Develops and maintains other recognition and compensation related reward programs
</li>
<li>Ensures partnership and cross-functional communication with Finance for applicable budgeting and forecasting of compensation and benefit programs</li>
<li>Provides advice to management regarding pay decisions, policy and guideline interpretation, and job evaluation, including the design of creative solutions to specific compensation-related programs</li>
<li>Creates and facilitates training and development of people managers on our incentive compensation philosophy, programs and internal processes</li>
<li>Works with third-party compensation partners as needed</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Conducts analyses of compensation programs to evaluate cost and organizational effectiveness, internal equity, external competitiveness, and legal compliance of the organization’s compensation practices and to help establish programs that pay for performance and drive engagement</li>
<br>
</ul>

<p><b>About Ontario Systems </b>Ontario Systems is a leading provider of enterprise revenue cycle management software to the healthcare, accounts receivable management, and government markets. Established in 1980 and headquartered in Muncie, Ind., with offices located in Vancouver, Washington, and Albuquerque, New Mexico, and nearly 500 associates across 29 different states, Ontario Systems offers a full portfolio of leading software platforms, including Artiva RM™, Artiva HCx™, Contact Savvy®, RevQ®, Full Court Enterprise ®, Full Case ®, and CitePayUSA. Ontario Systems’ industry-leading customers include 5 of the 15 largest hospital networks who actively manage over $40 billion in receivables collectively, as well as 8 of the 10 largest ARM companies and more than a hundred federal, state and municipal government clients in the U.S.</p>
