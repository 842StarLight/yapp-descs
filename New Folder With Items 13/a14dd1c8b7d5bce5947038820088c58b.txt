<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Responsibilities</b></h3>
<p></p>
<div>
<p>Serves as Store Planning Specialist within the Business Operations Directorate, Field Operations Branch, Business and Support Services Division (MR). Creates schematic design drawings of the retail sales floor for all Marine Corps Exchange (MCX) store types, new builds, remodels, expansions and resets. Works in conjunction with construction and other departments in all phases of projects, from inception to completion.
</p>
<p>Proficient in reading and reviewing project documentation such as architectural/engineering plans, specifications and cost estimates. Reviews construction contractor drawings/submittals and highlights areas of concern and provides corrective feedback. Produces drawings with a high level of accuracy ensuring they meet the integrity of brand standards. Works with intermediate and senior level staff to review store layouts and renderings for accuracy and brand integrity. Actively pursues continuous improvements, researches and identifies industry trends and incorporates them into daily practices.
</p>
<p>Responsible for selection and placement of standard and vendor fixtures, interior retail store planning and design, and merchandise layouts. Analyzes store performance data to create sales floor layouts that best support store sales and maximize productivity of space, while enhancing the customer shopping experience. Updates layouts based on changes in new planogram and merchandise direction, building dimension, and new company initiatives.
</p>
<p>Develops and maintains a library of both Auto CAD and PDF files that contains current floor plans with department adjacencies, product placements and department square footages. Works with the Visual Merchandise Manager to create 2D Auto CAD fixture blocks for both standard and vendor fixtures.
</p>
<p>Develops standard store models based on store type, current sales volume, and new or existing field conditions. Creates 3D renderings of interior and exterior elevations of prototype stores.
</p>
<p>Aids in preparation of design presentations and has the ability to produce renderings to communicate design intent in support of the brand standards.
</p>
<p>May visit sites as required, to ascertain all field conditions that may impact the architectural design and floor layout phases prior, during and following project planning. May attend conferences and meetings. Prepares supporting documentation for presentations and proposals. May prepare correspondence, briefs, action documents, routing sheets, point papers and slide presentations for use by management officials.
</p>
<p>Provides World Class Customer Service with an emphasis on courtesy. Assists customers and communicates positively in a friendly manner. Takes action to solve problems quickly. Alerts the high-level supervisor or proper point of contact for help when problems arise. Adheres to safety regulations and standards. Promptly reports any observed workplace hazards, and any injury, occupational illness, and/or property damage resulting from workplace mishaps to the immediate supervisor.
</p>
<p>May travel to conduct work assignments. May work a fluctuating work schedule to conduct work assignments outside of the typical workweek and work hours.</p>
<p>
Performs other related duties as assigned. This is a white collar position where occasional lifting up to 20lbs. may be required.
</p>
