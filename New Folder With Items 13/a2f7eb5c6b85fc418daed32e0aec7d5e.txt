<p>MFS co-op positions are a 6-month commitment (January 7, 2020– June 26, 2020), working Monday – Friday and work between 35-40 hours. Our program is designed for undergraduate students who are currently enrolled in a co-op program through their college or university and can meet our requirements.
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>About DevOps Engineering Co-op
</b></p>
<p>This is a great opportunity to understand application development in a corporate environment. We are looking for a talented student that’s interested in a DevOps Engineering Co-Op . You’ll have the opportunity to work with a fun, hardworking and talented team to help troubleshoot issues, develop tools, manage licenses and maintain processes that support our development and agile environment.
<br>
<br>
<b>Principal Responsibilities
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Develop tools and processes to support our software development team build, deploy, and test our applications
</li>
<li>Manage deployment of software and licenses that support our development teams
</li>
<li>Perform updates and configuration changes to our infrastructure as requested by the software development teams.
</li>
<li>Work with other members of the DevOps Team to migrate our applications and data.
</li>
<li>Participate in monitoring, maintenance and reporting status of our current production applications and releases.
</li>
<li>Research and experiment with new technologies that can improve day to day operations
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>Qualifications
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>An awareness of DevOps processes is a plus
</li>
<li>A strong interest in exploring a wide range of technologies
</li>
<li>Familiar with configuring/installing and desktop software
</li>
<li>Familiar with Microsoft Excel
</li>
<li>Knowledge of development technologies and tools such as Java, C#, Eclipse, Visual Studio, GIT etc.
</li>
<li>Solid problem solving abilities
</li>
<li>Able to work both independently and in a group
</li>
<li>Working toward a Bachelor’s Degree, preferably in Computer Science, Information Technology or a related field
</li>
</ul>
