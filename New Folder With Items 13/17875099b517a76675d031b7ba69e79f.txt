<p><b>
Purpose of Role:</b></p>
<p>
The Supply Operations Planner is responsible for leading the development of the master production and inventory plan which will drive the production, finished goods inventory, and financial cash plans, balancing the customer service, product freshness, finished goods inventory and cost goals. Responsibility, scope and scale, and experience differentiates the</p>
<p></p>
<br>
<p><b>
Key Responsibilities:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Develop and communicate weekly production plan based on demand to meet overall customer service levels as well as finished goods inventory and non-quality cost targets.</li>
<li>
Develop detailed understanding of the manufacturing constraints and capabilities of the locations being planned.</li>
<li>
Lead the weekly Master Production Schedule (MPS) meeting for assigned segment, linking in plant teams to eview issues and actions relating to the 16 week production schedules, consistent with the agreed supply policies.</li>
<li>
Publish production and finished goods inventory plans each period as part of the Sales &amp; Operations Planning (S&amp;OP) cycle, which includes the development and execution of the JDA planning software models to solve for capacity and inventory using the demand forecast and agreed inventory policies.</li>
<li>
Perform and publish root cause analysis on key metrics including customer service and inventory levels.</li>
<li>
Participate/lead continuous improvement projects and problem solving cards.</li>
<li>
Manage timing of production and/or co-packing for promotional events and launches such that desired product quantities are available.</li>
<li>
Identify required changes to seasonal inventory pre-builds, re-balancing of production and production shift requirements.</li>
<li>
Coordinate with segment IE’s to review capacity plans and define optimal SC policy and sourcing decisions (service, cost, and inventory).</li>
<li>
Represent Operations Planning as a link to Activity Management teams. Participate in the after action review of marketing / sales activities and ensure that relevant learning is incorporated into future forecast and production plans.</li>
<li>
Collaborate with Segment Planning Manager and Operations Planners in analysis of new ideas for feasibility to support innovation, capacity, and efficiency, including seasonal, customization and in-sourcing.</li>
<li>
Provide input into Step 3 as part of the S&amp;OP process. Work to identify and resolve supply, capacity, and product age issues with recommendations to the Segment Planning Manager; support the business and operational planning needs over the 0-36 month horizon and assist plants in crewing patterns.</li>
<li>
Coach and provide technical direction and support to plant scheduling and logistics associates.</li>
<li>
Collaborate with crossfunctional Supply team to lead continuous improvement initiatives.</li>
</ul>
