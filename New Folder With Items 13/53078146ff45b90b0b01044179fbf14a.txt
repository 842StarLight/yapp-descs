<b>
Responsibilities</b>
</div>
<div></div>
<div>The Aerospace Corporation, a world leader in space-related science and technology and the only Federally Funded Research and Development Center dedicated to the United States Space Enterprise, is seeking a Senior Project Engineer to support Business Development in the Civil Systems Group Commercial Program Office as a customer support account representative. The mission of the Commercial Programs Office is to create opportunities for technical engagement based on Aerospace’s exceptional ability to perform technology characterization through the use of its unique test facilities and physical science laboratories, which are comprise of over 80 specialized laboratories used to research, test, analyze, and troubleshoot virtually every aspect of rocket, satellite and space system design, development, construction and operation. This includes basic materials science, propulsion science, materials processing, optical communications, applications of atomic physics (atomic clocks, quantum communications, and computing), space particles and fields, ionospheric and thermospheric physics, remote sensing, space instrumentation and testing, the effects of radiation, plasma, and the effects of elements of the atmosphere and ionosphere on systems designed to operate in space.</div>
<br>
<div></div>
<div><b>Key Functions</b></div>
<ul>
<li>Develop and maintain strong relationships with the US and International commercial space enterprise and create lead and capture opportunities base on the Aerospace Corporation’s extensive technology characterization tool set.</li>
<li>Provide insight and leadership to help align Aerospace capability owners with commercial space technology readiness and development challenges. Facilitate communication of the Aerospace Corporation’s unique value add in technology characterization, test and space systems verification.</li>
<li>Assist management in the evaluation, pursuit and capture of new commercial opportunities consistent with Aerospace policies/practices while serving as the primary Commercial Program Office interface with Aerospace contracts, legal, pricing and proposal review teams.</li>
<li>Create and develop multiple, concurrent pursuits opportunities through a structured pursuit and proposal development process.</li>
<li>Participate in customer meetings, industry conferences, lead industry panels and author presentations to increase awareness of Aerospace’s unique space systems characterization capabilities.</li>
<li>Review update and maintain capture and proposal pipeline databases on a regular basis.</li>
<li>Work closely with Aerospace capability owners to align opportunities with corporate and customer strategic technical needs.</li>
<li>Report and track: (1) performance against goals; (2) resource expenditure actuals vs. plan; (3) qualified opportunity pipeline and utilization of bid and proposal funding.</li>
<li>Report organization's activities, progress and achievements through written and oral reports to management and customers.</li>
</ul>

<div><b>Company Statement</b></div>
<div>The Aerospace Corporation has provided independent technical and scientific research, development, and advisory services to national security space programs since 1960. We operate a federally funded research and development center (FFRDC) for the United States Air Force and the National Reconnaissance Office, and support all national security space programs. We also apply more than 50 years of experience with space systems to provide critical solutions to technologically complex systems in such areas as communications, shipping, law enforcement, and cyber, among others.</div>
<div>From our inception, our highly skilled technical staff has focused on ensuring the success of every mission and developing the most effective and economic space-related hardware and software in the world. Our greatest asset is the technical expertise of our people. Our state-of-the-art laboratory facilities are staffed by some of the leading scientists in the world.</div>
