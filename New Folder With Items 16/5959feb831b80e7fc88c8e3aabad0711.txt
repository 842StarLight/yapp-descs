<h2>Machinist</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<p>This position entails producing machined parts by programming, setting up, and operating a computer numerical control (CNC) or manual machines. A machinist will be responsible for maintaining quality and safety standards; keeping records; maintaining equipment and supplies. The successful candidate should have the necessary skill sets listed below or should be willing to learn others as needed.<br>
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b><i>ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES
</i></b></p>
<ul>
<li>Operate a multi axis CNC Machining Center, manual mills and lathes.
</li>
<li>Perform quality assurance inspection of parts according to guidelines.
</li>
<li>Ability to machine various metals, including, but not limited to, carbon steel, stainless steel, and various high alloys.
</li>
<li>Ability to operate CNC machinery (vertical/horizontal mills and lathes), manual machinery (straighter, bed mills, lathes) with high complexity.
</li>
<li>Ability to edit programs on CNC machinery for each part being machined.
</li>
<li>Interpret Blueprints for production orders.
</li>
<li>Other relevant duties may be assigned<br>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<h1 class="jobSectionHeader"><b><i>SKILLS
</i></b></h1>
<ul>
<li>Ability to perform workshop math (trigonometry, geometry etc.)
</li>
<li>Be able to use non-powered hand-tools (mallets; hammers; pry-bar; screw-drivers; wrenches; micrometers; vernier calipers, indicators, height gauges etc.).
</li>
<li>Be able to plan, coordinate, implement and evaluate an activity adhering to specifications for quality control
</li>
<li>Capability of reading and interpreting blueprints for production orders.
</li>
<li>Prior experience and knowledge to operate jib cranes and hoists.<br>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<br>
<h1 class="jobSectionHeader"><b><i>EDUCATION AND EXPERIENCE
</i></b></h1>
<ul>
<li>Minimum High School Diploma or General Education Degree (GED).
</li>
<li>Mastery experience of the essential duties and responsibilities listed above with at least ten years’ experience.
</li>
<li>Ability to read blueprints is required.
</li>
<li>Basic math skills required including metric conversions and understanding basic right angle trigonometry and geometric concepts (square, plumb, parallel etc.)
</li>
<li>Be able to Identify and resolve a problem associated with a product, part and/or design (work order, blue print, mechanical drawing etc.) problem.
</li>
<li>Be able to conduct Level I (1) and Level II (2) work-area skill instruction.
</li>
<li>Be able to research subcontract details and solve a problem.<br>
</li>
</ul>
