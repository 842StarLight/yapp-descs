<h2>Director for Leadership and Major Giving - FA068</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<div><div><p>To provide services which ensure a fair, equitable, quality working and learning environment for all employees and members of the university community. To proactively and creatively provide all customers with superior, innovation, and cost-effective programs and services. To continuously seek and supply all customers with the opportunities and resources needed to enhance their knowledge, skills and abilities so they may better support the mission of the university. The division seeks a Director for Leadership and Major Giving to cultivate relationships with alumni and prospects interested in a variety of planned gifts that will benefit Norfolk State University, and its students. Incumbent: 1. Considerable knowledge and experience in identification, cultivation, solicitation, and stewardship strategies and techniques, particularly in area of major gift fundraising or high-end sales, as well as a record of success in generating significant gift commitments or sales. 2. Responsible for the development and management of an active portfolio of up to 150 prospects with the identified potential to support Norfolk State University at or above the $25,000 level. 3. Excellent written and interpersonal communications skills, including the political acumen needed to establish and maintain good working relationships throughout the organization and with outside constituencies. 4. Ability to maintain confidentiality in matters related to sensitive University and/or donor relations issues. 5. Highly donor-centered and focused on balancing the donor's funding interest with the University's interest. 6. Ability to work effectively with academic and University leaders and volunteers to achieve fundraising goals and knowledge o a broad range of academic and institutional issues related to a university environment. 7. Well-developed organizational skills and the ability to prioritize and manage multiple and complex tasks and projects concurrently. 8. Demonstrated ability to work effectively and collaboratively with other advancement staff in devising, analyzing, modifying, implementing, and evaluating development cultivations plans. 9. Self-motivation and discipline to regularly set and achieve work goals, as well as excellent organizational, interpersonal and networking skills with both large groups and individuals. <b>EEO Statement NSU is committed to providing equal employment opportunities for all persons and applicants, without regard to age, color, disability, gender, national origin, political affiliation, genetic information, race, religion, sexual orientation, sex (including pregnancy) or veteran status. NSU encourages and invites minorities, women, individuals with disabilities and veterans to apply.
</b></p></div></div>
<div><div>
<p><b>Minimum Qualifications </b></p>
<p><b>1. Considerable knowledge of best practices in planned giving, including relevant rules, regulations, and programs/policies which comply with various planned giving vehicles. 2. Considerable knowledge and a successful record of donor cultivation, solicitation, and stewardship 3. Knowledge of non-profit financials. 4. Extensive knowledge of and skill in using fundraising databases and planned giving software. 5. Considerable experience in development and implantation of a successful marketing plan for a planned giving program. 6. Demonstrated ability to establish and maintain relationships with a diverse constituent base, including, but not limited to students, faculty, staff, alumni, and benefactors in the community. 7. Candidate must have an undergraduate degree and at least three to five years of demonstrated success in corporate business development, university or nonprofit fundraising, and/or sales.
</b></p>
</div></div>
<div><div>
<p><b>Preferred Qualifications </b></p>
<p><b>1. Experience with relationship-based major gift fundraising in a higher education or a comparably complex organizational setting. 2. Significant previous experience leveraging planned giving tools and concepts to close gift commitments. 3. Master's Degree is preferred.
