<b>Your Role and Responsibilities</b><br>
<b>Preferred location - Cambridge, MA</b><br>
<br>
The Oncology &amp; Genomics Marketing manager is responsible for leading the marketing strategy and execution for the IBM Watson Health suite of Oncology and Genomics software products, one of the most exciting portfolios in Watson Health. Working with colleagues in Offering Management and Sales, the leader will understand the market, the offerings, and the buyers, and differentiate our O&amp;G value propositions vs. our competitors. They will prioritize programs, routes to market, and channel enablement requirements to meet the revenue and profit goals of the business. And they will work with our content, campaign and digital marketing teams to ensure that this differentiation is pulled through across our marketing activities.<br>
This global role is responsible for a team that translates product value propositions into positioning, messaging, and demand generation strategies that must be activated in key growth markets worldwide. The holder of this leadership position works with an extensive cross functional team that includes offering management, region marketing, communications, sales, and other marketing specialties. He or she requires expertise and excellence in oncology, marketing, and software technology to be successful.<br>
<br>
<b>A Day in the Life Essentials</b><ul>
<li>
Strategy development: Create marketing plans and budgets for the overall portfolio, and for product launches and sustaining efforts. Identify and solve for target segments, including regional adaptations of the strategy.</li>
<li>
Messaging strategy: Define the core and supporting messages that convey the product value propositions. Support the scoping of studies and analysis that supports the value proposition, and effectively communicate the evidence in the marketplace. Work with internal constituencies and agencies to create platforms and executions that will activate demand.</li>
<li>
Demand generation: Help develop and execute demand generation campaigns through digital channels, trade shows, public relations, and other media</li>
<li>
Channel enablement: Ensure that both direct and indirect sales teams have the tools and training required to succeed</li>
<li>
Market intelligence: Use data and insights to become the expert on the challenges and needs of health systems and oncologists, as well as the ecosystem of competitors and partners that participate in this industry</li>
<li>
Executive engagement: Serve as a key member on the leadership staffs for both Watson Health Marketing and the Oncology business unit. Provide clear updates to senior leaders, actively participate in planning discussions, and lead workstreams as part of the annual budgeting cycles.</li>
</ul>
<b>
Desired Attributes</b><ul>
<li>
Cross-functional leadership, organizational, and teamwork skills: The Product Marketing leader must directly manage his/her own team of Product Marketers, as well as all cross-functional marketing efforts that are supported by peers and “dotted line” reports.</li>
<li>
Strong communicator: ability to think carefully about the intended audience, and to present ideas and facts persuasively and at the right level of detail</li>
<li>
Results-orientation, with ability to take independent ownership of the product portfolio and the tasks required to succeed</li>
<li>
Executive presence and mindset. Ability to convey business results and plans to senior leaders, as well as the ability to translate objectives set by the business into strategies and tactics for the team</li>
</ul>
