<b>Position Purpose</b><br>
<br>
The Director of Special Events for ARCW is responsible for leading, directing, and implementing ARCW fund development strategies through ARCW special events.<br>
<br>
ARCW has developed a reputation for producing events that are popular, well-supported through sponsorship, logistically well-managed and clearly communicate the mission of the organization. ARCW events reinforce the organization’s brand, which is evolving as the organization grows nationally. On average, corporate event sponsorship covers 41% of the department’s budget and ARCW is intent on doubling this while growing all event revenue in the coming years.<br>
<br>
The Director of Special Events reports directly to the Vice President and Chief Development Officer (CDO), is a member of the ARCW management staff, and supervises non-management staff and volunteers so ARCW special events meet and exceed the fundraising goals of the Development Department and the Board of Directors. The Director of Special Events works closely with the CDO to achieve the agency’s fundraising goals and is responsible for developing best practices in both special event fund raising and in the recruitment of our event attendees for the sake of growing private revenue while achieving cost of fund raising efficiencies.<br>
<br>
<b>Scope of Responsibilities</b><br>
<br>
The AIDS Resource Center of Wisconsin (ARCW) is one of the nation’s largest providers of HIV prevention, care and treatment services with a budget approaching $120 million and employing over 300 professionals. Our organization also includes both St. Louis Effort for AIDS (EFA) and Denver's Rocky Mountain CARES (RMC).<br>
<br>
The unique, nationally recognized ARCW HIV Medical Home model of care assures that everyone with HIV has access to medical, dental, mental health and social services and provides the best opportunity for patients to achieve high quality health outcomes. ARCW also provides aggressive HIV prevention services to gay men, injection drug users and others at the highest risk for HIV infection.<br>
<br>
ARCW operates in the states of WI, CO and MO. It embraces a Market Culture to increase access to care, enhance quality outcomes and achieve and surpass financial performance expectations and has an aggressive plan to expand into additional states.<br>

<p><b>Position </b><b>Responsibilities</b><br>
</p>
<ul>
<li>Meet or exceed the annual ARCW special event revenue goal through effective leadership and management of ARCW’s special events.</li>
<li>Develop innovative strategies to increase sponsorship and all participation revenue for all events. Assure comprehensive and consistent outreach, recruitment and promotional efforts that are appropriate for each event.</li>
<li>Develop and lead a solicitation strategy to increase corporate sponsorship of ARCW special events.</li>
<li>Provide leadership and direction to assure an appropriate and purposeful volunteer system for special events that has a primary focus of sponsorship development and event participant recruitment.</li>
<li>Manage event logistics to assure quality and cost effective production of all events.</li>
<li>Provide leadership and strategic direction to build participation and increase revenue for ARCW’s largest event, AIDS Walk Wisconsin.</li>
<li>Develop the annual special events budget that includes revenue and expense for each event, contributes to the annual development goal and meets cost effectiveness standards of the department. Monitor budget performance to assure compliance with budget goals.</li>
<li>Provide a report on the performance outcome of each event including budget to actual financial performance, participation levels, logistics, and recommendations for improvement. Maintain a historical database on event financial performance and outcomes.</li>
<li>Collaborate with the ARCW Public Affairs Department to develop marketing and communication strategies for maximum promotion of ARCW special events to meet event goals.</li>
<li>Collaborate with the Foundations and Corporations fund raising staff to assure the most effective approach to corporate solicitation is implemented so that sponsorship and philanthropy solicitations are complimentary and provide the best opportunity for maximizing corporate donations.</li>
<li>Evaluate all special events to assure appropriate themes, messages, and financial performance. Consistently analyze special event fund raising outcomes in relation to all costs, including staffing and assess opportunities for new event production. When appropriate, provide recommendations to the CDO for adding, ending or significantly changing events that are produced by ARCW.</li>
<li>Provide effective stewardship to ARCW special event participants and sponsors including the nurturing and enhancement of relationships, recognition of donors and acknowledging and thanking all donors.</li>
<li>Effectively lead and manage special events staff to achieve special event goals and objectives. Conduct regular professional management interviews (PMI), implement effective communications systems, conduct annual performance evaluations and provide support for professional growth of staff.</li>
<li>Provide leadership and direction to assure an appropriate and purposeful volunteer system for special events that has a primary focus of event participant recruitment.</li>
<li>Other duties as assigned by the Vice President and Chief Development Officer (CDO).</li>
</ul>
