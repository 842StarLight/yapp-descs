<p><b>Build Your Career. Build America’s Future.</b></p>
<p>
Vulcan Materials Company is the nation’s largest producer of construction aggregates and a major producer of aggregates-based construction materials including asphalt and ready-mixed concrete. When you join Vulcan, it’s more than starting an exciting career – you get to make a difference for millions of people every day across the country.</p>
<p>
When you join Vulcan, you join a dynamic culture in which career development is encouraged, excellence is rewarded, and diversity is valued. No matter the role or the location across the country, every member of the Vulcan team lives through the Vulcan Way: <i>doing the right thing, the right way, at the right time</i>.</p>
<p><b>
What You’ll Do:</b></p>
<p><b>
Identify Environmental Requirements. </b>Analyze a wide array of environmental requirements including permits, plans and regulatory requirements and determine applicability to our operations. Develop and maintain a compliance framework that communicates plant responsibilities for the each compliance program.</p>
<p><b>
Conduct Inspections and Audits.</b> Conduct frequent inspections and audits at each plant location to assess regulatory and environmental management compliance, control significant environmental risks, and ensure the adequacy of environmental management systems. Utilize findings to identify opportunities for improvement.</p>
<p><b>
Develop and Deliver Training. </b>Develop material to engage and educate operations on environmental awareness topics and required tasks. Provide training on an individual and group basis.</p>
<p><b>
Interact with Various Groups. </b>Provide assistance to local plant and sales personnel on environmental issues and compliance on a day-to-day and project basis. Interact with governmental agencies regarding new legislation, regulations and compliance issues. Participate in trade association activities. Manage the use of consultants to improve return and minimize costs.</p>
<p><b>
Complete Compliance Support Activities. </b>Prepare and submit permit applications, modifications and renewals. Prepare and maintain plans (SPCC, SWPPP, fugitive dust, etc). Track and complete reporting requirements (discharges, storm water, emissions, TRI, etc.) and manage recordkeeping requirements.</p>

<b>
Qualifications</b><div>
</div>
<p><b>
Skills You’ll Need:</b></p>
<p><b>
Education. </b>Bachelor’s degree in Engineering, Chemistry, Geology, Biology, or a similar technical area is preferred. An Environmental Engineering degree and/or an advanced degree in some aspect of environmental studies is desirable. P.E. Certification strongly preferred.</p>
<p><b>
Experience. </b>Previous experience in the environmental area is preferred. Must have a high level of hands-on experience in reviewing regulatory requirements and assessing their applicability and impact to the field.</p>
<p><b>
Regulatory Knowledge. </b>Must have expertise in environmental regulatory compliance and numeric groundwater and surface water modeling. Must have strong knowledge of the federal and state environmental regulatory process and experience with environmental regulatory areas (RCRA, CWA, CAA, TSCA, etc.)</p>
<p><b>
Interpersonal Skills. </b>Must have excellent verbal and written communication skills and interact well with all levels of personnel.</p>
<p><b>
Technology Skills.</b> Must have the ability to use computerized equipment and technology. Must demonstrate proficiency in Microsoft Office Suite, Google, Oracle Business Environment, and other software packages relevant to the position.</p>
