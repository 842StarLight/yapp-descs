<h2>Regional Safety Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div><div><div>
<div><div><p><b>Job Summary:
</b></p></div></div>
<br>
<div><div>
<p>This position reports directly to the Director of Safety and will provide Armada Hoffler Construction Company (AHCC) with full time Environmental, Health, and Safety (EHS) services and administration of multiple potential projects in the Southeastern United States including North Carolina, South Carolina, and Georgia.
</p>
<p>This position will be responsible for carrying out daily EHS functions on our projects in accordance with the AHCC Corporate Safety Program. Additionally, this position must promote and instill Armada Hoffler’s safety culture, business values, and vision into the workplace.
</p>
</div></div>
<br>
<div><div><p><b>Specific Functions and Responsibilities:<br>
</b></p></div></div>
<br>
<p></p>
<div><div><ul>
<li>Work collaboratively with AHCC project teams, and subcontractors to identify and mitigate hazardous working conditions and unsafe behaviors in order to ensure a safe and healthy working environment for all personnel working on our projects.
</li>
<li>Lead AHCC project teams and subcontractors to ensure projects follow all AHCC Safety Requirements, as well as all applicable federal, state, and local laws regarding EHS matters.
</li>
<li>Monitor the arrival of new personnel on projects in order to ensure all personnel receive site specific safety orientation.
</li>
<li>Review and make recommendations on all safety related project submittals including safety plans, safety data sheets, and job hazard analysis to ensure they meet the requirement of the AHCC Safety Program and OSHA.
</li>
<li>Attend all monthly project safety huddles and weekly subcontractor coordination meetings and provide a safety leadership presence, while addressing and ongoing safety issues on the project.
</li>
<li>Conduct special safety meetings or training sessions as needed for AHCC project teams and subcontractors in order to ensure the highest level of safety awareness and training for our project teams and partners.
</li>
<li>Coordinate the investigation and reporting of all incidents and ensure every effort is made to eliminate a repeat of the incident.
</li>
<li>Provide project teams assistance with the supply, inspection, and accountability of personal protective equipment for AHCC personnel and visitors to the site.
</li>
<li>Review EHS related documentation to ensure all required records and reports are complete, accurate, and submitted per established procedures and implement appropriate corrective actions as required.
</li>
<li>Accompany federal, state, local authorities or insurance personnel on all EHS related inspections or walk throughs. Coordinate the immediate correction of any issues identified. Assist in preparing any written appeals for any citations issued during these activities.
</li>
<li>Work collaboratively with AHCC project teams and subcontractors to ensure adequate safeguards are in place in order to protect the environment, this includes ensuring a site-specific Spill Prevention, Control and Countermeasures Plan (SPCC) is in place and all Safety Data Sheets (SDS) are maintained on site.
</li>
<li>Ensure that all signs and postings are established and maintained on site in accordance with AHCC and OSHA requirements.
</li>
<li>Plan, implement, and supervise all project emergency preparedness and response efforts including assignments, procuring equipment and supplies, staffing and training to ensure the highest level of preparedness for each project.</li>
</ul></div></div>
</div></div></div>
<br><div><div><div>
<div><div><p><b>Qualifications:<br>
</b></p></div></div>
<br>
<p></p>
<div><div><ul>
<li>Bachelor’s degree in a Safety, Environmental, or Emergency Management discipline from a four-year college or university. A combination or education and experience may be considered.
</li>
<li>3-5 years of safety experience in commercial construction.
</li>
<li>OSHA 30 hour certified, construction outreach instructor certification preferred.
</li>
<li>First Aid / CPR and AED Certified.
</li>
<li>Hazwoper 40 Hour or Hazmat Technician Certified preferred.
</li>
<li>Training and or experience in crane operations, rigging, fall protection, trench and excavation safety, scaffolds, and heavy equipment safety is required.
</li>
<li>Ability to write clear and concise professional reports, correspondence, and technical procedures.
</li>
<li>Must have solid recordkeeping, organization, and time management skills.
</li>
<li>Proficiency in the use of MS Office as well as the ability to learn new software applications and adapt to changing technology.
</li>
<li>Ability to communicate and influence AHCC and subcontractor personnel at all levels of an organization.
</li>
<li>Valid driver’s license with a good driving record is required.
</li>
<li>Ability to develop and present site-specific safety training to address ongoing or developing safety hazards.</li>
</ul></div></div>
