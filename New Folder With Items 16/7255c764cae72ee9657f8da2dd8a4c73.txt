<div><b>The Role: IT Desktop Architect Supervisor<br>
</b></div>
<div></div>
<br>
<br>
<br>
<div>
<b>Purpose
</b><br>
ENGIE is a leader in Energy Transition providing Energy and Service solutions to its customers. Be part of our dynamic team leveraging technology to bring innovative energy solutions to our customers. The Desktop Architect Supervisor ensures a quality engineered, innovative and technically capable desktops, laptops, and mobile devices; and they meet or exceed company requirements and SLA’s.<br>
</div>

Salaried – Exempt<br>

<br>
<div><b>Essential Job Functions
</b></div>
<div>
<br>
Under the direction of Service Delivery Manager, the Desktop Architect Supervisor is an experienced, hands-on engineering technologist and leader responsible for delivering reliable and manageable end user computing devices, standardization, life cycle management, patching, asset tracking, acquisition, desktop project management, KPI reporting, end user experience management and training. The Desktop Architect Supervisor also serves as liaison between IT infrastructure, application owners, developers, vendors and customers to ensure IT managed devices meet company requirements and expectations.<br>
<br>

<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Qualifications<br>
</b></h2>
<div></div>
<br>
<div><b>General Job Requirements<br>
</b></div>
<div></div>
<br>
<ul>
<li><div>The position requires the candidate to work effectively as part of a larger team in an infrastructure technology, operations and architecture group.
</div></li>
<li><div>Requires you to make critical technical decisions to solve and engineer problem/solutions from a tiered support desk.
</div></li>
<li><div>Position requires analyzing and reporting on business and technical problems and working with division IT directors for individual BU requirements.
</div></li>
<li><div>Requires building and suggesting strategies for planning, engineering, documenting and presenting to management teams for the corporate desktop environment.
</div></li>
<li><div>Requires knowledge of Citrix, VMWare, Virtualization technologies, Active Directory, Microsoft Operating Systems and Powershell scripting.<br>
</div></li>
</ul>
<div></div>
<br>
<br>
<div><b>Specific ENGIE NA Job Requirements
</b></div>
<ul>
<li><div>Design, engineer, deploy, manage and administer the Ivanti Landesk/EPM server for the deployment of patches and application on a regular basis.
</div></li>
<li><div>Expert understanding and packaging of ENGIE NA applications utilizing VMWare, Microsoft Installshield .MSI and Powershell scripts.
</div></li>
<li><div>Manage and maintain the Landesk remote servers for the ENGIE NA corporate image for use in North America sites.
</div></li>
<li><div>Manage and administer the workstation environment for patching all ENGIE NA client machines located both in corporate and offsite locations along with providing reports on security status of workstations.
</div></li>
<li><div>Package, test and deploy applications in a Citrix XenServer environment supporting several thousand users.
</div></li>
<li><div>Work with applications teams and BU IT Directors in to troubleshoot and maintain applications deployed in the ENGIE NA client operating system environment.
</div></li>
<li><div>Work directly with the client service desk to provide third level support for any escalated problems.
</div></li>
<li><div>Provide metrics and reports to management of the status of the common operating environment and report to the Infrastructure and Service Delivery Manager.
</div></li>
<li><div>Establish with hardware vendors, UI, hardware, security, operating system and mobile standards for ENGIE NA.<br>
</div></li>
</ul>
