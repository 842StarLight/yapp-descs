<p><br>
The Senior Corporate Counsel, is responsible for providing legal research and advice, both proactively and reactively, in all assigned subject areas, specifically identifying legal requirements, emerging trends and reoccurring issues, and develop strategies to minimize legal liability. This position will focus on the review of an assortment of agreements across the enterprise, including customer contracts and arrangements, corporate documents and agreements, and real estate leases, purchase and sale agreements and related documents. This position may also assist with mergers and acquisitions, including handling those matters that arise from such transactions, and certain corporate filings.<br>
</p>
<p></p>
<p>Primary Job Functions:</p>
<p></p>
<ul><li>
<br>
Provide proactive legal advice and counsel on assigned subject areas.</li></ul>
<p></p>
<ul><li>Draft and review a wide-assortment of corporate agreements and documents covering designated areas of the business, and provide advice and counsel to the internal clients regarding same. Such agreements may be in the areas of customer arrangements, general corporate matters, real estate, and mergers and acquisitions.
</li></ul>
<p></p>
<ul><li>Recommend and implement best practices and proactive strategies consistent with legal requirements and emerging trends to minimize liability and promote the Company’s strategic plan.</li></ul>
<p></p>
<ul><li>Proactively identify recurring issues in assigned subject areas and provide advice and counsel to business leaders on complex issues that could impact organization.</li></ul>
<p></p>
<ul><li>Collaborate with stakeholders to develop, review and revise, as required, company policies and procedures in assigned subject areas. Recommend changes to policies based on legal developments.</li></ul>
<p></p>
<ul><li>
<br>
Prepare reports for senior management and provide recommendations, as appropriate.</li></ul>
<p></p>
<ul><li>Pursue ongoing professional development in current areas of responsibility and in relevant, emerging areas of the law.</li></ul>
<p></p>
<ul><li>Conduct legal research and analysis as necessary.</li></ul>
<p></p>
<ul>
<li>Draft, review and/or negotiate contracts.</li>
<li>Devise and conduct employee training as needed on relevant issues.</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<ul><li>May make employment decisions and/or recommendations on hiring, improvement plans, transfers, promotions, corrective actions, terminations, pay adjustments, etc.</li></ul>
<p></p>
<ul><li>
<br>
Select, manage and oversee outside counsel in assigned subject areas and within the parameters set by the Chief Legal Officer</li></ul>
