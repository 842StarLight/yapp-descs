<h2>Telecom Tower Top Hand I (Sacramento)</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div><div>
<div>Are you a highly detailed person who’s not concerned with heights and willing to perform work at altitudes of 300+ feet? Do you love working alongside highly skilled and well-trained tower climbers? Are you passionate about working for an organization that is safety conscious and also offers industry leading benefits, paid training, guaranteed hours and career advancement opportunities? Ericsson is that place, and we have an exciting opportunity for you in a professional, dynamic, growing environment.</div>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<div>
Our Exciting Opportunity:</div>
<div>
— Work on a team of tower climbers performing upgrades and repairs on cellular equipment mounted to various elevated structures such as guyed towers, lattice towers, water towers, monopoles and rooftops.</div>
<div>
— Use state of the art tools, equipment, and resources by joining the company that manufacturers the software and hardware you work on.</div>
<div>
— Become involved in a mentoring program to both continue learning new skills and teach out your talent to others.</div>
<div>
— Cross-train and seek career path opportunities as a crew foreman, ground tech, integrator, and more.</div>
<div>
— Influence performance and daily procedures by breaking down barriers with feedback and innovation from the field.</div>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<div>
You Have:</div>
<div>
— Tower climbing experience while performing troubleshooting, cellular radio and antenna swaps, legacy cable upgrades from coaxial to fiber, site upgrades, reading customer blueprints, and capacity expansions.</div>
<div>
— Expertise in producing closeout packages and completion of JSA/JHA forms.</div>
<div>
— A high level of proficiency with utilizing computers, Microsoft Office, and industry test equipment.</div>
<div>
— Excellent communication, organizational, and administrative skills while working as a great teammate.</div>
<div>
— A knowledge of networking principals, ethernet standards, power systems, and cable testing.</div>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<div>
You might also have:</div>
<div>
— Experience identifying potential add-on sales to increase company revenue.</div>
<div>
— Training to serve as a rescue climber if and when needed.</div>
<div>
— Completed an OHSA Construction 30-Hour training course.</div>
