<div><b>Role Summary/Purpose</b></div>
<div></div>
<div>Baker Hughes Remote Diagnostics Service team provides valve diagnostic services and digital product sales support for the process and energy industries. The Digital Service Leader will build and lead the F&amp;PT Diagnostic Service center. This newly created position will build a team that is focused on providing complete technical and commercial support for Baker Hughesâ€™ Diagnostics and Analytic services.</div>
<br>
<div></div>
<div><b>Essential Responsibilities</b></div>
<div></div>
<div>In the role of Digital Service Leader, you will:</div>
<ul>
<li>Setting the Digital Service strategy leveraging the global infrastructure and market requirements</li>
<li>
Work with Product Management and engineering to refine and optimize the diagnostic collection tools</li>
<li>
Build and train a team of valve diagnostic specialists to support remote valve diagnostics monitoring</li>
<li>
Develop tools and infrastructure to deliver valve diagnostics reports and associated spare parts recommendations quickly and cost effectively</li>
<li>
Monitor and own the Diagnostics Service center cost structure and ensure overall profitability</li>
<li>
Support Sales team presentations to new and existing customers on the benefits of Valve Lifecycle Management, digital valve positioners, valve diagnostic tools, and associated software</li>
<li>
End user support including equipment troubleshooting as well as software installation and configuration</li>
<li>
Support valve diagnostics services by creating/communicating valve fleet diagnostic reports to plant operators and reliability/maintenance teams</li>
<li>
Prepare and deliver training classes to internal and external audiences</li>
<li>
Represent the Company and products at Trade Shows and Technical Seminars</li>
<li>
Provide feedback to Corporate on market conditions, competitive issues, customer needs, and marketing/sales tool requirements</li>
<li>
Work closely with engineering to better refine/improve product offering</li>
</ul>
<div><b>Qualifications/Requirements</b></div>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor's Degree in Engineering from an accredited college or university</li>
<li>
Minimum 5 years of relevant experience in the Process Controls industry</li>
</ul>
<div><b>Desired Characteristics</b></div>
<ul>
<li>BS in Computer Systems, Chemical, or Process Control Engineering</li>
<li>
Minimum 5 years of relevant experience with Digital Valve Products and Valve Diagnostics</li>
<li>
Working knowledge of control valves and how they operation</li>
<li>
Detailed knowledge of control valve operations and applications in the Process Control Industries</li>
<li>
Strong grasp of the application of HART and Foundation Fieldbus Protocols and FDT/DTM Technology</li>
<li>
Working knowledge of asset management software: Emerson AMS, Yokogawa PRM, APM or similar systems</li>
<li>
Working knowledge of pneumatically operated valves and their failure modes</li>
<li>
Strong written and verbal communication skills, able to communicate clearly with a wide range of people from different professional, cultural and business backgrounds</li>
<li>
Able to present and discuss complex technical material in a concise yet meaningful manner to large groups</li>
<li>
Proven record of success in a results-driven environment</li>
<li>
Solid Microsoft Office knowledge is required</li>
<li>
Must be a self-starter and thrive with limited supervision while in the office or on the road</li>
<li>
