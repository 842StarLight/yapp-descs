<p>This position exists to provide the technical expertise to evaluate human health and environmental risks, review environmental investigations and determine appropriate soil, sediment, air and ground water cleanup goals and corrective actions at agricultural chemical incident sites and to provide related technical support to the department.</p>

<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Minimum Qualifications</b></h2>
<p>
A Bachelor’s degree in Hydrology, Geology, Hydrogeology, Soil Science, Water Resources, Engineering or closely related field AND two (2) courses in hydrogeology. Candidates must list courses on resume to qualify and all applicants who are invited to interview may be required to bring a copy of their college transcript(s) to the interview.</p>
<p>
Knowledge and ability to analyze and interpret complex technical data and reports and evaluate the need for additional actions at incident sites.</p>
<p>
The ability to understand and comply with complex sample collection, technical and administrative procedures.</p>
<p>
The ability to effectively represent MDA before a variety of groups relative to social, economic and environmental issues.</p>
<p>
Interpersonal skills to work effectively with colleagues and constituents, individually and in teams.</p>
<p>
Good organization, decision making and problem solving skills.</p>
<p>
Good working skills in Microsoft Excel, Outlook, PowerPoint, Word and geologic and environmental software.</p>
<p>
The ability to celebrate diversity and a strong commitment to a respectful and inclusive work environment.</p>
<p>
Must currently possess and maintain an active/valid driver’s license with an acceptable motor vehicle record subject to review.</p>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>
Preferred Qualifications</b></h2>
<p>
An advanced degree in Hydrology, Geology or Hydrogeology or closely related degree and extensive coursework in Hydrogeology, contaminant fate and transport and environmental chemistry.</p>
<p>
Knowledge of the Minnesota Ground Water Protection Act, state and federal Superfund regulations, the Land Recycling Act and an understanding of the interrelationships of these laws and how they apply to agricultural chemical incident response.</p>
<p>
Experience working with agricultural chemical environment cleanup sites and the Minnesota Environmental Response and Liability act sites.</p>
<p>
Knowledge of state and federal pesticide and fertilizer laws, rules and regulations.</p>
<p>
Knowledge of state and federal litigation procedures and processes and knowledge of MDA enforcement procedures.</p>
<p>
Direct experience (including farming experience) or significant training in the use and management of pesticides or inorganic fertilizers in an agricultural setting.</p>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>
Physical Requirements</b></h2>
<p>
This position also requires occasional work in heavy dust, around agricultural chemicals and entering buildings, warehouses, sales areas and cultivated fields to conduct inspections and investigations.</p>
<p>
This position must be able to drive five (5) hours a day, if needed. This position requires sometimes difficult, physically demanding labor, occasionally long work days, outdoor work in sometimes harsh conditions and strict attention to details and protocol. The incumbent must be able to independently collect samples from wells in any seasons and to collect soil and other samples. This requires driving and hiking to sampling locations, manually bailing wells, collecting samples and carrying them to a vehicle and the ability to lift up to 50 pounds.</p>

<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Why Work For Us</b></h2>
<p>
This position is an opportunity to help protect and improve Minnesota’s environment and enhance public health. This position exists to perform project management on sites contaminated with agricultural chemicals. Environmental investigations are conducted to determine the extent of contamination, and corrective actions are completed to reduce or eliminate the contamination. The work is challenging and requires persistence and creativity to meet cleanup goals and restore sites to productive use.</p>
