<h2>IT Cloud Specialist, MGC</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div></div>
<div>
<div>
<div>The Massachusetts Gaming Commission (MGC) is seeking to hire a Cloud Specialist. Under the direction of the Chief Information Officer (CIO), the Cloud Specialist is responsible for responding to, triage, and resolving or escalating customer IT issues. These responsibilities include implementing and supporting Office 365, Azure, SAN environments, virtual environments, Microsoft core business applications, firewalls, and networking. The incumbent must be self-motivated, process driven, highly customer focused, and has a strong desire to continuously improve at a personal and professional level.</div>
<br>
<div></div>
<div><b>Duties and responsibilities include, but are not limited to, the following:</b></div>
<br>
<div></div>
<ul><li>Implementation and support services for Microsoft related technologies: traditional Windows Server environments, Azure, Office 365, AD, SQL, SharePoint, Exchange, etc.</li></ul>
<ul><li>
IT support relating to technical issues involving Microsoft's core business applications, as well as virtual environments built on Citrix, Microsoft, and VMware</li></ul>
<ul><li>
Develop, document, and understand internal IT processes and procedures</li></ul>
<ul><li>
Maintain specific knowledge of the customer and how IT relates to their business strategy and goals</li></ul>
<ul><li>
Create and maintain system documentation to include system reviews and recommendations</li></ul>
<ul><li>
Communicate with customers to keep them informed of incident progress, and notify them of impending changes or agreed outages</li></ul>
<ul><li>
Support and troubleshoot basic network and firewall issues</li></ul>
<ul><li>
Monitor service boards, telephones, and emails and respond to all problems that arise with clients</li></ul>
<ul><li>
Achieve and maintain professional IT certifications, such as Microsoft MCSA or MCSE, HPE ASE or Master ASE, VMware VCP, Fortinet NSE</li></ul>
<ul><li>
Evaluate client opportunities and forward recommendations to sales team</li></ul>
It is the policy of the Massachusetts Gaming Commission and the Commonwealth of Massachusetts to afford equal employment opportunities to all qualified individuals, without regard to their race, color, ancestry, religion, sex, sexual orientation, national origin, age, physical or mental disability, citizenship status, veteran status, gender identity or expression, or any other characteristic or status that is protected by federal, state, or local law.<div>

The successful candidate will be required to pass an extensive background check that includes a full credit check, CORI, drug screen, and fingerprinting.<div>
The Massachusetts Gaming Commission is responsible for the implementation of the expanded gaming law (Chapter 194 of the Acts of 2011). Under the law, the Commission is tasked with establishing a regulatory framework for the solicitation, licensing, taxation, and oversight of a maximum of three casino licenses and one slots parlor license in Massachusetts.</div>
