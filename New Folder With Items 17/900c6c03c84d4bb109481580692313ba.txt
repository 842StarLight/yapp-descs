<h2>Regulatory Analyst - Energy Operations</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div>
<b>JOB SUMMARY:</b><br>
The Utility Rates &amp; Services Department performs a wide variety of analytical, research and administrative functions in the regulation of investor-owned electric, natural gas, water, and wastewater utilities. This highly analytical position will be responsible for assessing electric utilities' compliance with all state and federal laws, regulations and company policies. Candidates will coordinate, prepare and submit various reports, testimonies, and other correspondence to regulatory agencies with jurisdiction over utility facilities and operations, including, but not limited to, the Public Service Commission of South Carolina (Commission). This position has a direct impact on the customers of regulated utilities in the state of South Carolina.<br>
<ul>
<li>Prepare and assists in the preparation of testimony and exhibits for formal regulatory proceedings before the Public Service Commission of SC.
</li>
<li>Analyze and interpret state and federal regulatory legislation, proposals, and other relevant material.
</li>
<li>Develop cost recovery proposals and other analyses while ensuring accuracy and adherence to regulatory policies and guidelines.
</li>
<li>Perform prudency review with recommendations to Deputy Director, staff and other relevant agency management.
</li>
<li>Analysis of regulatory proceedings to include review, development and preparation of:</li>
</ul>
1) Testimony, reports, discovery responses;<br>
2) Cost of service studies and cost recovery proposals;<br>
3) Depreciation studies, forecasting expenses, revenues, and sales;<br>
4) Annual revenue requirements;<br>
5) Utility rate design;<br>
6) Utility financial reporting; and<br>
7) Utility research on various topics.<ul>
<li>Provide assistance in the monitoring of federal activity to determine impact on state regulations and policies and assist with special studies and reports.
</li>
<li>Coordinates and collaborates with ORS departments, outside agencies, utilities and various stakeholders; serves as a liaison with public and private organizations; provides information and serves as a resource.</li>
</ul>
<b>
EXPERIENCE/KNOWLEDGE/SKILLS:</b><ul>
<li>Ability to conduct independent research on complex issues involving policy, economic, financial or technical matters.
</li>
<li>Strong analytical and problem-solving skills.
</li>
<li>Ability to apply regulations, requirements and policies applicable to the subject matter or issue.
</li>
<li>Ability to write clear and concise memoranda, reports or other materials appropriate for various audiences.
</li>
<li>Ability to speak in a clear and concise manner appropriate to the audience. Excellent interpersonal, verbal and written skills.
</li>
<li>Ability to testify before the Public Service Commission of SC.
</li>
<li>Ability to manage time and meet applicable deadlines.
</li>
<li>Ability to develop and maintain cooperative and effective working relationships with various stakeholders.
</li>
<li>A self-starter with experience working in a fast-paced, multi-task environment requiring considerable attention to detail while conducting complex research and analysis.
</li>
<li>Confer with regulated utility representatives (executives, attorneys, operating personnel, etc.) and other external parties on issues related to electric utility regulation.
</li>
<li>Collaborate effectively with other staff, assisting other where needed, sharing knowledge, and being a team player.</li>
</ul>
</div>
