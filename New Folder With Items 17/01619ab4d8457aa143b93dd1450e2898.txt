<h2>Demand Planning Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div>
<p><b>Summary:</b></p>
<p>
As Demand Planner you are responsible to deliver a solid monthly Demand Plan for your region, built on the combination of the sales forecast, statistical forecasting practices and scenario planning. You are an ambitious professional in supply chain management; analytical, with a strong drive for continuous improvement and a passion to contribute to Navico’s global business results. Low in ego and high in energy level, you are at ease in your planning expertise, analyzing and challenging where needed, always concise and clear, and defending your case as a key member of a truly global team.</p>
<p><b>
ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES </b><b>INCLUDE THE FOLLOWING:</b></p>
<p>
You will collaborate with, and challenge where needed, your regional sales colleagues in their submission of the monthly sales forecast. Through a fixed cadence of different statistical forecasting and scenario planning steps as well as through analysis at your own initiative, you will combine these into the best possible monthly Demand Plan for your region.</p>
<p>
You will continuously conduct analysis to improve the predictability and accuracy of your monthly Demand Plan across sales trends, sales forecast accuracy, forecast consumption, statistical forecasts, sales channel POS, sales channel inventory, industry trends and other demand related data, and you will share these improvements with your colleagues in the global SIOP team.</p>
<p>
In support of the continuous launch of new Navico products and bridging between the regional sales team and the global NPI and EOL teams, you will secure the proper reflection of product launch- and end of life allocation requirements in your monthly Demand Plan.</p>
<p>
In addition to your regional duties, you will be the single point of contact and expert advisor for the Demand Planning of one of the three Navico brands, conducting consolidation, validation and analytical tasks.</p>
<p>
As member of the global SIOP team, you will act as initiator and collaborator in identifying and resolving SIOP process issues as well as in establishing new or improved SIOP business capabilities. This includes a key contributor role in the establishment of new Supply Chain planning and inventory management software and IT tools.</p>
<p>
You are a core member of the global SIOP team with shared responsibility for maintaining an entrepreneurial team spirit and highly professional working together relationships.</p>
<p><b>
Secondary functions:
</b></p>
<p>In addition to general office requirements and due to the global nature of Navico’s business, you will also do your work outside the normal business hours of your location, and you will be required to travel for about 20% of your time.</p>
<p><b>
EDUCATION, EXPERIENCE AND SKILLS REQUIRED</b></p>
<p>
This position requires a BSc level of education in a Business Administration, Finance or Supply Chain curriculum, preferable complemented with an APICS CPIM or equivalent certification.</p>
<p>
A minimum of 3 years of experience in a regional Supply Chain planning or program role is required, preferably in a global business in the High Tech sector.</p>
<p>
The successful candidate is competent, smart, analytical yet practical, and has excellent verbal and written communication skills in the English language.</p>
<p>
In the collaboration with his colleagues he or she brings a strong yet humble personality, is collaborative, eager to learn, open to admit mistakes, and always driving results and solutions.</p>
<p>
Experience with professional Supply Chain planning and inventory management software is a preferred qualification.</p>
<p>
Finally the successful candidate is accurate, precise, disciplined, organized, flexible, able to prioritize and a self-starter.</p>
