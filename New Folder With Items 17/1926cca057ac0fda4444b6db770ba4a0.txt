<h2>Seasonal Tax Sr. Manager, Tax Controversy Services – Service Center / Campus Account Analysis Group</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div>
<div>Seasonal Tax Sr. Manager, Tax Controversy Services – Service Center / Campus Account Analysis Group</div>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<div>
<b>
Position summary</b><br>
Are you a tax professional who wants to specialize in tax controversy for large global and dynamic midsize clients? You should consider a career in Deloitte’s Business Tax Services (Federal tax) practice – specializing in Tax Controversy.</div>
<div>
<br>
<b>
What you’ll do</b><br>
As a Sr. Tax Manager within the Tax Controversy group, you will be a valuable member on tax engagements. Responsibilities include:<br>
<ul>
<li>Compute interest computations regarding refunds and tax assessments.</li>
<li>Prepare and file claims for refund of interest.</li>
<li>Assist client and Deloitte engagement teams with recurring IRS account related matters.</li>
<li>Daily interaction with IRS Practitioner Priority Service.</li>
<li>Prepare written correspondence to IRS and Deloitte engagement teams regarding account resolution.</li>
<li>Interact with clients and Deloitte engagement teams via e-mail and telephone regarding IRS inquiries and resolution of IRS matters.</li>
<li>Coordinate services with Deloitte offices globally.</li>
<li>Provide support for the individual and business tax return compliance process.</li>
<li>Use internal technology to update/maintain client records and prepare reports.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div>
<br>
<b>
The Team</b><br>
Deloitte Tax LLP, Tax Controversy Services Group (“TCS”) provides responsive, innovative, and practical resolution of matters before the Internal Revenue Service (“IRS”). The TCS team utilizes their extensive range of skills, knowledge, and experience with IRS to assist clients through conflicts by supplementing and assisting IRS departments, legal counsel and other tax practitioners. TCS tax professionals help clients manage all of their global and domestic interactions with the IRS. Professionals are comprised of CPAs, Enrolled Agents, and para-professionals who work with clients to resolve matters such as audits, appeals, and IRS processing and customer service inquiries and procedural issues. These tax professionals come from various backgrounds including industry, private practice, and the IRS.</div>
<div>
We believe that experience is invaluable in the above situations. Our IRS Account Analysis specialists all have IRS experience. Each member of the group has an average of 25-plus years of experience in dealing with IRS tax controversy and each have the skills and knowledge to help clients work with IRS personnel on processing, procedural, and account related matters.</div>
<div>
We understand how IRS systems work, the documents and facts needed to address specific tax issues, methods to present those issues, and ways to resolve each tax problem. These qualities are essential for addressing the many types of tax and tax account situations companies encounter today.</div>
<div>
Our Interest Account Analysis professionals can advise in such areas as: Interest computations as it relates to Revenue Ruling 99-40 and Net Rate Netting claims. The professional should have a full working knowledge of Tax Interest Netting software or other comparable interest computation software. In addition to these duties, processing and monitoring Forms 1040, 1120, and 1041 refunds and tax assessments, determine qualifications for abatement of interest and penalties, and understand when special handling and monitoring of unique IRS account actions is required. These actions include, but are not limited to; management of accounts for payment application issues, misapplication of extension requests, Net Operating Loss Carryback Refund Applications, Form 911, Application for Taxpayer Assistance Order, and all Entity related matters.</div>
<div>
<br>
<b>
Qualifications:</b><br>
<b>Required</b><br>
<ul>
<li>Minimum 10 years of IRS Service Center/Campus experience in Accounts Management or Compliance Functions.</li>
<li>Bachelor’s degree preferred, but not required.</li>
<li>Minimum GS 9 Grade Level of experience.</li>
<li>Extensive working knowledge of the IRS Integrated Data Retrieval System (“IDRS”).</li>
<li>Ability to research and understand the IRS methods of account resolution, desk guides, and the Internal Revenue Manual (“IRM”).</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div>
<br>
<b>
Preferred</b><br>
<ul>
<li>Previous Big 4 or large CPA firm experience</li>
<li>Excellent research and writing skills</li>
<li>Professional demeanor and strong communication skills, both written and oral.</li>
<li>Must demonstrate the ability to research effectively, document case files, maintain inventory management, and have excellent organizational skills.</li>
<li>Ability to balance multiple assignments and other responsibilities.</li>
<li>Basic knowledge and use of Microsoft Outlook, Excel and Word.</li>
<li>Working knowledge of Tax Interest Netting or Decision Modeling software.</li>
</ul>
