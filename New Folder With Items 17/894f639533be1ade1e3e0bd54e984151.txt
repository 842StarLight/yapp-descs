<h2>Manufacturing Engineer II (3088)</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div><div>
<p>ITW Shakeproof has an opening for a Manufacturing Engineer II at our Elk Grove Village facility. ITW Shakeproof Automotive serves all the major Automotive OEMs and their tier suppliers with metal fasteners totaling approximately $170M in revenues. Our major plants are in Elgin IL, Machesney Park, IL, Elk Grove Village, IL, Darlington WI, and Bedford Heights, OH. Our Sales Center is in Troy, MI.</p>
<p><b>
Position Summary:</b></p>
<p>
Our Manufacturing Engineers are critical members of our Plant Core Teams, including the Plant Manager, Production Control, Manufacturing, Finance, Human Resources and Quality departments.</p>
<p><b>
Responsibilities:</b></p>
<p><i>
Improve manufacturing efficiency and solve production and quality issues</i></p>
<ul>
<li>
Use lean techniques to reduce waste and improve manufacturing performance.</li>
<li>
Work side by side with Operations Production to trouble-shoot manufacturing concerns.</li>
<li>
Support Quality in addressing and driving root cause analysis of customer concerns.</li>
<li>
Manage change for updates to engineering prints, standards or processes.</li>
</ul>
<p><i>
Continuous Improvement</i></p>
<ul>
<li>
Collaborate cross functionally with Production, Maintenance, Quality and Finance Departments to identify, monitor and continuously improve key performance drivers.</li>
<li>
Maintain and establish engineering standards.</li>
<li>
Establish and share best practices across all Plants.</li>
<li>
Collaborate across engineering teams throughout the Division.</li>
</ul>
<p><i>
 New Product Quotation, Feasibility &amp; Launch</i></p>
<ul>
<li>
Lead activities associated with New Product Launches (i.e. APQP in automotive) from quoting through the successful completion of quality records (such as PPAP) and launch.</li>
<li>
Drive timeline and resolution of issues with cross-functional team.</li>
<li>
Interface with the Sales, Engineering, Accounting Departments as well as vendors to gather or clarify information needed.</li>
<li>
Collaborate with manufacturing to develop manufacturing prints and establish controls and operational limits for operators.</li>
<li>
Create 2D and/or 3D models heading and rolling tools used in manufacturing of metal fasteners</li>
<li>
Create process routing for newly awarded business.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>
Required Qualifications:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Bachelor’s degree in Engineering</li>
<li>
Minimum 3 years’ experience in the manufacturing industry; product and/or tooling development for metal manufacturing (fastener) experience preferred.</li>
<li>
Experience in automotive industry is highly beneficial.</li>
<li>
Working knowledge of APQP or equivalent gated product development processes.</li>
<li>
Working knowledge of lean techniques.</li>
<li>
Equivalent experience with front office support to customer service, manufacturing or engineering.</li>
<li>
Strong experience with Microsoft Office software products.</li>
<li>
Strong experience with 2D and 3D CAD software; preferably SolidWorks and AutoCAD.</li>
<li>
Ability to read technical drawings and specifications &amp; a basic knowledge of metal manufacturing practices.</li>
<li>
Skilled in document management, organization and prioritization</li>
<li>
Ability to exercise discretion and independent judgment and show flexibility.</li>
<li>
Proven ability to deal with changes in a business environment.</li>
<li>
Aptitude to multi-task while maintaining highest levels of accuracy.</li>
<li>
Proven ability to focus and prioritize tasks and objectives.</li>
<li>
Excellent interpersonal skills with the willingness to communicate and work with other departments and disciplines.</li>
<li>
Proven ability to work on a team while generating respect and trust from staff as well as external customers.</li>
<li>
Personal accountability and inner drive to understand, communicate, and exceed established goals.</li>
</ul>
