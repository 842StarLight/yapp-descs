<div>The Medical Marijuana Bureau has primary responsibility for developing, implementing and maintaining Montana’s medical marijuana program for the safety, health and well-being of those we serve.</div>
<div></div>
<br>
<div>This position is responsible for planning and directing all activities of the Montana Medical Marijuana Program that inspects and certifies a broad range of medical marijuana providers and manages the issuance of medical marijuana cards to ensure that providers, labs and cardholders are compliant with state law and regulations. Responsibilities include interpreting and administering state and local laws within a federally unsupported program; providing consultation and assisting providers, labs and applicants in meeting state requirements, investigating and resolving complaints and maintaining the State medical marijuana licensing and seed-to-sale program. This position creates policy, makes interpretations and assures compliance with numerous laws and regulations for a statewide network of clients, providers, manufacturers, and testing facilities associated with the program.</div>
<div></div>
<br>
<div>
This position directly supervises 11 staff and indirectly supervised 18 employees.<br>
</div>
<div></div>
<br>
<div><b>Major Duties or Responsibilities:<br>
</b></div>
<div></div>
<br>
<div><b>Bureau Administration and Program Management</b></div>
<ul>
<li><div>Creates, plans and administers Bureau organizational structure, budget, and personnel requirements and operations policies.
</div></li>
<li><div>Proposes and drafts legislation and administrative rules needed to implement bureau objectives by communicating with the department director, division administration and the department legal staff. Represents the bureau before legislative committees in order to obtain and ensure sufficient statutory and budgetary support for bureau programs. Proposes and drafts regulations, and interpretive guidelines that implement statutory provisions.</div></li>
<li><div>
Represents the Bureau, Division or Department at meetings of advisory groups, provider and consumer organizations and other public meetings on order to present and explain programs, regulations, and interpretive guidelines. Receives suggestions and recommendations and elicits public support.</div></li>
<li><div>
Responds to departmental, legislative and public requests, questions and directives regarding operation of bureau programs. Assures all necessary bureau actions are completed by communicating and coordinating with bureau staff, division staff and other agencies.
</div></li>
<li><div>Establishes policy and makes determination of how the state program operates within the state and federal guidelines.
</div></li>
<li><div>Establishes, implements and maintains the program for issuing cardholder registration IDs; issuing licenses for medical marijuana providers, marijuana infused product providers, and testing facilities; and issuing endorsements for chemical manufacturing. Makes final decisions regarding license approval, licensure, and revocation and cardholder registration.</div></li>
<li><div>
Establishes, implements and maintains a program for testing requirements for medical marijuana products.</div></li>
<li><div>
Develops, implements, and monitors work plans, priorities, quality standards, and evaluations for performance of routine and complaint surveys, inspections, and evaluations conducted by all Bureau staff. Identifies weaknesses in the regulatory process and develops and implements corrective solutions.</div></li>
<li><div>
Oversees IT contracts and software development for seed-to-sale and licensing.
</div></li>
<li><div>Coordinates inspection activities with other State regulatory and reimbursement agencies.</div></li>
<li><div>
Coordinates as necessary with local law enforcement.
</div></li>
<li><div>Writes administrative rules, program policy and procedures; interprets state and regulations within statutory and department parameters.</div></li>
<li><div>
Ensures compliance with state and laws, regulations, and policies.</div></li>
<li><div>
Reviews and responds to state audit findings and recommendations
</div></li>
<li><div>Testifies and represents the department in court hearings and other administrative sessions.</div></li>
</ul>
<div><b>Supervision</b></div>
<ul>
<li><div>Develops job descriptions and bureau structure to accomplish the implementation of the medical marijuana program, monitors and adjusts to ensure effective program management.</div></li>
<li><div>
Provides mentoring, coaching, guidance, training, and support.
</div></li>
<li><div>Delegates special projects and establish priorities as necessary.
</div></li>
<li><div>Handles performance appraisals, hiring/interviewing, and constructive discipline.
</div></li>
<li><div>Ensures compliance with personnel rules, regulations, and policies.</div></li>
<li><div>
Conducts meetings and shares updates or procedural changes with staff.
</div></li>
</ul>
<div><b>Data Management
</b></div>
<ul>
<li><div>Ensures the provision of medical marijuana provider information to the Department of Revenue to facilitate the collection of tax revenue for medical marijuana.</div></li>
<li><div>
Coordinates with DOR in developing public service announcements and public education campaigns related to Montana’s medical marijuana and tax laws as needed.</div></li>
<li><div>
Ensures that necessary action, including possible revocation of a license, against a provider that has not met its tax requirements under the law.</div></li>
<li><div>
Establishes, implements and maintains IT solutions for tracking medical marijuana from seed to sale, and for the effective and efficient issuance of registrations and licenses.
</div></li>
</ul>
<div><b>Other Duties</b></div>
<ul>
<li><div>Assures conformance with laws and regulations regarding confidentiality of Bureau information.</div></li>
<li><div>
Has responsibility under Division Administrator, Deputy Administrator and Department Director to disseminate public information concerning providers and laboratories.
</div></li>
<li><div>Informs and assists the executive and legislative decision makers concerning legislation, rules, and appropriations that affect medical marijuana providers, patients and laboratories.</div></li>
</ul>
<div>
