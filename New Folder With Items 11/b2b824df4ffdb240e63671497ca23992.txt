<h2>Armor Project Engineer</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><b>PRIMARY EMPLOYMENT OBJECTIVES</b><br>
<br>
<b>Starts in Newport , VT - not to exceed 12 months - then going to Portsmouth, NH</b><br>
</p>
<p>
The Armor Project Engineer is responsible for detailed design of ballistic helmets and other composite components for soldier protection as well as the detailed design of tooling, equipment and process characteristics for helmet, mandible and applique manufacturing. The candidate will liaise with the Armor BU product team, Industrial Design, Sales/Business Development, Supply Chain Management and Revision manufacturing operations in order to design advanced ballistic personal protective gear for troops worldwide. The candidate will be responsible to coordinate Designs of Experiments (DOE’s), conduct testing and provide detailed reports. The candidate will liaise with the R&amp;D,product team, Industrial Design, Sales/Business Development, Supply Chain Management and Revision manufacturing operations in order to design advanced ballistic personal protective gear for troops worldwide.</p>
<p><b>
 </b><b>SPECIFIC ESSENTIAL DUTIES &amp; RESPONSIBILITIES</b></p>
<p>
This is a hands-on position to work closely with R&amp;D other ballistic engineers on Armor development projects with the following primary responsibilities:</p>
<ul>
<li>
Primary responsibility to conduct DOE’s for ballistic recipe design, collect data, analyze and report.</li>
<li>
Use software tools to conduct statistical analysis of performance and create performance/cost/weight comparisons.</li>
<li>
Gain understanding of fiber performance normalized against specific threats.</li>
<li>
Participate in cross-functional teams responsible for bringing new products and continuing products improvements.</li>
<li>
Interface with engineering and industrial design (internal and external) resources providing information, direction and prioritization of required features.</li>
<li>
Sourcing activities for new material and part acquisition.</li>
<li>
Develop prototypes with the Design team and the Operations group as a resource to support new product direction.</li>
<li>
Work with the Operations team to ensure that the needs of the customer are met while allowing for established manufacturing processes.</li>
<li>
Work closely with the Armor product development team to ensure alignment with product development and product pipeline goals.</li>
<li>
Ensure clean hand-off to the plant process engineers to ensure that the shell or other ballistic component developed is ready for production to include process controls, layup techniques, information for BOMs, plybooks, and Syteline cost set-up.</li>
<li>
Creation, review, and approval of drawings and other technical documentation.</li>
<li>
Recommend future improvements for improved processing techniques, processes, or equipment, and lead the technological effort for said improvements when deemed a program of record.</li>
<li>
Utilize Engineering background to suggest process or material changes that could yield lower production costs and or higher quality.</li>
<li>
Training and transfer of knowledge related to various manufacturing operations, techniques, and processes.</li>
<li>
Coordinate product testing with the Revision Testing Services, as well as external testing services.P</li>
<li>
Perform other duties as assigned.</li>
</ul>
