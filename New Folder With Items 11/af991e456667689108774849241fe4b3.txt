<h2>Regulatory Affairs (FDA and EU) Project Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>The Regulatory Affairs Project Manager will work with our consultants and clients in supporting projects involving the collection of clinical data in either phase I, II, III or IV clinical studies. In addition, the Regulatory Affairs Project Manager will provide training to clients and internal staff. This is a full-time position</p>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>
Overall Responsibilities:</b></h2>
<p>Provide overall management of FDA and EU regulatory strategy and submissions for clients to assure development and execution of regulatory strategies that achieve expeditious market introduction in compliance with all relevant domestic and international regulations.</p>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>
Position Duties and Responsibilities:</b></h2>
<p>Develop and implement efficient and effective regulatory strategies to bring products to market (domestic or international). All strategies must assure compliance to relevant regulations and standards for the intended market.</p>
<p>
Responsible for the timely completion of the following activities:</p>
<ul>
<li>
Design and manage testing of medical products to satisfy regulatory requirements</li>
<li>
Marketing submissions (510(k)’s, PMAs, CE Product Certifications, Design Dossiers, International Licenses, etc.)</li>
<li>
Creation and maintenance of Technical Files</li>
<li>
Review and approval of Labeling, Marketing, Sales Advertising and Promotional materials</li>
<li>
Identifying relevant standards for product development and manufacturing</li>
<li>
Review of Design History File to ensure Regulatory Compliance</li>
<li>
Interactions with representatives of U.S. and International Governmental Agencies and Notified Bodies</li>
<li>
Defining design control regulatory requirements for domestic and international markets</li>
<li>
Review and approval of Engineering Change Orders (ECOs)</li>
<li>
Assure positive relationship with U.S. and International Government Agencies and Notified Bodies</li>
<li>
Assure all communications with U.S. and International Government Agencies and Notified Bodies are documented and available for future reference</li>
<li>
Develop overall project plans for domestic and international submissions</li>
<li>
Assure all regulatory documents are complete and accurate</li>
<li>
Stay abreast of domestic and international regulatory requirements and standards</li>
<li>
Write departmental procedures and participate in training programs</li>
<li>
Design and conduct Training in areas of Good Engineering Practices, Protocol Design, Use of Statistical Techniques, Design Controls, Product Validation, Risk Management, Regulatory Requirements/Submissions, and so on</li>
<li>
Support special projects and other regulatory initiatives as assigned by management</li>
</ul>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>
Education &amp; Professional Experience:</b></h2>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor’s degree in engineering or science field, or similar work experience</li>
<li>
5 to 10 years experience in medical device, drug and/or biologics regulations (including premarket submissions for domestic and international markets)</li>
<li>
Previous supervisory or team leadership experience</li>
<li>
Knowledge of domestic and international medical device, drug and/or biologics regulations</li>
<li>
Strong attention to detail</li>
<li>
Strong computer skills, including word processing, spreadsheet and data programs</li>
<li>
Good oral and written communication skills</li>
<li>
Good leadership skills</li>
<li>
Ability to effectively manage multiple tasks while continuously assessing company and departmental priorities</li>
</ul>
