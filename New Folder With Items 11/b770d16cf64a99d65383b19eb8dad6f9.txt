<h2>LOAN ORIGINATOR ASSISTANT</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<p><b>GENERAL DESCRIPTION</b></p>
<p>
The Loan Originator Assistant (LOA) is responsible for supporting loan originators and operations staff in the mortgage origination process within assigned channel(s). Verify each loan submission meets FGMC’s requirements and moves through processing, underwriting, and closing in a timely manner. Respond to questions from borrowers and third-parties and maintain a high level of customer service. Work under immediate supervision and report to Manager, VP, or SVP.<br>
<br>
<b>ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Review applications from the loan originator for accuracy and completeness and input borrower information into the loan origination system (LOS)</li>
<li>Obtain, examine, and index necessary documents from the original loan file</li>
<li>Order standard loan requirements and verifications as needed (e.g. title, escrow, appraisal, verification of employment, etc.)</li>
<li>Verify and document that loan amounts meet loan requirements by using pre-programmed worksheets</li>
<li>Manage tasks and update LOS file in order to move the file to into the processing/underwriting phase of the loan process</li>
<li>Interact with all interested parties throughout the closing process to ensure a smooth transition of responsibilities from Sales, Processing, Underwriting, Compliance, and Closing</li>
<li>Manage a pipeline of loans and ensure pre-determined productivity and quality requirements are met</li>
<li>Communicate effectively with borrowers and third-parties by answering questions as needed</li>
<li>Ensure all pre-closing conditions have been met and schedule loan closings by effectively communicating with loan originators, underwriters, borrowers, and settlement agents</li>
<li>Review escrows, first payment date, application of refunds and credits, and cash to/from borrower to ensure loans meet FGMC requirements</li>
<li>Perform other duties as assigned</li>
</ul>
<p><i>
A Loan Originator Assistant may not perform any activities of a loan originator. By definition, the activities of a loan originator include: Taking a consumer credit and/or loan application; offering, arranging, or assisting a consumer in obtaining or applying to obtain a loan; and negotiating, or otherwise obtaining or making an extension of consumer credit for another person. 12 C.F.R. § 1026.36(f). A loan originator assistant may not, through advertising or other means of communication, represent to the public that he or she can or will perform any of these activities. While a loan originator assistant is prohibited from taking a consumer credit application or offering or negotiating available credit terms, he or she may be permitted to perform purely administrative or clerical tasks on behalf of a person who does engage in such activities as a loan originator.</i><br>
</p>
<p><b>
KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS, AND ABILITIES</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Excellent verbal, written, and interpersonal communication skills</li>
<li>Ability to problem-solve and develop effective and constructive solutions to challenges and obstacles</li>
<li>Ability to interface with other departments to resolve issues as applicable</li>
<li>Ability to maintain a customer focus and efficiently and consistently produce accurate work in both individual and team settings</li>
<li>Solid knowledge of computer systems including MS Office (Word, Excel, and Outlook)</li>
</ul>
<p><b>
REQUIRED QUALIFICATIONS</b>
</p>
<ul>
<li>High school diploma or GED</li>
<li>2+ years of work experience in a sales/financial environment that includes experience with real estate loans and/or customer service</li>
<li>Knowledge of residential loan documents, lending practices, and regulations applicable to loan programs, closings, and real estate transfers</li>
<li>1+ years of mortgage industry experience</li>
<li>1+ years of customer service experience</li>
</ul>
