<div><b>Position Summary</b></div>
</div>
<br>
<div>
<div>The Business Manager shall provide direct management, general oversight, or consultation to the Corps and Family Services/Food Bank in the areas of fiscal responsibility, labor &amp; industry compliance, donor accountability, business ethics, human resource utilization, property and equipment assuring the outstanding quality of stewardship and accountability are maintained within FASB and Salvation Army accounting and business requirements.</div>

<div><b>Essential Functions</b></div>
</div>
<br>
<div><div>
<b>The Business Manager is, under the direction of the Officer(s) in Charge, responsible for the following:</b>
<ul>
<li>The day-to-day business operations of the Renton Corps and Community Services to guarantee that finances and all business related items are in place and maintained so that, the delivery of service to individuals and families in need of assistance through the emergency and family services office, the food bank or seasonal assistance programs.</li>
<li>Oversee to guarantee that financial operations of the Corps and Family Services/Food Bank are done in a timely and efficient manner:</li>
<li>Payroll, Income, Deposits, Receivables, Payables, GIK, Contracts, Kettle Counting, Grant Management and Reporting, Donor Relations, Grants, Budget Management &amp; Development of Budget</li>
 </ul>
<br>
<b>Human Resources</b>
<ul>
<li>Responsible for all Human Resources of the Corps and Family Services/Food Bank, adhering to all Wage and Hour regulations in hiring and payroll</li>
<li>Oversee and support Officers with all employee evaluations for timeliness and disciplinary procedures in compliance with Divisional Headquarters Human Resource department</li>
<li>Responsible for all background checks for staff</li>
<li>The Business Manager will be expected to participate in staff meetings.</li>
</ul>
<br>
<b>Finance-Budget</b>
<ul>
<li>The Business Manager will be responsible to assist the Officer in Charge with the budgeting process, for all programs.</li>
<li>The Business Manager will be expected to keep an accurate record of all funding received and disbursed and to complete monthly reports to Divisional Headquarters (DHQ) and accounting.</li>
<li>The Business Manager is responsible for the proper recording and reporting of in-kind donations in collaboration with Community Programs Manager.</li>
<li>Provide leadership supporting all stewardship for Corps programs</li>
<li>Oversee Kettle Counting and provide supervisory leadership to Kettle staff with regards to all seasonal income and deposits</li>
 </ul>
<br>
<b>Social Services</b>
<ul>
<li>Oversee all aspects of Social Service operations: Food Bank, Financial Assistance, Meals, Showers, all other</li>
 </ul>
<br>
<b>Safety-Reports-Records</b>
<ul>
<li>The Business Manager is responsible for all OSHA (Occupational Safety &amp; Health Administration) reporting and recordkeeping. Divisional Headquarters and the Officer in Charge should be made aware immediately in the event of any accident involving employees or the general public on property owned by The Salvation Army. Care should be taken to maintain adequate incident reports in accord with OSHA requirements.</li>
</ul>Compile and complete monthly food bank reports as required by our Emergency Food Assistance Program (EFAP) and United States Department of Agriculture (USDA) contracts and give to the appropriate person. Responsible for all aspects of reporting and seasonal statistics with Salvation Army and Local Food Distribution entities( NW Harvest, So King County Food Coalition, Life-Line).
<ul>
<li>Facility upkeep and record keeping (includes telephones and IT issues)</li>
<li>Keep and maintain a copy of all transportation and vehicle related records</li>
<li>Property Upkeep and maintenance contracts and contractors</li>
<li>Oversee these areas of operations - Office Supplies, Inventory, Supplies and Purchasing</li>
<li>Collaborate with Corps Officer(s), Community Programs Manager, and Warehouse Manager in regards to annual training plans for The Salvation Army Renton Rotary Food Bank and Social Services Center staff and volunteers, and to provide intra-agency and interagency trainings at the request of the Corps Officers</li>
</ul>
<br>
<b>Community Relations &amp; Fundraising:</b>
<ul>
<li>Responsible, in consultation with the Officer(s) in Charge and the Advisory Council, to work towards the development and implementation and funding of new programs to meet the perceived needs in King County.</li>
<li>Responsible, in tandem with the Officer(s) in Charge and the Advisory Council, to arrange for an annual Open House to be held at the corps and social service building to promote the work of social services and other Salvation Army programs.</li>
<li>Follow through and manage reporting and accounting for social service and other program grants.</li>
<li>Responsible to monitor service contract requirements and progress on contracted services, to initiate the renewal of existing service contracts, to identify additional service contract funding opportunities, to strategize responses with the Officers in Charge and prepare contract grant proposals for their consideration.</li>
<li>Serve as Salvation Army’s primary communication representative with Advisory Council</li>
</ul>
