<h2>Associate Product Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<p><b>About the job</b></p>
<p>
The Associate Product Manager is a key member of the Product Management team for our Work and Asset Management product suite, with responsibility for TheWorxHub solution and shared platform functionality. You’ll work closely with our agile teams and engineering partners to define functional product requirements, while managing priorities in accordance with key delivery milestones for releases. Primary responsibilities include serving as the product expert to improve the functionality of the product using client feedback as well as address both internal and external issues.</p>
<p>
This business line includes products that help hospitals, senior living and other healthcare organizations manage their costs, maintain compliance with code requirements, leverage technology to operate more efficiently and improve the user experience of their requesters that engage with them through Dude products and solutions. Our products are focused on meeting the needs of communities across the country using technology to support new/existing use cases, expanding third party integrations and improving user experience and visual design.</p>
<p><b>
What you’ll be doing</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Become a subject matter expert in relevant product domains, and develop an understanding of target markets and users, to guide product decisions</li>
<li>
Monitor activity from compliance bodies to ensure the products comply with the most recent compliance requirements</li>
<li>
Educate stakeholders from other departments on industry compliance issues and how our products help our clients address those issues</li>
<li>
Serve in the role of Product Owner in an agile development environment</li>
<li>
Work closely with the rest of the Product Management and UX team to decompose the market requirements into detailed functional product requirements (i.e., user stories and acceptance criteria) that enable engineering to build it and QA to test it in an Agile environment</li>
<li>
Agile team member responsible for prioritized stories in the product backlog for the team, participates in team planning meetings, and is available to provide advice, clarification, &amp; guidance during the execution of each Sprint</li>
<li>
Responsible for communicating priorities and goals at beginning of each sprint in accordance with release goals, roadmap, and associated timelines / dependencies</li>
<li>
Serve as the champion of the customer experience and partner with UX/design, Product counterparts, Sales, and Professional Services to deliver delightful user experiences across all customer touchpoints</li>
<li>
Establish and foster key relationships with clients and domain experts to meet market needs</li>
</ul>
<p><b>
What does success look like?</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
<b>
Collaborate with business stakeholders:</b> You actively listen to strategy and needs from colleagues in sales, professional services, and management roles. You transform those needs into product designs, discrete features and requirements (while not losing sight of the higher priority market needs), and then keep the teams informed and involved throughout the product development process with excellent communication skills.</li>
<li>
<b>
Design for users: </b>You communicate directly and regularly with internal and external stakeholders to fully understand their workflows, mindsets, and experiences using our products. You serve as a voice of the customer, incorporating client feedback into requirements, designs, and feature roadmaps. You work closely with your User Experience and Design counterparts to deliver a high-quality user experience.</li>
<li>
<b>
Work well with software engineers: </b>While you might not have programming experience, you are savvy with technology, understanding databases, mobile applications, cloud-based software, web development, etc. You are not afraid to have interactive discussions with your Engineering counterparts to ensure that short- and long-term roadmap requirements are achieved.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>
What you need</b></p>
<p><b>
Qualifications &amp; Experience</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
The successful candidate will possess 2 years minimum of experience working in a healthcare facilities or biomedical capacity</li>
<li>
Experience with Asset Management, Inventory Management, Procurement Management, Facilities Management, Clinical Engineering or Operational software a plus</li>
<li>
Strong evidence of keeping commitments and delivering results</li>
<li>
Demonstrated ability to interpret and analyze data to support decision making</li>
<li>
Experienced at managing complex projects with multiple stakeholders and communicating project statuses throughout the business</li>
<li>
Superb communication skills for both oral and written communication; ability to communicate confidently across all levels in the organization.</li>
<li>
Bachelor’s Degree in business, Computer Science or other analytical or business acumen-based field</li>
<li>
Ability and willingness to travel up to 5% of the time, within the US</li>
</ul>
<p><b>
Behavioral Competencies</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
<b>
Entrepreneurial</b>: High energy level, sense of urgency and intellectual curiosity, confident, thorough, not afraid to make decisions, oriented toward practical solutions. Passion for developing great products!</li>
<li>
<b>
Self-confidence: </b>Confident, without an ego, to have both the conviction and willingness to make decisions as well as the confidence to seek collaborative solutions</li>
<li>
<b>
Strong work ethic</b>: Ability to overcome setbacks and enthusiastically persist until ambitious goals are achieved—must be resourceful, creative and innovative.</li>
<li>
<b>
Accountable: </b>Results-oriented team player who leads by example, holds self-accountable for performance, takes ownership, and champions efforts with enthusiasm and conviction.</li>
<li>
<b>
Great attitude: </b>Scrappy, resourceful, collaborative, adaptable, fun, solid sense of humor, and able to influence others.</li>
</ul>

Be the Dude</b></p>
