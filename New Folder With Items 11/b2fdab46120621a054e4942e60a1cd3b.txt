<div><b>Duties Description </b></div>
<div>The Desktop Engineer will report to the Information Technology department under the supervision of the EIS manager. The Desktop Engineer will function in a dual role: 1) supporting the Auxiliary Service Corporation’s Campus Card technical requirements, and 2) providing ITS department support. The Desktop Engineer will support various technologies in the Instructional Technology area and production environments at SUNY Old Westbury. This is a full-time position with occasional evening hours and weekends.<br>
Responsibilities:<br>
<ul>
<li>Support and troubleshoot Campus Card hardware/software, Point of Sale, PAX Terminals, Light Networking, Laundry Readers/Controllers for ASC and integrated partners. Also be on-call for card system interruptions.</li>
<li>Assist faculty, staff, and students with the installation and maintenance of desktop/laptop and related hardware/software.</li>
<li>Install and operate computer lab equipment for academic, administrative, and students.</li>
<li>Maintain, analyze, troubleshoot, and install Information technology systems, hardware and computer peripherals, including but not limited to networking equipment, point of sale devices, printers, mobile devices (laptops, iPhone, iPad, Android, etc.).</li>
<li>Maintain computer desktop and laptop systems inventories and repair records.</li>
<li>Process and track service desk repair and maintenance requests. Document failures of workstation, and peripheral equipment and resolutions.</li>
<li>Assist in scheduling and overseeing service desk student employees.</li>
<li>Troubleshoot alongside vendor technical support on enterprise solutions.</li>
<li>Maintain contact with vendors through their ticketing system and phone support.</li>
<li>Manage backups and system/network access of certain campus systems.</li>
</ul>
