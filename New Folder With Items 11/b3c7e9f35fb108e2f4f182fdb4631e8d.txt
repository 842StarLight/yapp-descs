<br>
The University of Tennessee Knoxville (UTK) is seeking an Associate Director or Director of Gift Planning at the University of Tennessee Foundation, Inc. The Associate Director or Director of Gift Planning is a key member of the University of Tennessee, Knoxville’s development team. The position is responsible for the identification, cultivation, solicitation and stewardship of planned gifts that will benefit colleges and units across the UTK campus. The position advises and collaborates with development staff from all colleges and units in the design of strategies and gift plans. The position will also provide information and assistance to estate and tax planning professionals (attorneys, CPAs, financial planners, stock brokers, life insurance agents and bank trust officers) about UTK programs and funding needs.<br>
<br>
The Associate Director or Director of Gift Planning will secure significant funding that will assist students and faculty across campus in achieving their full potential. The Associate Director of Gift Planning is responsible for annually visiting a minimum of 180 alumni and/or potential donors, delivering 30+ written proposals and raising $7,5000,000 plus per year. The Director of Gift Planning is responsible for annually visiting a minimum of 180 alumni and/or potential donors, delivering 45+ written proposals and raising $10,000,000 plus per year.<br>

<b>
Qualifications</b><div>
</div>
<b>
Qualifications:</b><div>
Bachelor’s degree required. Graduate degree/Professional degree preferred. 3 plus years of planned giving or related experience (sales, law, financial planning, or direct fund raising) is required for the Associate Director level. 5 plus years for Director level.<br>
<br>
<b>Job Skills:
</b>
</div>
<div>The ideal candidate will have knowledge of planned giving strategies and techniques (Charitable Remainder Trusts, Charitable Lead Trusts, Gift Annuities, Remainder Interests in Residences and Farms, Pooled Income Fund, Life Insurance, Retirement Plan Beneficiary Designations, TOD/POD Designations, Bequest Expectancies, Marketable Securities, Real Estate, Closely Held Securities, Tangible Personal Property), probate processes and administration of planned giving assets.<br>
<br>
Must have excellent telephone manner, strong verbal and written communication and capacity to build strong relationships with donors and prospects. Must display sensitivity to diversity and multicultural issues.
<br>
<br>
Ability and willingness to engage in extensive travel, sometimes overnight.<br>
<br>
Experience using planned giving software such as Crescendo or PG Calc, as well as Microsoft Word, Excel and PowerPoint is preferred. Knowledge of ANDI software is preferred. Strong analytical ability, attention to detail, organizational skills and facility for mathematical and financial concepts required. Must possess excellent time management skills, flexibility in daily responsibilities and ability to manage multiple and complex projects simultaneously.<br>
