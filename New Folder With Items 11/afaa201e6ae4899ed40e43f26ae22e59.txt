<h2>Senior Security Supervisor - Columbus, OH</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div></div>
<div>
<div>
<p>Job Summary</p>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<p>
The Sr. Security Supervisor should possess the skills and abilities to coach and train supervisors and officers on team development including but not limited to: Control room procedures, emergency procedures, MDO, progressive Corrective Action, certified to teach CPR/1st aid. A Security Sr. Supervisor, in addition to leading the teams who directly report to them, is a SME (Subject Matter Expert) whose expertise is used across the Retail Safety Services LOB to address departmental issues, create, revise, implement procedures, and leads retail wide initiatives/projects. The Sr. Security Supervisor may have enterprise oversight of teams in multiple locations.<br>
</p>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<p><b>Reports to</b></p>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<p>
Manager or Sr Manager</p>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<p><b>
Essential Job Functions</b></p>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<p>
Leadership Development - Leads, coaches, and develops supervisors and officers to ensure they are prepared and can perform all tasks and responsibilities required of their position to ensure Alliance Data is a safe and secure work environment. Specifically, hire and develop team members to not only appropriately perform the core responsibilities of an officer but additionally develop/execute supervisors and officers development plans, succession plans, work reviews, annual appraisals, associate satisfaction plans, and coaching. Administers progressive corrective action of officers as needed. Prepares and performs department communications to include procedure updates, memos and manages department meetings.</p>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<p>
Life Safety - Leads in the effort to ensure retail LOB life/safety procedures are current (Security Policy and Procedure Manual) and develop and implement new policies and procedures. Ensure security officers across the retail LOB are appropriately prepared to react to emergency events, lessen the impact of these events, and/or prevent the likelihood of such events from occurring. Assist business continuity department in handling mock scenarios and learn from these to implement corrective procedure/actions. These mock scenarios include selecting and training wardens ensuring they know how to respond in an emergency situation and documenting all appropriate trainings for the members.</p>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<p>
Technology - Properly maintain and keep all life/safety systems (CCTV, Access Control/Intrusion Alarms Systems, phone systems/cell phones, AED and Oxygen units, etc.) to current technology to lessen the potential risk and compliment documented procedure in the event of an emergency incident. Work with vendors across the Retail LOB when issues arise at the facilities and execute a solution.</p>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<p>
Administrative - Day to day administrative responsibilities to include but not limited to; control room, staffing schedules, review/storage of departmental documents/logs, review, route, and store incident reports, ensures PMs and inspections of systems, assets, and equipment are completed/recorded, ensures all office supplies, medical supplies, etc. are at correct inventories and ordered as needed, system generated reports and requisitions are routed, etc.</p>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<p>
Financial - Assist in the day to day to management of security budget and procurement.</p>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<p>
Regulatory - Leads in the effort to collect, provide, and manage all requested information/documentation for regulatory compliance. These audits include (SSAE16, SOX, PCI, etc.) and Retail/Corp internal compliance (Door Owner Policy, NPPI Policy / Audits, Corp Physical Security Verification Audits, etc.).</p>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<p>
Leadership - May have enterprise ownership of a remote facilities security team. Act as a SME when the Security Manager is not available.</p>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<p>
Must have the ability to respond quickly to emergency, medical and security events anywhere on company premises, including but not limited to buildings and parking lots. The Senior Security Supervisor must be able to respond to medical incidents with first aid bags and other equipment, carrying injured individuals(s) as required.</p>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<div><b>
Education/Certifications</b></div>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<p>
High School Diploma or GED required. Bachelor's Degree in Criminal Justice, Criminology, Homeland Security, Leadership, Management, Business or Security related field preferred.</p>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<p><b>
Work Experience</b></p>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<p>
6-8 years of experience leading teams of 10 or more people and experience preferably in either a corporate operations setting or a proprietary security group. Prefer candidates with experience in either a Corporate Headquarters or Customer Care environment within the Financial Service Industry.</p>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<div><b>
Knowledge, Skills and Abilities</b></div>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<div>
<div>First Responder and/or CPR AED trainer certified</div>
<div>Ability to use computer software to complete reports, analysis, memos, e-mails, policy and procedures, schedules, etc. Ability to communicate with all levels of associates, management, and vendors</div>
<div>Proven ability to coach and develop subordinates and constructively evaluate performance to drive results</div>
<div>Conduct training both one on one and class room environments</div>
<div>Proven ability to coach and develop/complete and administer performance evaluations</div>
<div>Strong decision making skills to quickly determine an action and execute that action during an emergency incident</div>
<div>Interpersonal skills to provide superior service to our associates and build rapport with their immediate team and facility management</div>
<div>Ability to interface and work with city emergency personnel, vendors, visitors, and auditors</div>
<div>Ability to manage and multi task projects, incidents, and facilities in correct prioritization by procedure</div>
<div>Ability to train other supervisors on the essential job duties, even in remote facilities</div>
<p>Ability to develop procedures, budgets, facility communications, training documents, etc.</p>

<p><b>Working Conditions/Physical Requirements</b></p>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<p>
This position may lead staff in a typical office environment, customer care center or production environment. The Sr. Security Supervisor is expected to "lead by walking around" and will spend a good amount of time on their feet interfacing with their associates and others. May be called upon to work outside, in inclement weather or other situations during special events. Sr. Security Supervisors are expected to be able to respond quickly to security related events and medical incidents, lift or carry equipment, participate in defensive tactics/personal protection training and numerous other physically demanding tasks. The physical demands described below are representative of those that must be met by an associate to successfully perform the essential responsibilities and functions of the job and are not meant to be all inclusive. Reasonable accommodation may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential responsibilities and functions of the job if providing such accommodation does not result in an undue hardship to the company or eliminate an essential function of the job, including the physical demand requirements listed below, which are essential functions.</p>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<p>
Bending, Climbing, Kneeling, Reaching, Sitting, Standing, Walking, Lifting (Up to 50 lbs.), Vehicle operation.</p>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<p>
Must be able to obtain and/or maintain CPR/First Aid certification within 30 days of employment to be able to appropriately respond to emergencies. This role requires special certification to be able to teach CPR/First Aid.</p>
