<h2>Oil and Gas Specialist I</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><b>SCOPE/CONTACTS:</b> Bank contacts include officers and employees; outside Bank contacts include public accounting firms, trust accounting software vendors, and other requesting information concerning trust activities. Provide technical and administrative support to the land manager and other designated personnel. Assist in preparing oil and gas contracts, leases, assignments, and other related duties.</p>
<p><b>
MINIMUM QUALIFICATIONS:</b> Bachelor’s Degree and 1-3 years of related experience in an administrative support role. Proficient with standard Microsoft Office applications, including Word, Excel and Outlook. Ability to perform tasks with minimal supervision, work with decimal and fractions. Excellent oral and written communication skills. Must have a high degree of accuracy. Knowledge of computerized accounting systems and PCs, including spreadsheets and word processing. Preferred to have knowledge of oil and gas industry.</p>
<p><b>
ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS:</b> Assist with the timely execution of oil and gas contracts, and other mineral related documents. Assist with the setup and removal of assets on oil and gas software platform. Provide support in title requirement research. Locate and research mineral files as directed by Oil &amp; Gas Manager. Ability to prepare detailed custom spreadsheets and reports for internal audit and annual reviews. Ability to draft certified letters to be sent on behalf of Oil &amp; Gas Manager. Ability to interact with clients and/or industry professionals via telephone and email. Assist with the preparation for Ad Valorem tax payments. Research AFE requests and prepare appropriate consent letters for Oil &amp; Gas Manager. Consequence of error: loss of income, loss of customer confidence and account, criticism by internal/external auditors and bank examiners, and possible litigation. Actively participate in the sales and service culture, support the values of organization and follow established bank policies and procedures.</p>
