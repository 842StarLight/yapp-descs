<h2>Communications Coordinator</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p><b>COMMUNICATIONS COORDINATOR (FULL-TIME / NON EXEMPT) </b></p>
<p>The Communications Coordinator reports to and will work closely with the Director of<br>Philanthropy and Visibility and the Development team to successfully implement marketing communication strategies and plans.</p>
<p><b>Responsibilities: </b></p>
<p> Draft and distribute press releases and calendar listings.<br> Organize, maintain and update media lists.<br> Coordinate social media postings with relevant staff. Monitor and report social media analytics.<br> Coordinate and process updates to web site. Review and report website statistics.<br> Respond to inquiries from media for institutional program and event information, drafting copy and supplying images.<br> Draft E-news and E-blasts, editing and/or drafting copy and designing in Mail Chimp.</p>
<p>Develop quarterly calendar for staff submissions.<br> Organize and manage digital image files for marketing and program use<br> Design, produce and distribute printed promotional materials. Support Director of</p>
<p>Philanthropy and Visibility in overseeing outside graphic designers<br> Work with outside graphic designers; supplying copy drafts and image choices; negotiating placements with vendors; researching opportunities)<br> Manage rack card and brochure distribution by direct mail, distribution services and brochure/poster drops.<br> Process marketing accounts payable; review budget monthly for variances<br> Promote and manage group sales<br> Coordinate special projects and perform additional duties as assigned</p>
<p><b>Qualifications: </b><br> Minimum 2 years of Communications experience required.<br> B.A. or equivalent required<br> Excellent writing and verbal skills<br> Highly motivated, results-oriented self-starter, enthusiastic to take initiative and work independently and as part of a team<br> Proactive in planning and organizing projects<br> Excellent computer skills. Proficiency in MS Office and Mac; familiarity with graphic design and image software<br> Ability to operate a digital still and video camera, download and edit images and video files.<br> Demonstrated ability to deal with multiple audiences, including the media, colleagues, trustees and members of the public<br> High standards of credibility, integrity, reliability and confidentiality.<br> Commitment to the mission and goals of the Harriet Beecher Stowe Center</p>
<p>Applicant should submit cover letter, resume and three professional references to Indeed addressed to Melissa Combs, Director of Philanthropy and Visibility.</p>
<p>Applicants are subject to a background check. Review of applications begins immediately and continues until position is filled. Applicant reviews will begin after November 15th.</p>
