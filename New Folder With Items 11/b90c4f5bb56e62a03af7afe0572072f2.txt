<h2>Chief Financial Officer (CFO)</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p>Chief Financial Officer<br>The Chief Financial Officer (the “CFO”) partners with the leadership team at Katayama American<br>Company, Incorporated (“KACI” or the “Company”) to provide guidance and support that enable<br>the Company to move towards achieving its targets. The role’s primary day-to-day responsibilities<br>include planning, implementing, managing and controlling all accounting and finance related<br>activities at KACI. This includes direct responsibility for accounting, finance, forecasting, strategic<br>financial planning and budgeting, compliance, and managing financial supplier relationships. The<br>CFO effectively articulates company strategy and financial performance to team members and<br>investors.<br>Strategy Planning and Management<br> Provide leadership in the facilitation of corporate goals, governance, and to act as a<br>sounding board for the executive management team.<br> Assess and evaluate financial performance of the organization with regard to long-term<br>operational goals, budgets, and forecasts.<br> Engage all relevant members of the leadership team to align financial management with<br>short and long term financial planning and projections. Work successfully across functional<br>groups to achieve key operational goals that have financial implications.<br> Identify and implement process and systems to provide critical financial and operational<br>information to manage, enable, guide, and scale an emerging business.<br> Establish key financial objectives that align with the Company’s plan for growth and<br>expansion.<br> Experienced in decision leadership: a keen ability to combine forecasting, modeling, and<br>strong business acumen to create if/then scenarios for each part of the business, and to<br>advise the CEO and relevant members of the leadership team in big picture company<br>decisions.<br> Assist Sales Department in preparing quotes for OEM customers by accurately capturing<br>various cost items in quotation processes<br> Collaborate with Japanese counter-parts in the head-quarter on financial-related matters,<br>such as transfer pricing, royalty calculation, and tooling transfer.<br>Financial Analysis, Budgeting, and Forecasting<br> Prepare monthly financial reports by the 7th day of the following months.<br> Review and analyze monthly financial performance and make recommendations to KACI’s<br>leadership team at monthly management meetings scheduled in the 3rd week of the<br>following months.<br> Identify, develop and execute analysis and forecasts of business initiatives, product<br>launches and/or new service offerings.<br> Develop and maintain monthly operating budget and annual company operating budget.<br>Annual operating and financial budgets are finalized by the end of November and<br>presented to the Board for approval in December.<br>Accounting, General Ledger, Administration and Operations<br> Oversee accounting to ensure the proper functioning of all systems, databases and<br>financial software. Provide regular maintenance and backup of all accounting systems and<br>supervise company financial staff.<br> Review and ensure application of appropriate internal controls, compliance and financial<br>procedures.<br> Ensure timeliness and accuracy of financial and management reporting data for leadership<br>and investors.<br> Oversee the preparation and communication of weekly, monthly and annual financial<br>statements.<br> Oversee the preparation and timely filing of all local, state and federal tax returns.<br> Oversee the month-end close process (including general ledger accounts, balance sheet,<br>and overhead cost allocation) and constantly review procedures to eliminate inefficiencies.<br> Enhance and implement financial and accounting systems, processes, tools and control<br>systems.<br> Hire, develop and manage accounting team members as needed.<br> Serve as a key point of contact for external auditors. Manage preparation and support of<br>all external audits.<br> Understand currency implications of international business and hedge strategies.<br>Financial Management<br> Manage cash flow planning process.<br> Oversee cash, investments and asset management area.<br> Explore new investment opportunities and provide recommendations on potential returns<br>and risks.<br> Maintain outstanding banking relationships and strategic alliances with vendors and<br>business partners.<br> Utilize forward-looking models and activity-based analyses to provide financial insight into<br>the organization's plans and operating budgets.<br> Remain current on audit best practices as well as state, federal and local laws regarding<br>company operations.<br>Finance and Accounting Team Management<br> Proven experience building, mentoring, motivating, and leading teams. Ability to effectively<br>tap into the strengths and compensate for the weaknesses of individuals in each of the<br>financial roles.<br> Manage work allocation, systems training, performance evaluations, and the building of<br>an effective and efficient team dynamic.<br> Maintains strong network and keeps up with trends to bring best practices to team and<br>business.<br> Provide technical financial advice and knowledge to all team members.<br>Cash Management<br> Oversee weekly cash management.<br> Supervise Accounts Receivable management and provide guidance relating to the<br>collection process.<br> Develop a reliable cash flow projection process and reporting mechanism that includes<br>minimum cash threshold to meet operating needs.<br> Negotiate and manage payment processing relationships.</p>
