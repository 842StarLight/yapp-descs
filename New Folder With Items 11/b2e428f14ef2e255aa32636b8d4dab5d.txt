<h2>Patron Services Representative – Part Time Teleservices</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<div><div>
<p>Job Title: Patron Services Representative – Part Time Teleservices
</p>
<p></p>
<p>Department: Patron Services
</p>
<p></p>
<p>Reports to: Teleservices Manager
</p>
<p></p>
<p>Supervision Exercised: N/A
</p>
<p></p>
<p>FLSA Status: Part Time, hourly, non-exempt
</p>
<p></p>
<p>SUMMARY: Serves as front-line representative of the Oregon Symphony to the public, generating
</p>
<p>revenue through outbound sales and fundraising calls.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES: Other duties may be assigned.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>1. Sell Oregon Symphony (OSA) concert subscriptions, tickets and memberships by phone at the<br>
facility provided by the OSA, from leads furnished by the OSA.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>2. Develop and be able to utilize a detailed working knowledge of concert event information,<br>
program content, target audience profiles, membership benefits and donor events.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>3. Develop and be able to utilize a working knowledge of seating sections and pricing at<br>
Symphony venues.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>4. Develop competency in Tessitura database software to process orders, receive donations<br>
and review constituent data.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>5. Participate in sales campaigns as directed by the Teleservices Manager and Patron Services<br>
Manager.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>6. On leads furnished by the OSA, generate a minimum of 15 sales calls per hour during each<br>
hour of the assigned shift.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>7. Use an organized bring-up system on all call-backs to ensure best and most efficient sales<br>
strategy.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>8. Promptly follow up on all leads furnished.<br>
</p>
<p>9. Function as a member of the Patron Services Team, providing support to other Team<br>
members as well as assisting in the training of new staff.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>10. Attend weekly Team Meetings.<br>
</p>
<p>11. Accurately complete all necessary records and paperwork on time.<br>
</p>
</div></div>
<p></p>
<div><div>
<br>
<p>12. Maintain strict confidentiality regarding all information and leads provided by the OSA as<br>
proprietary to the OSA.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>13. Represent the OSA with integrity, handling all business related to the OSA and its employees<br>
and patrons courteously, professionally and in an efficient and confidential manner.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>14. Teleservices PSRs are also required to assist the operation of the Ticket Office in the<br>
following areas:
</p>
<p>a. Be available to work a minimum of 1 concert or ticket office shift per month.<br>
b. Assist the ticket office during peak sales times, as assigned and when available
</p>
<p>within their weekly 29.75 hour maximum.
</p>
<p>c. Spend a portion of each shift handling inbound calls from the ticket office queue,<br>
taking inbound calls from 6–9 pm and at other times as directed.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>REQUIRED HOURS / WORK SCHEDULE: The call-center is open from 1–9 pm, Monday through
</p>
<p>Friday. Teleservices PSRs must regularly work the prime calling hours of 5–9 pm, Monday through
</p>
<p>Thursday, to be of maximum effectiveness. In addition, PSRs are expected to work some Friday shifts
</p>
<p>as directed by Teleservices Manager. Friday shifts begin no later than 6 pm and continue until 9 pm.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>PSRs must work at least four days per week, working a minimum of 20 hours and a maximum of
</p>
<p>29.75 hours per week. Ticket-office and concert shifts do not count towards these minimums.
</p>
<p>Additional hours, up to 40 per week, may be allocated at peak times during the Season, but only with
</p>
<p>prior approval of the Vice-President of Marketing.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>Scheduling is flexible, but all changes require prior approval by the Teleservices Manager.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>QUALIFICATIONS: To perform this job successfully, an individual must be able to perform each
</p>
<p>essential duty satisfactorily. The requirements listed below are representative of the knowledge, skill,
</p>
<p>and/or ability required. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with
</p>
<p>disabilities to perform the essential functions.
 </p>
<p></p>
<p>Education and/or Experience: HS diploma and 1-2 years sales experience preferred.
 </p>
<p></p>
<p>Language Skills: Ability to quickly learn to pronounce foreign names of composers,
</p>
<p>compositions and artists.
 </p>
<p></p>
<p>Mathematical Skills: Ability to accurately add, subtract, multiply and calculate discount
</p>
<p>percentages.
 </p>
<p></p>
<p>Reasoning Ability: Ability to apply common sense understanding to carry out instructions
</p>
<p>furnished in written, oral, or diagram form. Ability to deal with problems involving several
</p>
<p>concrete variables in standardized situations.
 </p>
<p></p>
<p>Computer Skills: Ability to quickly learn and use Tessitura database/ticketing software,
</p>
<p>working proficiency in Microsoft Outlook. Training in Tessitura is provided and two weeks’
</p>
<p>proficiency period is given.
 </p>
<p></p>
<p>Certificates, Licenses, Registrations: N/A<br>
</p>
