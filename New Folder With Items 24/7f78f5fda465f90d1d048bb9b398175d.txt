<p>Essential Job Functions/Accountabilities:</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>The Mechanical Engineer will use software such as ANSYS Mechanical and Mechanical APDL to provide:</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>1. Technical Support - Provide Support to end users of ANSYS by phone, e-mail, or in person.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>2. Pre-sales Support - Work with our account managers to determine the customer's software needs, answer customer questions during the sales process and provide software demos/presentations on a variety of subjects.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>3. Consulting</p>
<ul>
<li>Work with customers to determine their analysis requirements and expectations.</li>
<li>Develop quotes/proposals by determining scopes, costs and schedules</li>
<li>Work with customers to propose alternative design ideas</li>
<li>Write analysis reports and other technical documents requested by the customer</li>
</ul>
<p>4. Technical Training - Teach ANSYS classes at client sites, on-line, and in SimuTech offices across the U.S. and Canada</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>DESIRED SKILLS AND EXPERIENCE</p>
<p>Non-Negotiable Hiring Criteria:</p>
<ul>
<li>Must be a U.S. citizen or permanent resident.</li>
<li>Professional appearance and presentation required.</li>
<li>Good communication skills and proficient in Microsoft Office.</li>
<li>Must be able to work well in a group and independently.</li>
<li>Excellent English-based technical writing and verbal skills</li>
<li>Documented industry experience with: ANSYS Mechanical (Workbench) and ANSYS Mechanical APDL (formerly called ANSYS classic)</li>
<li>Ability to communicate with customers on a technical pre-sales basis to help drive software and services sales</li>
<li>Creativity</li>
<li>Superb problem-solving and analytical skills</li>
<li>Solid theoretical background in structural mechanics and numerical methods backed up by real-world, production-level experience</li>
<li>Documented experience (as evidenced by industry experience and/or publications in reputable journals) in at least one of these areas:</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Structural linear and non-linear static implicit<br>
analysis methods (non-linear materials including plasticity and hyperelasticity as well as contact and large strain/large deformation)</li>
<li>Implicit dynamics including linear (modal, harmonic, response spectrum, random vibration) and time domain transient Thermal analysis including steady-state and time domain transient</li>
<li>Explicit dynamics</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Familiarity with grid generation and meshing techniques (Workbench Meshing, Gridgen, Hypermesh, Mechanical APDL or ICEM)</li>
<li>Personable attitude with an ability to work well with clients and other staff</li>
<li>Bachelor's degree in Engineering required; Master's degree considered a plus</li>
<br>
</ul>
<p></p>
<br>
