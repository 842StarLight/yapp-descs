<h2>EDM Applications Engineer</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>Makino is a world leader in metal-cutting and machining technology. As a leader in advanced manufacturing machine tool technology, Makino enables its customers to realize the highest level of quality with the fastest time to market. When companies make the parts that matter for automotive, aerospace, medical, die-mold and other industries worldwide, they make them with Makino. Makino enables its customers to realize the highest level of quality with the fastest time to market.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p><b>
The Opportunity</b></p>
<p>
Makino is hiring an EDM Applications Engineer for the Newark, NJ area.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p><b>
Essential Duties and Responsibilities</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Evaluate the customer part geometry to determine optimum processing using the company’s products including tooling selection, cutting time, and surface finish.</li>
<li>Utilize third party products in developing solutions. This interface includes researching product capabilities, integrating these tools into solutions, defining installation and maintenance needs, and conducting training classes.</li>
<li>Create and edit CAD data.</li>
<li>Write and edit NC tool paths (includes tool path and macro data).</li>
<li>Prepare pre-sale cutting demonstrations (both custom &amp; general) which match the customers concerns to assist in closing the sale and differentiating Makino from the competition. Design and procure equipment in order to set up these demonstrations. Document results of demonstrations for use in future sales.</li>
<li>Provide on-going customer applications assistance after the sale including customer site visits. Leverage distributor knowledge and resources as appropriate.</li>
<li>Support regional and national machine tool shows with cutting demonstrations and technical discussions with customers.</li>
<li>Participate in meetings, seminars, and training sessions to stay apprised of new developments in the field and associate support. Apply state-of-the-art technology (including peripheral equipment, and supplies) to develop the optimal customer solution.</li>
<li>Provide technical information as needed to other departments such as Marketing, Legal and Engineering.</li>
<li>Make sales calls with Regional Manager and/or distributor salesman (salesmen) to potential customer(s).</li>
<li>At times, perform light service work duties on machines. This includes troubleshooting for Makino Service, fixing minor machine issues, and performing software upgrades.</li>
<li>Create and perform technical presentations for Makino events (Open Houses, Trade Shows, Customer Visits, Webinars) highlighting Makino’s technology and competitive advantages.</li>
<li>Evaluate, record, and distribute information (email or presentation) on competitive equipment to Sales and Applications via test cuts, word of mouth, internet, and trade magazines.</li>
<li>Perform Turn-Key/Share-Key services on appropriate projects such as:</li>
<ul>
<li>Fixture Design &amp; Engineering</li>
<li>NC Program Generation</li>
<li>Process development and testing</li>
<li>Project Management: Provide scheduling timelines and work with customer and/or 3rd Parties</li>
<li>Project Run-Off: Deliver the final and verified process to the customer with appropriate documentation</li>
</ul>
<li>Investigate and recommend new or different procedures that can help Makino operate more efficiently.</li>
<li>Work with the Makino local and global team and share technical ideas and information on new sample parts and machine tool improvements.</li>
<li>Provide technical assistance to other Makino engineers (AE, FSE, Sales) and distributors at all times.</li>
<li>Perform all training duties at both Makino and customer facilities.</li>
<li>Apply 5S and Lean Manufacturing principals to both Makino showroom facility and Applications areas.</li>
<li>Accompany and work with Makino and distributor sales forces on pre-sales efforts. This includes customer research, phone discussions, Web/Video Meetings, and customer facility visits.</li>
<li>Inspect and document all features of test cuts and sample parts utilizing the appropriate metrology methods (Vision, CMM, Profilometer, Hand Tools, Gages, etc.).</li>
<li>Work with Single Source Technologies Consumables and understand their product offerings, test, and evaluate products when required and promote these products with customers when appropriate.</li>
<br>
</ul>
<br>
Required Skills<br>
<ul>
<li>Basic job shop, work holding, and hand tool knowledge.</li>
<li>Self-motivated and able to work in a team environment.</li>
<li>Documentation and written communication skills.</li>
<li>Excellent communication and customer relations skills. Able to present a professional image.</li>
<li>Project management skills.</li>
<li>Rational decision making skills.</li>
<li>Basic CNC knowledge.</li>
<li>Technical knowledge and creative problem solving skills.</li>
<li>Basic Computer Skills (Microsoft Office – Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook).</li>
<li>Up to 30% travel required. Must have the ability to travel domestically and internationally.</li>
<br>
</ul>
<br>
Required Experience<br>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor's degree in Engineering (Manufacturing or Electrical Engineering) or equivalent experience.</li>
<li>Prefer at least 3 years of relevant experience but would consider a new college graduate.</li>
<li>Basic CNC knowledge.</li>
<li>EDM Wire and Sinker application experience (preferred).</li>
<li>Blue print reading and GDT experience (preferred).</li>
<li>Metrology experience (preferred).</li>
<li>U.S. Citizen or Permanent Resident of the United States required.</li>
<br>
</ul>
