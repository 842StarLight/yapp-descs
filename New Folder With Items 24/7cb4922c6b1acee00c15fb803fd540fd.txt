<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Summary of Position</b></h2>
<p>Federal Signal has an immediate opening for an Emergency Vehicle Supervisor based at our University Park, Illinois facility. The successful incumbent will direct a team of Technicians and Laborers involved in the installation of Federal Signal and 3rd party products used on police, fire and work truck vehicles.</p>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>
Essential Job Duties and Responsibilities</b></h2>
<ul>
<li>Manage a team of Technicians (Non-Union) and Labors (Union) to build quality vehicles that meet customer specifications in a timely manner.</li>
<li>Perform quality inspections of each vehicle and provide quality control metrics to improve final quality inspections.</li>
<li>Present KPI’s (Key Performance Indicators) on a weekly/monthly basis (i.e. Number of Builds, Actual Hours per Build vs Billed Hours and etc.)</li>
<li>Coordinate and manage weekly/monthly builds with Manager, NA Upfitting and the Sales Team.</li>
<li>Interface regularly with customers regarding potential business opportunities.</li>
<li>Responsible for ordering (3rd Party Products), moving, retrieving, and storage of all inventory/materials.</li>
<li>Assist team members in the preparation/installation of the vehicle to be built, which can include the following:</li>
<li>Install emergency warning equipment and products according to prints, assembly drawings, process documents and other job requirements sheets.</li>
<li>Conduct programming of installed equipment, test its full functionality and perform troubleshooting.</li>
<li>Recruit, develop, train and retain current staff by providing guidance through performance management, corrective action measures if necessary and/or promotion/compensation.</li>
<li>Manage the team to provide an orderly and clean working environment, keeping the shop always organized.</li>
<li>Monitor stock of supplies for each work station; order supplies as needed.</li>
<li>Complete and organize all required paperwork (Build Sheets, Time Studies, Timecards, Customer Database, etc.)</li>
<li>Develop the build sheets for each pilot build.</li>
<li>Communicate with the Manager, NA Upfitting regarding all activity to help grow the business.</li>
<li>Follow and enforce all safety and departmental rules.</li>
<li>Up to 10% local travel required.</li>
<li>Perform other related duties as assigned.</li>
</ul>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Position Requirements</b></h2>
<ul>
<li>Associates or Technical Degree or equivalent experience.</li>
<li>10+ years’ customer interface experience in the automotive field</li>
<li>5+ years’ prior Supervisory experience</li>
<li>Must be EVT-certified (Emergency Vehicle Technician Certification) and be recertified every 5 years</li>
<li>Experience with Law Enforcement or Fire/EMS preferred</li>
<li>Ability to interpret technical data, processes, operating and maintenance instructions and procedure manuals (Build Sheets)</li>
<li>Effective communicator, both verbally and in writing</li>
<li>Intermediate knowledge of automotive electrical systems &amp; vehicle wiring
</li>
<li>Ability to operate power tools and hand tools (drill, wrench, nail gun, staple gun, gauge, vise, etc) as required.</li>
<li>Knowledge/Ability to use our JDE (“ERP”) software system to pull useful information needed to operate the day to day functions of the position.</li>
<li>Must be mechanically inclined to disassemble mechanical parts for all types of vehicles</li>
<li>Use of good judgment and knowledge acquired to perform custom fabrication</li>
<li>Must be innovative and willing to learn in a fast-paced environment</li>
<li>Must have a valid driver license.</li>
</ul>
