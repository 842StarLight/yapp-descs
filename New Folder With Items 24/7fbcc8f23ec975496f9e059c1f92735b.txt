<p><b>Job Description
</b></p>
<p>Monitor feed and fertilizer facilities to collect samples, conduct inspections and prepare reports.
</p>
<p></p>
<div>Responsibilities :
<br>
<ul>
<li>Travel to and/or call on feed and fertilizer manufacturers, distributors, integrated operations and consumers to inspect and sample products distributed within the purview of the Texas Commercial Feed and Fertilizer Control Act.
</li>
<li>Examine and review products and all product labels and documents on incoming ingredients for compliance and label for compliance as needed.
</li>
<li>Prepare, complete and submit all required reports in a timely, legible fashion.
</li>
<li>Develop knowledge of the assigned area of duty and of Office of the Texas State Chemist policies and procedures.
</li>
<li>Under direct supervision, carry out complaint investigations, issue stop-sales or undertake condemnation actions against products, individuals or firms.
</li>
<li>Complete training for Inspector and participate in training for Investigator duties and commissioning by FDA.
</li>
</ul>
</div>

<ul><li><b>Reside in one of the following counties: Harris (Preferred), Montgomery (Preferred), Brazoria, Chambers, Fort Bend, Galveston, Liberty, San Jacinto

<p><b>( </b>Texas Feed and Fertilizer Control Service (FFCS) Inspectors visit feed and fertilizer manufacturers, distributors, integrated operations and consumers to inspect and sample products distributed within the purview of the Texas Commercial Feed &amp; Fertilizer Control Act. Inspectors work independently and primarily operate out of a vehicle provided by Texas A&amp;M AgriLife Research in order to transport sampling equipment, official samples, safety equipment, and other materials necessary for the performance of duties. Inspectors drive approximately 3,000 miles per month and travel overnight about 50% of time depending on seasonal movement of feed and fertilizer movement, emergency response, and training.
</p>
<p>An Inspector working in the Houston area is primarily responsible for surveillance and monitoring within the following Texas counties: Brazoria, Chambers, Fort Bend, Galveston, Hardin, Harris, Jefferson, Liberty, Montgomery, Orange, and San Jacinto. A total area of about 10,000 square miles. Due to traffic in the Greater Houston area, the Inspector must be centrally located within the primary area of responsibility in order to provide uniform surveillance and monitoring, and make efficient and effective use of state resources. <b>)
</b></p>
