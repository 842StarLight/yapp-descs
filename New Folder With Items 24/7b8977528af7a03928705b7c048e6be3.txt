<h2>Field Service Technician</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Overview
</b></h2>
<div><div><div>Services and maintains Canon Solutions America supported cut sheet products and certain large format products in accordance with Service and Parts Standards to achieve efficiency and a high level of customer satisfaction. Diagnoses routine mechanical and system failures using established procedures and perform basic customer network installs. Unresolved problems will be escalated in accordance with standard procedures.
</div></div></div>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Responsibilities
</b></h2>
<div><div><ul>
<li>Diagnoses routine mechanical and system failures, using established procedures for products on which you are certified.</li>
<li>Services and repairs designated equipment, as assigned, to Canon standards and specifications and are successful on those repairs with support as required.</li>
<li>Reports to manager product failure trends and serviceability issues with necessary supported documentation ensuring accurate information and record keeping.</li>
<li>Meets customer demands by providing efficient, responsive and accurate Field/Shop/onsite repairs.</li>
<li>Properly maintains all technical information, FSR's, Expense Reports and Canon property assigned.</li>
<li>Communicates with supervisors and other departments, if necessary, regarding the solution of escalated technical and/or customer service related problem areas.</li>
<li>Possesses good customer communication and satisfaction skills.</li>
<li>Responsible for maintaining the performance of machines to which you are assigned as the primary engineer.</li>
<li>Responsible for maintaining performance at a level which help to achieve the districts overall metric targets.
</li>
</ul></div></div>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Qualifications
</b></h2>
<div><div><div>Typically requires general education and/or vocational training but little to no experience.<br>
<ul>
<li>Associates degree in electronics or electrical engineering capacity from an accredited college or technical school or equivalent experience.</li>
<li>Possess a basic understanding of internet environments. Successful completion of the 120 day introductory period and completion of the PDIF new hire class.</li>
<li>May require some travel (valid driver's license and acceptable driving record necessary.</li>
<li>Must be able to work in a 24x7 environment, perform shift work and do on-call rotations (applies to those servicing PPS\LFS equipment).</li>
<li>Must be able to lift 50 lbs and be in a mobile activity more than 50% of the time (walking, standing, stooping/kneeling).
</li>
</ul>
</div></div></div>
