<h2>PROJECT/PRODUCT ENGINEER</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>This Product Engineering position is a part of the North American Customer Engineering organization. It is a broad role with many interfaces that supports the development, fabrication and ongoing support of equipment that APCI supplies for various customer industrial gas applications. The market segments supported include food freezing, industrial cryogenics, metals processing, primary metals, and wastewater. This position is located in Allentown, PA.<br>
<br>
Geographical Scope of Customer Engineering: US &amp; Canada<ul><li>
The successful candidate will work with internal and external customers, suppliers and fabricators in a multidisciplinary role as both the product/project engineer and the design engineer. The primary responsibilities for this position include the following:<ul>
<li>
Fabrication and refurbishment of equipment in existing product lines for customer deployment.
</li>
<li>Participation in the design and development of new product equipment offerings.</li>
<li>
Supporting the development of standard product design packages for existing equipment offerings.</li>
<li>
Providing technical support to internal Commercial Technology resources and external customers to solve problems with installed equipment or improve existing operations.</li>
<li>
Provide engineering support to Air Products’ customers, sales personnel, and the Advanced Technology team.</li>
</ul>
</li></ul>
<br>
<b>
Responsibilities:</b><br>
<ul>
<li>
Participation in Air Products’ Basic Safety Program to drive safety performance to zero incidents.</li>
<li>
Preparation of scope of work documents detailing system design criteria and customer versus Air Products installation roles and responsibilities.
</li>
<li>Creation of equipment fabrication cost estimates, bills of materials, and detailed equipment layout, foundation, and flow schematic drawings as necessary.</li>
<li>
Completion of project closeout activities, including spend reports and developing as-built drawings.</li>
<li>
Provide commissioning and start-up support as required.</li>
<li>
Follow all internal work processes for safety, engineering, project management, and operations.</li>
<li>
Manage and maintain BOM's for specific Products developed by and owned by the team.</li>
<li>
Participate in troubleshooting and Quality management issues as needed.</li>
<li>
Establish and maintain vendor relationships enabling product development and management.</li>
</ul>
<ul><li>
Participate in CI events to identify and leverage system work process improvements and cost reductions.</li></ul>
<b>
</b><br>
<b>Skills and Experience:</b><br>
<ul>
<li>
B.S. Mechanical or Chemical Engineering with 3-5 years of engineering experience</li>
<li>
Mechanical design skills as well as knowledge of piping, instrumentation and controls are required.</li>
<li>
Strong electrical aptitude is a plus and familiarity with Allen-Bradley PLCs.</li>
<li>
An understanding of the foods industry, cryogenics and process skills are a plus.</li>
<li>
Prior experience with controls checking and function testing is preferred</li>
<li>
Knowledge of CAD software for schematic and drawing development (AutoCAD, Solidworks, or equivalent).</li>
<li>
Job duties require 5 to 10 % domestic travel annually.
</li>
