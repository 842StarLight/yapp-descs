<h2>Studio Leader</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>SmithGroup is an award-winning, multinational integrated design firm with a team of 1,300 experts across 15 offices in the U.S. and China. We employ research, data, advanced technologies and design thinking to help clients solve their greatest challenges. Our specialists—from artists and engineers, to nurses, workplace strategists and beyond—develop beautiful, sustainable, future-focused solutions for healthcare providers, science and technology organizations, higher education and cultural institutions, urban environments, diverse workplaces, mixed-use and waterfront developments, and parks and open spaces.<br>
</p>
<p></p>
<p>The San Diego office of SmithGroup is looking for a Studio Leader to join our team.<br>
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>STUDIO LEADER ROLE
</b></p>
<p>The primary purpose of this position is to win new work for the San Diego Studio, which is an integrated team of architectural, interiors, lab planning, and engineering professionals that compete and operate in the highest levels of our profession.<br>
</p>
<p></p>
<p>In collaboration with the Office Director, the Studio Leader establishes the general direction and strategy for Studio activities such as business development, client targeting, client relationship management, and advocacy for design excellence within the office through a holistic and collaborative approach to design and the practice. The Studio Leader consistently demonstrates a commitment to SmithGroup core values – Collaboration, Creativity, Community, Integrity and the Environment. As a whole, these items create a culture for the studio that shapes it as a high-achieving, very market-competitive, and sustainable enterprise.<br>
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>BUSINESS DEVELOPMENT
</b></p>
<p>The successful candidate for the Studio Leader position demonstrates, via a proven track record, the ability to consistently win work, develop and maintain client relationships and assist teams in profitably delivering design excellence and client satisfaction. Responsibilities include:
</p>
<ul>
<li>The marketing of our services by working closely with the Office Director, Business Development, Marketing and Design and Engineering Principals of the firm
</li>
<li>Identification of target clients and development of a business development plan with the Office Director and Business Development professionals
</li>
<li>Development and management of the Studio Marketing, and Sales budgets
</li>
<li>Consistent application and participation in all Studio go/no-go evaluations
</li>
<li>Marketing Leadership via active participation in projects through all phases, from initial client contacts through marketing pursuits, sales efforts, contract negotiations
</li>
<li>Attendance and active participation in firm-wide internal marketing meetings
</li>
<li>As client ambassador and team leader, the Studio Leader will often remain engaged in the project, participating in the project kick-off, feasibility studies, strategic planning, master planning, programming, conceptual studies, schematic, design, design development, construction documentation, bidding and award, construction administration, move-in, follow-up and on-going client relationship maintenance<br>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITES
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Provides leadership and management necessary for the continuing development and operation of the studio
</li>
<li>Develops and implements strategic plan and budget
</li>
<li>Responsible for making sure the studio goals are met
</li>
<li>Responsible for maintaining and enhancing SmithGroup’s reputation as a leading design firm
</li>
<li>Works closely with the Office Director and the Director of Operations to ensure that revenue targets are achieved and that our services are effectively marketed
</li>
<li>Keeps management informed on any studio issues
</li>
<li>Provides technical and administrative supervision to the architectural staff assuring the technical, administrative and schedule targets are met within the framework of corporate policy and in accordance with applicable professional and corporate standards
</li>
<li>Makes authoritative decisions and recommendations of significant impact on the firm’s primary architectural activities, guidelines, quality control standards and details
</li>
<li>Involved with office wide recruiting and staffing issues
</li>
<li>Coordinates project resource requirements
</li>
<li>Leads/coordinates communication efforts within assigned studio and between other studio leaders
</li>
<li>Coordinates and reviews work of Architects I, II, III, IV and Interns
</li>
<li>Responsible for staff assignments and discipline budgets
</li>
<li>Sets annual discipline goals, reviews performance, counsels and mentors staff
</li>
<li>Manages architecture discipline staff to include recruiting, dismissal and disciplining of staff, salary negotiation, and bonus recommendations, associate nominations and year end and/or other distributions
</li>
<li>Visits construction sites to gather information, answer contractor questions and prepare punch list as owner’s representative<br>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>OPERATIONS
</b></p>
<p>The Studio Leader also oversees the operational requirements of the Studio and will
</p>
<ul>
<li>Participate in monthly Operations meetings
</li>
<li>Review financial forecasting and budgeting prepared by Project Managers to help create a viable Studio budget in concert with the Office budget
</li>
<li>Work with the Office Director and the Director of Operations to ensure that revenue and project performance targets are achieved and studio issues are addressed
</li>
<li>Coordinate communication efforts within assigned studio and between other studio leaders
</li>
<li>Provide input on hiring prospective employees, especially Project Manager, Arch/Interiors/Engineering 3 and above
</li>
<li>Make decisions and recommendations in concert with the Office Leadership Committee of significant impact on the Office’s primary activities, strategic objectives, guidelines, quality, standards and principles<br>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>REPORTING
</b></p>
<p>Supervision and Direction Received: <i>from the Office Director
</i></p>
<p>Supervision and Direction Exercised: <i>Supervises, directs and mentors the staff in the studio<br>
</i></p>
<p></p>
<p><b>IDEAL CANDIDATE QUALIFICATIONS AND EXPERTISE
</b></p>
<p>The ideal candidate will meet minimum education and experience requirements, demonstrate proven leadership abilities and demonstrate proficiencies with professional and technical tools commonly utilized in this position.<br>
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>LEADERSHIP
</b></p>
<p>The most important attribute for this position is the successful track record of winning work in several of the project typologies described above.<br>
</p>
<p></p>
<p>Other attributes:
</p>
<ul>
<li>Ability to inspire and motivate others
</li>
<li>Demonstrate a high degree of self-awareness, self-management, social awareness and relationship management skills.
</li>
<li>Develop and implement strategic objectives in concert with the corporate practice balanced score card and the office objectives.
</li>
<li>Demonstrate effectiveness in working in a team setting, communicating with others, customer satisfaction and technical competence.
</li>
<li>Ability to synthesize information and respond to questions from groups of managers, clients, customers, and the general public
</li>
<li>Involvement in professional and civic involvement aligned with the overall goals of SmithGroup
</li>
<li>Equally balanced analytic and creative strengths<br>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>PROFESSIONAL AND TECHNICAL SKILLS
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Strong writing skills for communication, project and design narratives
</li>
<li>Independent working capability in the Microsoft Office Suite of Programs: Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook as fundamental professional tools required for Studio and Client communications
</li>
<li>Familiarity with the Adobe Suite of graphics and production programs in support of marketing and business development activities
</li>
<li>Minimum of entry-level proficiency with primary production platforms: Revit, AutoCAD to support work in support of projects and teamwork<br>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>EDUCATION AND EXPERIENCE
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>A minimum of a professional degree in Architecture, Interior Design, Engineering or equivalent combination of related experience and/or training
</li>
<li>A minimum of 15 years related experience preferred and/or training (or equivalent combination of education and experience) in the AE Industry
</li>
<li>Established client relationships in San Diego Region
</li>
<li>Project experience in Healthcare, Higher Education, Science &amp; Technology, or Workplace preferred
</li>
<li>Professional registration as an architect, interior designer, or engineer preferred<br>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p>SmithGroup offers competitive compensation and an excellent benefit package, including 401(k) and continuing educational reimbursement. We offer 3 weeks of paid time off within the first year of employment, with the option of purchasing additional time off, and pay for 8 federal holidays. We also offer an alternative work schedule that provides employees with an additional 15 days off per year (usually Fridays) to help with work/life balance.</p>
