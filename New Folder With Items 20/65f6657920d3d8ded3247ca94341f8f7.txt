<h2>R&D Engineer II</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<p><b>Job Summary:
</b></p>
<p>Designs and develops new implants and instrumentation systems. Directly communicates with surgeons, sales consultants, and product managers to understand clinical issues. Defines design parameters, and ensures by design the correct product is provided to meet customer requirements. Independently completes device and instrument designs in support of Project Leader and Project Core Team. Responsible for initiation and completion of development projects on time and within budget.
</p>
<p><b>Essential Job Functions: </b>List the top 5-7 principle high level job functions/responsibilities along with percentage of time spent. Percentage of time should total 100%.
</p>
<p>Applies engineering knowledge and project management skills to the design and development of new products as well as product changes and enhancements to existing devices in our product portfolio.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>Responsible for reliability and manufacturability of products or technologies developed. Contribute to the intellectual property position of the company via invention and patent.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>Responsible for compliance with QSR, international compliance standards, and company procedures.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>Solve mechanical engineering problems at the component through system level. Conduct testing utilizing existing test protocols or develop new ones as needed
</p>
<p></p>
<p>Complete projects (including product enhancements) in a manner consistent with corporate objectives. Contribute to the intellectual property position of the company via invention and patent.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>Provide mechanical engineering and technical support of products introduced into both the domestic and international markets as well as physician in-service programs.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>Oversee outside vendors and consultants as required. Provide mechanical engineering support in the resolution of product complaints and/or safety issues.
</p>
<p></p>

</b></p>
<p></p>

</li>

</ul>
