<div>
DePuy Synthes is recruiting for a Drexel Product Development Co-op located in West Chester, PA to support the Trauma /CMF Surgeon Response Group (SRG) within the Research and Development (R&amp;D) department.</div>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<div>
The DePuy Synthes Trauma / CMF SRG Team in West Chester is focused on the development of innovative medical devices based on an individual surgeon request. This specific Co-op will interface with surgeons and sales consultants to design and develop new instrumentation.<br>
</div>
<div></div>
<div>Co-op Expectations/Responsibilities/Project Details:</div>
<ul>
<li>
Co-op will be part of a team responsible for the design and development of complex mechanical medical devices within an FDA QSR and ISO 13485 design control environment.</li>
<li>
The co-op will be assigned to the Surgeon Response Group within the R&amp;D team focused on a specific customer request. Products in scope will range from the DePuy Synthes Trauma and CMF portfolios.</li>
<li>
They will participate in all aspects of the design control process including the identification of patient and regulatory needs, development and evaluation of design specifications, identifying and controlling of risks, and creation and documentation of design outputs.</li>
<li>
Required to learn and utilize Creo 3D CAD to produce models and drawings to company drafting standards.</li>
<li>
Support product development by regularly interfacing with members of the sales force, R&amp;D, Prototype Manufacturing, and Design Quality.</li>
<li>
Design Work will be assigned based on candidate’s background and successful completion of tasks.
</li>
<li>Co-op must be able to multi-task and have strong time management skills to release engineering documentation quickly.</li>
<li>
Expected to manage and release multiple new instrument requests during their co-op cycle.</li>
<li>
Project difficulty will depend on the co-op’s ability to solve challenging design problems and work efficiently.</li>
</ul>
<div></div>
<br>
<div>
</div>

Successful candidates for this role possess high mechanical aptitude as well as strong problem solving and communication skills. Engineering students who like to “tinker” and/or work with their hands are preferred. Must have the ability to work independently with minimal supervision. Minimum GPA: 3.0</div>
