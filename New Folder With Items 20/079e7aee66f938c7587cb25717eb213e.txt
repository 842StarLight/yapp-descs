<p>The position of Scientist - Analytical is focused on characterizing R&amp;D materials using a wide range of techniques.<br>
</p>
<p><br>
<b>Responsibilities<br>
</b></p>
<p></p>
<p><b>ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES:
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Characterization of catalyst materials using established methods (ICP, XRF, XRD, FT-IR, SEM, BET, TGA/DSC, Chemisorption)
</li>
<li>Conduct research and apply new methods for catalyst characterization (method development)
</li>
<li>Work with external laboratories (academic labs, national labs) on advanced characterization techniques
</li>
<li>Keep current with scientific developments in material characterization
</li>
<li>Trains and guides less experienced employees in specific skills and/or duties
</li>
<li>Manage projects related to catalyst characterization
</li>
<li>Propose new capital investments
</li>
<li>Participate in knowledge transfer<br>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>OTHER DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES:
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Performs other functions as necessary or as assigned
</li>
<li>Work constructively with other Clariant groups
</li>
<li>Maintain proper housekeeping and ensure EHSA compliance
</li>
<li>Participate in 5S events, performance dialogues and other teamwork activities<br>
</li>
</ul>
<br>
<p></p>
<p><b>Requirements<br>
</b></p>
<p></p>
<p><b>KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES REQUIRED:
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Advanced degree (Masters or Ph.D.) in Chemistry or Chemical Engineering
</li>
<li>Eight or more years of industrial laboratory experience
</li>
<li>Skilled in Microsoft Office, Outlook, SAP, LIMS, statistical software, scientific software
</li>
<li>Detailed knowledge of established characterization methods (see above)
</li>
<li>Must be highly self-driven and independent
</li>
<li>Must have excellent communication and presentation skills<br>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES PREFERRED:
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Supervisory experience
</li>
<li>Experience in Lean Sigma and DFSS (Clariant Excellence)
</li>
<li>Knowledge of advanced characterization techniques (TEM, EXAFS, SS-NMR, XPS, AFM, SAXS, Operando/In-situ, synchrotron methods)<br>
</li>
</ul>
