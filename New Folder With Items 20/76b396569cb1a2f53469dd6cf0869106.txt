<h2>Creative Play Professional II, Huntersville</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<div><div><div>
<p><b>General Summary</b>: Engages Museum visitors and facilitates extraordinary learning experiences in the Museum. Assists the Program Specialist in the planning, development, and acquisition of educational programs and supplies for the Museum as needed. Presents demonstrations, programs, Museum activities, and acts as the lead manager on duty, including cash handling supervisory responsibilities, in the absence of the Museum Director and Coordinator. Other duties include teaching school programs and Summer Camps, training staff members, and other tasks as outlined below or assigned.</p>
<p><b>Essential Duties and Responsibilities
</b></p>
<p>Assists in the planning, development, and implementation of educational activities, public events, public shows and special programs.</p>
<ul>
<li>Aids in planning monthly themes and activities.</li>
<li>Purchases supplies for Museum programs.</li>
<li>Develops Museum programming for children utilizing Museum exhibits to augment standard teaching methods and adapts content and complexity to ages and interests of children.</li>
<li>Understands and is knowledgeable of current state standards for school program/class development.</li>
<li>Develops activities for special events.</li>
<li>Trains staff members for new programs and professional development.</li>
<li>Develops and analyzes methods of programmatic evaluation.</li>
</ul>
<p>Assists in the management of the Museum.</p>
<ul>
<li>Acts as the lead staff member in charge in the absence of the Coordinator and Director and assumes related management responsibilities.</li>
<li>Ensures that staff members and volunteers are adhering to Museum policies, procedures, and general duties.</li>
<li>Aids in the responsibilities of other staff members when help is needed.</li>
<li>Performs exhibit checks and either corrects the problem or reports the issue to other management.</li>
<li>Performs cash handling management for the front desk.</li>
</ul>
<p>Conducts programs, presentations, demonstrations, and lead play activities in a variety of settings.</p>
<ul>
<li>Interprets programs for children in various themes.</li>
<li>Utilizes Museum exhibits to augment standard teaching methods.</li>
<li>Selects and assemble materials to be used in daily demonstrations and programming.</li>
<li>Adapts content and complexity to ages and interests of children.</li>
<li>Plans content for a variety of programming and methods of presentation, and prepare outlines of materials to be covered for submission to the Site Coordinator.</li>
<li>Conducts public shows on a daily basis.</li>
<li>Facilitates school classes, workshops, and camps as needed.</li>
<li>Displays a high level of enthusiasm, energy, and positive behavior to children and their caregivers.</li>
<li>Evaluates success of experiences through feedback provided by guests.</li>
</ul>
<p>Works as a front-line customer service representative at the admissions desk.</p>
<ul>
<li>Greets guests as they enter the Museum.</li>
<li>Checks in guests through the point-of-sale system at the front desk.</li>
<li>Accurately performs admissions transactions/reporting and follows cash handling procedures.</li>
<li>Conducts group orientations.</li>
<li>Informs guests of daily and special programming, learning opportunities, Memberships, and special events.</li>
<li>Provides input to management on feedback and information from customers.</li>
<li>Monitors inventory levels of consumables at the front desk and within the Museum and notify management of needs when apparent.</li>
<li>Processes Membership sales.</li>
</ul>
<p>Monitors the Museum exhibits, activities, and environment to ensure safety.</p>
<ul>
<li>Performs exhibit checks and either correct problems or report them to management.</li>
<li>Enforces safety and other policies/procedures within the Museum .</li>
<li>Assists in emergency response procedures.</li>
<li>Performs daily and nightly housekeeping routines for Museum activities and exhibits.</li>
<li>Provides detailed cleaning, sanitization, and organization of exhibits and areas during down time.</li>
<li>Maintains a clean and orderly work environment.</li>
</ul>
<p>Provides a high level of customer service.</p>
<ul>
<li>Approaches and interacts with guests in a respectful and professional manner.</li>
<li>Displays care and concern when dealing with any guest issue.</li>
<li>Always communicates and maintains a positive attitude (verbally and nonverbally).</li>
<li>Responds promptly to customer needs.</li>
</ul>
<p>Demonstrates behavior consistent with the organizational culture.</p>
<ul>
<li>Continually maintains an outwardly positive demeanor toward coworkers and visitors.</li>
<li>Maintains a team-oriented approach and can-do attitude.</li>
<li>Assists other team members and departments in their responsibilities when help is needed.</li>
<li>Displays flexibility in an ever-changing environment.</li>
<li>Assists in training and shares business-related knowledge with fellow employees.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Competencies:
</b></p>
<p>To perform the job successfully, an individual should demonstrate the following competencies:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<b>Informal and Formal Pedagogy</b> – Is well versed in both formal informal educational methods and is able to apply these methods when interacting with visitors, students, and groups; carries out assigned interactions with content and/or attitude based teaching objectives in mind.</li>
<li>
<b>Oral Communication</b> - Speaks clearly and persuasively in positive or negative situations; adapts language to audience (e.g. children, adults, etc.); listens and gets clarification; responds well to questions; demonstrates group presentation skills.</li>
<li>
<b>Written Communication </b>- Writes clearly and informatively; able to read and interpret written information.</li>
<li>
<b>Reasoning </b>- Solves practical problems and deals with a variety of variables in various situations.</li>
<li>
<b>Interpretation </b>– Processes a variety of instructions furnished in written, oral, diagram, or schedule form.</li>
<li>
<b>Cost Consciousness</b> - Conserves organizational resources.</li>
<li>
<b>Diversity </b>- Shows respect and sensitivity for cultural differences.</li>
<li>
<b>Ethics </b>- Treats people with respect; upholds organizational values.</li>
<li>
<b>Organizational Support</b> - Follows policies and procedures; supports organization's goals and values.</li>
<li>
<b>Adaptability </b>- Manages competing demands; changes approach or method to best fit the situation; able to deal with change, delays, or unexpected events.</li>
<li>
<b>Initiative </b>- Volunteers readily; asks for and offers help when needed.</li>
<li>
<b>Innovation </b>- Displays original thinking and creativity; meets challenges with resourcefulness.</li>
<li>
<b>Planning/Organizing</b> - Uses time efficiently.</li>
<li>
<b>Professionalism </b>- Approaches others in a tactful manner; reacts well under pressure; treats others with respect and consideration regardless of their status or position; accepts responsibility for own actions; follows through on commitments.</li>
<li>
<b>Quality </b>- Looks for ways to improve and promote quality; applies feedback to improve performance; monitors own work to ensure quality.</li>
<li>
<b>Safety and Security</b> - Observes safety and security procedures; reports potentially unsafe conditions; uses equipment and materials properly.</li>
<li>
<b>Problem Solving</b> - Identifies and resolves problems in a timely manner.</li>
<li>
<b>Computer Skills</b> – Must be able to effectively operate a computer, work with point-of-sale software, and have a base knowledge of Microsoft Word and Microsoft Outlook.</li>
</ul>
