<h2>Manager, Provider Data & Reimbursement Management</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">The Manager, Provider Data Network is responsible for network analysis, provider payment review, and composition to support network strategies and growth nationally. This role will provide analysis and review of competitive networks, leased networks and owned networks to effectively solution the most effective solution for clients. The manager will participate and lead direction of provider data management in partnership with other organizational leaders. In addition, the manager will provide oversight associated with vendor management as it relates to network data and provider communication. This position will directly manage employees who are responsible for provider analysis, reporting, provider communication, and contracting.
<br><br>
<b>PRIMARY JOB RESPONSIBILITIES:</b><br>
<ul>
<li>Analyze provider networks and fee schedules, offering recommendations for high performance network targets  Partner with Provider Engagement and Sales leaders to determine appropriate fee schedules and network strategies  Analyze networks to determine competitive position.  Develop, implement and maintain policies, processes and controls associated with vendor agreements to ensure compliance with federal and state guidelines, regulatory requirements, and that service level commitments are met.  Collaborate with Operations and IT business partners to implement and maintain leased network data.  Lead, manage and maintain GEO software to ensure appropriate reporting for networks and provide training to report specialists and business intelligence as needed or required  Assist in the development of stacked network methodology  Manage network data as needed for client audits and Request for Proposals (RFP)  Provide oversight of vendor management including, but not limited to: Quest Analytics, NetMinder and leased network companies. Manage contractual requirements, negotiate pricing where applicable, and establish invoicing and PO process for managing payment.  Manage network tracking and reporting to support national network growth for government programs  Build and manage a high performance work team that is responsible for provider reporting and analysis of network size, fees, composition and structure  Lead team responsible for effectively managing provider contracts and communication for all product lines  Maintain monthly report metrics for network development builds, RFP programs, and provider engagement client reporting.  Create efficiencies and improved processes that support provider networks, analysis and communication  Provide management support associated with the successful implementation and training for Salesforce or other MDM initiatives as it relates to provider data.  Provide oversight and execution of provider mailings and fax blast communications to all providers for new network builds and current network program communication.  Coordinate and implement appropriate performance management and development strategies for team  Other duties as assigned</li>
</ul>
<b>JOB REQUIREMENTS:</b><br>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor’s degree (business, healthcare, or relevant field)  Five years’ business experience, preferably in an insurance and/or healthcare setting</li>
<li>Knowledge of provider reimbursement methodologies  Experience with provider contracting  Expert level proficiency with Microsoft Office Suite of products (e.g. Word, Outlook, PowerPoint, Excel, and Visio)  Excellent oral and written communication skills  Demonstrated analysis, negotiation and presentation skills  Ability to manage staff and work well with others  Ability to prioritize and organize multiple tasks and remained organized with multiple interruptions  Ability to meet multiple deadlines  Ability to travel (approx. 5%)</li>
</ul>
<b>PHYSICAL DEMANDS:</b><br>
<ul>
<li>Ability to work in a traditional professional office setting/remote environment.  Ability to effectively operate computer equipment.  Work schedule may vary and is determined by project schedules.  Ability to travel as projects dictate  Ability to efficiently operate all job-related office equipment.  Ability to communicate via telephone and work in virtual teams  The office environment is active with high voice levels and interruptions that may challenge hearing and concentration.</li>
</ul>
