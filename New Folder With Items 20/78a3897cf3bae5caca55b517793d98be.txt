<div><b>
Description</b></div>
<div>The Systems Engineer will be responsible for the development of a wide variety of Manufacturing Hardware and Software Systems. You will apply your experience and knowledge to the design, development, and implementation of software and hardware solutions to support and enhance Manufacturing Operations, focusing on new innovations, and Lean Manufacturing principles. The Manufacturing Systems team is a critical element of the Brilliant Factory and Lean Manufacturing initiatives, and as a Systems Engineer, you will be a key part of taking the Machine Automation Systems manufacturing operations to the next level of excellence.</div>
<br>
<div></div>
<div>
<b>Responsibilities</b><br>
</div>
<ul>
<li>
Explore and develop new innovations in hardware and software systems to support and enhance Manufacturing Operations.</li>
<li>
Look for new ways to utilize existing data structures to provide insights on opportunities for improving operations (Data Mining).</li>
<li>
Partner with Lean team to continuously eliminate waste, simplify and improve processes.</li>
<li>
Utilize tools such as (but not limited to):</li>
<ul>
<li>
SQL,</li>
<li>
HMI/SCADA,
</li>
<li>Web Portals,
</li>
<li>MS SSRS/BIDS,
</li>
<li>Label printing,</li>
<li>
Laser marking,
</li>
<li>RFID,
</li>
<li>Maintenance Management,</li>
<li>
SAP (ERP) &amp; middleware</li>
<li>
Debug and enhance existing Manufacturing Execution Systems.</li>
</ul>
<li>
Create technical system architecture drawings, information flow maps, database diagrams.</li>
<li>
Create user and system admin manuals for manufacturing systems, conduct training for all users.
</li>
<li>Provide training and guidance to line techs and team leaders to ensure standard processes and practices are followed consistently.</li>
<li>
Complete specific sub-projects as identified by the Mfg. Systems Leader.</li>
<li>
Acquire hardware, software and services required to support the system implementations.
</li>
<li>Create queries or Ad-Hoc reports to support data needs from quality, materials, operations and EHS.</li>
</ul>
