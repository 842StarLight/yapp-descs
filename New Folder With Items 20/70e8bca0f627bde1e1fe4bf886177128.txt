<p><b>
DUTIES:</b></p>
<p>
The District Administrative Assistant is responsible for assisting the District Manager and staff in all Advantage Cleaning Services, Inc. (ACS) activities in accordance with ACS policies and procedures. These include:</p>
<ul>
<li>
Recruitment.</li>
<li>
Human Resources.</li>
<li>
Office Administration.</li>
<li>
Operations.</li>
<li>
Accounting.</li>
<li>
Marketing.</li>
<li>
Customer Relations.</li>
<li>
Safety.</li>
<li>
Training and evaluation.</li>
<li>
Supply.</li>
</ul>
<p>
 <b>MAJOR GOAL:</b></p>
<p>
 To assist the District Manager and staff to maintain a profitable District and meet District profit margin and growth goals.</p>
<p>
 <b>SUBORDINATE GOAL AND KEY RESULTS:</b></p>
<p>
 1. To maintain an effective recruitment program, the District Administrative Assistant will be successful in this Key Results Area when:</p>
<p>
 a. Help wanted advertising is timely and cost-effective.</p>
<p>
 b. All-District positions are filled.</p>
<p>
 2. To maintain an effective human resources program, the District Administrative Assistant will be successful in this Key Results Area when:</p>
<p>
 a. New hires are screened in accordance with ACS policy, i.e., screening and police records checks.</p>
<p>
 b. Personnel files are orderly and proper.</p>
<p>
 c. Disciplinary actions, including terminations, are proper. If disciplinary actions are challenged in court or by an administrative hearing, ACS prevails.</p>
<p>
 d. Unemployment compensation is minimized and ACS prevails in Unemployment Compensation hearings.</p>
<p>
 e. Recognition programs, Employee of the Month, Employee of the Year, are established and ongoing. Employee recognition is publicized in the Newsletter.</p>
<p>
 f. Group interviews/Orientations/ safety training is conducted for potential new hires.</p>
<p>
 g. The prospective employee database is maintained.</p>
<p>
 h. He/she ensures that each employee receives an accurate and timely error-free paycheck on the scheduled pay date, i.e. no hand checks.</p>
<p>
 i. Attend the ongoing Supervisor’s training program. The minimum requirement is the Quarterly Supervisor’s meetings.</p>
<p>
 j. Uniform inventory is maintained and available for issue.</p>
<p>
 3. To maintain efficient office administration, the District Administrative Assistant will be successful in this Key Results Area when:</p>
<p>
 a. Files are orderly.</p>
<p>
 b. Correspondence is timely.</p>
<p>
 c. Records are properly maintained.</p>
<p>
 d. All necessary ACS forms are on hand and easy to locate.</p>
<p>
 e. Telephone messages are communicated promptly.</p>
<p>
 f. Customers calling about complaints are reassured that action will be taken.</p>
<p>
 g. Customer complaints taken over the phone are immediately referred to as the proper Manager/Supervisor.</p>
<p>
 4. To ensure timely and effective administrative, accounting and clerical assistance to the operations staff, the District Administrative Assistant will be successful in this Key Results Area when:</p>
<p>
 a. Quickbooks Online Financial Management reports are distributed to the Managers so that they can comply with the management information requirements of the Operations Manual.</p>
<p>
 b. Procedures outlined in the Administrative Manual are followed.</p>
<p>
 c. Clerical assistance to the operations staff is timely. This assistance includes typing inspection reports, lesson plans, job descriptions, etc.</p>
<p>
 d. Account Operations Manuals are prepared for the staff as needed.</p>
<p>
 e. Vehicle Logs and assignments are maintained.</p>
<p>
 5. To ensure that District accounting practices are proper, reports are timely and accurate, the District Administrative Assistant will be successful in this Key Results when:</p>
<p>
 a. Timekeeping errors are under 1%.</p>
<p>
 b. Prompt adjustments to timekeeping reports are made.</p>
<p>
 c. Reports to the Corporate Office are on time and accurate.</p>
<p>
 d. District accounting practices are in compliance with the ACS District Procedures Manual.</p>
<p>
 6. To ensure that Administrative Assistance to the marketing program is timely and effective, the District Administrative Assistant will be successful in this Key Results Area when:</p>
<p>
 a. He/she maintains a prospect data on sales leads provided by telemarketing and direct mail efforts.</p>
<p>
 7. To ensure that the District Administrative Assistant participates in customer relations programs, he/she will be successful in this Key Results Area when:</p>
<p>
 a. He/she maintains a calendar of Job Status calls and reacts to customer needs.</p>
<p>
 8. To ensure that the District Administrative Assistant assists in the District Safety Program, he/she will be successful in this Key Results Area when:</p>
<p>
 a. A book of Safety Data Sheets (SDS) used in the District is maintained.</p>
<p>
 b. Monthly safety meetings are held.</p>
<p>
 c. Accident/incident reports are forwarded to the Corporate Office, the state and the insurance company as required by the ACS Safety Program/Manual.</p>
<p>
 9. To ensure that training aids are on hand for entry level, Supervisor and Manager training, the District Administrative Assistant will be successful in this Key Results Area when:</p>
<p>
 a. ACS Lesson Plans are available in the District Office.</p>
<p>
 b. DVD’s and books are available for in-office and checkout use.</p>
<p>
 c. The Building Service Contractor’s International (BSCAI) Independent Study Guide is available for use.</p>
<p>
 d. Other BSCAI materials, including the Carpet Care and Hard Surface Care Manuals, are on hand.</p>
<p>
 10. To ensure that supply procedures are timely and proper, the District Administrative Assistant will be successful in this Key Results Area when:</p>
<p>
 a. Managers have the supplies and equipment that they need while the on-hand inventory is limited. (This requires using the supplier as the warehouse.)</p>
<p>
 b. Purchase orders are prepared in accordance with ACS procedures. Opportunities for resale are exploited.</p>
