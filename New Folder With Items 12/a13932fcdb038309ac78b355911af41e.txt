<h2>Federal Procurement Project Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<div>Grant Thornton is seeking a Manager to join its Operations Transformation service line and take an active role in engagement execution, project management and key business development activities.<br>
<br>
Grant Thornton Public Sector helps executives and managers at all levels of government maximize their performance and efficiency in the face of ever tightening budgets and increased demand for services. We give clients creative, cost-effective solutions that enhance their acquisition, financial, human capital, information technology, data analytics, and performance management. For more information, visit grantthornton.com/publicsector.<br>
<br>
At Grant Thornton, our professional staff applies traditional, cutting-edge approaches and methods to a variety of analyses. As part of our team, the Manager will utilize various methodologies and models to execute client projects.<br>
<br>
<br>
ESSENTIAL DUTIES:<br>
This position will assist in the development and execution of a new IPT in support of integrated procurement functions on behalf of a CIO organization, including:<br>
<ul>
<li>Planning and facilitating meetings with stakeholders.</li>
<li>Providing procurement and contracting best practices and thought leadership recommendations to senior stakeholders.</li>
<li>Tracking action items, developing and reviewing artifacts on behalf of IPT members, and briefing stakeholders on status.</li>
<li>Requires understanding of traditional and Agile project management methodologies.</li>
</ul>
Perform engagement management responsibilities, including performance reviews, task delegation, project scheduling, project financials, quality review and client management.<br>
Meet or exceed sales targets for new and follow-on work.<br>
Manage business development activities, such as proposals, capture, account teams, whitepapers, conferences, and/or other thought leadership materials.<br>
Meet or exceed targeted billing hours (utilization).<br>
Act as performance manager (coach) for up to four coachees outside of engagement responsibilities.<br>
Interview campus and/or experienced candidates.<br>
</div>
<p></p>
<div>
<b>Qualifications</b><div>
</div>
<div>
This position will have a strong understanding of federal procurement and contracting processes and best practices.<br>
Advanced understanding of pre-award activities, including market research and planning, requirements elicitation, and source selection planning.<br>
Assist in the development of market research tools [RFI, Market Survey, Industry Day planning], analysis of alternatives, acquisition strategy development, solicitation documentation, source selection criteria.<br>
Bachelor’s Degree required from an accredited college or university in a related field.<br>
Ability to obtain and maintain certain job-related certifications if no job-related advanced degrees.<br>
U.S. citizenship may be required. Ability to work in the United States indefinitely required.<br>
Travel may be required.<br>
Coach and mentor junior staff members.<br>
Ability to work overtime required on occasion.<br>
Ability to sit in an office environment for long periods of time.<br>
Ability to obtain and maintain a security clearance.<br>
Ability to communicate clearly in writing and verbally.<br>
Ability to obtain and maintain firm independence and abide by firm ethics requirements.<br>
Meet or exceed continuing professional education (CPE) requirements.<br>
