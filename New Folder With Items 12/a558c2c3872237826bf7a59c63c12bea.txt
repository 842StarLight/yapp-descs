<b>Job Description</b><br>
<p><b>Job Title: </b>Director, Supply Chain Planning<br>
<b>Requisition ID: </b>104475BR<br>
<br>
When you join us at Thermo Fisher Scientific, you’ll be part of a smart, driven team that shares your passion for exploration and discovery. With revenues of $22 billion and the largest investment in R&amp;D in the industry, we give our people the resources and opportunities to make significant contributions to the world.<br>
<br>
<b>Location/Division Specific Information:</b><br>
<br>
The Director, Business Planning Partner is part of the Laboratory Products Division for Laboratory Equipment (LE) which brings together a winning combination of innovation and customer focus. LE serves a wide range of market segments including life science, blood and tissue banking, cell culture, immunotherapy, food science, bioprocessing clinical, biopharmaceutical, and more. This position will be based ideally at our facility in Asheville, NC. Travel expectation will be between 25-50%, depending upon location. This will include both domestic and international.<br>
<br>
<b>How will you make an impact?
</b><br>
<br>
The Director, Supply Chain Planning, will improve the planning function across all operating groups, divisions and regions for LET. In this role, you will collaborate and help develop targeted improvement plans with for product availability, working capital optimization and productivity improvement and will follow-up until implementation is complete. You will be accountable for annual improvements in the company’s Product availability (LIFR/OTIF), Customer Allegiance Score (CAS) for Order Fulfillment and inventory days of supply (DOS).
<br>
<br>
<b>What will you do?
</b><br>
</p>
<ul>
<li>Develop and apply a standard Sales, Operations, and Inventory Planning process across all factories and distribution centers</li>
<li>
Establish an objective measurement of the usefulness of the demand plan, supply plan and order fulfillment processes.</li>
<li>
Maintain plan attainment and schedule adherence metrics at &gt;95%, maintain past due work order performance at &lt;2%.</li>
<li>
Establish an inventory health metric around gross to net ratio to support corporate DOS target.</li>
<li>
Establish mechanisms to achieve Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) to drive business results, including leading indicators which will serve as early warning signs of problem performance.</li>
<li>
Work with IT and Procurement to identify and standardize around a core set of information system solutions and data analytics platforms.</li>
<li>
Develop a talent acquisition strategy to continuously improve the planning function.</li>
<li>
Develop an educational platform to develop employees in the planning function</li>
</ul>
