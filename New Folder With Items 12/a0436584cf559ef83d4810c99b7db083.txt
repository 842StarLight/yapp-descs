<h2>Marketing Manager-Business Development (Non-Civil Service)</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div><div>
<div><p>As head of the Marketing and Research team, this position will work to implement the City economic development strategy as well as the promoting the City of Dallas to both internal and external audiences.</p></div>
<p></p>
<div><b>Essential Functions</b></div>
<p></p>
<div><ul>
<li>Oversee marketing, branding, and communications strategies for all OED Programs: business recruitment and retention, real estate development, multifamily housing, workforce development, small business, research, and board and investor communications. Work closely with OED staff on communication needs.
</li>
<li>Manage, maintain, and advance OED image and brand identity throughout multiple media (print, collateral, online, video, and advertising) to internal and external audiences. Manage all social media accounts and websites.
</li>
<li>Direct all marketing and communications programs including written communications, print collateral, newsletters, reports, trade shows, PR, advertising, signature events, and social media.
</li>
<li>Oversee the organization and promotion of OED's marketing events and trips.
</li>
<li>Develop press releases and oversee media relations through existing contacts or vendors. Maintain strong relationships with local, regional and national media.
</li>
<li>Write and edit printed and electronic marketing materials, brochures, newsletters, reports, and press releases.
</li>
<li>Develop, procure, and order marketing collateral as needed.
</li>
<li>Supervise the Marketing and Research staff, as well as oversee vendors providing marketing and design services including creative and web development.
</li>
<li>Assist with the development and budgeting for the annual marketing plan to implement OED's program of work.
</li>
<li>Work with regional partners to understand the assets in their communities and act as a resource for their PR efforts.
</li>
<li>Lead efforts to conduct research, analyze data, perform economic and fiscal analysis using LOCI, IMPLAN, and other economic models.
</li>
<li>Fully implement the OEDs CRM (e.g. Salesforce) and maintain the CRM with active projects.
</li>
<li>Prepare annual profiles for each council district, as well as the city's annual economic profile.
</li>
<li>Prepare research reports, GIS maps, and other research information on an ad hoc basis.
</li>
<li>Other related duties as assigned.</li>
</ul></div>
<p></p>
<div><b>Minimum Qualifications</b></div>
<p></p>
<div><ul>
<li>Bachelor's Degree in Marketing, Communications, Public Relations, Business Administration, or Economic Development. MBA or equivalent Master's Degree is a plus.
</li>
<li>5 - 7 years of relevant industry work with experience in a Chamber of Commerce, economic development organization, or other related organization.
</li>
<li>Excellent writing and editing skills. Ability to synthesize complex subject matter and present it in a user-friendly and understandable manner.
</li>
<li>Experience in media relations, public relations, and handling media inquiries.
</li>
<li>Ability to oversee marketing projects and multiple vendors (PR firm, printers, designers, videographers).
</li>
<li>Familiarity with graphic design principles. Knowledge or general understanding of Adobe Creative Cloud software (especially InDesign).
</li>
<li>Excellent computer skills, including Microsoft Outlook, Publisher, Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Access.
</li>
<li>Familiarity with Salesforce, CoStar, JobsEQ, ESRI, IMPLAN (Economic Impact Analysis), LOCI (Fiscal Impact Analysis), Synchornist (Business Retention Software), and ArcGIS.
</li>
<li>Ability to work independently, meet deadlines, and handle multiple projects.
</li>
<li>Ability and willingness to travel. Able to work events outside of normal business hours.</li>
</ul></div>
<p></p>
<div><b>Knowledge, Skills &amp; Abilities</b></div>
<p></p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>Knowledge of local, state, and federal laws and regulations relevant to the operational unit or functional program and the principles and practices of public administration and management.
</li>
<li>Ability to manage unit/program activities through the supervision of subordinate supervisors.
</li>
<li>Ability to establish and obtain operating long-range goals and objectives for unit.
</li>
<li>Ability to devise solutions to administrative problems and to plan, assign, delegate responsibility.
</li>
<li>Ability to develop and evaluate administrative polices and procedures.
</li>
<li>Ability to develop and implement budget.
</li>
<li>Ability to communicate effectively, at all levels, both orally and in writing.
</li>
<li>May be required to be certified, registered, or licensed in a specialty area.</li>
</ul>
