<b>Position Overview</b><br>
<br>
At Isobar, the Project Manager (PM) provides day-to-day strategic and tactical leadership for the delivery team on client engagements. The scope of responsibilities span the entire project lifecycle from strategy and planning to project initiation, execution, tracking, and closure. Managers are responsible for client satisfaction, project delivery, project financials (P&amp;L), and cross-discipline team management and development. Within the project management discipline, Isobar Managers apply industry standards, best practices, and Isobar specific methodologies and tools in the delivery of projects.<br>
<br>
Project Managers must be able to generate and validate estimates across the lifecycle of the project (requirements, design, development, etc.), and understand technical details and complexities to accurately report project status and manage dependencies, risk, and day-to-day development activities. PMs must be able to lead all members of the project team by example and occasionally drive technical tasks with the development team.<br>
<br>
Project Managers must also manage the day-to-day relationship with the client. This includes responsibilities to establish, maintain, and grow relationships with clients throughout the lifecycle of a project. Isobar PMs work directly with clients daily to strategize, plan, and report on project needs. They are also responsible for tactical project communications with clients and stakeholders.<br>
<br>
Isobar Managers are intelligent problem solvers with strong written and verbal communication skills. Candidates must be self-starters, able to quickly learn and apply new knowledge in a fast-paced, client-driven environment. Managers lead by example, emulating Isobar Core Values in the leadership of teams. They work closely with Engagement Directors providing project oversight, client management, and the securing of new opportunities within an existing account relationship. Managers are expected to contribute to the larger Isobar community providing thought leadership and developing / refining processes and tools.<br>
