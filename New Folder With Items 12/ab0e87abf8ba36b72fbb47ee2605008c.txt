<h2>Low Voltage Regional Operations Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>E2 Optics is an award-winning Woman-Owned IT Solutions Provider. We specialize in Data Centers, Structured Cabling, Audio Visual, Video, Electronic Physical Security, Wireless, and DAS Solutions. Our Core Value Culture is built on the foundation of Safety, Passion, Family Centric, Respect, Integrity, Accountability, Collaboration, and Agility. E2 Optics’ Core Values guide our business, our employees, and our relationships with customers and partners. We embrace these values as a way to not only be a better business, but to be better human beings. We are looking for candidates who align with our Core Value Culture and eager to become a part of a growing team, focused on delivering the very best in customer service while utilizing cutting edge technology and practices, and having fun!<br>
<br>
<b>POSITION SUMMARY</b><br>
The Regional Operations Manager oversees the day-to-day business operations for an E2 Optics regional area and is responsible for its overall profitability on a monthly, quarterly, and annual basis. Duties include recruiting, estimating/proposals and developing employees, and ensuring and improving the performance, productivity, efficiency and profitability of the region through the provision of effective methods and strategies. <b>This position requires 7 – 10 years’ telecommunications/low voltage management and revenue growth experience.</b><br>
<br>
<b>DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES:</b><br>
<b>
</b><br>
<b>Core Duties &amp; Responsibilities:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Safety is our number one priority. Follow, and expect employees to follow safe work practices and company and client work site policies.</li>
<li>Promote company Core Values to foster and safeguard family-centric culture.</li>
<li>In coordination with sales, conducts monthly, standing pipeline meetings with key partners.</li>
<li>Directs operational aspects for a designated geographic area including project management, field operations, and customer service.</li>
<li>Develops, forecasts, financial objectives, and business plans.</li>
<li>Work in conjunction with estimating staff, or independently, to prepare estimates with a detailed review of plans, specifications and bid form: gather or develop data such as scope of work, subcontractor quotes, floor plans, etc. to produce customer proposals.</li>
<li>Meet goals and metrics as assigned by ownership:
<ul>
<li>Meets or exceeds monthly, quarterly, and annual revenue goals</li>
<li>Oversees execution of project management using WIP (Work in Progress) report system</li>
<li>Oversees management of and minimizes regional overhead costs on a monthly, quarterly, and annual basis.</li>
<li>Manages and anticipates labor needs, shortages, and overages</li>
<li>Anticipates work levels to best maintain “even” workloads throughout the year.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Develops and manages budget and allocates funds appropriately.</li>
<li>Coordinates with corporate human resources, information technology and administration as needed to accomplish business objectives and meet legal and compliance requirements.</li>
<li>Develops regional personnel by:
<ul>
<li>Providing training, coaching, and motivation.</li>
<li>Identifying areas of improvement and propose corrective actions that meet challenges and leverage growth opportunities</li>
<li>Holding leaders accountable for doing the same with their own people</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Shares knowledge with other regions, divisions and corporate on effective practices, competitive intelligence, business opportunities and needs.</li>
<li>Addresses customer and employee satisfaction issues promptly.</li>
<li>Adheres to high ethical standards, and complies with all regulations/applicable laws</li>
<li>Networks to improve the presence and reputation of the region and company</li>
<li>Stays abreast of competing markets and provide reports on market movement and penetration</li>
</ul>
<b>Travel:</b> The individual in this role should be able and willing to travel from time to time as required by E2.<br>
<br>
<b>POSITION REQUIREMENTS:</b><br>
<b>
</b><br>
<b>Education:</b>
<ul>
<li>High School Diploma required</li>
<li>Associate’s or Bachelor’s Degree is preferred</li>
<li>BICSI Technician Certified preferred</li>
<li><b>RCDD Certification is required</b></li>
<li>OSHA 10 required. OSHA 30 is preferred</li>
</ul>
<b>Experience:</b>
<ul>
<li>7 – 10 years’ telecommunications/low voltage management and revenue growth experience</li>
<li>10+ years construction/telecommunications experience.</li>
<li>Experience with security, audio visual, videoconferencing, wireless communications preferred.</li>
<li>Proven project management experience on time, on budget, with safety and quality required.</li>
</ul>
<br>
<b>KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS, AND ABILITIES:</b>
<ul>
<li>Decision Making and Judgment:
<ul>
<li>This position requires the ability to assess information and interpersonal interactions to negotiate business that meets or exceeds client expectations, while providing a profitable revenue stream for E2 Optics.</li>
<li>This position involves considerable decision-making authority and creative problem solving skills regarding procedures, plans, and schedules. Although there are sometimes problem-solving guidelines for particular problems, there may be no existing procedures or instructions for those problems.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Strong leadership, human resources management and conflict resolutions skills</li>
<li>Ability to meet sales targets and production goals</li>
<li>Ability to influence and negotiate</li>
<li>Proficient in using a computer and Microsoft Office (Outlook, Word, Excel, Visio, etc.) and MS Project</li>
<li>Ability to understand and effectively quote every aspect of the structured cabling system including pathways, spaces, horizontal cabling, backbone copper, backbone fiber optic, cross- and inter-connections, and testing.</li>
<li>Proficient with reading and understanding blueprints of building floor plans and construction designs.</li>
<li>Knowledge of data infrastructure industry including technology, quality and safety standards.</li>
<li>Ability to supervise and train employees, to include organizing, prioritizing, and scheduling work assignments.</li>
<li>Knowledge of the PMBOK Guides and Standards.</li>
<li>Familiarity with industry’s rules and regulations</li>
<li>Excellent organizational, delegation, and communication skills</li>
<li>Results driven and customer focused</li>
<li>Critical thinking and problem-solving skills</li>
<li>Impeccable problem-solving skills</li>
<li>Demonstrated and effective communication and interpersonal skills.</li>
<li>Ability to interact effectively and professionally with all levels of employees, both management and staff alike, vendors, clients, and others.</li>
<li>Strong ability to work in teams and foster confidence in all.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>WORK ENVIRONMENT &amp; PHYSICAL DEMANDS:</b></p>
<p>The standard work environment for this position is an indoors business office and construction environment. Ability to use a computer and/or hand tools while sitting or standing for extended periods of time. The noise level in the work environment is usually moderate. While performing the duties of this job, the employee is regularly required to sit, talk or hear, use hands, stoop, kneel, bend, rotate, push, pull, reach with hands and arms on intermittent to regular basis daily. The employee is frequently required to stand and walk. The employee must regularly lift and/or move up to 50 pounds and frequently lift and/or move up to 25 pounds. The employee must regularly carry and climb ladders to 20 feet. The employee may be required to work in tight, confined spaces. The employee must demonstrate regular and on-time attendance. There could be a requirement of occasional travel by conventional means including aircraft, motor vehicle and the like within the region and to other locations as required. The physical demands described here are representative of those that must be met to successfully perform the essential functions of this job.</p>
