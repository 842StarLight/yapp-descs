<h2>Business Compliance Officer</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<div>
<div>As a leading worldwide provider of navigation devices and wearable technology, our focus is on developing, designing and supporting superior products. Our advanced technology promotes performance, safety and ease of use in every market we serve. That makes us an engineering and technology company with a keen focus on recruiting talent in those fields. But the same vertical integration business model that keeps all design and manufacturing processes in-house also gives us the incentive to hire top performers from different backgrounds, including operations, finance and accounting, information technology, sales, marketing and communications.</div>
<div></div>
<br>
<div>
We are looking for a full-time Business Compliance Officer for our headquarters in the greater Kansas City area. In this role, you will be a member of the Aviation Engineering team, who serves as the cross-functional leader accountable for ensuring program compliance and operational excellence in managing Garmin’s compliance with external governing body requirements. Primary area of responsibility will include Export Administration Regulations (EAR) and International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR). Secondary activities will include Spectrum Requirements (FCC, CE, IC, ANATEL, ITU), ROHS, and REACH. You will ensure company wide compliance to Garmin’s Controlled Data Policies and Practices. Champion requirements internally and with Garmin’s customers. Proactively drives collaboration across all organization departments. Other essential functions include:</div>
<ul>
<li>
Maintain knowledge of current diverse governing body requirements and ensure Garmin maintains compliance to those requirements.</li>
<li>
Interface within engineering to ensure product development procedures support compliance.</li>
<li>
Leverages leadership skills on developing Garmin’s complex EAR-ITAR compliance system to ensure communication of strategy, processes and procedures on all active controlled programs.</li>
<li>
Leads effective meetings to drive cross-functional, global team effort toward EAR-ITAR program success while identifying and resolving conflicts within the team and escalates to senior management as necessary.</li>
<li>
Engage outside resources and legal counsel as necessary to constantly evaluate and determine Garmin’s compliance with the Department of Commerce’s EAR (Export Administration Regulation) and the Department of State’s ITAR (International Traffic in Arms Regulation) requirements.</li>
<li>
Manage the coordination and communication of changes with internal teams and responsible for the flow down of new controlled program classifications and requirements.</li>
<li>
Act as the primary point of escalation in the event of a compliance, customer or Garmin driven non-technical issue in regards to compliance issues.</li>
<li>
Ensure the review of new programs to ensure that regulation classification, business requirements and Garmin’s current capabilities are fully communicated, documented and approved by all key stakeholders.</li>
<li>
Possess a core understanding of all programs/projects that require controlled data.</li>
<li>
Provides reliable solutions to a wide range of difficult problems using sound problem solving techniques</li>
<li>
Exemplifies Garmin’s Mission, Vision, Values and Quality Policy and proactively works to improve Garmin’s image and culture.</li>
<li>
Monitor and guide the business in best practice when new compliance requirements develop.</li>
<li>
Perform other job-related duties required to complete the task assigned</li>
<li>
Demonstrate a high level of professionalism in all internal and external customer contact activities</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div>
<b>
Qualifications</b><div>
</div>
<div>
Qualified candidates must possess a Bachelor’s Degree in the disciplines of Engineering, Management, Operations, Supply Chain or related field from a four-year college or university and a minimum of 5 years related experience. Other requirements include:</div>
<div></div>
<br>
<div>
Excellent academics (cumulative GPA greater than or equal to 3.0 as a general rule)</div>
<ul>
<li>
Ability to lead a team to successfully execute projects of any scale or complexity</li>
<li>
Advanced understanding of production and operations issues as they relate to program management, including the ability to successfully identify and mitigate risks</li>
<li>
Must be detailed-oriented and possess the ability to prioritize, organize and multi-task in a flexible, fast paced and challenging environment to effectively meet deadlines</li>
<li>
Proven excellent communication, presentation, interpersonal, organizational and analytical skills</li>
<li>
Demonstrated success in bringing together cross functional teams for effective problem resolution</li>
<li>
Previous experience managing multiple projects simultaneously</li>
<li>
Proficient computer skills in MS Office Suite (Microsoft Word, Excel, Project and PowerPoint)</li>
</ul>
<div>
Candidates with the following desirable qualifications are preferred:</div>
<ul>
<li>
Knowledge of all areas of compliance.</li>
<li>
Aviation background.</li>
<li>
Juris Doctor or Master’s degree in the disciplines of Engineering, Management, Operations, Supply Chain or related field</li>
<li>
Previous EAR or ITAR compliance experience</li>
<li>
Strong working knowledge of Department of Commerce EAR (Export Administration Regulation) and relevant compliance systems.</li>
<li>
Strong working knowledge Department of State ITAR (International Traffic in Arms Regulation) and relevant compliance systems.</li>
<li>
Global program development and program management experience.</li>
<li>
Relevant knowledge, experience and/or training in avionics systems, engineering, and/or aviation</li>
<li>
Experience working in AS9100 and AS94900 environments</li>
</ul>
