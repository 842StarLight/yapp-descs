<h2>Accounts Receivable Specialist</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Position Description
</b></h2>
<div><div><div>
<div><b>At a Glance</b></div>
<div></div>
<br>
<div>
<b>
Legrand </b>has an exciting opportunity for an Accounts Receivable Specialist to join the <b>Building Control Systems Team</b> in <b>Richardson, TX</b>. Manage the collection of account receivables in compliance with established policies. This includes account reconciliation, backlog management, credit limit management, and assisting Sales Operations with dispute resolution and customer communication. Timely releasing of order holds. Monthly AR meetings with sales to discuss over 90 day past due balances and problem customers. Provide key direction for internal and external customers to ensure the timely processing of orders while providing prudent management of investment in accounts receivables.</div>
<div></div>
<br>
<div><b>
What Will You Do?</b></div>
<ul>
<li>
Prepares weekly customer trial balances and internal reporting</li>
<li>
Processes customer payments</li>
<li>
Researches and resolves payment issues such as chargeback’s, unauthorized deductions, and policy compliance</li>
<li>
Manage the efficient collections of assigned accounts within the terms and conditions of the company.</li>
<li>
Develops and implements policies for billing, collections, and cash applications.</li>
<li>
Maintains constant communication and builds relationships via phone and email conversation with dealer personnel, sales and customers.</li>
<li>
There will be regular interaction with many internal teams for dispute resolution and collection of accounts receivable items in a timely manner.</li>
<li>
Recommend ways to improve processes to avoid future collection issues.</li>
<li>
Maintain over 90 day past due under 5% consistently.</li>
<li>
Provides excellent customer service and follow through for all issues regarding customers.</li>
<li>
Performs other similar and related duties as required.</li>
</ul>
</div></div></div>

</div>
