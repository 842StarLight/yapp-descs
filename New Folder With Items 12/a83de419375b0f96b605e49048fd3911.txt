<p><b>POSITION DETAILS</b></p>
<p><b><i>Position Summary</i></b></p>
<p>The Project/Program Manager, is responsible for the day-to-day management and welfare of a product or a key customer in middle to mature stages of the product life cycle, including low-rate initial production, full-rate production, and long-term product viability and profitability. In this role, the project/program manager will allocate, deploy, monitor, and support the complete set of resources required to complete/deliver projects within defined scope, time, and cost parameters. New product development may be an added responsibility of the Project/Program Manager. If so, it is usually limited to product improvement or product line extension. Most often, the Project/Program Manager is responsible for formulating and implementing the business goals and strategies for the programs the position supports.</p>
<p><b><i>Principal Activities</i></b></p>
<ul>
<li>Interface with customers with the goal of meeting and exceeding customer expectations and being able to respond to their requirements</li>
<li>Organize and implement projects, including development of project plans meeting technical, schedule, and budget requirements</li>
<li>Be the point of contact for customers and interact with customers to understand customer requirements, provide updates on product/program status, while working closely with sales account managers and other associates</li>
<li>Work closely with operations and other support groups within cross functional departments to ensure that customer requirements are met</li>
<li>Involved in collaborating with other company disciplines assigned to the program from technical, manufacturing and administrative areas.</li>
<li>Oversees the program execution to ensure customer requirements are met, while maintaining/updating scope, schedule and costs. Gathers and interprets customer requirements and collaborates across the extended enterprise to identify and acquire relevant assets/resources. Identifies risks and creates opportunities to manage the operational or market risks for a project or program.</li>
<li>Monitors and stays abreast of customer changes that affect the activities and work processes of the program; makes recommendations for, develops and carries out improvements to the line to improve profitability.</li>
<li>Represents the customers in meetings (Voice of customer) with other functional areas and responsible for customer satisfaction, maintaining communication, and the overall management of the customer relationship.</li>
<li>Prioritizes and allocates available resources; reviews and evaluates product line requirements and hardware delivery.</li>
<li>Performs administrative duties requiring oversight, attention to detail and analysis; aids in strategic planning and financial analysis, including assisting in the preparation of specialized products, budgets, staffing projections, and other operational needs.</li>
<li>Negotiates, develops, monitors and administers a variety of contracts and agreements in partnership with other areas of the business.</li>
<li>Develops systems and maintains records that provide for the proper evaluation, control and documentation of assigned activities; prepares a variety of written correspondence, reports, procedures, directives and other materials.</li>
<li>Other duties as needed and assigned.</li>
</ul>

<p><b>CANDIDATE REQUIREMENTS</b></p>
<p><b><i>Knowledge Skills &amp; Abilities</i></b></p>
<ul>
<li>Strong project and or program management skills, including the ability to prioritize, balance, and manage multiple efforts.</li>
<li>Solid analytical skills and solution-driven thinking.</li>
<li>Ability to communicate effectively across mediums, audiences, and situations.</li>
<li>Be available to customers when needed</li>
<li>Ability to develop and give presentations for regular program reviews and key customer communications</li>
<li>Knowledge of and experience working with customer and industry-specific requirements/standards across the product life cycle.</li>
<li>Applied experience working with engineered mechanical valves, pneumatic and hydraulic systems, and other similar products within the aerospace industry</li>
<li>Thorough knowledge of Word, Excel, Power Point, Visio, Project, and other applicable tools.</li>
<li>Because the Program Manager does not have line authority over other functional areas, success in achieving sales, profitability, market share, and individual performance goals is largely driven by his/her product knowledge, business partnering skills, and overall ability to influence others.</li>
</ul>
