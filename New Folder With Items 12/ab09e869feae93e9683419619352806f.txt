<h2>Contract Cost Analyst and Funding Requirements Specialist -19109</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<p>Huntington Ingalls Industries - Mission Driven Innovative Solutions Group (HII-MDIS) provides mission-critical engineering and technical service to Information Technology, Federal Civilian, and National Security, Aerospace &amp; Defense and International government and commercial customers at over 100 locations worldwide.</p>
<p>HII-MDIS is currently seeking a Cost Analyst to work onsite at Picatinny Arsenal.</p>
<p><b>Duties and Responsibilities (to include but not be limited to the following):</b></p>
<p>Cost analyst with the ability to advise, analyze, recommend and establish objectives related to a wide variety of military acquisition cost areas within the Federal Acquisition Regulations System.</p>
<p><b>Duties and Responsibilities</b> (to include but not be limited to the following):</p>
<ul>
<li><p>Perform price and cost analyses on various elements of major contractor proposals, reviewing in detail the significant direct and indirect cost elements such as: material, labor, overhead, general and administrative expenses, facilities, engineering, special tooling/special test equipment, packaging and packing, transportation, and other costs for allowability, allocability and reasonableness.</p></li>
<li><p>
Develop and recommend price/cost objectives for negotiations.</p></li>
<li><p>
Analyze available data such as direct labor hours, the contractors past performance, costs of previous procurements, and/or standards and other techniques in producing like or similar items.</p></li>
<li><p>
Evaluate the contractors experience, production, and technical ability on current and previous orders as to manufacturing, tooling, engineering, research and development, overhead, general and administrative expenses, and profit.</p></li>
<li><p>
Forecast price trends, economic factors and efficiencies in production for future accounting periods using analytical techniques such as random sampling, cost models, learning curves, and parametric cost models.</p></li>
<li><p>
Request, coordinate and integrate into a comprehensive pricing report the various technical evaluations of cost elements such as engineering, facilities, subcontractor overhead and profit, inefficiencies of idle labor, special delays and standby costs, escalation factors, job site/field service costs, overhead, and profit.</p></li>
<li><p>
Evaluate technical, audit reports, and legal opinions to determine the reasonableness of the various cost elements or justified exceptions in terms of factors such as risk, extent of government assistance, problems of capital investment, and extent of subcontracting.</p></li>
<li><p>
Review advisory reports to assure appropriate coverage, and initiate issuance of supplemental audit or technical reports and legal advice when required.</p></li>
<li><p>
Prepare pricing reports of findings, determinations, and conclusions reached, which reflect the comprehensive review and evaluation of the total pricing team effort.</p></li>
<li><p>
Review and evaluate contract price proposals for adequacy and compliance with laws and regulations.</p></li>
<li><p>
Review contractor proposals for compliance with promulgated standards, rules, and regulations including Disclosure Statement (DS) requirements.</p></li>
<li><p>
Initiate and coordinates meetings with contractor personnel such as the contract manager, contract administrators, engineers, cost estimators, administrative, and shop personnel to determine that pricing, make-or-buy decisions, and all cost impact decisions are in the best interest of the government.</p></li>
</ul>
