<h2>Regional Site Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>M3 Wake Research Inc. is one of the largest independent clinical research site services companies in North America. Wake Research has more than 18 owned and managed research sites across 8 states in the US and continues to grow.
</p>
<p>We are currently seeking a Regional Site Manager to oversee all aspects of clinical trials management and operations for our four sites located in central North Carolina and to act as a point of contact in site management practices.</p>
<p><b>
Responsibilities:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Responsibilities include: site assessments, pre-trial assessments, site initiation, subject recruitment and retention, monitoring and close-out</li>
<li>Conducting pre-study site selection visits and assessments</li>
<li>Ensures site study supplies (such as Non-IP, lab kits, etc.) are adequate for trial conduct</li>
<li>Scheduling of staff to cover subjects visit needs</li>
<li>Conducting staff reviews
</li>
<li>Work to assure enrollment goals of the study center are being met</li>
<li>Monitor subject visits</li>
<li>Support the team and investigators in overall conduct of the trials
</li>
<li>Follow the established monitoring SOP’s both corporate and clinical as well as protocol for specific monitoring plan</li>
<li>Work with QA/QC program prior to sponsor visit</li>
<li>Oversee employees work before monitoring</li>
<li>Review budgets that clinical research costs and physician’s costs are covered</li>
<li>Work with the corporate office for evaluating recruitment plans and study start up</li>
<li>Oversee training for all protocol trainings as well as required for regulations, compliance and up to date training</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Requirements</b></p>
<ul>
<li>5+ years of relevant experience as a clinical research associate or clinical research coordinator is required</li>
<li>1+ years of clinical research site management experience is required
</li>
<li>Certified Clinical Research Coordinator (CCRC), Certified Clinical Research Associate (CCRA) or equivalent certification, preferred.</li>
<li>Must have experience managing trials and clinical site staff.</li>
<li>Sound knowledge of FDA regulations, ICH-Good Clinical Practices (GCP) and ISOs.</li>
<li>Solid teamwork, organizational, interpersonal, attention to detail, and problem-solving skills.</li>
<li>Strong written and verbal communication skills</li>
<li>An ability to consume, absorb, and analyze large amounts of information.</li>
<li>Ability to multi-task, work under time constraints, work independently and cooperatively with interdisciplinary teams.</li>
<li>Strong computer skills in appropriate software and related company clinical systems is required</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Benefits</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Competitive base salary
</li>
<li>Comprehensive benefits: Medical, Dental, Vision, Life , FSA and 401K</li>
<li>Strong vacation and paid time off package</li>
</ul>
