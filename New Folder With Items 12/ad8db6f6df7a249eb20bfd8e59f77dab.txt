<h2>Tax Auditor Supervisor (Limited Term</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<div>This limited-term position supplements DOR (Department of Revenue) staffing during a significant IT project. This project is an assignment that is projected to exist for several years. The incumbent supervised tax audit personnel within the Division of Revenue, with primary responsibility for ensuring the proper computation of gross receipts taxes, license fees, and similar taxes requiring an in-depth understanding of the tax audit function and substantive tax laws to be able to properly supervise other tax audit personnel.
</div>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Essential Functions</b></h2>
<div>Essential functions are fundamental, core functions common to all positions in the class series and are not intended to be an exhaustive list of all job duties for any one position in the class. Since class specifications are descriptive and not restrictive, incumbents can complete job duties of similar kind not specifically listed here.
<ul>
<li>Performs audits of taxpayers accounting records such as invoices, sales journals, general ledgers, bank statements, and inventory entries to determine compliance with tax laws, rules, and regulations; assesses liabilities and explains assessments to taxpayers.<br>
</li>
<li>Conducts interviews/field investigations, attends meetings or works at the taxpayer's location to gather pertinent information, records and/or documentation.<br>
</li>
<li>Conducts research of corporate, business and personal income tax, licensing and withholding tax records, motor fuel receipts and/or sales and usage tax records to determine whether a field audit is necessary.<br>
</li>
<li>Estimates tax liability from taxpayer records or other available information.<br>
</li>
<li>Evaluates financial information and may arrange installment schedules for payment or prepares legal documents to enter judgement against delinquent taxpayers or secures legal authority from the court to garnish wages.<br>
</li>
<li>Interprets laws and regulations relating to the administration and enforcement of tax provisions.<br>
</li>
<li>Applies Generally Accepted Accounting Principles in the analysis of taxpayer records.<br>
</li>
<li>Compiles spreadsheets and audit notes; prepares audit reports and conclusion summaries.<br>
</li>
<li>Utilizes personal computer software to perform audit and analysis.
</li>
</ul>
</div>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Job Requirements</b></h2>
<div>
<p><b>JOB REQUIREMENTS for Tax Auditor Supervisor</b><br>
Applicants must have education, training and/or experience demonstrating competence in each of the following areas:</p>
<ul>
<li>Three years experience in tax auditing which includes auditing tax documents and records to determine tax liability in accordance with Generally Accepted Auditing Standards, including assessment of internal and external controls.
</li>
<li>Three years experience in interpreting laws, rules, regulations, standards, policies, and procedures.
</li>
<li>Six months experience in writing reports in a structured format containing an introduction, presenting the thesis, purpose or proposal with supporting evidence, explanation, facts, and analysis or examples with a conclusion or recommendations.
</li>
<li>Knowledge of staff supervision acquired through course work, academic training, training provided through an employer, or performing as a lead worker overseeing the work of others; OR supervising staff which may include planning, assigning, reviewing, and evaluating the work of others; OR supervising through subordinate supervisors a group of professional, technical, and clerical employees.</li>
</ul>
</div>
