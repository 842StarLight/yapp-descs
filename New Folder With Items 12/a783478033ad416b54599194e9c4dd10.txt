<p><b>Job Description:</b><br>
Candidate will work in a small technical group that is focused on providing our worldwide customer base with a variety of testing services including vibration, strain gage, and radio telemetry, both in-house and on-site. Typical applications for our services include experimental modal analysis, natural frequency measurements, strain gage installation, Campbell testing, and moment weighting with an emphasis on turbomachinery.</p>
<p></p>
<p>Initial placement will be supporting a multiple wind turbine testing project in Texas. Effort will focus on the assembly and installation of test hardware, followed by ongoing support and data acquisition.</p>
<p></p>

</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Prepare for tests by coordinating with SimuTech staff and customers</li>
<li>Perform tests in-house at SimuTech including quality assurance and strain gage installation</li>
<li>Perform tests at customer facilities that entail extensive travel and extended hours</li>
<li>Document test results and provide comprehensive reports to customers</li>

<p><b>About SimuTech Group:</b></p>
<p>SimuTech Group is an engineering services company specializing in the entire ANSYS suite of software, providing the most comprehensive coupled physics software tools, combining structural, thermal, CFD, acoustic, electromagnetic and system simulation capabilities all on one platform.</p>
<p></p>
<p>As the largest, full-service, provider of ANSYS Multiphysics Simulation Software in North America, we have offices in Atlanta, Chicago, Cincinnati, Cleveland, Denver, Rochester, San Diego, Seattle, Montreal and Toronto.</p>
<p></p>
<p>We provide sales, technical support, training, mentoring and consulting services for ANSYS simulation software.</p>
<br>
<p><br>
Training is provided at nine facilities throughout North America. Customized on-site training is also available.</p>
<p></p>
<p>A Key component of our services organization is the Test Team. Major aspects of this division involve wind turbines, gas and steam turbine blades, and aircraft vibration. Team members work closely with the simulation engineers to provide combined testing and analysis services.</p>
