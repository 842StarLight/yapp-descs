<b>Summary:</b>
<br>
<br>
To train with casino manager and learn overall operation of casino. Areas to be trained include the following: customer service; learning to make supervisory decisions that are consistent with Town Pump standards, policies, and procedures; understanding controllable expenses, sales increases, inside gross margin, shortage controls, and promotions programs; setting the proper example for co-workers in terms of professionalism, attitude, and teamwork.
<br>
<br>

<br>

High school diploma or general education degree (GED); or equivalent work experience; or equivalent combination of education and experience. Experience in the area of supervision and casino operations with a strong background in customer service and marketing is a plus.

<b>ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES:</b>
<br>
<br>
Under the direction of the Training Manager, the Manager in Training will perform the following:
<ul>
<li>Customer service to include communicating with customers, resolve complaints and inquiries.</li>
<li>
Daily bookwork using computer to record employee information, inventory and sale information.</li>
<li>
Make bank deposit.</li>
<li>
Fill in for absent employee.</li>
<li>
Order supplies and merchandise.</li>
<li>
Answer telephone and screen calls.</li>
<li>
Light maintenance and cleaning of store and restrooms.</li>
<li>
Stock cooler and shelves.</li>
<li>
Effectively prioritize, organize, delegate, and follow up on casino tasks to be accomplished.</li>
<li>
Security of all casino assets. Control of cash and inventories. Control of casino merchandise and labor hours.</li>
<li>
New employee hiring, orientation, and training.</li>
<li>
Merchandising, to include receiving, pricing, stocking, displaying, and rotating of bar inventories.</li>
<li>
Maintain and/or perform basic repairs of gaming machines and bar equipment. This includes sending in appropriate machines for repair.</li>
<li>
Audit gaming machines</li>
<li>
Coordinate with corporate office on casino promotions</li>
<li>
Ensure a safe environment for employees and customers.</li>
<li>
Inventory of merchandise.</li>
<li>
Proper entry of merchandise invoices.</li>
<li>
Disciplining and/or terminating problem employees.</li>
<li>
Enforcing all company policies and procedures.</li>
<li>
Supervisory decisions that are consistent with Town Pump Inc. standards, policies and procedures.</li>
</ul>
