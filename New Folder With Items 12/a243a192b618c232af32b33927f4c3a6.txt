<div>
<p>As part of the continued growth and innovation of our Oil &amp; Gas (O&amp;G) Sector, <b>ERM </b>has an opportunity for an experienced leader to join our global consulting firm as a key client-facing <b>Partner, Environmental Permitting Services for Midstream Oil &amp; Gas </b>and to become a shareholder in a business with Sustainability at its heart. We would consider strong candidates in ERM office locations throughout <b>North America, including our Gulf Coast, Mid-Atlantic, Mid-West, South Atlantic, or Northeast Regions </b>.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>We are looking for an established leader who is interested in being part of this thriving global community. This individual would need to be an outstanding professional who combines strong consulting and business development skills with a solid technical foundation to foster and expand a network of excellent client relationships in the O&amp;G industry with a focus on the midstream market, including natural gas, petroleum, natural gas liquid, and other product pipelines; liquefied natural gas (LNG) facilities; natural gas storage facilities; and other O&amp;G infrastructure. We seek an individual to help us drive the continued growth of ERM’s business through selling and delivering world-class services , recruiting and developing our next generation of leaders, and further advancing our reputation for supporting development of some of the world’s most complex projects.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>ERM’S MIDSTREAM O&amp;G SECTOR:
</p>
<p>ERM assists companies in planning, permitting, and managing compliance for midstream O&amp;G projects, including capital expansion projects as well as operation and maintenance of existing facilities. Our team works closely with clients to identify their needs and achieve their business goals in a manner that minimizes the environmental and social impact of O&amp;G activities throughout the lifecycle of their projects.
</p>
<p></p>
<p>ERM supports clients at every stage of project development, construction, and operation by:
</p>
<ul>
<li>Identifying environmental issues and public concerns, evaluating alternatives, and analyzing potential routes and sites;
</li>
<li>Assessing permit requirements and gauging cost and schedule implications;
</li>
<li>Conducting constructability reviews and environmental field surveys;
</li>
<li>Using state-of-the art Geographic Information System (GIS) technology to map and analyze environmental resources and potential constraints, compare alternatives, create map products, and present geographic information in various formats to meet the needs of project and agency personnel;
</li>
<li>Securing permits for new facilities, facility replacement or abandonment, and operation and maintenance activities;
</li>
<li>Developing mitigation strategies;
</li>
<li>Developing customized stakeholder and public outreach programs;
</li>
<li>Providing environmental training programs and environmental inspection/construction compliance services;
</li>
<li>Enabling compliance with existing and emerging environmental regulations;
</li>
<li>Serving as a third-party contractor to support agencies in developing Environmental Impact Statements and Environmental Assessments; and
</li>
<li>Providing post-construction monitoring to verify and document compliance with regulatory requirements.
</li>
</ul>
