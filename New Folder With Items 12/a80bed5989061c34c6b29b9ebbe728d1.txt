<h2>Service Advisor</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><b>Summary</b>:
</p>
<p>The Service Advisor job requires administrative, technical and sales skills. The expectation is for accuracy and timeliness related to opening jobs, posting transactions to jobs and for job closing. Develop technician schedule per job/customer request. In many cases, this position is the primary contact with our Service customers. Accordingly, the expectation is for a high level of professionalism regarding customer relations and the ability to promote all sales opportunities.</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>About Western Branch Diesel</b><br>
</p>
<p></p>
<p>Western Branch Diesel is a distributor and service provider for Detroit Diesel, John Deere, MTU, MTU Onsite Energy, Volvo Penta, WheelTime, and Allison Transmissions.<br>
</p>
<p></p>
<p>Headquartered in Portsmouth, Virginia, Western Branch Diesel operates out of 11 branches strategically located throughout Virginia, West Virginia, Ohio, Kentucky, and North Carolina. These branches provide sales and service to the construction and industrial, marine, mining and quarry, oil/gas, on-highway, and power-generation markets. The company’s 300+ team members include over 120 highly trained service technicians. For more company information, please visit our website www.westernbranchdiesel.com.<br>
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>Essential Duties and Responsibilities Include the Following:</b>
</p>
<p>The Service Advisor must support and comply with all policies and procedures established at the corporate or branch level.<br>
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>Professional Communication</b>
</p>
<ul>
<li>Answer customer Service Department inquiries with the intent to accomplish the following:
</li>
<li>Gather information using the WBD Service documents (including the physical inspection of the unit and validating the unit serial number prior to start of repair).
</li>
<li>Schedule new service appointments based on the nature of the complaint and shop capacity. This should be done in conjunction with the On-Highway Foreman
</li>
<li>Utilize all resources available on all units in for service to determine unit repair history and warranty coverage information.
</li>
<li>All customer interaction must be professional and courteous. Customer issues or complaints should be addressed promptly. Matters requiring escalation should be forwarded to the Service Manager. Under no circumstances should the Service Advisor engage in unprofessional argument with the customer.<br>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>Administrative Processing</b>
</p>
<ul>
<li>Managing all procedural, system, and policies
</li>
<li>Develop competitive customer cost estimates based on analysis of current market conditions and relevant company metrics including labor and parts costs, in order to ensure profitability.
</li>
<li>Issue and receive purchase orders for shop requested sublet repairs.
</li>
<li>Ensure that all outside expenses related to RO’s are charged to the appropriate jobs. Post expenses as they are incurred – including: Freight, Travel &amp; Meals, mileage, and EPA charges. The writer should be familiar enough with the jobs to identify any missing charges.
</li>
<li>Oversee current Work-in-Process (WIP) utilizing appropriate software applications continuously to ensure accurate and timely processing of all work orders. Any RO’s in WIP or on the Completed Log beyond the company standard should be addressed and resolved daily.
</li>
<li>Review low margin jobs with the Service Manager prior to job close.
</li>
<li>Service Advisors must gain an understanding of up sell opportunities and secure additional business whenever possible.
</li>
<li>A Service Advisor, in conjunction with the Foreman, must monitor and manage the Service process in order to meet target times, advance the RO stages.
</li>
<li>Moving labor for any reason other than an error is prohibited. It is WBD policy that jobs should stand on their own regardless of labor margins and any evidence showing time transfer to make a job look better will result in disciplinary action.
</li>
<li>A thorough understanding of warranty guidelines, as well as a general technical understanding of our products, is required to accurately complete all warranty transactions.<br>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>Profitability</b>
</p>
<ul>
<li>Maximize recovery of mileage, freight, EPA and other job-related expenses.
</li>
<li>Minimize and maintain expense levels on each quote to ensure they do not exceed optimum levels for the amount of revenue being produced.
</li>
<li>Review repair order pricing to assure jobs are competitively priced compared to current market conditions.<br>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>

</li></ul>
