<div><b>The Role</b></div>
<div>As a Senior Life Underwriter, you have the responsibility of underwriting individual life insurance applications up to $5,000,000 up to age 85 on a cross functional underwriting team. In the underwriting capacity you have the responsibility to evaluate risk to determine individual’s insurability adhering to medical &amp; financial underwriting guidelines.</div>
<br>
<div></div>
<div><b>Responsibilities</b></div>
<ul>
<li>Evaluates and handles all underwriting aspects of formal &amp; informal life insurance applications</li>
<li>Underwrites cases with a graduated amount limit up to $2,000,000 to age 70 with less than 5 years underwriting experience and up to $5,000,000 to age 85 with 5 years or more of underwriting experience</li>
<li>Identifies trends and issues and offers the best underwriting solutions</li>
<li>Provides second signature review</li>
<li>Works on assigned projects pertaining to underwriting and enterprise initiatives</li>
<li>Understanding of sales themes and concepts of varying levels of complexity</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Understand underwriting policies, guidelines, practices and procedures, and has the ability to influence and champion changes to improve processes while maintaining mortality expectations</li>
<li>Responsible for technical underwriting knowledge of the Swiss Re underwriting manual</li>
<li>Interprets documents such as policies, proposals, claims and financial reports</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Interacts with customers to enhance relationships</li>
<li>Demonstrates the ability to adapt and customize content and communication style to fit a variety of specific external and internal target audiences such as: agents, brokers, distribution partners, and all levels of employees</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Demonstrates dependability, flexibility and sound judgment by adhering to schedules and meeting all productivity, quality and performance standards as well as taking ownership for your actions</li>
<li>Determines whether to accept, modify, or decline a risk through evaluation of an individual's medical history or physical condition, occupational hazard, and financial background.</li>
<li>Collaborates effectively with stakeholders to gather information necessary for decision making and to select profitable risks.</li>
<li>Represents the company by responding to questions during agency visits and developing relationships with agents and partners</li>
<li>Provides training to less experienced team members on underwriting procedures and guidelines</li>
<li>Enhances departmental outcomes by sharing knowledge with team members and providing advice on more complex underwriting determinations.</li>
<li>Communicates effectively with customers to facilitate placement of business</li>
<br>
</ul>
<div></div>
<div><b>Education</b></div>
<ul>
<li>Undergraduate degree or 4+ years of comparable work experience</li>
<li>Industry Exams – ALU 101, 201, 202, &amp; LOMA encouraged</li>
<li>EKG Certification preferred but not required</li>
<br>
</ul>
<div></div>
<div><b>Experience</b></div>
<ul>
<li>3 – 6+ years of experience in Underwriting Individual Life Insurance</li>
<li>Demonstrated Excellence in: Problem Solving / Analysis; Comprehension of Complex Information; Influencing Others; Risk Assessment; Demonstrated decision making using sound judgment; Negotiation Skills; Managing Conflict</li>
<li>Developed Skills in: analyzing and interpreting complex financial documents including profit/loss statements, income statements, and balance sheets</li>
<li>Developed Skills in: Estate Growth Calculations and Life Expectancy Calculations</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Strong Written and Verbal Communication skills</li>
<li>Intermediate in: Microsoft Office (Outlook, Word, PowerPoint, Excel)</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>A demonstrated track record of working with minimal supervision, being a self-starter and working with others in a team environment</li>
<br>
</ul>
