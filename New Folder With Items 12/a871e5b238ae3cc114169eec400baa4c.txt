<h2>Maintenance Electrical Technologist</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">Description
<br><br>
Dow is seeking a Maintenance Electrical Technologist to support its businesses at its Plaquemine, Louisiana site. The Maintenance Electrical Technologists will provide the technical expertise to assist the Work Coordinators and Scheduler in performing electrical work and to participate in the installation, acceptance testing, commissioning, maintenance, troubleshooting, and repair of complex electrical systems and equipment.
<br><br>
This job provides the technical expertise to calibrate, repair, evaluate, and apply test equipment for the determination of the service condition and acceptability of a wide variety of electrical equipment.
<br>The job requires a "hands on" technician applying expertise daily in those areas to insure the safe and reliable operation of the site process plants to maximize production as well as to maintain the lowest long term cost of ownership of the site facilities.
<br><br>
This candidate will also be required to mentor the contract electricians in the assigned Maintenance area when necessary.
<br><br>
Responsibilities / Duties
<br>Diagnoses and corrects equipment problems.
<br>Calibrates and aligns equipment.
<br>Completes preventative maintenance activities.
<br>Checks sizing of components and parts.
<br>Specifies, requisitions, and installs parts and equipment.
<br>Coordinates maintenance spare parts.
<br>Assists in planning, prioritizing, and scheduling maintenance through planner, work coordinator and operations input.
<br>Sets up and calibrates, tests, measures and repairs all types of controls and instruments with pneumatic, electrical, and electronic variables.
<br>Repairs, maintains and installs equipment and machinery which is used in the process.
<br>Troubleshoots and repairs malfunctions and documents failure analysis.
<br>Collects equipment operation and history data to facilitate reliability improvements.
<br>Works within established Maintenance Work Processes and helps resolve issues that surface as a result of planning and scheduling work.
<br><br>
<b>Required Qualifications:</b><br>
An Associate’s degree (or Technical diploma) in the field of Electrical Technology/Electronic from an accredited Educational Institute (College/ School)
<br>OR
<br>4+ years relevant military experience
<br>OR
<br>A minimum of 3 years' experience working in electrical and/or maintenance related activities required.
<br>Ability to demonstrate the use of test equipment such as Multimeter, Continuity Tester, Megohmmeter (Megger), and Voltage tester such as a Fluke T5-1000 or Tegam.
<br>Demonstrated ability to read and interpret a SEOLD -- Safe Electrical One Line Diagram and identify and understand auxiliary circuits associated with each piece of equipment.
<br>Working knowledge of the operation and construction of low and medium voltage equipment including motor starters, circuit breakers, control circuits, induction/synchronous DC motors, motor drives, UPS systems and other power conversion devices.
<br>Working knowledge of the National Electrical Code
