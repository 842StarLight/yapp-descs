<div><b>Graduate Development Program – Agribusiness Underwriting Job Summary</b></div>
<br>
<div></div>
<div>Starting Salary: $48,500</div>
<br>
<div></div>
<div>When it’s time to start, advance, or change your career, proper guidance and direction are essential. At Westfield Insurance, we invest heavily in preparing you for a career within the insurance industry.<br>
</div>
<div></div>
<div>The Graduate Development Program (GDP) prepares recent college graduates, experienced professionals looking for a change of industry, and veterans for an exciting career at Westfield. The GDP combines a variety of learning techniques, including:</div>
<br>
<div></div>
<ul>
<li><div>Classroom-based training on insurance-related topics, critical thinking, marketing, collaboration, and customer service.</div></li>
<li><div>
Observation and practice elements are included to increase exposure to the business unit and to complete assigned research topics.</div></li>
<li><div>
Self-study eLearning to support project work and individual development.</div></li>
<li><div>
Team-building activities with peers.</div></li>
<li><div>
Networking with leaders across the organization.</div></li>
<li><div>
Coaching and mentoring throughout the program from experienced employees.
</div></li>
</ul>
<div>The GDP training will take place in our home office in Westfield Center, OH and will begin in June of 2020.</div>
<br>
<div></div>
<div>Agribusiness underwriters dive deep into a niche area of agriculture business. At Westfield, we rely on technical underwriting skills, sales acumen, and strong relationships with our agency partners to achieve growth and profit within each territory. By joining our team, you become a part of a high-performing group that is critical to our success. Working as an Agribusiness Underwriter involves:</div>
<br>
<div></div>
<ul>
<li><div>Analyzing and making decisions on the acceptance, pricing, and conditions offered to individual commercial customers on a new and renewal basis.</div></li>
<li><div>
Networking and building relationships with insurance agents.</div></li>
<li><div>
Territory management responsibilities for production, retention, and profit of commercial lines business within a defined territory.</div></li>
<li><div>
Developing, coordinating and monitoring agency business plans.</div></li>
</ul>
<br>
