<h2>Manager, Logistics ECNA</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>Oversees the planning and controls the flow of materials, products, services and related systems information from point of origin to point of delivery. Areas of responsibility include but are not limited to supply chain services, inventory control, or critical parts availability, material handling, import-export licensing, third-party warehousing and shipping/receiving activities. Develops and implements logistics plans, budgets, and procedures to maximize compliance with customer needs within budget constraints. Provides financial reporting and reconciliation of inventories. Exhibits thorough understanding of international Letters of Credit and other forms of banking documents and international shipments. Interacts with vendors and peers in Manufacturing, Sales, Finance, and Product Development personnel to optimize systems and procedures. Selects, develops, and evaluates personnel to ensure the efficient operation of the function.
<p></p>
<p><b>Position Summary
</b></p>
<p></p>
<p>This position leads critical logistics activities in the supply chain including outbound finished goods transportation planning, inbound raw-material transportation planning, carrier management, fleet management, freight bill reconciliation, new carrier acquisition, and carrier on-boarding.
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>Essential Job Functions
</b></p>
<p></p>
<ul>
<li>Facilitates on-time delivery by managing daily logistics operations of the inbound raw material transportation planning and outbound finished goods transportation planning processes and teams
</li>
<li>Manages existing GPS tracking software on all mobile assets, makes recommendations for upgrades or replacement of software solutions
</li>
<li>Ensures all bulk trailer equipment meets quality requirements and is utilized fully
</li>
<li>Manages all titling and registration of companywide tractors, trailers and other assets.
</li>
<li>Actively engages with truck, rail &amp; ocean carriers and recommends preferred shipper of choice to upper management
</li>
<li>Drives freight management optimization and improvement objectives towards improving cost and customer service
</li>
<li>Ensures accurate and on-time payment of carriers and partners
</li>
<li>Ensures departmental adherence to following established Supply Chain work processes, controls, procedures &amp; practices
</li>
<li>Maintains active relationships with key 3rd Party partners including 3PL’s, carriers, and 3rd Party Warehouses
</li>
<li>Helps develop logistics strategy for short and long terms needs. Drives strategy execution in department
</li>
<li>Drives technology and best practice adoption in department
</li>
<li>Administers key HR processes for direct reports i.e., performance management, employee development, compensation, etc.
</li>
<li>Monitors and communicates results of key supply chain metrics for his / her area
</li>
<li>Provides input for, maintains, and adheres to freight budget
</li>
<li>Ensures processes associated with on-time delivery are in place and followed
</li>
<li>Assists in troubleshooting and resolving Distribution or Supply Chain Service issues
</li>
<li>Participates in “Continual Improvement” events; acts as Process Owner for actions impacting his or her area of responsibility
</li>
<li>Participates as an active member of the Supply Chain Leadership Team (and Pueblo Plant Leadership Team if in Pueblo)
</li>
<li>Ensures that all members of department maintain training and follow training schedules.
</li>
<li>Responsible for managing, maintaining, and complying with key SOX controls in materials logistics including revenue recognition, cost recognition, and title transfer.
</li>
<li>Stays informed on all federal, state and local regulations, requirements and guidelines impacting distribution
</li>
</ul>
<ul><li>Abides by all safety procedures and practices applicable to location and position; participates in appropriate safety training; demonstrates commitment to and support of EH&amp;S principles and values; champions safety daily
</li></ul>
<ul><li>Performs other duties assigned by management that fall within the generally expected scope of this position
</li></ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>Experience Requirement
</b></p>
<p></p>
<p>Minimum 7 years in logistics activities such as dispatch, load planning, procurement, or inventory control; preferably in a high-volume, liquid bulk HAZMAT environment
</p>
<ul>
<li>Minimum 5 years supervisory experience
</li>
<li>Strong knowledge of Supply Chain Work Processes i.e. Logistics, Materials Planning, Inventory Control, Order Fulfillment, Etc.
</li>
<li>Power user of logistics management software platforms preferably in SAP LE, SD, and/or MM modules
</li>
<li>Prior participation in Continuous Improvement activities a plus
</li>
<li>Customer service focus including the ability to speak diplomatically; problem solver; eagerness to satisfy customers; ability to work with diverse personalities
</li>
<li>Supervisory skills; ability to manage, motivate, and coach; ability to influence and negotiate successfully with others
</li>
<li>Ability to multitask and prioritize; excellent planning and organization skills; teambuilding skills
</li>
<li>Understanding of Federal, state and local environmental regulations impacting distribution
</li>
<li>Excellent English communications skills, oral and written; proven listening skills; ability to read various charts and tables and apply information
</li>
<li>Excellent computer skills including Microsoft Office Suite, various email and internet applications, sales databases, financial systems, and other programs or networks normally used in a customer service environment
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>Education Requirement
</b></p>
<p></p>
<ul><li>Bachelor’s Degree in Supply Chain Management, Logistics, Engineering, Business Management, or related field
</li></ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>Work Environment
</b></p>
<p></p>
<p>This employee will work in multiple environments including a temperature-controlled warehouse, an office environment, and outdoors. Will be subject to occasional extremes of cold or heat when outdoor work is required. Will experience exposure to noise and vibrations from warehousing equipment typical in a warehouse environment. The noise level in the work environment is usually moderate. The employee should not have regular exposure to fumes, odors, toxic conditions, dust or poor ventilation.
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>Physical Demands
</b></p>
<p></p>
<p>While performing the duties of this job, the employee is regularly required to walk, sit, speak and hear. The employee is required to stand, use hands to finger, handle, or feel; reach with hands and arms; the employee may be required, on occasion, to climb or balance, stoop, kneel, or crouch. The employee may occasionally lift and/or move up to fifty (50) pounds. Specific vision abilities required by this job include close vision, peripheral vision, depth perception and ability to adjust focus. Employee may be required to travel by air, rail, bus, or automobile.</p>
