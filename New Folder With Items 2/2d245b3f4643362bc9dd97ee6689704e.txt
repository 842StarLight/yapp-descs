<h2>Commercial/Industrial Supervisor</h2>

<div>
<p><b>POSITION TITLE: COMMERCIAL/INDUSTRIAL</b><b> </b><b>SUPERVISOR</b></p>

<b>

<br>
</div>
<div>
<b>SUMMARY:</b>
</div>
<p>The supervisor is responsible for planning, supervising, and evaluating the work performance of commercial assessors and assistant clerk; participate in the selection of new commercial employees.
</p>
<p>Performs the most complex and highly specialized assessments of industrial and high end commercial properties; reviews assessment values and data changes performed by staff for accuracy, completeness, and compliance with all state regulations; conducts field inspections; takes measurements of buildings; collects data, type of construction, and other statistics and conditions affecting values; analyzes statistics and data; for ad valorem taxation, and keep current in his/her assigned jurisdiction.</p>
<p>
The C/I Supervisor must have in depth knowledge of the appraisal process, assessment law, real estate practices and principles, and personality to communicate with the public. Essential is the ability to analyze income and expense statements, knowledge of income capitalization, and reading cost manuals. The Commercial Supervisor will assist the Chief Assessor with LERTA and PURTA programs. Enter field data and create detail sketches of improvements to property utilizing a Computer Assisted Mass Appraisal (CAMA) system.</p>
<p>
The Commercial Supervisor assists the Board Solicitor with ongoing assessment appeal litigation by testifying as an expert witness, answering discovery requests, certifying records, and providing other assistance as requested.<br>
</p>
<div></div>
<div>
<b>ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES</b> include the following:</div>
<br>
<div></div>
<ul>
<li>Setting work priorities, planning, organizing, assigning, advising, assisting, motivating and training as necessary. Evaluate the work performance of assigned staff and participate in the selection of new assessors.</li>
<li>Assessors assigned in districts to perform technical work required in assessing real property by gathering real property including, put not limited to, measuring structures, physical inspection of exterior and interior of buildings, photographing property, and noting quality and intended use of the property.</li>
<li>Value properties uniformly in accordance with Pennsylvania law, enter field data and create detailed sketches of improvements to property utilizing a Computer Assisted Mass Appraisal (CAMA) system.
</li>
<li>Review appeals; site inspection; research and review current pricing guides and market trends; attend tax appeal hearings and testifies regarding the basis and factors used in determining the market value and assessment placed on property.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Provides testimony as an expert industrial and commercial property appraiser witness on behalf of Montgomery County,
</li>
<li>Reviews building permits issued to assess new construction, additions and alterations; reviews and evaluates drawings and plans to determine quality of construction.
</li>
<li>Analyzes sales information to assist in the development of trends, market studies and other informational works.
</li>
<li>Ability to keep accurate and detailed records using CAMA system; apply organizational skills efficiently with attention to detail, process.</li>
<li>Answer questions regarding assessments evaluations, appeal process and general questions regarding assessment administration.</li>
<li>Establish and maintain cooperative, effective, and productive working relationships using tact, patience and courtesy.</li>
<li>Attends continuing educational classes to maintain state required certifications and to obtain further and more in-depth knowledge above and beyond the requirements of the State of Pennsylvania.</li>
</ul>
<ul><li>Maintain accurate records for properties that are in the LERTA programs. Also, explaining to taxpayers, taxing authorities and governmental agencies LERTA procedures.<br>
</li></ul>
