What you'll do
<br>--------------
<br><br>
The Chief of Staff is a key leadership position on the revenue team. This highly visible role is a key partner to the Chief Revenue Officer to drive operational excellence and deliver repeatable business results. This position assists the Chief Revenue Officer in determining and managing priorities, driving operational cadence, and represents the Chief Revenue Officer across many scenarios. The ideal candidate must be passionate about high quality repeatable processes that produce short, mid and long-term results. Must possess excellent analytical, critical reasoning, interpersonal, communication, influencing and leadership skills. Success depends on building rapport and credibility with multiple stakeholders across the organization.
<br><br>
<ul>
<li>Sales Cadence – The ultimate measure of success is continuing to drive revenue as a collective organization. The Chief of Staff is responsible for ensuring programs are in place to drive sales cadence globally in service of management to the number and high utilization of resources. This includes ensuring we are driving pipeline driving activities, QBRs, large deal reviews, executive involvement, and high deal closing velocity. The Chief of Staff should constantly be evaluating whether we are managing and allocating resources effectively.</li>
<li>Sales Leadership Team – Partner with global revenue leaders to ensure an accurate pulse on priorities on a weekly cadence. This includes driving the agenda for Functional Leads and meeting with global leaders (and ideally teams) regularly. In addition, this role should include active participation and contribution in quarterly and annual reviews. This leader should ensure best practices are shared across sales teams and communicate critical information from global teams to cross-functional partners.</li>
<li>Drive cross-functional buy-in for sales needs – Maintain regular communication with stakeholders in product, marketing, compliance and legal teams and partner with cross-functional stakeholders to prioritize global needs.</li>
<li>Effectively triage escalations – Our global teams often rely heavily on quick root-cause analyses and fast information sharing. Time sensitive escalations may include driving cross-functional conversations to get to the bottom of complex issues that have become a business blocker or helping ensure fast turnaround for specific prospect and customer needs.</li>
<li>Communication – Ensure consistent impactful communication to the revenue team, customers, partners, and other key stakeholders. This includes driving internal transparency and playing an active role in company-wide escalations. This also includes sharing revenue narratives company wide (e.g. win/losses at BEER meeting).</li>
<li>Catch-All – Things to do are constantly changing. The Chief of Staff should be open to taking on various initiatives that are important at any given time. Some examples may include launches of new offices, ensuring new leaders ramp successfully and teams with capacity gaps get additional coverage.</li>
</ul>
