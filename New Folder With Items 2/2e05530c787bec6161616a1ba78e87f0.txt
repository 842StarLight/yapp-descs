<h2>Sr Manager PMO - CO</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<p>Danone North America is the largest Benefit Corporation in the world. Our mission is to bring health through food to as many people as possible.</p>
<p>Danone North America is looking for a Sr Manager PMO - CO in Broomfield, Colorado.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p><b>POSITION DESCRIPTION:
</b></p>
<p><b>Summary:
</b></p>
<p>The SR Project manager works with Program Champion (from functional group with greatest interest in Project succeeding) to define Program Scope and receive approval from Danone North America Senior Leaders to commence work on a Program. Once approved, SR PM obtains resources from functional areas (Research &amp; Innovation (R&amp;I)/product development, R&amp;I/packaging, Marketing, Creative, Supply Chain, etc.) and builds master project timeline based on timelines from each functional area, modifying &amp; coordinating the functional areas’ timelines to build a feasible, unified, master program timeline.
</p>
<p>SR PMs run projects against these timelines, holding weekly/bi-weekly project team meetings and less frequent, periodic Gate meetings. PMs ensure project teams’ work (in Stages) is preparing the project team to meet the Gate criteria so a decision can be made for each project at its next Gate (hold, kill, send back for rework, or move it forward to the next Stage). The Gate keepers (decision makers) are the Business Unit (BU) cross-functional representatives for product Programs (BU General Manager, Senior Marketer, R&amp;D, PM representing Supply Chain, Sales, Consumer Insights, etc. who range from Senior Managers to SVP levels).
</p>
<ul>
<li>The Types/Complexity of Projects Managed by SR Project Managers
<ul>
<li>Packaging with no formulation changes</li>
<li>Line extensions / refreshes where product formulation is similar to existing formulas</li>
<li>Line extensions / refreshes / innovation with formulation issues different from existing products, with support provided by Managers/Senior Program Managers</li>
<li>New innovation launches in new markets, categories that may or may not require new technology</li>
<li>PMO-related technology development</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Manages Programs: master-plans, executes, communicates about, manages risk, etc.</li>
<li>Manages Program revisions and “Scope Creep”
</li>
<li>Creates basic processes to improve efficiency of projects</li>
<li>Produces reports, timelines, etc. on program status, gate deliverables, etc. for communication</li>
<li>Obtains formal approvals for the initiation of a program, prior to commencement
</li>
<li>Ensures that required resources are assigned to programs and appropriately utilized</li>
<li>Balances competing objectives and overcomes constraints to deliver results
</li>
<li>Ensures Stage Gate work on Programs aligns to WW Enterprise Gate standards</li>
<li>Understands Stage Gate well and ensures his/her programs adhere to Danone North America standards</li>
<li>Shows understanding of EPM and linkage between Stage Gate &amp; EPM processes / criteria</li>
<li>Prepares Project Champions, with help from Managers/Sr Managers for successful Gate meetings</li>
<li>Constantly drives Stage Gate/EPM adherence &amp; offers ideas and works with Manager/Sr Managers to drive SG/EPM evolution Enterprise-wide</li>
<br>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>Education and Experience (Requirements):</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Four-year undergraduate degree in engineer, business or other relevant discipline required</li>
<li>Four to eight years of PM experience in fast moving consumer goods company in similar conditions (high number or projects interacting together, managing big projects, in a fast pace rhythm)</li>
<li>Require technical background in manufacturing, R&amp;D, engineering</li>
<li>Prefer PMP certified</li>
<li>Prefer experience with Project &amp; Portfolio management software</li>
<br>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>Knowledge, Skills and Abilities:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Understands end to end business</li>
<li>Has well developed communication skills - oral, written, listening.
</li>
<li>Has self-confidence to work independently and make important decisions independently<br>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p>Leadership Skills required in role and which will grow through PMO employment.
</p>
<p>Sr Project Managers are expected to meet some of the leadership skills listed below, but not all.</p>
<ul>
<li>Program Leadership – Establish vision, exhibit “can do” attitude, treat adversity as opportunity vs setback, manage emotional tenor of team, hold individuals accountable to deadlines/timelines, establish trust, establish importance of results vs effort, promote healthy conflict, and require team’s commitment to shared decisions</li>
<li>Communications – Establish/use standard formats for exchanging information; make communication action-oriented, concise, and timely; keep team focused and shut-down tangents; set agendas in advance and follow them; listen carefully and seek first to understand
</li>
<li>Planning – Insist on clear, written scope statements; collaboratively plan projects with project team; have others create their own deadlines; create end-to-end timelines that reflects functional requirements and interdependencies; and manage project to timelines.
</li>
<li>Performance measurement &amp; management – Collect and analyze status of project, require no-blame assessments of variances to plan, use plan to manage project, hold regular status meetings, encourage “no surprises,” measure and report performance vs plan</li>
<li>Risk management – Identify, work to mitigate/take advantage of, and respond to key risks; seek to understand probability of risk realization and impact; create contingencies; use past experiences and plan for them</li>
<li>Team building – Guide team on how to navigate organization and get work done; ensure team members get support needed from team and their functions to be successful; build trust and accountability to shared results and sense of team; insist on best effort; motivate by appreciation</li>
<li>Coaching &amp; people development – Foster personal and professional growth of colleagues, teammate, and stakeholders; insist each team member provides his/her best effort; provide position feedback regularly and constructive feedback without judgment frequently; and delegate appropriately</li>
<li>Decision making – demonstrate and instill in others confidence when making decisions under uncertainty; leverage others’ input and knowledge to make best possible decisions; emphasize making best decisions with information available at time; use structured decision-making approach
</li>
<li>Problem solving – recognize when problems exist, identify possible solutions, propose options and gain buy in; implement change management and solutions; seek inputs on facts and opinions; make problem solving a collective process to engender buy-in and achieve best solution</li>
<li>Negotiating &amp; Managing conflict – create clarity on what will be done and goals; seek solutions that meet needs vs positions taken/espoused; seek innovate/creative solutions; evaluate solutions against objective criteria; preserve and use challenges to build working relationships</li>
<li>Organizational effectiveness – Manage up and down the organization to get things done; show respect regardless of position; leverage network of contacts; understand and work within formal and information structures of organization</li>
<li>Customer relations – Maintain good relationship between project team and customers/ stakeholders; take partnership attitude with customers/ stakeholders; seek to understand customer/ stakeholder needs; be responsive to customers/stakeholders; seek feedback
</li>
<li>Perspective – Take broad organization POV vs functional/team view; Seek to see total business perspective; understand role of project/team/business in context of its’ role within total enterprise; invest to learn business</li>
<li>Visioning – Continually seek to identify gaps between current state and capability set the business strategy requires; anticipate coming changes that will require reaction/accommodation; identify options and build support of those that would be affected by change; make case for change and move organization toward solution</li>
<br>
</ul>
