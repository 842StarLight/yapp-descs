<h2>Construction Project Estimator</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>CASE Design/Remodeling, an award-winning design/build firm in San Jose, is seeking a residential construction project estimator to join our team. We are a 30-person company of remodeling professionals, highly skilled in planning, designing, and building everything from kitchens and baths to whole-house remodels and additions. We are looking for an enthusiastic team player, ready to hit the ground running accomplishing the following critical responsibilities. This is a new position at our company working directly for our President /CEO and the successful candidate will have the opportunity and require the skill to transition our process to a new model working in cooperation with our project developers (sales), designers, and project managers.</p>
<p>
SUMMARY</p>
<p>
The Construction Project Estimator is responsible for managing the activities associated with developing, populating, organizing, and maintaining our estimating system and data base of items and assemblies, working closely with production, sales, and providing accurate and complete estimates for projects in design by performing the following duties. Performance measurements include:</p>
<ul><li>Meeting design and sales milestones for projects in development</li></ul>
<ul><li>Accuracy of estimates compared to actuals measured by variances</li></ul>
<ul><li>Up-to-date and accurate maintenance of the data base aligned with current and evolving construction techniques, subcontractor pricing, and published sources of construction material costs and labor hours</li></ul>
<ul><li>Employee and manager satisfaction with the services provided</li></ul>
<p>
ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES include the following:</p>
<p>
Strategic and Operations Planning</p>
<p>
 Actively participates in strategic direction setting for the company as a key member of the business team. Key role in determining the need for alliances with and the roles of trade partners.</p>
<p>
 Owns the estimating process for the company and makes recommendations for enhancements, changes, and upgrades to the process, tools, and systems.</p>
<p>
Estimating and Contract Support</p>
<p>
  Assist the Project Developers with pre-contract pricing of integrated labor and material tasks, exterior finish materials, and subcontractor pricing.</p>
<p>
  Maintains an up-to-date estimating data base including the development of custom line items in the estimating system and assemblies for kitchens, baths, laundries, and additions.</p>
<p>
 Recommends construction material pricing changes based on variances and monitoring of key construction materials such as lumber and electrical.</p>
<p>
 Creates and maintains construction task description templates for remodeling contracts.</p>
<p>
 Works with accounting to ensure that integration of the estimating system with the accounting (QuickBooks) and project management (BuilderTrend) systems are straightforward, accurate, and produce consistent results</p>
<p>
Construction Training and Education</p>
<p>
 Develops and orchestrates and/or delivers a basic construction training curriculum for new design and sales personnel through a combination of field and outside training resources.</p>
<p>
Construction Process and Technology</p>
<p>
 Maintains proactive awareness of new energy efficiency residential construction methods and products and researches cost/benefit to homeowners and proposes key areas for adoption in our construction practices and product/service offerings.</p>
<p>
Miscellaneous Duties</p>
<p>
 As it relates to estimating and contract approval, ensure operations, plans, policies, procedures, and transition/migration plans are consistently executed in a timely manner aligned with the overall company and the project development and production teams’ goals/directions/objectives/processes.</p>
<p>
 Effectively communicates relevant estimating function related information, challenges, and opportunities to management and other departments.</p>
<p>
 Follows Case processes/standards, IT systems, and local and federal laws in the development of projects</p>
<p>
 Secures input from team members and trade partners to ensure their estimating needs were met and to get ideas for future development and growth.</p>
<p>
 Ensure actual costs are aligned with estimated costs through analysis or cost variance reporting, costing updates, and pro-actively obtaining trade partner price updates, etc.</p>
<p>
 Continually defines ways to increase internal team member satisfaction and paths to deepen those relationships.</p>
<p>
 Maintains internal forms that need to be updated to comply with policies and procedures.</p>
<p>
 Maintains the public, shared, and cloud drives and files so they maintain their organization and properly support those using them.</p>

QUALIFICATIONS</p>
<p>
To perform this job successfully, an individual must be able to perform each essential duty satisfactorily. The requirements listed below are representative of the knowledge, skill, and/or ability required. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions.</p>
<p>
EDUCATION and/or EXPERIENCE: 5 to 10 years related experience and/or training; or equivalent combination of education and experience in residential remodeling. A degree in construction management is highly desired. Must possess the following knowledge bases:</p>
<ul><li>Knowledge of construction principals and equipment capabilities</li></ul>
<ul><li>Knowledge of building construction, materials and methods</li></ul>
<ul><li>Knowledge of the laws and ordinances regulating building construction</li></ul>
<p>
LANGUAGE/COMMUNICATION SKILLS: Ability to read, and interpret documents such as construction contracts/agreements, drawin, procedure manuals, safety rules, estimates, and operating and maintenance instructions. Ability to respond to common inquires or complaints from customers or co-workers. This person needs to have the ability to write routine reports and correspondence, and to verbally present information to customers, co-workers, suppliers, and the general public.</p>
<p>
MATHEMATICAL SKILLS: Ability to add, subtract, multiply, and divide in all units of measure, using whole numbers, common fractions, and decimals. Ability to calculate figures and amounts such as discounts, commissions, proportions, and percentages, and to apply concepts of basic algebra and geometry.</p>
<p>
COMPUTER AND OFFICE SKILLS: Ability to operate computer software, to include MS Windows, MS Word, MS Excel, MS Outlook, and Web Browser. The individual needs to have the ability to learn the company’s BuilderTrend (client management and scheduling) and estimating programs. Ability to operate a copier and the company phone/voice mail system</p>
