<h2>Fleet Diesel Mechanic</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p><b>Overview</b></p>
<p>This position is responsible for preventive maintenance, troubleshooting, diagnosis and repair of fleet vehicles and equipment as assigned.</p>
<p><b>Responsibilities</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Maintain, troubleshoot and repair all fleet vehicles within assigned DS Services locations.</li>
<li>Respond to field service calls.</li>
<li>Assist in ordering mechanical components, services and parts.</li>
<li>Perform and document all phases of vehicle maintenance and repair, including diagnostics.</li>
<li>Maintain communication with drivers and management regarding maintenance and service of vehicles and equipment.</li>
<li>Maintain a unit history file for each vehicle.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Requirements</b></p>
<ul>
<li>High School Diploma or GED.</li>
<li>Experience with both diesel /propane vehicles and air brake systems.</li>
<li>Engine and Chassis Diagnostic software experience.</li>
<li>Focus on safe work practices and high quality while executing duties under minimal supervision.</li>
<li>Accurately record all maintenance activity on Company documentation.</li>
<li>Interactions with vehicle maintenance tracking software, work orders, and inventory management.</li>
<li>Work Flow -- Daily Vehicle Inspection Report (DVIR) and US DOT requirements for commercial vehicles.</li>
<li>Perform assigned tasks with minimal supervision.</li>
<li>Ability to lift parts and equipment weighing up to 50 pounds.</li>
<li>Must have own set of personal tools.</li>
<li>Ability to meet Department of Transportation (DOT) driver qualification policy requirements.</li>
<li>Valid Driver's License, CDL preferred; ability to obtain within six (6) months of entering the position.</li>
</ul>
