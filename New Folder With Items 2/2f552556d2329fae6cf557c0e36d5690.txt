<br>
<div>Would you like to help thousands of businesses drive breakthrough agility, innovation, and engineering efficiency? Do you have the technology depth and breadth to help set the standard for modern software development, influencing senior engineering leaders, software development teams, IT system administrators, customer CXOs, and the industry in general? Do you thrive in taking ambiguous ideas, opportunities, or problems and turning them into data-driven plans and initiatives to meaningfully impact enterprise customers? The Enterprise Modernization Program at AWS is on a mission to help enterprise customers transform their technology portfolio by refactoring traditional applications using modern development methods, architectures, &amp; tooling and we are seeking an executive leader for our team.<br>
<br>
As Head of the Enterprise Modernization Program, you will have the exciting opportunity to define the future of Enterprise IT, including a prescriptive framework for modern software development, customer offerings to build modern applications, and new products to support modernization at massive scale. You will work closely with the cross-functional global enterprise and industry virtual teams, including Business Development, Solutions Architecture, Professional Services, Training, Sales Enablement, Sales Operations, Marketing, Services/Product, and Sales teams. You must be comfortable with leading and influencing cross functional global teams in dynamic organizations, as most of your responsibilities will have interdependencies with other teams within AWS.<br>
<br>
To be successful in this role, you must understand what enterprise software teams need and bring deep technical experience in modern architecture patterns and practices of software development, specifically in building and designing web-scale systems on AWS using cloud native architectures (micro-services, containers, serverless) and refactoring monolithic applications using micro-services-based architectures. You will have deep enterprise IT domain expertise and appreciate how AWS can help enterprises transform their IT and business. You will need strong analytical acumen, synthesis, structuring, and problem-solving skills to translate ambiguous, and often incomplete, information into action plans and insights. You will need a strong bias for ‘invention’ and ‘simplification’, data-driven mechanisms, and effective metrics measurement. You will also need passion for narrative style writing and be comfortable developing compelling narrative memos.<br>
<br>
