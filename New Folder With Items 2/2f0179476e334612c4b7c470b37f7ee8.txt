<h2>Division Supply Chain Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div>
<p><b>Org Marketing Statement</b>
</p>With annual sales of $14.3 billion in fiscal year 2018, Parker Hannifin is the world's leading diversified manufacturer of motion and control technologies and systems, providing precision-engineered solutions for a wide variety of mobile, industrial and aerospace markets. The company has operations in 50 countries around the world. Parker has increased its annual dividends paid to shareholders for 62 consecutive fiscal years, among the top five longest-running dividend-increase records in the S&amp;P 500 index.<p><b>
Department Marketing Statement</b>
</p>Parker Aerospace is a global leader in the research, design, integration, manufacture, certification, and lifetime service of flight control, hydraulic, fuel and inerting, fluid conveyance, thermal management, lubrication, and pneumatic systems and components for aerospace and other high-technology markets. The company supports the world’s aircraft manufacturers, providing a century of experience and innovation for commercial and military aircraft.<p><b>
Essential Functions</b>
</p>Develop and manages the direction and daily activities of the division's materials and services with the focus on people, organization, process and information technology. This includes the S&amp;OP Process to support the manufacturing / processing cycle, and ensuring timely logistical processing of product. Responsible for embracing and driving lean enterprise systems throughout the division supply chain. Scope/Supervision and Interaction: _X_ Has Direct Reports ____ Does Not Have Direct Reports Position will directly report to the general manager with additional accountability to the group supply chain manager. Will manage the complete material control process including procurement, production/inventory control, and transportation logistics. Will interact with all levels of division management including Operations, Sales, Engineering, Quality Control, Marketing and Finance. Will coordinate best practices with other division supply chain managers and the group supply chain manager. Key supplier and key customer contact essential. Direct reports will include division procurement manager, division PC&amp;L manager, compliance manager, and logistics manager and supplier development engineer where applicable.<p><b>
Responsibilities</b>
</p>• Manages the materials function within the division considering manufacturing capacity, schedules workloads, recommends in house manufacturing vs. outsourcing, quality control and workforce balance. • Establishes and maintains premier customer service. • Leads all Supply Chain Management activities within the division. This will include establishing preferred supplier selection criteria based on supplier evaluations of price, quality, delivery, certifications, compliance with governmental procurement regulations and group programs. • Will establish and implement standard/automated re-order procedures. Will develop crisis management and supplier backup contingency programs. • Negotiates long term contracts in coordination with group staff and policies. • Responsible for continuous improvements programs with suppliers. • Responsible for managing the division operational inventory to ensure adequate stocking levels for immediate production items and forecasting for future requirements. • Will establish and implement standard/automated delivery procedures for just in time supplier stocking items. • Responsible for facilitation of the S&amp;OP Process. • Accountable for managing the division's overall strategic production planning practices taking into consideration short term/long term production requirements and integrating materials availability. • Will establish and implement procedures for special order request, engineering changes and part substitutions, and sales market demand for products. • Responsible for integrating the division with electronic interfaces throughout the supply chain. • Manage the division’s transportation logistics to ensure distribution of products direct to customers, or through distribution centers / warehouses are completed with optimum service at the most efficient cost. • Represents the supply chain at the division level. • Provides leadership by promoting team concepts, coaching, counseling, retaining, mentoring, training, talent management, and individual development. • Drives a culture of accountability and results through strategy deployment and effective performance management.<p><b>
Qualifications</b>
</p>5-7 year’s managerial experience in supply chain management. 4 year college degree in business, engineering, or materials management required with certification in supply chain management preferred. Possesses analytical and technical skills required to understand business practices and recommend proper automation opportunities. Must have advanced computer skills and be able to understand electronic data processing. Systems (IT) experience beneficial. Excellent written, verbal, and interpersonal, communication skills for effective interface with all internal and external contacts. Must be an innovator and embrace lean enterprise principles and practices. Practical experience in implementing lean technologies and practices. Excellent problem solving skills.
