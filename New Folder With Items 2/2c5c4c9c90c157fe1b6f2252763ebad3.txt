<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Overview
</b></h2>
<div><div><div><p>Under general direction, the purpose of this position is to perform district-based duties associated with processing business and other operational records. Some analysis of solutions to situations is necessary to complete work; department resource for procedural direction for support staff. This position is that of a team leader or of a position that utilizes a narrowly defined area of technical expertise. Employees in this classification are responsible for maintaining accountability for a wide variety of property and equipment, to include inventory, property recording, and maintaining and updating all tangible property above a prescribed value. Position coordinates the maintenance of records and ensures reconciliation of official district property data through a computerized information system. Performs related work as directed.
</p></div></div></div>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Responsibilities and Qualifications
</b></h2>
<div><div><div>
<p><b>EXAMPLES OF ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS</b><br>
<br>
Responds to internal and external customers in a timely, accurate, courteous and empathetic manner representing OCPS in a positive light.<br>
<br>
Tracks tangible property valued at $1,000.00 and above district wide in accordance with state statutes, policies, and regulations; records and maintains tangible property.<br>
<br>
Analyzes and reviews property report for new tangible property; records and inputs assets to receive accounts.<br>
<br>
Creates and maintains mileage program on all district vehicles.<br>
<br>
Schedules preventive maintenance on white fleet vehicles.<br>
<br>
Sets-up all District vehicles in the Fuel Program and create fuel keys for vehicle and lawn maintenance equipment for various departments.<br>
<br>
Creates, prepares, and processes applications/affidavits for purchase or transfer of vehicle titles and license plates district wide; maintains vehicle records for all vehicles from acquisitions to disposal.<br>
<br>
Keeps property records current by adjusting property transfers, lost, stolen, or deleted items.<br>
<br>
Records all transfers, acquisitions, changes, and/or deletions to existing property records, to include all related data processing files.<br>
<br>
Transports or coordinates the transportation of excess equipment to designated central location for sale or disposal.<br>
<br>
Inspects quantity and quality of goods received against specifications, and accepts or rejects such goods accordingly.<br>
<br>
Maintains a computer data base of property control, property accountability, and all inventory received.<br>
<br>
Coordinates workload and schedules of clerical support staff.<br>
<br>
Advises workgroup of departmental policies and procedures.<br>
<br>
Provides guidance and trains employees in workgroup.<br>
<br>
Maintains budget account strips, keeping balances, reconciling monthly reports, purchase orders, invoices, for the entire department or work location.<br>
<br>
Prepares invoices for billing customers. Analyzes vendor invoices and researches any variances.<br>
<br>
May be responsible for contacting the districtâ€™s worker's compensation offices to schedule light duty workers and coordinate the logistics.<br>
<br>
Researches and conducts analysis of data to prepare district-level reports for the State Department of Education and other similar entities.<br>
<br>
Create and revise Transportation forms as needed.<br>
<br>
Create purchase orders and execute goods receipts for OCPS Transportation items, goods and services. Deposits checks as needed for Transportation Services.<br>
<br>
Performs less routine clerical duties as needed.<br>
<br>
Provides backup for other staff positions within the Transportation department.<br>
<br>
Operates a computer to enter, retrieve, review, or modify data; utilizes word processing, database, and software programs, such as SAP, TransIT4U, Trans Fleet, FA AssetWorks, FuelMaster and other vehicle location software.<br>
<br>
Operates a variety of machinery, equipment, and tools associated with department activities, which may include copy machine, fax, SAP, FA AssetWorks, FuelMaster and other related software programs.<br>
<br>
Responsible for keeping up to date on current technology, as job appropriate, being used by OCPS. With the support of the district, attends training to ensure skill level in various technologies is at the level required to perform in current position.<br>
<br>
Responsible for timely and accurate information they maintain as part of their job responsibilities.<br>

<b>MINIMUM TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE</b><br>
High school diploma or GED; supplemented by up to three (3) years previous experience and/or training in an office environment, or any equivalent combination of related education, training and experience which provides the required knowledge, skills and abilities to perform the essential job functions.<br>

<br>
