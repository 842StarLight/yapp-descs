<h2>Audiology Assistant</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p><b>Hearing Associates, LLC.</b></p>
<p><b>Audiology Assistant</b></p>
<p>The role is to support the audiologist in performing routine tasks and duties, so the audiologist(s) are more accessible for diagnostic, management and treatment required by the level of education of a licensed audiologist. The goal of the audiology assistant is to maximize the schedule to save time and provide an excellent experience for our patients.</p>
<p><b>Audiology Assistant Job Overview</b></p>
<p>Equipment maintenance, software updates, hearing aid repairs and modifications, preparation of patient for appointment with appropriate equipment, non-diagnostic otoscopy, explanation of benefits and financing options, oversee schedule to ensure all appointments are scheduled appropriately and to maximize our time, order all hearing aid and supplies from manufacturers, creating a relationship with manufacturer sales representatives, schedule appropriate trainings for office on new technology or shifts, create and explain purchase agreements to patients, counsel patients as audiologist asks, maintain clinical side of practice.</p>
<p><b>Primary Responsibilities</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Preparation of patient for testing, performs non-diagnostic otoscopy and additional tasks at the direction of the Audiologist.</li>
<li>Providing a tour of our facility to new patients.</li>
<li>Over-see schedule to ensure all appointments are scheduled appropriately to maximize our time.</li>
<li>Build a relationship with patients and vendors.</li>
<li>Performs checks and safety/maintenance of clinical supplies and equipment.</li>
<li>Assist on appointments as needed by the Audiologist.</li>
<li>Cleaning and checking of hearing aids, minor repairing of hearing aids and processing hearing aid repairs to manufacturers.</li>
<li>Process hearing aid orders by filling out appropriate paperwork and/or calling manufacturers.</li>
<li>Assist with clerical duties such as record keeping, assisting in clinical research, preparing materials and scheduling appointments and meetings as directed by the Audiologist.</li>
<li>Work directly with insurance to ensure denials are processed correctly by submitting supporting documentation to companies.</li>
<li>Counsel patients on use, cleaning and maintenance of hearing aids and accessories.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Continuing Education</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Attend industry functions, such as association events, manufacturer trainings, and conferences, and provide feedback and information on market and creative trends per audiologist request.</li>
<li>Scheduled and participate in manufacturer trainings on new technology and shifts.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Supplies and record keeping</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Maintains patient records by recording and updating patient electronic charts after appointments.</li>
<li>Maintains supplies inventory by checking stock; anticipating needs; placing and expediting orders; verifying receipt.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Education</b></p>
<p>High School Diploma required. A good understanding of the hearing healthcare industry and medical insurance billing as well as 2 years general office experience is advisable but not required.</p>
<p><b>Other Skills and Qualifications</b></p>
<p>Critical thinking and problem-solving skills, influencing and leading, public speaking, delegation, team work, multi-tasking skills, customer relation skills, conflict management, networking, writing, general office skills, and Microsoft Office.</p>
<p>Position is full time from 8:15-4:45 with ½ hr lunch. We offer paid time off and 401K (after a specified time for each).</p>
