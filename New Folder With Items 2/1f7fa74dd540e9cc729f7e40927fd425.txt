<p><b>Job Description
</b></p>
<p>As a member of Workday's Pricing team, the Sr. Pricing Manager will play a key role in evolving and reshaping Workday's revenue growth strategy. Key objectives for the pricing team are to improve revenue expansion from existing customers, elevate the success of the sales force through better pricing presentations, and maintenance of pricing integrity as Workday grows market share across all industries and geographies. Reporting to the Senior Director of Pricing and working closely with Product Management, Product Marketing, Sales Operations, Finance, and Legal, this is a highly visible role with excellent growth potential. The position is based in the San Francisco Bay Area.
</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>Responsibilities:
</b></p>
<ul><li><p>Lead transformational changes to Workday’s pricing and commercial practices, independently as well as in charge of virtual teams.
</p></li></ul>
<ul>
<li><p>Define and implement pricing, quoting and licensing policies related to new product and service offerings.
</p></li>
<li><p>Perform quantitative analysis of deal data and customer usage data and translate into problem solutions or improvement opportunities.
</p></li>
<li><p>Cultivate close relationships with, and gain trust of stakeholders up to and including VP level, primarily within the product and sales organizations.
</p></li>
<li><p>Present proposals to Pricing &amp; Packaging Committee and coach product and service offering managers on preparing their proposals for presentation.
</p></li>
<li><p>Identify opportunities for new pricing, packaging and bundling strategies that will contribute to our strategic goals.
</p></li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>Qualifications:
</b></p>
<ul><li><p>Minimum 8 years of relevant experience from software or enterprise services organization; SaaS experience is desirable.
</p></li></ul>
<ul>
<li><p>Direct experience with pricing, packaging or software licensing required. Professional services pricing experience is a plus.
</p></li>
<li><p>Proficiency in advanced analytical methods, incl. multivariate statistics.
</p></li>
<li><p>Excellent oral and written communication skills required. Ideal candidate has proven success in organizational change management.
</p></li>
<li><p>Must be a self-starter, able to work independently across functional and organizational lines.
</p></li>
<li><p>Bachelor’s degree in a quantitative field of study is required. Economics highly desirable. MBA is a plus.
</p></li>
<li><p>Must be a good cultural fit for Workday's high-achievement, yet fun, casual and respectful environment.
</p></li>
</ul>
