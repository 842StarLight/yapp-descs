<h2>Radar Systems Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>The ADAS Group of Visteon is looking to hire professionals who have experience in utilizing RADAR Systems for solving ADAS use-cases such as Adaptive Cruise Control, Automatic Emergency Braking, Blind Spot Detection etc. The ADAS group of Visteon is part of the Technology Office, and has presence in multiple locations such as Detroit (US), Karlsruhe (DE), Sofia, Bangalore (India), Pune (India).</p>

<p><b>
The Role</b></p>
<p>
Manage a team of RADAR Application Engineers to deliver world class perception system to solve ADAS use cases. The responsibilities include the following topics:</p>
<ul>
<li>Develop, implement and test real-time algorithms for RADAR object detection/tracking and localization.</li>
<li>Benchmark the performance of RADAR detection and tracking algorithms.</li>
<li>Contribute towards overall architecture and SW design of the ADAS application.</li>
<li>Have through understanding of simulation platforms that can be used for testing the robustness of the RADAR modules.</li>
<li>Understanding of sensor calibration.</li>
<li>Evaluating new RADAR sensors and integrating their perception output into the existing perception SW module.</li>
<li>Associating RADAR detection outputs with other sensor output.</li>
<li>Understanding of principles of other sensors like Cameras and LiDARs.</li>
<li>Basic understanding of Machine Learning.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>The Requirements</b></p>
<p>
To be a good fit for Radar Systems Manager you will have:</p>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor’s Degree in Electrical, Electronics or Computer Engineering.</li>
<li>At least three years of experience in algorithm development within an automotive Tier-1 or Tier-2 company.</li>
<li>Good understanding of OS like Linux and QNX.</li>
<li>Good C/C++ programming skills.</li>
<li>Experience with CAN, UDP, TCP, and network routing systems.</li>
<li>Good understanding of RADAR signal processing and principles of tracking &amp; estimation.</li>
</ul>
