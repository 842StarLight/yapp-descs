<h2>Administrative Supervisor - Brands Enforcement</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<div>
<p>Department of Livestock is a small agency that exists to protect the Montana livestock industry from theft and disease. We have many field staff as well as a core group of Helena office compliance staff that manage all the data and paperwork generated in the field. This office interacts extensively with livestock producers, many other agencies, and our own staff.
</p>
<p>The Administrative Supervisor supervises 6 compliance technicians as well as 1-2 part-time, temporary staff. These compliance staff handle a variety of programs:
</p>
<ul>
<li><div>Livestock brands: issuing new, transferring, and managing over 50,000 livestock brand records;</div></li>
<li><div>
Brand liens: records of liens against livestock brands, dealing with banks and other lenders across Montana and surrounding states;</div></li>
<li><div>
Cattle and horse inspections: data entry, compliance, and quality control of about 80,000 inspections annually. We are working towards moving inspections to an electronic solution;</div></li>
<li><div>
Maintaining internal and public access to contact information for over 500 livestock inspectors;</div></li>
<li><div>
Creating and maintaining a training manual for livestock inspectors;</div></li>
<li><div>
Compiling and analyzing animal sale data from 13 Montana livestock markets;</div></li>
<li><div>
Trouble-shooting software issues from several custom programs; and</div></li>
<li><p>
Many other permit and reporting programs.
</p></li>
</ul>
<p>The incumbent will handle multiple, shifting priorities – they will make plans of action, procedures, and backup plans in case things change. They must have excellent leadership skills to coach, train, hire, discipline, provide meaningful performance appraisals, and promote a team dynamic to keep all the many aspects of this office operating smoothly. This position will manage the schedules for the Division, track the training files for all staff including law enforcement field staff, compile the annual Brands Enforcement Division Annual Report, and manage many of the files and documents for the Enforcement Bureau and the Division Administrator. This position may also assist the Administrator with drafting documents, presentations, Administrative Rules, policies and procedures, and website content. This position troubleshoots a variety of software applications and seeks to improve inefficient or ineffective processes by developing alternative solutions as needed.</p>
<p></p>
<br>
</div>
<div></div>
<div>
<b>Qualifications</b>:<div>
</div>
<p>
The successful candidate must have at least 3 years’ experience supervising 2 or more people, a bachelor’s degree in business management or similar field, excellent organizational and time management skills, advanced skills in <i>Office</i> (Excel and Word), willingness and ability to learn and troubleshoot a variety of specialized software programs, a direct, positive, team-oriented leadership style. They must have a working knowledge of personnel procedures and management, including hiring, discipline, and terminating employees within EEO guidelines. Incumbent must utilize effective time management and organizational skills and be flexible in accepting and implementing change. They should be energized by interacting with people in a fast-paced environment. A valid driver's license and the ability to operate a state vehicle is required. Preferred qualifications include experience in the cattle industry and ongoing leadership training. Other combinations of education and experience which could provide such knowledge, skills and abilities will be evaluated on an individual basis. Tests administered thru the Job Service will assist in the evaluation of skills.<br>
</p>
