WHAT DO WE NEED HELP WITH?</b></div>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<div>
Our marketing team needs is looking for a Director of Global Demand Generation and Campaigns. In this highly collaborative role and hands-on role you will partner with Regional Marketing Heads and Portfolio Leaders to develop and execute on an integrated buyer-centric GTM plan.</div>
<div>
<br>
</div>
<div>
We need someone to integrate omni-channel journeys to engage prospects and existing customers, resulting in revenue growth and increased loyalty. You will develop buyer-centric demand generation programs to attract and convert new qualified accounts. You’ll execute customer loyalty programs and campaigns to create upsell opportunities.
</div>
</div>

<div><div>
<h3 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>WHAT WILL YOU DO?</b></h3>
<div><ul>
<li>
Deliver repeatable, sustainable and predictable marketing generated / influenced revenue in regions/verticals and among target accounts</li>
<li>
Collaborate in the create ION’s first centralized global demand generation and pipeline performance organization</li>
<li>
Lead global demand generation and providing expert guidance to the regions. Manage metrics and operations reports for the CMO.
</li>
<li>Partner with Regional Marketing Directors, the Portfolio Marketing team and sales leaders to design and build targeted and integrated marketing &amp; sales funnel.</li>
<li>
Align on target accounts and metrics</li>
<li>
Develop and share plan, activities and results</li>
<li>
Develop and orchestrate sales and marketing plays</li>
<li>
In support of the “categories” (Treasury and Commodities) plan omni-channel activities to drive new qualified leads (MIL’s) and nurture existing leads (MQL’s)
</li>
<li>Develop robust closed loop reporting to effectively measure and optimize marketing investments
</li>
<li>Identify tools and develop standards and processes necessary to support data capture and analysis</li>
<li>
Manage overall marketing (media) budget to meet or exceed metrics
</li>
<li>Regularly report on progress, wins and areas for improvement</li>
</ul></div>
</div></div>
