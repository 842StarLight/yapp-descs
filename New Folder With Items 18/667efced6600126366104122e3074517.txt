<h2>Systems Stress Engineer</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<div>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Job description</b></h2>
<div>
<p>We have a Stress Engineering opportunity to join our highly motivated and multi-functional team responsible for designing, developing, and testing pilot control systems, throttle control systems, actuators, and related flight control products for the next generation of business, regional, military and air-transport aircraft.</p>
<p>
Job Responsibilities<br>
</p>
<div><ul>
<li>Develop high-fidelity FEA models of Actuation and Pilot Control product assemblies.</li>
<li>Perform static, dynamic, and fatigue analyses using ANSYS Workbench or similar FEA tools (Nastran, Abaqus)</li>
<li>Perform Rigid Body Dynamics analysis using ANSYS Workbench or similar tools (MSC Adams)</li>
<li>Generate component/interface loads based on system level analysis using rigid body and flexible body system models.</li>
<li>Perform component stress analysis of Actuation and Pilot Control assemblies and recommend design improvements based on the analysis results.</li>
<li>Perform endurance and vibration fatigue (LCF and HCF) analyses on various components of pilot control and actuator assemblies.</li>
<li>Support structural testing and perform test data (strain gage and accelerometer) correlation with analysis models.</li>
<li>Prepare structural analysis reports and presentations in support of product design reviews (PDRs, CDR) and FAA/EASA/JAR certification.</li>
</ul></div>
<p></p>
</div>

<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Job requirements</b></h2>
<div>
<p>Qualifications<br>
</p>
<div><ul>
<li>Bachelor's degree in a Science, Technology, Engineering or Math (STEM) discipline</li>
<li>US Citizenship or Permanent Residency, required</li>
<li>0-4 years of Stress related engineering experience required</li>
<li>Strong knowledge of stress analysis fundamentals, such as engineering mechanics, structural dynamics, and finite element method.</li>
<li>Expertise performing hand calculations based on fundamental equations.</li>
<li>Hands-on experience with ANSYS Workbench, ANSYS Classic, or other commercial FEA tools (Nastran, Abaqus) for structural analyses.</li>
<li>Understanding of commercial regulations (FAR Part 23 and 25, and DO-160) as they relate to materials, product design, and analysis.</li>
<li>System level certification testing and how it correlates to modeling and theory</li>
<li>Industry standard practices for development and sizing of load carrying structures.</li>
<li>The selected candidate must be motivated to learn at a fast pace, have strong written and verbal communication skills, and be able to work autonomously or in a highly interactive team environment.</li>
</ul></div>
