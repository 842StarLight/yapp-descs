<h2>Senior Financial Analyst</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">Saint-Gobain, one of the world’s largest building materials companies and manufacturer of innovative material solutions, helps create great living spaces by combining comfort and sustainability. For more than three-and-a-half centuries, Saint-Gobain has been enhancing the wellbeing of people everywhere, whether it’s by addressing climate change, delivering true sustainable development, advancing the information revolution, participating in medical breakthroughs or playing a vital role in space exploration. Focusing on the intersection of people, innovation and purpose, Saint-Gobain helps to address the needs of today and the challenges of tomorrow – offering employees the opportunity to invent themselves and reshape the world.
<br><br>
<b>Position Summary:</b><br>
Saint-Gobain North America is looking for a Senior Financial Analyst who enjoys forming innovative, conceptual solutions to financial and profitability analysis issues. It is also important that this person be a motivated self-starter with strong judgement, as their primary job purpose will be to assist with providing financial analysis support to all of Saint-Gobain’s North American businesses and with the main office in France. Most of this support will come from monthly reporting and analysis, including economic analysis and research. In addition, responsibilities include analysis and reporting to management of key liability accounts, dividends and incentive plans.
<br><br>
<b>Responsibilities:</b><br>
Key Account Analysis
<br><br>
Analysis of critical and sensitive liability accounts at multiple levels - North America, SGC consolidated world-wide, and legal entity sub-consolidations. The responsibilities include recurring and ad hoc analysis for the North American Chief Operations Officer (“COO”) in order to support reporting to the external auditors, CSG, and Regional Management. Activities will include coordinating change management, and significant transactions including acquisitions and divestitures.
<br><br>
North America Consolidations
<br><br>
Perform financial analyses and prepare consolidations of US and Canadian businesses in North America. The responsibilities will include analyses of equity, elimination of intercompany transactions and balances relating to sales, dividends, borrowing, and investments. Financial reports and presentations for the COO will be prepared periodically.
<br><br>
Support Regional and SSC Departments
<br><br>
Collaborate with various corporate departments such as Treasury, Benefits, Risk Management, Compensation and Shared Services on cross-discipline projects and analyses. In particular, this position must contemplate impact of change and transactions in an environment where lines of business and legal entities are not always perfectly aligned.
<br><br>
Business Decision Analysis
<br><br>
Support the Manager of Financial Reporting in performing financial analyses and review for key local book consolidations on a legal entity basis which support North American external financial arrangements and relationships. This involves working closely with other Financial Reporting team members, staff of functional areas and Finance Shared Services. The Financial Analyst will also analyze monthly revenue, operating profit and non-operating results and provide commentary to identify drivers.
<br><br>
Budget, Business Plan, and Forecast Development
<br><br>
Prepare and distribute key economic and benefit assumptions for North America.
<br><br>
Bachelor’s Degree in Accounting or Finance.
<br>Minimum of 5-7 years of related business experience with a broad understanding and perspective of financial and accounting principles. Strong emphasis should be put on experience in these areas: accounting and reporting in a manufacturing environment; analysis of operating results; and development of financial models.
<br>Expert user of Microsoft Windows software. (Excel, Word, PowerPoint, etc.)
<br>Expert in consolidation.
<br>CPA or MBA preferred.
