<h2>Controls/Electrical Engineer</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<b>Department:</b> Engineering
<br><b>Reports to:</b> Engineering Manager
<br><b>Date:</b> April 1, 2019
<br><br>
<b>Summary:</b><br>
As a member of the technical staff, the electrical and controls engineer is responsible for the design and
<br>development of all aspects of electrical control systems, equipment and machinery. Includes but not
<br>limited to, development of structured programs, system design, and machine testing and troubleshooting.
<br>Key project team member providing engineering support, design and development of PLC’s and motion
<br>control systems to support sales, manufacturing and service. Other responsibilities include technical
<br>support of existing products currently in production.
<br><br>
<b>Essential Duties &amp; Responsibilities:</b><br>
<ul>
<li>Responsible for installation and technical support of PLC based hardware and software.</li>
<li>Plans, conducts, and coordinates detailed phases of the design of PLC’s and motion control</li>
</ul>
equipment for Ross Packaging/Processing Equipment.
<br><ul>
<li>Creates, reviews, and documents the design of new or revised assemblies and parts. Insures</li>
</ul>
design is based upon sound Engineering judgment, is not a safety hazard, and is designed with
<br>manufacturing in mind. Understands that he/she is accountable for results.
<br><ul>
<li>Participates in pre-production and design reviews.</li>
<li>Releases well organized electrical packages using fully documented Engineering Change Notices.</li>
<li>Creates and edits service bulletins, reviews machinery manuals.</li>
<li>Maintains and displays a professional positive demeanor with a willing to help attitude.</li>
<li>Monitors the urgency of tasks to not impact shipment schedules.</li>
<li>Provides controls support to sales, manufacturing and service.</li>
<li>Required electronic design with electronic circuit technology assembly processes (analog, digital</li>
</ul>
and/or high voltage design).
<br><ul>
<li>Demonstrated knowledge of field’s concepts, practice and procedures, including design, repair</li>
</ul>
and analysis of electronic systems.
<br><ul>
<li>Ability to model in 3D</li>
</ul>
<b>Education and Experience:</b><br>
<ul>
<li>BS in electrical engineering and 5+ years of experience in field or related area.</li>
<li>Proficient in Solidworks and Auto Cad.</li>
<li>Exposure to CE and OSHA guarding.</li>
<li>Working knowledge of machinery design for the food industry.</li>
</ul>
<b>Requirements:</b><br>
<ul>
<li>Successful candidate must have exposure to electrical engineering design principles and</li>
</ul>
methodologies.
<br><ul>
<li>Experience working with analog circuit simulation tools, must be proficient using Solid Works</li>
</ul>
and ability to model in 3D, proficient in MS Office.
<br><ul>
<li>Excellent verbal and written communication skills and demonstrated ability to work in a team</li>
</ul>
oriented environment.
<br><ul>
<li>Must be able to visit customer locations to include meat processing divisions, withstand extreme</li>
</ul>
cold and warm conditions, install and/or test equipment to assist with customer needs and
<br>expectations.
<br>This position is full time and includes a comprehensive Benefits Package; and the typical work schedule
<br>is Monday – Friday. Interested candidates should forward their resume and/or employment application to
<br>Human Resources for consideration at hr@rossindinc.com.</div>
