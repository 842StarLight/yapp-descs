<h2>Business Process Manager</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><b>Pacific Coast Companies, Inc.</b> is a dedicated team of professionals that provide a comprehensive range of business services and support to the Pacific Coast Building Products family of companies and their customers.</p>
<br>
<div></div>
<div>The <b>Business Process Manager</b> is responsible in performing a variety of complex supervisory, administrative, and technical work in the development, installation and maintenance of office automation and management information systems. Performs other duties as required.<br>
</div>
<div></div>
<div>
<b>ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES: </b>may include some or all of the following. Other duties may be assigned.</div>
<br>
<div></div>
<ul>
<li>Manages and oversees assigned operations to achieve goals within available resources; plans and organizes workloads and staff assignments; trains, motivates and assesses assigned staff; reviews progress and directs changes as needed.
</li>
<li>Provides leadership and direction in the development of short and long-range plans; collects, interprets, and prepares data for studies, reports and recommendations; coordinates department activities with other departments and agencies as needed.
</li>
<li>Furnishes professional advice to supervisors; makes presentations to supervisors, boards, commissions, civic groups and the general public.
</li>
<li>Announces official company plans, policies and procedures to staff.
</li>
<li>Insures that assigned areas of responsibility are performed within budget; performs cost control activities; monitors revenues and expenditures in assigned area to assure sound fiscal control; prepares annual budget requests; assures effective and efficient use of budgeted funds, personnel, materials, facilities, and time.
</li>
<li>Decides work procedures, prepares work schedules, and expedites workflow; examines and standardizes procedures to improve efficiency and effectiveness of operations.
</li>
<li>Issues written and oral instructions; assigns duties and inspects work for exactness, neatness, and conformance to policies and procedures.
</li>
<li>Preserves harmony among workers and resolves grievances.
</li>
<li>Performs or helps subordinates in performing duties; adjusts errors and complaints.
</li>
<li>Completed a variety of studies, reports and related information for decision‑making purposes.
</li>
<li>Confers with management to determine information requirements of departments to determine boundaries and priorities of data processing and office automation projects, and to discuss system needs.
</li>
<li>Deliberates with department heads involved with proposed projects to insure cooperation and further define nature of project.
</li>
<li>Does project feasibility studies; establishes work standards; prepares progress reports to inform management of project status and deviation from goals.
</li>
<li>Coordinates with management staff, support staff, technical personnel, or vendors to clear up problems, as required.
</li>
<li>Reworks computer operating schedule to introduce new program testing and operating runs.
</li>
<li>Reviews reports of computer and peripheral equipment production, malfunction, and maintenance to determine costs and plan operating changes.</li>
<li>Prepares and requests proposals for hardware and software solutions.</li>
<li>Helps staff to diagnose and solve computer equipment problems; participates in technical projects such as writing equipment specifications or developing computer programs for specified applications.
</li>
<li>Documents enterprise objectives and budgets to promote the orderly and efficient capture, storage, processing and dissemination of information.</li>
<li>Examines the operating and capital budgets and expenditures for MIS functions and operations.</li>
<li>Develops and maintains statements of necessary policies and procedures to assure proper documentation and communication of essential controls.</li>
<li>Observes procedures to ensure that, for potential MIS projects of any size, a comparison is made of internal development versus purchase</li>
<li>Supervises the inventory of installed or planned-to-be installed MIS technology and facilities.</li>
<li>Assists in training staff in the use of computer hardware and software.
</li>
<li>Represents the organization at various conferences and meetings; serves as a member of various employee committees.</li>
<li>Provides backup to subordinate staff and related positions as needed or assigned</li>
</ul>
<p><br>

Ability to apply mathematical operations to such tasks as frequency distribution, determination of test reliability and validity, analysis of variance, correlation techniques, sampling theory and factor analysis.</li>
<li>
