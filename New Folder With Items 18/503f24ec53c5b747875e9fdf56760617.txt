<p><b>
Position Summary:</b></p>
<p>
The Clinical Liaison is responsible for building sales relationships by daily interactions with physicians and discharge planners at hospitals to ensure referral/admissions. Maintains sales territory in order to meet census goals. Must be comfortable with sales techniques such as cold calling and contact planning. Monitor marketing activity through CRM software.</p>
<p><b>
Essential Functions:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Facilitate complete assessment of patients being referred to facility to determine patient’s clinical needs and appropriateness of admission.</li>
<li>Ability to review and present admission analysis, referral analysis, needs of referral contacts, and how to meet these needs.</li>
<li>Select most effective strategy (fate-to-face, group presentation, direct mail, tours, CEU’s, etc.) for each contact. Exchange ideas, information and messages readily and effectively, both in oral and written form, as required by the job.</li>
<li>Work with facility clinical department to coordinate new admissions as well as re-admissions.</li>
<li>Observe confidential nature of patient and/or organizational business.</li>
<li>Identify potential problem issues or customer satisfaction issues; intervenes appropriately and reports to the Vice President of Business Development. Develops action plan to track progress, conclusion, and follow up.</li>
<li>Attend local events at which company representatives may exhibit or attend to achieve the maximum return on investment.</li>
<li>Builds and maintains positive internal and external</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Position Requirements:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Absolute and complete professionalism and confidentiality continually maintained.</li>
<li>Flexibility and adaptability.</li>
<li>Must possess exceptional organizational and communication skills.</li>
<li>Must demonstrate the ability to relate positively, effectively, and appropriately with residents, families, community members, volunteers, and other facility staff members.</li>
<li>A special interest in working with long-term residents and the elderly is preferred.</li>
<li>Working knowledge of office machines, such as copiers, scanners, faxes, phone systems, etc.</li>
<li>General computer literacy and good knowledge of Microsoft Outlook, Excel, Word, and Google</li>
<li>Must have excellent attention to detail.</li>
<li>Must attend and participate in continuing education programs and all mandatory in-services.</li>
<li>Must be in good general health and demonstrate emotional stability.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Education: </b>High School Diploma or equivalent. Bachelor’s Degree from an approved school of Social Work is preferred.</p>
<p><b>
Experience</b><b>:</b> Must have a minimum of one (1) year supervised social work experience in a health care setting working directly with individuals.</p>
