<h2>Field Trainer – Northeast</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Overview
</b></h2>
<div><div><div>
<p>The Education &amp; Training Specialist is responsible for delivering engaging, informative and timely training sessions that educate our customers in the Northeast Region on Oticon products and technology.
</p>
<p>The ideal candidate is someone with advanced skills in training delivery and large group presentations, and who can collaborate effectively with diverse stakeholders. Must be a self-motivated professional who is internally collaborative and externally dynamic in representing the company.
</p>
<p>This position reports to the Manager of Field Training, with a dotted line to the Regional Manager in the Northeast Region.</p>
<p><b>
The Northeast Territory covers, ME, MA, VT, CT, NH, RI, and NY (Westchester County and upper state).</b></p>
</div></div></div>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Responsibilities
</b></h2>
<div><div><div>
<div>Includes the following. Other duties may be assigned.</div>
<ul>
<li>
Provide both in office and group training in Oticon products and fitting software.
</li>
<li>Conduct dynamic and motivating workshops and seminars on advanced audiological topics at national, regional and local seminars. Audience size can range from one person to hundreds.</li>
<li>
Deliver CEU training sessions, and execute the CEU administration process for each training session delivered.
</li>
<li>Measure the effectiveness and impact of training, and use the data to continuously improve training delivery</li>
<li>
Conduct follow-up visits in target customers’ offices to improve counseling, fitting, and fine-tuning of Oticon products and to motivate them to use Oticon products and accessories.</li>
<li>
Participate in quarterly Regional Sales meetings to influence the direction of the sales and training efforts for your assigned region.
</li>
<li>Participate in training meetings as necessary to shape and influence the development of effective training materials.
</li>
<li>Assist in onboarding new Account Managers; conduct 1:1 coaching during Week 2 of the new Account Manager’s 10-week new hire onboarding program</li>
<li>
Must be able to prioritize and organize planning for Regional impact.</li>
</ul>
<ul><li>
Extensive travel (approximately 80%) required</li></ul>
</div></div></div>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Qualifications
</b></h2>
<div><div><div>
<div>The position requires five years of experience selling hearing devices to consumers or two years of experience as an Oticon Account Manager. A minimum of a Master’s degree in Audiology is required. Exceptional public speaking abilities are an essential requirement.<br>
</div>
<div></div>
<div>To perform this job successfully, an individual must be able to perform each essential duty satisfactorily. The requirements listed are representative of the knowledge, skill, and/or ability required. Specifically, this job requires the individual to travel 80% of the time. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions.</div>
