<div><b>POSITION OVERVIEW</b></div>
<div><b>Contracts Analyst
</b></div>
<div>National Instruments is seeking a strategic Contracts Manager to become a member of the Deal &amp; Contractual Structuring Services organization. The candidate will have the primary responsibility for managing complex technology contract negotiations at National Instruments. This is a strategic role that requires a candidate to ensure contracts and contracting procedures are used to execute business strategies while enabling and securing business performance.</div>
<br>
<div></div>
<div><b>Key Responsibilities</b></div>
<ul>
<li>Negotiating, structuring, drafting, and reviewing a variety of commercial and public agreements related to technology sales including hardware, software, partner, and services agreements.</li>
<li>
Coordinating with other functional teams such as Legal, Finance, Operations, and Sales on the development and management of complex commercial and public technology transactions.</li>
<li>
Providing contract management support to National Instruments business teams</li>
<li>
Serving as an essential business partner in shaping business strategy, identifying business risks and defining company policies and procedures</li>
<li>
Training internal clients on various corporate policies, procedures, contractual provisions, issues, approaches and preferred practices.</li>
<li>
Partner with International Contract Managers to align on global account strategy and agreements.</li>
<li>
Drafting contract summaries for internal review</li>
<li>
Escalating non-standard terms and conditions for approval according to Austin, TX Escalation Matrix</li>
</ul>
<div><b>Knowledge and Skills:</b></div>
<ul>
<li>Sales and Contract Operations Business Fundamentals</li>
<li>
Accounting and Procurement Management</li>
<li>
General Business Acumen and Financial Principles</li>
<li>
Strong negotiation, drafting, analytical and problem-solving skills</li>
<li>
Exceptional attention to detail, initiative, and ability to use independent judgement</li>
<li>
Time management and the ability to manage multiple projects and clients</li>
<li>
Excellent business judgement and ability to think strategically</li>
<li>
Ability to communicate effectively at all levels and to both legal and non-legal professionals</li>
<li>
Ability to build and maintain strong client relationships</li>
<li>
Ability to influence different functions and geographies and to build consensus where lines of authority may not be direct</li>
<li>
Proficiency in seeking input from senior business management based upon articulate and succinct evaluation and recommendation</li>
</ul>
