<h2>Technical Applications Scientist, qPCR - Carlsbad, CA</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<b>Job Description</b><br>
<p>When you’re part of the team at Thermo Fisher Scientific, you’ll do important work, like helping customers in finding cures for cancer, protecting the environment or making sure our food is safe. Your work will have real-world impact, and you’ll be supported in achieving your career goals.</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>
Location/Division Specific Information</b></p>
<p>
The Technical Application Specialist II is part of the Life Sciences Solutions Group (LSG) which is one of six groups within Thermo Fisher Scientific and is an important part of the company’s total value proposition. With an innovative and broad life sciences portfolio, a strong presence at bench, best-in-class web and e-commerce channels, industry-leading on-site supply center coverage and one of the most technically experienced sales and support teams, LSG brings incredible core strength to the broader company.</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>
How will you make an impact?</b></p>
<p>
The Technical Applications Scientist is responsible for providing remote pre and post sales technical support of Thermo Fisher Scientific products to customers and field personnel, as well as relaying customer feedback to the organization.</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>
What will you do?</b></p>
<ul>
<li><p>
Provide technical support for Thermo Fisher Scientific qPCR and/or genetic analysis product lines and instruments by answering customer technical inquiries. Inquiries may be received via telephone, email or web chat.</p></li>
<li>
Provide customer support on technical issues by utilizing all available resources, and escalate issues or problems when warranted.</li>
<li>
Participate in frequent training on products and continuing education on new applications and technologies to remain at the cutting edge of scientific knowledge.</li>
<li>
Accurately record pertinent information from customer contacts in Customer Relationship Management system.</li>
<li>
Summarize trends and technology as technical tips, FAQ’s and troubleshooting guides.</li>
<li>
Recommend changes to knowledge databases, website, and other company-managed databases of technical information.</li>
<li>
Present technical updates to colleagues and contribute to department meetings.</li>
<li>
May present technical and product information at trade shows and in customer field visits.</li>
<li>
Participate in additional special team or individual projects.</li>
<li>
May be required to perform other related duties as opportunities arise.</li>
<li>
Some travel may be required for the position</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>
How will you get here?</b></p>
<p><b>
Education</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Minimum Qualifications: B.S., B.A. or M.S. in life sciences or chemistry or a similar field.</li>
<li>
Preferred Qualifications: PhD or a PhD. with post-doctoral experience. Experience with assay and instrument troubleshooting.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>
Experience</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
3+ years of hands-on experience with quantitative/real-time PCR techniques, including sample preparation, data acquisition, and analysis is required.</li>
<li>
Requires in depth knowledge of real-time instruments, software, kits and services.</li>
<li>
Ability to partner effectively as part of a team to solve problems, negotiate solutions and arrive at a consensus.</li>
<li>
Prior experience in customer service is a plus.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>
Knowledge, Skills, Abilities</b></p>
<ul>
<li>
Proven ability to quickly learn large amounts of new, complex technical information.</li>
<li>
Demonstrated ability to effectively communicate, both orally and in writing, to customers and colleagues</li>
<li>
Ability to manage multiple priorities.</li>
<li>
Problem solving ability.</li>
<li>
A minimum two-year commitment to Technical Support is required due to the extensive up-front training needed to fully support our expansive product line.</li>
</ul>
