<div><div><div>Business Development Manager - Moldmaking
</div></div></div>
<div><div>
<p>Identification Number: 2018-55</p>
<p>York, SC
</p>
</div></div>
<div><div>There is currently an opening for a Business Development Manager (BDM) for the Moldmaking market.
</div></div>
<div><div>
<p>In this role, the BDM will participate in customer meetings in cooperation with our Sales representatives and Technical Service to understand customer requirements and lead internal support to move opportunities through the sales cycle to closure. Having the ability to work with and influence internal cross-functional teams will be crucial to the success of the candidate and to ensure sales growth.
</p>
<p>The BDM will provide support to Sales, Legal, Marketing, Supply Chain, Manufacturing, Quality and HSE functions to ensure that project milestones are delivered on time in full. It will also be important to assist marketing and R&amp;D in business case development for new technologies, product customization, and R&amp;D product development projects related to the Moldmaking market.
</p>
<p>Finally, this role is responsible for providing broad direction to our Sales function for the development of new accounts and for coaching sales people in Moldmaking market applications and product knowledge.
</p>
<p>A high degree of understanding of the market, customer needs, and our industrial capabilities and product strengths is necessary to position Elkem for growth. Conducting analysis of the potential customers and our current and future product offering will be essential for choosing the right targets to ensure success.</p>
<p>
The Business Development Manager has a key influence on the future (including the short term) of the Moldmaking business and must be proactive in working with customers and prospects to learn about future business potential to fit with Elkem capabilities. The incumbent must then present and persuade enterprise management of the value and fit to strategy of potential new business particularly if it will require capital investment or investments in product development. Success will depend on the incumbent's ability to manage the internal coordination of the sales strategy to commercial realization.</p>
</div></div>
