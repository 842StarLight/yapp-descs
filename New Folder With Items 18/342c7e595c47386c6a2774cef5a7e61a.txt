<h2>Manager, Product Development - NBC Sports</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div><div><div>
<div>
<div>Role Purpose<br>
The Manager, Product Development is responsible for creating and maintaining product documentation for the NBC Sports digital properties (namely NBCSports.com and Gold) and delivering innovative solutions across all platforms<br>
<br>
Responsibilities<br>
<ul>
<li>Elicit and gather product requirements for documentation and planning<br>
</li>
<li>Create documentation including PRDs and wireframes for new products<br>
</li>
<li>Understand the competitive landscape, and prepare competitive analysis and research<br>
</li>
<li>Partner with external agencies to develop new products concepts and designs<br>
</li>
<li>Prototype product features across multiple platforms<br>
</li>
<li>Manage existing products and features sets ensuring User engagement and business value<br>
</li>
<li>Work with technology teams to create functional / tech requirements<br>
</li>
<li>Drive the design and review of test cases, process change requests and manage project scope, acceptance, installation and deployment.<br>
</li>
<li>Work closely with sales team to deliver on advertising requirements for sponsors<br>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

<p><b>About Us</b></p>
<div>NBC Sports Group serves sports fans 24/7 with premier live events, insightful studio shows, and compelling original programming. The sports media company consists of NBC Sports, NBC Olympics, NBCSN, Golf Channel, Olympic Channel: Home of Team USA, NBC Sports Regional Networks, NBC Sports Radio and NBC Sports Digital, which includes NBCSports.com, NBCOlympics.com, GolfChannel.com, the digital assets of the NBC Sports Regional Networks, Rotoworld, the NBC Sports Talk franchise, multiple apps, and two transactional sports businesses, GolfNow and SportsEngine. NBC Sports Group possesses an unparalleled collection of television rights agreements, partnering with some of the most prestigious sports properties in the world: the International Olympic Committee and United States Olympic Committee, the NFL, NHL, NASCAR, INDYCAR, PGA TOUR, The R&amp;A, PGA of America, Churchill Downs, Premier League, Tour de France, French Open, and many more.</div>
