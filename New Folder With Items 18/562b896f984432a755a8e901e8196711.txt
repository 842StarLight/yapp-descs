<h2>Director, Business Development</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div><div><div>
<p><b>Forbes List of America's Best Employers 2018</b><br>
Move forward with us! At NJ TRANSIT, you'll join us in transforming the third-largest transportation agency in North America. We are committed to delivering safe, reliable service that gets our customers to their destinations on time—and we’re looking to hire talented folks with a love of learning to make it all possible.<br>
<br>
<b>Main Functions</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Directs all aspects of the Office of Business Development’s (OBD) Disadvantaged Business Enterprise (DBE) outreach and DBE/Small Business Enterprise (SBE) compliance operations support. Manages the development of NJ TRANSIT’s DBE strategic outreach plan, including the enhancement of DBE/SBE program best practices to support small business development at NJ TRANSIT and development of all federal, state and local requirements for D/S/M(inority)/W(omen)-Owned Business Enterprise reporting. Supervises all external coordination and communication with government and community stakeholders in NJT’s DBE/SBE program and compliance activities.<br>
</li>
<li>Drives effective execution of OBD’s DBE outreach which targets and recruits high-potential S/M/W/BE-qualified applicants for participation in DBE certification. Manages outreach, including but not limited to the supervision of NJ TRANSIT’s attendance and participation in target market-rich events, the development of DBE recruitment and networking events, and developing ongoing mutually beneficial partnerships with key community stakeholders. Tracks, monitors and analyzes DBE performance in the New Jersey Unified Certification Program (NJUCP) across various critical characteristics, as well as outreach return on investment, to inform and refine outreach plans in partnership with OBD Compliance Operations.<br>
 </li>
<li>Serves as project manager for OBD special projects and initiatives that implement DBE/SBE best practices and enhance OBD’s effectiveness in supporting DBEs and SBEs, including but not limited to the development of disparity studies, bonding and capacity building workshops, and mentor-protégé programs. Develops and enhances training programs to support DBE business development including but not limited to OBD’s DBE Certification and onboarding workshops. Serves as project manager and primary liaison with DBE/SBE compliance software vendor and other external training partners.<br>
</li>
<li>Serves as designated liaison to external business partners and community stakeholders. Takes a lead role in managing all aspects of NJT’s relationship with NJUCP partners Port Authority of New York and New Jersey and the New Jersey Department of Transportation in developing and aligning on strategic initiatives, special projects and events, and creating standard operating procedures. Ensures timely and accurate reporting on NJT’s D/S/M/WBE performance to federal, state, local and internal stakeholders, in compliance with procedure and regulatory requirements, and upon request. Represents OBD in external meetings with key business partners and stakeholder groups on DBE/SBE related issues as assigned. Partners on all initiatives (internal and external) with the Director, Business Development Compliance Operations, as needed.<br>
</li>
<li>Leads development and implementation of OBD’s marketing, communication and social media programs, maximizing use of NJT’s website as well as print and digital resources to expand awareness of NJT’s DBE program and OBD services. Owns development of all DBE/SBE external communication and training materials.</li>
</ul>
<ul><li>Performs DBE/SBE certification and contract compliance functions as needed, and supports other OBD special projects and initiatives as assigned.</li></ul>
<br>
<b>Education and Experience Requirements</b>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor’s Degree and a minimum of seven (7) years applied experience in S/M/W/D Business Enterprise program and/or procurement contract administration in a supervisory capacity. One year of closely related experience can be substituted for each year of education required.
</li>
<li>Direct experience developing marketing materials, event planning, grants management and/or corporate communications/relations.
</li>
<li>Proficiency with high-level professional communication (oral and written), presentations, building business partnerships and networking.
</li>
<li>Direct experience developing and delivering training, managing projects/processes and developing internal/external corporate reports. Proficiency in Microsoft Office suite, including Word, Excel and PowerPoint required.<br>
