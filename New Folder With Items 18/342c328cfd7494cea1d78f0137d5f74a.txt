<h2>Senior Product Manager, Developer Toolkit</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><b>New Relic</b><br>
<b>SDK &amp; Tools Product Manager</b><br>
<b>Portland, Oregon</b><br>
</p>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>
Your Opportunity
</b></h2>
<p>At New Relic, we believe building an amazing developer experience is rooted in a deep understanding of modern software operators and developers who are building the world’s most amazing applications. The mission of this newly forming team is to provide developers with a set of tools and utilities, e.g. SDKs &amp; CLIs, that to make it easy for New Relic to be embedded into automation workflows, integrated with source code management tools, work with CI/CD pipelines, and other applications commonly used by modern software teams.</p>
<p>
To be successful in this role, you’ll need a good balance of technical skills, strong product sense, and an understanding of how teams deliver and monitor software. You’ll need excellent written and verbal communication skills, including the ability to represent the team with senior executives. And, you’ll need strong customer relationship skills, including the ability to discover the true requirements underlying customers’ feature requests in order to identify the most meaningful problems we could solve for.</p>
<p>
As the product manager, you will be responsible for the strategy, define product features, drive the system architecture, and spearhead the standard methodologies that enable a quality product. In addition, you will be working, through open source, to build a thriving ecosystem of developers. This is a dynamic role for someone who is a strategic thinker, that understands the need for process and prioritization on high demand projects, and who can turn planning into great product experiences by jumping in and getting their hands dirty.<br>
<br>
Opportunity to work from a remote office may be available depending on applicant level of experience.<br>
</p>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>
What You’ll Do</b></h2>
<ul>
<li>
You'll drive Product strategy: Drive the product strategy for New Relic’s platform capabilities, informed by customer and market insights, product and business KPIs, bold pressures, technical capability, and internal initiatives; craft the product story by understanding the landscape, developer needs, and New Relic principles</li>
<li>
Planning: In collaboration with Engineering, define a detailed, rolling 4-quarter product roadmap based on the product strategy and drive the alignment of key releases to deliver on the roadmap; adjust the roadmap as needed based on key metrics, market efforts, and changes in execution resources. Lead the business during the full product lifecycle – from requirements definition, to delivery, to end of life – collaborating with other teams across the company, including Engineering, Design, Support, Marketing, and Sales counterparts.</li>
<li>
Partake in Internal leadership: Maximize efficiency in a constantly evolving environment where the process is fluid and creative solutions are the norm; Consider the business impact, return on investment, and other implications when taking important decisions</li>
<li>
Customer &amp; Technology Partner interactions: Engaging with key technology partners on integration; Optimize developer experience including on-boarding, documentation, tools, and support.</li>
<li>
You'll be a part of Metrics and research: Conduct user research, integrate usability studies and market analysis into product requirements to enhance developer satisfaction; Define and analyze metrics that inform the success of products</li>
<li>
Positioning: Enable the go-to-market teams (sales, support, marketing)</li>
<li>
Product champion: Advise development teams at New Relic about API design impacts; Represent New Relic as a product and domain guide in front of Industry Analysts, customers, industry events/conferences, and other events; Lead sales engineer and support education sessions</li>
</ul>
