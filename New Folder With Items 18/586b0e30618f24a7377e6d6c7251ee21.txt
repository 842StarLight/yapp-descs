<h2>Continuous Improvement PM</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div>Brink’s Global Services (BGS) is a division of Brink’s Inc, the world’s premier provider of secure logistics and security solutions in more than 122 countries across 5 continents. Brink’s Global Services specializes in the secure transportation and handling of valuable goods throughout the logistic value chain, from raw materials and components to finished products within the mining, banknote, precious metal, jewelry, security, art and pharmaceutical industries. The company has a proud history of providing growth and advancement opportunities for its employees.
<br>
<br>
With over $3B in annual revenue and 60,700 dedicated employees in more than 40 countries, Brink’s has true global reach and a reputation as the most reliable name in the industry.
<br>
<br>
At Brink’s our culture is about living our Values of Safety, Customer Focus, Integrity, Engagement and Continuous Improvement every day, which makes a difference for our employees and our customers. Our values ensure that we work safely to protect ourselves and others, deliver successful customer solutions, always display the highest standards of ethics, and use technology to achieve results. We have a challenging opportunity for a Continuous Improvement Project Manager!
<br>
<br>
Job Summary:
<br>
Reporting to the BGS US Vice President, this position will work primarily in support of assessment and development of new process improvements across all business lines. He or she will work with all departments within business units to analyze where there is room for improvement. They will be required to assist with the execution of new process improvement methodologies and toolsets. They will be assisting with building proficiency for different departments through knowledge sharing and coaching. They will help build the Business Process Improvement Team’s credibility by engaging effectively with stakeholders, deploying appropriate practices and create and maintain a reputation as the internal consultant of choice by senior management for any problem solving or design initiatives.
<br>
<br>
They will also assist with assuring each project is delivered on time and within budget. The Continuous Improvement Project Manager will act as a change agent to help facilitate effective deployments/modifications to current practices. Focus should be on the effective use of resources, within both people and technology, for the successful completion of each task.
<br>
<br>
Key Responsibilities:
<br>
<br>
Identify improvement opportunities and assist in managing improvement requests from the business

<br>

<br>

<br>
<br>

<br>
<br>
Deliver process improvement training to staff
<br>
<br>
Identify, assess/validate, document and share local best practices for potential company-wide adoption and standardization
<br>
<br>
Help create process flows and documentation for all departments
<br>
<br>
Ability to manage results, process and relationships while driving to identify outcomes.
<br>
<br>
Act as a liaison between departmental end-users, information technology analysts and consultants
<br>
<br>
Knowledge of Accounting and ERP systems to ensure optimal operational performance
<br>
<br>
Assist in developing an overall change management strategy and process
<br>
<br>
Takes initiative to directly support and solve both short-term and chronic Voice of Customer issues
<br>
<br>
Has the ability to conduct and prepare cost/benefit analysis
<br>
<br>
Behavior-based leadership: uses Influencer methodology to drive change management success at branch/district level
<br>

Participate in various business process and technology integration initiatives
<br>

Special projects as assigned by management

Analyze and Negotiate contracts and agreements

<br>
Perform other duties as assigned or necessary.
