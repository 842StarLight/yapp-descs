<h2>Bookstore Operations Supervisor</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">Colleges, Housing, and Educational Services (CHES) is a multi-funded organization within the Business and Administrative Services (BAS) division at the University of California (UC) Santa Cruz. CHES provides leadership in the areas of college student life and residential services, housing services, facilities, employee housing and capital planning, dining services, conference services, early education services, the Bay Tree Bookstore, ID card services, and business and financial analysis for these areas. Through these units, educational and developmental programs and services are offered to all members of the campus community including students, faculty, staff, children and external constituents.
<br><br>
Within CHES, the Bay Tree Bookstore is a self-supporting auxiliary enterprise whose mission is to provide convenient and affordable access to books, digital coursework materials, school supplies, and services which complement the academic environment and support the diverse needs of the campus community. The bookstore strives to provide products and services to students, faculty, staff and campus units in an economical and efficient fashion.
<br><br>
Under the general supervision of the Bookstore Director, the Operations Supervisor will participate in directing, planning and controlling the operations of the Bookstore as a fiscally viable Auxiliary Enterprise. Acting as Operations Supervisor, will be responsible for buying, managing and administering the buying and merchandising of non-book items including but not limited to computers and associated products, general merchandise, oversight of Shipping and Receiving, and oversight of the Express Store.
<br><br>
Qualifications / Competencies
<br><br>
Experience in a college or university bookstore environment, with general knowledge of all aspects of operations including purchasing, inventory management, retail controls, staffing and human resources issues.
<br><br>
Experience with retail business equipment and systems, including Point of Sales (POS) systems and computerized inventory management systems and applications.
<br><br>
Demonstrated knowledge of purchasing, retailing, and merchandising a wide variety of products offered by a university bookstore.
<br><br>
Demonstrated knowledge of current terms, policies and procedures used on purchase orders, packing lists, invoices and vendor agreements.
<br><br>
Experience in effectively supervising and managing staff, with the ability to hire, provide training, coaching, mentoring, performance management, and set goals for development.
<br><br>
Familiarity with typical purchasing policies, procedures, practices, and terminology.
<br><br>
Experience with personal computers and typical software applications(word processing, spreadsheets, communications, databases).
<br><br>
Excellent interpersonal skills to work collaboratively with faculty, students, other internal clients, vendors, etc.
<br><br>
Excellent written and oral communication skills sufficient to assure high quality communication with all stakeholders including faculty, students, vendors, etc.
<br><br>
Ability to assess situations thoroughly and make appropriate decisions in a timely manner, prioritize workload, define goals, work under pressure and meet deadlines.
<br><br>
Demonstrated ability to work independently and use good judgment.
<br><br>
Strong mathematical skills.
<br><br>
