<h2>Insurance Intern</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div></div>
<div><div>
<div>
<p>The Iowa Insurance Division is the state regulator which supervises all insurance business transacted in the state of Iowa. Our team oversees companies and individuals in the sale of insurance in Iowa and has general regulatory oversight over all aspects of their business. The Iowa Insurance Division also has statutory authority over many activities related to the sale of securities and other regulated products in the state. As part of the Insurance Division, interns will provide administrative assistance in a variety of tasks including consumer advocacy, complaint resolution, public communications, and the investigation of potential criminal violations. Candidates for this position should have a skillset which should include some of the following:<br>
</p>
<ul>
<li>Data analysis and visualization
</li>
<li>Reviewing material to identify details
</li>
<li>Identifying policy language and terminology
</li>
<li>Gathering evidence
</li>
<li>Conducting interviews
</li>
<li>Creating investigative or consumer reports
</li>
<li>Interest in public policy
</li>
<li>Good written and verbal communication skills.</li>
</ul>
<br>
Description:<br>
<br>
The intern in this position may handle tasks included below as well as take part in special projects as needed. Interns will be assigned to work with Fraud, Market Regulation, or Communications and Consumer Outreach staff. Interns can expect to work with division staff in a team-oriented office dedicated to serving consumers and industries of Iowa. The internship is located in Downtown Des Moines and involves up to 15 hours per week depending on availability. Work hours will occur during the 8 a.m. - 4:30 p.m. Monday-Friday work week.<br>
<br>
Duties may include:<br>
<br>
Reviewing incoming referrals from insurance company investigators to determine information such as line of insurance business, location, date of loss, and any prior referrals involving the same individual to identify any trends or patterns present in claims.<br>
<br>
Working with Division personnel in reviewing case information to determine specific details such as circumstances of loss, involved parties, potential witnesses, additional leads, and other details relevant to the investigation. This includes photos, statements, and audio recordings. Documenting findings for review by Division staff through the use of written reports as well as link charts, timelines, and other graphical representations. Reviewing consumer complaints and how they may be resolved under Iowa's insurance law. Conducting social media and online review of advertisements and solicitations for insurance and investment products that may be in violation of law.<br>
<br>
Providing administrative support to the communications director and consumer advocacy officer, including research, correspondence, proofreading, and other administrative tasks. Providing administrative support to the selected Bureau, including correspondence, proofreading, and other administrative tasks. Creating, editing and posting press releases, infographics, social media posts and other consumer focused communications materials, Attending events put on by the Division, industry or outside organizations in which Division staff may attend to assist with photography and video; photography and video editing. Assisting in reviewing the Division's website and Chabot answers to ensure current content.<br>
<br>
Requirements:<br>
<br>
The intern holding this position must be able to:<br>
<ul>
<li>Take and accurately apply general instructions
</li>
<li>Be well organized, be detail oriented
</li>
<li>Be self-motivating
</li>
<li>Successfully follow assignments to completion
</li>
<li>Be proficient in computer skills
</li>
<li>Be able to efficiently and accurately review and organize documentation
</li>
<li>Have excellent analytical ability, be capable of working independently and meeting established deadlines.</li>
</ul>
<br>
The individual selected for this position will have excellent writing, speaking and interpersonal skills, convey a professional presence, and have the willingness to learn federal and state insurance and securities laws and guidelines. This position requires confidentiality and discretion, and due to the accessibility to sensitive material a criminal background check will be conducted as part of the selection process.<br>
<br>
Interested persons may be in an associated undergraduate program or graduate level program of study, with a focus in political science, public administration, data analytics, criminology, pre-law, paralegal, sociology, communications, consumer and family sciences, or other applicable program of study.<br>
<br>
This internship is <b>unpaid</b> but may be structured to fit your school's guidelines to be used for college credit and/or work study, depending upon the requirements of your school. The intern will be assigned to a specific bureau based on a best fit to their background, skillset, and interest.</div>
