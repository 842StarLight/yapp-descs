<h2>Senior Controls Engineer, Service Engineering</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<div>Position is located in Jupiter, FL.<br>
<br>
<b>Job Summary</b><br>
Provide controls, tuning and commissioning support to PSM’s Service Engineering group.<br>
<br>
<b>Reports to: </b>Manager, Tuning &amp; Commissioning / Service Engineering<br>
<br>
<b>Essential Duties and Responsibilities:</b><br>
The essential functions of the job include, but are not limited to, the following:<br>
<ul>
<li>Perform controls commissioning tasks such as regular system maintenance, IGV &amp; fuel valve calibration, repair and/or replacement of controls software, hardware and instruments</li>
<li>Perform control software upgrades, includes development support to PSM product engineering</li>
<li>Perform combustion tuning</li>
</ul>
</div>
<ul>
<li>Assist in controls-related diagnosis &amp; trouble-shooting</li>
<li>Generate specifications for the modification of existing control logic or the creation of new control logic to meet certain design criteria</li>
<li>Work directly with external vendors, perform Factory Acceptance Tests and support field validation of control logic</li>
<li>Write standard procedures and Work Instructions allowing non-controls engineers to perform basic and limited control systems operation</li>
<li>Provide technical support to the sales and tendering teams</li>
<li>Mentor and assist other less experienced engineers</li>
<li>Must be able to travel up to 75% of time on very short notice, including international travel</li>
<li>Other duties as assigned</li>
</ul>
<br>
<b>
Minimum Requirements</b><br>
<br>
<b>Education:</b><br>
<ul><li>Bachelor's degree in Mechanical or Electrical Engineering plus seven (7) years of progressive experience in commissioning of industrial gas turbine engines</li></ul>
<br>
<b>
Skills/Experience:</b><br>
<ul>
<li>Minimum of 7 years’ experience creating, modifying, simulating, implementing, field testing and troubleshooting logic sequences and Siemens’ T3000 and WDPF HMI screens and interfaces. Working knowledge of Siemens’ TXP (T2000)/Simadyn also required. Knowledge of Ovation or MHI’s Netmation environment a strong plus.</li>
<li>Minimum of 7 years’ experience utilizing appropriate design/analysis tools as necessary, such as control systems sequence builders and compilers, control system simulators, Human Machine Interface (HMI) screen editors, HMI consoles and controls, data gathering and reduction (HMI historian, PI, MS Excel, Matlab, etc.), scheduling (MS Project and Visio), reporting (MS PowerPoint) and others.</li>
<li>Minimum of 5 years’ experience with GT general architecture and design, working knowledge of GT support and ancillary systems such as fuel delivery, purge, instrument air and hydraulic supplies, understanding of associated limits and alarms and ability to troubleshoot and correct issues</li>
<li>Minimum of 5 years’ experience with instrument calibration procedures and typical tasks associated with recommissioning of GT after inspection, ability to perform associated hands-on fieldwork.</li>
<li>Multilingual candidate is a plus.</li>
</ul>
<br>
