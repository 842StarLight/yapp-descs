<h2>Senior GxP Systems Specialist</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>American Regent, Inc, a Daiichi Sankyo Group Company, develops, manufactures, and supplies high quality sterile injectables for healthcare providers, clinics and hospitals across the United States and Canada. We have locations in New York, Ohio, and Pennsylvania and sales territories nationwide.<br>
<br>
<b>Nature and Scope</b><br>
The Senior GxP Systems Specialist will be responsible for generating, reviewing and remediating computer system validation protocols in order to ensure that all regulatory requirements are met. They will undertake a wide range of duties relating to the leadership of projects. These responsibilities will include but are not limited to: the planning, execution, monitoring, controlling and delivery of projects.<br>
<br>
The Senior GxP Systems Specialist will report directly to the GxP Manager and work closely with members of the GxP team, IT, QA Compliance team.<br>
</p>
<div>
<b>Essential Duties and Responsibilities</b><br>
<ul>
<li>Collaborate with IT to gather infrastructure and architectural requirements pertaining to relevant projects.</li>
<li>Manage all project and compliance related issues associated with their project.</li>
<li>May support, coordinate and interface with regulatory agencies as required in support of regulatory agency inspections.</li>
<li>Support and maintain Quality programs, policies, processes, procedures and controls ensuring compliance with current Good Manufacturing Practices (cGMP) expectations and established agency requirements/guidelines.</li>
<li>Consistently demonstrate QUALITY culture and behaviors and perform all duties in accordance with Luitpold and Daiichi Sankyo Business Principles, corporate directives, SOPs, policies, and government and industry regulatory guidelines.</li>
<li>Create and implement business continuity plans, procedures and enhancements</li>
<li>Review IT Infrastructure vendor operations and outsourcing arrangements and collaborate with major service providers of IT Infrastructure</li>
<li>Perform infrastructure qualification activities, testing sequences and change controls. Oversight of execution with IT team members.</li>
<li>Collaborate with System Owner to develop Design Qualifications, Functional Requirements, User Requirements, Unit test scripts, UAT scripts, validation plans, validation summary report, traceability matrix, IQ/OQ/PQ</li>
<li>Develop department SOPs and provide SOP review for new systems.</li>
<li>Perform Validation/Part 11 Assessments for new and existing computer systems.</li>
<li>Maintain GxP Systems Inventory Log and update as necessary.</li>
<li>Participate in cross-functional project teams.</li>
<li>Identify and implement opportunities for continuous improvement related to validation or other compliance practices/issues based on internal compliance issues, industry best practices as well as latest regulatory guidance’s and regulatory updates.</li>
<li>Conduct validation related training to individuals performing various roles within validation projects.</li>
<li>Participate in the development and improvement of computerized systems to ensure quality and adherence to cGMPs.</li>
</ul>
<br>
<b>
Qualifications and Requirements</b><br>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor’s degree and a minimum of 5+ years’ experience dealing with GxP computer systems in the pharmaceutical or similar regulated industry. A technical background a plus.</li>
<li>Strong understanding of the current pharmaceutical industry and computerized system regulations, including CFR Part11.</li>
<li>Strong knowledge of appropriate Quality Systems and current Good Manufacturing Practice (cGMP) requirements (FDA, EU, ICH) with the ability to assess compliance risks.</li>
<li>Experience in providing oversight in implementing and maintaining GxP computer systems in a validated state.</li>
<li>Strong working knowledge in validation methodology and software development life cycle (SDLC) principles.</li>
<li>Demonstrates ability to perform detail-oriented work with a high degree of accuracy.</li>
<li>Excellent verbal, written and interpersonal communication skills.</li>
<li>Strong Business English for documentation including spelling, grammar and punctuation.</li>
<li>Effective time management and interpersonal skills.</li>
<li>Strong organizational skills, planning skills and must work effectively within teams.</li>
<li>Demonstrates good coping skills and analytical problem solving skills.</li>
<li>Ability to resolve conflicts and disputes on regulatory compliance issues.</li>
<li>Ability to perform limited travel both domestically and internationally.</li>
<li>Ability to work overtime as needed.</li>
</ul>
<br>
<br>
<b>Physical Environment and Physical Requirements</b><br>
<ul>
<li>Physically able to wear cleanroom/protective gowning and equipment, including but not limited to gowning, goggles, face shields, respirators, and protective gloves. PPE and Respirators are essential for the health and safety of employees.</li>
<li>Specific vision requirements include 20/20 near vision (can be corrected with eyeglasses or contacts) and color vision.</li>
<li>Employees must maintain a clean-shaven appearance each working shift to wear tight-fitting respirators properly.</li>
</ul>
