<h2>Interior Design Consultant</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p>POSITION SUMMARY: Plan and design furniture interiors for educational, governmental, commercial, or industrial buildings. Formulate design that is practical, aesthetic, and conducive to intended purposes, such as raising productivity, selling merchandise, or improving life style.</p>
<p>ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES</p>
<p>1. Confer with internal and external clients to determine factors affecting planning interior environments such as budget, architectural preferences, and purpose and function.<br>2. Advise clients on interior design factors such as space planning, layout and utilization of furnishing or equipment, and color coordination.<br>3. Gather information about projects by visiting job sites, taking measurements for products and designs, and coordinating often with sales representatives.<br>4. Estimate material requirements and costs, and present design to client for approval.<br>5. Work with sales representatives on the selection of pieces and products.<br>6. Prepare formal and informal presentations with sales representatives for customers including design boards, binders, and presentations.<br>7. Render design ideas in the form of computer drawings using computer-aided drafting (GIZA) software to produce construction documents.<br>8. Assist Operations as needed in coordinating construction or installation activities with the furniture department.<br>9. Review details of technical drawings or specifications.<br>10. Develop artistic or design concepts for decoration, exhibition, or commercial purposes.<br>11. Maintain inventories of materials, equipment, or products.<br>12. Maintain and organize resource room, keeping brochures and other items up-to-date and in stock.<br>13. Perform other duties as directed and needed.</p>
<p>MINIMUM JOB REQUIREMENTS</p>
<p>EDUCATION AND EXPERIENCE:</p>
<p>1. Bachelor’s degree in Interior Design preferred<br>2. 2 years’ experience or more preferred<br>3. Experience with a design software package<br>4. Experience with CAD, PowerPoint, and Adobe Creative Suites</p>
<p>KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES:<br>1. Knowledge of design techniques, tools, and principals involved in the production of technical plans and drawings.<br>2. Knowledge of large scale printing and plotters.<br>3. Knowledge of principles and processes for providing customer and personal service. This includes assessing customer needs, meeting quality standards for services, and evaluating customer satisfaction.<br>4. Knowledge of administrative and clerical procedures and systems such as word processing, file and record management, design forms, and other office procedures and terminology.<br>5. Ability to listen to and understand information and ideas presented through spoken words and sentences.<br>6. Ability to develop creative ways to solve problems.<br>7. Ability to imagine how something will look after it is moved around or when its parts are moved or rearranged.<br>8. Ability to communicate information and ideas clearly and effectively to interior and exterior customers in spoken and written communication.<br>9. Ability to think creatively by developing, designing, or creating new applications, ideas, relations, systems, or products, including artistic contributions.<br>10. Ability to develop constructive and cooperative working relationships with others, and maintain them over time.<br>11. Ability to organize, plan, and prioritize work by developing specific goals.<br>12. Ability to use basic math skills including addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.<br>13. Ability to use the Microsoft Office package.</p>
<p>WORK ENVIRONMENT</p>
<p>Physical Demands:<br>This position may spend long hours sitting and using office equipment and computers, and may require occasional bending, kneeling, stretching and reaching. The Interior Designer may occasionally stand on concrete floors. May require occasional travel by car to visit customers or worksites</p>
<p>Working Conditions:<br>The Interior Designer works in a closed office. The area is well-light and climate controlled. This position must meet with others on a regular basis and must spend long hours in intense concentration. The individual may also spend long hours on the computer designing, which requires attention to detail and high levels of accuracy. The noise level is moderate and there are frequent interruptions with walk-ins, employees and phone calls. There are a number of daily deadlines associated with this position. This position must also deal with a wide variety of people on various issues via phone, in person, or through electronic and written communications.</p>
