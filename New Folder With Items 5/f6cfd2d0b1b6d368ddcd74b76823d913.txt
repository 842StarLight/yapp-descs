<h2>Sr. Contract Administrator</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div><div><div><div><div>
<div>Imagine a working environment where people trust each other to achieve exceptional things.<br>
Imagine a business that employs over ten thousand of the world’s most sought-after engineers and technologists.<br>
Imagine an enterprise where a belief in personal accountability continues to drive groundbreaking innovation and global growth.<br>
This is the reality of Moog – and you can be part of it.<br>
</div>
<div></div>
<div>
As a Sr. Contract Administrator in Moog’s Industrial Group you will be responsible for reviewing and analyzing complex contractual requirements, including terms and conditions, and be able to determine which exceptions are necessary to protect Moog’s contractual position. When an exception is required, the candidate must develop the Division's position and support at negotiations. The Senior CA may be called upon to assist Contract Administrators and Data Administrators in the interpretation of contract requirements and offer strategies to resolve contract problems.<br>
<br>
What you can expect day-to-day:<br>
<ul><li>The Senior Contract Administrator is responsible for reviewing, writing, and initiating contracts and subcontracts documentation for Moog Inc. and to protect the Division's legal position. When legal support or advice is required, the incumbent consults with Corporate Legal Counsel. "Contracts" in this document, means contracts between MOOG and the Customer. "Subcontracts" means subcontracts between MOOG and Its major suppliers.</li></ul>
<br>
<ul>
<li>The Senior CA will review assigned Contracts prior to award to assure that all technical requirements, prices, terms and conditions, and delivery agree with Moog’s proposal, negotiated agreement, and division policies. The Senior CA will write any subcontract document for all of the division’s major subcontractors as well as some of the division's international customers. The Senior CA negotiates new contracts and contract change orders.</li>
<li>The Senior CA is the primary focal point between the customer (and supplier) and the division. Any verbal or written communications from the customer and major suppliers must be documented and disseminated to the pertinent persons in the division in a timely manner. In turn, communications generated by program or engineering personnel must be forwarded to the customer or supplier in the same manner.</li>
<li>The Senior CA will summarize the Contract requirements, as well as any modifications, in a Contract Status Report. This report is distributed to relevant personnel in the division and serves as a Sales Order.</li>
</ul>
<br>
<ul>
<li>This role may also be responsible for administration of foreign contracts. This requires the Senior CA to be thoroughly knowledgeable about the legal ramifications of proposing to foreign nationals, selling and shipping of U.S. high technology products and documentation overseas.</li>
<li>Insure integrity of Moog’s contractual performance.</li>
</ul>
<br>
<ul><li>Review all contractual documentation for compliance to division policy and prior agreements.</li></ul>
<br>
<ul><li>Review Terms and Conditions provided by Marketing.</li></ul>
<br>
<ul><li>Initiate new job by issuing the Contract Status Report.</li></ul>
<br>
<ul><li>Insure all incoming and outgoing correspondence between the customer and the division is being distributed to the responsible division personnel.</li></ul>
<br>
<ul><li>Process and negotiate contract changes.</li></ul>
<br>
<ul><li>Initiate shipping documents (packing slip, commercial invoices, etc.) and insures that hardware and deliverable documentation are compliant with export licenses.</li></ul>
<br>
<ul><li>Process milestone billings.</li></ul>
<br>
<ul><li>Maintain excellent customer and supplier relations.</li></ul>
<br>
<ul><li>Serve as the primary focal point between the Customer and the division.</li></ul>
<br>
<ul><li>Obtain all export licenses and other necessary government authorizations required for exporting hardware, documentation, and technical services.</li></ul>
<br>
<ul><li>Insure export compliance in the division by training/communicating requirements to senior management and contract, marketing, and program office personnel.</li></ul>
<br>
<ul><li>Writes and negotiates subcontracts.</li></ul>
<br>
<ul><li>Has signature authority for contracts and subcontracts up to $500,000.</li></ul>
<br>
You’ll need...<br>
Minimum Bachelor's Degree<br>
Minimum of 5 years of on the job experience
<br>
Must have working knowledge of the Federal Acquisition Regulations (FAR) and Uniform Commercial Code (UCC). The person designated as the responsible Export Administrator will have a working knowledge of the U.S. Export Administration Regulations (EAR) and International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR).</div>
