<h2>Curriculum & Instructional Designer</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">Job Introduction
<br>The Curriculum &amp; Instructional Designer is required to maintain relationships with a wide range of interested parties on the team (Management, Subject Matter Experts, Support Teams) with the least amount of friction as possible. This would require enormous amounts of communication of all kinds (writing, speaking, listening, presenting) as this job essentially personifies "collaboration" in order to keep teams running as well-oiled machines, implementing decisions after thorough discussions, and managing people where their roles entwine.
<br><br>
Main Responsibilities
<br>The Curriculum &amp; Instructional Designer needs to wear multiple hats, playing the role of facilitator, innovator, researcher, and developer. They work with other departments and subject matter experts to develop learning objectives, conduct needs assessments, and design programs. They select and use a variety of techniques to define and develop instructional materials, leveraging available technology to deliver. At times, they need to use creative, outside-the-box methods to deliver materials and projects, using what is available and recommend programs, as needed.
<br><br>
Role Responsibilities
<br><br>
Conduct a needs assessment to determine the appropriate need for the business request. After proper assessment, determine the information to be shared, method for sharing (training, job aid, team huddle, email, etc.), deliver to the correct audience, and update the relevant training manuals as needed. If offline training is needed, develop the training and ensure documentation is updated for the pertinent training manual
<br>Design a curriculum or department program by using Web-based learning, Instructor-led training, Virtual classroom training, Guides (such as job aids, quick reference guides, infographics) and blended solutions
<br>Select and use a variety of techniques for determining instructional content
<br>Select and use a variety of techniques to define and sequence the instructional content and strategies
<br>Develop new instructional materials and select or modify existing instructional materials
<br>Design and develop and provide effective implementation of instructional material and programs in a corporate and consultative setting that reflects an understanding of the diversity of learner
<br>Evaluate and assess instruction and its impact
<br>Plan and manage instructional design projects
<br>Promote collaboration, partnerships and relationships among the participants in a design project
<br>Design a standard process for training material that includes variations of approach to suit all learning styles that includes exercises, activities, discussions, role plays, etc.
<br>Responsible for creating and maintaining the Customer Solution Center knowledge management tool
<br>Create and deliver communication including but not limited to department wide announcements, weekly newsletters
<br>Facilitate product launches and new releases through e-learning content and knowledge management updates, or other outlets as needed
<br>Act as the Communication Expert, using email, alerts, knowledge center, and other programs as needed to communicate important information for teams as needed
<br>Create feedback channel for associates to request future communication content
<br>Employ strong people skills, including but not limited to the ability to facilitate meetings and handle both ambiguity and constant change within the team and with the client
<br>Deliver on time and within an efforts-estimate budget
<br>Conduct a post training review to assess the curriculum and training effectiveness and modify and update as required by the business
<br>Proactively seek and strive for professional growth to increase effectiveness of skill sets
<br>Other duties as assigned
