<b>GENERAL SUMMARY:</b><br>
The Schedules Supervisor is an analyst role and a support position that focuses on operational service level improvement, EBITDA improvement, and general process improvement. The role performs analysis as requested by Executives and the Operations team. As a Business Analyst you are expected to acquire knowledge of all facets of the business to be able to present accurate analytical findings and other value added insights.
<br><br>
PRINCIPAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES (The below listed duties are not all inclusive. This position must also perform other duties as assigned.):
<br><br>
<ul>
<li>Setup COPS (back office application) for all new account startups, acquisitions, and integrations in Nashville.</li>
<li>Take and complete all financial, operational, and service related analytical requests for the Nashville branch.</li>
<li>Complete P&amp;L research and develop reporting that will allow the Nashville team to manage and analyze specific P&amp;L items more efficiently.</li>
<li>Audit company data as requested by the business team.</li>
<li>Process customer bids (RFPs) and collaborate with Sales and Executives to assist with adding new business</li>
<li>Perform route audits and other various reviews of Nashville service center costs to identify EBITDA improvements. Propose those findings to management team and assist with implementation.</li>
<li>Assist with creating, implementing, and auditing Standard Operating Procedures for the business</li>
<li>Work with teammates to provide assistance during startups, training, and other initiatives</li>
<li>Supervise and train all scheduling personnel.</li>
<li>Attend weekly branch supervisors meeting.</li>
<li>Ensure daily route coverage with correct vehicle type.</li>
<li>Evaluate and adjust routed activity on a daily basis to meet required service levels.</li>
<li>Work closely with Corporate Support Services (CSS) Department to ensure costs are in line and are approved prior to updating scheduled tickets.</li>
<li>Communicate updated route changes to CSS daily so changes to route database can be made.</li>
<li>Ensure drivers have updated route sheets on a daily basis.</li>
<li>Manage driver non-availability requests to ensure adequate coverage on a daily basis and complete route coverage sheet for the following day.</li>
<li>Monitor next-flight-out financial work and communicate any problems with solutions to customers and branch personnel.</li>
<li>Collect, account for and file all completed route documentation as soon as available but no less than weekly.</li>
<li>Fulfill requested confirmations, route sheets, etc. to the customer.</li>
<li>Print out route check-in sheet for dispatch each night and ensure dispatch has access to updated route sheets.</li>
<li>Secure, assign and track keys and keycards for applicable customers.</li>
<li>Coordinate and assist with Fleet Ops Coordinator for Owner Operator orientation and enter commission tickets.</li>
<li>Complete scheduled Owner Operator commission adjustments and send to the accounting department at the corporate office.</li>
<li>Work with CSS to innovate new processes and procedures that increase efficiency, service levels and profitability.</li>
</ul>
<b>KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES REQUIRED:</b><br>
<b>Education/Experience Required:</b> Four-year college degree preferably in Logistics, Business Management, Management Information Systems or similar field of study.
<br><br>
<b>Abilities Required:</b> Must have strong analytical skills, be self-motivated, self-managed and be able to lead projects through completion. Also must have extensive knowledge of Microsoft Excel and some experience in T-SQL. Knowledge of SQL Server Reporting Services and SharePoint or willingness to learn the software is preferred. Must be able to travel at least 30% of the time.
