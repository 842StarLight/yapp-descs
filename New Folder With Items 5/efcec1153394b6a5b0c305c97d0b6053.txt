<p>POSITION SUMMARY:</p>
<br>
<p>
</p>
<p>Strives to return a satisfactory profit on dealer capital invested in parts inventory. Accomplishes objectives through the use of proper purchasing procedures, inventory control, staff utilization, security, pricing, merchandising, displaying, and advertising.</p>
<p>
ESSENTIAL DUTIES and RESPONSIBILITIES</p>
<p>
Essential Duties include the following. Other duties may be assigned.<br>
Forecasts goals and objectives for the department and strives to meet them.<br>
Hires, trains, motivates, counsels, and monitors the performance of all parts department staff.<br>
Prepares and administers an annual operating budget for the parts department.<br>
Maintains a balanced inventory consistent with the requirements of the service department, body shop, wholesale accounts, and the do-it-yourself public.<br>
Establishes pricing parameters in each customer category that generate sufficient gross profit to produce a satisfactory profit while maintaining customer loyalty.<br>
Establishes individual parts inventory levels and balances them for maximum turnover.<br>
Ensures that parts inventory turns specified times per year, as determined by Division Manager.<br>
Monitors and adjusts inventory to minimize obsolescence.<br>
Ensures that all parts are properly tagged so that the dealership recovers full credit from the factory.<br>
Supervises stock order procedures.<br>
Sets and enforces a policy on the special ordering of parts. Ensures that stock orders represent at least the specified percent of all orders, as determined by the Division Manager.<br>
Analyzes sales, expenses, and inventory monthly to maintain profit goals.<br>
Develops and administers an aggressive wholesale parts program to produce profit.<br>
Ensures that the same high quality level of service provided to outside customers is also provided to internal dealership customers.<br>
Works with the service manager and body shop manager to ensure a timely turnaround of parts needed for internal jobs.<br>
Attends managers meetings.<br>
Holds weekly department meetings.<br>
Analyzes departmental operations and storage layout and revises as needed for maximum effectiveness.<br>
Provides technical assistance to parts department employees.<br>
Monitors parts department employees' payroll records.<br>
Coordinates a prompt, efficient, and timely flow of paperwork.<br>
Directs shipping and receiving efforts to ensure timely processing.<br>
Monitors daily reports such as DOE, DOC, and sales productivity.<br>
Develops and utilizes a lost sales tracking report.<br>
Directs outside parts and counter sales efforts, including a telemarketing program to keep in contact with customers.<br>
Develops sales promotions.<br>
Takes advantage of all manufacturers' inventory co-op advertising.<br>
Develops, enforces, and monitors guidelines for working with customers to ensure maximum customer satisfaction.<br>
Handles customer complaints immediately and according to the dealership's guidelines.<br>
Ensures that all dealership purchases are properly accounted for before payment is made.<br>
Assists in the collection of past-due accounts.<br>
Supervises custodians to ensure that work area is kept clean.<br>
Enforces safety requirements, including but not limited to forklift training and operation, processing of MSDS sheets on all products carried, and OSHA Right-to-Know.<br>
Understands, keeps abreast of, and complies with federal, state, and local regulations that may affect parts sales.<br>
Maintains professional appearance.<br>
Adheres to all safety rules and regulations<br>
Performs other duties as assigned.</p>

REQUIRED SKILLS</p>
<p>
Ability to read and interpret documents such as safety rules and procedure manuals.<br>
Ability to write routine reports and correspondence.<br>
Ability to speak effectively before groups of customers or employees of organization.<br>
Ability to respond to common inquiries or complaints from customers, regulatory agencies, or members of the business community.<br>
Ability to effectively present information to top management, public groups, and/or boards of directors.<br>
Proficient in business math to include factoring discounts, interest, commissions, and applying common sense reasoning to compile a business plan. Ability to apply concepts of basic algebra and geometry.</p>
