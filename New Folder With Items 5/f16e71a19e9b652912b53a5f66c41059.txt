<h2>Chief Financial Officer</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<b>Salary Range:</b> DOE
<br><b>Reports to:</b> CEO Job Location: Main Office
<br><b>Hours/Week:</b> 40 FLSA Status: Exempt
<br><b>Employment Status:</b> Regular, At Will Pay Basis: Semi-Monthly
<br>The Opportunity
<br>Habitat for Humanity of Sonoma County is Non-Profit Housing Developer providing a range of housing and human
<br>services. Habitat for Humanity of Sonoma County seeks a talented Chief Financial Officer ("CFO"). The CFO is responsible
<br>for all aspects of financial planning, accounting and execution, for a non-profit housing developer, including construction
<br>retail sales and human services.
<br><br>
<b>Reporting Relationships:</b><br>
The CFO reports to CEO. The CFO will direct his/her own internal staff including the Accounting Manager, Accounting
<br>Specialist, Payroll Administrator and any Volunteers working in the Finance Department and will also direct those
<br>responsible for producing annual Tax and other compliance documents, audits and reports.
<br>The Ideal Candidate
<br>The CFO will establish strategy and vision to build the HFHSC financial structure and allocate all revenue and expenses
<br>appropriately. Working with the CEO, and senior management the CFO will set important financial goals for the
<br>organization and work systematically to meet them. The CFO will lead the design and implementation of tools and
<br>procedures for financial accounting and management of a non-profit housing developer/ builder and retail store(s).
<br>Core Responsibilities
<br><ul>
<li>Present and report accurate and timely historical financial information, information which meets the needs and</li>
</ul>
informs decision making by managers, CEO and Board
<br><ul>
<li>Project and report cash and financial condition with the information meeting the needs and informs decision</li>
</ul>
making by managers, CEO and Board
<br><ul>
<li>Accurate analysis and projection of cost and revenue relating to current and proposed housing developments,</li>
</ul>
programs and initiatives
<br><ul>
<li>Identify financial weakness and opportunities and recommend options for improvements to decision makers</li>
<li>Serve as internal consultant to the CEO, COO, Board, CDO, Human Resources and other key stakeholders on all</li>
</ul>
financial matters, making recommendations and suggesting pro-active strategies to keep HFHSC on track
<br><ul>
<li>Work in conjunction with the Homeowners Services Department to ensure the proper execution of financial</li>
</ul>
procedures for all home loans and mortgages
<br><ul>
<li>Provide oversight for and manage all budgets, forecasts and internal financial plans and processes</li>
<li>Support accounting, general ledger and operations functions, ensuring that systems are in place to guarantee</li>
</ul>
timeliness and accuracy
<br><ul>
<li>Review short and long-term goals, considering existing and projected financial resources available</li>
</ul>
Establish annual priorities and financial benchmarks and conduct regular reporting, measuring projections
<br>against actual performance
<br><ul>
<li>Build relationships with banks, donors, vendors, outside consultants and others as needed to keep close to our</li>
</ul>
major expenditures, sources of revenue and In-Kind donations
<br><ul>
<li>Build a top-tier internal finance team by actively recruiting, training and developing talented accountants,</li>
</ul>
analysts, consultants, auditors, tax planners and payroll staff, as needed
<br>Key Credentials and Personal Qualities
<br><ul>
<li>Degree in Accounting or Finance strongly preferred</li>
<li>Experience with housing development and construction financials strongly preferred</li>
<li>Experience with home loans and mortgages preferred</li>
<li>5 years' senior financial leadership experience</li>
<li>10 years' experience total accounting/finance experience</li>
<li>Commitment to results; 'can-do" mindset; outstanding problem-solving ability</li>
<li>Strong motivational and staff leadership abilities</li>
<li>Excellent analytical, communication and presentation skills</li>
<li>Sense of humor, integrity, and personal sense of accountability</li>
<li>Knowledge of accounting and management software for housing development, construction, retail, and nonprofits</li>
<li>Experience with Office Suite and Dropbox strongly preferred</li>
</ul>
<b>Physical Requirements:</b> While performing the duties of this job, the employee is required to use a computer both in the
<br>office and off-site. It is also required to sit in meetings, requiring concentration. The employees may also be required to
<br>stand while executing activities or behalf of the organization talk and hear extensively, both in person and using the
<br>telephone and occasionally lift and move items up to 25 lbs.
<br><b>Time Requirements:</b> This is a full-time, exempt, 40 hours per week position. It may be necessary to occasionally work
<br>extra hours or some evenings and weekends, as determined by supervisor.
<br><b>Benefits:</b> We offer full time employees Health, Dental and Life Insurance coverage, sick and vacation leave and all
<br>standard Holidays.
