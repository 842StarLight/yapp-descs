<h2>Project Engineer, Fixation</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><b>ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES
</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Designs, develops and processes implants and instruments utilizing Solidworks CAD software.</li>
<li>Leads the development of new products and manufacturing processes and/or serves as a member of a project team.</li>
<li>Directs the development of working prototype models to be used for project design evaluation.</li>
<li>Generates protocols for testing and analyzing new and current products.</li>
<li>Generates design assurance documentation for the project Design History File (DHF).</li>
<li>Develops quality control procedures and inspection methods</li>
<li>Initiates design changes relative to manufacturability while maintaining critical features of each product for in house manufacturing or vendors</li>
<li>Leads and/or serves on cross-functional product development teams responsible for new product development from concept through product launch.</li>
<li>Provides technical input to marketing counterparts on the development of collateral marketing materials</li>
<li>Provides technical expertise to marketing and sales as to intent of design function.</li>
<li>Provides technical experience to Regulatory Affairs to support FDA 510K submissions.</li>
<li>Creates and processes Engineering Change Orders (ECOâ€™s)</li>
<li>Other duties as assigned.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>QUALIFICATIONS
</b></p>
<p>The requirements listed below are representative of the knowledge, skill, and/or ability required. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions.</p>
<ul>
<li>Experience with 3D CAD system, preferably Solidworks</li>
<li>Experience with phase gate design control processes</li>
<li>Demonstrated success in leading cross-functional product development teams from concept through product launch, including demonstrated planning, organizing and time management skills</li>
<li>Prior experience in a manufacturing environment, including knowledge of manufacturing methods, predominantly with metals and plastics</li>
<li>Knowledge in the use and interpretation of geometric dimensioning and tolerancing</li>
<li>Strong verbal and written communication skills; comfortable presenting to senior management</li>
</ul>
<p><b>EDUCATION and/or EXPERIENCE</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Undergraduate degree in mechanical or biomedical engineering, with an emphasis in biomaterials and biomechanics</li>
<li>4-6 years of product development experience in implantable orthopedic medical devices, preferably in spine.</li>
</ul>
