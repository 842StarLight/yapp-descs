<h2>Contract Bid Senior Analyst</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div>
<p>American Regent, Inc., a Daiichi Sankyo Group Company, develops, manufactures, and supplies high quality sterile injectables for healthcare providers, clinics, hospitals and veterinary practices across the United States and Canada. We have locations in New York, Ohio, and Pennsylvania and sales territories nationwide.<br>
<br>
<b>Nature and Scope</b><br>
This position is responsible for contract administration by facilitating and organizing inter-departmental efforts to produce compelling, high-quality terms, conditions and proposals that are responsive to client and contract objectives, and adhere to detailed requirements, and meet strict deadlines.</p>
<p></p>
<p>Within all your activities, striving to develop and implement a consistent contracting approach and process, and to proactively strengthen and support the development of contract knowledge within the target markets and business teams.<br>
<br>
<b>Essential Duties and Responsibilities</b><br>
Act as a liaison between Commercial Management Leader &amp; Team Members, Compliance, Accounting, and Legal teams where applicable to ensure thorough and appropriate assessment of contractual documents while adhering to required timelines.<br>
Assist Commercial Management team in bid and proposal activity by reviewing proposal after bid/no bid decisions are made.<br>
Utilize and apply previously agreed upon contract language and fall back provisions to mitigate risk, and help ensure compliance with the regulatory requirements in the target markets.<br>
Liaison for internal/external communications throughout the proposal process between Group Purchasing Organizations (GPO) and Integrated Delivery Networks (IDN’s) Customers and Legal Department.<br>
Ensure all certifications, representative reports and exhibits required for contractual compliance are prepared, maintained and submitted to customer.<br>
Receive and respond to Request for Proposals (RFP’s) and bid invites.<br>
Upload projects to approved system along with updates to each project for Legal review.<br>
Assist in generating and compiling all the data and supplemental information necessary to be submitted with quotes (i.e. pricing exhibits, return policies, etc.)<br>
Advise all necessary departments of contractual rights and obligations, and work with Commercial Leadership Management to resolve contract issues.<br>
Request, review and implement modifications to or extensions of contracts or amendments of solicitations.<br>
Responsible for the completion of Bid packages and Pre-Qualifications forms due to Vendor.<br>
Review terms and conditions on contracts for accuracy, completeness and conformity.<br>
Check contract files for proper maintenance, documentation and other necessary paperwork.<br>
Requesting updates from vendors for projects out to bid and assist with project close out.<br>
Maintain a professional relationship with vendors/contractors and ensuring all materials and documents pertaining to bids are made available to affected parties.<br>
Assist with pre and post proposal inquiries; Follow up with customer on award status of contract/RFP’s.<br>
Monitor and control documents on multiple projects.<br>
Process addendums and revisions as required.<br>
Working collaboratively, effectively and efficiently with all internal and external partners and stakeholders.<br>
Providing internal stakeholders with information regarding department processes, procedures and contract development as requested.<br>
Consistently adhere to contract and document tracking requirements.<br>
</p>
<p><b>Qualifications and Requirements</b><br>
Bachelor’s degree in Business Management or equivalent years’ experience required.<br>
Minimum of six years’ experience required; Pharmaceutical or Healthcare contracting preferred.<br>
Superbly organized and extremely detail oriented.<br>
Technology savvy professional with an ability to actively learn and utilize proprietary software; proficiency in Microsoft Word, Excel and Outlook a must.<br>
Must possess strong verbal and writing communication skills, excellent time-management and organizational skills; ability to prioritize and follow through on assignments to completion with limited supervision.<br>
Must be flexible and able to work independently under tight deadlines; handle multiple tasks, and respond quickly to changing priorities.<br>
Able to work in a collaborative work environment, be willing to assist/support others, think creatively and take initiative.<br>
