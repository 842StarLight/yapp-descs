<h2>Unemployment Tax Representative</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div><div><div><div>
<div>
<p>Monitor and audit accounts and activities to ensure employer compliance with state and federal Unemployment Insurance (UI) Tax Laws. Travel within designated service area to conduct on-site audits. Follow established protocol to notify employer; coordinate logistics; complete audit; document results; and conduct follow-up work. Ensure activities comply with state Unemployment Tax Laws and meet Tax Performance System (TPS) requirements. Determine UI tax liability for new and existing employers and individuals providing remuneration services. Verify proper wages so that UI benefits and taxes may be accurately determined; and contact employers when claim wages, financial information, or reports are missing or incorrect. Initiate actions to collect delinquent taxes and quarterly tax reports. Conduct investigations and skip tracing to locate responsible parties and assets. Secure bankruptcy information to protect agency interests. Prepares written Liability Determination letters. Perform tax enforcement actions, including representing the Virginia Employment Commission (VEC) at Sheriff sales and in court. Authorize tax liens to seize bank accounts and other accounts receivable belonging to the delinquent employer or liable party. Conduct on-site asset investigations; and issue subpoenas, arrest warrants, Memoranda of Lien, and requests of FiFAs. Interact extensively with various parties (taxpayers, attorneys, accountants, corporate officers and public); respond to e-mail, telephone and written inquiries; obtain and verify information; resolve issues; and interpret and apply UI tax law. Use automated systems to track, monitor and document activities.</p>
</div>
<br>
<div>
<div><b>Minimum Qualifications</b></div>
<p>Knowledge of regulations and procedures related to UI Employer Tax requirements; quality assurance and cash management principles; generally accepted accounting principles and practices; and collections activities. Strong investigative, negotiation, customer service, and computer skills, including using word processing and spreadsheet software (Microsoft Office Professional). Ability to interpret and apply laws and regulations. Ability to communicate effectively both orally and in writing with various parties. Ability to work independently and collaboratively to meet goals. Ability to conduct research and audits; review and analyze financial and accounting records; collect, analyze report data; and make sound recommendations. Ability to prepare reports; and provide court testimony and technical assistance.</p>
</div>
<br>
<div>
<div><b>Preferred Qualifications</b></div>
<p>Graduation from an accredited college or university with a degree in business, accounting, finance or related field; or an equivalent combination of experience and training. Experience in tax investigation, auditing or accounting. Experience working with state and federal UI tax laws and related policies and procedures. Ability to speak Spanish.</p>
</div>
<br>
<div>
<div><b>Special Requirements</b></div>
<div>
<p>The selected candidate must successfully pass a background investigation. Travel is involved to visit employers and courts. Position does work an assigned area. Must have a valid driver’s license and availability of a vehicle.</p>
<p>
This position is prohibited from performing tax or accounting services for compensation during or outside of business hours that could result in a conflict of interest.</p>
