<h2>Business Manager I</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Overview
</b></h2>
<div><div><div>
<p><b>Position Summary:</b><br>
This Business Manager position will work with Event Services and report to the Director of the Event Services and to the Financial Officer for Auxiliaries. USU Event Services provides and serves as a one-stop shop and coordination point for scheduling, logistics, and contracting of on/off site University related events. USU Event Services is dedicated to strengthening the University community by providing comprehensive event management services, facilitating the effective and efficient year-round use of university resources, and cultivating lasting relationships with clients, partners and service providers.</p>
<div>
The Business Manager's scope of responsibilities is broad and includes being a resource and business consultant/partner both within the department and outside of the department. This role will include supervising coworkers, business and transaction analysis, program/event monitoring, internal and external transaction processing, monthly financial reporting, and ownership for department business processes and transactions. This position will require strict attention to the details and the ability to resolve questions/issues in a courteous and timely manner. As the team member focused on financial aspects, you will orchestrate and foster a positive work environment while providing sound, accurate, and timely business analysis and counsel.</div>
<div></div>
<br>
<div>
ACA Full-time position.</div>
</div></div></div>
<h2 class="jobSectionHeader"><b>Responsibilities
</b></h2>
<div><div><div>
<div><b>Responsibilities:</b></div>
<ul>
<li>
Function as a primary resource and business consultant to the Director of Event Services.
</li>
<li>Oversee and manage all general business aspects and transactions related to the department.</li>
<li>
Review, approve, analyze, and generate transactions and reports related to all aspects of the business processes within their assigned units and areas of responsibility (including but not limited to Banner, Travel, EZ Buy, Workflow, Service Now, etc.</li>
<li>
Contribute to, collaborate with, and participate with other business service units, leaders and personnel across the University.</li>
<li>
Assist administration and staff in all human resource, budget, and financial matters within their areas of responsibility.</li>
<li>
Provide monthly accounting reports and status of the financials regarding each department and each conference that the department is coordinating.</li>
<li>
Review and approve all contracts for the department in regards to future financial obligations.</li>
<li>
Process all month-end entries in a timely manner to ensure accurate month-end reporting.</li>
<li>
Analyze financials and create budget projections. Present financials to staff as directed.</li>
<li>
Compare actual revenues and expenses to budgeted revenues and expenses, analyze discrepancies.</li>
<li>
Advise Director and staff on all financial activity such as purchasing, transfers, budgeting, forecasting, etc.</li>
<li>
Ensure all financial transactions are properly recorded, processed, and documented.</li>
<li>
Communicate and coordinate with other colleges and departments within and outside USU regarding financial matters.</li>
<li>
Process scholarships for department and others as needed.</li>
<li>
Supervise assigned co-workers.</li>
<li>
Participate in external organizations as needed.</li>
<li>
Handle discrepancy resolution - Resolve problem(s) by clarifying the customer's complaint; determining the cause of the problem; selecting and explaining the best solution; expediting correction or adjustment; following up to ensure resolution.</li>
<li>
Develop and maintain databases.</li>
<li>
Commit to integrity; perform duties with diligence in accordance with professional standards and enforce University policies and procedures; use relevant information and individual judgment to determine whether events or processes comply with laws, regulations, or standards.</li>
<li>
Serve the interest of Utah State University in a lawful manner, while maintaining high standards of conduct and character.</li>
<li>
Handle confidential information and other financial/accounting responsibilities designated by the Financial Officer. Manage assigned projects effectively and demonstrate reliability and strong work ethic.</li>
<li>
Participate in short/long-range budget planning and the development, implementation, and maintenance of financial objectives.</li>
<li>
Maintain annual budget using proper accounting/budgeting methods and retain records of financial figures for comparison with estimated budget.</li>
<li>
Must understand accrual accounting/cost accounting.</li>
<li>
Other duties as assigned.</li>
</ul>

Work Scope:</b>

<li>
Knows and applies fundamental financial and/or some administrative/business functions directly related to the management or general business operations of the University or the University's customers.</li>
<li>
May relieve administrator responsibilities by assisting with special projects.</li>
<li>
May have supervisory duties.</li>
<li>
Exercises discretion and independent judgment.</li>
<li>
Conducts and analyzes research in different areas such as financial and business related studies; cooperates with other departments in the preparation of analysis.</li>
<li>
Prepares charts to illustrate analysis and other important information.</li>
<li>
Interprets/explains the findings of data analysis to inform customers in a clear and understandable manner.</li>
<li>
Recognizes inefficiencies and applies/recommends innovative or more effective solutions.</li>
<li>
Follows precedents and procedures; sets priorities and organizes work based on deadlines and level of importance determined by the financial officer.</li>
<li>
Identifies the underlying principles, reasons, or facts by breaking down data into separate parts to inform, solve problems, or determine an appropriate course of action.</li>
<li>
Identifies information by categorizing, estimating, recognizing differences or similarities, and detecting changes in circumstances or events.</li>
<li>
Coordinates business/financial responsibilities directly related to the management or general business operation of the University or the University's customers.</li>
<li>
Ensures compliance of unit.</li>
</ul>
