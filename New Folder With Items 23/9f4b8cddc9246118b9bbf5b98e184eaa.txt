<h2>Regulatory Affairs Specialist</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>The Regulatory Affairs Specialist is responsible for a variety of tasks to maintain product compliance which includes, but is not limited to, EPA pesticide registration, REACH registration, authoring and reviewing Safety Data Sheets, reviewing graphic proofs, and maintaining ERP and Intranet with required information.</p>
<p>
This role develops master labels or supplemental labels, maintains registration renewals and regulatory reporting calendars to avoid lapses. The Regulatory Affairs Specialist responds to EPA data calling or audits, customer inquiries, and stays up to date with regulation changes and develops action plans to stay ahead.</p>
<p>
This position also assists with various reporting functions to organize documentation and labels. The right candidate for this position will be self-motivated, possess the ability to work independently and in a team environment, and have the ability to meet strict deadlines.
</p>
<p><b>Duties &amp; Responsibilities:</b></p>
<p><b>
EPA - Pesticide</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Maintain Federal and State Registrations, registration renewal calendars, Master Registration data, Company number and address changes as needed</li>
<li>Register new product labels or label revisions</li>
<li>Complete Annual State Registrations for current EPA product offering</li>
<li>Utilize X3 to maintain compliance and block sales in States not registered</li>
<li>Conduct Audits of PI sub-registrations to ensure compliance</li>
<li>Review Pesticide labeling against latest EPA guidance</li>
<li>Maintain any 6(a)(2) reporting requirements for Adverse effects</li>
<li>Maintain and update efficacy testing as needed, per approved labs and test protocols</li>
<li>Respond to any Data Call In’s as required by EPA via CDX website</li>
<li>Complete comparison reviews as requested by PI, BDM</li>
<li>Work with Purchasing and Lab to verify CSF is followed.</li>
<li>Ensure Notice of Arrival, import paperwork is completed and filed through customs for any EPA registered products produced outside the US</li>
<li>Review raw material suppliers against approvals from CSF, request new suppliers to be approved if needed for FIFRA compliance</li>
</ul>
<p><b>International</b></p>
<p>
Harmonized Tariff Schedule - Maintain X3 system with any new updates for products shipped internationally.</p>
<ul>
<li>EU<ul>
<li>REACH reporting and registration</li>
<li>Create SDS in EU format per CLP-GHS regulations</li>
<li>Reviews Detergents Directive and Biocides Directive compliance, country-specific regulations and develop and implement action plans</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Canada<ul>
<li>Periodically review Canada Gazette for any new or updated regulations applicable to our business</li>
<li>Maintain Canadian and Drug registrations</li>
<li>SDS and labels - Review WHMIS 2015 regulations and create compliant SDS based on our sales to Canada</li>
<li>Review Canada Inventory information, DSL, NDSL</li>
<li>Shipping - TDG, be familiar with and understand the differences from DOT, utilize X3 Outside field to maintain any difference</li>
<li>Trade Secret - Submit formula information to Health Canada with justification for why this should be CBI</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Korea, China, South America, Thailand<ul><li>
Assist with SDS formats and any country specific registrations</li></ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p><b>SDS - GHS</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Author SDS for finished products
</li>
<li>Review new raw material EH&amp;S profile for product development team
</li>
<li>Research each CAS# and properly classify in Lisam system</li>
<li>Load and Maintain data in Lisam system for generation of SDS</li>
<li>Work with IT to keep System Versions up-to-Date for software applications</li>
<li>Maintain an SDS for all chemical products following new GHS – OSHA regulations</li>
<li>Update X3 for MSDS</li>
<li>Review all new Raw Materials, ensure up-to-date information from supplier and ensure proper breakdown is entered</li>
<li>Review all Resale SDS's and maintain active copy</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Additional Regulatory Review</b></p>
<ul>
<li>NSF, if requested submit formula for review by NSF for use in Incidental Food Contact
</li>
<li>VOC compliance</li>
<li>Green Seal create SDS and labels in compliance with additional restrictions to comply with certification process</li>
<li>BSE/TSE, Animal Origin, use EU Directives for compliance</li>
<li>Ingredient Disclosure – New regulations pending for 100% disclosure, New York and California</li>
<li>Letters of Guarantee, review products against former USDA, follow checklist for approval</li>
<li>Formula Disclosure / needs Confidentiality Agreement</li>
</ul>
<p><b>General</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Maintains knowledge of regulatory laws and requirements through continued education
</li>
<li>Supports Product Stewardship initiatives
</li>
<li>Assists with maintaining the regulatory budget
</li>
<li>Participate in company and ISO meetings as required
</li>
<li>Assists with contracts and BHC’s website to ensure compliance
</li>
<li>Answer internal and external questions</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Skills &amp; Requirements:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor’s degree preferably in life sciences or related field</li>
<li>2-5 years relevant experience in regulatory environment</li>
<li>Chemical background or lab experience preferred</li>
<li>Understanding of Domestic and International regulations</li>
<li>Basic knowledge and understanding of various regulatory reporting requirements</li>
<li>Knowledge of OSHA (29CFR), EPA (40CFR), and DOT (49CFR) regulations</li>
<li>Proven abilities as a self-starter</li>
<li>Must have proficient PC skills in Microsoft Office programs</li>
<li>Ability to communicate effectively orally and in writing</li>
<li>Ability to organize, work under stress and handle many tasks at one time</li>
</ul>
