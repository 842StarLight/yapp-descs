<h2>Buyer</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<ul>
<li>Plan purchasing requirements based on customer volume, menu selection and production scheduling specifications.</li>
<li>Ensure proper inventory levels of Dry Goods, meats, fish, poultry, fruits and vegetables are on hand.</li>
<li>Coordinate product delivery schedules to ensure items remain fresh to minimize spoilage.</li>
<li>Coordinate a just-in-time delivery process for certain high volume and perishable products.</li>
<li>Implement purchasing procedures to ensure effective product cost control.</li>
<li>Evaluate and select vendors that meet quality, price and delivery specification.</li>
<li>Analyze and interpret numerical data and perform basic financial analysis.</li>
<li>Report item cost of goods purchased compared to projected cost for menu items.</li>
<li>Facilitate uniform quality standards in food safety.</li>
<li>Ensure compliance of Corporate Foodservice Standards.</li>
<li>Responsible for ETEC system accuracy for purchasing and receiving product.</li>
<li>Conduct periodic inventory of purchased product storerooms</li>
</ul>
<br>
<b>Other Responsibilities and duties will include, but not be limited to:
</b><ul>
<li>Competent to work independently within area of expertise</li>
<li>Ability to solve purchasing related problems and deal with a variety of pricing variables in situations where some standardization exists.
</li>
<li>Must be able to function within a team environment.</li>
<li>Performs tasks under general supervision.</li>
</ul>
<br>
<b>Qualifications:</b>
<ul>
<li>Food Service experience required</li>
<li>A minimum of 4 years supervising a purchasing function</li>
<li>Knowledge of Food Industry quality and sanitation standards and requirements</li>
<li>Knowledge of ETEC or other purchasing IS software</li>
<li>Excellent written and verbal communication skills
</li>
<li>High degree of integrity and openness in reporting responsibilities</li>
<li>Proficient with Microsoft Office suite, specifically in Excel<br>
</li>
</ul>
