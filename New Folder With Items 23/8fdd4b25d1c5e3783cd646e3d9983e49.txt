<h2>Product Analyst</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><b><i>PowerLine provides transactional-based services for the pharmacy industry driven by reliable technology and responsive customer support.
</i></b></p>
<p><b>Who Are We Looking For: </b>At the highest level, we are seeking an intuitive problem solver. As a <b>Product Analyst,</b> you will develop moderately complex functional specifications to assist in traditional development activities such as a user interface design, documentation, training, testing, and functional reviews. Reports to a designated manager. Typically requires a minimum of three years' experience as a product analyst.</p>
<p><b>Major Duties</b>:</p>
<ul>
<li>Serves as a liaison between the PM team and marketing, sales and client services as it relates to product enhancement or features.</li>
<li>Gathers and documents functional, system, and data requirements for assigned product features.</li>
<li>Develops functional specification within VersionOne for assigned features</li>
<li>Responsible for grooming functional specifications with PM, Dev and QA to verify accuracy and product consistency.</li>
<li>Assists with user interface design and documentation.</li>
<li>Assists with development of training materials used to train the end user.</li>
<li>Assesses available technologies and recommends solutions to PM team.</li>
<li>Conducts customer site visit for consultations and research as required.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Specialized Knowledge and Skills:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Knowledge of software development methodologies, design, and implementation with a specialized knowledge of assigned QS/1 applications.</li>
<li>Analytical and design skills at multiple product levels.</li>
<li>Must possess research and analysis, project planning, and implementation.</li>
<li>Good understanding of business issues and how they impact overall project plans.</li>
<li>Good time management, decision making, organization, presentation, and problem resolutions skills.</li>
<li>Good communication, human relations and interpersonal skills.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>What Education You'll Need to Have:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor's degree in computer science, software engineering, business, or mathematics.</li>
<li>Typically requires a minimum of three years' experience in product development and/or product support.</li>
<li>Relevant experience plus an AS degree may substitute for bachelor's degree.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>What Experience Would Be Beneficial:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Prior experience, specifically with the claims adjudication process or with ePrescribing.</li>
</ul>
