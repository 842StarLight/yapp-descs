<h2>RECREATION LEADER I - YOUTH AND ADULT SPORTS</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<p></p>
<div><div>
<div>
<b>Job Title: RECREATION LEADER I</b><br>
<b>Department:</b> Parks and Recreation<br>
<b>Reports To:</b> Recreation Coordinator or Recreation Supervisor<br>
<b>FLSA Status:</b> Non-Exempt<br>
<b>Job Status:</b> Part-time Seasonal; Non-Classified; Safety Sensitive<br>
<b>Pay Grade:</b> REC1<br>
<br>
<b>SUMMARY</b><br>
Incumbents support a variety of daily recreational services and programs, including youth and adult sports leagues, youth/teen programs, tennis, adaptive programs, campus operations, and special events. Incumbents are part of daily operations, set up site and equipment, assist in scheduling and conducting activities, perform customer service, and compile simple reports. Incumbents may serve as cashiers receiving checks and cash.</div>
<p></p>
<div></div>
<div>
<p><b>ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES</b><br>
<br>
The duties listed below are intended only as general illustrations of the various types of work that may be performed. Specific statements of duties not included does not exclude them from the position if the work is similar, related, or a logical assignment to the position. Job descriptions are subject to change by the City as the needs of the City and requirements of the job change.</p>
<ul>
<li>Assists a Recreation Leader II or III in planning and conducting a variety of recreational services and activities for children of all ages and adults at a recreation facility
</li>
<li>Roves/maintains security and safety of facilities throughout the shift; ensures the proper maintenance of recreational facilities and equipment to provide a safe and sanitary environment, and notifies supervisor of any problems and/or concerns
</li>
<li>Notifies patrons of activity schedules and registration requirements
</li>
<li>Monitors spectators and participants on all age levels and/or special populations at a variety of recreation events, spring training games, trips, tours, and adaptive programs to ensure safety and orderly conduct
</li>
<li>Officiates youth games and events, maintains standards of play and ensures game rules are observed, and enforces rules of play on the field/court during youth sports games
</li>
<li>Performs a variety of clerical activities in support of office operations, which may include entering information into databases, answering phones, filing, maintaining calendars of events, delivering flyers, and/or performing other related activities
</li>
<li>Monitors sites, picks up paper and trash
</li>
<li>Responds to requests for information from the general public
</li>
<li>Performs inventory of maintenance activities
</li>
<li>May be required to perform cashier duties during a scheduled shift, which may include accepting payment of fees, processing ticket sales, handling cash, issuing passes, and completing all necessary reporting and recordkeeping documentation
</li>
<li>Completes written reports, maintains accurate written records of accidents and incidents that occur on park grounds or in recreation facilities, notifies supervisor of accidents and incidents, and keeps accurate recording of time worked
</li>
<li>Provides customer service support to department, welcomes customers/visitors, answers incoming telephone calls, and handles customer inquiries
</li>
<li>Performs the essential duties of the job in the conditions described in the physical demands and work environment sections below
</li>
<li>Maintains regular attendance and punctuality
</li>
<li>Performs other duties of a similar nature or level</li>
</ul>
<b>
SUPERVISORY RESPONSIBILITIES</b><br>
None.</div>

<p><b>QUALIFICATIONS</b><br>
<br>
To perform this job successfully, an individual must be able to perform each essential duty satisfactorily. The requirements listed below are representative of the knowledge, skill, and/or ability required. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform essential functions.<br>
<br>
Knowledge of:</p>
<ul>
<li>Principles and practices of public recreation
</li>
<li>First aid methods and safety precautions utilized in recreation
</li>
<li>Rules and regulations governing various competitive athletic games such as softball, baseball, basketball, hockey, etc.
</li>
<li>A variety of active and passive game activities suitable for children, adolescents, adults, senior citizens, and/or special populations
</li>
<li>Tools and techniques of teaching music, drama, crafts, sports, or other specialties involved
</li>
<li>Basic recordkeeping practices and procedures
</li>
<li>Basic bookkeeping practices and principles
</li>
<li>Time management, and organizational practices and procedures
</li>
<li>Basic arithmetic such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division
</li>
<li>Proper English, spelling, and grammar
</li>
<li>Modern office practices, procedures, methods, and computer equipment and software applications
</li>
<li>Basic recreation operations in assigned area of responsibility
</li>
<li>Customer service principles
</li>
<li>Handling cash<br>
</li>
</ul>Skill in:<ul>
<li>Using standard office equipment including a telephone, 10-key calculator, and copy and fax machines
</li>
<li>Using a computer and related software applications<br>
</li>
</ul>Ability to:<ul>
<li>Provide customer service
</li>
<li>Perform basic bookkeeping
</li>
<li>Assist with implementing recreational programs, special events, and activities
</li>
<li>Work as a team player with other City staff
</li>
<li>Work flexible hours to include evenings, weekends, and holidays
</li>
<li>Work cooperatively with groups of all age levels and socioeconomic backgrounds
</li>
<li>Work safely without presenting a direct threat to self or others
</li>
<li>Learn job-related responsibilities primarily through oral instruction and observation which take place mainly in an on-the-job-training setting
</li>
<li>Communicate and use interpersonal skills to interact with coworkers, supervisor, the general public, etc., to exchange or convey information and receive work direction<br>
</li>
</ul>
<b>EDUCATION and/or EXPERIENCE</b><br>
Any combination of education and experience which provides the knowledge, skills, and abilities to perform the work.<br>
<br>
<b>CERTIFICATIONS, LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS</b><br>

<div><p><b>PHYSICAL DEMANDS</b><br>
<br>
The physical demands described here are representative of those that must be met by an employee to successfully perform the essential functions of this job. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions.<br>
<br>
While performing the duties of this job, the employee is regularly required to walk, reach with hands and arms, and talk or hear. The employee frequently is required to stand; use hands to finger, handle, or feel; climb or balance; stoop, kneel, crouch, or crawl; and taste or smell. May require continuous and repetitive arm, hand, and eye movement. The employee is occasionally required to sit. The employee must regularly lift and/or move up to 50 pounds of force occasionally, and/or up to 20 pounds of force frequently, and/or up to 10 pounds of force constantly to move objects.<br>
<br>
<b>WORK ENVIRONMENT</b><br>
The work environment characteristics described here are representative of those an employee encounters while performing the essential functions of this job. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions.<br>
<br>
Based on assignment to program or services, incumbents may be subjected to chemicals, extreme temperatures, and intense noises for extended periods of time, and moderate exposure to dust, pollen, noise, inclement weather, temperature extremes, and uneven terrain. Assignments to office or administrative work are not substantially exposed to adverse environmental conditions.<br>
<br>
Due to the safety sensitive nature of this position, candidates must successfully pass a pre-employment drug screening prior to start date.</p></div>
