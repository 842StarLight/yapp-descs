<p><b>Development Manager – Performing Arts Services, University of Illinois Springfield</b></p>
<p>The department of UIS Performing Arts Services seeks a Development Manager. This position manages the FRIENDS of UIS Performing Arts Center program which includes responsibility for developing and implementing the overall fundraising and donor development strategy for the Performing Arts Services unit with direct responsibility for recruiting volunteers and personally soliciting donations of less than $25,000. This position also assists the UIS Advancement Office with identifying and developing donors for gifts greater than $25,000. Developing and managing the unit’s corporate sponsorship program (excluding basic program book advertising sales or media trade partners) is also a responsibility of this position.</p>
<p>UIS is a small public university offering innovative, high-quality liberal arts education, professional programs, and public affairs activities. Performing Arts Services manages the UIS Performing Arts Center comprised of two performance venues: The 2,000-seat Sangamon Auditorium and the 125-seats UIS Studio Theatre. The Performing Arts Center hosts over 100 public events annually attracting over 77,000 patrons during the last school year.</p>
<p>The Development Manager will primarily work a normal daily work a variable schedule but duties will include attendance at major events to allow for donor interaction, including evenings and weekends, driven by the event calendar.</p>
<p>More information about the UIS Performing Arts Center can be found online at UISpac.com</p>
<p>Minimum requirements: Bachelor’s degree plus a minimum of three years previous experience in fundraising in a not-for-profit or higher education setting is required. Also required is general knowledge of the performing arts and the ability to communicate to others the social benefits of the performing arts. Previous fundraising experience in the field of the performing arts is highly desirable.</p>
<p>The position is a 100% full-time equivalent position with a variable working schedule determined by the event schedule. Projected salary of $52,785 annually with exceptional vacation, health and retirement benefits package.</p>
<p>Please submit a cover letter, resume, and contact information for three professional references. Applicant screening begins immediately and will continue until the position is filled or the search terminated; for full consideration, please apply by September 13. Projected start date for the position is November 1 or as soon as possible.</p>

<div>Duties:
</div>
<div>
<div>
<p><b>Typical Duties/Responsibilities</b>:</p>
<p>Donor Relationship Development</p>
<p>Works to develop positive relationships with current donors, volunteers and prospects. This includes one-on-one meetings (i.e. lunches, home visits, etc.), attendance at performing arts center events to greet donors, volunteers and prospects, phone calls to donors, handling of donor requests (i.e. access to prime seats at shows or meeting the artist), providing backstage tours, representing the unit at funerals/visitations, etc. Ensures active donors and volunteers receive all appropriate “perks” or “benefits” afforded to donors such as advance ticket purchase opportunities for events at the Performing Arts Center. Involves the unit Director for higher-value donors as deemed appropriate.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Donor Solicitation</p>
<p>Proactively asks prospects for donations. This includes mass requests for smaller amounts (such as large mailings) as well as one-on-one solicitation for gifts in the $1,000 - $25,000 range.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Donor Acknowledgement &amp; Recognition</p>
<p>Acknowledges all donations in a timely manner and ensures all recognition of donations is provided as appropriate. This includes the mailing of thank-you letters, phone calls or personal visits to thank higher level donors, and ensuring all public recognition is up-to-date such as program book listings and lobby donor walls listings.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Special Event Planning &amp; Execution</p>
<p>Develops and manages any special events related to the FRIENDS of UIS Performing Arts Center or for donor prospect development. This includes special pre &amp; post-show events held in conjunction with a public event occurring in the Performing Arts Center or special private events such as gala dinners/fundraisers. Works closely with the unit’s House Manager to plan end-of-year volunteer recognition events.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Campaign Strategy/Planning</p>
<p>Develops an overall short-term and long-term development strategy/plan for the unit to continually increase the overall number of donors and donations to the unit. Ensures this strategy works in harmony with UIS Advancement Office’s overall university development plan and best practices. Develops a strategy/plan to ensure the unit always has an optimum number of active volunteers to work events at the Performing Arts Center. Communicates these plans effectively to the unit’s leadership team and to the UIS Advancement Office staff. Develops a corporate sponsorship program for the unit.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Volunteer Recruitment</p>
<p>Actively implements the volunteer recruitment plan to ensure the unit maintains an active volunteer pool of sufficient numbers to meet its event staffing requirements.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Donor Research</p>
<p>Analyzes data from unit’s ticketing system, the UI Foundation’s donor management system, UIS Advancement Office staff, publicly available information, and information acquired from other individuals to identify potential donors and to categorize them by potential giving levels.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Corporate Sponsorship &amp; Grants</p>
<p>Identifies, solicits and manages unit’s corporate sponsors (excluding basic program book advertising sales or media partners). Serves as the unit’s grant researcher and writer. Researches possible grants. Coordinates any grant solicitations and reporting with the appropriate UIS offices.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Coordination with UIS Advancement Office</p>
<p>Serves as the unit’s representative to the UIS Advancement Office. Attends Advancement Office regular planning meetings and other meetings as needed. Continually communicates relevant donor research information to the UIS Advancement Office staff, especially for higher level prospects which can be future major-gift donor prospects (greater than $25,000). Works cooperatively with the UIS Advancement Office Director of Development assigned to the unit for major gift development to assist with major donor recruitment and development. Coordinates all mass donor solicitation campaigns with the UIS Advancement Office staff.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Record Keeping</p>
<p>Maintains accurate records of all current volunteers &amp; donors, donations received, individual donor recognition preferences, etc. Ensures the databases used UI Foundation and by the unit’s marketing staff for communicating to donors and volunteers is always up-to-date and accurate. Processes change of address notifications and “do not solicit” requests.</p>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Other Duties</p>
<p>Other duties as assigned. This may include representing the unit at campus fundraising events, providing staff support to the UIS Advancement Office for their major events, speaking appearances at local clubs/service organizations, assisting the Ticket Office on major on-sale days, etc. It is noted here for clarification purposes that this position, while responsible for recruiting volunteers and ensuring an ongoing positive relationship between the volunteers and the unit, is specifically NOT responsible for volunteer training, scheduling, work assignments, or performance evaluations.</p>
