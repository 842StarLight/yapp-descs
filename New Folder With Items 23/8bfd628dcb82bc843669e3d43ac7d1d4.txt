<h2>Regional Marketing Manager, West</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText">
<div>
<p><b>Flatiron School</b> is looking for a<b> </b><b>Regional Marketing Manager</b> based in Seattle to start support our West regional marketing efforts! In this role you’ll have the unique opportunity to drive student acquisition and build brand awareness in some of our West markets to support our existing campus, launch new campuses and programs, and create and deploy successful marketing tactics and campaigns regionally. You’ll collaborate with marketing channel leads from our centralized team to deploy a hyperlocal omni-channel marketing strategy that achieves applicant and enrollment targets in these markets.</p>
<p></p>
<p>
The Regional Marketing Manager will be responsible for developing and executing the overall marketing plan to achieve our student application targets for every course available in the West - Denver &amp; Seattle - from our on-campus Software Engineering, Data Science, and UX/UI Design Courses to our Online offerings. We’re looking for a strategic thinker and strong leader who’s also able to get in the weeds and tactically execute while also overseeing or mentoring other marketers. The ideal candidate has data-driven omni channel marketing experience with a passion for user research and market positioning.</p>
<p></p>
<p>
You’ll collaborate closely with the regional commercial leadership, the admissions team, and marketing channel leads.</p>
<p></p>
<p><b>
KEY RESPONSIBILITIES:</b></p>
<ul>
<li><p>
Develop and execute successful local field marketing programs to drive awareness and qualified applications.</p></li>
<li><p>
Funnel Performance: Actively manage each local funnel. Surface trends and insights and work together with a Sr. Manager overseeing the West to deploy creative solutions and tactics to increase performance.</p></li>
<li><p>
Reporting: Develop and report weekly on key KPIs (visitors through enrollments) for Seattle &amp; Denver's programs.</p></li>
<li><p>
Local Marketing Mix: Determine local marketing mix to drive acquisition success and plan out how local budget will be deployed across different marketing tactics on a monthly and quarterly basis.</p></li>
<li><p>
Market and Campaign Launches: Execute, and help project manage new market, new product line, and new feature launches.</p></li>
<li><p>
Act as an expert in the West Coast markets specifically Seattle &amp; Denver for the marketing team and use that knowledge to help localize marketing assets and messaging.</p></li>
<li><p>
Support Campaign Development: Turn data-driven and user research discoveries into ideas that develop into acquisition and engagement campaigns.</p></li>
<li><p>
Ideate and Execute Research: Identify key prospective student needs in the UK through user research, data analysis, and testing. Go deep on understanding local prospective customers.</p></li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><b>
WHAT WE’RE LOOKING FOR:</b></p>
<ul>
<li><p>
5+ years experience in a regional marketing, field marketing, or omni-channel marketing.</p></li>
<li><p>
Detailed oriented and able to execute a project from start to finish</p></li>
<li><p>
People management experience a plus</p></li>
<li><p>
Experience overseeing events programs and field marketing</p></li>
<li><p>
Experience building local marketing plans.</p></li>
<li><p>
Scrappiness: You seek out creative ways to personally execute on the projects you put into motion.</p></li>
<li><p>
Skilled with data and market research: You’re good at quickly analyzing data and user research and turning it into actionable, digestible insights.</p></li>
<li><p>
Bias for action: You’re someone who tends to push the pace of innovation by moving fast and testing efficiently.</p></li>
<li><p>
Excellent storyteller: You’re a natural at conveying our core value proposition to the user, and crafting the right motivational nudges with effective copy.</p></li>
<li><p>
Passion for education and our product: You love what Flatiron School is about and truly think our product can make a difference.</p></li>
</ul>
