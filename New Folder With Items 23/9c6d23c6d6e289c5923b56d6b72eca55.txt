<h2>Durable Medical Equipment Repair Technician - Carle Medical Supply</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div><div><div>
<div>
<div>
<p>Position Summary:<br>
The Carle Medical Supply (CMS) Equipment Specialist is a self-motivated individual that is multi-tasking orientated, with customer service and mechanical skills. Holds a valid driver's license and is certified to work on a variety of home medical equipment. Specializes in the knowledge base of function, coverage criteria, authorization, and sales of lift chairs, power operated vehicles (POVs) / wheelchairs, and vehicle lift installs.
</p>
</div>
<div><p>Qualifications:<br>
<b>EDUCATIONAL REQUIREMENTS</b><br>
None specified<br>
<br>
<b>CERTIFICATION &amp; LICENSURE REQUIREMENTS</b><br>
Durable Medical Equipment (DME) and Driver's License. Must obtain DME certification pertaining to product lines sold and serviced within one (1) year of start date in position.<br>
<br>
<b>EXPERIENCE REQUIREMENTS</b><br>
Mechanics<br>
<br>
<b>OTHER REQUIREMENTS</b><br>
Must possess the following skills: computer/software, mechanical/electrical, trouble shooting, inventory, and customer service. Good multi-tasker and knowledge of DME billing.<br>
</p></div>
<div>
<p>Essential Functions:<br>
</p>
<ul>
<li>Answers incoming repair calls, provides trouble shooting techniques and makes in-home repair/service calls as needed.</li>
<li>
Collaborates closely with supervisor to ensure all daily assignments are completed in a timely fashion and all information is documented correctly.</li>
<li>
Delivers equipment or makes arrangements for delivery to verify fit, equipment function, and performance with complete, accurate documentation.</li>
<li>
Develops and maintains service manuals and catalogs (electronic and on shelf) for equipment repaired and sold by company.</li>
<li>
Ensures all required authorizations, verifications, and waivers for proper payment are processed.</li>
<li>
Evaluates the needs of patients (customers) pursuing purchases of Lift chairs, POV - Scooters, and Vehicle lifts to determine each patient's qualifications to meet coverage criteria required for equipment purchase. Knows equipment requirements as related to patient assessment and needs. Ensures proper prescriptions, documentation, authorizations and co-payments are in place.</li>
<li>
Explains and demonstrates correct operation and preventive maintenance of medical equipment as needed.</li>
<li>
Maintains accurate records of all sales with follow-up checks to ensure all payments and balances are fully collected.</li>
<li>
Maintains accurate repair and preventative maintenance records on all rental equipment.</li>
<li>
Maintains adequate tool inventory for both oxygen and non oxygen equipment.</li>
<li>
Maintains and monitors inventory for appropriate levels.</li>
<li>
Orders and receives repair inventory for customers and departmental needs.</li>
<li>
Places and receives orders for appropriate, approved equipment.</li>
<li>
Repairs all types of customer owned equipment and generates appropriate service order.</li>
<li>
Sets appointments and conducts demonstrations, fittings, and home evaluation for use of in-home equipment.</li>
<li>
Test and calibrate components and equipment following manufacturers' manuals and troubleshooting techniques, using hand tools, power tools and measuring devices.</li>
<li>
Utilizes all manufacturer's warranty options for both rental and customer owned equipment repairs.</li>
</ul>
