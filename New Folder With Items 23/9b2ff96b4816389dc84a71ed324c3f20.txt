<h2>Customer Service Coordinator, Textile Museum</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p>The GWU Museums is searching for a sales oriented Customer Service Coordinator to provide direct customer service to community patrons under direct supervision. This role typically handles routine customer questions and concerns, and escalates complex issues to management. Most interactions are transactional and routine in nature, often involving or revolving around a point of sale. This role is responsible for maintaining a balanced register, receipts, documenting customer concerns, maintaining a well-organized and presentable environment, and participating in applicable events highlighting the organization.</p>
<p>
Located in the heart of George Washington University’s Foggy Bottom Campus, Washington, D.C.’s newest cultural destination fosters the study and appreciation of art, history, and culture—both within the university and throughout the global community. As a cornerstone of the university’s growing focus on arts and culture, the museum unites The Textile Museum, The Albert H. Small Washingtoniana Collections, and artwork from the University’s collections.</p>
<p>
The Customer Service Coordinator is responsible for:</p>
<ul>
<li>Museum Retail Store responsibilities include, overseeing cash handling and customer service performance of other retail store employees; preparing, analyzing, and reconciling all financial reports; maintaining accurate inventories; assisting with software system maintenance and upgrades; and assisting with the development and implementation of marketing plans for merchandise.</li>
<li>Receptionist responsibilities include greeting and welcoming visitors to the Museum and being the first point of contact for visitor information; handling incoming telephone calls and queries; liaising with Facilities to ensure safety, security, and compliance; overseeing and reconciling admissions donations; and maintaining the education program registration and processing payments and registration for other Museum programs.</li>
</ul>
<p>The position is based at GW’s Foggy Bottom Campus in Washington, DC. The incumbent may perform other related duties as assigned. The omission of specific duties does not preclude the supervisor from assigning duties that are logically related to the position.</p>
Minimum Qualifications:
<p>Qualified candidates will hold a high school diploma/GED plus 1.5 years of relevant professional experience, or, a Bachelor’s degree or higher in a relevant area of study. Degree must be conferred by the start date of the position. Degree requirements may be substituted with an equivalent combination of education, training and experience.</p>
Additional Required Licenses/Certifications/Posting Specific Minimum Qualifications: Preferred Qualifications:
<ul>
<li>Merchandising and display skills.</li>
<li>Strong sales oriented knowledge, abilities and skills.</li>
<li>Demonstrate excellent customer service, verbal and written communication skills.</li>
<li>Demonstrated skills in operating a personal computer, using word processing spreadsheet and database formats required by the museum and university: Word for Windows, Excel, Photoshop and others.</li>
<li>Strong ability to communicate with diplomacy, tact and confidence.</li>
<li>Ability to meet deadlines and handle concurrent tasks with minimum supervision.</li>
<li>Demonstrate knowledge of or ability to learn University online purchasing.</li>
<li>Experience supervising volunteer and student staff.</li>
</ul>Typical Hiring Range Commensurate with Experience How is pay for new employees determined at GW?
