<h2>Mechanical Electrical Systems Engineer</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<p><b>Fit To Operate (FTO) Team Member – FTO Technician</b>
<br>
Clyde Bergemann needs team members committed to personal and professional development to join our Fit To Operate Department. We are seeking a friendly, outgoing professional who knows about industrial equipment and can work to ensure we assist our customers with determining the operational fitness their equipment. Maintaining a high level of customer engagement and satisfaction is critical to your success in this role. Our Fit To Operate Department is a Team driven environment where we focus on personal development so that we may better serve our customers and each other. We need someone with strong desire to learn, grow, and lead by example. A personal commitment to service is a must. If you love working in a dynamic, fast-paced environment, we’re excited to discuss this position with you.<br>
</p>
<p><b>General Purpose:</b></p>
<p>The FTO Team Member is responsible for performing a full range of field survey duties for boiler cleaning equipment from various manufacturers. Assignments include electrical and mechanical equipment inspections, diagnosis and correction of problems with equipment, system condition assessments, system performance assessments, instruction of customer personnel on operation, maintenance and servicing of equipment, proposal development and technical sales support and professional technical report writing.<br>
<br>
<b>Responsibilities/Task List:</b><br>
1. Conduct field surveys in support of Boiler Studies assessing the mechanical fitness of equipment on industrial and power boilers.<br>
2. Good verbal and written communication skills a must.<br>
3. Ability to work in the US and Canada preferred.<br>
4. Position can be home based.<br>
5. Must have multi craft maintenance electrical/mechanical skills<br>
6. PLC experience a plus.<br>
7. Must be comfortable working around industrial boiler equipment.<br>
8. Working knowledge of pumps, motors, pneumatics and general mechanical ability are a must.<br>
9. Able to work in confined spaces required.<br>
10. Experience with UT and Phased Array NDT a plus<br>
11. Additional business development<br>
a. Work with Business Development Department and Sales Reps to support clients with mechanical risk assessments.<br>
b. Travel to companies and installations and conduct evaluations of existing maintain boiler cleaning equipment and port rodders,<br>
c. Coordinate with Subject Matter Experts (SME’s) to perform credible risk assessments and economic evaluations at plant sites as to</p>
 <p>whether to rebuild/refurbish/replace boiler cleaning equipment.<br>
12. Service driven attitude.<br>
13. Strong desire to develop personally and professionally.<br>
14. Provide training to customers to broaden awareness and knowledge of the equipment and risks.<br>
15. Previous experience working with sootblowers or industrial boilers in the pulp/paper or utility industries is a plus.<br>
16. Welding experience a plus.</p>
<br>
<p><br>
<b>Education and/or Training:</b></p>
<p>Cross-discipline (Mechanical/Electrical) familiarity is preferred. Periodic educational updating. Good written and oral communications ability and interpersonal skills. Must be a self-starter and able to work with minimal supervision. Good knowledge of computer operations.<br>
</p>
<p><b>Physical Requirements:</b></p>
<p>Clear speech, hearing/listening, well-groomed appearance, ability to move distances, standing, walking, sitting, seeing.<br>
Travel: Extensive (70-80%) US, Canada and South America travel and at times on short notice, and some foreign travel may be required. Candidate must meet the requirements to work in Canada.<br>
</p>
<p><b>Environmental Working Conditions:</b></p>
<p>Work is performed inside and outside in an industrial environment (i.e., power plants, paper mills, refineries, etc.).</p>
