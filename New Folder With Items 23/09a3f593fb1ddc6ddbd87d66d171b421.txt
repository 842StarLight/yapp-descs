<h2>PURCHASING AGENT (WAREHOUSE SUPERVISOR)</h2> 
 <div id="jobDescriptionText" class="jobsearch-jobDescriptionText"><div>
<div><b>PURCHASING AGENT</b></div>
<div><b>JOB DESCRIPTION</b></div>
<div></div>
<div>POSITION TITLE: PURCHASING AGENT</div>
<div>POSITION ID NUMBER: 4401</div>
<div>DEPARTMENT: ACCOUNTING &amp; FINANCE</div>
<div>REPORTS TO: VICE PRESIDENT OF ACCOUNTING &amp; FINANCE</div>
<div>PAY GRADE: 53 - $55,598.40 PER YEAR</div>
<div>FLSA STATUS: EXEMPT-EXECUTIVE</div>
<div>KEY WORDS : WAREHOUSE ; SUPERVISOR ; PROCUREMENT ; PURCHASING ; SHIPPING ; RECEIVING ; COACH ;</div>
<div></div>
<div>
<b>Job Summary</b><b>: </b>Supervises the District’s procurement, supply chain and facilities maintenance operations; purchases materials and contracts services to fulfill District needs. Investigates new developments relative to materials, supplies, and services; makes recommendations designed to reduce costs and improve quality. Manages facilities maintenance and repair actions. Develops facility capital needs and repairs for budget planning and executes contracts to support the improvements and repairs.</div>
<div></div>
<div><b>Incumbents in this position will be held to the District’s Gold Standard for Customer Service.</b></div>
<div></div>
<div>
<b>ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS AND RESPONSIBILITIES</b><b>:</b>
</div>
<ul>
<li>Responsible for developing and conducting District strategic and non-strategic procurement following guidelines in State Statutes, the Special District Handbook, the District Policy Manual and the Uniform Commercial Code.</li>
<li>Determines the method of procurement. Accomplishes commodity and service purchasing actions below $25,000; generates purchases through quote solicitations, the formal bids/proposal process, or through the use of State of Florida term contracts and cooperative purchasing agreements.</li>
<li>Develops bid documents and specifications for competitive bids. Serves as the central point of contact for bids, conducts bid opening, and approves bid tabulations.</li>
<li>Prepares contracts from bid awards, manages expirations, renewals, and change orders to contracts.</li>
<li>Reviews and processes requisitions for purchases and checks for accuracy and conformance with District policies.</li>
<li>Manages the District’s supply chain, manages inventory levels to avoid shortages, and coordinates delivery schedules to support the needs of end users and to maximize construction and maintenance efficiency. Maintains inventory and stock records, prepares reorder reports, and enters requisitions for purchases to replenish inventory.</li>
<li>Investigates new developments relative to materials, supplies, and services; makes recommendations designed to reduce costs, improve quality and reduce waste stream output.</li>
<li>Assists departments with selection of materials, supplies, and equipment including furnishing prices, available products and sources of supply.</li>
<li>Develops and uses blanket purchase orders to sustain the District’s supply chain.</li>
<li>Evaluates and approves vendors. Monitors open purchase order reports for vendor performance related to on-time delivery and coordinates corrective action as needed.</li>
<li>Manages the receipt, handling, and storage of all materials in the warehouse. Discusses defective or unacceptable materials with users, vendors, and others to determine corrective actions.</li>
<li>Manages the flow of inventory between central and mobile warehouses.</li>
<li>Maintains Material Specification Data Sheets (MSDSs) / Safety Data Sheets (SDSs) as described within the District’s Hazardous Communication Program.</li>
<li>Manages the sale and disposal of surplus property. Ensures required custody/disposal forms are completed. Estimates value of surplus property according to knowledge of market price.</li>
<li>Manages the removal of obsolete inventory.</li>
<li>Selects, trains, supervises, and evaluates staff. Coordinates employee schedules and leave time to ensure the distribution center/warehouse and facilities maintenance operations are properly staffed.</li>
<li>Manages facility repair and maintenance activities including scheduled preventative maintenance.</li>
<li>Defines facility capital improvement projects and O&amp;M facility repairs for the District budget and executes projects.</li>
<li>Prepares and administers division budgets.</li>
<li>Develops and maintains appropriate metrics to ensure efficient operation of the purchasing, warehouse, and facilities functions.</li>
<li>Develops and maintains policy and procedure manuals related to areas of responsibility.</li>
<li>Other miscellaneous job-related duties as assigned.</li>
</ul>
<div>The preceding is intended to describe the general content of and requirements for the performance of this job. It is not to be construed as an exhaustive statement of duties, responsibilities or requirements. The principal duties and responsibilities enumerated are all essential job functions.</div>
<div></div>
<div>
<b>EDUCATION AND EXPERIENCE</b><b>:</b>
</div>
<div>Associate Degree plus five to nine years of experience – or</div>
<div>Bachelor’s Degree plus two to five years of experience – or</div>
<div>Master’s Degree plus zero to two years of experience.</div>
<div>Certification as a Certified Public Purchasing Buyer (CPPB) and/or Certified Public Purchasing Official (CPPO) desired.</div>
<div></div>
<div><b>ESSENTIAL COMPETENCIES:</b></div>
<div>1. Strong interpersonal and communication skills with the ability to work effectively among a wide range of people in a diverse environment; skills to foster cooperative work environment.</div>
<div>2. Capacity for making administrative/procedural decisions and judgments.</div>
<div>3. Aptitude to develop, plan, and implement short and long-range goals.</div>
<div>4. Reasoning skills to assess contract compliance and product/service quality.</div>
<div>5. Ability to evaluate procurement bids, quotes, and proposals in accordance with established criteria.</div>
<div>6. Understanding of total ownership cost, price analysis, and life cycle costs.</div>
<div></div>
<div>
<b>TECHNICAL SKILLS REQUIRED</b><b>:</b>
</div>
<div>1. Must possess and maintain valid Florida driver’s license and a driving record satisfactory to the District and its insurers.</div>
<div>2. Ability to communicate effectively, both orally and in writing.</div>
<div>3. Knowledge of purchasing principals, contracting processes, and associated local, state, federal, and other applicable regulations.</div>
<div>4. Knowledge of automated purchasing and inventory systems desired.</div>
<div>5. Ability to create and maintain policy and procedure manuals.</div>
<div>6. Knowledge of internal controls for inventory commodities and non-capital assets.</div>
<div>7. Strong organizational skills and knowledgeable, accepted record keeping practices.</div>
<div>8. Skill in use of personal computer with proficiency in using MS Office (Word, Excel, PowerPoint); knowledgeable of applicable computer purchasing and inventory software application; working knowledge of District’s general ledger as needed for inquiry.</div>
<div>9. Managerial skills to supervise and train employees, to include organizing, prioritizing, and scheduling work assignments; demonstrated ability to deliver accurate and detailed work.</div>
<div></div>
